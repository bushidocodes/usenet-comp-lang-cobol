something like:

SRCHFOR 'BILL'

Would be the equivalent of BILL AND BOB AND JOE

SRCHFORC 'BOB'
DPLINE 'JOE'
DPLINE 'BILL'

Would be the equivalent of BOB NOT ( BILL OR JOE)

To properly parse your input first you might want to remove multiple spaces
from the input, then use the UNSTRING command and DELIMITTED BY SPACES to
pull off each key word for processing. However the rst of the explaination

At first I thought that this sounded like a "homework" request - then I
thought that it sounded like a REALLY HARD homework request - then I thought
that it is a good prompt for the "right tool for the right job" statement.
(I would fully expect a REXX or PERL - or even SAS or SPITBOL fan to jump in
at any time to tell us how easy it would be in one of those languages), but
regardless of the background, let me outline how I would go about solving
this NON-trivial task.

First, as I understand it, you have two "inputs" - one includes the "boolean
command" and one the "string to be searched".  (Assuming this is correct), I
think you need to

1) Tokenize your boolean command.  Then syntax scan it (for "valid" commands
and "strings" and combinations of the two).

2) Once you have the tokens to be looked for (from the boolean command), you
need to set up a "truth table" based on the boolean operators in this input.
(Again, a non-trivial task - for example, what do you do about parenthesis -
does NOT apply only to the following operator or all subsequent operators -
can you parenthesis what is after the not, etc, ETC)

3) Finally, (and this is almost the easiest part - in COBOL), you need to
scan the other input for each of the strings requested by the boolean
commands - and check their presence/absence with your truth table.

   ***

As I say, in COBOL, (certainly depending on what constraints there are on
your 2 inputs), this would be a fairly complex "assignment".
