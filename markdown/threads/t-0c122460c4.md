I can provide a little testimonial on the UNIX version of Unikix.

The company I work for has been installing the Unikix product at our
 client sites for over 6 years now.  In the early days, most things
 worked quite well but there were problems.  The UNIX version of
 UniKix has matured very well.

Now I can't quite say the same thing for the NT version.  I was just
 brought to help finish up our first NT install and from what I've
 seen so far, the NT version is not quite ready for prime time.  It
 is functional and most everything that I have used appears to work
 properly.  Maybe it's just the transition from the (stable) UNIX
 world to the (finicky) NT world.

One area that is causing us tremendous grief is the shell environment
 itself.  We have developed an entire suite of tools (via UNIX
 scripts mostly) to control and maintain the Unikix regions.  Our
 tools ported over to NT after extensive modifications - that was to
 be expected.

One item we're trying to understand better is the kxloadenv program
 that is used when starting a shell.  What exactly does this program
 do?  Does it only set the environment variables in the .env file for
 a region.  Or does it also perform other functions?
