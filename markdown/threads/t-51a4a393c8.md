› Can one get Cobol to treat the system date as a 4 digit year (1997) rather
› than as a two digit year(97)?

Currently, only by hardcoding the "19" in the program. A few of the
programs where I work just push off the 2K problem with an "IF" sentence
something like:

IF System-Year > 90
Move 19 to Date-CC
Else
Move 20 to Date-CC
End-if.

Works now, might work until 2090 rolls around and everybody forgets that
this was coded. I'll probably not be working at that point, but I'd rather
not put a work-around like that into the code if at all possible. It also
will not work if you have to deal with dates from the 1800's as well.

BTW, in case you think that last case is ludicrous, consider those people
who were born before the turn of the century and whose demographic data
is stored on computers. I worked for the local country election team,
and we had people on file who were born in 1894 for instance -- if you
used the above calculation, they wouldn't be old enough to vote! There
are work-arounds for that, but rather more complicated.
