I've put together a short presentation called "The Future of Mainframes is Now"
on our website. It's a short exploration of z/OS. I've given it for brown bag

minutes, depending on your style.

happening (big picture, but techy big picture) on IBM mainframes today. Feel

discussions.

Been on Z/OS @ Charles Schwab (*) for the last 6 months - it's the same
old same old. As with most mainframe enhancements - ask me 10 years from
now, and I'll have a definite opinion.

Right now, I'm more interested in the DB2 V7 XML extenders. I'm half way
through a 1 year project to bring real time data replication across 7
different environments, and what I can do with Websphere MQ, Toplink, AIX
&  Oracle 9 is the trick.

OS changes seem to take far longer to sink in than application
"accommodations" (with each other).

*Senior CS architects have claimed it as the worlds largest commercial
data processor installation, in terms of MIPS - anyone got any reliable
source to prove/disprove that ? All I know is that after 20 years in the
industry, CS do things with technology, that I hadn't even dreamed of - I
feel like a kid again (not a bad trick, having turned 40YO on 10.02.02
!!! ). Furthermore - CS have a relationship with it's employees like no
other company I ever worked for (or contracted with). I have 15 years to
retirement - I have NO plans to go anywhere !!!
