I had "anthropos" right as the nominative singular, but not "andros", which 
is the *genitive* singular. The nominative for the latter is "aner" (alpha, 
nu, eta, rho).

The Strong's number for "anthropos" is 444, and that for "aner" is 435.

Note that the Latin words "homo/hominis" and "vir/viris" correspond in 
semantics  to the Greek "anthropos/anthropou" and "aner/andros" 
respectively, although there's a bit more crossover in the Greek than in the 
Latin, and the Greek words are related whereas the Latin are not.
