```
Given recent discussions in both comp.lang.cobol and IBM-MAIN, I would
suggest that any readers of this note that

A) are "part of" a SHARE  member organization
B) are interested in increasing the existing documented limits on specific
COBOL data structures
C) want to express an opinion (PRO, CON, or just interested)

Check out a new requirement that I submitted:

 SSLNGC0313583 - Increase Individual Item and Table Compiler Limits

This requirement is NOT a request for either AMODE 64 or RMODE 64 support -
but just a requirement for an increase in specific existing COBOL compiler
limits.

If you already have a SHARE user-id, you can see it (and add your comments)
by going to:

http://www.share.org/REQS/reqmain.nsf/Req%20by%20Status?OpenView&ExpandView

If you are part of a SHARE member organization, but do NOT already have a
user-id you can request one (before indicating COBOL, PL/I, & LE as one of
your interest areas) by going to:

http://www.share.org/share/website/shareweb.nsf/1d99cd65f2badf1e86256b78006ccb15/d84e1cb707541ed686256bf90066b343?OpenDocument

If you are a part of an IBM mainframe (or mainframe-related) organization
that is NOT currently a member of SHARE, you may want to check out:

http://www.share.org/share/website/shareweb.nsf/1d99cd65f2badf1e86256b78006ccb15/945dac6c7826442086256b9300686abc?OpenDocument

P.S.  To repeat what I have said in previous notes, if you are a part of a
SHARE member organization and want to be notified by "private" email of new
COBOL, PL/I, or LE  requirements for discussion or voting - please send me a
private email.
```
