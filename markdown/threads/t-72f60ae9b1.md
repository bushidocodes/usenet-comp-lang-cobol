A general comment.  Previously there was a suggestion to preceed subjects
with OT (for off topic). Other prefixes where not adopted.

During the discussion of RW, another prefix occured to me.

It is CH (change hats).

Why?  Some discussions about COBOL programming vasilate between
the programming mind set, and the manager or CEO mindset.

In the case of the RW feature in COBOL a great deal of progress has been
made to assist the "programmer." Altimately, this helps both groups of hats.
The discussion of an altimate tool such as a word processor would move the
programming to the user. Those hats may or may not be willing to do it
themselves. An ideal CEO might be one who has very few adminstrative
personel. This CEO knows how to type, or press buttons, or talk to some
interface to let him do all he wants him or her self.  In the ultimate
solution from
a systems view point, one CEO may not be interested in the same things as
the previous CEO. So hire or promote someone to push the keys.

I believe we have this situation now.  We just have to put on the right hat.

Warren Simmons
