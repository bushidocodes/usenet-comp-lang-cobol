Greetings, fellow CLC'ers.

Website www.LegacyGuild.org version Beta v.01 is now up and running for limited consumption by the Legacy Developer Community.

Distribution and publicizing to a wider client community is targeted for 1 Jan 2014.

This website is not currently associated with any 501c3 or 501c4 non-profit, but is presented as a Public Service Project by my (tiny) Sub-S corporation.

It is dedicated to the support, maintenance, preservation, enhancement, and transformation of Legacy Systems, with emphasis on modernization with Service Oriented Architecture (SOA).

Not exclusively for CoBOL, but heavily weighted towards CoBOL. Modernization aspect leaves wiggle room for Java and O-O proponents as long as there is a Legacy angle to it, and a general recognition of peaceful co-existence with CoBOL whenever possible.

So enter it (www.LegacyGuild.org) into your url, and take a look around. Be sure to click on Website Development Log menu item on the left to see where we are development wise. Comments welcome here in CLC or offline to me initially, but at some point (soon), we will carry the discussion into the Forum capability of the website.

For those interested in web development, this is my second attempt. First attempt was pretty simple using google tools to build a website for a Homeowners Association. This second attempt is MUCH more aggressive with functionality, and is based upon an ocPortal framework. See www.ocportal.com for details. I have found a bug or two (those damn PHP programmers, that should have written the whole thing in CoBOL! :-)), but through determination and stubbornness, I found workarounds. Using something called cpanel and internal ocPortal tools for website construction. I have not had to do a lick of PHP coding.

Later,

webmaster and Chief Correspondent of LegacyGuild.org .
