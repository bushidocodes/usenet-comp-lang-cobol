Congratulations on the A.  I have a BABA (Bachelor Arts Business Admin.)
and for a living I am a Systems Analyst (& yes I do a great deal of
programming, sometimes in COBOL). There were no Computer Science degrees
in those far off days (although the program was in development at the
time)

The Business Degree gave me a profound understanding of Financial
systems, I could chat comfortably with Accountants and Financial
Statements folk, I could also easily understand the Estimating Community
(how much did it cost, how much will it cost next time).  I found I have
no personal inclination to be a manager and prefer the more technical
tasks.

YOur degree will give you some framework to decide what you enjoy doing
and a VISA into the business world, where you will with time, find your
true calling (be it technical or managerial or something else).
