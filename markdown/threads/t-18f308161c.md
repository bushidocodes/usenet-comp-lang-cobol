OK,  let me try and summarize:

As far as WG4 goes (the ISO-ish group), individuals are NEVER welcome to
submit comments or input documents.  Instead what you need to do is to send
something to your "national body" (or its WG4 membership) for THEM to submit
to the group.  I have frequently put out a web-page where you can find your
national body, but if you have trouble finding information on this, send me
private email and I will help you. As far as I know most (almost all?) of the
active WG4 member countries are QUITE willing to pass on individual comments
for the entire committee to consider - but this is a "country by country"
decision and there is no guarantee of this. (Only your specific national body
can tell you how to provide input via this channel.)

This restriction on WG4 input applies at all time but is particularly
important during "official" public review periods.  You need to submit your
comments to your country and they will submit a "single" country ballot on
the revision.

As far as J4 goes (the ANSI-ish) group, there are two very distinct types of
"input."  Questions on the current Standard, comments on the current DRAFT
revision, ideas for the next revision are *always* "in order" and may be
submitted to the J4 chair at ANY TIME.  The J4 chair can be reached via email
at:
      doncobol <at> mediaone.net

Although they are under no "official" obligation to do so, the committee will
usually reply to you (informally) on their processing of input of this type.
Such "submissions" are *always* processed by J4 at a meeting.  (Right now,
the committee is placing ideas for the NEXT revision - after this one -
fairly low on the agenda, so they may not be processed quickly - but they
WILL be processed.)

"Official" ANSI Public review comment periods (usually 2 to 4 months) are a
time for providing input to ANSI on a publicly available "official" draft
document.  Comments during this time *must* be submitted thru ANSI - actually
NCITS, I think - (not J4).  An official public review press release is issued
and it includes instructions on how to submit comments during this period.
J4 is REQUIRED to reply to all such comments (in writing) and there is an
appeal process if you are not happy with what happens with these comments.
The J4-TAG (Technical Advisory Group?) takes all such US/ANSI input and
determines which of these comments are passed on to the ISO process as the US
ballot on an outstanding ISO COBOL Ballot.  (Timing of these two processes
can get complicated and even lead to some comments being delayed by a cycle,
but they are ALWAYS processed.)

If you are interested in being notified of an OFFICIAL public review period,
then you can contact the J4 chair - or follow the J4 external web site at:
  http://merant.microfocus.com/Standards/

One thing that MAY be causing some concern from non-committee members is the
fact that we (J4/WG4) are fairly far along in (and severely overdue for)
revision work.  Therefore, if you submit a "new" comment or suggestion at
this stage of the revision which requires J4 to take committee time on
something that has already been resolved (often multiple times), J4 does tend
to get "annoyed" by this.  However, even with such input, I know of no case
where the input wasn't processed fairly and in a manner appropriate to the
current stage of revision work.

    ****

Does this help?
