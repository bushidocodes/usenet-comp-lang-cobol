```
Life imitates Art imitates Life imitates Art imitates... or, Dilbert
in Action: I participated in the following interaction and I'd just
like to... 'share' it with you all.

I was working on that program I posted about earlier, the one giving
me a S0C4 in IGZEQOC (thanques for the help, all!); the function of
it is rather simple... the program loads about 24 WS tables from DB2
tables, reads a file, looks up descriptions in WS via SEARCH ALL
based on codes from the infile and writes an outfile w/ codes &
descriptions... no brain-surgery. Since I am a
contractor/consultant I am not allowed to write such things from the
ground up; the bulk of the program was written by someone who
recently quit and it was handed over to me with the usual 'All Ya
Gotta Do Is...'. Well, I did it and was going over the output with
the Business Analyst when our discussion took the following Curious
Turn:

Me: 'I noticed that no provision has been made for an input code
not being found on the lookup tables; it wouldn't be much for me to
code a couple of AT END error rtns for the SEARCH ALLs... what would
you like to see?

BA: 'We don't want any error routines.'

Me: 'How interesting... and what might the reason for that be?'

BA: 'Errr... (nervous giggle)... well, we don't want you to put
anything in there becaaaaause... there isn't anything in there
already.'

Me: 'That is confusing... if there were something in there already
I would not have to put anything in there; the reason I asked is
precisely *because* there is nothing in there... am I missing
something here?'

BA: 'Well... (nervous giggle)... you gotta understand The Process
behind all this; we had a Meeting about this and we went around the
room, with each participant voting on how the error text should
read... and after an hour nothing got decided so we left it blank.'

Me: 'This tells me that one should not leave such decisions to that
form of life with six-or-more legs and no brain, The Committee...
were *I* to have any say in the matter I would toss off a simple bit
of text like 'NO DESC RGN CD: XX' and then say 'Alright, the code
now does *this*... any of you don't like it you can fill out a
Requisition for Change and allocate some budget to make it
different...' ... As I said this the Business Analyst got a look of
utter horror on his face... how dare I even *consider* taking such
action without Proper Approval... maybe I wasn't (*gasp!*) a Team
Player!... so, without missing a beat, I continued '... but since I
am only a Consultant it is obvious that I have no say in such
matters and since the Committee could not decide what text was
suitable then absolutely no error checking will be done.'

BA: '(nervous giggle of relief) Well, I know it sounds kind of
strange but that's what we want, yes'

... and folks do this with straight faces... sigh.
```

```
had a branch mgr whose "Systems Utilities" floppy would always get

we'd send him another.

We saw the pattern, and came to the conclusion that he's storing near
some magnets and the magnets are causing the floppy to go bad.

I talked to him, and

He said: "No, I don't have any magnets. In fact, I keep it away from
everything else."

I said: "Where do you keep it?"

He said: "I keep it tacked up on my cubicle wall."

He was driving a tack thru it, and posting it on his cubicle wall.
Dilbert Principle in action.
```
