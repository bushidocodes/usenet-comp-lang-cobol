As a follow up to another thread, from my "usually reliable source",

"Bill,

We do not have any runtime charges. This was changed in V6 due to feedback
from the COBOL community. This is documented on our website at:
    http://www.adtools.com/news/prelease/lettertocobol.html

At Fujitsu Software, we are extremely committed to the COBOL market and
always try to put the needs of COBOL programmers first. We believe that it's
the key to earning your respect and building a long-term mutually beneficial
business partnership. We believe that COBOL programmers deserve a reasonably
priced COBOL compiler with high performance, unmatched reliability, superior
scalability, and royalty free COBOL run-times.

In the Fujitsu COBOL Version 6 Enterprise Edition, we offer a royalty-free
multi-threaded run-time system targeted for higher performing enterprise
server-based applications. COBOL programmers will continue to have access to
the royalty-free single threaded Fujitsu run-time system via the
Professional and Standard Editions and may develop and deploy COBOL
applications with no license fees required (i.e. no strings attached...)."

   ***

If anyone gets an answer DIFFERENT from this (from a Fujitsu sales person or
other person claiming to represent Fujitsu) please get the persons name and
send it to me (via private email) and I will follow up with the
"appropriate" people.

Micro-Focus and MS used to have competing Cobol-74 compilers on CP/M
(CIS Cobol and Cobol-80). These moved to various other platforms
including MS-DOS and Xenix.  MF also did Unix and Concurrent-DOS (which
MS would not do).

options in some versions) rather than write their own.

MS's hands, especially as MS were only supplying DOS and Win3.1 versions
and these were licence fee free.  So MF opened a US office and the MS
distribution rights were revoked (alledgedly by MF) just as MS started
to sell MS Cobol 5.1 (cf MF 3.1).
