```
Apparently a 6.0 around 90 Miles from DC.  My whole house  shook for about 
15 seconds and then the intensity increased for 5 to 10 seconds such that 
the chair I was sitting in started moving around.  Then it was over.  The 
water in my coffee cup, about a third poped out.  Floppy disks and things in 
piles fell off of shelves.  Even some books fell to the floor.  My computer 
desk actually came up off the floor. You could actually hear it and I 
thought maybe it was a nearby plane crash. Then I asked myself, could this 
have been a bomb set off in DC?

I can't even imagine a stronger one like New Zeland, Japan, and Hati had. 
There was just no time to react, even to go stand in a doorway.  It really 
brings it home in a personal way just how powerful these things are, 
especially when it is relatively shallow.  I have never felt anything like 
this before.

We once had 3.x earthquake in nearby Columbia, MD but I never felt it and if 
it was not on the news I would not have known about it.
```

```
Yes, earthquakes are frightening. Even when you've been through dozens of 
them it is still a bit frightening. You never quite know how hard it is 
going to be or how long it will last...

There was another one in Christchurch yesterday although the multiple daily 
shocks they have been experiencing are now subsiding a bit. There are still 
several hundred households camping in their backyards because the house is 
unsafe. And it has been really cold with freezing temperatures and below. 
For most people life is returning to normal (or as normal as it can be... 
"normal" means having water, electricity, phone, sewerage, and a roof over 
your head...). The chaos and devastation is like a war film but they are 
looking to the future and seem determined to rebuild. Many people have left 
(I don't blame them...) but there are some whose families have been there 
for several generations and they are not about to go.

Damage has been estimated to $40 billion  but the Government are 
surprisingly upbeat about it. Insurance policies have already been increased 
and there is likely to be a tax surcharge at some point, but mostly, it has 
been handled very well. We did get some incredible help from overseas (both 
in practical and financial terms... the Australians were outstanding, given 
they had their own problems at the time, and Japan was one of the first to 
arrive here with teams for searching the rubble. Ironically, those same 
teams were required at home a few weeks later.) I have never seen the 
country so united in support as it has been for Christchurch. I guess every 
cloud has a silver lining but it is cold comfort to the people who lost 
friends and family.

Can't help but wondering if there is some reason for what certainly seems 
like a huge increase in these events Worldwide.
```
