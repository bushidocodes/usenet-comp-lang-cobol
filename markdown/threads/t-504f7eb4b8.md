```
Steve,

First, I usually don't open the unidentifiable (to me) attachments found in
messages on newsgroups.  So your code remains unknown to me.  (I have no
idea if other people feel the same way about downloading attachments from
usenet.)

Second, it is a fair standard to post your code in the actual message (lurk
around for a while and see), and provide a description of what you're
program does, and what has gone wrong.  If you're program is too big to
post, post only the appropriate portions of the Environment, Data, and
Procedure divisions.  Don't forget the error messages your compiler is
giving you, or highly accurate descriptions of what is happening to the data
if the error is a logical one.

Third, if you had lurked around for a while you would have seen that
chatroom abbreviations are not normal.  In fact, I've seen people be
attacked from time to time for having used them.  This newsgroup is
monitored by many professional programmers from many areas of industry, and
you may want to put your best "face" on when you, virtually speaking, walk
in and make a first impression.


Please take the time to find and read the newsgroup FAQ.  If it isn't
currently listed in your message download queue, you can go to
www.dejanews.com and do a search on comp.lang.cobol in the newsgroup field
and FAQ in the subject field.
If you do not have a browser available (as many don't), you can monitor the
messages for a while and wait until the FAQ is posted.
```
