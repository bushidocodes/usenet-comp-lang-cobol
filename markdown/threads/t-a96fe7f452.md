…[4 more quoted lines elided]…

Wait. A pointer can't 'be' an array of structures. A pointer can point to an
array of structures, on problem.

Is the problem on the PL/I side or the COBOL side?

On the PL/I side, to pass a [fixed number] of pointers, why not:

     declare cobsub entry (pointer, ..., pointer) options(asm byvalue);

then

     call cobsub (ptr1, ptr2, ,,,, ptrn);


On the COBOL side,

Linkage section.
01  ptr1 pointer.
01  ptr2 pointer.
.
.
.
01  ptrn pointer.
01  struct-01.
     02 ...

procedure division using by value ptr1, ptr2, ..., ptrn.
       .
       .
       .
       set address of struct-01 to ptr1



Something like that. There are a lot of possibilities and you haven't given us
much detail, so I hope this gets you started.
