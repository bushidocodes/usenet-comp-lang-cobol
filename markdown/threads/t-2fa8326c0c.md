I am not positive what your problem is, but have a couple of ideas.

1) Are the data-areas that you expect to remain "filled-in" defined in
Working-Storage in A *only* and in the Linkage-Section of B and C? (One of
your comments makes me think that it is in Working-Storage of B - which
would mean that anytime you go back from C to A - you will lose it)

2) You do not seem to be using the EXTERNAL attribute on any of your data
(available with VS COBOL II or newer compilers).  I think that this may
actually be the best way to do what you want to do.  In this case, you
define the same data structure in all 3 programs in the Working-Storage
section - but with the External attribute on the 01-level. With this feature
you do NOT pass any data in the CALL statement (or put anything in the
called program's linkage section).  In my opinion, this would make your
application "cleaner".

3) Under MVS (or OS/390), there are some serious issues related to multiple
ways of calling the same program and getting it in last used state.  In
order to INSURE that this happens correctly, you will want to make certain
that you use
  - the RES compiler option (if you are on VS COBOL II or OLDER)
 - the CALL identifier option (rather than CALL literal)
 - Do NOT use the "IS INITIAL" statement on the Program-ID
 - Do NOT try to link-edit all your programs into a single load module

Normally, you could use the DYNAM compiler option to make this work, but you
have mentioned using DB2 - and DB2 will "die a horrible death" if you
compile your COBOL programs with DYNAM (but it will work fine if you use
CALL identifier for your application subroutines).
