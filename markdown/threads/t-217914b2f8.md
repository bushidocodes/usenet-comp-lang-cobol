```
I have been using cobol (mostly RM) under
unix for the last 18 years. This has been
on a "character" (as opposed to graphical)
environment. I have done some PC work
with MF cobol the last few years, but it has
all been done with the RM compatibility mode.

Anyway, we must now move from unix to a
Windows NT environment. Obviously, this
will be a graphical user interface, and must
be event-driven (click and point) as opposed
to procedure-driven (the way I have always
programmed).

I figured I better "go with the flow", so I started
learning Microsoft Visual Basic 5.0. Its a neat
tool, but it is so very different from cobol, the
learning curve has been very steep. I think
I am better off staying with cobol, only because
it is what I know.

QUESTION: Is there such a thing as a "Visual
Cobol", which offers a similar development
process as Visual Basic? The ability to just
"draw" screens, with various input tools, is
very attractive. Is there a cobol that is truly
integrated into the Windows95 environment,
as opposed to just adding a few routines?

At this point, I will be starting from scratch,
so I'm not tied to RM, MF, or any other
particular compiler.

LOOKING for suggestions and experiences!!

thank you,
don ferrario
```

```
I usually a lurker here, but another alternative is Acucobol/GT. They are at
www.acucobol.com. I went through the 'I gotta start getting GUI'ed', but
real life comes everyday. I looked at VB, Delphi, C++ and finally remembered
I'm a COBOL jock.

With acucobol, and others I assume, it doesn't have to be all or nothing.
You can port your code, probably nearly as is, and get stable running
character based on the new platform. (Make sure you have a valid business
reason to switch platforms ). Then bite off a piece at a time doing new
development or rewrites making them as GUI as you want, with radio buttons,
drop down boxes, etc. The GUI stuff gives you a much smaller learning curve,
because you already know COBOL. Meanwhile, the business continues in an
orderly fashion. My clients don't know or care what its written in. When it
hits the screen, it looks like any other windows app.

In 2Q98, they are coming out with a new product for development. When I saw
the demo, it reminded me of ive VB and Delphi development enivronment, which
I found scary, but it gives you the flexibility to go as deep as you want.
```

```
there are many visual tools for PC's, and i think
cobol is amoung them. the real question is, what
language do you use on your UNIX machines that you
are porting from?

i would not use vb if you already have cobol code, because
the more programming languages, the less chance of tracking
a system error.

of course, the other side of the coin is that there
are more pc programmers who might know vb, so finding
a person to maintain the code will be more expensive

the third problem with vb, in addition to the learning
curve, is the complete lack of portability, even between
versions. then you have different *types* of VB, such
as vbscript, vbapp, vb16, and vb32.

finally, if you are simply trying to give PC users access
to current cobol code, you might want to look at the client
server products available for whatever compiler you have
for unix.

so far, i have learned on this newsgroup of 3 products which
give platform independance, such as netexpress, netcobol
and relativity. there are other companies,
which are also major cobol players, which i havent looked at
yet, but am sure their products are similiar.
```
