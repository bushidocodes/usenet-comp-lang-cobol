A lot of variables here. First, you can rarely find these offerings in the
universities any more: most colleges do C and Unix and other stuff, very
little practical mainframe related training (this is why so many
corporations are having trouble finding COBOL programmers to maintain,
modify, and extend their bread-and-butter, run-the-corporation
applications). Many two-year colleges and technical colleges still offer
these.

A few professional training companies offer these kinds of training, but
their pricing and scheduling are usually designed for corporate customers:
do you work for a corporation that could send you to public courses, or
that could schedule courses on site for you and some co-workers? My group
(The Trainer's Friend, Inc.) provides instructor-led training in all the
topics you mentioned and more, but mainly at corporate sites.

Sometimes a customer will open up a class they want us to teach so that
outside students can attend (this can bring class size up to enough
students to justify the cost). This kind of training is usually called
"consortium" training.

If you like, we can contact your employer about holding classes on site.
Alternatively, if you prefer, I can post you when slots appear in our
consortium classes offerings.
