```
Remember "Musical Chairs", where everybody marches around till the music
stops then try to grab a seat? Well, the music has begun, the clients
are marching around the chairs, but the programmers own the chairs. More
and more clients join the game as they face the reality of Y2K. Sorry
folks - there aren't anymore chairs!

Corey's observations about increasing number of Cobol ads are correct.
There are 2 factors at work: Employers are recognizing their needs, and
the talent (you guys!) is becoming more and more willing to jump ship
and go for new opportunities. Case in point: Ford orders for replacement
mainframe programmers has doubled in the last week. These guys aren't
retiring - they're going for the bucks.

We've decided to rename this venture "Y2K-USA" The basic idea remains
the same: Bring the buyers(clients) together with the
sellers(programmers) and let the free market determine the price. No
hype, no chaff, no BS. This is a grass routes movement - word of mouth -
no marketing weenies - only professionals need apply.

Let me know what you think.

Regards,
Steve Wray - "Too many people quit looking for work when they find a
job."
```
