```
Overheard conversation in mainframe shop, "Under COBOL III the rules
changed. You now have to say ....". Being curious about this new
language, I Googled for "COBOL III" and found these references:

Resume describing experience at Deutche Bank on mainframe:
"All the existing programs that were in COBOL
II were migrated to COBOL III by using a pac-base tool. "

Another resume:
"full life cycle analysis and program development utilizing COBOL III,
IMS and DB2, in a MVS/ESA mainframe environment. "

Another resume:
"Environment: COBOL III, PL/I, DB2/SQL, FOCUS, Easytrieve, TSO/ISPF"

Many resumes skipped ...

Job advertisement:
"Skills: HP3000, COBOL III, View/VPlus, Turbo Image, Supertool"

Masters thesis:
"This thesis gives an overview of the ANSI standard definition of
COBOL III, presents an algebraic specification of the syntax of COBOL
III, and describes its specification process."
http://citeseer.ist.psu.edu/manuel96ansi.html

Contracting company:
Lists COBOL I, II and III under skills. Nice picture of Grace Hopper.
http://www.perlusa.com/services_cobol.html

MVS help board (September 2004):
"Can you please give me COBOLII and COBOL III differences, new
features etc..?"

Reply 1:
"There is no such language as "COBOL III" (COBOL 3?).

"While quite a few folks refer to Language Environment (LE) versions
of COBOL on "MVS" (OS/390, z/OS) by this name, there is no such
animal."

Reply 2:
Ironic - in my shop, I OFTEN get questions about COBOL 2 versus COBOL
3. Our standard compile panel has the latest COBOL listed as option 3;
thus - it takes on the identity of "COBOL 3".

I wish I had a dime for everytime I had to explain that "there ain't
no COBOL 3"

http://mvshelp.com/cgi-bin/ubb6/ultimatebb.cgi?ubb=get_topic;f=9;t=001760
```

```
--------- schnipp -----------------------------------------

Within this document:

- "First Standard COBOL" refers to ANSI X3.23-1968, IS0 R-1989:1972.

- "Second Standard COBOL" refers to ANSI X3.23-1974,
IS0 1989:1978, which superseded first Standard COBOL.

- "Third Standard COBOL" refers to ANSI COBOL X3.23-1985,
IS0 1989:1985, which superseded second Standard COBOL.

-  Both "Amendment 1" and "Intrinsic Function Amendment" refer to
ANSI X3.23a-1989, IS0 1989:1985/Amd.l :1992.

-  Both "Amendment 2" and "Correction Amendment" refer to
ANSI X3.23b-1993, IS0 1989:1985/Amd.2:1993.

- "Amended third Standard COBOL" refers to third Standard COBOL
updated first with the content of amendment 1 and then with the content
of amendment 2. The terms "Standard COBOL" and "COBOL" also refer
to amended third Standard COBOL.


------------------ schnapp --------------------------------

  So "COBOL 3" may be meant to refer to COBOL-85.
```
