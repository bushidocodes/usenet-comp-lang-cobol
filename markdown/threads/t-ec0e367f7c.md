There have been a couple of other suggestions, but here is my input.

1) Do *not* override the AMODE/RMODE settings for your VS COBOL II modules.
Let them be what the compiler generates - but DO continue to use the
DATA(24) compiler option.

2) Make certain that your OS/VS COBOL modules were ALL compiled with the RES
compiler option. (If you have any that were compiled with NORES, then you
are bound to get some ABENDs).

3) Make certain that your VS COBOL II programs (with multiple entry points)
are compiled with the NAME(ALIAS) compiler option (and then do NOT "play
with" the compiler generated linkage editor options.

Bruce,

When we upgraded to Cobol 370 from Cobol II we had mixed sucess. The
major problem was that the internal calls generated by cobol (IGZ
modules) were statically link into the subroutines not resolved at the
final call. If you link ensure that you use only object decks or link
your subroutines NCAL (never call).

It is generally better to compile the old stuff in the new compiler.
You might be pleasantly surprised when it works. You may not have to
alter a single word of you old code, but you know what it is and what it
does.

Hope this helps
Ken
