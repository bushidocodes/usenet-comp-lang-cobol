I can tell you that here in Edmonton, Canada, the CoBOL market is very
much alive and well, thank you very much.  Perhaps mainframe CoBOL use
has declined in recent years, simply becuase the use of mainframes
themselves has gone down, but the need for PC CoBOL programmers
(Merant and Fujitsu come to mind as popular packages) is still pretty
strong.  I personally don't see CoBOL ever going away.

As for Y2K, I wouldn't refer to it as a fiasco.  I spent 18 months on
our Y2K project...the changes weren't high profile, but everyone at
our company knew full well they had to be made.  I've seen some
wingnuts complain in print of a high-level conspiracy by consultants
to make ourselves a lot of extra money, but any level-headed person
I've ever talked to seems to agree that Y2K was a very real potential
problem.
