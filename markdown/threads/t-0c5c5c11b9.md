```
Even if you "reset" year count to zero, most companies assume it's a date in
the 1900s because 2 digit dates are not qualified. Leaving the software the
way it is would wreak havoc with your life: eg: your life insurance policy
could be 1900 - 1980 or -80 years old instead of 20 (2000-1980). A way
around this is "windowing" where you make ANOTHER assumption (therefore
buying time) that any 2 digit date of <50 is 2000 century and anything >=50
is 1900 century. This still leaves companies with >100 year calculations
in a lurch.
```
