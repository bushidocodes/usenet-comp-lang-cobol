Ther's an easy lesson to be learned here, Michael.

Some time ago I stopped to consider what the overall effect of providing
support here is.

Being of a generous disposition (like many here) and feeling that it doesn't
do any harm to help, I spent time (just as you outlined) researching
problems and providing answers. I found that in many cases the people
concerned never even say "thanks".

We have people posting here for support who are actually taking jobs from
programmers in Europe and America, then requiring assistance from those same
programmers. And when they get it, they accept that it is their right, and
CLC is here to provide free COBOL support.

Nowadays, I am very selective and careful about who I respond to. The last
time I actually spent more than an hour of my time on something for CLC was
last week when Carlos was having problems with COBOL pictures on forms in
PowerCOBOL. Because he is a regular poster here and often provides help to
others (in other words, his heart is in the right place <G>) I saw no
problem in setting up a project and experimenting on his difficulty.

On the other hand, today I see a post in broken English (I know it isn't
everybody's first language, but it is necessary to at least get a clear
statement of the problem) for some help with Dreamweaver and a tenuous
connection to COBOL. My first impulse (when I remembered the strife I had
when I first moved to Dreamweaver after using Notepad and hand crafted HTML
<G>) was to try and help. (I have extensive experience now with Dreamweaver
and regularly embed components with it). But there are several problems
here... I don't believe the poster would understand the response (this is
due to language difficulty; I'm not suggesting for a moment he is not
capable of understanding <G>..) and I believe it would require several hours
to establish exactly what his problem is and solve it. Why would I invest
that amount of time, for free, and in the likelihood that once he has the
solution, he will not visit CLC either as a poster or a lurker unless he has
another problem he needs solved urgently for free...?

Helping youngsters do their homework (with broad suggestions), debating
matters COBOL, and the occasional laugh are all good reasons to frequent
CLC.

Providing professional programmers with information they are too lazy to
find out empirically for themselves (as most of us had to), or providing
free consultancy to Companies who SHOULD be buying the expertise is another
thing entirely.

Like you, I was disappointed the poster neglected to share the solution
(mainly so I could correlate it against my own thoughts on the subject and
maybe learn something) but it just demonstrates the attitude that is
becoming far too prevalent here.

It is not for me to say what others here should do. But I would urge all of
you to think about providing free advice just because you happen to know the
answer.

My own criteria  before responding are as follows:

1. Is this poster likely to contribute in the future?

2. Does this poster have a right to expect free advice? (like, would he do
the same for me, is he gainfully employed and doing this on behalf of a
corporation that could/should be buying it.)

3. Has the problem been clearly stated? (If I get into this, am I going to
spend time finding out what his REAL problem is?)

We all need advice occasionally (self included), but we should EARN the
right to ask for it, if it is going to be provided free.

Pete.
