```

Just something to watch out for, actually.

I have a job that I want to run at night, 9:pm (2100hrs), after the 
billing cycle had completed and before any of the early-morning crew 
entered any new charges.  I'm a consultant/contractor/hired gun; such 
folks are not allowed to have remote access to the mainframe.  (I am sure 
there is a Very Good Reason for this).  Rather than disturb the operators 
with an unusual request I decided to code a wait-step based on a 
utility-program to call ILBOWAT0 for a parm-determined period.

I found the basic code here (thanks, Mr Jacot!) and expanded it a bit, 
giving it a routine to check a PARM in D HH MM SS format, convert this to 
seconds and loop through CALLs to ILBOWAT0.

I submitted the job at 1558 and a delay of 5hr 3min... and came in the 
next day to find it had been CAN'd.  Seems that after almost an hour of 
swapped-out waiting the system began to throw console messages of:

16.49.36 JOB00676  **MYUID01 HAS BEEN IN WAIT STATE FOR -*5*- MINUTES**CHECK SYSTEM
16.55.53 JOB00676  **MYUID01 HAS BEEN IN WAIT STATE FOR -*5*- MINUTES**CHECK SYSTEM
17.02.11 JOB00676  **MYUID01 HAS BEEN IN WAIT STATE FOR -*5*- MINUTES**CHECK SYSTEM

... and Ops, being... Ops, saw this as an anomaly worthy of cancellation.

A few emails back-and-forth with the Ops Manager got a notice sent to 
folks which stated 'If job MYUID01 tells you it is waiting then let it 
wait' and all went well.

('... all went well' includes things like getting zero rows returned for 
an SQL statement containing:

AND DATE_TIME_STAMP BETWEEN  CURRENT TIMESTAMP
                        AND (CURRENT TIMESTAMP - 24 HOURS)

... but that's why things get tested, aye)

Anyways... folks might want to see what happens at their sites when a job 
is waiting for a couple of hours, both during business hours and 
off-hours.

DD
```

of console commands.  Perhaps the way the "/* SCHEDULE" directive is

control card at JCL conversion time, puts the job in HOLD status, and
