 In fact when C++ adoption was approved we had many people globally
interested in the national adoption for $18. ISO was listing their document
as  $245."

What I believe to be still under discussion are the exact contents of areas
such as the copyright and distribution policy statements (the cover and the
"Roman numeral" pages, exclusive of the Table of Contents).

I would be VERY surprised if the TECHNICAL content (pages 1 onward) of any
official standard that might be distributed by ANSI differed in ANY way,
much less any SUBSTANTIVE way, from that now available from ISO/IEC.

Note that the private communication that Bill cites refers to this as the
"ANSI adoption", not "ANSI version"; the latter term can be read to imply
the possibility of a meaningful difference between the two; the former makes
it clear that that will not be the case.   The intent, I believe, is
precisely the adoption of the exact technical content of the International
Standard as an American National Standard.  Any technical difference, no
matter how minor, would be in conflict with that intent.

    -Chuck Stevens
