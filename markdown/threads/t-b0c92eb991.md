I wish I did ... I threw one out many years back.  Got tired of lugging 
it arround.  The PDP-8E (4k SR-33 tape reader punch) was my first home 
computer, circa 1974.  I paid better than 70 grand for it ... 4k, paper 
tape software.

I still have the 110/300 baud modem that went with it ... I used a dial 
up PDP-10 to do most of the programming using the PAL3 cross-assembler. 
  The modem carries the bell telephone logo, and serial number 142.  It 
is almost an exact match in size for my new 1 gig, 1 Mhz motherboard.  I 
could fit, (without enough room for cable or air circulation, granted) 
12 80 gigabyte hard disks in the case.

Damn, what a wonderfull time to live.  Such neat toys.  I always wanted 
my own PDP-10, actually.  I suppose what I have now is better, but 
nowhere near as neat a looking piece of gear.

You know, it would not be hard to make a neat virtual PDP-8, complete 
with a GUI front panel.  I could write an assembler for it in Cobol in 
about two hours ... write the output as a binary "tape" that could be 
dropped onto the SR-33.
