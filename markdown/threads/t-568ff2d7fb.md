```
2001-01-28 20:03:41 MET

As we see on http://britneyspears.ac/lasers.htm ,
the physics community really has found a way of
attracting teenagers to physics. What keeps us
from doing the same thing for COBOL?

Of course, a few subtle changes would be
necessary, apart from rewriting the current
draft to something analoguous to the above link.
"KEWL" would be a reserved word in all context,
and the strict concept of logic might be
discarded because of the intellectual limitations
of the average female teenager, but apart from
that - COBOL is AFAIK the only language with
optional keywords - ideally suited for everybody
used to a often meaningless way of communication.

Britney Spears would surely attract legions of
future programmers to the language. And if she
ever becomes a Nobel Laureate inSemiconductor
Physics, her name would nicely stand out among
the co-authors of the final Standard.

Herwig
```
