Hello developer,

I've solved the problem of the 2-digit Year in MF-Cobol when I do a
ACCEPT WD FROM DATE

I define WD as follows:

01 WD-DEF
05 WD-YEAR PIC 9999 COMP-5.
05 WD-MONTH PIC 99.
05 WD-DAY PIC 99.
01 WD-RED REDEFINES WD-DEF .
05 WD PIC 9(06).

Then I patched the Runtime/Library of the Compiler to return the
unmodified
Year (found in _ACCDATE in the module_ACCDIS) rather then the
ASCII-Digits
and so i've got a 4-digit Year.

If you want help for locating the right place for the patch, pls. ask.

Well, hardly worth the trouble. The current date is rather predictable
(e.g.. does never decrease), so a simple windowing test is good for
the next 100 years or so (in a 100 years people will deplore the
stupid programmers of the late 20th century that they could be
so shortsighted to believe that their programs would not be running
in the 22nd century!):

01 THE-DATE PIC 9(8).
01 THE-DATE-COMPONENTS REDEFINES THE-DATE.
02 THE-CENTURY PIC 99.
02 YYMMDD PIC 9(6).
02 YYMMDD-COMPONENTS REDEFINES YYMMDD.
03 THE-YEAR PIC 99.
03 THE-MONTH PIC 99.
03 THE-DAY PIC 99.

ACCEPT YYMMDD FROM DATE
IF THE-YEAR < 98
MOVE 20 TO THE-CENTURY
ELSE
MOVE 19 TO THE-CENTURY
.
All this on the assumption that ACCEPT even *works* after year 1999.

ACCEPT does work on a MF-Cobol (PC-Version):

look at the following code, found in MC-Cobol:
assume, cx is the binary year:
...
sub cx, 076c ;-1900
cmp cx, 0064 ;100
jb @@1 ;<100, ok
sub cx,0064 ;-100 (what about 2101?? :) )
@@1:
mov ah,cl ;use only the lower byte
....
