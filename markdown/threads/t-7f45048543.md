Sure.

There are three kinds of COMP: (1)BINARY(COMP-4), (2) COMP-5; and
(3)PACKED DECIMAL (COMP-3).
For BINARY and COMP-5, there are two sizes: 2-bytes and 4-bytes

BINARY is just what you think: powers of two by bit. Decimal 1234 = 04
D2 (for 2 byte) and 00 00 04 D2 (for 4 byte)
COMP-5 is the same as BINARY, but the bytes are flipped for
Intel-processor convenience. Decimal 1234 = 04 D2 (2 byte) 00 00 04 D2
(4 byte).

COMP-5 on an Intel machine is much more efficient for arithmetic than
BINARY.

On the other hand, if sorting, use COMP4 (BINARY).

Hope this helps. If not, make your question more specific (like why
you need to know).
