I agree with RoadRaat.  But there are two different categories of poor
English.  People for whom English is not their first language often do
not know English well enough to articulate clearly and precisely.  For
those people, it is cruel to criticize them for their improper spelling
or use of grammar.  After all, they may speak English far better than
we speak their native language. :-)

The other category is people who simply don't bother to articulate
clearly, or use language properly.  They type everything in all upper
case or all lower case, no punctuation, and horrible spelling.  If
they expect people to help them, then they should be willing to provide
a readable request for help.  Criticism of their laziness and carelessness
is deserved.  It may even prompt them to improve, to their own benefit,
which is the only valid basis for that type of criticism. :-)
