We started using REALIA (later CA-REALIA) in 1986. Fine product.
Then bought by CA. Their Improvements got bogged down in trying to
implement the Workbench product - which finally arrived. Their corporate
philosophy seemed to be directed toward mainframe development on the
PC. Another part of their problem was concurrent development of
Visual Realia, since abandoned.

In developing for Windows, we chose Fujitsu - mainly for price - and
were overwhelmed with the features it offered compared to Realia,
including native COBOL (such as "FUNCTION xxxxx").

Also, CA has their annual technical conference in New Orleans and
the mayor of that city doesn't like gun manufacturers.

In sum, while not knowing much about MF, it seems to be the
industry standard and you probably won't go wrong if you choose it.
'Course, it's not my money.

My take on the various compilers.

This does not cover all of them - only those with which I have
performed actual development.  

1.  Realia - Fastest file access there is.  Unless you use Btrieve,
however, you get file locking only.  Writing a new system using this
works just fine.  I have several networked systems and I just keep the
files closed when not accessing them.  You have to be careful with
open order and close order, to avoid deadly embrace. 

Windows development - 3rd party tools are the best bet - I use COBOL
sp2 from Flexus.  Natively, as I am sure you know, Visual Realia is
"Functionally Stabilized"  - So is my great grand father  - I think
you get the idea.

2.  MicroFocus -  Full suite of tools.  More than you want or desire
for the price sometimes.  Slow file access, but with good record
locking semantics.  Moderate integrity of files.  We had some serious
problems with "mysteriously growing files" when using compression that
MF eventually fixed.  This is the buggiest development system I have
used.  Their Windows GUI solution, Dialog system is inadequet, buggy,
unstable, alien in syntax, and difficult to grasp.  Even with that
though, I developed a full scale POS application using it, that does
work.  Many problems getting the systems to work when distributed
though.  I quit that job when Netexpress 2 came out.  Reportedly,
Dialog is better in v3, but my collegues there say that that the
projects still get corrupted.  MF has the most complete suite of tools
for offloading mainframe development, of the tools  I have used.

3.  Fujitsu - FASTEST and I mean FAST code execution.  Second fastest
file access.  Files seem stable, with record locking.  Documentation
is complete, but sometimes difficult to navigate.  Not great for
EBCDIC support presently.  Their Windows GUI solution is PowerCOBOL -
which I do not use.  I use the 32 bit Sp2 from Flexus with my Fujitsu
COBOL.  Fujitsu gives the most bang for the buck and it is my 32 bit
solution.  Not a buggy compiler, compared to the others.  Some
problems with printing to the "Generic Text Printer" - I overcome by
using another Flexus product - Form Print.  

For the PRICE especially, you can't beat Fujitsu COBOL - and for a
conversion FROM Wang to Fujitsu I think its a great choice.  I know
someone who has done just that.
