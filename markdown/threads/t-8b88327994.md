For Cobol on the PC, the DISPLAY very is most commonly used to display
information to the screen. The following example, which is a format
supported by most major Cobol compilers for DOS, use the following
method for formatted displays:

Display 'Hello World' At Line 12 Column 15
With Background-Color 7 Foreground-Color 4.

The example above will display hello world with a background color of
white and a foreground color of red at column 15, line 12.

I believe this Display symantic is based on an X-open standard.
Although most Cobol compilers support ESC sequences to format
information to the screen, it is very tedious in comparions to the above
example. I quit using ESC sequences, for the most support, when my
compiler began supporting this much easier format.
