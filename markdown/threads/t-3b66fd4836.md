Offshore Bank In Antigua Closed Down
The European Union Bank of Antigua, which billed itself as the first
offshore bank on the Internet, was shut down by the Antigua government in
July. It had about $6 million in deposits, according to the solicitor
general of Antigua and Barbuda--but not more than $100,000 is left on the
island.

The bank had about 100 depositors, from Eastern Europe and Hong Kong. The
bank claimed its depositors were insured by the International Depositors
Insurance Corporation, but the U.S. Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation
says it has never heard of the IDIC.

The bank's cofounders, who are Russian nationals, have left the island.
Antigua has more than 50 offshore banks; three are targeted to be closed
because of their alleged involvement with Russian crime rings. (Straits
Times, Singapore; August 11, 1997)
