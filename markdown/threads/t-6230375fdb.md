People keep asking for this. First of all I would like to say that it isn't
Cobol that is boring, but your brothers lack of skills. An experienced
programmer will never say a programming language is boring.
This is just like the numerous religious "wars" of what is a "real"
programming language. If all the arguments being used in these discussions
had been true, we would all do machine level byte code.

I for one would say it like this: If you can do a program with it, it is a
programming language. Considering whether Cobol is boring or not, what is
its purpose? to entertain the programmer? Your brother have missed a very
important fact, Cobol, as any programming languages is a tool. Nothing more,
nothing less. I could say that a saw is a boring tool. However, I guess many
would find it to be an exciting tool if I were to cut a 10 Megawatt power
cable ungrounded...

A tool can't be boring, but the one who performs with the tool.

Cobol and the future to come. "Wise" men has predicted the end of Cobol for
years, if not before, at least at the y2k. Time has proven them to be wrong.
My 2 cents is that Cobol will increase even more in the future. Why? At the
current time, computing has never been more open, and it will be even more
open in the time to come. There are things happening behind the scenes,
which I can't tell you about, but you will understand when you see, read and
at not at least experience. So far has the big issue been the fact that once
inside a programming environment you're locked. This is not quite true, to
some extent there has been options of workarounds (linked in binaries, file
communication, CORBA, COM, DLL) These options will increase in numbers,
flexibility and how easy they are to use. In parallel, the strength of each
individual language in targeting their particular core task will be even
more appreciated. The need for replacing what some would consider obsolete
languages will fade away.

Another important issue to consider, is that there are an infinite number of
lines of Cobol code out there, which cannot be replaced, both because it
would take too long and because it would be too expensive. It is my believe
that the demand for Cobol developers will increase as downsizing from
Mainframes to cheap unix clusters (may be wolfpack too) will increase.

On the other side, it is jerks like your brother who make sure I have a good
pay today, please tell him to continue to spread his nonsense and I will
have a comfortable pay well beyond my retirement.

Yours sincerely,

Cheesle
