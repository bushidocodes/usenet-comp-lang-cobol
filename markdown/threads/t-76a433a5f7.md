simplistic but then again it would be tough to get a real world program to fit
in a book much less try to explain it. And I doubt if any systems are ever
developed using anything close to their methods. But having said that I still
think the Murach books are closer to reality than anything else on the
marketplace. I use the CICS reference book constantly. The VSAM books are
equally good. It is hard to spin thru the Cobol books without getting some new
ideas (and that's after 20 some years of coding). Unless, of course, one feels
he knows everything there is to know about Cobol & programming.

I have never been in a mainframe shop where there were no Murach books. Usually
the opposite with 2 or 3 books per cubicle - bought by the programmers not the
company. That's got to mean something.
