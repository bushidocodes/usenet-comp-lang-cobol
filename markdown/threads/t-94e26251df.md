RR> The lesson of the last three decades has been that it is cheaper
RR> to get it right first time (eg desk checking) than to take the
RR> bugs out later.

This is fine if you do it and you have the time. Programmers are
*always* optimistic that it will work first time. Even if it doesn't,
the machine can find the errors quicker and more cheaply that you.

With proper design, then the test runs are to prove the program, not
look for bugs.

Unfortunately, not all programmers are so careful. I'm maintaining a
system written over 10 years ago. There's an oddity in the system
software and Abend-Aid can't locate the program so I do have to work
with IBM's formatted dump.

Still, that's better than a colleague had to work with in the early days
of EPoS: a dump printed on a till roll!
