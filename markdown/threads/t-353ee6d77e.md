First of all, BYTE is not a C type! It is more likely a macro
for 'char' and may be 'signed' or 'unsigned' depending
upon the macro or the implementation. These things are
important!

In terms of physical representation, PIC X is the
closest; however, arithmetic cannot be done, directly,
on PIC X.

Standard COBOL has no equivalent for PIC X as a
numeric. The draft standard provides BINARY-CHAR
as either SIGNED or UNSIGNED..

Many implementations do provide an equivalent. It will
depend upon the implementation you are using. The one
you did not mention. This too is important!
