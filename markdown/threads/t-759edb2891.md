I have been browsing through IBM's Year 2000 support site
(http://www.software.ibm.com/year2000/index.html). Looking at their
FAQ on COBOL, I see frequent references to something called "ILE
COBOL".
What the deuces is that?

For example, at one point in the FAQ it says:

<< In ILE COBOL, .. you can retrieve an 8-digit date [as follows]:
<< ACCEPT my-date8-fld FROM DATE YYYYMMDD

This doesn't work for either COBOL II or COBOL/370 (now tiringly called
"COBOL for MVS and VM").

In COBOL/370 right now, to get a 4-digit year for current date, I am
using the Intrinsic Function of CURRENT-DATE, like so:

Move Function Current-Date(1:8) to my-date8-fld.

Any other way of getting a 4-digit year for the current date?
