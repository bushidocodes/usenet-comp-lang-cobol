```
There are "Working Draft Technical Reports" (WDTR) available on the web for two 
features that aware currently scheduled to be reach public review within the 
next year - and hopefully reach official ISO TR status in 2006 for COBOL.  They 
are:

 "COBOL Collection Classes"
at:
 http://www.cobolportal.com/j4/files/04-0187.doc

and

 "Native COBOL Syntax for XML Support"
at
 http://www.cobolportal.com/j4/files/04-0184.doc

There are also related documents (discussing issues - both resolved and 
pending - and historical information)

 "Collection Classes - Historical Discussion "
at
 http://www.cobolportal.com/j4/files/04-0188.doc

and

"Issues for the XML TR - Resolved and Open"
at
 http://www.cobolportal.com/j4/files/04-0185.doc

   ***

The actual "WDTR's" are written as "change instructions" to the 2002 ISO 
Standard, so reading the detailed information may be a challenge.  However, they 
do include "Concepts" sections (although the "Collection Classes" concept 
section is a little sketchy in my opinion).

In order to avoid having delays in the processing of these documents when they 
actually reach the official "Public Review" process, I would suggest those 
interested in these topics review them NOW and send your comments as soon as 
possible.  You can submit comments to the J4 chair at:

 Don.Schricker <at> icrofocus.com

Or, if you are outside the US *and* you want your specific country to form a 
position on this, you can contact your "National Standards Body"  (J4 will 
accept input from outside the US, but you may - or may not - have more impact by 
contacting your specific standards group.  To determine what Standards Body 
represents your country in ISO, see:

 http://www.iso.ch/iso/en/aboutiso/isomembers/MemberList.MemberSummary?MEMBERCODE=10As WG4 (the ISO Working Group) responsible for COBOL is meeting 18-21 October2004 - in the Netherlands, I would suggest you providing your input in the mosttimely manner possible.--Bill Klein wmklein <at> ix.netcom.com
```
