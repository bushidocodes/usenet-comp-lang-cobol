Hello. I am making a general inquiry to the entire list. Please let me know if
this is not the appropriate list for this.

I currently work as a computer consultant in a permanent full-time position.
My hours are dependent on the client's hours, and of course it has not been
possible to count on any kind of regular non-overtime schedule. I would like
to work flex-time (four 10-hour days) or slightly less hours (35-hour work
week) to pursue other creative interests and projects, which do not pay
anything. (I was a music major and got into computers to pay the bills.)

I am considering contracting or working for European companies, some of which
have four weeks of annual vacation. I'm not good at self-marketing, though, so
I don't think I'd do well as a completely independent contractor. Can anyone
advise me on their experiences with working for contracting companies or
four-day work week schedules in doing Y2K COBOL work? I have 15 years of
COBOL, JCL, Easytrieve, and some CICS background. Also, how long do the Y2K
projects tend to be? And what have been your experiences with consulting as a
way to travel?

Thanks for any feedback.

Most European shops have some kind of flexi-time, but normally of the
'almost any 35 hours over 5 days' kind of thing (eg very few are happy with
4 dau weeks). If you are contracting, in theory you can take as many
holidays as you like (as you don't get paid for them). In practice, it never
works that way - you are always at the start/middle/end of a project and
it's 'not really convenient' (I get bored with holidays anyway). At the
moment COBOL is fairly 'hot' so shouldn't be a problem getting work - you'll
probably need other skills, CICS and DB2 or Adabas/Natural should suit most
of Europe (you may have to speak the lingo for France and some parts of
Germany though) - has to be the UK for the music scene though!
