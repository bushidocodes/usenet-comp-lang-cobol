I remember one year when I was looking for work that I went 
on about 30 interviews.  At each interview I was asked 
almost all the same questions.  I formulated them into what 
I called the SST questions, SORT, SEARCH and TABLES.  I 
concluded that if a person knew those answers, he could 
bluff his way into a COBOL job, not even knowing COBOL. 
(Though the bluff would be seen through soon enough.)

At one of the last interviews, for a company I wouldn't work 
for even at twice my going rate, I was asked if I had any 
questions.  I said "Yes, is there a standard list of 
questions that all interviewers have because you are the 
30th. person to ask me the same questions."  Of course I 
wasn't offered the job there, as I greatly insulted the HR 
person with my sneering tone.

So, if you want a job as a COBOL programmer, know COBOL.  It 
will be obvious that you are entry level and they will 
expect a learning curve.  But if your knowledge of COBOL is 
that "it is some kind of English-like computer language" 
then McDonald's has a place for you.
