I wish I had an easier answer. I grabbed every part-time, summer, temp.,
and co-op job (that had anything to do with programming) that I could get
my hands on while in college. I co-op'd at $700+/month and even did
contracting at $4/hr., of course back then the minimum wage was $1.60/hr.
It made a HUGE difference, I found work fairly quickly, I don't remember
how long it took. In fact I never graduated but that was because I got
screwed transferring and 5 years of college (and 140 hours) was more than
enough. Anyway, it worked.

experienced programmers wanting to get experience in more current
technology - is to do volunteer work for free somewhere e.g. nonprofits for
a short time, enough to prove yourself, and then see if you can arrange to
do some work for them (or for some place they could give you a lead to) for
very cheap, then you have actual paid experience on your rï¿½sumï¿½. Call it an
internship or something to explain away the low rate, so you don't get
stuck with a ridiculously low rate or salary on your first serious full
