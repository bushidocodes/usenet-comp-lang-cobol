Disagreeing (but only a little).

"Line Sequential" is "by definition" not ANSI/ISO conforming (at least in any
implementation that requires you to "say" Line Sequential).  Therefore, it
may (or may not) work as described below.  (I think it probably does - in
most implementations).

HOWEVER, in any ANSI/ISO conforming implementation WRITE (with no
AFTER/BEFORE clause) of a file that is ANSI/ISO conforming (i.e. nothing to
"say" it is line or record sequential) *MUST* work like a WRITE AFTER
ADVANCING 1.

Now what that means in "practice" will vary TREMENDOUSLY on whether you are
talking about files that are really intended for "printers" (or the like) or
just as "data".  This also varies a LOT on the operating system, compiler,
and "file system" (which may or may not be designed with COBOL in mind).
