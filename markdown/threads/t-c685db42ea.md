Darren,

I'm afraid trying to find out IBM offerings is like trying to milk a
bull. Best bet go to objectz.com and check out Bill Klein's FAQ. I can't
recall the author but there was a recent article there, (in
objectz.com), about a German bank (Dresden ???) using OO with an IBM
mainframe. (There is the VisualAge version also).

Are you already conversant with OO from other languages ? You could
write wprice@objectz.com, who has just produced his second edition of
Elements of OO Programming. It is a good tutorial. You can check out Ed
Arranga (also objectz.com) and Frank Coyle via amazon.com for their
book. Both these books go beyond theory with examples - but stress both
are NetExpress - and PC-based. Merant(Micro Focus) offer a University
Edition for about $100; same goes for Fujitsu - go to their site).

In terms of collections/dictionaries I could send you code, but it would
be meaningless without the NetExpress support classes and besides might
be totally different from what IBM have as classes. As yet collections
haven't even been dreamed about by the Standards (J4) and collection
methods between Merant and Fujitsu are poles apart. The nearest approach
to the OO Standard is Hitachi, but they recently informed somebody they
are not marketing Hitachi OO COBOL outside of Japan.

I agree; I didn't much care for the Dice example - shows how to do OO -
but it doesn't really jive when it comes to thinking about business
applications. You would do better looking at his file example, although
he has wrapped that inside Rational Rose - and confused the hell out of
me. Anyway that particular author has just taken a new job and
completely dropped OO COBOL - part of his reasoning - the outrageous
price of COBOL compilers !

If you make any headway stay in touch with me privately.

Jimmy, Calgary AB
