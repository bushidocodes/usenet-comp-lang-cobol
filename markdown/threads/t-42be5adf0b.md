They got married here last week.

So far, the honeymoon is all kissey-kissey and 'baby snookums.'

Whether this union survives is still an open question, but, for now,
there's every indication that future difficulties, if any, can be
overcome.

As an aside, XP moved onto a machine formerly inhabited by another
couple: Win2K and FJ5. The installation process was quick, clean, and
problem-free.

XP is cute and clever. For example, XP, on its own initiative, prowled
throughout our network and found every sharable printer. It then
installed drivers for these printers and added them to XP's
'Printers' table. XP also converted/configured everything about our
network - no human decisions needed.

The resulting XP desktop is BLANK except for the "Recycle Bin' icon,
which, when you think on it, is a tad silly because there's nothing IN
the recycle bin after an install.

If any family fights develop between the slim maiden and the burly
giant, we'll report them here.
