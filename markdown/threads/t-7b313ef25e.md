> As far as a "reference manual" goes, the 2 products TEND to be so similar,
> that I would think that looking at a DFSORT manual would answer MOST
> questions about Syncsort (features and syntax).

That's a very iffy proposition since each product has exclusives that the other
doesn't.

Looking at the DFSORT books, you would NOT be able to tell which documented
features were also available in Syncsort . For example, you could read about the
VLTRIM=byte, TRAN=ALTSEQ, TRAN=UTOL, TRAN=LTOU, REMOVECC, FTOV, DATEn, DATEn(c),
DATEnP, TIMEn, TIMEn(c), TIMEnP, DTn, TMn, etc options in the DFSORT book and
not know they weren't available in Syncsort.

Or you could read about the DISCARD, FIRSTDUP, LASTDUP, INDENT, BETWEEN,
STATLEFT, E'pattern', etc options of DFSORT's ICETOOL and not know that they
weren't available in "SYNCTOOL".  Of course, you could read the Syncsort book
and still not know what was available in "SYNCTOOL" since it's undocumented.

Obviously, the online DFSORT books and website document what's available with
DFSORT, not with Syncsort, so you'd be going down a lot of blind alleys by
trying to use the DFSORT books to figure out how to use Syncsort.   On the other
hand, the DFSORT books and website are freely available so there's no guessing
about what's supported by DFSORT.
