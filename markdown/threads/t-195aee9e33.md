```
<gripe mode on>
It has been one of those weeks.  I've been working on one of our programs from
the underworld (the one with that ugly IF statement) putting in what should
have been a fairly easy change.  Of course the BA who made up the specs didn't
bother to note that the same change had to go into 2 other programs, but that's
beside the point.  I'm not real familar with the whole system so it wasn't
obvious at the time I got the specs to ask the other teams about their
programs.  It has been duly noted in my 'lessons learned' notebook that when I
get specs from this particular BA to immediately ask around about the change -
especially when he says it doesn't affect anything else.

Anyway, I put it into production and about an hour later the one of the
warehouses calls and says it isn't working and a truck is about to leave and
needs their paperwork (truckers are always about to leave - they never seem to
stick around for a cup of coffee and chit chat).  Pull out the program and put
in the prior version. 

Run the new one thru Xpediter again - don't see anything particularly wrong so
let's compare to the original.  Hot dang - the original does exactly the same
'mistake'! Which means the dang thing never worked right in the first place -
but since I'm the last one to touch it I have to fix it!  Putter around in the
program, figure out what has to change and put it back into production.

It's some gawd awful time of the morning and the phone rings.  Actually I'm
already up but my first cup of coffee is just begining to circulate but I'm not
out of the navel contemplation phase of the morning.  It's my boss - the
program is doing something weird again - am I coming in today?  Duh - I guess
so - did I give you any notice that I'm taking time off?  Maybe I will since
you've asked.  Do you have any idea what's the problem.  Uh duh no - but then
right now I have the brain of an ameoba and the reflexes of a snail so don't
expect any miracles, call back after my 2nd cup and I'll consider the
situation.

I'm at work - in once piece - no thanks to the Greyhound on the x-way that
wanted to be in the same lane I was in at the same time and place that I was
already occupying.  He probably flunked physics which is why he's driving a
bus. 

I grab another cup of coffee (caffein junky) and put the program back into test
and Xpedite again.  Interesting - there is a field that isn't justify correctly
so it doesn't read this other file correctly so the incorrect information is
put on the file when it is updated.  Pull in the original - check it out - well
hot dang again!  It doesn't read the file correctly either - but it did some
crazy tweaking of the incorrect field so that when the update was done it had
the right record but the update was wrong because the original fields weren't
there due to the not found the first time thru. The change corrected the
original mistake and let the other come glaring out as the change removed the
funky tweaking. Wasn't found in original testing because I couldn't get to all
the permutations of some of the IF statements.  Ok - put that change into
production - or try to.  Sent the paperwork down to the helpless desk at 4:30. 
All paperwork has to be in by 5pm. We're supposed to have 15 minute turn around
for on-line programs.  Batch goes in whenever during the day unless you have a
job down and then it's supposed to be now (assuming you can get thru on the
phone to tell them it has to go now).  I wait, and wait, and wait.  No response
that the program has been moved.  It's now 5:10 and no production move.  My
boss calls down and everyone has gone home except for one gal working late that
is strictly a network person.  Call the on-call and wait. He calls about 5:30
grumbly he's late for the theater but he'll get to it when he gets home.  Uh -
sorry - but (you guessed it) the truck is about to leave and needs their
paperwork.  Program goes in.  I pick up my purse and hightail it out the door.

It's now about 7:30pm and I'm elbow deep in nailpolish (hey, gotta keep those
digits nice to key all day).  My cell phone rings.  It's the on-call.  The
warehouse can't get another set of paperwork done in a different program.  The
support person of the other team says it's the fault of 'that' program. (The
on-call for the other team is this young snert who lectured me about how he or
another one of his team are called when one of their programs doesn't work and
how he doesn't appreciate being called when he's in bed at 11:00pm etc etc -
and why can't I move my process to some more human like time like when he's at
the office instead of at 11:00pm and so on -like I had no idea they woke people
up at that time of night!) Ok - no way am I going to be near a computer for
another hour so the on-call passess the call on to the next in line on-call
because he's at the theater.

Get home - find out that quick change has to go into this other program.  Put
it in, compile it, check it out, no obvious problem.  Call the 2nd on-call to
move it in. I get a call back - it didn't compile clean.  Look at the errors -
END-IF not defined, huh?  Invalid character (reference modification), huh? 
Back to the top of the compile.  Compiled on May 18 1901!  Turns out the
production compile proc is using OS/VS Cobol while the test proc is using
OS/390 Cobol.  How long has this been going on?  Probably since Nov 1999.  Why
no one has noticed I have no idea since we've had several new programs go in
written by people who do use END-IF and reference modification all the time
that are of this ilk (on-line cobol w/DB2).

Monday I get to listen to the young snert again,  explain the tech manager that
the production compile proc is incorrect (he never makes mistakes), scan the
source library for all programs of this particular type and see how many we
have to recompile (they want everyting above the line if possible).  If I'm
lucky the warehouse won't call and I can get back to more important things like
tracking down the 7000 headlamps that are lost in traffic.

I love my job, I love my job, I love my job.................
<gripe off>
```

```
I thought that very strange too.  Now that I've had time to get some sleep and
think about it I have a hunch that this is a quirk of Librarian and the the
program has a bad history record.

During the Y2K conversion we changed the history records on a lot of programs
to cause them to use the then Cobol II compiler instead of the OS/VS compiler.
Once LE was completely installed the compiler was changed to OS/390 but the
type tag on the Librarian history records remained the same as the Cobol II,
only the proc was changed to point to the new compiler.  If the change in
history record was not done correctly it would appear that it had been changed
when it wasn't.  This may be the case and since no one else who has changed the
program used END-IF or any other 'new' construct it wasn't apparent until now.

Something to look into Monday morning (after coffee of course).
```

```
There are days I want to say BadA** but it really means Business Analyst.  This
is a person who may or may not at one time have been a programmer but gets all
requests for maintenance/enhancements/development and checks out the systems
and determines what has to be changed and how long it will take.  They are
supposed to know the business to determine the impact of the request over all. 
They then make up the specs we work from.  These can be as simple as a note on
an envelope to a whole booklet.
```
