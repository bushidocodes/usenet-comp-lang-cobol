FF LLLL DD MON YY  (first 2 of first name, first 2 of last name, rest fairly

having a good (schooled) grasp of ASM, i'd rather not de-construct this
puppy;besides, i think the ASM is unnecessarily convoluted (or is that a
prerequisite with ASM?) in achieving its goal.

now, if we could just go with how to accomplish this (which my first inclination
is to unstring based on......something which my brain hasn't fully grasped yet),
and not get into any of the potential side issues such as y2k and asm and
not-so-brilliant designers of systems and shops that don't support things and
don't tell the IS staff until....wait, they never did announce it, i had to dig
it out of them.... anyway, is unstring in this instance the way to go? and if i
do an unstring (delimited on spaces) of the whole operand, how do i then go
about determining which piece is in which slot--size won't work, numeric won't
work, order in the slots can't be counted on to be consistent given the various
combinations that are possible/permissible.

or maybe i just need to step back,sleep on it, something.

that, we
never use it 'cause it doesn't work' (you can always hope).

Anyway, hope that's a start.

Susie:

One technique that may be of some help is to MOVE the string you must
decipher into a temporary string, then use an INSPECT...CONVERTING on
the temporary string to derive a 'character type' for each character in
the string.  I put 'character type' in quotes because this is not some
formal COBOL thing but rather a procedural aid to decoding the string.

To take your example, and assuming that:

1. names do not contain numbers (which may or may not be true in
Wisconsin  :)  );

2. names may contain '#' (which might actually be a placeholder for an
embedded space in the last name, not just a nuisance placeholder for
trailing spaces; e.g. "van#der#Floogl"); and

3. actual space(s) are delimiters between fields,

then do something like this:

01  the-operand   pic x(50).
01  char-type     pic x(51).
* TRICK!! length difference guarantees a SPACE 
* in the last position of char-type
01  operand-count        PIC 99.
01  operand-array.  02 occurs 5 indexed by operand-index.
    03  operand-value pic x(50).
    03  operand-position pic 99.
    03  operand-length pic 99.
    03  operand-mask  pic x(50).
    03  operand-type pic X.
        88  operand-is-not-present value "N".
        88  operand-is-alpha value "A".
        88  operand-is-mixed value "M".
        88  operand-is-numeric value "9".
01  the-operand-length   PIC 99.
01  operand-count        PIC 99.
01  beginning-position   PIC 99.
01  op-length            PIC 99.
01  i                    PIC 99.
01  op-type              PIC X.

[please imagine all other WS definitions the procedural example needs.]

  MOVE 50 TO the-operand-length.
....
  MOVE the-operand TO char-type.
  INSPECT char-type CONVERTING
       "#ABCDEFGHIJKLMHOPQRSTUVWXYZabcdefghijklmonopqrstuvwxyz"
       TO ALL "A".
  INSPECT char-type CONVERTING "0123456789" TO ALL "9".
* at this point char-type identifies the character types of all
* the characters in the-operand.
  MOVE 0 TO operand-count.
  MOVE 1 TO beginning-position.
  INSPECT char-type (beginning-position:)
          TALLYING beginning-position FOR LEADING SPACES
  PERFORM VARYING operand-index FROM 1 BY 1 
            UNTIL operand-index > 5
      SET operand-is-not-present TO TRUE
      IF beginning-position NOT > the-operand-length
          MOVE beginning-position TO operand-position (operand-index)
          MOVE 0 to op-length
          MOVE char-type (beginning-position: 1) TO op-type
* TRICK!! described above used here
          INSPECT char-type (beginning-position:)
              TALLYING op-length FOR CHARACTERS BEFORE INITIAL SPACE
          MOVE 0 TO i
          MOVE op-type TO operand-type (operand-index)
          INSPECT char-type (beginning-position: op-length)
              TALLYING i FOR ALL op-type
          IF i NOT = op-length
              SET operand-is-mixed TO TRUE
          END-IF
          MOVE op-length TO operand-length (operand-index)
          MOVE char-type (beginning-position: op-length)
              TO operand-mask (operand-index)
          MOVE the-operand (beginning-position: op-length)
              TO operand-value (operand-index)
          INSPECT operand-value (operand-index)
              REPLACING ALL "#" BY SPACE
          ADD 1 TO operand-count
          INSPECT char-type (beginning-position:)
              TALLYING beginning-position FOR LEADING SPACES
      END-IF
  END-PERFORM.

At this point, you have a much more manageable array of operands, you
know the operand count and type of each operand, you have a character
type mask for handling mixed alpha and numeric strings (if needed), and
those pesky "#" are already replaced with spaces.

Note:  this routine is offered where-is, as-is, since it hasn't been
compiled much less executed (but I do this kind of thing from time to
time).  You may be required to debug it.

I hope this has helped.

Best regards,
