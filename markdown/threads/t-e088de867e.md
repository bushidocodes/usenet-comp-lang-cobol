HIPAA is a set of "laws" that give specifications for the
handling of data by insurance companies, hospitals and other
health care providers (even equipment vendors). I'm just in
the process of coming up to speed on this myself (I know
enough to be truly dangerous and not enough to know how to
explain what I do know).

The why you would care has to do with whether or not some
software you deal with or work on is HIPAA compliant and/or
is required to be HIPAA compliant. The other thing has to do
with how long you keep backups, what has to be archived and
for how long, physical security of data, what part of the
data is confidential and what part is not, etc.

One modest correction: software is not and can not be certified

clearinghouses) may be "HIPAA-compliant."
