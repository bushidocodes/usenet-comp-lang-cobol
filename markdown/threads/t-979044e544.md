Ross, here is what I did. It is a bit of a kludge, but it is the way I do
Laserjet work anyway. All of my reporting out of an MS/DOS application running
under Windows/95 comes by way of WORD 6.0. My apps create temporary files which
are picked up by WORD 6.0. The report is formated by a WORDBASIC macro.

Given that I already use WORD, I selected a token to turn on barcodes, and I
could have had one to turn off barcodes, but my application did not need this
"feature." I have a barcode font. My token is the { character. When ever the
macro sees a { it takes the next characters up to the new line character to be
part of the barcode and renders it in the barcode font. I did some further work
to scale the barcode, but that was just for pretty.

I am sure that there are more clever ways, but this does the job for me. If you
are interested in the details, drop me an E-Mail message and I would be glad to
share my approach with you.
