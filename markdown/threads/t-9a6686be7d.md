I believe a degree day is the average difference in degrees
less 72. In otherwords on a 50 degree (average) day you
will have to supply enough heat to bring the house to
72 degrees. Such a day would count as 22 degree days.

This is only half the foumula, you also need a
profile for each house indicating how many BTUs it takes
per degree day. This is usually acquired from
historical records and does not vary much unless
they leave windows open or something like that.

>From memory (the climatological table in the NY Times used to explain this,
but alas, that's been dropped from the paper):

Degree days is the difference between the 65 degrees and the average daily
temperature (defined as the arithmetic mean of the max and min values).

So if the the temperature ranged from 39 to 49, the degree days would be

65 - (39 + 49) / 2
