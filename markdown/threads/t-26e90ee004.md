```
The Whole Syntax is called the ANSI / ISO standard Document. The current
issue is the 1985 version. To my knowledge it is only available in
hardcopy. Last time I purchased one is was about $45 from ANSI in NYC.
No doubt either Bill Klein or Don Nelson can fill in the current details.

For IBM implementations the defintinitions are available in either hard
copy or softcopy form. The softcopy requires a special reader program
called BookManager (available for a wide variety of platforms). The hard
copy books are somewhat pricey, the softcopy books are generally
inexpensive when purchased in sets of many books.
```

```
If you are in the US, then contact ANSI and if you are in another
country, contact your national Standards body. In the US, the
current COBOL Standard is defined by

X3.23-1985
amended by X3.23a-1989
amended by X3.23b-1993

There are also ISO equivalents to each of these.

All of this will get you the complete definition of the
"Standard" COBOL language. If you want a "complete definition"
of a specific implementation (DEC, IBM, Tandem, Micro Focus, etc)
- then you will need to talk to that vendor.
```
