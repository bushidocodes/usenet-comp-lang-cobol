A few month ago, I told about a WebSite where a
free implementation of a Quantum Computing Language
(QCL) could be found, which could serve as a
prototype for quantum computing language
elements within any language, such as COBOL2020.

Today, I tell about another text which is quite
worthwhile to read - a book this time:

"Quantum Computation and Quantum Information" by
Michael Nielsen and Ike Chuang.

The problem in understanding Quantum Computing
is a full understanding of Quantum Theory first
- but there are a few permissible shortcuts which
these authors take. They introduce in exceptionally
clear language QuBits and Quantum-gates, introducing
lighthandedly the Dirac-Notation as they go
along, and spare the reader the Schrï¿½dinger
equation and dull exercises in solving it. No
wavepackets, no hydrogen-atom, no harmonic oscillator
- for a basic understanding, these are not
(yet) needed.

As early as page 27 an understanding of quantum
teleportation is reached, quantum cryptography
and all other issues come somewhat later. After
the easy introductory parts there is, beginning
at page 61, a more detailed course in quantum
physics. The book has some 676 pages - the
greater part of which I have not yet read.

As far as I can see, the book places more
emphasis on Quantum Networks than on Quantum
Programming Languages. Also, philosophical issues
are not addressed - no "MWI or not MWI" discussion
which other authors (D. Deutsch) see as essential
in the subject. Only one Page is dedicated to the
poor Schrï¿½dinger's cat. Finally, the physical
construction of Quantum Computers is not addressed
- for that, you must look elsewhere.

I do not regret having invested the money
(DM 104.21, US$ 47.95).
