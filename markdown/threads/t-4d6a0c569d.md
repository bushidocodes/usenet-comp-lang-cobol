Henk,

Your investigation led you to the correct conclusion.  The following rule is
found in the 1985 ISO standard:

"Whenever a match occurs, tallying or replacing takes place as described in
general rules 10 and 13.  The character position in the data item referenced
by identifier-1 [w-fld in your example] immediately to the right of the
rightmost character position that participated in the match is now
considered to be the leftmost character position of the data item referenced
by identifier-1, and the comparison cycle starts again with the first
literal-1 [the all 'h' associated with ctr1 in your example]." *

So, when the first all 'h' matches and ctr1 is incremented, that is the last
time that the matching 'h' is considered, and the inspect goes on to the
next character to the right.

You gave the small nudge that I needed to solve this problem. After reading
your post it dawned upon me that I had been staring at the answer the whole
time. Each time I was reasoning I said that I could not duplicate the
problem with the [inspect replacing all 'h' by '*' ] because all the 'H' 's
would be changed to '*' 's
So it was proving the rule the whole time that the first "all" match will
match all the specified characters and any other match (leading, first ,all
,before/after) will find nothing because the first match got them all. So if
an 'all' match (this in include all before/after depending on the string
data) is done any other match type should be done in a separate inspect.
This will obviously only work with tallying as a replace all will also
render the separate inspect replacing's useless.

The IBM manuals are not clear and I do not have a MicroFocus manual.
However, the ANSI standard is pretty clear. Once you have a match
(have tallied an a character or string), you do not tally it again,
even if you have more TALLYING statements.  Kinda like EVALUATE ---
once you get a match, you stop checking.

HOWEVER, a combination of TALLYING and REPLACING is _specifically_
treated as 2 INSPECT statements.
