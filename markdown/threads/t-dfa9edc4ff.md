Hi:

Remember the Great Programming Contest?

To remind you, the GPC arose out of heated
arguments in COMP.LANG.COBOL about the pros
and cons of OO, structured style, other
languages, RAD and stuff like that.

The idea was for people to develop a simple
Time-Clock System (TCS) using their chosen
style/method, etc., so as to demonstrate to
the others that their way was superior. Un-
fortunately, nobody entered the contest,
except me. :(

You can read more of my minimalist views
concerning inexpensive and rapid development
of business applications at foodman123.com/tcs.htm
where you can download a working copy of my entry
in the GPC.

There are approximately 4,500 lines in the
compiled TCS. They break down as follows:

    New Code     1,213
    SP2 copies   2,279 (Generated by SP2)
    Other copies   963 (Standard copies)

TCS.EXE is 175k. The distributable
version is 1.15mb which includes SP2.DLL,
the WAV files, the panel file, and, the
various COBOL runtime things.

The TCS was designed from scratch, pro-
grammed, tested and packaged for dis-
tribution in about 16 hours. This represents
about 76 lines per hour of debugged code
(1213/16). (4500/16=281!).  Applications
like TCS can easily be created using
personnel with minimal training (no
expensive programmers required with
elaborate skill-sets. :)

If you can't improve on 16 hours, and
cannot produce a program which is better
in ANY way, using currently-popular
methods and languages, then who needs
those methods and languages?  The answer,
of course, is 'NOBODY'.
