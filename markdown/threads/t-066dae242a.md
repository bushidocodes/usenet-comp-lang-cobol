so years I know where I stand with MF.  I don't have any serious

ago.

```
The more I look around the more I think doing a callable C program will be the
solution.  For example, AccuCOBOL works more or less the same as Micro Focus
and doesn't support intrinsic functions (which I happen to like).  RM/COBOL
works more easily with non controlling tty devices but doesn't support free
format coding (which I consider mandatory - I've had it with margin "A" and
"B" and after 30 years of COBOL am not going back to a format dictated by 80
column punched cards) and also doesn't support intrinsic functions.  Fujitsu
COBOL may or may not easily work with serial devices, I dunno, but it appears
they don't have a SCO Unix version.  And then there's Relia, now CA, and with
the reputation Computer Associates has I won't touch that one.

So, in the end, there's Micro Focus, kinda like my wife, still looking pretty
good after all these years.
```
