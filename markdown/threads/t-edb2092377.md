It was a lot of fun. I've never done this before. Neither Dave nor his
producer Dan Fuller ever asked me if I had done this before, and I
didn't volunteer the information. Dave had originally sent me an email
asking if I did interviews. I never actually answered that question, I
just said I was happy to talk.

I did a couple radio interviews for medical marijuana, but those were
taped, and I was being interviewed by a fellow advocate, for a show with
relatively low listnership. Here I was trying to talk coherently with a
host and callers who weren't trying to help me make my point, live, on
one of the biggest stations in Seattle. I had butterflies, and I got a
little meally mouthed at points, but I survived. At least I don't know
anyone in Seattle. Being on an LA show might have been too scary.

Dave Ross does "Chip Talk" on CBS radio, but he is not a techie. He
knows a lot about the computers, but from a journalist's perspective.

I wasn't assertive enough. I just barely plugged the URL of my web site
at the end of the hour. I should have just done that in the middle of
the show, but I was expecting him to ask me to plug my URL. Also, he
led the discussion, so I sounded rather alarmist, because he kept
hitting topics where I said there was cause for concern.

I have been listening to talk radio since I was 8yo. I know a lot about
how the business works. The absolute worst situation is when you're
scheduled for the whole hour, but at the half hour (or a little earlier)
the host says "thank you, and now let's open up the phones for
whatever's on your mind...." This did not happen.

The best is when the host asks on the air if you can be on his show
again. This did not happen. Second best is when he asks *off* the air
if you can be on again. This didn't happen either.

The hour started at 11:08, he went to me at 11:10, yet didn't go to a
caller until 11:33. I've never heard Dave Ross's show, I don't know how
he likes to run the show, but in LA this would mean he wasn't getting
calls. When this happens, it doesn't matter how good you are, you don't
get asked back.

But I had a good time, and I want to do it again. I'll tell Dave to
keep me in mind for when the topic is getting more interest. Meanwhile
I may start offering myself to radio stations. I like being a Y2K
pundit.
