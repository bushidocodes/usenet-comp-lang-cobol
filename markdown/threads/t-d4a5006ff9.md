Hi. Working in MVS COBOL II/CICS/DB2 on some sort of IBM mainframe, I tried
to do something like this:

IF INITIAL-FUNCTION
PERFORM
VARYING LINE-SUB FROM 1 BY 1
UNTIL LINE-SUB > LINE-MAX
IF ACTION-FLD (LINE-SUB) > SPACES
MOVE LINE-SUB TO ACTIVE-LINE
NEXT SENTENCE
END-IF
MOVE 1 TO ACTIVE-LINE
END-PERFORM

it's close enough to get the gist of the problem. The code seems
syntactically correct, but the compiler complained that NEXT SENTENCE
wasn't allowed there and refused to compile until I removed it. I took
it out and got around it somehow (and the program works). But why did it
complain about NEXT SENTENCE? I wanted to use it to jump entirely and
immediately to the statement following the period. Can I not do that?
