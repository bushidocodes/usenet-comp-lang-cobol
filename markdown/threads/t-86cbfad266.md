Pride has nothing to do with it.  Maintaining standards of excellence

In the US, society pays for education through tax dollars.  This
includes elementary, secondary and post secondary education.
Community colleges and state universities are funded through local,
state and federal funds.  Students can almost always enroll in
work-study programs and obtain education loans.  Almost every college
requires the parents to complete financial need forms in order to
determine the "expected family contribution."  Based upon the expected
contribution, those with a lower contribution are generally given
higher priority for need-based scholarships and grants.

Those qualified candidates who also happen to be needy can indeed
obtain an excellent college education through their local community
college or through a state university at a much lower cost than MIT,
Harvard, Yale or Princeton.

non-publicly funded schools.  I happen to know a brilliant young lady
