There are two different questions here:

1) Why do I only see some of the answers to a post?

The simple answer is that usenet/email/the internet isn't a perfect system. To
quote 'The Moderated Newsgroups FAQ':

    Also, e-mail, net news, and robo-mod software are imperfect, and
    human moderators are, after all, human.  Your message may have
    failed to reach the moderator, or been lost in a system or disk
    crash, or been misplaced by the moderator, or been approved and
    then failed to propagate correctly via net news back to your site.

In other words, somewhere between the moderator and your news server the
articles will have gone missing. For the same reasons you sometimes get articles
in the wrong order.

If you need to read the whole thread, the best advice is to try another news
server or a site like www.deja.com.

2) Too much old news

Whichever news server you're connecting to will have expiry times set up for
each newsgroup, which defines the minimum and maximum length of time the
articles will hang around for. Independant of that your newsreader (Outlook
Express in this case) keeps a record of which articles you've read. When you
open the newsgroup it should display headers for all the articles available,
with the ones you haven't read in bold.

Tim, if the problem is that it's not remembering which artices you've read then
it's a problem with your OE setup. However if it knows what you've read but just
has a long list of previously read articles then that's because your news server
is not expiring the articles very quickly. I wouldn't knock it - most people
complain that articles dissappear too quickly!


This is all a bit off-topic; for more info check out news.newusers.questions.

Dan.

As I understand it, there is no "master" newsserver to insure everyone
is synchronized.  All newsservers are logically interconnected, but
(unlike email servers) each only speaks directly to a few immediate
neighbor(s).  Smaller ISP's may have only a single feed (one neighboring
server), while larger ISP's tend to have multiple feeds.  When someone
enters a news item, it propagates from his newsserver to its neighbors
in the network, to the neighbor's neighbors, etc. until it eventually
reaches all newsservers -- provided there isn't a hardware glitch, a
software glitch, or a shortage of resources or self-imposed message
count limit on one of the receiving nodes to break the chain.  You can
also expect that some messages will arrive at your newsserver in
different a order from that seen on other newsservers.   The fewer the
feeds into your ISP's newsserver, the higher the odds of lost messages. 
Several years ago when I went from a smaller ISP with one feed to one
with multiple feeds, I saw a five-fold increase in the number of
messages seen in one of my newsgroups.  It's also possible that your own
ISP may have insufficient hardware resources dedicated to the newsserver
and is at times rejecting some inbound news traffic that exceeds its
capacity.

You might try comparing message counts with someone who has a different
newsserver or perhaps with dejanews.  If your ISP has significant
message-loss problems on news groups that are important to you and is
unable or unwilling to improve, try another ISP.
