You're sure the 3490's haven't already got their hardware level compression
permanently on?  Ours was permanently on.

Do you know for sure what kind of tape is being used?  3480 or 3490E?

Since the average 3480 tape holds about 500 megabytes of data (550 feet of
tape at something like 38,000 bytes per inch and with x2 for hardware
compression).  The figures for the posted problem are 3,200,000recs x
500bytes = 1.6 gigabytes, and well, that's where the problem is.  1.6
gigabytes is above the capabilities of a 3480 tape.

A 3490E tape has twice as many data tracks across the width of the tape, and
is 1100 feet long, and so holds roughly x4 data.  However, because of poor
tape quality, the full 2 gigabytes are probably not available.

The more modern Magstar tapes start at 10 gigabytes.


However, what I don't understand is why this needs to be on "one" tape (see
the post below).  Why not just extend the dataset onto multiple tapes?  Even
without adding parameters, it should extend on up to at least 5 volumes
before taking an error.

1. LABEL= will not affect the number of tapes needed
2. VOL= is not needed
3.TRTCH may be needed, but COMP is usually the default

You may have more than 1 type of tape drive at your disposal, check the
UNIT= parameters available for tapes.

Check the Block Size on the CA-1 Volume report.

Check with your Systems people, and see if the Storage Management System
supports VIRTUAL TAPES.
If so, they should be able to tell you about it.

You may want to use BUFNO= to speed up the I/O.

Try writing to dasd and then copy the dataset to tape (in a followup JOB).
