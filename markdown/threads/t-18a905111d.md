There is no problem in converting at all. You simply save the generated
COBOL source code, compile from that and toss out the SCOBOL. The
problems come in when you then have to maintain the new COBOL. If your
shop is full of SCOBOL people, you need to retrain them to work in a new
(though similar) language. If you have made signicant use of the bells
and whistles in APS (like APS macros, for instance), you will need to
allow time to do that work manually.

We have a largish system written in SCOBOL for APS (1700+ programs). We
decided it would be too expensive to maintain in regular COBOL. Thats
to bad, casue real COBOL is much more fun...
