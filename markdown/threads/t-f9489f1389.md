Telon is a product that generates Cobol or PL/1 code.  In its earliest form
its purpose was to assist in the creation of online (IMS or CICS) screens by
hiding many of the environmental details from the programmer, who was then
free to concentrate on the business logic (well, in theory anyway).   Since
then it has evolved to support other platforms such as AS400, Unix, DOS,
OS/2 and possibly more.  I don't know if it generates GUI screens, the
version I am familiar with is strictly character based.

The vendor is CA, and you can probably find out more on their website.

Mr. Leahy has correctly summarized TELON (the full name these days is
Advantage CA-Telon Application Generator).  It was the market leader
world-wide in the 80's and early 90's for a code generator which
assists in the development of "green-screen" and batch programs for
several IBM mainframe environments, as well as iSeries, UNIX and
Windows platforms.  In addition to generating environmental details
for the programmer, it also allows for high-level specification of
data access for a variety of methodologies, including IMS/DB, DB2,
Datacom/SQL, IDMS/SQL, and VSAM for OS/390, as well as DB2 on iSeries
and Windows, and Oracle and Ingres on UNIX.

As far as GUI is concerned, CA-Telon has supported for several years
an interface with the Jacada product, which allows the
Internet-enabling of existing applications, with no changes to
existing legacy code.  In addition, CA will soon be providing a
similar interface with the Advantage Gen product.

Typical CA-Telon customers are those with very large programming
shops: government agencies, insurance companies, banks, airlines, oil
companies, etc.  These companies have typically built thousands of
programs using Telon, with some shops even today having as many as 600
Telon programmers.

For more information,
http://www3.ca.com/Solutions/ProductFamily.asp?ID=1527
