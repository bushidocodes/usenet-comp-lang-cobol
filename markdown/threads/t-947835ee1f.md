Hi Sue,

I have been mainly a COBOL programmer for about 15 years and I'm more
in demand now than ever before.

That is because very few new people are opting to COBOL. But at the
same time demand for COBOL programmers is increasing. Partly because
of the lack of supply, and partly due to a renewed interest in it.

Mainframe's are not being replaced by client/servers. Client/servers
are instead hooking into mainframes, taking over distributed systems,
but not the mission critical systems. Almost all of Mainframe and
most of midrange applications are written in COBOL.

Client/server is very hot right now. Application development code has
doubled in the past 3 yrs. COBOL wasn't initially invited to the
party, but now the systems are maturing and maintainability is
becoming very important.

COBOL is getting it's share of the C/S market too.


Here's the source Infoworld, April 8, 1996, page 56.

Sentry Market Research surveyed 700 IS mangers what language they used
for client/server application development:

Visual Basic 23%
Cobol 21%
C++ 18%
C 15%
4GL 15%
Other 8%

Key findings:

The use of languages has doubled since 1993.

The fastest growing are Cobol and VB.

4GLs, Object Cobol, and C++ are the highest rated.

The use of model driven development tools is growing.

VB's strength is in graphical tool development, but placed in the
middle of the ratings list.
