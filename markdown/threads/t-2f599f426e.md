Look at the "text" datatype in MySQL.

http://www.mysql.com/doc/en/Column_types.html

You can also do this with multi-row inserts.  Add a "sequence number" 
field to your table, and store the text 255 characters at a time, in 
sequence.  It's a little more code to break it out and put it together, 
but it works great - we're doing something similar (except we're 
breaking on 6,000 characters, supporting a max of 100,000).

This may actually be more efficient than a large item anyway, as there 
is some significant overhead associated with the larger data types such 
as BLOB and TEXT.  And, you know that you won't be storing any more than 
necessary, within 255 characters.  (And, if you define them as VarChar, 
you won't be storing -any- extra data.)

Hope that helps...
