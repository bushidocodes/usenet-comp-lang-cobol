```
Sorry, had to get this in here.

I am still an intermediate programmer, but pretty strong on the mainframe.

We had an outage of one our company's main file servers.  The corporate
office suffered a 24 hour outage that was supposed to be 1 or 2 hours.  When
they brought it back up, the goddamn rights data was corrupted.  So all our
mainframe FTPs started failing.

I thought, just how many man hours of productivity are being lost here?  At
least Lotus Notes still worked.

We had a meeting with the director today about the outage and stuff.  I
didn't have anything to do with it.  So I just played dumb or whatever.  I
wanted to get some comments in there.  One of the systems people and myself
reminded the room of the strengths of the mainframe, and that such an outage
would be, shall we say, laughable?

So what I learned today was:

--->>> Data is the most important piece of any computer system.

--->>> The mainframe is not dead, and neither is COBOL.

--->>> Novell blows.

OK, outta here...
Jeff
```
