When asked correctly?

The interview for a consulting assignment should be a two-way interview;
that is, the prospective consultant should be interviewing the prospective
client as well as vice-versa. If the prospective consultant can't ask the
right questions, he/she deserves to get stuck doing ISTs. And if the
prospective client turns out to have misstated the terms of the engagement,
I can't see a moral barrier to giving a suitable notice and abandoning the
assignment.

This may seem a rather severe attitude about accepting/remaining on an
engagement, but it seems fair to me as long as once the consultant says,
"yes" and the work requested by the client is within the range of what was
discussed in advance, then the consultant does the work - no matter how
crummy or ill-advised - with the same amount of professional effort and
dedication as he/she puts into the 'cool' or 'fun' stuff.

My perspective is somewhat jaundiced by the fact that I rarely accept
engagements which call for "time" to do "whatever" - I look for "projects"
where there is a definable completion point. Those who accept "time"
engagements must accept the "whatever". Kinda like eating your vegatables in
exchange for having dessert, but that's life.
