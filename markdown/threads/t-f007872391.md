Companies have recognized that unused capacity costs money, and many
have consolidated, or moved to more efficient and cheaper platforms.

Typically, today's mainframe is used for nationwide processing.
Meaning that end-user interface starts at 6am until 7pm in order to
span all (48 state) time zones. Meaning that batch processing won't
start until 8-9pm, and will finish around 2-3am, sometimes longer.
Monthend processing might actually be completed after the next day's
business activities have started.

OTOH, that's the Production environment (region). The Test
environment(s) are a another story. A second shift of programmers is
possible, but performance will be slower for the 2nd shift programmers
since all primary resources will be used on production batch
processing.

Also typical is ACF2 or RACF security systems on mainframes, which
decide what your login ID can have access to. Usually, I cannot
directly update a production file, but I can view it. I believe
security can be set also keep you from viewing a production file.

The real problem is who will manage a second shift? And are they
smart enough to look at results, and not just count "elbows and
a**holes".
