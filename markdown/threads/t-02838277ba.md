   I have a web site < OperatingSystems.net >. The web site originally

general operating system comparison site. More recently, I am starting an
open source project to build an operating system for children (especially
for pre-literate and semi-literate young children).

   Unfortunately, the web site keeps getting too much traffic. It has been
kicked off of several different ISPs for generating too much traffic. I
have finally found an ISP (1-host) that can handle the traffic without
complaining, but they are charging more money than I can afford ($350 a
month).

   If I can't get financial assistance by March 24, 2001, then I will have
to shut down the web site.

   The last few weeks I have been sending e-mail to large corporations
asking if they will sponsor the web site, but either they completely
ignore me or they reply with a completely off-topic message that indicates
that they didn't even read the e-mail I sent.

   I should emphasize that not one penny of the money I am asking for will
go to me. Any sponsor will pay directly to the ISP.

   In trade for a sponsorship, I will place a 72 by 72 dpi picture along
with a URL link at the top of every web page on the site.

   I am willing to work for the money (again, none of the money will go to
me, the sponsor will pay directly to the ISP). I am willing to work up to
80 hours a week in trade for a sponsorship. I am a skilled assembly
language programmer (more than two dozen different processors, including
supercomputers, mainframes, minicomputers, and microprocessors), as well
as being skilled at Ada, C, C++, COBOL, Forth, FORTRAN, LISP, Lingo,
Pascal, PL/I, RPG, and SmallTalk. I also am skilled at digital and hand
illustration, hand painting, animation (digital 3d, digital 2d,
traditional cel, scratch, and drawing directly on film stock), video and
film editing, videography, cinematography, digital and photochemical
photography, lighting, film and video directing, sound recording, sound
editing, music composition, music performance (piano, keyboards, guitar,
bass, hand drums, percussion, and didgeradoo), copy editing, proofreading,
and writing. I am willing to do any work that is legal and moral,
including taking out the garbage, cleaning the bathrooms, or heavy lifting.

   I am also willing to trade a kidney for sponsorship. I only ask that
the recipient of the kidney pay for both the costs of the surgery and at
least a year of ISP fees. Again, NONE of the money will go to me, so that
gets around U.S. laws that only allow medical corporations to sell human
organs.

   If anyone knows anybody who might be able to pay the cost of the ISP
fees to keep this web site on-line, please have them contact me
immediately.

   Thank you.

--Milo (CatGuy@lamg.com)     <http://www.operatingsystems.net>
