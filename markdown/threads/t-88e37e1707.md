In reading these posts, I'd just like to throw my $.02 into the mix...

First of all, lying isn't necessary to get a job... if you got the skills
that the employer needs, then you have a legitimate chance of getting the
job. However, I see no real harm in embellishing a little or tailoring a
resume; maybe putting stuff that you were capable of but never actually did.

My sister basically did this on her job search... she has a master's degree
in botany, but found that there are practically no jobs out there, so she
learn a little programming and HTML and agressively looked for a job.
Specifically, she'd spend 40 hrs/wk sending out tailored resumes & cover
letters, contacting to companies to check if they'd received the resume thus
showing interest, and studying up on the company before the interview. She
eventually landed a job doing some HTML and she's now learning DB admin
stuff.

I just got a job myself after a pretty half-assed search for about 4 months.
Actually, I'm typing this from the training course... they're teaching
elementary UNIX right now. Anyway, I just graduated w/ a BS in Comp. Sci.;
other than that, I worked on a co-op for 6 months one year before and I was
working at a R&D facility on campus programming in Java. I fully utilized my 
campus career services; I brought in my resume probably a dozen times and it
was reviewed by like 10 different people. I attended the interview skills
class, I went to each job fair, and signed up for on campus interview.
Granted I didn't get my current job this way (I got this one by passing out
my resume at a job fair not run by the school), I know at least 10 people
that did. I think what prevented me from getting second interviews or not
getting offers was that I didn't show any knowledge of whatever company I
was interviewing with. All I did on the interview for my job was ask some
lame question about one of their products... I got an offer 2 days later. I
guess the best advice is just be persitent and have faith that you are good
enough and there is a job out there for you.

Hope this helps and good luck....
