In almost 20 years of COBOL programming and thousands of programs
and applications, I've had need maybe twice to do bit level
manipulation on any MVS or mainframe type COBOL program. I've had to
deal with a few mainframe "C" language programs in the recent past
that didn't do bit manipulation either. The "C" programs were
incredibly difficult to maintain because of the lack of structure.
Even our accomplished "C" programmer in the shop had trouble
figuring out what they did. In my opinion, not being able to access
bit fields in COBOL is "C" bigot reasoning and not valid. Bit fields
are extremely rare in mainframe business applications. Review your
data and your applications and see what best suits your needs. Why
would you choose "C" to process bit fields if you don't have very
many bit fields? As far as PC applications, Micro Focus COBOL is
definitely capable handling bit fields. But again, pick the language
that best suits your needs. COBOL is far more functional than it
was 15 years. Review what is new.

That's true however some vendors have included this via extends/calls.
Basically, any language that allows a binary integer representation of
data allows bit manipulation so that common routine can be written to
set/unset individual bits.

However, if the project you are considering depends "heavily" on bit
manipulation, C may well be a better choice. If however the project
depends mainly on database work (generic stuff not "external" (SQL
relational ...) go with COBOL. COBOL also has some very powerful
string manipulation primatives. Most versions of COBOL will allow
you to link COBOL and C so you might be able to have the best of
both worlds. And of course there are a lot of other language
choices out there. Personally, I believe language selection is one
of the least important choices in project development.

Good Luck,
