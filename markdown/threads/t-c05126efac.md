```
It's a cloudless Monday morning here in Tauranga and we are heading into the 
home straight for Xmas Day.

Today I'll be fiddling with the cobdata web site, picking up a turkey, and 
taking a walk along a less populated beach. A time for pleasant relaxation.

I usually like to spend Xmas morning thinking about, and drinking to, 
"absent friends".

That includes you guys... :-)

It is just one day of the year when I think about the people who are 
overseas, or have shuffled off this mortal coil, and recognise that they are 
or were an essential part of my own ongoing growth. I'm thankful for all of 
them.

It isn't sad or maudlin; I usually feel refreshed and energised by it 
(although that could be the alcohol, of course...) and ready to meet friends 
in the afternoon and share my turkey in the evening.

To all of you, I wish you a pleasant time over the holidays. If this is a 
religious occasion for you, I hope you find peace and serenity. If it isn't, 
just enjoy your families and friends; remember they won't always be there. 
If you are on your own (and don't want to be) remember you are part of a 
family which is approaching 6.6 billion people. To help make this real for 
you, here's a link:

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zlfKdbWwruY
(I strongly recommend watching this in HD; click the little link to the 
bottom right of the image.)

And, despite the uncertainty of the times, let's hope the New Year is 
prosperous for all of us. :-)

Sincere best wishes to all in CLC.

Pete.
```
