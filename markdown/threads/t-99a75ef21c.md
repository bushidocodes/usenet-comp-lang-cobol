```
I'm guessing you're moving something to W-DATE-2?  That's what's called 
a "group item."  It is implicitly alphanumeric, and by moving something 
to it, you'll replace what's in it, which will make the individual 
variables no longer have their values.

You have two options.  The first, which I prefer, is to move the three 
pieces individually.  Instead of

MOVE MY-DATE TO W-DATE-2

, do something like

MOVE MY-YEAR  TO W-YEAR
MOVE MY-MONTH TO W-MONTH
MOVE MY-DATE  TO W-DAY

, which will leave your slashes intact.  The second option, which is a 
bit more complex (and, some would argue, overly complex just to save two 
lines of code), is to make the sending and receiving group items have 
elementary items (those with a PICture clause) with the same name.  So, 
given

01  MY-DATE.
     05  W-YEAR       PIC 99.
     05  W-MONTH      PIC 99.
     05  W-DAY        PIC 99.

you could type

MOVE CORRESPONDING MY-DATE TO W-DATE-2

(CORRESPONDING can be abbreviated CORR).  The disadvantage to this is 
that now, any time you reference the elementary items, you have to 
qualify which one you're talking about using an OF clause, like...

IF MY-YEAR OF W-DATE-2 > 50

...or something like that.
```
