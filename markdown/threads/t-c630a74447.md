To avoid any further speculation or spin from users of this group I will
make the following statement:

I stress that the following is personal opinion, based on my own experience.
It can (and probably will) be hotly argued by others.These comments may also
not apply if you are a Corporate Licenser with many seats and the resulting
leverage this gives you with Fujitsu Marketing.

1. The Fujitsu products are excellent. Technically superb.

2. Unfortunately, as the success of their products has grown, the company,
at least in the United States, has failed to maintain the early
responsiveness which was a hallmark of their support.

Four years ago it was possible to communicate with people in the
organization on technical issues. Now you register on a database and
eventually get assigned someone who will write to Japan on your behalf...

There ARE (or WERE) some extremely good technical people retained by Fujitsu
in the US, but the SYSTEM has changed and this has resulted in deteriorating
service to the customer base.

For example, around four years ago I was very impressed when a senior
manager there actually phoned me (I was in the U.K., he was in the US) to
resolve a problem. The same manager today doesn't even acknowledge my e-mail
(even when it is in response to his request). His workload has increased so
much he simply doesn't have the time that he did when the enterprise was
getting off the ground.

The overall standard of administration has visibly declined as the volume
has increased. In the US, this is not such a problem because you can pick up
a phone and get it sorted. For those of us outside the US, this is much more
problematic. Notwithstanding the cost of being put on hold or waiting for
IVR on an International call, there are the time differences...

E-mails frequently get bounced by the Fujitsu server but they won't
acknowledge that this happens, even when presented with the mail daemon
output. Maybe it only happens to mail OUTSIDE the US...(To be fair, I have
not communicated with them for some time now and this problem may have been
handled)

3. Jimmy is wrong when he says I will not implement Version 6. I haven't
done so to date but I reserve the right to do so (especially as I have paid
for it!).

My main problem with V6 is that it REQUIRES contact with Fujitsu if you want
to install or re-install it. Given the reservations expressed above, and the
fact that I am unlikely to be living and working in the USA in the
foreseeable future, would you take the risk? Install a product that is key
to your business and HOPE that the communication with the vendor will
improve?

Scenario: My hard disk crashes and I need to rebuild my system at 2 in the
afternoon (local time) on a working day...  With Version 5, no problem. With
Version 6, I need to contact Fujitsu and it is 5 in the morning in
California...

I don't think so...

My other problems with V6 are:

3.1. It cannot be installed with V5 so the systems can be compared and
benchmark tests can be run. If you install it and find there are problems,
you are then stuck with having to uninstall it and all the rigmarole that
that entails. What used to be a simple straightforward process is now
unwieldy and high risk.

3.2. The Professional Edition offers little improved functionality over V5.
(For Marketing reasons the exciting stuff has been moved up into the
Enterprise Edition.) The main one I would want is Java/COBOL but it is
limited in Fujitsu V6 anyway, and I can write my own interface in V5, using
Java NI...(there are some implications with this when it comes to remote
method invocation, so I will probably just drop COBOL altogether in this
environment and go to Java Servlets. Any wonder people are moving away from
COBOL...?)

4. I am not perturbed by ANY of the Licensing for Fujitsu products, which I
consider to be fair. It is the MECHANICS of implementing this, and the
difficulty of efficient and timely communication with the organization that
bothers me.

5. The lack of Run Time fee is to be applauded and other vendors could take
note.

SUMMARY:

1. For me, Fujitsu COBOL products have run their course. I no longer need
support (in fact, I have provided it to others (also disillusioned with
Fujitsu support) for free) on Version 5. If I go to V6, I will probably need
some support and that is not an attractive prospect. The gains are not worth
the sweat.

2. NetCOBOL is a whole different ball game (and a different division of the
Company) and I await with interest its arrival.

3. I cannot (and will not) recommend the use of Fujitsu COBOL products to
Corporates outside the US, not because there is anything wrong with the
products, but for the reasons outlined above. It is just too high a risk.

Pete.

PS I will not be posting publicly on this subject again, unless the
situation changes and I can be more positive.
