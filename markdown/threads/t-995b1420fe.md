Ahhhh, the ol' pre-engagement discussion... this causes me to dust of T'ai
Ch'i manuals so that I might recall the proper stance for Repulsing the
Hideous Brain-Leech.  I remember an interview...

PHM: 'Well, it looks like you've got the experience for the job, sure...
you came up with the difference between an index and a subscript right
away!  But tell me, just for curiousity's sake... how would you go about
changing a bact-based system to an online one?'

Me: 'Well, the first thing I'd do is look into the overall transaction
volume involved.'

PHM: 'Transaction volume?' (glances down to pad on desk, scribbles a note)
'That's an interesting approach; why do you think that's important?'

Me: 'Because the design of the resulting system is predicated by the
quantity of transactions which are expected to go through it... with an
appropriate percentage for expansion, of course; one always designs for
growth.'

PHM: (more scribbling) '... growth... alright, alright, and once you've
found that out where would you go next?'

Me: 'Well, I would canvass the user-base in order to determine which of
the processes would need modification to psuedoconversational code, what
could remain as batch and what might be put into a sort of 'psuedobatch'
mode, with transactions accumulated into intermediate files and then
master-files updated based on a command ot a trigger.'

PHM: (scribbling) 'psuedobatch triggers... hmmmmmm... oh, and *then* where
would you go with it?'

It went on like this for a few minutes... and then, miracle of miracles,
he was called out of the office; before he left he slipped the notepad on
which he had been scribbling under another pad.  I stood up, pulled the
pad, tore off the sheet of notes he made and when he came back said that I
*really* had to be on my way.
