trendy Java, VB, Powerbuilder types (or whatever the trend of the day is in
2002/2003) are standing in the unemployment lines beside some of those Cobol
types. Because guess what, kids? ... there will be so much money spent in
Y2k remediation over the next 5 years that IT shops (and let's not forget
gov't) will need a recovery period for their budgets (if they survived) ...
a period when precious little new development will occur (in ANY language,
on ANY platform). The recession of 2000(1999? 2001?) will soon be followed
by the "Great IT Flat Spot". And when the market recovers, guess who is
going to be trained in the latest and greatest ... with a ton of *real
world* experience and a demonstrated ability to work in crisis situations.

Greg, I'll wave to you when I roar past the unemployment office in my Vette!
