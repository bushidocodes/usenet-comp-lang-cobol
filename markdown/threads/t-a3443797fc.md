------ PRESS RELEASE --------


The press has been talking a lot lately about Indian Employment sites on the
Internet.

However, there is a problem-

1. Most sites are extensions of the Employment Agency Business. Where An
employer has to pay
heavily to employ.

OR

2. They take hefty fees from the employee (as much as 500 Rupees per resume!)

OR

3. They lack the technology and capability to hold huge quantities of
resumes. For which a
Database backend is required.

Here's a new site that is different. http://www.recruitmentindia.com

1. It has a Database backend and therefore can handle huge volumes.
2. It has an easy to use ActiveX Tree-control to enable easier drill-down
3. It has Index Server based Search which enables you to search the entire
database for text
from within individual resume!
4. It offers FREE advertisements for employers. (Only for advertisements
received over the Web)
5. It offers FREE resume listing for employees. (Only for resumes received
over the Web)
6. It offers FREE searching for the entire database.

For advertisements and resumes not received over the Web, there is a nominal
handling charge
of Rs.100/-

Now that is one hell of an employment site!!

So where's the catch??

1. If you do not have Internet Explorer 3.01 or above, you can't use it
effectively.
2. Aesthetics could be better. It is too stark and functional.

Visit it, recommend it, write about it, after all --- ITS FREE!!!!!!!!
Being free, nobody gets cheated OR feels cheated!

The site is new and does not have any content.

To see what the drill down would look like, you could try the following

1. Choose "Top Management / Administration" on the Tree Control.
2. Choose "Post Graduate / Management / Administration" on the sub-tree that
comes up.
3. Submit the form that comes up by keeping all the other boxes at "Not
Relevant"
4. You will get two matches. Follow them!

To see what the Index Server search would look like, try this-

1. Choose "advanced search"
2. Type in the word "Army"
3. Submit and follow the matches!

To see what the FREE Employer advertisement will look like, Try this-

1. Choose "Jobs Available" from the home Page
2. Choose "Information Technology" on the Tree Control
3. Follow the Links !

Thanks for your time.

My $0.25 (US) worth...

Being an American both by birth and temperament I can understand (I think)
the problem Goobers has with the concept of an exclusive site for a
particular national group advertising on an international, open NG. Being
an employed person both by necessity and temperament I can understand the
concept of limited resources, and the compromises which have to be made in
response to them. And, being a tech weenie by fate and temperament, I
think a technical solution is available here.

It should not take much extra expense to place, somewhere on your pages, an
explanation of your policy, and the resource limitations which engender it
(your current "About Us" page could use a little more clarity on that
issue).

Next, place a page for links to other sites, serving other constituencies,
so that those who come to your site and do not qualify for your services
can easily continue with their search (thereby feeling good about you,
rather than feeling that you wasted their time).

Lastly, add a page detailing how to contact you to have another site added
to the above mentioned links page. You should include what your
requirements would be to be a valid link (policies, availability, etc.).
It would, of course, be reasonable for you to expect a corresponding return
link. You might also want to specify how to contact your management to set
up a likely mutually beneficial exchange of qualifying postings (some
people with jobs in which your candidates might be interested might *not*
post directly to your site, for example).

Adding this small amount of content should not strain your budget too much,
will improve the level of service to your targeted constituency, and will
legitimately blunt some of the objections others will have to your site's
narrow focus.

As a further suggestion, if you really want to invest some time (but not
money), you may want to make some information on your site available to
others who would want to set up similar sites for their own countries, or
other targeted constituencies (maybe even offer your code...). This runs
the risk of assisting other employers and job seekers in competition with
your constituency, but also affords the opportunity to build a truly useful
world-wide employment exchange. Such an action would definitely help put
to rest a skeptic's legitimate fears that you do not intend to offer a free
service, but are simply trying to prime your database before you start
charging for access (like a number of others before you).

As a technical solution I think this can be easily implemented. Isn't that
what all we COBOL wonks like to find, a simple and elegant solution which
meets the clients' needs?
