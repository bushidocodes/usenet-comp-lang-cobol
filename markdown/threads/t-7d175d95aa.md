Urgent Virus Warning -- Read and Forward Immediately

A new email virus has been unleashed, a new type of virus for 
which there is no existing protection.  But the true horror is 
this virus knows no bounds.  Previous email viruses attacked 
the vulnerabilities of one email program (such as Microsoft 
Outlook) or one OS (such as Macintosh) or web based "html" 
email interfaces (such as Hot Mail).  This new virus is 
platform independent -- users of Linux, UNIX, Telnet, 
mainframe email systems, and completely custom email packages 
are equally at risk.  Some have speculated that fax machines 
and conventional mail may also be vulnerable.

Once any virus has been spotted, it is usually easy to add a 
new virus signature to virus scan programs.  Unfortunately, 
"second class" hackers then modify the existing virus to slip 
by the virus scans.  In the case of this new virus, though the 
technology is novel, it's easily grasped by any and all hackers.  
Millions of malicious hackers possess the knowledge and means 
to modify this new virus in ways that will evade existing 
virus scans.

The new email virus is called the Read and Forward Virus 
(R&F), because of its method of propagation, and also because 
of its subject line:  "Fwd: Re: Urgent Virus Warning -- Read 
and Forward Immediately".  (Note that hackers may have 
modified the subject line before the virus reaches you.)

The virus spreads by two principle means:

1)  The R&F virus convinces the reader that the email is in 
fact a warning about another virus.  The user then unwittingly 
forwards the R&F virus to all the users they share bug and 
virus warnings with.  Or,

2)  The R&F virus convinces the reader that it is a joke.  The 
user then unwittingly forwards the R&F virus to all the users 
they share jokes with.

Believe me, it is vitally important that we distribute this 
announcement as quickly as possible.  Please forward this email 
rapidly to every email address that you know. Post the warning
to every newsgroup and chat board you know of.  If unsure 
if someone's email address is correct, send it by fax, or send 
copies by snail mail.  If you receive this message from a 
user who sent a hard copy, scan it into your computer and 
send it by email to everyone you know.  If you received this 
at work, forward this to your home email, and vice-versa.  If
users send you additional copies of this warning, that only 
shows how important this warning is.  You should forward this 
again every time you receive it.  

The one limit on the R&F virus is that the reader has to know 
English, but there are early reports that hackers have 
translated R&F into other languages.  Therefore, if you are 
fluent in any language besides English, please translate this 
warning into every language you know, and distribute it as 
soon as possible.

The only thing we can do about R&F is distribute this warning.
