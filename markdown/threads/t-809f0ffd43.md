trailing spaces, the spaces WILL be dropped with a carriage
return and line feed at the end of the data.

CBL_WRITE_FILE, etc. you can accomplish what you want.

character record with x'30303020202020202020', the record being

LINE SEQUENTIAL files always strip trailing spaces from
the record and insert a "line end" (which is a CR/LF on
OS/2, Windows and DOS, on UNIX systems it is just a LF)

If you want and need 90 byte records use RECORD SEQUENTIAL
not LINE SEQUENTIAL. Note that RECORD SEQUENTIAL will
not insert a "line end". To get a "line End" define the record length
as 92 and place 0x13 in 91 and 0x14 in 92 (I think those are the
correct hex codes for CR and LF - but you had best look them up
to be sure)
