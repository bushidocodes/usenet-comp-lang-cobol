Because the Charter of my Project was Y2K remediation; it was my
assigned job to make sure that the code functioned the same way *after*
31 Dec 1999 as it does now.  The newbie, in fact, *violated* said
Charter when he made the mod.  Truth be known I didn't even notice the
original error as it was part of a statement of equality (IF WS-YRMO =);
we had been instruced to leave such statements alone.

Now you may indeed ask 'which is better, to do what you have contracted
for and been specifically instructed to do or to do That Which Is
Right?'... and lo, to dwell upon such matters at great length is indeed
praisworthy.  Doing Y2K remediation is allotted x amount of time; doing
Y2K remediation *and* fixing broken programs is x + y amount of time. 
One can say 'But it was only one little line in one little program'...
but every program contains many lines; to start fixing things then
brings in what is called by some 'scope creep'... and alowing the scope
of a project to increase is a surefire way to miss a deadline... in this
case the deadline is 31 Dec 1999.
