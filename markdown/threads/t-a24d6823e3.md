```
Hi John,

I guess what you're asking is how do I wind  up with 1 line without the CR
bytes.  Assuming you can just ignore the CRs once you have a single record set
up, you can do the following:

In Working Storage  

01  2-PART-REC.
     05  PART-1.
          10  TEXT-1        PIC X(079).
          10  CR-1           PIC X(001).
     05  PART-2.
          10  TEXT-2        PIC X(079).
          10  CR-2           PIC X(001).

In the PROCEDURE DIVISION

        READ IP-FILE INTO PART-1
        READ IP-FILE INTO PART-2

If the CRs REALLY annoy you you can:

In Working Storage  

01  2-PART-REC.
     05  PART-1.
          10  TEXT-1        PIC X(079).
     05  PART-2.
          10  TEXT-2        PIC X(079).

In the PROCEDURE DIVISION

        READ IP-FILE INTO PART-1
        READ IP-FILE INTO PART-2 

In the 2nd example the CRs are truncated by the generated move into the 79 byte
PART-x data fields.

HTH, Jack.
```

```
Usually with header and detail records, there is a field that we can use to
determine which type of record we are reading.  If you don't have that, you
have to assume that every time one record will be followed by the next and
you won't ever have skips.

Can you do this:

01    MY-RECORD.
        05    MY-RECORD-TYPE             PIC X(05).
        88    MY-HEADER                                           VALUE
       'HEAD'.
        05    FILLER                                PIC X(75).

Or possibly
   IF MY-FIELD NUMERIC
           MOVE MY-RECORD          TO MY-DETAIL
   ELSE
            MOVE MY-RECORD          TO MY-HEAD
   END-IF.
```
