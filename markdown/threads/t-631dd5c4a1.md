Hi 

I am also currently doing a course using the book you are using.  However,
I am doing it as part of a home study course which  I bought on the
internet.  The course is offered by King Computer Associates in Tujunga,
California.  This company belongs to Mo Budlong, the author of Teach
Yourself Cobol in 21 Days.  Along with this book, the course gives you the
MF Personal Compiler, a course guideline, a COBOL cookbook, printer spacing
sheets, etc (basically all you need to get through the book).  I believe
you should be able to get the MF compiler directly from King Computer.  I
think it costs about $100.  I have found it to be an excellent learning
tool.

I am also, concurrently, reading "COBOL for Dummies" (which has some useful
stuff on Y2K) and using a disk based learning tool called "Mastering COBOL"
which is offered by John Wiley and Sons (also very useful).

work on the Y2K problem (hence the need for COBOL).  I am running on gut
instinct here as I have no jobs lined up, or even ideas bout where to look
for them.....yet.  But I think the demand will be there and the money good.
 I look forward to keeping in touch with you, regarding how the course is
going, and job prospects.

BTW, I have found a couple of minor problems with the MF Personal compiler,
and maybe someone reading this can help me out.  After compiling, the
"checker" is supposed to list the syntax errors found.  However, mine
immediately defaults back to the editor screen, so I can't get an overall
list of the errors.  Any ideas about what is going on?

Thanks,

Rory Moore.
