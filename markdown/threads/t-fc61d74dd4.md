This is a small report on several 1997 ANSI NCITS J4 COBOL Committee
meetings. It has been some time since I reported on these meetings,
so I will give you a summary of what's happening.

Membership stands at 10. Most of the meetings in 1997 were concerned
with addressing the 2000 or so comments we received on the first
public review. We had to address each one and either agree or
disagree. For those where we agreed, we had to make a change via a
change proposal. As a result, we have processed several hundred
change proposals. The vast majority of the changes are for
clarification or of an editorial nature. The major changes we made
were to the OO facility. We attempted to simplify it by taking out
some of the more complex features and streamlining it somewhat. We
also removed some other complicated features, but not very much.
Basically, the standard is frozen as far as features go. All our work
now is to tighten it up. We hope to have a version for internal
review after the next meeting, but I would bet that it will take us
two more meetings to get it to that point. There are still about 50
or so outstanding proposals (about a dozen or more are mine). The
standard is about 1000 pages, and there is a great deal of information
in those pages.

The next NCITS J4 meeting will be in, Las Vegas, Nevada, USA, on 22-27
February, 1998. The main topic will be on the revisions needed for
satisfying the comments on the working draft review.

The web page for J4 is http://www.microfocus.com/Standards/. There is
a lot of useful information there as well as pointers to minutes and
descriptions of the major features in the new standard.
