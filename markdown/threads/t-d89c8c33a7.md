Hate to burst your bubble, but I don't think you'll get anything like
this unless;

- the client knows you (very well) and believes you're worth it.
- you have such an obscure skill (other than COBOL), that the client
has no choice;  accept your rate, or miss the deadline.

I'd love to have that arrangement, but don't.  As a contractor, you
need to ask for those things up front.  And if the answer is no, ask
again (in a very diplomatic tone) that it be allowed after a
'probationary period,' where the client will be able to more
accurately judge your contribution against what he/she's paid for you.
I'd imagine that if it was granted, you'd take a cut in rate.  But
that's worth the money, all around savings.  

But we all know, money isn't everything.
