```
Has anyone else noticed that the Cobol compiler manufacturers
have ignored the small developer? MicroFocus adds royalty agreements to
every tool that they sell that comes close to producing a modern
interface. If you wish to have a GUI application you by their expensive
tools and then are told that you have to license any of the runtimes
necessary to make your program have a modern interface. I am disabled
and work out of my home. I cannot afford to pay royalties on the
programs I distribute. I started programming in Cobol for the benefits
of a higher level language than C. Now I am forced to do mixed language
programming to achieve a modern look. If Microfocus only wants corporate
customers I wish they would clearly state this. After paying for an
expensive compiler and considering the support costs ( I always thought
900 numbers were for phone sex) I am beginning to rethink my position on
Cobol for microcomputers. I think it would have great potential for a
development language on the micros if only the compiler manufacturers
would wake up. If any third party vendors manufacture a GUI library for
Cobol without royalty fees I would be happy to purchase your product. I
think MicroFocus has sold more C++ and Visual Basic Compilers than their
own compiler as a result of their rigid corporate policies. Is there a
manufacturer out there who wants the small developer on the micros? It
might be appropriate for MicroFocus to change their name to CorporateFocus.

                                             pro··.@net··m.com
```
