Being National is not necessarily a good thing. Here's some things I've
seen. Of course, your experiences may vary. I am not familiar wth Ciber,
so this is no indication of what they might be like.

1. There is a heirarchy of yacht-owners that are looking for their
earnings to come from the efforts of you, the grunt. The national CEO, 4
Executive VP's in the National Office, the Director of the Regional
Office, the Manager of the local branch and the staff in each of them
depend on your keystrokes. Thus your cut of the billing rate is chopped
to the bone. And their billing rates must be higher than the local
firms, just to support those marine diesel gas bills.

2. Often the only one who specifically cares about your job is the
guy/gal who got you into the position with the client (and only because
he loses his commission if you leave). The rest view you as a
money-creating humanoid, interchangeable with whatever next person they
can recruit. Especially if you are so obstinate as to expect a
meaningful raise after years of work for them.

3. In a never-ending quest to get bigger yachts, directives are sent
from National Hqtrs that require more and more squeezing of the locals.
The turnover at the local level (of office people) is incredible. They
mostly work on incentives, and the more they make, the more the National
Office wants to juice from them. When they quit, this drops down onto
you, especially if you were counting on the locals' 'connections' with
the clients in the area.

4. Any disputes are sent up to the Regional or National office. The
general response from them is 'Our Lawyer will talk to yours (and not to
you, you scumbag).

5. The client (some remote unknown entity on an account ledger, as seen
to the CEO) is viewed as an open pocketbook to be exploited. You may get
caught in the backlash of sub-ethical stab at placing more people,
especially unqualified ones.

On the other hand, locally-owned firms may be comprised of people who
were once programmers in the local scene. They know the clients' bosses,
do care about their own reputation, can't afford to screw the client,
and other nice things. Not saying they're all angels, but there is a
chance that they might be a better company to work for.

John (all of whose opinions are from his own twisted mind and do not
represent any other entity.)
