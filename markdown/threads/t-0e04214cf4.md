I can't tell you about "Teach Yourself Cobol in 21 days", but I have bought
and read through "Cobol for Dummies". While the book does tend to explain
things in a clear and entertaining manner, when you get into the real coding
world, be prepared to do a much better job naming, indenting, aligning, and
otherwise making your code look neat and readable, because no such effort is
made in this book. And like the majority of all programming books (but not
all of them), this one contains no examples of good code documentation. Oh
well.
If you are on a PC, or especially Unix, Cobol environment, the software on
the CD-ROM is okay. But there's nothing here for MVS, the predominant Cobol
environment.

If you are on an MVS environment, then Structured ANS COBOL Part 1: An
Introductory Course and Structured ANS COBOL Part 2: An Advanced Course are
pretty good in all except a tiny handful of examples and explanations (there
are one or two places where the explanations do more to confuse that help,
particularly about 88 level data items).

COBOL for Dummies costs about $30 bucks. Structured ANS COBOL Part1 costs
$36.50.

For the extra seven or so dollars, you get infinitely better code
arrangement in the examples, along with a complete explanation of how to
work in the MVS environment itself (concepts, editor, compiling, etc.).

After these there are a large number of books available, though a scan
through one of the online book stores show that many are out of print.


Chris
