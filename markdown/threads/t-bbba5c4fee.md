```
This will makes sense to folks in the USA, perhaps not so to those in 
other lands; it has to do with matters of taxation and legal status 
unique to this country.

I've recently started a new contract that's paying me corp-to-corp on a 
1099.  Ever since I had a 1099 audit in 1990 I've made sure to be a W2 
employee of the agency but this time I can't.

My question is... years ago I recall seeing advertisements for places that 
served as intermediaries; they'd receive your check and - after taking a 
small cut - write you a check and make sure the appropriate authorities 
received their payments as well.  Such places became popular after the 
passing of Senate Resolution 1706 which changed the status of many 
computer programming consultants; as such they were called '1706 Houses'.

Does anyone know if such places still exist?  If so would such a person be 
willing to tell me where I might look in order to find one convenient to 
the Washington, DC area?

Thanks much.

DD
```

```
Congratulations on finding an assignment this challenging environment.

If this is a long term assignment, you may want to consider creating a LLC or 
Corp, paying yourself, paying the requisite taxes, taking legal business 
deductions, rather than paying someone to act as a middleman with *your money*. 
Quicken Payroll does most of the payroll grunt work for me at a reasonable 
price, $129 per year. (I'm not affiliated with Quicken)

I surely remember the horror show caused HR1706, but I've not heard much about 
it lately, although it is surely still the "law of the land" here in the USA. 
BTW I remember it excluded: Lawyers, Real Estate Agents and Writers. I feel the 
bill sponsored by Sen. Moynihan (Deceased), was designed to reduce consulting 
rates charged by IT professionals and engineers who were the primary target, 
mostly replaced now by compliant and more lowly paid H-1Bs.
```
