I have some (I kept them for historical reasons - basically, I am a 
pack rat).  However, these forms were designed for programmers to 
code on and then turn into keypunch to get punched.  I would think 
that nobody uses keypunches today.  Even without tools, I assume that 
they enter the programs on a terminal or PC of some sort.  The forms 
are of little use in this situation.  It is easier to write out the 
program on paper and then type it in if you are going to use paper.  
Writing in the little boxes on forms is lots harder.

I even have report layout sheets.  They might be of marginal use.

If you want any of this, let me know (along with your address) and I 
can send a couple of each.  You would have to photocopy them.
