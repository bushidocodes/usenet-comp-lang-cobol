Since an OO system requires the application of engineering that is
absent/very different in the procedural world, IMO the best you can do
is capture some high-level business rules out of the old COBOL, to "aid"
in an OO re-engineering effort. An OO design effort would still have to
occur. A direct mapping, via an automated tool, from old COBOL to OO
COBOL would provide no benefits (with the possible exception of being
able to stamp 'OO' on the marketing doco). There are tools that can aid
in this effort, such as those from Viasoft and Microfocus (Revolve). At
least they claim they can do it - I have not used them. Visit the WEB
sites....
