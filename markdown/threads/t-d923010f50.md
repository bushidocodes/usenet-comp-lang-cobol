Why is this awkward? Even if C is really needed, COBOL and C work
really well together, provided you have an implementation that
facilitates this.

In the UK, COBOL was used by one manufacturer for the hardware
controlling the laser barcode scanner used at supermarket checkouts.
Proved to be a far sighted decision, as when they later upgraded to a
different processor the code was portable, all that was needed was a new
runtime. Things wouldn't have been so easy and quick had the code been
C I suspect. The other advantage is the old one of maintenance, the
COBOL style generally makes it far easier to pick up someone elses work
compared to C.

Huh? COBOL has never been that popular on 68K systems. There was Mac COBOL
some years ago and various implentations on 68K UNIX boxes some time ago
before almost everyone went RISC. The Mac was never really a programmers
machine and it had to contend with IBM mainframe sites where if it wasn't
IBM, it wasn't bought. Other computers such as the Atari and Amiga didn't sell
enough to the right people to warrant a compiler. The idea of an Amiga COBOL
compiler might appeal to me but not to that many other people. AFAIK there has
never been a 68060 specific implentation of COBOL. Or for that matter 040
specific. Mac COBOL was 68K, pre-32bit clean era.

The 68K line of chips isn't extinct either. Apple may have dropped it but
other manufacturers are still using it and if Motorola can solve their
manufacturing problems with the chip, we may see a minor resurgence. They are
still quite popular in the imbedded controller world and at least for the next
6 months to a year before the Amiga goes PowerPC, the 68060 will be the only
option for power hungry Amiga owners on a budget (unless someone gives me a
Raptor+ Alpha station ;-) )
