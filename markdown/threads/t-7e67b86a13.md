```
I had some mail yesterday from someone who went to download some of our
COBOL software and was told by his Browser (Opera):

"Malicious site warning!"

It went on to advise him against visiting the page. (He ignored it and
is still alive with his system perfectly intact...)

Apparently there is a Russian service called Yandex that provides lists
of sites it considers "dangerous"...

Other people have reported the same thing from Chrome, and reports
discouraging use of the site from McAfee.

I have never seen it myself even though I do use Chrome occasionally for
testing stuff. (My usual browser has become Edge and it serves me well.)

For software developers this can be a real heartbreak.

PRIMA spends money to be certified on the Web site and for our code.
But, much more importantly, I feel a personal responsibility for the
safety and integrity of people visiting our site or downloading our
software.

We have NEVER released malware and neither would we ever do so.

And yet, we can be on a "blacklist" and never even know we are.

How does it happen?

Someone being spiteful, someone thinking its funny, I have no idea.

The processes to get yourself removed can be very convoluted and not
always successful.

For example, if you are not a registered McAfee user (and I'm not) they
don't pay much attention.

BOTTOM LINE:

Please, when you are confronted with messages like this from your
browser, stop and think. Is it a site or company you know nothing about
and have never dealt with? Fine, proceed with caution and maybe run some
searches before going there.

But if it is a company or site you are visiting on purpose and you "know
where they live" :-) then use your own judgement.

Cheers,

Pete.
```
