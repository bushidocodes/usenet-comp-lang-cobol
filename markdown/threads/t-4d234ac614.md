```
As a follow-on to a separate (degenerated) thread, I thought that I would
point out to those who are interested, IBM's own "performance analysis" of
various data types and compiler options for their Enterprise COBOL V3R1
compiler.  You can get the full performance paper from:


http://www-1.ibm.com/support/docview.wss?rs=203&q=7001475&uid=swg27001475&lo
c=en_US&cs=utf-8?=en

and within it look for the section:

   "Comparing Data Types"

Please note that my FAVORITE quote from this entire paper is on page 33
where it states

"The biggest performance improvement you can make is to use an  efficient
design. After the design is determined, then you can look at other things
such as efficient algorithms, data structures and data types, and coding
style."

Although this appears in an IBM mainframe targeted document, as many
participants within CLC have often stated, this is certainly a BASIC truth
that beginning programmers need to learn - LONG before they get into
"micro-tuning".
```
