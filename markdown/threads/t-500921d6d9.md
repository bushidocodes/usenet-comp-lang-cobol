No it is not.  But the discussion has been the fine distinction in the
context of a MOVE statement.  In this context, editing only occurs on
elementary moves, and a group source or destination operand changes the
rules.

We are now swerving into different territory.  I would conjecture that most
compilers do not enforce the rule quoted by Mr. Bart, since the programmer
can use REDEFINES to achieve the result, only with more aggravation and
obfuscation.  [RM/COBOL does not, even though it appears in our language
reference manual.]

Related to this, but not directly addressing the issue, is the fact that the
class of a group item is treated at object time as alphanumeric regardless
of the class of the elementary items subordinate to that group item.

Also, the USAGE DISPLAY clause on a group item applies to the elementary
items subordinate to that group item, not to the group item itself.
