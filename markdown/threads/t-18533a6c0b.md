Maybe these two points will help a little.

1. COBOL allows multiple record definitions (01's) under an FD for this
purpose, among others. Multiple record definitions under an FD implicitly
REDEFINE the same record area. Note that you can REDEFINE an 01 with another
01, even outside the FILE SECTION, and they don't have to be the same size.

2. The reason you 'READ a File' and 'WRITE a Record' in COBOL is because you
don't generally know which record format you are going to get from the read
until after it is read, but when you WRITE you need to specify which record
you are writing, because they can be of different lengths.
