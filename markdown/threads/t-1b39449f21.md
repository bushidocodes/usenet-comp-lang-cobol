P. Raulerson wrote:
> ...
> But as far as I know, nobody has a HP3000 emulator right now, and that
> wonderful MPE is now in the same position as WangOS.

Not quite.  The Wang VS OS is alive and well, running seamlessly in
Linux on Dell PowerEdge and IBM xSeries.  IBM OpenPOWER was trialed but
found to be a poor performer in this context.

Deployment of the New VS, now officially the VS22000 series, began in
December, 2005 in Europe, followed immediately by the first U.S.
installs.  The product is now established, with multiple sites in the
U.S., the U.K. and continental Europe.  The largest site regularly runs
over 500 concurrent user sessions on one system and employs virtual VS
clustering (RSF) for coupling with another  New VS system.

Since the New VS provides 100% seamless compatibility with the legacy
VS line there is zero conversion.  The New VS runs the VS OS and all VS
software unchanged, and works with unmodified VS volume images.  VS
volumes are copied into the New VS or moved by any of the available VS
backup/restore tools.

Most VS SCSI devices still in use are directly supported, including VS
4mm, VS DLT, VS 18-track and VS 9-track.  In addtion, many more SCSI
devices are available than were ever supported by the legacy VS,
including DDS-4, DAT72, and all variants of DLT and SDLT.  LTO could be
supported but there is no call for it as no existing legacy VS has LTO
tape.  Most VS SCSI disks are supported but useful only for high-speed
volume transfer, since the New VS always has internal RAID and much
higher storage performance than any VS disks or RAID.

Lightspeed NVS connectivity, which became the standard for the Wang VS
in the 1990s, is still available but no longer requires external PC
gateways.  The LS NVS gateway has been integrated into the New VS and
clients connect directly and seamlessly by TCP/IP to the New VS instead
of to gateway PCs.

Oracle connectivity from VS programs is now a reality, too.  In 2007
it's likely that ODBC and other forms of interoperability with non-VS
systems will be implemented.

Thomas Junker
<a href="http://www.tjunker.com/">The Unofficial Wang VS Information
Center</a>
<a href="http://www.transvirtualsystems.com/">TransVirtual Systems</a>
