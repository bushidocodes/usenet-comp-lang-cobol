Checking body parts - nope - not male - grumpy maybe (gotta check with the resident teenager) - but not male.

DD gave a very good answer.  I too would be glad to point someone in the right direction if some indication of effort to solve the problem was exhibited in the asking of the question.  

To directly ask 'How do I......' implies that research wasn't done.  To say 'I've done A, B, C and D and it just doesn't seem to come together' implies that an effort was put forth and another pair of eyes would help.

Many of the questions asked could easily be answered by looking in the table of contents or index of the text book being used as they are basic coding structures that should be covered in your average COBOL 101 class.

Most of us when we were learning our trade didn't have newsgroups to ask questions of, some here may have even learned by the seat of their pants because they didn't have the benifit of formal instruction and were in the right place at the right time.  We learned by trial and error and finally either turned in the assignment wrong or got direction from the instructor while doing the lab work (for those of us who had computer labs to work in) or from others who have completed the assignment or upper classmen who have completed the course.  

I don't do my kids' homework for them.  I get their textbook, look thru it to find the reference to what they are trying to do, go over it with them, walk them thru a problem or 2, and then they are on their own.  They don't dare to ask until they have rattled it around in their brains first because my  reponse to a question is  1) Did you look it up?  2) Have you tried it yet? 3) What was your result?  And they better answer 1) Yes, 2) Yes, and 3) This (show me an example)  Why should I do any different with the students here?

Da (maybe) grumpy (Old??!!) woman.
