```
Adm Hopper was an incredible woman. The data processing world owes much
to her.

She worked for Remington Rand in the 1950s (Univac) and was one of the
people who pushed for high level languages. Back then everything was
programmed in assembly language or even machine language, and every
computer had its own unique language. Programming was slow and error
prone.

Adm Hopper pushed for a standard language, and one result of her effort
was the creation of COBOL, the standard computer language of business.


Unfortunately, published literature of her is limited. Reader's Digest
did a tiny piece on her a few months ago. I would suggest checking the
Reader's Guide to Periodical Literature in the library (that's on CD-ROM
now, making it easy) for references. When she died (a few years ago)
there were several obituaries.
```
