No, I won't 'share' anything with you... I'll tell you what I think,
though!

I *adore* being on-call but nobody will let me do it... that's because I
am an independent who bills by the hour; if someone calls me to ask
*anything* except about my health or the weather I charge for a minimum
of four hours (1936 Fair Wages & Standards Act).

As for it being a part of being an employee... I could see it working to
one' advantage iff *very* close track is kept of how much one is called
upon to do and how well one does it; this is not the case in most shops
I've seen. The classic was a fellow who had to go battle the HR
department because they refused to grant him comp-time for calls he
received while on vacation. ('Whadday mean, you want comp-time... you
were on vacation, comp-time rules don't apply!')

You are, as you describe it, a junior programmer, though... and this
might be a necessary part of 'paying dues'.
