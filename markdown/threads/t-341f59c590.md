The topic recurs frequently. Here are some de facto guidelines:
+++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++
› Is it O.K. to advertise job openings in this newsgroup?

Not really. According to the charter of comp.lang.cobol it is for
the discussion of all aspects of the use of the programming
language COBOL (eg Object-oriented COBOL).

The charter does not mention job ads. But here are some informal
guidelines to avoid irritating too many of us, eg those who happen
to live and work outside the USA:

Mark the Subject line with asterisks and say WHERE the job is
eg Subject: ** USA Ca AS400 Cobol A/P
Keep it brief (20 lines + 4 sig)
NO APPLICATION FORMS or MISSION STATEMENTS
For multiple positions, post 1 ad + pointer to others
Agencies restricted to 1 ad per week.
+++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++
Hope this helps any ethical agencies that _read_ newsgroups :-)

Hi Keith,

I think that your attempt for coming back to this subject is a
pregnant idea even it has been discussed at lenght for quite some
time by a large number of posters. It has always been itching me to
participate to this debate, but I have not done so awaiting the the
ongoing debate cristallize and mature my views. Then, I have been all
in a sudden too busy to take part. Now that the job adds have
practically invaded the this newsgroup these days, the timing is well
chosen for reopening the debate. I will express my views below after
having asked a few questions.

Can anyone please tell me what the charter of this newsgroup says
about the job adds?

Is this really an international newsgroup or is it a one, limited to
the geographical/political borders of the United States?

If the charter of this newsgroup allows the posting of the job adds, I

think we have not much to say on the subject. If it does not do so,
the job adds must be refused in respect of the charter. If the charter
has no rules on this subject, my opinian is that this is not the right
place for the job adds.

If this is not an international newsgroup, but simply a newsgroup,
reserved for the residents of the United States, for us, the non-US
residents, there remains one of the two things to do:

1) Put up with the job adds of the US Companies and hunt for the
interesting and often very high quality articles of the posters, lost
among the job adds. And of course, continue to pay your connect time
to subsidize the US head hunters who would not like to spend money to
do it the right way.

2) Forget about the comp.lang.cobol newsgroup and try to find out if
there exists a similar newsgroup that can be qualified with the
adjective "international".

I think that creating a newsgroup, which might be, for instance, named
comp.lang.cobol.us.jobs would be an excellent solution to the problem
for everybody involved, except for the US head hunters.

The US resident job seekers with COBOL language would find direct
access to the first source of information on the subject without going
into the trouble of sorting out the job ads in the comp.lang.cobol
newgroup postings.

The non-US residents would not have to pay connect time for the ads
of the US head hunters unless they have a peculiar curiosity about the
US job market related to COBOL language, in which case, they could
connect to comp.lang.cobol.us.jobs to satisfy their curiosity.

The only losers would probably be the US head hunters, because they
would probably have to spend some money to finance the oplerating
expenses of a relevant newsgroup, named, comp.lang.cobol.jobs.

But, if it matters to them at all, I would say that this would be an
honest attitude. This would also create an excellent example for the
other countries who may feel a similar need and create similar
newsgroups suchs as:

comp.lang.cobol.jobs.uk (United Kingdom)
comp.lang.cobol.jobs.gr (Germany)
comp.lang.cobol.jobs.fr (France)
etc.

I hope that the views that I have expressed here will create a
feedback.
