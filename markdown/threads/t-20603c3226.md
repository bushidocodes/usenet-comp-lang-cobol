```
(Sent to both IBM-MAIN and comp.lang.cobol) - Please feel free to pass this
on to anyone else who might be interested/have input but doesn't watch
either of these lists.

I am curious (and this is NOT an official - much less "scientific" survey).

Given this week's IBM announcement and what is listed in its "compatibility"
section:

1) How many existing IBM COBOL sites still have any COBOL programs compiled
(and maintained) with the CMPR2 compiler option?  When VS COBOL II V3.0
first came out (MANY years ago) this was medium common - especially to avoid
file-status=39 problems.  I think besides FS=39 issues, there are still some
ODO (Occurs Depending On) issues that some sites MIGHT still be using it
for.  Others may be using it just because "that's the way we have always
done things" - but as it never was supported with VisualAge COBOL and is now
"going away" on the mainframe, I am curious how many sites this will impact.

2) How many existing sites have any "production" (whatever YOU mean by that)
COBOL code running under VM/CMS - *not* under VSE under VM?  When I worked
for a 3rd party software tool vendor, we had about 2 sites worldwide using
COBOL under VM.  I am curious whether the fact that V3R1 won't be available
for VM will actually impact anyone.
```

```
I was contracted by a financial services firm in the New York metropolitan
area at the end of last year to perform the analysis of what it would take
to migrate from VS COBOL II to COBOL for OS/390.

Needless to say, the proposal was all that was ever done on that project.
Management felt that a gradual, rather than forced, migration would work for
their limited budget.

From what I recall, there were about 16,000 batch programs and about 9,000
online programs.  Approximately 75% of them used CMPR2.

I was astonished - but the firm processed this stuff every day and every
night...
```
