function exp(arg)
function exp10(arg)
and its reciproc:
function log(arg)
function log10(arg)
where arg is numeric.

For example, if you have a and b defines as real :

move function exp(a) to b

That's it!

Doc, a number is a number is a number.  Period.  The classics may be
your background, but physics and math are mine.  COBOL is one of the
very few languages that make the artificial distinction between a number
computed on the fly and a numeric variable, in use.  Through years of
experience it has proven more of a stumbling block than any benefit.
Which is why those restrictions are generally being removed in the draft
standard.  You can bet that more time has been wasted by programmers
mistakenly trying to use MOVE with the COBOL 85/89 numeric functions
than has been saved by requiring COMPUTE; by several orders of magnitude
at least, even assuming that requiring the use of COMPUTE has saved
anyone a single bug or minute of time, which I seriously doubt.
"COMPUTE =" even takes longer to type than "MOVE TO". :-)

COBOL has many great features, but requiring COMPUTE to produce the
result from a numeric function isn't one of them.
