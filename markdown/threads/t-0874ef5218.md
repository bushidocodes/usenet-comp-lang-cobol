hi together,

first, i am a (old) german programmer and my english is verry bad, sorry
therefore (not for the old german).
second, my question:
i have a copybook like this:

01 WKCRX-03.
    05 WKCRX-03-AMOUNT         PIC S9(15).
    05 WKCRX-03-SIGN                 PIC S9(01).
    .
    .
i will replace the prefix WKCRX by HKCRX.
I have coded :

COPY WKCRX-BOOK RELACING ==WKCRX== BY ==HKCRX==.

i know that i can not replace parts of not numeric literals. but i believe i
have used this method some years ago. but i am not sure.

my question is general: can i replace a prefix (a part of a field name) with
REPLACING??

thanks for answer

Rolf
```

```
You need to have delimiters, I used colons in the example below.
Make the copybook look like:
01 :WKCRX:-03.
     05 :WKCRX:-03-AMOUNT         PIC S9(15).
     05 :WKCRX:-03-SIGN                 PIC S9(01).

Then say:
COPY WKCRX-BOOK REPLACING ==:WKCRX:==  BY  ==HKCRX==.

Ron
