COBOL. Panels 2, the GUI library, is mostly C. Compiler, Code generator,
Animator, file handler and virtually everything else is COBOL.

The Visual Object COBOL product has a fairly high C++/MFC content but that's
because it's a smallish Windows only product. It's mainly only the front end
that's in C++/MFC. At the time of development, it made sense to use that as a

cross platform and written heavily with COBOL.

another but it mostly seems stupid to use someone elses technology when we can
roll our own. Being at the whim of some other companies compiler bugs is not
fun. At least if it's our compiler bug, we can get a fix fairly rapidly. I
can't remember the last time I found a compiler bug mind!

It's always been the case that our own programmers are one of the best tests
of our products. If we won't use them, the customer generally won't either. We
do however listen a lot to Mainframe programmers and they have quite a
different background than a fair amount of the programmers at MF who've never
seen a mainframe compiler in their lives. What's easy for someone with a PC
background is sometimes not obvious for a mainframe programmer and vice versa.
GUI interfaces aren't that common on Mainframes ;-)

It's also why I hang around here every now and again. Some different
viewpoints are nice to have stored in the back of your head.
