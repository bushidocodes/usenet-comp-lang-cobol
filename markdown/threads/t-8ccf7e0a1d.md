If you use a disk image backup program, such as Power Quest's Drive
Copy or Drive Image, or Norton Ghost, you can restore your entire
boot partition and/or any other partitions to their identical backed up
state. Even finicky 'copy protected' software can be backed up and
restored this way.

> It's a pain
> setting up all the applications again, though - it takes time to get
> them tweaked again the way I had them.

It takes me several days to set up a new PC the way I want it. Several
years ago, after a system crash had me down for nearly a week, I made
the decision to, from then on, buy my primary development PCs in
identical pairs. Everything is identical except the serial numbers. It's
more expensive, but you can buy what was state of the art technology
three or six months ago for much less. It's also handy having an extra
PC around to burn CDs and stuff while you're working on the other
machine.

I keep all files, including email, updated on both PCs and notebook at
least once a day. Using LapLink's 'clone folder' function over a LAN
takes only seconds. When I am writing code, I back up those files
several times a day, usually every hour or so, and before/after major
changes, to Zip disk or CD. My intention is to put master backups in
my safe deposit box every month. I make the master backups, but am
too lazy to take copies to the bank. :-)
