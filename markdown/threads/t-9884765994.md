Richard is absolutely correct.

As a follow-up, the current ANSI/ISO Standard *assumes* that the entire
world is a single run-unit.  It really has NO rules about what happens in an
environment where multiple run-untis may co-exist (i.e. - depending on OS -
multi-threading, multi-tasking, whatever).

Many COBOL implemenations that DO work in this (now common) environment, do
provide rules (and/or extensions) to handle this, but such "rules" are NOT
necessarily portable.

CAVEAT coder!!!

The draft Standard *begins* to discuss this, but certainly not in depth. In
fact, in its definition of its scope, it explicitly states,

"This International Standard does not specify: ...
    - The sharing of resources other than files among run units."
