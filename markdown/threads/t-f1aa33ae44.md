```
From my "usually reliable sources" - the following may be of interest

1) The "official" announcement on the status of Visual COBOL is available on
the web at:
    http://www.merant.com/products/microfocus/vcob.asp

2) That page includes both an upgrade path and a phone number for "additional
questions"

3) MERANT (well then it WAS Micro Focus) did NOT purchase this product for
its name, rather,

 "It was purchased to have an offload solution, in Europe, for the Siemens
Nixdorf mainframe market, as that is the only non-ANSI COBOL dialect that the
Visual COBOL product line supports.  The current product that we sell in
Europe for that task is the BS 2000 version of the Visual COBOL product.
There is no market in North America for this type of product, so the BS 2000
product  was never introduced here.  The Visual COBOL product was never a
significant product in the North American market, neither when Mbp marketed
it here nor when EDS owned the product, prior to our purchase of it."

4) Concerning support of the product,

"In the past 4 years that the Visual COBOL support staff has reported <to a
US MERANT manager>, there has only been one complaint and that was due to the
customer trying to call the wrong phone number to get to support.   As with
all of our support, we take our customer's satisfaction very seriously. Our
satisfaction surveys (both by our external surveying company and through our
surveys of each customer on each closed Case) consistently rate our service
at 95+% overall (as to satisfying their needs).  We couldn't be receiving
those satisfaction levels if we  only "support one compiler adequately...".
The fact is we support over 40 individual products (not counting the
DataDirect and PVCS products), which encompasses at least 6 different
compilers and a lot of other tools, few of which would be considered simple
products."
```
