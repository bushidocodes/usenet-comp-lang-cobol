```
ok, so we have this program which produces (according to the jcl) 12
outputs, 7 'file' and 5 'direct to printer'. only in the program, 3 of
the 5 'output' are not declared at all. and in the jcl, 3 of the 7
'files' are never catalogued, and are deleted when the job is complete.

so i'm asked to do maintenance on this beast. to wit, take the paper
copy which the customer gets and append it to the primary 'file' which
then altogether is wanting to be sent electronically to place x.

so, silly me, i look at the jcl and assume (we know what _that_ means)
that the outputs are printing because they are the sysouts, not the
files. this assumption is based upon the fact that this is the last step
in the job and there are no successors and the customer does not
intervene in any to get the output. But......
(see paragraph one, sentences two and three) since we _know_ the
customer is getting 6 different reports in a nice connected bundle,
let's do the math:

12 output altogether - 3 sysout not declared in program = 9 remaining -
3 non-catalogued,deleted in jcl = 6.

yeah!!

so i run a test. change all the sysouts and files to catalogued
datasets.

of the six 'remaining' files, three are empty. where the %@?* are these
reports coming from?????

well, strange you should ask......

the 3 noncat,del in jcl are being opened as output in the cobol, written
to (in complete report format, headers, totals, the whole enchilada),
closed, opened as input, and then written out to one of the three
remaining 'outputs' at the end.

the only justification we can _even remotely_ think of for this is that
the customers wanted the reports, of which they want 3 copies on
continuous feed paper, in order (a,b,c,d)x3,(e)x3,f instead of
a,a,a,b,b,b,c (you folks surely get the gist of this by now). and the
person (unknown at this point) who was assigned this !!!!!

argh!!!!!!!!!!

please, please, all you folks out there who are learning/beginning in
cobol, please learn your other tools as well. cobol is wonderful (it
and i are still in our honeymoon--only 4 years now), but there can be
more logical, easier to maintain ways of doing things than making a
cobol program perform jobs that are really beneath it, like
concatenating files!!!!!

thanks for listening, got some cobol to chop........
suzie
```

```
I once dit such things to speed up: input file contains records not in
sequence of key. load a new, empty, indexed file with them. access mode is
random has dramatic low performance. Only 1% of the input is out of
sequence. rewritten the program wit access sequential on de indexed file,
self-check on key-seq-mismatch. when out of order records are found, those
put apart in a seq file. at end reopened the index file "random" (other FD
pointing to the same file in jcl) and reopened the work file input. little
loop to add the 1% inserting them ramdomly. performance went up like a space
rocket.

When you look at the JCL: the same file in the DSN= of two DD's (o dear,
that Goobers might see this). Another DD for a file with
DISP=(NEW,DELETE,DELETE). Operators were stunned. Could almost not resist to
change it. Had to explain a lot.
```
