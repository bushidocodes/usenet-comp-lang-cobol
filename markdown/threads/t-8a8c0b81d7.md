During the last few weeks I have been wondering to myself what
is going on with Visual Object COBOL for Windows. I have been
thinking that it would be, I think, a good idea for Microfocus
to hook up with some publishing company; and together the two of
them (Microfocus and the publishing company) could find somebody
to write a good book on this product (Visual Object COBOL) that
Microfocus has already, no doubt, spent a lot of money on creating.

It has already been mentioned in this newsgroup that the product
doesn't come with any sort of true "manual", so basically it
seems that COBOL developers that want to work with the product
are being bluntly told to go "figure it out for yourself".

For the publishing company it would seem to be a profitable
idea too. For something like VisualBasic, or Visual C++, or
Java, any book they publish will have to compete with two or three
other dozen (at least) books in the bookstore... but here with
Visual Object COBOL they would be the lucky people that have
the only good book out on this new development environment. In
other words, they'd have the market cornered.

Maybe I might be wrong about how profitable it would be, but to
me it sounds like a venture worth the risk. Maybe the good folks
at Microfocus could chip in a little cash to lessen the publishing
company's risk ?

Just a few thoughts...
