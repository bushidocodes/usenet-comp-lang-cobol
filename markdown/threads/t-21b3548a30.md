```
I was at a trade show this past week and was visited by a technoid
from a company with whom our product interfaces. The interesting part
of the conversation went like this:

Him: "Java, of course."
Me: "And what makes Java so swell?"
Him: "Obviously 'Write once, run anywhere.'"
Me: "So the ability to move the code from one platform to another is
Java's main strength?"
Him: "You bet!"
Me: "And that characteristic, platform independence, is the necessary
and sufficient condition in determining the worth of a programming
language?"
Him: "That, and no other!"
Me: "Can you tell name another language even more platform independent
than Java?"
Him: "Ah, no, there isn't one!"
Me: "COBOL"
Him: "Hack, hack, spit, gag, hack-hack, yuk, ptooey! You've got to be
nuts! Crazy! What in the hell are you talking about!"
Me: "Well, is Java (for example), like COBOL, an ANSI standard? Does
the U.S. Government publish levels of acceptance testing for Java to
guarantee it meets or exceeds standards irrespective of platform?"
Him: "Hack-hack, twitch, squirm, moan-moan, well, well...."
Me: "So, by your definition, COBOL is at least equal to Java, maybe
even superior?"
Him: "Well, I wouldn't go so far as... What I mean is that COBOL is,
well, so .... (color change here) It's not the same thing as, well
some could say, but I wouldn't exactly... Here! Let me point out that
COBOL won't run on hand-held devices like, er, well COBOL...
hackety-hack, spit-spit, (much head rolling here), mumble-mumble,
(significantly more twitching), moan...(begin fondling of naughty bits
about now), phlegm, ..."

I never take pleasure in the discomfort of others: This was the
exception.
```
