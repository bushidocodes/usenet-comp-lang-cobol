 > So I put 10% on ML (under the probably incorrect assumption that ML

I haven't bothered looking at the survey, but I'd guess ML refers to the
language Standard ML, or more generally to the whole ML family.  The
"ML" stands for "Meta-Language".  It's a functional language that's
popular in some quarters largely because it's amenable to program
proofs, including automatic proving systems.

SML is a bit like LISP, in that it's a functional language (programs are
typically expressed as functions operating on the results of other
functions, as opposed to the one-instruction-after-another style of
"procedural" languages like COBOL).  However, it has strong
type-checking and strict evaluation, and a very un-LISP-like syntax, so
it's not much like LISP after all.  And pretty much entirely unlike
COBOL, or assembly (unless you've written assembly for some very unusual
processors...).

But since the results of this survey will no doubt be useless anyway, no
harm done.
