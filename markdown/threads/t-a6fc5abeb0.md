Hi, I just started reading this group, so please pardon my newbie
questions.

I've just started learning COBOL. I picked up the book "Teach
Yourself COBOL in 21 Days," and then purchased Micro Focus Personal
COBOL for DOS. The book seems ok for a beginning, but it goes nowhere
close to teaching the whole language. It doesn't even seem to cover
any of the ANSI 1985 stuff. (I couldn't believe all the
if/else/if/else statements in there! Luckally I discovered the
"evaluate" statement in the PCobol manual.)

Anyway, even though I managed to find the evaluate statement and
figured out how to use it, the PCobol manual does not really teach you
how to use COBOL. It just shows you what options are available,
assuming you already know the language. So my question is, how can I
learn the rest of the language? Does anyone have any other books to
recommend? The bookstores around here have very few COBOL books, and
all the others I've found already assume you have a lot of COBOL
knowledge. I even tried a college bookstore and couldn't find a
single book.

BTW, I already know Pascal, C and C++ so I don't need a book for
absolute beginners.

Frank
