Hi!

This is the major COBOL Newsgroup on Usenet.(there are a few others but they
don't get the traffic this one does.)

If you are learning COBOL or have just finished training and are looking for
examples, encouragement and help, it is a good place to lurk. (Read the
"rules" in the COBOL FAQ before you ask for help with homework...)

There are highly experienced people here who collectively know just about
everything there is to know about COBOL.

However, there are a few pitfalls also.

The Newsgroup, as well as being a place for exploring technical issues, is
also a place where people who share an interest in COBOL will seek some
light intellectual relief by opening discussions that are way off topic
(however, they are often interesting and amusing). Be prepared to find stuff
that has only a tenuous relationship with COBOL.(These threads are supposed
to be prefixed with "OT" (off topic) but sometimes we forget...<G>)

Like all groups of people, there is a spectrum of personalities here.

Before you start, here is some advice:

1. If you read or see anything here that conflicts with what your tutor is
telling you, show it to him and get his arguments.

2. Do NOT be "bullied" by people here into a certain style of coding. The
ONLY style of coding that is acceptable while you are training, is the one
being taught to you. Once you have finished the course, and written a number
of COBOL programs, make up your own mind about questions of style. There are
MANY ways to write COBOL; they are not "right" or "wrong" (provided the
program does what it is supposed to, consistently) but they ARE a matter of
personal preference.
Many factors will influence your personal "style" as it develops;
(installation standards, methods of formatting code so it is easier to read,
consistent ways to use complex constructs within the language, are just a
few) and your style will change as you become a mature professional.

3. Don't be afraid to contribute and, if you see something that puzzles you,
ask about it.

Finally, this is an unmoderated forum. People can post whatever they like
(even if it exceeds good taste...). If you get "flamed", don't respond. If
you see stuff that upsets you, ignore it. Remember you are NOT required to
respond to anything posted here (although sometimes it is very hard not
to...<G>)

Above all, think for yourself and make your own decisions about what "works"
for you.

Enjoy!

Pete.
