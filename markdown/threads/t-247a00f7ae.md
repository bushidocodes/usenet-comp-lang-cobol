I assume you already familiar with the OS and have the real working 
experience on COBOL. You also know what is a DBMS and how's it works.

For batch, it will be more easy that online.

I've been worked with IDMS for many years and I switch to my current job
which requires IMS and DB2. I borrow an IMS book from the company and
it takes me two days to complete it and I'm right on production
afterward. For DB2 programs, I don't have a time to read. I was given
a model program and I code base on that model program. (Yes a copy
approach)

My current job is on batch only. So don't have an idea about online.

One way to find out how your ability to work on IMS, try to find a 
book that talk about IMS and see if you can understand it or not.
The book I read is called "IMS programming techniques - a guide
to using DL/I" by Kapp and Leben. A very good book for programmer,
not much bullshit on theory.

BTW I still have a clear picture about what are the differences 
between a hierarchical, network and relational DBMS before I
work on IMS and DB2. Need to thanks for the professor that
taught me many many years ago about DBMS and gave me tons of
assignments. And as usual I finished them all.

Good luck.

Rgds,
