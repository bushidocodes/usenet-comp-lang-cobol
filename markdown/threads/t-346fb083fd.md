FTP has this built in if you use the ASCII mode.  It detects that you
are transmitting a file between systems that only use a line feed
(Unix) and systems that use a CR and LF (DOS, windows, NT).

FTP will do the necessary translation, stripping the CR when xfering
from DOS to unix, and adding it when transferring from unix to DOS.

I am assuming that your mainframe uses only the LF, and your clients
are on dos.

Hope this helps,

Paul

Bill,

FTP puts in a line feed for you when you transmit (the client
FTP).  The FTP server on the other end takes linefeed and
translates it into the local format (Mac uses CR only, Unix leaves
it alone, and DOS puts in the additional CR).

Rob,

The answer is that it is the recipients job.
-  If they are on Unix then tell them to jump.  They may need to
map on the way out of the Unix server to a PC box (which is not
your problem).
-  If they are on DOS tell them to get a better FTP server.

As pointed out the ASCII option might help (it does send commands
down the line to kick start the other side).

I have FTP'ed both ways several hundred times to DOS, UNIX/LINUX,
and Mac successfully without changing our JCL.

Good luck
Ken
