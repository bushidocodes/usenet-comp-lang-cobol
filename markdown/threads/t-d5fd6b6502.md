```
I have used the tool as part of  VisualAge COBOL (running it on both 
Windows and OS/2), and more recently on OS/390.  It works acceptably, in 
my opinion.  Our migration has been from OS/VS COBOL to Enterprise COBOL 
for z/OS and OS/390.

For OS/390 and z/OS, there are additional choices.  Prince Software 
markets a conversion aid named

The great majority of our programs (around 95%) did not need conversion, 
because we have had standards in place (and a standards checking program 
to enforce them) for many years.  Obsolete verbs like EXHIBIT, EXAMINE, 
and ON have been prohibited.  If your programs still use them, however, 
CCCA will translate them to 1985 ANSI COBOL verbs that give the same 
results.  Many of the programs (say, 3/4 of that 5%) which needed a 
little tweak or two were simply converted manually.  You might consider 
starting by compiling your "old COBOL" programs with your "new COBOL" 
compiler, and see what falls out.  With luck, you may not need to use 
CCCA very often.

At my mainframe shop, we ordered IBM Debug Tool with the optional 
Utilities and Advanced Functions module.  It cost about 1/10 as much as 
the debugger we were using previously (which I don't want to name), and 
one of the utilities was CCCA.  It was my impression that the entire 
debugging package cost less than ordering CCCA separately.  Debug Tool 
can be used with COBOL, as well as other languages, such C/C++ and 
PL/1.  It even does Assembler to a degree these days.  So if your shop 
isn't 100% COBOL, it may be of  great value to you for those "other" 
languages.

It appears that the Debug Tool is available for VSE as well.  I am a big 
fan of this debugger, and if you do not currently have an interactive 
debugger, I would recommend you take a look at it.  I Googled on "IBM 
Debug Tool VSE" and it looks as if the advanced functions stuff can be 
ordered, but I didn't see the "utilities" part mentioned.  You can 
always ask your IBM rep about that, or search IBM.com.

Good luck!
```

```
Be careful about your terminology (when talking to vendors) - every COBOL 
compiler for VSE *still* supports the "'74 Standard" of COBOL (via the CMPR2 
compiler option).  Only Enterprise COBOL (on OS/390 and z/OS) and VisualAge (on 
Windows) have dropped support for "COBOL '74".

You *probably* want conversion from "DOS/VS COBOL" (possibly with LANGLVL(2)) to 
an LE conforming compiler for VSE.  If you don't these terms, then you may end 
up with a product (or service) that doesn't do what you really want to do.
```
