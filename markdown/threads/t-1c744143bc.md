- **Message-ID:** `<dbryantFB8nCF.C1A@netcom.com>`

```

OK, the May issue of  Hot VS  (a Wang-centric e-zine) has 
been released to the public.  Late, but out.   

   www.hotvs.com

The utility I wrote, the one that helps you unpack fields,
is in there but in the rush to meet the deadline I had do 
leave out support for binary fields and a formal readme.txt.  
I'll get those in v1.1 and uploaded in the next week or two.

This utility helps you get rid of the binary, overpunched,
and packed decimal fields that makes moving data to a PC
difficult.  The format of the outgoing numeric is under
your control... number of digits (left & right of decimal),
leading/trailing signs, etc.

It also lets you insert literals and move fields 
around... handy for Y2k work:  MMDDYY --> "19"YYMMDD. 


Though it's written in Wang VS Cobol-74 and Wang Procedure
Language (scripts) the writing is sufficiently generic that any
programmer worth his/her salt can adapt it to other platforms.

I'll help you if you need explanation for any Wang-isms. 
```
