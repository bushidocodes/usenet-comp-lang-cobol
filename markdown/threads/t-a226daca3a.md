Bill Klein was a giant for COBOL.

He was passionate about the language and his knowledge of it was
encyclopaedic.

Over more than 25 years he was a frequent poster to this forum and he
could be relied on for accurate information regarding COBOL standards
and usage.

He fiercely resisted the idea of any Company trying to own COBOL and
insisted that the original Codasyl charter making it free to all must be
published in vendor manuals. (There could be a charge for writing the
compiler but NOBODY could own it or charge for the language.)

He was fiercely attacked and threatened with legal action because of
this and he phoned me about it when he needed some support for a hearing
he was being required to attend. I offered to fly to Chicago to assist
him but, fortunately, in the end it was resolved and the charges were
dropped. He was a tireless campaigner to keep COBOL free and available
to all.

As his health declined he became less active here and I think the
diminishing vision made it very hard for him.

I considered Bill to be a friend, even though I never met him and I was
saddened by his health problems.

Bill was proudly Gay at a time when that was hard, and I always admired
him for his honesty and courage. I'll remember him for those attributes
and his great contributions here and elsewhere, to the COBOL language.

RIP Bill.
