Today's Guardian "Online" section has an article on Y2K awareness in
the UK highlighting surveys from two consulting companies, one working
with the Department of Trade and Industry on Y2K, suggesting
management complacency in many major UK companies. Selected nuggets
include:

"Of the 201 major UK companies polled [...] only 60 had put in place
any procedures to deal with the 2000 date change. [...] in the ï¿½20m to
ï¿½100m turnover range, only two-thirds had done anything at all."

"Although 70 per cent of IT staff reported that they were aware of the
date change problem, and 76 per cent said they considered it to be
ï¿½criticalï¿½, their bosses did not share their concern. "

"Only 15 per cent of senior managers [...] were fully aware of the
date-change issue. Just 9 per cent of the companies surveyed had
considered making an audit of their computer systems",

"Failure to act now is likely to cost companies dearly: IT consultants
are already making provision for services they will offer non-
compliant firms at the dawn of the millennium, and they are likely to
charge at least ï¿½1,000 an hour."
