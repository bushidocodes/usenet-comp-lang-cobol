Mike Sheehan wrote in message <8m48db$drt$1@kermit.esat.net>...
>Hi,

>- What implementation\compiler are you using?

Fujitsu.

>- How did you find the change in paradigm i.e. using OO features?

Not difficult.  The real change was in having enough machine resources
to build **really LARGE** systems, more a result of hardware evolution than
software.

>- How has it affected the way you code in COBOL?

It has allowed me to take a much more "bottom up" approach to
system design.

>- What are the advantages of using OOCOBOL rather than switching to
>something else?

The biggest advantage that I was looking for was the ability to get to
the imbedded part of the system without exiting back up, then back
down a menu tree.  Example -- old system: I am entering an order
for a contract, for a customer, and I discover that the product the
customer wants is not in the product master.  I need to exit back to
the main menu, and correct, then re-enter the contract update module.

New system: the product handling object contains *everything* for
product maintenance, including creating a new product master listing.
I am in the contract module, and I invoke the "check-product-present"
method.  On failure, I am in position to add, get a new listing, then
when I exit stack fashion, continue on where I left off.

As to the COBOL as opposed to other languages, the answers are the
same as they have always been.
