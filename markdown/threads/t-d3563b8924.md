I remember a utility someone wrote once to copy as many files as possible
onto as few floppy disks as possible.  I usually use your procedure,
moving largest available files which could fit.  What this author did was
to calculate every possible combination.

With your sample choices, this would work - but if you have thousands of
usage charges, it would be too inefficient unless you had no choice.
Obviously, you can eliminate all usage charges and sums too large to
work, building up a table of usage charges and sums.

01   USAGE-TABLE     OCCURS 10000
       05   USAGE-SUM     PIC S9(5)V99.
       05   USAGE-CHARGE PIC S9(5)V99   OCCURS 10.

After you have the table created, you do a search for the largest
available sum, and then plug in the usage-charges associated with that
sum.

Are your quantities such that this solution would work?
