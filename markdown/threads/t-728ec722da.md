James J. Gavan wrote:
>... I hate the thought, but I think this BEAUTIFUL language is
>headed down the tubes.

I also hate the thought, but I am seeing things that disturb me.  One
local government I know of has people in upper management who have
decided that COBOL and mainframes are a waste of time.  One of these
same people said about 10 months ago "Y2K is a hoax."  So the word is
"Throw out the mainframe and COBOL, and rewrite everything on PC based
client server using proprietary tools and languages, and do it as fast
as humanly possible."  The MIS staff and budget are going to at least
quintuple, system reliability and up-time are going to take a serious
hit, but they really don't care.  They do not want to hear about recent
surveys showing only 16% of such attempts succeeding, industry wide.

When people start making such decisions for all the wrong reasons, it
does not bode well for COBOL, or the future of business in general.
Business decisions are supposed to be made based on careful analysis
of cost/benefit, not 'whim du jour'.  If this were an isolated thing,
it wouldn't bother me so much.  But I am seeing this attitude spread
in other organizations I know.  If this kind of 'thinking' continues
to spread, it won't matter what COBOL's advantages are, because the
bottom line is now subordinate to being P.C.  If this is the way we
are going to conduct business in the new millennium, all I can say is
"God help us."
