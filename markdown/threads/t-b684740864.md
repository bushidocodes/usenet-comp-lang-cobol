I don't work for Micro Focus (now) and have NO real information on this.
HOWEVER, dropping support for DOS and OS/2 doesn't completely surprise me.
As with the change in price/product availability (removal of a $600
compiler-only product) this would seem consistant with a "we target only
large enterprise" mentatlity.  I won't say whether this is good or bad - or
more accurately for WHOM it is good versus bad, but it most certainly does
seem to be the current Micro Focus positioning.  I worked for MF when the
trend away from OS/2 to Windows in (even IBM) large shops was occuring and
no that they find they have minimal market there.  Similarly, most large
shops have long since dropped DOS in favor of OS/2.  On the other hand, I
think that UNIX (for the "server side of things") is still very much in
their target audience - but significantly less so for the development side.

Again, all of this is personal observation and should not be taken (in any
way) as a Micro Focus position.
