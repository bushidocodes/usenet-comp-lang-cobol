```
The following post was put out in IBM-MAIN. It probably sounds like
"gibberish" to most MVSer who don't do systems programming, but it points to
a CRITICAL policy document.  (I can't even imagine what it sounds like to
non-MVS types) I would strongly suggest that if you are running on either an
OS/390 system (below 2.5) or still on MVS, that you make certain that your
systems programming staff have reviewed it. It includes important
information on what level of your operating system you need to be on by Jan
1, 2000.

"I was about to ask the same question (concerning upgrades from an old
version of OS/390 to a current one). I discovered the following document on
IBM's site:
http://www.s390.ibm.com/stories/year2000/coexist.html

Basically, IBM are saying that anyone still on OS/390 1.2 or lower have to
IPL all systems at once to upgrade - 2.6 and higher can't co-exist with
these releases. I imagine this is not an attractive prospect for most
customers still on 1.2 - it is certainly not for us. Interestingly, MVS back
to V4 is supported even up to OS/390 R8 - only OS/390 releases  have the
problem. (I wonder whether I still have any MVS V4 backups ;-))

I know that the 4 consecutive releases policy has been around for a while,
but given that OS/390 1.2 is still supported, and 2.5 can't be ordered
anymore (as I understand it), what is the upgrade path from V1.2? What do
other V1.2 or 1.1 customers think? V1.3 can't be far away from the same
problem, either."
```
