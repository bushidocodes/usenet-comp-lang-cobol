In this case, the notification by William in the posting below is being
sent to these groups because SHARE, an IBM mainframe user group now
allows multiple individuals at the same installation to participate in
the process and will allocate separate ID's.  If you are using another
COBOL product on a different platform, check to see if your organization
is a member of a user group for that platform or compiler.  There may be
similar processes for requesting improvements.

Since COBOL programmers are not as likely to be SHARE attendees, it may
be helpful for them to be notified here so that if they are interested
and are at an IBM shop, they can hunt down their systems people and see
if their shop belongs to SHARE.  If so, I think that multiple
individuals from the same shop can have SHARE ids and that if more than
one person from the same installation votes on a requirement, the votes
are averaged.  
Several of these requirements may be hitting long standing sore spots
for you or your shop so it is important to vote.  This helps determine
where IBM will put its resources.
