ranjay wrote:

> What are advantages of migrating legacy code to object cobol?. Is this
> a good solution?

This has to be your decision based on 'knowledge'. Pete Dashwood has
recommended learning Java to complement COBOL. If Java gives you the right
feel for OO, then you can start translating your thoughts and 'visualize'
how it works in OO COBOL.

But before you actually make the move - look at two good introductory
books - I'm not going to repeat details here - but Will Price and Ed
Arranga of cobolreport.com. Check with them on availability of their
respective books.

Armed with the Java/OO COBOL background - now you can make a reasoned
decision. At this point in time OO COBOL compilers are - Fujitsu, IBM
Visual Age and Micro Focus - Net Express. There is also Hitachi, but it is
only marketed in Japan.

Jimmy, Calgary AB
