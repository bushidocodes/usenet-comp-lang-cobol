They may change, but I don't see it happening this year.
The problem with head-hunter's in Canada is nearly all
of them DEMAND 2 yrs current hands-on experience. So you
should forget about relying on them.
Pursue the companies, check the Library to see what the
companies' are using, mainframe/midi models etc...
It's all in there, in one reference book, somewhere under
Business Directories.
However, the market's not hot here yet for Y2K. Also,
don't expect these ultra conservative big businesses
to accept PC Cobol + ASCII courses as sufficient for
allowing you to program their Mainframes.
Not to discourage you too much Steve, try the smaller
companies first. I hope you have other languages in
your skill set, those may be the crack in the door.
Good hunting,
Bill H.
