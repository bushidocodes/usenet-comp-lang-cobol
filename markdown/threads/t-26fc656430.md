My experience in New Zealand is that for someone that is any good it is more 
like $75-$100 /hour.
For the high end rate you are generally looking for a degree of input in the 
system analysis area and hopefully a rare programmer who can interpret user 
feedback at a deeper level than what they are actually being told.
Fixed quotes has always been the way I have worked in the past 15+ years. 
Your rate is then of no concern for me, the quality of your work and your 
ability to meet a fixed timeframe is the only thing of consequence. This 
brings cost certainty to projects which is what is fundementally what 
clients want. My job is always to protect the programmer from having to deal 
with a moving target. Feedback from programmers I have had is that I do that 
well.
This coming project I will try to break down into small enough portions to 
lessen the risk of programmers going over their time estimates.
I run Microfocus products on a windows 2008 terminal server as well as a 
Centos virtual machine with NX client access with VNC server running for 
remote demonstrations. I have Redmine for project planning, issue tracking 
and user submissions as well as holding my SVN repositories. This helps with 
good written communication. I am currently setting up an asterisk VoIP 
server with a variety of SIP accounts to hopefully improve the quality of 
verbal communication with programmers around the world. I use Xoom and 
Paypal for payment although for Malaysian and I guess NZ programmers, direct 
credit from a local bank account will be possible. Anyone is welcome to add 
their name to my pool of resources if they feel they can provide value for 
money.
