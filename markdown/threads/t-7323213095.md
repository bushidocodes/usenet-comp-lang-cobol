```
For anyone interested in US-only (ANSI - "American National Standards
Institute") final/official (July 16, 2003) adoption of the 2002 ISO
Standard, see:

 http://public.ansi.org/ansionline/Documents/Standards%20Action/SAV3432.pdf

which says (in part),

"ITI (INCITS) (InterNational Committee for Information Technology Standards)

New National Adoptions

INCITS/ISO/IEC 1989-2002, Information Technology - Programming Languages -
COBOL (identical national adoption and revision of ANSI INCITS 23-1985
(R2001), ANSI INCITS 23a-1989 (R2001), ANSI INCITS 23b-1993 (R2001)):
7/16/2003"

Among other "implications" of this (AS I UNDERSTAND IT), this means that as
far as ANSI goes, the "'85 Standard" is no longer a *currently in effect*
ANSI COBOL Standard.

As stated in another thread (in comp.lang.cobol) and as announced on the J4
"public website" at:
  http://www.cobolstandards.com/

"The revised COBOL standard was approved by ISO in September 2002 and
published in December 2002. Copies can be obtained from ISO

http://www.iso.ch/iso/en/CatalogueDetailPage.CatalogueDetail?CSNUMBER=28805&ICS1=35&ICS2=60&ICS3
or your national member body of ISO
     http://www.iso.ch/addresse/address.html.
ANSI adopted the ISO standard as a U.S. standard in July 2003. Copies can be
obtained from ANSI
    http://webstore.ansi.org/ansidocstore/dept.asp?dept_id=10
for $18."
```
