Hi Tyrone.

I've seen this type of question a few times but in relation to countries
that I have no experience of but, no offence meant, I'm surprised to see
the question about the UK.

COBOL in the UK is extremely common, in 12 years of programming in it
I've never been short of work. Although the market, like any other, has
its peaks and troughs it is currently extremely healthy. Recently
British Gas just about trawled through the major agencies for
contractors, BT has also on occasion required large numbers. Most water
authorities, public sector organisations (DSS, local authorities etc.)
have large projects on the go currently and there are an extremely large
number of maintenance projects out there. The system I'm working on now
was developed in 1977 and is frequently amended to cater for new
interfaces, methods etc. An inordinate number of manufacturing companies
rely quite heavily on the language.
It may not always be wildly exciting, most of my work has been on batch
and database systems that deal with accounting, stock control, order
processing and the like, but that is the type of thing that COBOL was
primarily designed for.
If you've been presented with the opportunity to learn an additional
language then go for it. It's always worth learning other methods and
languages.
