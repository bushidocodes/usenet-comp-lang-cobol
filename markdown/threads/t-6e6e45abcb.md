```
Given the (relatively unproductive) thread that is going on and also given
my (personal) desire that the majority of those of us who "post regularly"
to this newsgroup use "common terms" for the same "types of COBOL" - I
thought I would start a thread to solicit comments on the following terms
and definitions.  As this is an "unmoderated" newsgroup, there is NOTHING
that any of us can do to enforce these terms, but if we agree on the
appropriate terms, then we can be more consistent and less confusing to
others.

 - Conforming COBOL - source code conforms to ANSI/ISO Standard (current
ANSI/ISO Standard).

 - 'nn Conforming COBOL - source code that conforms to the "19nn" or "20nn"
ANSI/ISO Standard

 - Conforming XYZ COBOL - source code conforms to the XYZ Standard (e.g.
X/Open, MIA, SAA, etc)

 - Clear (and usually maintainable) COBOL - source code that can
(subjectively) be picked up and understood/maintained by a programmer OTHER
than the one who originally wrote it.  (Sorry, Tony - this doesn't include
your code that is "clear" to you, but not to many/most others)

 - Valid XYZ COBOL - COBOL which gets a "clean compile" with the XYZ
compiler.  The XYZ compiler vendor describes the rules for this source code
and whether informational or warning message may or may not be issued for
such code.  (The phrase "valid COBOL" may be used without mentioning XYZ -
if the context makes it clear which compiler - and possible release and
possibly operating system are being used.  The same is true for the "XYZ" in
"Conforming XYZ COBOL").

 - Portable COBOL - COBOL that compiles "cleanly" and produces the same
run-time results in multiple environments.  In most cases, one actually
needs to (at least initially) talk about which environments are in question,
as almost no "real world" COBOL is fully portable to ALL known ANSI/ISO
conforming compilers/operating systems.

   ***

Depending on what modifications others have and/or how wide the "acceptance"
of these (or replacement) terms are, I may or may not add them to the FAQ to
"encourage" their use in future posts.
```

```
Bill,

I've watched with absolutely no enjoyment the exchange between yourself and
Richard.

Both of you are extremely valuable contributors in this NG, I have respect
for the knowledge and usually very sound advice provided by both of you, and
I ALWAYS read threads contributed by each of you.

However, recently, it has become like two bald men fighting for a comb...

While terminology is important, (it is pretty difficult to have ANY kind of
meaningful discussion unless terms are agreed), it surely shouldn't become
so intense that the discussion descends to personal abuse...

I read the post below, Bill, I see and understand why you posted it, but I
have to be honest here...it confuses me!

I don't know about the other readers of this NG, (hopefully some of them
will respond to your post with their feelings about it) but personally, I
don't need to have definitions of exactly what I mean when I use the term
"valid COBOL" or "Clear COBOL" or "conforming COBOL" because it really
doesn't matter. As for accepting the ANSI/ISO standard as being " valid
COBOL", well you know how I feel about that...basically, just heartsick.

I believe that when COBOL is discussed here it is pretty obvious what
specifically is being discussed. There is no need to obfuscate it with a
series of pedantic definitions so we can go off at a tangent and argue about
those instead of the points actually under consideration.

The COBOL FAQ serves an extremely valuable function and, in my opinion, it
would be a misuse of it to bog it down with a series of "definitions" as to
what constitutes certain (originally) informal descriptions of COBOL.

BOTTOM LINE:

1. Although it may be Life and Death to you and Richard (at the moment), I
believe that the majority of people here really don't care, and certainly
don't need MORE definitions of things that really don't need defining.

2. It is a pity to see two really intelligent and valuable people engaged in
such a waste of time... But, as you observed, Bill, it is an unmoderated NG
and I respect totally the right of both of you to pursue whatever you decide
to pursue....

The purpose of this post as been to try and re-introduce an element of
perspective and get you both to re-examine whether you REALLY want to pursue
this...?

My two cents...

Pete.
```
