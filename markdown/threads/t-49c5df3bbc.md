Hi Lisa,

I can't help you with finding 1099 COBOL-heads.

I've been tracking the Y2K problem since December 1, 1979. The demand
for programmers is just starting. Everyone here knows it (well almost
everyone.) If you expect to recruit high-demand talent, you must offer
something to them.

Here's some value added... learn the rates and rate projections,
learn what CICS-DB2 mechanics are getting and expect to get in the next
6 months. Tell this to your client firms. If they tell you they can go
$60/hour, and your research is $90/hour is the going rate; be firm and
honest with them.

Programmers tell me that they receive 1-2 cold calls/day. Don't count
on finding the one programmer left who thinks this is 1996 and $45/hour
is good money.

Even if you find that one programmer who will jump for $45/hour, they
will not last long at your client firm. In two weeks, someone will call
them or they will see the billboard across the street or hear the ad on
the radio.
