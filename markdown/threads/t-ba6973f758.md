I always feel good when I encounter software that exceeds my expectations,
but when it is free as well, it deserves some kind of acknowledgement.

There is a routine of regular code backups at PRIMA (sometimes even more
than once a day, depending on the nature of it) and we do the usual "normal"
backups of files which most (sensible) people do, but when it comes to a
full partition backup of the active partition, things become more sticky.

I used to use Acronis and found it very good but when I downloaded a test of
the latest version it was pretty dismal.

There is always that nagging doubt that a hard drive might crash on a
primary development machine and it would be very useful to have a partition
image that could be restored to other hardware so life could go on...

If you wish to clone your active partition it usually involves booting from
a disk and the whole process is kind of "fraught".

I have looked at many offerings but nothing is simple, easy to use, and
effective.

Until now.

AOMEI Backupper is a fairly new package that does the things you want to do
with partitions, including the active one.

It lets you carry on working while it clones or copies (yes, there IS a
difference...) your active partition (C: drive) (or any other partitions you
want saved).

It cloned my 500GB (144GB used) primary development C: drive, to an
external Seagate 1 TB drive at 1 GB per minute. Not blazingly fast, but it
doesn't matter if you don't have to stop working.

You can compress or resize partitions, write to an existing partition (many
of these tools require unallocated space), and the user interface is
simplicity itself. Every process is described in help, with step-by-step
screenshots and explanation.

This is an outstanding piece of software, I have no connection with the
authors, and this is a completely unsolicited testimonial.

As I programmer myself, I can admire other people's work.

The peace of mind you get from knowing you have an image of your C: drive on
external disk and don't have to tediously re-install and rebuild in the
event of disaster is beyond rubies.

Did I mention it is free...?

Pete.
"I used to write COBOL...now I can do anything."
