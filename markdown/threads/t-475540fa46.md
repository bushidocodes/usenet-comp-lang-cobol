I notice you don't say which company this is. That is probably a good
idea, since if it is who I think it is, they have lots of nasty lawyers who
would love to threaten you with a slander suit. While I am not a lawyer, i
have been told by a number of them (in classroom situations), that
something being 'true' is a 'perfect defense' under law to a claim of
slander. Hopefully, you won't get into a situation where that is relevant,
but keep it in mind.

As to what you can do at this point, I know two people personally who have
been through these 'training' programs at a certain large, international
consulting firm, who have walked out before their period of indenture is
up. In one case, the company said nothing, and let them walk. In the
other, the company filed a lawsuit, and the former employee caved. The
difference seemed to be the personality of their manager at the firm.

Your best bet is to consult a lawyer (an initial consultation is not as
expensive as you think). The hard part is finding a lawyer who knows this
area. On that, my advice would be to find a local law school, or a college
with a graduate business program, and see whether they have any adjunct
faculty who teach in the area of employment law or personnel management.
People who take the time to teach are *usually* decent folks, who will at
least point you to someone who can give you an informed answer (after
looking at your documentation) for a reasonable price.

Since what you are now dealing with is a contract dispute, sadly but truly,
you need competent expert advice. As in, you wouldn't want a bunch of
lawyers to code your application (I've seen the results...trust me, it
isn't pretty), so don't put too much stock in legal advice from a bunch of
programmers.
