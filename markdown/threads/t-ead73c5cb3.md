```
NOTICE NOTICE NOTICE NOTICE NOTICE NOTICE NOTICE NOTICE


Call for articles in all areas of client/server development using
IBM tools and platforms. The Data Management Journal (TDMJ) is an
on-line, subscription-free monthly whose readers desperately want
"how-to" articles. If you have real-world experience and the desire
to publish your experiences, check out the TDMJ at:

http://www.thenumberco.com/TDMJ.htm (case is unimportant)

We hope to publish our first on-line edition on May 1, 1996, so, if
you have an idea for an article, check out the web page to submit
your idea.

TDMJ is seeking authors and articles whose editorial content addresses
'how-to' issues in the areas of:

[1] Languages - '85 COBOL, OOP and COBOL, C++, Java, and
anything else of interest to IS professionals
trying to build c/s applications with products
like, CICS, DB2, IMS, MQSeries....
[2] CICS - It's alive and well. Shops moving from 2.1 to the
newest releases, the CICSplex, CICS for OS/2, CICS
Client Enablers, the forthcoming OOP extensions to
the CICS API, the API, the SPI.... [There may even
be room for a story on the origins, real and otherwise,
of the DFH!]
[3] IMS and DB2 - Administration issues, tricks, best practices,
performance studies and inferences....
[4] VSAM - This, too, is not dead. In fact, there are new dataset
types, a version of VSAM for OS/2, and an API for
accessing VSAM datasets on the mainframe from OS/2
workstation programs....
[5] MQSeries - Message queuing is an exciting old/new approach
to transaction processing. IBM is pushing this
product from its Hursley Lab, and many IS
organizations need to know what you may already
know assuming you know it at all....
[6] MVS/ESA - It's not your father's MVS anymore. There's PQS,
PTS, SysPlex, the PC/390, OpenEdition, and whole
lot of other features your peers are eager to learn
more about.
[7] SOM-DSOM - Objects are everywhere, and IBM's object model is
OMG compliant making it possible to locate functions
and services anywhere SOM and DSOM are supported,
BUT, do you know how to do it? Are you willing to
share what you know?
[8] OpenDoc - Applications may soon be collections of parts and
parts editors coordinated by a new technology that
makes building complex applications with reuseable
parts possible. If you know how to build OpenDoc
[9] Other Areas of Interest - Did we miss a category? If real IS
shops need 'how-to' articles to provide
guidance, we're the place to look for
it....

TDMJ is NOT a replacement for USENET or lists. Too often queries get answered,
but those answers are traded "off-the-list."

Remember, there's no subscription fee. You can even set the robot to tell you
when the TDMJ changes!
```
