"William M. Klein" wrote:

Let's take one extract from the document you referred to. Since being on
this side of the Pond I've noticed a N.American penchant for using the
word "shall" instead of "will" :-

"Certain words have significance and their correct use is necessary in
order to establish the requirements of the standard.  These words are:
shall
should
may
can

These words are defined in 5, Description techniques.   To explain them
simply:  
     -  'shall' expresses a requirement; 
     -  'should' is a recommendation (not a requirement); 
     -  'may' expresses permission (but not a requirement); 
     -  'can' expresses a capability available to the user.  ...."

It is my view that if documentors got down to writing things as "will"
rather than 'if's', 'but's' or 'maybe's' - we would have less
ambiguity in the decision making processes. If you have to use those
'qualifying' words enumerated above, either put the topic on ice for
later discussion or go firm on a ('will') defined solution now. You feel
you 'can't put it on ice'? Then beat the thing to death until you can
define it with a 'will'.

I might add, 'shall', 'should', 'may', 'can', leaves open a can of worms
for implementor defined functions - which has resulted in modules like
Report Writer being ignored by most developers. 

Let's have 'plain English' folks - not semantics.

Jimmy, Calgary AB
