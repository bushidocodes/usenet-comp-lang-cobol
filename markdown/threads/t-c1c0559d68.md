From Publisher's Weekly
-------------------
Energy SAP? Random's Shift to New System Creates Delays

When the Random House-BDD (Bantam-Doubleday-Dell) merger was first
announced, one of the main advantages trumpeted by Bertelsmann brass
was SAP, the system that would integrate warehouse operations and
presumably make the ordering process go more smoothly.

A look at numbers and a random survey of some booksellers indicate
that problems have arisen, in which SAP may play a part (demand for
big titles such as movie tie-ins and weather conditions may also have
contributed).

According to figures obtained by PW Daily, the Westminster DC had a
backlog of one million units as of January 6. Of those books, nearly
half were shipped, leaving the balance unfilled. As orders continued
to come in, the backlog grew, and by January 10, even workers on
Saturday shifts couldn't stem the tide of a 2.1 million-unit backlog.
Over the next two weeks, orders picked up, and a snowstorm didn't
help. At press time, the backlog has swelled to nearly 2.3 million.
But when the process is complete, the upheaval from the SAP
implementation, say sources, is likely to be less dramatic than when
BDD undertook same.

Random explained the delays as part of the normal course of system
changes. "From where we're sitting, it's so far, so good," spokesman
Stuart Applebaum told PW Daily. "As any bookseller or publisher who
has implemented a new system will tell you, you always allow for some
hiccups along the way. Considering how mammoth and fundamental the
system's realignment has been, it sounds like they are relatively
minor in the
greater universe of what we do."
