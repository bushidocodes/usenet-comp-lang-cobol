In our army, commissioned officers wear little splodges on their epaulettes
called 'pips'. (It is modelled of course on the British Army). I have no
idea where these 'pips' originated from, but one pip is a second lieutenant,
two pips is a lieutenant, and three pips is a Captain. Next step is Major,
who has a single crown.

For some reason lost in the mists of antiquity, these pips are coloured
brown and cream; mainly cream, with brown edging and flecks in them.

As new recruits (conscripts) and hoping not to incur the wrath of our
sergeant, one of our number (no, it wasn't me...I had resolved to keep as
low a profile as possible and only speak when spoken to...) addressed him as
'sir'.   Annoyed at being exalted, the sergeant replied: "Don't call ME
'sir. There's no bird shit on my shoulder..."

Ah, happy days... :-)

Ahhh, that reminds me of a totally unrelated, but humorous (in the 'very 
bad things' or 'american beauty' style of humor) incident.

A fellow soldier, lets call him Larry, and I were sent from Germany to 
Saudi Arabia in December of 1990.  In germany we were stationed with the 
3rd Infantry Division, signified by its "broken-tv-set" shoulder patch 
-- three white strips on a background of blue.

Somewhere between moving massive tonnage of armor from the port to the 
various assembly areas we were granted an unexpected reprieve.  We had 
an entire day with nothing to do.  I'm not sure why, but who looks a 
gift day in the mouth.  We went to the unit shower site and actually got 
fresh uniforms out of our long discarded duffle bags -- I'm not sure how 
long it had been, but it was weeks at least.

So decked out in our fresh uniforms, smelling nice from the unexpected 
showers, we boarded the transport to the PX to spend the rest of our 
surprise day of leisure.  The port transport was an open-backed HMMWV -- 
very similar to a convertible pickup truck in civilian terms.

Just as the truck pulls up to the PX a flock of sea gulls are startled 
and take flight.  In the process, one well-aiming sea gull drops a load 
perfectly on Larry's left shoulder -- perfectly on his shoulder patch.  
The logo of the 3rd ID was accented with a very marine style blob of 
white bird goo.

It took me almost an hour to stop laughing...
