```
Just wanted to comment on what is going on in the U.S. right now.  I am
an IT guy running my own business and have been fairly successful the
last few years(as has everyone doing this) contracting.  However, I am
starting to see budget freezes by companies in the Midwest and that
means a slight slowdown right now.

What disturbs me is the alarming numbers of H-1B Visas coming here.  I
did not used to think much of it until I started to get a barrage of ads
EVERYDAY trying to sell me on the Indian programmers, on Visas, coming
off their current projects.  I am starting to see the companies hire in
the Indians and Pakistanis instead of hiring U.S. citizens.  In essence,
many of these people are taking vey low salaries to get the U.S. jobs.
Further, companies don't mind spending the money to get the person a
green card.  Afterall, it appears to be cheaper than hiring a citizen
in.

I would like to hear what others are thinking or seeing out there.  Phil
Gramm, Texas Senator, has increased the number of H-1B visas to 200K.
That's a heck of a lot of visas per large metro area.  This will kill
rates in my opinion.  The other thing is that I am hearing that many of
the Indians coming in have faked credentials as a systems analyst just
to get here.  I believe the law states that one must have 3 years of
verifiable experience in order to meet the criteria.  The flip side is
how in the heck do you verify someone coming in from Madras.  Imagine
the call to India to ask about the credentials.

The company where I am on contract now has just hired in some of these
H-1B visas and I know that some of the people they passed up, who are
citizens, are more skilled because I have had them on my teams during
projects.  There appears to be a lot of angst among the
temporary/contractor/staff supplementation people here right now and I
am starting to see why they are all in an uproar over it now.
Eventually, they will break into the project management realm and THAT
will affect me.
```
