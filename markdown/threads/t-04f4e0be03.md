This is an old joke that's been around for sometime now but someone
sent it to me in an email and since we mainframers do love to hate
Windows, I thought I'd share this in a nostalgic sort of way and to
point out that no matter how much things change, some remain the same.

-------------------------

For all of us who feel only the deepest love and affection for the way
computers have enhanced our lives, read on.
 
 
At a recent computer expo (COMDEX), Bill Gates reportedly compared the
computer industry with the auto industry and stated,
 
"If Ford had kept up with technology like the computer industry has,
we  would all be driving $25 cars that got 1,000 miles to the gallon."

In response to Bill's comments, Ford issued a press release stating:
 
If Ford had developed technology like Microsoft, we would all be
driving cars with the following characteristics (and I just love this
 part):
 
1. For no reason whatsoever, your car would crash.........twice a day.
 
2. Every time they repainted the lines in the road, you would have to
buy a new car.
 
3... Occasionally your car would die on the freeway for no reason.
You would have to pull to the side of the road, close all of the
windows, shut off the car, restart it, and reopen the windows before
you could continue. For some reason you would simply accept this.
 
4.... Occasionally, executing a maneuver such as a left turn would
cause  your car to shut down and refuse to restart, in which case you
would have to reinstall the engine.
 
5..... Macintosh would make a car that was powered by the sun, was
reliable,  five times as fast and twice as easy to drive - but would
run on only five percent of the roads.
 
6...... The oil, water temperature, and alternator warning lights
would all be replaced by a single "This Car Has Performed An Illegal
Operation"  warning light.
 
7...... The airbag system would ask, "Are you sure?" before deploying.
 
8........ Occasionally, for no reason whatsoever, your car would lock
you out and refuse to let you in until you simultaneously lifted the
door handle, turned the key and grabbed hold of the radio antenna.

9........ Every time a new car was introduced car buyers would have to
learn how to drive all over again because none of the controls would
operate in the same manner as the old car.

10.......... You'd have to press the "Start" button to turn the engine
off.
 
PS - I'd like to add that when all else fails, you could call
"customer  service" in some foreign country and be instructed in some
foreign language how to fix your car yourself!!!!
 

Cheers,

I especially liked number 8... :-) I got locked out of Windows 7 Ultimate in 
the middle of the night recently while under pressure and nothing I did 
seemed to work. Eventually I got it back (without doing a reinstall) but 
there was much swearing and sweating and the things I did are beyond the 
average user. After restoring sanity I immediately made the password 
restoration files that are NOT easily visible or recommended in the standard 
first set up, and resolved that if it happened again I would move to 
Linux... :-)

Word of advice: Don't mess with accounts and passwords once they are set up. 
Renaming them can be fraught. Best to delete completely and re-install the 
account.

It hasn't happened again and I actually like Win 7 but they should have a 
prominent warning to people using it for the first time on a system, that 
they need to make password restore files.

Starting to get feedback on Win 8 although I can't currently install it 
myself.

(I don't have a machine available and the 64 bit platform BIOS doesn't 
support Virtualization, even though the CPU does, so I can't VM it. Never 
buy a Toshiba; they treat their customers like crap. There has been a number 
of requests through Toshiba forums for the BIOS to be upgraded but they 
won't entertain it. You have to buy a much more expensive server machine to 
get BIOS support for Virtualization. I was so pissed off about this I gave 
myself a quick course in BIOS  programming and decided to fix it. The actual 
fix is not difficult and involves simply flipping a bit in a certain BIOS 
location so that support for the AMD-V instructions is enabled. 
Unfortunately, the BIOS chip itself is moulded into the plastic so it can't 
be replaced with an EPROM and, although it is possible to update it in situ 
with a flash, this is risky and requires special software tools. I was all 
set to do it when I found out that even if I did succeed in enabling 
hardware virtualization, there were other chips in the chipset that would be 
required  for hardware virtualization and there was no support for them 
either. So the new Toshiba is really just a paperweight until I can get 
round to buying a decent machine with a decent BIOS.)

TOSHIBA IS BEST AVOIDED...

The word so far on Win 8 is mixed. All of us with 32 bit applications 
(COBOL) are concerned our stuff will continue to run (it should, provided 
the install process clearly marks it as 32 bit...).
