IBM's OO COBOL is based on the ANSI OO COBOL draft due out in 1997. The
nested program scenario is used by IBM as well. From a code perspective,
the two look very much the same. INVOKE verb, CLASS-ID, and METHOD-ID
are standard. MF gives you a vocabulary capability so that you can
essentially create your own OO statements. I have not seen anything like
that in IBM's VisualAge for COBOL. I am a fan of standardized
programming interfaces, so the vocabulary feature doesn't interest me.
