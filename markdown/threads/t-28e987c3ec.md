```
Michael, just so you don't get a wrong impression, I absolutely agree that
a GNU-COBOL would be great to have. My reservations are not based on the
desirability of such a product, but rather on the knowledge that to do a
reasonably complete job is a big project -- I would estimate a minimum of
four person years of very knowledgable people working on such a project
full time.

Now of course by subsetting COBOL, you can make the project arbitrarily
easier, but the question is how big does the subset have to be to be useful.

I am sure incidentally that a GNU-COBOL is MUCH easier to do than a
COBOL to C translator, since GCC provides a much richer environment at
the level of the GCC tree than C.

It is true that doing a GCC COBOL would require more extensive initial
knowledge, but that's a relatively minor hurdle in the long run.
```
