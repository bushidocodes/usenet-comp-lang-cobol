Hello Tim,

I am not an employment advisor, but it seems to me that you need to
send a CV in English to the prospective employer with a covering letter
to ask them what other documents are required.  Individual companies
may have differing requirements.

It is not a good idea to send originals of certificates, often it is
possible to get certificated copies or translations.

You could also get advice from the embassies of the countries you are
interested in and perhaps from your own government's employment
agencies.

It might cost you but approaching a reputable private employment agency
with a presence in both your country and the one you are interested in
working could also be a good idea.

Good luck, Robert
