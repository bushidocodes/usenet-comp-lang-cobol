Maybe this isn't the place to post this sort of message, but I felt like
venting and thought maybe some of you might have been in the same
situation as I'm in now.

I am a mainframe applications developer.  I have been doing this sort of
work (COBOL, CICS, VSAM, DB2, etc., etc., etc.) for 17 years.  I love
turning ideas into working code.  I think it's great, and most of the
clients I've had think I'm great at it.

Recently, due to circumstances beyond my control, the niche that my
little consulting company I started with a friend of mine 7 years ago
had, has sort of dried up.  So, we're now looking for some work as
individuals again.  I thought that it would be nice to find something
local, since I've been on the road for 10 years, and sort of missed my
family.

Anyway.......I've been doing all the normal job search things......going
to the web, looking at all the job boards, sending my resume out via
e-mail, snail mail, whatever they want.  I've been talking to
recruiters, friends, relatives, enemys.....everyone I can think of
talking to.  And, I've made some fascinating discoveries.

First.........an e-mailed resume is really easy to ignore.   Of all the
e-mails I've sent, I have not yet gotten a response.   Not even a
negative response.

Second.....with some companies, it's almost impossible to actually talk
to a human being.  There is one company (I won't mention the name in
public) that I've called, e-mailed and called again, and I either get
answering machines or automated e-mail back.  I have yet to talk to a
real live person.  I find the same attitude in other places as well.
They're "annoyed" that they actually have to talk to someone.

Third.......if you've been programming for a long time, and you're
making a good salary doing it.......don't change jobs!!!     Corporate
america is not interested in experience.  All they want are people with
2-5 years of experience that they can pay $40-50,000.  I suppose I
shouldn't be surprised.  I've seen it in every company I worked for as a
consultant.  They would give an assignment to one of their employees
because they said it would cost too much for me to do it........and it
would take the employee 2 weeks to do what I could do in 4 hours
(application expertise does have it's benefits).   Now, where's the
fiscal intelligence in something like that?

Anyway.............I know lots of you are out there, and some of you
must have changed jobs in the recent past.........what have your
experiences been?

Thanks

Dave Bretz

2-5 years of experience.

As far as the IBM stuff......I'm sure you could pick it up in no time.  When I had
my own company and needed an employee, I hired a friend of mine that I knew was a
good COBOL programmer, but he had worked on Tandem computers for his entire
career.  He wanted a change, so I brought him on board, and within 2 months had
taught him all he needed to know to be productive on an IBM mainframe.  After 6
months or so, I sent him to Seattle to head up an IBM project for me.

It's like I've said in the past.....programming, like most other disciplines, is
learned step by step.  Each step building on the other.  So, to learn another
language or technique, is just another step.  But, employers and recruiters are so
narrow-minded they can't see that.  All they see is no experience in this one
particular thing!!    Talk about myopic.

Well, good luck!   I hope things work out for you.
