if so, to what platform?

Be aware that WANG supports COMP-3 while being an ASCII based 
machine (COMP-3 as in packed decimal a la IBM Mainframe!). You 
will need this to migrate the DATA.

Also, the WANG workstation got programming from the processor to 
change the workstations from Data Processing mode to a word 
processing mode.

Above is the list of links I have in my favorites list for Wang->OS conversions in COBOL.
Some of them may be of interest. Personally, I would look at PerCOBOL myself,
since it has a direct dialog of Wang in it. I think that ICOBOL might also, but
I don't remember. Check Bill Klien's FAQ for the web address for ICOBOL.

There is a product called COBOL ReSource that does a fairly good job of just
recompiling and running Wang COBOL code, but the COBOL compiler was
apparently never completed to the COBOL-85 level. If your code takes advantage
of anything like intrinsic functions or the address-of clause, ReSource will not
work for you. Contact information for it is also in Bill's FAQ.
