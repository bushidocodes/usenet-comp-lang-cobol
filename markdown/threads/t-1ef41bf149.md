COBOL will always align the implied decimal point of the sending field with
the decimal point of the edited-numeric receiving field. But the sending
field must have a numeric picture clause and contain valid data. Be aware
that COBOL will truncate if there's a mismatch between the two picture
clauses.

If you move PIC 9(4)V99 with actual contents 123456 to PIC 999.99, your
edited-numeric result will be 234.56.
