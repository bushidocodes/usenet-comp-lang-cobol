bit.listserv.ibm-main is the news group "mirror" of the IBM-MAIN list. 
There is a two-way gateway between the list and the news group (which
works most of the time), but in order for your messages to make it to
the IBM-MAIN list (which uses E-mail), or to post messages directly to
the list, you must actually subscribe to the list itself.  Some people
view and post from the news feed; others view and post via the list. 
There are no limitations on who may subscribe to the list (unless you
get "abusive"), but the requirement to formally subscribe is used to
keep SPAM off the list.  The rejection notice you received indicates
your posting made it to the news group, but was rejected by the gateway
to the IBM-MAIN list (so those who only view from the list wouldn't have
seen it).
  The last communication I saw about subscribing to the list was...
To subscribe, send a 1 line e-mail message to:
listserv@ua1vm.ua.edu    (equivalent to bama.ua.edu?)
that says:
SUBSCRIBE listname Firstname Lastname
Example:  SUBSCRIBE IBM-MAIN Joe Smith
Once LISTSERV receives  your subscription request, it will  e-mail you a
confirmation  message. Once  you have  replied to  this message  and are
subscribed to the  list, if you want to read the list only from the news
group then send another 1 line e-mail  message to LISTSERV that says:
SET listname NOMAIL
Example:  SET IBM-MAIN NOMAIL
This will  tell LISTSERV  not to  send you mail  directly from  the list
(since you  are already reading it  from a newsgroup). Now  that you are
subscribed  with the  appropriate options,  you  may post  to the  list
(or post to the news group and have your postings also accepted by the
list gateway).

I believe the list subscription process is sensitive to the email
address you use, and any subssequent postings must come from that email
address in order to be accepted by the list.
