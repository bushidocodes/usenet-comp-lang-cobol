Your hardware background will probably be an advantage to you in some languages and environments,
and a distinct dis-advantage in others. So let's try this the real way. 

(1) Pick something interesting and exciting that you want to do on a computer. Everyone is different. I enjoy writing billing software. I also get a kick out of writing real time control software, that sort of defines what kind of projects I tend to like the most. In any event pick something you really think would be cool.

(2) Evaluate what platform it would be best implemented on. (You can expect some help from the group here.) 

(3) Evaluate and decide what language/software is appropriate and the best fit for the project. Again, you can expect help from here, though you should expect it will be weighted somewhat towards a COBOL style solution. If it turns out what
you really want to do is better done in C, we can also point you towards the appropriate C-Language (or whatever language
it winds up to be) group. 

Number one is all yours however - and it is often the most difficult part of any project. Choose wisely, if you don't follow it through and complete it, it will be wasted effort, and the start of a bad habit that is difficult to break. 

-Paul
