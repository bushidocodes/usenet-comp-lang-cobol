Sorry I was "away" during our latest skirmish with the "I know what should
be posted in the COBOL newsgroup and I am going to report anyone whose
opinion is different" discussion(s).

It is my hope (and was my intent) to document the following in the COBOL
FAQ. If any of this is unclear in the FAQ, please let me know.

1) There is a charter for this newsgroup and its location is listed within
the FAQ. (Among other things it says is that "cross language wars" are not
welcome and job postings are not listed)

2) According to the "Gods of Usenet" charters for *UNMODERATED* newsgroups
are intended as historical documents - of interest (and possible guidance)
when first starting a newsgroup - but of only historical amusement from the
2nd day of the newsgroup.

3) There is a general theme to both comp.lang.cobol and the (less preferred)
alt.cobol of COBOL topics. However, this is quite broad including many IBM
mainframe topics (such as IMS, CICS, and JCL) - general Windows and PC
techniques, etc.

4) This AND EVERY OTHER unmoderated NEWSGROUP is open to any and all posts.
If you don't like something, delete it! If you don't like someone who posts
regularly, then filter out their posts. Do not complain (or at least do not
expect any support for complaints) about posts that are "off-topic".

5) It is "nice" (and really appreciated) when you add "OT" or "off-topic" to
the subject of a thread that is off-topic or has become so. However, there
is no rule requiring this.

6) There is such a thing as newsgroup abuse. It is well (if strangely)
defined. It has to do with how many times and to how many groups you post
the same (really identical) post. If a note or series of notes does not
meet this definition then it is NOT abuse (according to the standing rules
for newsgroups).

7) Almost everything which I have said above differs for moderated
newsgroups. If you have a desire (and the resources) for creating a
moderated newsgroup AND you get the support for it, then go for it. The
topic could be anything from COBOL and its world to the use of COMP-3 in
non-IBM COBOL programs - you select the topic, get support for it, AND
dedicate the time to moderate it - and it will be yours forever! Unless you
do this, then please do not complain about "off-topic" posts in any
newsgroup (although - like the original post - you are obviously allowed to
post as many rants and raves as you like, just don't expect it to be acted
upon.)

As I stated in an earlier thread related to this topic,

I don't care if you beat a dead horse, but please don't expect him/her to
thank you for it.

Interesting questions,

After reading the previous post and WIlliam Keiln's reply to this here are my
two cents.

I would have to agree that the charter is a method to start up a new News
Group. But, from there on the group evolves based on the wants and needs of the
participants. One way to solve this would involve updating the charter on say
annual basis. This would be based on the assumption that a core group of users
stay around that long to contribute to the new version of the charter.

As for the FAQ, it is just that a place to answer the most frequently asked
questions. Updating that with answers to new questions that come up should be the
main objective. A consensus may be obtained on the answer to this new question(s)
but that is up to the person who maintains that FAQ. Keep in mind it is their time
and effort therefore what is in the FAQ is really up to their discretion.

Finally, the question of job posts draws us right back to the issue of on and
off topic posts. IMO, as long as the bulk of the posts stay reasonably close to
Cobol the group will do just fine. The free and open exchange of information is
much more important than some rules to suppress it.

Jeff Farkas

I cannot tell you what the use is of a Charter. As far as I can tell (from
the Usenet NEWS.ADMIN "gods" and messages) its primary use is during the
voting period for creating a new NG. It gives a clue for what is intended -
but those voting are supposed to understand that it will NOT be "binding" or
in any way a "stranglehold" on the growth and evolution of the NG. (Again,
the rules for MODERATED newsgroups are quite different and those can be
limited to a predetermined topic.)

As far as FAQs go, I was really quite surprised (when I took over the
current COBOL FAQ) about what it takes to create and maintain a FAQ. All it
takes is a volunteer who is willing to start and maintain it and a little
bureaucracy to get it posted and archived. The initial (but no subsequent
one) is reviewed by NON-technical people for appearing to be relevant - but
that is highly subjective. It is MY intent that the current COBOL FAQ
reflect "consensus" opinions where they exist and to reflect all
opposing opinions where no consensus exists. HOWEVER, there is no set of
"rules or regulations" which require this. If someone wanted to post
another (conflicting) COBOL FAQ, there are no rules against this and they
could post whatever they want. I have actually noticed that some NGs *do*
have multiple FAQs with multiple authors.

I suspect this is not the answer you wanted - but it does reflect "reality"
in the Usenet NG world - as I know it.
