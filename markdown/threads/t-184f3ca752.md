Micro Focus (Merant), IBM, and Fujitsu COBOLs (that I know of - there may be
others) all support compiling for SCO Unix environments and all make ILC
(Inter-language communication) between COBOL and C pretty easy.  I think all
3 of them have examples in their "programming guides" on how to do this.

maintenance of your COBOL programs EASILY separate from your C programs.
You just follow their documented features, comple, and LINK the two
applications together.

To call C routines (assuming they're not in DLL's on Windows)
you have to *relink* the runtime, incorporating the C routines you
wrote. To call COBOL from C you relink the C routine to
incorporate the GT runtime libraries. In both cases it's a relink,
required only when the C routines change. Changes to COBOL
objects do *not* require a relink.

Really just linking two programs together! (The Acucobol-GT
runtime and the C routine).

Best regards

Nigel
