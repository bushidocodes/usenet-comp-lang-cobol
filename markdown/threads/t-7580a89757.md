Tom -

I'm sure may of us have or are experiencing pressure to provide
graphical verions of the software. If your applications are already
written in Cobol, converting them to another language or proprietary
platform won't be quicker or better.

I don't know what your current use for the interface and keyboard
handling in your programs is, but the quickest way to salvage what you
already have is to get a good graphical screen-handling package. Yes,
you will probably have to redraw all your screens, and if you use
a DISPLAY/ACCEPT approach to build the screens, it will be more work
than if you were using a text-based screen handler for your current
applications. Considering the significant learning curve to use a
totally new product and environment, the work involved to convert your
current application will probably be minimal in comparison.

Micro Focus offers graphical screen handling solutions, as well as
a product from Flexus called SpII. The Cobol-based graphical products
are designed to work with Cobol.
