Now, as another partial answer to your question, you may have noticed that
much Windows software is written in other languages such as C and much
InterNet software is written using C and various other kinds of supporting
software; lots of these are less familiar to Cobol/CICS/etc. mainframers
such as your programmers and myself. However, don't assume that you have to
throw away your extensive code and knowledge base. There are many versions
of Cobol (MicroFocus, Realia, etc.) that work on Windows platforms as well
as others (Unix, OS/2, etc.) and many of them by now probably also have or
support web interfaces. Realia Cobol also has its own CICS environment and
perhaps some of the others do as well. These modern Cobol implementations
typically support all the good structured programming stuff like Cobol II
and Cobol/390, including object-oriented extensions, and I'd imagine that
the right ones could also do Windows GUI and InterNet and other interfaces
and client/server and n-tier and OOP and other things that most people just
blindly assume that Cobol can't do. You may be able to greatly simplify
your conversion effort (for your programmers as well as your programs) by
checking out this approach.

Now you can see why I'm also crossposting this to news:comp.lang.cobol - in
any case, good luck.
