If you don't know ANY COBOL at all, you can learn "any" COBOL at all, even a 
PC-COBOL. The differences between "any" COBOL and "Z/OS COBOL" are tiny.

CICS is different... that's a whole subsystem unique to the IBM mainframe 
environment. But if the COBOL you learn uses a "CD" (communications 
description) and "SEND" and RECEIVE" verbs, you can kind of get a feel for 
how you have to program in that environment.

If you already know COBOL, then you can focus on finding CICS and ROSCOE 
stuff because, as I pointed out, COBOL is COBOL.  (After all, that is the 
whole idea behind a high-level language).

While you are at it, you might also want to familiarize yourself with the 
source code editors commonly used in the mainframe environment. I haven't 
been on mainframe in a while so I might be out of date, but the TSO editor 
and the ISPF editors are the ones I worked with, except once I worked on a 
CMS system which used a different editor.

I agree with another post that if you don't know ANY COBOL, then learning that 
first is the way to go.  There are books (often with compilers with them) to 
teach you the basics (and use a PC to do so).

Once you get to CICS, things get a little more difficult to do on a PC.  There 
are PC versions of CICS, but I don't know of any that are reasonably priced.

As far as ROSCOE goes, I definitely doubt that there are any PC emulators for 
that. Unless you KNOW you will be using ROSCOE, I would definitely try to learn 
ISPF instead (as it is MUCH more common in IBM z/OS sites).

A million years ago (give or take <G>), I did a full entry level training course 
for MVS programmers. (It was a phone company and I was actually "re-training" 
operator-managers, phone office mangers, etc - to become programmers).  This 
company was a ROSCOE site.  ADR (I think this was pre-CA) had an EXCELLENT 
"self-taught" book for learning ROSCOE.  It did, of course, assume that you had 
access to a ROSCOE system (and, as I recall, I had to set up new accounts with 
specific files already existing).  If you are at a site that uses ROSCOE today, 
you might want to have them check with CA to see if such training material is 
available.  My GUESS is that it wouldn't work on a PC, but they may even have 
that in these days.

P.S.  I don't know how many (if any) other readers of this group were trained at 
IBM MVS sites, but I was trained with and actually used to train others - in 
COBOL - an old "DELTAK" course.  (This had online exercises - and when I used 
it - as a student and an instructor - it was '74 Standard COBOL)  It really was 
a reasonable course for those days when larger corporations would take a "group" 
of students thru entry level COBOL.  DELTAK also had courses for things like:
  - JCL
  - Utilities
  - CICS
  - IMS (DB & DC)

All of this was "pre" computer aided courses.  It included videos, workbooks, 
and machine exercises.

Just mentioning this for nostalgia.
