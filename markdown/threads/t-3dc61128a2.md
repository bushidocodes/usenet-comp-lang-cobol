Jeff,

Try to visit the site www.adtools.com, it's Fujitsu web site. You can
ask for a free copy for their window COBOL compiler. I've just received
my copy last week. I've waited 4 to 5 weeks for the delivery. I believe
it will be faster for you since you are in the States.

Obviously, you need a good COBOL books to guide you through. Along with
the help of the compiler. You will learn COBOL very easily. Try your
luck at your local bookstore and if you cannot find it. Try to order
one from the internet. There's a couples of messages in this new group
mentioned the book company's site and compiler company's site address.

It's not uncommon that University does not have a COBOL course. It
recalls my memory by the time I studied at the Polytechnic, all the
CS department at the Universities look down to the COBOL. I'm glad
that my polytechnic did have a COBOL course.

The first job I have after graduation is on a UNIX environment, I
decided to switch to IBM MVS/COBOL nine months later. By that time,
everybody said the mainframe is dying, so does COBOL. The hit of
the town is C and UNIX. I'm glad I've make the correct move...
