Our shop used MicroFocus COBOL many years ago (also on OS/2), and more 
recently, VisualAge COBOL (on both OS/2 and Windows NT).

I liked VisualAge COBOL, because it was pretty clearly a port of IBM's 
mainframe COBOL compiler to run in the PC environments.  Programs which 
were written for the mainframe were easily converted, compiled, and 
executed on the PC.  Programs written on the PC were easy to move to the 
mainframe, too.  Usually, the ASSIGN clause of the SELECT statement was 
all I would have to change in either direction.

I took a look at the IBM COBOL situation, though, and it does appear, as 
you say,  that VisualAge COBOL is being more or less adandoned.  The 
last release was in 2000, and that announcement letter is no longer 
available on the Web.

I expect you can depend on MicroFocus to continue to support mainframe 
development on the PC platform, but I'm not at all current on that set 
of tools.

I would also suggest that your shop might want to revisit its strategy 
of doing development and testing on the PC platform.  This strategy 
seemed to be "all the rage" in the mid-1990's, but IBM has developed 
some very good mainframe tools which might make your platform choice 
different if you looked today.  IBM Debug Tool is one example.  Other 
vendors have highly refined and useful products, too, such as 
configuration managers, file utilities, etc.

The shop where I worked did develop a Windows based version of some 
personnel software in 2001, and used some programs that had been written 
for the mainframe as part of that.  VA COBOL was the tool.  However, to 
a great degree, it is still a mainframe shop, and development and 
testing are done on a separate "test" z/OS image, then compiled and 
moved using a configuration manager (CA-Endevor) to production.

Just as an FYI,

The COBOL compiler in WSED (Websphere Studio Enterprise Development) *is* the 
follow-on product to VisualAge COBOL (and include Enterprise COBOL V3R3 
compatibility).

A pro or con for IBM's compiler is that they ONLY  provide compatibility with 
"currently supported" mainframe compilers, ie. if you need CMPR2 - much less 
OS/VS COBOL support, look at CA, Micro Focus or Fujitsu.  OTOH, I know that 
Fujitsu does *not* include updated to "Enterprise COBOL" support - and I do not 
think that CA does either (but I am not positive of this).

NOTE:
  Of course both MVS (XA, ESA) and OS/390 will be out of service on the 
mainframe by the end of this year, so "currently supported" may not be a high 
priority.  If on the other hand, you are looking at z/OS (or even z/OS.e) then 
be careful which compiler you select.

ALSO, if you really want "standard" portable to NON-IBM environments, then there 
are lots of other players in the field - but that didn't seem to be the original 
question.
