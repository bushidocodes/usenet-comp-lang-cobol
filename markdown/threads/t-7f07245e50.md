### Why do Headhunters get upset when you ask for rates

```
I get tired of the attitude that headhunters have when they are asked
for rates. This news group has shown how childish they are. If I want to
employ a plumber, I am forced to pay whatever he sets as fair or market
price, not me. What choice do I have? Pay the rate or call someone else.
Cheaper usually means lesser quality.
```

received many apologies and have seen subsequent postings which then
reflect the charter of the newsgroup.  It is like anything else... some
decent folks, some wretched ones.

> employ a plumber, I am forced to pay whatever he sets as fair or market

I *have* noticed that in Business In General there seems to be a game...
The One Who First Mentions A Number Loses.  To me it shows a kind of
childish insecurity in one's ability to bargain; it results in the
following interchange:

Me: 'Hmmmm... so the location is Atlanta, the duration is 6 months, the
kind of work is CICS/DB2... what kind of rate are they offering for this
kind of work?'

Pimp: 'Well, what kind of rate are you looking for?'

Me: 'Excuse me, that does not answer my question... I asked you first.' 
(notice the barely-contained 'nyah nyah nyah' in my reply... in formal
discourse, business or academic, I find the tactic of answering a
question with a question detestable)

DD
