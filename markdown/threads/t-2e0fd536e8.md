I'm going to be _very_ picky here - just like the compiler ;)

If your incoming data is something like:
05 RAW-DATA PIC X(04) VALUE "- 1".

Then you code:
MOVE RAW-DATA TO INPUT-NUM.

Then (assuming you don't get a data exception) you'll get a number you
don't expect.

One way to accomplish your goal would be to split your incoming data
into two pieces:

05 RAW-DATA.
10 SIGN-FLD PIC X(01).
10 FILLER PIC X(01).
10 RAW-NUM PIC 9(03).

Then, code something like:
MOVE RAW-NUM TO INPUT-NUM.
IF SIGN-FLD = '-'
COMPUTE INPUT-NUM = INPUT-NUM * -1.

For instance.


The major illustration I'm trying to make with this example is that a
PIC S99 field doesn't "correctly" hold a PIC X(04) VALUE '- 01' field's
contents.

One other assumption (which I didn't make) is that you're correctly
handling the S99 field, but not handling output correctly. (i.e. PIC
-Z9 in a report/output field).

Hope these help.
