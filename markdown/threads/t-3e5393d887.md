As much as it pains me to agree with faculty members, most of the consultants
that I know say "yes", there is life after Y2K.

Most firms do not have a data processing budget large enough to cover anything
other than Y2K and legal requirements.  In addition to deferred maintenance
requests, normal corporate activities, e.g. acquisitions, spin-offs,
out-sourcing, in-sourcing, etc., and the failure of two-tier client/server
technology are going to create a huge demand for COBOL programmers.

Also, a significant number of COBOL programmers/contractors will be retiring
after the Y2K feeding frenzy.
