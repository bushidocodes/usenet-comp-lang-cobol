Since the index order is sequential (by key value), it is not entirely
clear to me what you want to accomplish but you might try the
following:

Change the ACCESS MODE to DYNAMIC
In WORKING-STORAGE add the item:
01 EOF-FLAG PIC 9.

MOVE an-id-number TO ID-NUMBER.
MOVE 0 TO EOF-FLAG.
START INDEXED-FILE KEY IS NOT LESS THAN ID-NUMBER
    INVALID KEY
        MOVE 1 TO EOF-FLAG
END-START.
PERFORM VARYING RIGA FROM 10 BY 1
          UNTIL EOF-FLAG = 1 OR RIGA = 20
    READ INDEXED-FILE NEXT RECORD
      AT END
        MOVE 1 TO EOF-FLAG
      NOT AT END
        MOVE ID-NUMBER TO CMP1
        MOVE NAME-VALUE TO CMP2
        DISPLAY CMP1 LINE RIGA COLUMN 2
        DISPLAY CMP2 LINE RIGA COLUMN 8
    END-READ
END-PERFORM.

This will read 10 records displaying their values on lines 10 thru 19
of the display. The first of these records will be the record with an
ID-NUMBER value greater than or equal to the inital ID-NUMBER.
If fewer than 10 records are available, then only the available
records will be displayed.

If this is not what you intended, please explain in more detail.

COBOL does not allow the dynamic creation of an index. All indices
must be defined when the file is created. In the following, I added an
alternate key to allow moving through the file based upon the value
of the NAME-VALUE field.

   FILE-CONTROL.
   SELECT    INDEXED-FILE
                ASSIGN       TO  "F:\COBOLV3\PCOBOL32\OUTFILE"
                ORGANIZATION IS  INDEXED
                ACCESS MODE  IS  DYNAMIC
                RECORD KEY   IS  ID-NUMBER
                ALTERNATE RECORD KEY IS NAME-VALUE
                        DUPLICATES ALLOWED.

  DATA DIVISION.
  FILE SECTION.
  FD   INDEXED-FILE.
  01   INDEXED-RECORD.
       02  ID-NUMBER     PIC 9(6).
       02  NAME-VALUE    PIC X(40).

  WORKING-STORAGE SECTION.
  01 RIGA                    PIC 999.
  01 CMP1                  PIC 9(6).
  01 CMP2                  PIC X(40).
  01 EOF-FLAG         PIC 9.

PROCEDURE DIVISION.

      OPEN I-O INDEXED-FILE.

      MOVE a-name-value TO NAME-VALUE.
      MOVE 0 TO EOF-FLAG.
* In this START statement I tell the compiler to use the alternate key
* so that subsequent reads will be in the ascending order of that key.
* In this case, NAME-VALUE
      START INDEXED-FILE KEY IS NOT LESS THAN NAME-VALUE
          INVALID KEY
              MOVE 1 TO EOF-FLAG
      END-START.
      PERFORM VARYING RIGA FROM 10 BY 1
                UNTIL EOF-FLAG = 1 OR RIGA = 20
          READ INDEXED-FILE NEXT RECORD
            AT END
              MOVE 1 TO EOF-FLAG
            NOT AT END
              MOVE ID-NUMBER TO CMP1
              MOVE NAME-VALUE TO CMP2
              DISPLAY CMP1 LINE RIGA COLUMN 2
              DISPLAY CMP2 LINE RIGA COLUMN 8
          END-READ
      END-PERFORM.
      GO TO LOOP-POINT.
*

This will read 10 records displaying their values on lines 10 thru 19
of the display. The first of these records will be the record with an
NAME-VALUE greater than or equal to the inital NAME-VALUE.
If fewer than 10 records are available, then only the available
records will be displayed.

HTH

This will work for Micro Focus, and most others.  You didn't statement

READ INDEXED-FILE NEXT RECORD
    AT END . . . .
    NOT AT END . . . 
END-READ

You can also use the START statement to begin somewhere other than BOF.
