The IBM product is quite workman like. It gets the job done, with the
following areas uncovered.

File Status Codes - no direct mapping exists for the pre-85 codes to the
85 codes, since there is potentially a many to many map involved. CCCA
(the IBM product) gets you started but you have to finish yourself. BTW,
I beleive there may have been a recent announcement on CCCA adding
platforms where the product will reside (beyond MVS).

REPORT WRITER - no support at all, but IBM does offer a separate product
that handles RW in a variety of ways. The Report Writer Precompiler
package works just fine.

IDMS - if you need this (at the present time), better look at other
products - including the one from Prince Software.

Bottom line - for most users the IBM product is adequate, and may be the
lowest cost option.

One other thing.... Just the product will not get the conversion done. I
recommend two additional approaches.

First, education: to start this project education and knowledge of the
various pitfalls is needed. "Planning COBOL Migration" is a 3 - 4 day
instructor led class that will take you thru the entire process. Offered
by Edge Information Group, contact Carl Gehr 513 948 8906 or
734··.@com··e.com

Second, know what you have in production today: "Edge Portfolio Analyzer"
runs against existing load libraries, and details what is there, where
used, etc. Same contact.

survive in a legacy based world.

David,

As Rex suggests in his post, training is an important adjunct to the
conversion process. We have a 3-day course "Converting From OS/VS COBOL To
COBOL for MVS & VM" and a related 3-day course that may be of value
"Language Environment for MVS & VM: Fundamentals".

To run COBOL for MVS & VM, you must have Language Environment (LE). LE is
a common run time library for multiple languages (COBOL, PL/I, C, C++,
Assembler, FORTRAN) that also includes a collection of services that are
generally useful for many programs (storage management and date services
to name just two; the date services can be useful in addressing Year 2000
issues).
