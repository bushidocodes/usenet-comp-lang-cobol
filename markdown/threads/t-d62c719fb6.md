Of course, chaqu'un a son gout...

But there are some delightful local varieties of clam and cockle here which 
are really delicious.  One of my favourites is called "tua-tua". It is 
pleasant to find an area of deserted beach, take a small bucket and collect 
a few dozen of these. Bring them home and hose the contents of the bucket to 
remove excess sand. Place a knob of butter in a saucepan with some crushed 
garlic and heat it til the butter has melted, then add half a cup of water 
and a cup of dry white wine and bring to the boil. Put the tua-tuas into a 
steamer on top of the saucepan and let it simmer. The tua-tuas start to open 
as the steam heats them and within about 5 to 10 minutes they are done. (You 
can tell because they are all open; any that aren't can be discarded). Grind 
fresh black pepper over them and eat them directly off the shell.

This is a simply amazing snack while watching TV or just sitting outside 
with a glass of appropriately chilled Sauvignon Blanc or Chardonnay (both of 
which are cheap, of good quality, and plentiful here). Personally, I prefer 
red wines but I have to admit these are very good with seafood...

In addition to the large green lipped mussels (which are reputed to have 
anti-carcinogenic properties), supermarkets here have recently taken to 
stocking South Island clams. (These are presented in specially constructed 
stainless steel trays which have seawater circulated and sprayed over them 
so the shellfish are are alive at the time you buy them.) I was agreeably 
surprised at how cheap they are... a kilo of fresh, large, green lipped 
mussels for under $4...(mind you, they rip us off on everything else so I 
guess it cancels out... :-))

I really hate the idea that these seafood stocks could be at risk due to 
Climate Change and I sincerely hope the people who are persuaded of it are 
wrong. I'm inclined to believe that lack of conservation, management, and 
over-fishing are much more serious and immediate threats, but that doesn't 
diminish the threat from Climate Change, if it is real.
