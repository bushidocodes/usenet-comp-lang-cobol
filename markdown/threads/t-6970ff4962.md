With experience from both Cobol, Delphi and Visual C++, I would say: Yes.

All languages has their pros and cons, but, as stated out in many fine
contributions to this NG, when it comes to business logic, nothing beats
Cobol.

One issue is the built in support for numeric presentation, another one is
the built in database and finally, in my opinion, one of the best things
about Cobol: The readability.

Of course, there are things that are easier done using Delphi and VC++,
however, how likely is it that you will need them? And if you need them,
todays Cobol is open and willing to connect to other languages. My Cobol
knowledge is to Acucobol, but, from what I have understood in this NG, both
MF Cobol an Fujitsu Cobol are open to speak with other languages as well.

An other interesting phenomena here, is that those things that are hard to
accomplish using Cobol, most often are just a minor part of your development
project. While the tasks that make the most of your project are
significantly easier using Cobol than either of Delphi, VC++, VB. Well,
screen design and layout are easily accomplished using Delphi and presumably
VB, but, database access is slightly more complicated on the other side.

Everybody talk about performance. There is no performance issue. From my
point of view, they're all alike. The profit one may gain from one language
to another in one field, is most often punched back in another field.

The interesting stuff for this comparition is, when we start talking about
portability. How about going on the web without a number of add ons? How
about have your stuff run on Unix? How about, how about, how about...

Given otherwise equality, I would vote for portability. Not that one really
need it today, but who knows?

Anyway, I do think that you should offer some time experiencing modern
Cobol. Definitely.
