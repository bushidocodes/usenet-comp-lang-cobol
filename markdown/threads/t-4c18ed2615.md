Compute's intermediate results are implementor defined in all COBOL
standards to date - so beware - especially with complex operations or
division within COMPUTE. Especially DIVISION by an integer (some
compilers do truncation.

ADD, SUBTRACT, MULTIPLY and DIVIDE are portable in all but the division
by zero case.

Here's what one of our COBOL II manuals says:
After decimal point alignment, the number of places in the fraction of
the result... is compared with the number of places provided for the
fraction of the resultant...
Whgen the size ot the fractional result exceeds the number of places
provided for its storage, truncatation occurs unless ROUNDED is
specified. when ROUNDED is specified, the least significant digit of the
resultant identifier has its value increased by 1 whenever the most
significant digit of the excess is >= 5.

SO... for example

01 MONEY-FIELD PIC S9(9)V99.
01 TAX-RATE PIC S99V999.

01 MY-TAX-1 PIC S9(9)V9.
01 MY-TAX-2 PIC S9(9)V99.
01 MY-TAX-3 PIC S9(9)V999.

(Add COMP-3 to the above - I'm saving typing strokes.)

MOVE +5.89 TO MONEY-FIELD.
MOVE +0.999 TO TAX-RATE.

NOTE: 5.89 x 0.999 = 5.88411 (I think)

COMPUTE MY-TAX-1 ROUNDED = MONEY-FIELD * TAX-RATE. (+5.9)
COMPUTE MY-TAX-2 ROUNDED = MONEY-FIELD * TAX-RATE. (+5.88)
COMPUTE MY-TAX-3 ROUNDED = MONEY-FIELD * TAX-RATE. (+5.884)

However, there's more to this (at least there was with older compilers).
It had to do with the number of places COBOL set up for intermediate
results. This was based on the number of places in the arguments. Since
my COBOL II manual fails to mention this concept, maybe this no longer
is a worry.

I remember having to set up work fields with extra decimal places, so
COBOL would drag along the lesser significant digits in its
calculations. Otherwise truncation would occur earlier than the time for
ROUNDING to take effect. This may have only affected multiple
calculations such as a COMPUTE with many arguments.

For example, if I wanted to get MY-TAX-2 ROUNDED to the nearest cent,
and was multiplying it by NEW-TAX-RATE PIC S9V99, I would have moved
MONEY-FIELD and/or NEW-TAX-RATE to a three-decimal work field(s) before
the calculation. Then the intermediate fields would be the proper size.

Maybe some real experts can dig into this nuance - it's been a long
time.

Anyway, as someone else mentioned, the .06 or the .060 would not have
made a difference in your case. It will round to the nearest penny
because of the decimal portion of the PIC of your result field.

Good luck
John

Usage can also be specified at the group level if all the items are
the same usage. You can't override the group usage at the elementary
item level.

01 counters-accums comp-3.
05 detail-piece-count-accum pic 9(5).
05 detail-price-accum pic 9(6)v99.

comp-3 is generally interpreted as packed-decimal which happens to be
faster and more storage efficient on IBM mainframes. This does not
mean that it will be faster on other hardware, could be slower. Comp-3
does not guarantee that it will be packed-decimal or that it will even
compile on other computers.

If you need to guarantee packed decimal then specify the usage as
PACKED-DECIMAL.

In the same vein, if you want to guarantee binary don't use COMP, use
BINARY.

COMP means computational. A field defined as COMP should be put into
whatever form is best for math on the computer at hand. Usually binary
but could be decimal, base-3, or Roman numerals.

It varies from compiler to compiler but with IBM mainframe compilers,
where inv-tax-ws has pic 9(n)v99 and inv-nettot-ws pic 9(n)v99 and
the constant is ".060" or a dataname holding the constant (for the
structured programming purists out there) with pic 9(n)v999, you
could trick the compiler by commuting the operands around the
communtable or associative operands, hence...

compute inv-tax-ws round = 000.060 * inv-netttot-ws.

Even if you used ADD, MULTIPLY, DIVIDE, the results from the third
guard-digit in the tax rate could be truncated. Some "risk-averse"
programmers would move the inv-nettot-ws amount to a pic 9(n)v999
field then do the calculation, add 00.005, then move the temp field
results to inv-nettot-ws.

Less efficient but accurate, perhaps.
