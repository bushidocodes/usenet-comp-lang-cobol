I keep seeing people saying that they don't have to use COBOL because
they are going to c/s. Now, this doesn't make a lot of sense to me.
You can write clients using COBOL and you can write servers using
COBOL. In fact, Tandem users have been doing this for over 20 years
(maybe not quite like the current definition - whatever that is - but
very similar). Am I missing something here? I realize that many want
to use the latest wizbang visual something or other or C++ or whatever
because it is currently "in". However, there is normally nothing
keeping one from using COBOL for this stuff. MF and others have
provided access to the latest APIs and all.

IMHO, I think they're a number of reasons, none of which are good
reasons.

1. Cobol gets bad press, and in turn, a bad rap. So many people
equate Cobol with legacy. And in every instance legacy is look on as
something old that the new technologies have to a hard time dealing
with. That, I believe, gets people to think that will make their c/s
legacy instead of cutting edge of technology, which is completely
false. Just like buying a car, a lot of people get dazzled by the
marketing and promotion, and fail to pay attention to the real
substance of what they're buying.

2. Microsoft does an excellent job of selling itself, and its
products. This is why VB is the fastest growing on c/s for apps dev.
(but, Cobol is #2). Can we say the same thing about IBM?

3. Colleges and universities push hard for C/C++, and Unix. And at
the same time disdain Cobol. How many times have we seen posts from
college students, or recent grads that say something foolish like
"Cobol's a dead language", or even a professor that tries to say that
Cobol is 90% maintenance??? I've been hearing this Cobol is dead crap
for 20 years, and one of the first places I heard it was in college.

4. Object Cobol is late. If OOCobol was released 5 years ago,
instead of recently, I think Cobol would have had a better and much
bigger impact on the c/s. Now, Cobol needs to unseat some languages
that have already established themselves.

But OTOH, I think this attitude is changing. Many IS shops are
starting to realize that they can use their Cobol programming staff to
program in Cobol on c/s. But that's just IS shops. I think IBM,
MicroFocus, Accucobol and the rest, need to market Cobol better. And
colleges need to realize the need, potential and future of Cobol. Has
anybody considered giving away free Cobol to universities? If memory
serves me right, isn't that how Unix and C got it's start?
