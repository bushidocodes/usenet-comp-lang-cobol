Sue,

    COBOL 9x is the working name for the next COBOL Standard.  If you go to
the various associated sites, you will find that the new standard won't be
formally published until sometime in the middle of 2000 (if all goes well),
So the name of the standard may change (each has been based on the year of
publication so far, but COBOL 00 would look too much like COBOL OO (zeros
versus ohs).  Maybe the standards committee will get with the Y2K swing and
call the new standard COBOL 2000, instead of that obsolete two digit date
format.

    You can, however, download the current draft of the standard (runs to
more than 400+ pages in PDF, so be prepared to wait).
http://www.microfocus.com/Standards/  You can download the draft from this
site, if you really want to.
