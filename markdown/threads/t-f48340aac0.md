To talk about rates for COBOL programmers can be misleading, since almost
nowhere strict COBOL programmers are needed.  For starters, in most cases it
is Programmers/Analysts that are wanted.  This makes a lot of sense since
anybody with some experience in business applications will tell us that the
programming part is very rarely the difficult one, but understanding the
application, the data base components, and the business needs.

But, on top of being able to do good analysis, as well as test and
implementation plans, there is also the need for other technical knowledge:
IMS, IDMS, DB2, JCL, VSAM, CICS, are but a few that come to mind. And how
proficient the candidate is required to be in these techniques should add or
subtract to the rate that is offered.

Cost of living in the vicinity has little influence in the rate for the
contractor.  This is so because the candidate will not be moving permanently
to that place, and towns that have a low cost of living are normally as
boring as sucking an unsalted nail, and are (most of the time) far from
airports. If the candidate is young, he/she will want a place where they can
shake their bones after working hours. If he/she has a family, then he will
appreciate being close to an airport, and being able to work a compressed
week, so they can go home frequently.

Those brave enough to post jobs in these newsgroups are advised not only to
post rates, but also the requirements with must have, and nice to have, as
well as years of experience required in each of the important subjects. It
would also help if the recruiter has some idea of what is involved in
developing business applications. If a contract is for less than six months,
then deeper and broader knowledge might be in order, but if the contract is
for longer periods, some of the skills could be learned on the job. If the
resume shows that the candidate has been able to work with different
languages and environments in the past, this might be an indication that
he/she is able to learn fast.

And still have not mentioned a single rate, but I would say that $50/hour
does not prompt me to leave my job and apply. But, this is today. Maybe
tomorrow, if the assignment is in my  town, and/or I  have been without a
contract for more than 45 days, then I might consider that a very good rate.
