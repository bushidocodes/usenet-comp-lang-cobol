You told the compiler what to copy. Did you tell the compiler where to
look? There are two separate ways to do this:

1. Explicitly within the code as part of the COPY statement:

COPY 'V:\COBOL\SECT234\CLASS-A\EXER4\CPYBKS\FROG.CBL'.

2. Implicitly thru some external setting - usually particular to your
system - such that when the compiler sees:

COPY 'FROG.CBL'

the compiler uses the external setting to prefix your 'what' with a
'where.'

These external settings usually take the form of SET commands or they
are added to the compiler options.

What Bill is saying is you have to READ what you put in your SELECT and FD, and
WRITE what you put as the 01 of the output file. (BTW Bill - at my very first
programming job one of the old timers put up a poster with your comment in my
cube :D).

I suggest that you call your files FILE (Select and FD) and your records (01
levels) -RECORD or REC and then look at your code again.  This should help make
things a little clearer.

Example:  SELECT CUSTOMER-INPUT-FILE

FD CUSTOMER-INPUT-FILE
01 CUSTOMER-INPUT-REC

Also - what is the 88 level after the 01 in the CUSTOMER record (input)?  88's
are for nemoics for a switch or field in a record.

Example:
01  EOF-SW           PIC X  VALUE 'N'.
      88  NOT-AT-END VALUE 'N'.
      88  END-OF-FILE VALUE 'Y'.

IF NOT-AT-END
   do something

is the same as

IF EOF-SW = 'N'
    do something

Eileen
