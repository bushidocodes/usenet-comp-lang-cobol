```
I'm a former COBOL programmer who left the field during one of those
mean leveraged buyout / downsizing things during the eighties. I've
been in other fields but see a possible opportunity, and have to do a
fast review in a couple of months. I've since taught myself assembler
and C++ for the PC, but C++ seems really bleeding edge. It's a moving
target ;-) By the time you learn it, it's changed. I'm not sure if
I want to spend Every waking hour keeping up. Anyway, I just bought a
new computer, so I'm a bit cash short for Microfocus (but would
appreciate ordering information for later). I got a book/disk called
"The Revolutionary Guide to Cobol With Compiler" by Handel, Degtyar,
and Bronnikov. Has anyone heard of this, and is it a decent review of
COBOL?

The other thing is, I was in an MVS system, and know from sad
experience that you'd better know Something about the JCL. (I got in
big trouble trying to balance a program when a jobstream selective
sort was stripping records from my data Before it got to my program,
unbknowst to me And the systems analyst. And my trainer insisted
there was no such sort in the system. I found it, but just because I
didn't take his word and bought a book on OS JCL and learned enough to
find the thing, since I was certain my program was right. Not that it
helped when the Big Crook company from CA bought us and fired everyone
-- and also cheated all the customers )

So what I've been looking all over the internet for is some sort of PC
based tutorial that would cover MVS JCL and CICS, but all I find when
I use those keywords are thousands of resumes ;-) Is there such a
thing.
I work full time, use my computer a lot, have other duties, and simply
don't have the time or money to hang around a college computer lab
relearning this stuff. Besides, I know I can teach myself faster,
anyway.

As an alternative, I also hear that there is a lot of PC COBOL,
Microfocus I assume. Is this common? What is the common operating
system? Win 3.1 or Win NT? Could one have a good chance at
employment by learning the PC operating system, or are mainframe IBM
systems still the most common. And what opsys do the midframes use?
Also, is Microfocus COBOL a network language? I'd like to learn
something about network programming. And what sort of screen
display/user interaction system is used to replace CICS?

Lotsa questions, but I'm short of time and basically looking for exact
directions, since I can't afford to travel down every alleyway. So
signposters, guides, and flashlight holders will be more than
appreciated for their comments ;-)
```
