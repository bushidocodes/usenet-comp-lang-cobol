This doesn't make sense.  You are writing a program for yourself outside of your
class, but don't want other students seeing your work.

I'll pretend it makes sense:

1.  You need to come up with a reasonable project, and create requirements and/or
specs.    This is very important experience.   If you are serious, start a thread
to assist you in this endeavor.

2.  After you have good specs, write your test plan.   Again, this could be a good
newsgroup thread, which would be of interest to the rest of us.  Specs and test
plans are very important parts of programming.   These are the main part, with
coding the trivial part.

3.  Then start coding.  As you come to difficulties, post enough information so
that we know your environment, what you're trying to do, why you think what you're
getting is incorrect, and what you've done to try to resolve your difficulty.

We will give you clues to allow you to find your solutions.   We like to do this
publicly so that we all can learn from the project.


I would very much welcome thread(s) on "I am having trouble developing a
comprehensive test plan with my homework".   I suspect we don't have these threads
because the courses neglect the important stuff and concentrate on syntax.
