It always amazes me how you get to feel like you know people who post

I have my own mental image of many who post here and ...well, I just
hope I'm wrong <G>...

The good thing about it is, that despite all the noise and the
off-topic nonsense, the very action of interacting with a group from
hugely diverse backgrounds and locations, based around the single
common interest of the COBOL language, helps each of us to grow.

Over the years, interactions in this group have moved me through
almost every  possible emotion, from extreme anger to elation. None of
it has done me any harm and I am wiser for it (for example, I have
learned not to argue with Doc <G>).

The snippets of information, the humour, the insights into different
viewpoints, the cut and thrust of intense debate, winning some, losing
some, is all part of a rich pattern that beats the Hell out of sitting
in a chat room...<G>

I am (in all seriousness, now) thankful for the interactions and the
opportunity to get someone thinking about their future and their
career and maybe moving it in a better direction.

It goes way beyond COBOL Programming and technical advice.

My very warmest wishes to all of you and sincere hopes that 2003 will
be prosperous and fulfilling.
