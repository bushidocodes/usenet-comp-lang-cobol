It is not a question of COBOL being the main solution to the problem.
It is a matter of correcting the COBOL code to repair the dates. While
COBOL is probably the the most affected language, any language in use
can be affected.

› How does one train themselves to solve the year 2000 problem?
›
› Thanks.

It is not an issue of training, but of experience. The Y2k problem has
not happened before in the computer industry. The problems don't just
cover languages, it also includes operating systems, data and embedded
chips and more. This all adds up to a potentially very time-consuming
and expensive process.

My previous contract (finished at Christmas) was with a massive
financial and other services corporation. They have 110,000 different
software systems around the world. By the dreaded date, they expect
to have disposed of about 40,000 of these systems (one easy way of
getting compliance) and they expect to have achieved compliance on all
business critical systems.

In fact, one area that concerns them is other organisations' systems
that they link to for EDI purposes. So, while they're happy-ish with
their own progress, they are scared that other systems will cause them
severe problems by not being compliant. At my present site, one of
their EDI connections has stated plainly that they will continue to
pass 2-digit years thus placing the burden on my client to do the
work. Not fair, I would say, but it is effective because of the
pivotal nature of the connector to the entire marketplace.

In the UK, there was an item on television the other day (I mean in
Jan 98) that illustrated that the National Health Service - the
largest employer in the country - was just starting the analysis phase
of y2k compliance. So at least it looks as though there's a fair bit
of work left there, if they don't kill us all off in the process. ;-)

I digress but I just had a vision of being taken into hospital with a
broken leg and treatment being refused until after I fixed a bug in
their payroll or some administrative system. I can kind of picture
the triage situation:

Nurse : Programmer?
Patient : No.
Nurse : OK, wait there and hold this dressing over the wound.
Next!
Programmer?
Patient : Yes.
Nurse : Languages?
Patient : COBOL, Assembler
Nurse : Any JCL and/or VSAM?
Patient : Yes.
Nurse : Doctor, we've got a live one here for the Admin system!

If that was bad taste, I apologise. In fact, I'll send myself to bed
early. Goodnight.
