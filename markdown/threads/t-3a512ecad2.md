SAP/DB is a source-fork of Adabas-d and made open source.
SAP/DB is now known as Max/DB and part of the MySQL family.
The SAP/Max fork is developed further and they dropped the COBOL
precompiler.
Adabas-D (v12) is not developed any further (who cares, it's a great
database) but still contains the COBOL pre-processor and the evaluation
copy can be downloaded for free. When you want to use it (you know, more
than 1 user, more than 100 Mb in the database) you must buy a licence.

Confusing? Yes. Wanna be confused more? Okay:
the previous evaluation version (v11) of Adabas-D was included in the
SuSE6.x distributions. When you installed that you also got an
evaluation version of ACUcobol. Not many people know this......

O yeah, this is a cobol forum so here is some extra explanation: A SuSE
distribution is linux. The 6.x version is rather old, more than 5 years
I guess. Starting from the 7.x versions I did not find the
adabas/acucobol mix any more.
