"Legacy Systems" is a term the industry has created to refer to systems
running business which have been around for years, but are crucial. The
terms "out-dated" and "dinosaur" were first used by the PC industry in a
derogatory way, primarily directed towards mainframe systems and Cobol.
This, in my opinion, was done in an effort to capture (through much
hype) the lucrative and important business appliation dollars.

Through time and things such as Client/Server, etc. not living up to the
hype, "Legacy" seemed to evolve. I dislike the term because it is still
primarily directed towards the mainframe an Cobol, etc. The fact is,
that there are many languages and operating systems that are as old are
nearly as old as Cobol to be coined "Legacy"--C, Basic, Pascal.

This is my perception of the term, anyway.

Mike Dodas
