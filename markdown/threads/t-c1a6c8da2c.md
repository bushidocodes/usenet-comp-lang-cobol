I was using MetaCOBOL on the mainframe about 20 years ago. At that time
it seemed to me the best thing after sliced bread. I am not referring to
pre-canned macros or the automatic reformatting capabilities, although
both could be tailored to your taste and needs.
It allowed me to generate (if memory serves me well) 45,000 lines of
reasonably good code in a couple of weeks. That included the time to
write and debug my own macros, such as date conversions, file access,
error handling, specialized table searches, balance line technique for
transaction/master file updates etc. The strength of MetaCOBOL was in
the fact that it's macros had the "knowledge" of the data items it
operated on and of COBOL in general, unlike general purpose macro
(pre-)processors.
In the meantime, I got out of the "COBOL business" to pursue other
matters. Now I want to pick-up the thread. However, the purveyors of
MetaCOBOL have changed (from ADR to CA), the environments have changed
(no PC version then), and the COBOL language itself has changed.
To get any info I started with CA by asking them about the current
MetaCOBOL capabilities, performance etc., but their response was
underwhelming.
>From the newsgroup it hasn't been all that hot either. I guess, the
inescapable conclusion is that MetaCOBOL if not dead already is on the
detah bed.
