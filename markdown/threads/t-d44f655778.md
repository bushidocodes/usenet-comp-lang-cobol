The only way to display things to the screen in MS-DOS is to DISPLAY
them. Under Unix, with everything being files, you can just print to
/dev/tty??, as you have found out. But DOS is different. ;)

A lot of people have solved this problem by just printing to a file, and
then writing a program to read a print file and display it to the screen.
If you make this general enough, it will work just fine under Unix as
well, and then you don't have different programs under different
environments. Just a thought.
