Everyone,

I have the problem to write a DATASET of variable length in ASCII on
an IBM-Host in a COBOL-Program.
Seams to be easy:
I put    ALPHABET ALPHABET-NAME IS STANDARD-1 to the  SPECIAL-NAMES.
I put    FD DS-OUT
            RECORDING MODE IS V
            BLOCK CONTAINS 0 RECORDS
            CODE-SET IS ALPHABET-NAME
            RECORD VARYING DEPENDING ON DS-OUT-LENGTH.
         01 DS-OUT-RECORD.
            05 DS-RECORD  PIC X(1)  OCCURS 9  TO 9000
                                    DEPENDING ON DS-OUT-LENGTH.

I put    the DCB-parameter OPTCD=Q in the JCL

but I get file-status 90 or 39 at the open depending on diferent
changes of the JCL.

What's my mistake?
Thank you for your assistance.

G.Chalupka
```

In the FD, try "RECORD IS VARYING IN SIZE FROM 9 TO 9000 CHARACTERS
DEPENDING ON DS-OUT-LENGTH".

Define the 01 level as DS-OUT-RECORD PIC X(9000).

In WS, define DS-OUT-LENGTH as PIC 9(09) BINARY. Make sure it's
unsigned.

Move the length that you want to write to DS-OUT-LENGTH prior to the
actual WRITE instruction.

The length you had specified in DS-OUT-LENGTH will be the length of
the record that was written.

Bill
```
