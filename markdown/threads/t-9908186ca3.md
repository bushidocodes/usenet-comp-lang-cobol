> The external drive is plugged into a USB port.

If both the Mobikey and the external drive are USB devices they should be 
individually recognised by PnP when you connect them. You should hear a 
sound when they are connected and disconnected and it is OK to connect and 
disconnect them "warm"  (You don't have to power down or reboot).

If you don't hear the audio confirmation (and assuming you have working 
audio on your system) then either the USB hub or PnP are malfunctioning.

Try This:

1. Disconnect both devices. Disconnect your USB cradle.
2. Reboot from power Off (Cold start).
3. Reconnect the External drive, directly to a port on the machine.
    Did you hear an audio acknowledgement? Did you see a pop-up on the task 
bar?
    If no, open up Start>control panel>system>Hardware>attached devices  and 
see if any entries under USB have a little yellow exclamation mark beside 
them. If they do you may need to re-install USB drivers. 
http://www.softwarepatch.com/tips/usb2-driver.html
USB 2 is much smarter than the earlier versions and is worth installing.
If installing USB 2.0 drivers doesn't fix it, and your external disk is 
still unrecognised try this: http://support.microsoft.com/kb/925196

    If you hear the audio confirmation, then attach the Mobikey directly to 
a port on the machine.

4.  Did you hear an audio condirmation that the Mobikey was found by PnP?

If you did, then drives will have been assigned to both devices and your 
problem should be solved. If you didn't, and you have USB 2.0 drivers 
installed (see above) then the problem is probably with PnP. You will have 
to detect the device manually so it can be properly assigned. Mobikey does 
not require anything on your machine other than a working USB port, a 
working Internet connection, and 20 MB of available memory.

I suspect that your problem is with the USB drivers and (re-)installing USB 
2.0 drivers should solve it.
