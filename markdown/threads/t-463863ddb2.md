 Wrong again.  COBOL originates from a language developed by Grace Hopper, and came in use sometimes in the 50ies.  It was designed to provide simple string handling, to be easy to learn, to be highly portable, and to be self-documenting.  Since then it 
has gone through several major revisions, upgrades, etc. She didn't do it single-handed but could be called the mother of the language. Check out these sites:

	http://www.chips.navy.mil/chips/grace_hopper/
	http://www.ccs.org/hc/9612/hopper.html

"Amazing Grace", as she was known, received her Doctorate of Mathematics from Yale University in 1934. She held an Associate Professorship at Vassar until 1943 when she joined the Navy's WAVE (Women Accepted for Voluntary Service) program. Dr. Hopper was
 assigned to work on the Harvard Mark I computer project as its third programmer .Her work with the Mark I convinced her that higher level programming languages were necessary if any real progress were to be made. By 1949 she had joined the Eckert-Mauchl
y Computer Corporation then developing its Univac I computer. By 1952 she had developed and implemented the world's first compiler for a programming language called B. By 1957, this led to the development of Flow-Matic, the direct predecessor to COBOL (C
ommon Business Oriented Language).
