Back in the days where vendors had to prove their software/hardware
solution worked, we performed benchmarks on our mainframes. The
vendors often had to convert the "standard" languages to their
version of the language. On one of the projects I used a program
called BALCO, which converted ibm's BAL to COBOL. This was back
in the 70's. You might try a search for that name, but because
COBOL is a "dead" language, the company may have gone the way of
the dodo. As I recall, the program did work, but the code was not
very maintainable. It performed a 1 to 1 conversion, 1 BAL instruction
to 1 cobol sentence.
