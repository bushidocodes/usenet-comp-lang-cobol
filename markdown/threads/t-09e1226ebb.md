```
Steve,
  Are you looking for any "help" on your problem, or are you able to solve
them (one by one).

Personally, I would be VERY surprised if this is a problem with any specific
release of LE (or OS/390 - z/OS).

There are a couple of things that I can think of that MIGHT impact a
"translation" from DOS/VS COBOL to OS/VS COBOL (which I think is what your
compiler change is).  If you are actually converting between other
compilers, can you tell us from which to which.

Things to look at:

1) What was the LANGLVL setting under VSE?  If it was LANGLVL(1) are you
using LANGLVL(1) or (2) with OS/VS COBOL?  If not, some problems may occur.

2) Are any/all of the "problem" files multi-volume tapes?  I remember there
being some "problems" in the VS COBOL II days of VSE with end-of-volume
processing.  This may WELL cause problems when changing OS.

3) Are any of the problems with VSAM files?  If so, you might want to check
not only the IDCAMS info (to insure they are identical in the 2
environments) - but also whether or not there are DOCUMENTED restrictions
that might have been ignored under VSE, but enforced under z/OS.

***

If you are actually doing a conversion from DOS/VS COBOL to a "currently
supported" compiler under z/OS - which compiler are you using?  There are
LOTS of migration issues there.

If you are staying with OS/VS COBOL, does the program work when STEPLIBing
to the OS/VS COBOL run-time library (or is that what you are using already -
in which case how does it work with the LE run-time library).

Finally, if you are able to "fix" the problems "one by one" - what type of
underlying fixes are you finding?  This may give me (us) an idea of what the
general problem is.
```
