Hmmm....I'm starting to get real cynical on this "programmer shortage"
issue. We interview a lot of people at my shop and there is always some
total BS reason why talented people are passed over. I've been told
"they only know Expeditor and we use Intertest, therefor they aren't
'ready to hit the ground running'". What BS. Or they have been doing
only DB2/COBOL II for the last 5 years - implying that they aren't
capible of learning CICS. So instead of having a proven (ie more
expensive) programmer on board, we'll hire a college grad (ie cheaper)
and put him or her through a 3 month training course and then teach them
on the job for at least another year to get them productive - all
because we don't want to give that experienced programmer a couple weeks
to come up to speed with basic CICS. It just doesn't make sense. Or the
usual - well they just wanted $XXXXX per year and that's as much as
manager's make here! 

So we have teams that are literally running the company that have 4 or 5
people left where 2 years ago there was 20. The project I'm on is
"highest priority" for moving the company forward strategically and
there are 8 people out of an alloted 21 - and two of them have already
accepted offers with other companies. And we are deemed at almost full
staff...It's going to be interesting what type of glass ceiling
corporate america throws up for the year 2000 programmers when they
start to apply back with corporations - I think that there will be quite
a grudge held against them - wrongly so, but it will be there none the
less. I've heard people make comments like 'we'll see what they will be
making back here in a couple of years'.

Seems like there are plenty of people out there - it is a matter of
finding people who will work cheaply enough and long enough that is the
issue. We seem to be much more willing to give an unproven programmer a
chance to learn everything about programming in the real world than to
pay an experienced programmer to come in and learn our environment. Is
this just in my area of the country or is this type of double speak
going on everywhere? 

Steve
