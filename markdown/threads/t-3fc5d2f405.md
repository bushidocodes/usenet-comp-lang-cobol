Howard,

I'll list books below. But you have an initial problem, assuming you are
thinking mainframe. There was a one-page blurb posted saying that Unisys
was 'renting" OO technolgy from Hitachi - but nothing since and no
details. IBM - from what I've gathered so far, the mechanism to write
clases but no pre-packaged classes that give you a kickstart; they pass
you through the gateway to C++ and Smalltalk. See my recent query on
Smalltalk NG  "COBOL and Smalltalk" and the response I got.

To allay one of your concerns, (truly legitimate),the NetExpress source
for ALL classes is available to the developer - you don't need it - but
it gives you a comfort-zone - and of course it gives you ideas on how to
write your own classes.

Now to the books :-

1. objectz.com for Wilson Price's "Elements of OO COBOL" from which I
took my file design (You have my oofiles.zip I believe ? If not mail me
direct and I'll send it). Tutorial style and excellent. (I'm told Will
is currently out there among the Pharisees lecturing to the
unconverted).

2. Arranga & Coyle "OO COBOL" - also excellent - developing step by step
through OO concepts. Pity it didn't have an accompanying disk showing
their source examples.

Both the above used MF COBOL to produce the results. I really fail to
see how you can teach a new topic without examples actually compiled.

3. Useful - Ray Obin's "OO An Introduction for COBOL Programmers" a
paperback quick-reference; he is MF (talk about nepotism <G>) serves on
the standards OO Group. Only available source would be Merant - if still
available.

4. While I'm at it, I'll throw in Adele Goldberg's "Smalltalk-80" - not
for the Smalltalk language per se - but her insight on designing
collections/dictionaries - Merant, (NetExpress)  unashamedly pay
Smalltalk the compliment for their own collection/dictionary classes.

In the Raspberry category :-

4. Doke & Hardgrave "An Introduction to Object COBOL". - Arrived in mail
at 12:00 - finished it 15:00 - sorry guys it didn't tell me a damn thing
that wasn't better covered by 1 and 2. (To be fair to them - they have
also written "COBOL to Java" - looks interesting, but haven't read it in
detail yet).

5. Andrew Topper "OO Development in COBOL". Goes on and on in reams
about theory, lots of diagrams, quasi-code based on that mythical
COBOL-XXX, and quotes 'Standard OO'. Sorry - I'm a meat and potatoes man
- I want concrete examples in source code.

(Wonder if the Standards people, when they meet in Amsterdam next
Spring, might actually get conclusive - perhaps if their Dutch hosts
take them out on the town for Nasi Goreng and a looksee at the
magnificent tulip displays ? Waat sekt U Meneer Frog ?)

Not specifically COBOL - but got the following two as a result of
belonging to one of those damned book clubs for a short while :-

Webster "Pitfalls of OO Development". This gentleman has a computer
without a disk or CD, he doesn't mention data-files at all - Have I lost
it or somethin' - isn't the game all about storage/retrieval of data ?

Coad and Yourdan "OO Design" - YAWwwwwnnnnn . (Ken - you misread me re 
"What did Horace say Winnie ?". You did an excellent job of explaining
in plain Anglo-Saxon Pommie/Ausie speech what this guy Yourdan should
have done - it's no use treating the subject as an abstract - source
'Coad' examples please Mr. Yourdan. (The abstract bit was fine back in
Xerox PARC days when they first invented Simula/Smalltalk).

In my not humble opinion Howard, 1 and 2 will get you flyin'. Who knows,
you might become less "underwhelmed" about OO as a concept <G>

Jimmy, Calgary AB
