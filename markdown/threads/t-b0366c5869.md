[Warning! if you do not have access to a copy of the Fujitsu NetCOBOL
for Windows and Fujitsu PowerCOBOL compilers, the following is not of
interest to you.]

As I am winding my efforts down into the holidays for a well-earned
break, I have been reflecting on the response to the new PowerCOBOL
migration package.

Frankly, it has been disappointing.

>From 436 unique views on the web page for it...
(https://primacomputing.co.nz/PRIMAMetro/PwerCOBMigration.aspx)
... there has been ONE legitimate download. :-)

(There were a couple who lied about stuff on the download form and our
security and update management system (RAV -
https://primacomputing.co.nz/PRIMAMetro/RAV.aspx) stopped their use of
the product after it was installed. I offered to re-instate them if they
corrected the misinformation but no one did...)

I am trying to understand what is wrong here.

436 views indicates there is some interest, but there seems to be a fear
of actually trying it... (It uninstalls cleanly through ADD/REMOVE...)
It even comes with a couple of sample PowerCOBOL projects so you can try
it out before applying it to your own code.

The latest version of this product is really amazing and it makes
converting existing PowerCOBOL projects to .Net really easy. You can
salvage 100% of your existing screens (without having to redraw them)
and you can salvage 100% of your existing COBOL (Scriptlets in
PowerCOBOL). Many aspects of the process that were previously manual are
now automated.

It is also CHEAP. (You can convert 12 screens and the code that drives
them for FREE! After that, it works out at around $5 per screen. An
average small business has around 200 screens, but if you need more we
discount the price. Bear in mind that doing this manually with a
Programmer will price it at around $200 PER SCREEN!)

So, I don't think the price point is the problem...

Maybe it comes down to the sheer complexity of dealing with screens and
COBOL... but the package takes that away. The COBOL in the Scriptlets is
automatically converted to OO COBOL Classes for running as objects in
the .NET environment. You don't need to be an OO COBOL programmer. The
screens from PowerCOBOL are analyzed, and all the controls on them are
replicated into the .Net environment with their own VS project for each
screen. All of the event wiring from PowerCOBOL is wired into each new
screen automatically.

Yesterday, I took a PowerCOBOL project with 16 quite complex (accounting
system) screens and moved it to .Net in around 40 minutes; I reckon the
time to do this manually would be at least 4 man/days.

So, the capability of the package doesn't seem to be a sticking point.

Maybe the COBOL boat has sailed and all the people who I thought could
be up for this have moved (or are currently moving) to alternative
solutions.

Maybe I haven't done enough to publicize it. I'll try harder in the new
year... :-)

Nevertheless, in the new year, I plan to provide a capability to replace
all of the existing PowerCOBOL grid-style controls (List View, List Box,
and Table) with the newly released Windows DataGridView Control. Doing
this will provide facilities that are simply not available under
PowerCOBOL without a Custom control (Data Binding, for one...) AND we'll
be looking at 64-bit COBOL code-behinds which are certainly not
available with PowerCOBOL currently.

If you know anybody who is using PowerCOBOL, please, do them (and us) a
favour and let them know this package exists :-)

Pete.
-- I used to write COBOL; now I can do anything...
