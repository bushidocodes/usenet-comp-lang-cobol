LoreKeeper:

The answer is yes on the multiple platform support question. Many of
the PC compilers support both DOS as well as Windows 32 bit platforms
(Win 95/NT). Most of them still support the 16 bit Windows platform
(Windows 3.1/3.11).

I know that the older Micro Focus and CA-Realia COBOL compilers
supported MS-DOS, Windows 16 Bit and OS/2 with just a single compiler.
Perhaps my pals at MF & CA could confirm whether their recent
offerings support MS-DOS as well as the 32 bit Windows environments in
a single compiler package.

mbp Visual COBOL (available from Micro Focus) has DOS and 32 bit
Windows compilers. You may have to purchase more than one compiler.

I believe that Acucobol & Ryan McFarland COBOL, still support the DOS
& 32 bit Windows environments. Both of those products support object
level portability, which means that you can buy a single compiler and
compile in your preferred development environment and then license
runtime systems from them to run in whatever other environments for
which you require support.

The ICOBOL compiler from Egan Systems does have an MS-DOS and a 32 bit
Windows version as well.

As far as Visual Age COBOL from IBM goes, I'm not sure if they have an
MS-DOS version. I peered into my official crystal ball from Doc Dwarf
to find out and all I got is a blue haze on that one (a "big" blue
haze). Come to think of it, that's all I ever get from that crystal
ball! Perhaps one of our Big Blue Buddies can offer some advice here.

Regarding the DB2 question, I doubt very seriously if you'll find an
MS-DOS compiler that allows you to also access DB2 data. You'll
probably have to settle for the 32 bit Windows environment to access
DB2 data.

I know that Micro Focus and CA-Realia has ODBC driver support which
may provide access to DB2 data. I also know that both compilers
support XDB which is a PC based DB2 DBMS. (Did Micro Focus recently
acquire DB2? I heard that rumor.........)

The Flexus COBOL page has links to all the compiler company home
pages. You're always welcome to drop by our home page, but make sure
that you wipe your feet on the mat before entering!
