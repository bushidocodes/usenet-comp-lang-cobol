Unfortunately you take a very American view of the rest of
the world and make an assumption that all cultures use
the same symbols and conventions.  Thus you feel that this
symbolism is 'universal' in a way that English is not.

Even in such simple things as representation of numbers
there is no 'universal' representation:

        1.025,65      1,025.65

With Cobol, though the words are English, they can be easily
understood with any standard dictionary giving equivalent
words.  Now where can I fnd a 'standard text' that will
describe '~' as meaning 'logical NOT' in any language, and
this assumes that the character '~' is not only part of
the langage but is also on their keyboards and screens.
Many European languages, let alone Asian ones, substitute
their own character sets and this may replace symbols.

Using a restricted character set (such as using words
rather than hen scratchings) ensures that as wide as
possible numbers of computers and people use a common
representation.  Your assertion may be true of non-English
speakers in America, but, as usual, the rest of the 
world remains a mystery to you.
