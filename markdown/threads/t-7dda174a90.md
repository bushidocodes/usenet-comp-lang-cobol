COBOL is _the_ language of the workforce. recent IBM/IDC study estimates
5 million programmers and 59 billion lines of code.

and yes, of course, COBOL has been updated in recent years. this usually
happens in 2 ways. one is by committee thru' ANSI (eg. 74/85/89). ANSI
(together with compiler vendors) is working on the 97 revision which will
add object-orientation (and other things) to the language. some vendors
have already implemented various cuts of the 97 draft in their compilers
(eg. Micro Focus released a 32-bit Object COBOL compiler in JAN 95).

the other is by vendors of COBOL compilers (eg. Ryan McFarland, AcuCOBOL,
Micro Focus, IBM, CA etc) who deliver their own sets of extensions with
each new product release. much of this syntax is eventually incorporated
into the next ANSI standard (in one form or another).
