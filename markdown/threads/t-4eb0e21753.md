I have recently attended a one week seminar on IBMs Visual Age for
COBOL. I found it to be an exciting new product that addresses cross
platform needs. Specifically, you can write OO COBOL on an OS/2
workstation and upload the source to an MVS mainframe, recompile and
you're on your way. The new IBM OOCOBOL makes use of a direct to SOM
compiler that elimates the need for SOM IDL and significantly simplifies
the OO process across unlike platforms (AIX, MVS, NT, W95, AS/400, OS/2).

The product is in it's infancy so there are some things to be worked
out...but hey, isn't that always the case?

The product is not being positioned as a mainframe emulator. IBM is
working at establishing connectivity across unlike platforms, not trying
to make them all look the same.
