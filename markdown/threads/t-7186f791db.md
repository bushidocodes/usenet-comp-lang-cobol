 Open Letter to Dept of Labor to Protect workers rights
 ======================================
 Alexis Herman
 Secretary of Labor
 U.S DOL
 200 constitution Ave, NW,  Room S-1032
 Washington, DC, 20210

 Sub: Loss of US jobs to fraudulent Immigrant workers, Immediate action
requested by the Department of Labor to comprehensively investigate and
prosecute companies engaged in illegal H1-B trade.

 In a congressional testimony to judiciary subcommittee on Immigration
chaired by Congressmen Lamar  Smith, INS Deputy executive commissioner
Bill Yates testified on the H1-B fraud schemes prevalent in the
industry.

 Bill Yates testified that INS service centers couldn�t authenticate the
claims made by H1-B applicants regarding their education or experience.
He also testified that over 45 % of all the credentials were outright
fraudulent.

 Many US companies are engaged in the illegal trade of bringing in
unqualified H1-B immigrants and training  them in marketable job skills.
H1-B Immigrants are then placed with US companies as contract workers.
Many of the unqualified H1-B Immigrants are underpaid and abused. Many
major US daily�s newspapers ran several articles on this issue.

 Some H1-B dependent companies are also outsourcing H1-B immigrants to
non-technical jobs as contract  workers. No Immigrant will ever complain
to authorities over the abuse on the fear of being fired from the job.
 H-1B dependent companies are also Illegally engaged in obtaining fixed
priced, time and material contract at a  lower cost using underpaid
Immigrants. This hurts competition and bottom line of companies hiring
American
 workers.

 Congressmen Lamar Smith himself wrote a letter to the acting INS
director to address the issue, INS is  struggling with the law which
does got provide a clear direction on what should be the qualifications
of an H1-B  applicant.
 Bill Yates in this testimony conceded that its difficult for INS to
verity the credentials as they originate from foreign sources and that
the format or form of submission by foreign business and schools is not
 standardized.  INS relies on American consulate personnel as the means
of verification.

 Even American consulates are handicapped due to the prevalence of
corruption and lack of standardized information in overseas countries.
American Consulate in Chennai -India relies on a computerized database
 of employees of major software and technology firms in India to verify
and authenticate experience.

 Fraudulent companies are getting around these using payoffs to HR
officials at major overseas companies to  enter the names of H1-B
applicants into the database.

 H1-B fraud hurts American workers, especially African Americans, other
minorities and new college graduates.

 We request that Department of Labor launch a comprehensive
Investigation of companies engaged in  defrauding American workers using
the H1-B program. Lack of any administrative action and monitoring is
 also encouraging companies to illegally profit from the program.

 We also request Department of Labor to set guidelines and qualification
standards for Immigrant workers and
 raise the issue with INS.


 Sincerely,



 An American Worker

This makes sense. Now I can answer.

Missusing a TIME function for a random generator is possible -
in principle, in any programming language. It depends on
details of the algorithms calling this self-made random
function.

PGP, for instance, uses the times of keystrokes of the user
in order to generate a new random pair of private/public key.

But one has to know what one does. If one needs a huge amount
of random numbers fast and thusly calls TIME many times
within a short time interval, the return values of this calls
are certainly correlated - and are anything else than random.
(This again applies to any programming language) I am
not surprised that your program failed what you expected
it to do.

Look into any textbook about the subject. What you want is a
"linear congruent method"-generator. In certain cases -
cryptography for example - DO NOT rely on a random generator
which any programming language provides! Write your own
random generator.

That, again, is possible in any programming language.
