```
The situation seems to be very similar in the UK. I am a freelance
COBOL/CICS/DB2 analyst/programmer and have managed to keep working in these
difficult market conditions, but a number of my ex-colleagues have been out of
work for months now as the numerous Y2K and COBOL1 - COBOL2/370 conversions
finish. Pundits are saying the market will recover next year, but I am of the
opinion that the main thrust of new development will involve e:commerce or
client/server development. I still feel the mainframe has a lot to offer -
companies have invested billions of pounds worldwide in this technology - but
we will need to look at developing new skills to remain competitive in the
labour market. I do feel the freelance market will shrink significantly as this
shift occurs.
```
