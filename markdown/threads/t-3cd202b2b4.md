Report on December 5-9, 1994 ANSI X3J4 COBOL Committee meeting

Most of this meeting was spent discussing proposals for the next
revision. Our present timetable requires that all major proposals be
complete by February 1995 (formerly August 1994). After that, we will
start the ISO review cycles (first review in March '95), culminating in
a new standard in 1997 (or so we hope). Anyone who wants to see any of
these proposals or the draft standard can contact me. None of them are
on-line or in any generally accessible electronic form, so a paper copy
is all I can provide.

The following proposals were passed by letter ballot before or at this
meeting: Common exception processing and Object-Oriented COBOL. These
will be slightly revised during discussion of the letter ballots.

The major topics/proposals we discussed were:

1. A new revision of the base document (the third) was distributed to
the Committee. We expect to have a new revision a couple of weeks
before every meeting (except that there will not be one before the
January meeting). The latest revision includes about 25 proposals that
have passed, including MOCS (multi-octet character set), standard
arithmetic, bit/boolean, free format source, and lots of other fairly
minor items. We had a special editing meeting the Sunday before the
regular meeting. Several minor problems in the document were found and
some assignments for writing missing parts and fixing parts that don't
conform to the ISO rules were made. There is a great deal of this kind
of work that needs to be done. In addition, the committee has hired an
additional editor to do the updating of the document and is having
problems getting funding for this effort.

2. As a result of reviews of passed proposals and letter ballots,
several are now ready for addition to the base document: Common
exception handling, Standard arithmetic concepts (the description of how
it works - the main proposal - is already in the base document),
compiler directives, and dynamic tables.

4. We spent quite a bit of time discussing the letter ballot comments
on my exception processing proposal. No major changes were made, and it
was felt that the proposal is now compete and it was passed unanimously
(I have spent 16 years working on this proposal - huzzah, it is done!).
It will be added to the next revision of the base document.

4. The Object-oriented COBOL proposal and the comments from the last
letter ballot (failed 8-4) were discussed for a day (the OO COBOL group
met in parallel with the main group for another day). Two of the
negative votes that resulted from the last letter ballot were changed to
yes, and the proposal passed. A rewrite will be done and checked before
it is added to the base document. Most of changes made to this version
are minor clean-up. The primary concern of the two members (CA and
Wang) who voted against the proposal is the complexity of the facility.
They feel it should be simpler, should not have multiple inheritance,
and so on.

5. Some minor topics: the chairman's current term is up and he has
reapplied for another term; the issue of DISPLAY referencing characters
that the hardware won't support was discussed and it was felt that it
should be left implementor-defined; putting in-line comments into
fixed-form format (they are only allowed for free-form format) was
discussed, but no decision was made; the proposal for user-defined types
was discussed at length and lots of major changes were made (there were
lots of problems with it, mainly how strong should we make the typing) -
it was sent back to the author for rewrite; comments on CIB (COBOL
Information Bulletin) 28 were discussed and some changes were suggested
- it will be revised; and the ACCEPT/DISPLAY enhancements for screen
processing was discussed and will be rewritten.

The next meeting is in Seattle, Washington, on January 30 to February 3
1995. The main topics will probably be: object-oriented COBOL (review
changes to the proposal and have it added to the base document) and
preparation of the first review draft.
