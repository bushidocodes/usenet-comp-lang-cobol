```
First you will need a copy of the IBM MVS DATA AREAS manuals (5 volume
set).

I will assume that you are in TCB mode and not SRB mode. All you need
to do is get to the PSA. Get the pointer to the current TCB (PSATNEW as
I recall), then get the TCBTIOT pointer to get to the TIOT. The first 8
characters of the TIOT contains your JOBNAME.

PSA is obtained by setting the pointer to x'00000000'. Beware, with
z/ARCHITECTURE PSA's structure has changed. So what will work on a
machine in 31bit mode (S/390 architecture) may not work under
z/ARCHITECTURE (64 bit mode).

Later,
Steve.T
```

```
<gwlemyre@earthlink.net> wrote :

 [asking how to determine JOBNAME from a COBOL program]

It's pretty easy.  Just call the JOBINFO intrinsic and pass an itemnum of 2.
The corresponding item will return the value of the job name, left justified
in an 8-byte field, and padded with spaces.

Or call the HPCIGETVAR intrinsic and retrieve the value of the session
variable HPJOBNAME.

> I know it can be done but cannot remember how.  I suspect that
> additional data is available beyond just the JOBNAME.

You bet!  Using  either JOBINFO or HPCIGETVAR, you can get a ton of
information about your job or session..
```
