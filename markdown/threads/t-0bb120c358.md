Slow down there, pal, the green card is a ways off. You start with a
visa. H1-B seems to be the preferred route these days. That lets you
into the country to work provided that you have a skill that is in
demand. Not a hard case to make in the IS industry these days.

The nice thing about H1 is that it's multi-entry. That means that you
can visit your friends up north and still get back to your job. I had a
friend from China that didn't realize the difference and got stranded in
Canada after we hopped across the border to see Niagra Falls. We mounted
a clandestine rescue operation sneak him back.

Most consulting companies will set this up for you, but since you are a
fresh grad, you may have a bit of trouble getting someone to bite (most
consulting companies like to see a couple years experience.) You might
try some of the big firms that are overextended, like Keane or EDS.

Best of luck!

Wait, wait wait! It's a TN1 visa you need - NOT an H1b -- IF you are
a Canandian Citizen -- oodles of websites exist with details, mainly
provided by US/Canadian law firms, hoping for business.

Essentially, you need a letter from a U.S. employer, specify job title
(not just any title but those approved and specified in NAFTA -- e.g.
"consultant" won't get you across the border, but "Computer Systems
Analyst" will. The letter must also state salary level, what you will
be doing, and so on. The TN1 visa process is fast and simple -- H1b
process considerably less so.

As for first jobs? You may want to get on board with some of the
myriad consulting firms who have training programs. My beau, a
non-computer science major, went for a grueling Y2K ten week program
at one of the world's largest consulting firms, CSC. The salary was
more than decent versus what he was earning as a recent master-level
grad of environmental engineering.

Computer World has had several articles on these sorts of programs --
go to their website at www.computerworld.com to do the research. I
think Arthur Anderson has a program as does Computer Task Group --
email me private for my buddie's name there that recruits for "SEDP".


Good luck, best wishes,

Carmela
