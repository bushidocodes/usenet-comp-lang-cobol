With OnNet I have to go into properties and the go to file transfer and set the

translation, and control charcter.  After this is done I go to option 6 on the
TSO menu (I'm assuming here that most TSO menus are the same from what I've
heard) and then on the top of the window there is an option for services
(send/receive) which I click on this is from the PC point of view so you'll be
receiving.

ie c:/myfile.txt - or whatever I want to call it, then in the next box I key in
the mainframe file - 'UPPER CASE AND IN QUOTES'.  At the bottom of the box it
has several options and I select TSO - the hit the receive button a there's my
file on the PC.  On the TSO screen is the IND$FILE command. I suppose you could
copy it down for future reference.

Attachmate wasn't quite as complicated. In this case I got out of ISPF (to the
ready prompt) before going to the send/receive at the top of the window.  The
box that came up was pretty much the same as the OnNet box and I filled it in

The "[" and "]" problem go way back to some management decision
at IBM concerning VTAM (so I was told by VTAM L2).  What happens
is, you have to tell ISPF that you are using a "text" terminal
type (3276T?).  This causes a different translate table to get
loaded.  Then, you may have to put a second translate table in
for the LUs that you have defined (or was it PUs?).  If you check
IBM Link on this, you may find an old APAR for this.  Why all the
translate tables?  I think it was to keep from sending
non-displayable, control characters to a 3270 type device (if you
did this you would get different errors such as the ~470 [where ~
is the lightening bolt]).

I had forgotten what this was all about, as I first ran into this
back in 1990/91 while doing work for IBM at STL (south of San
Jose CA).  There we were doing upload/download of ASCII/ECDIC
data while working on some programming languages/environments.  I
ran into it again through the mid 90s while dealing with text and
C/C++ (I was the sysprog who had to figure things out and make it
work).

At any rate, QWS has handled things properly every time I've used
it.  In fact, I would use QWS to test 3270 data streams if I
didn't have a real 3270 available (until I could get my hands on
one), as I knew that Jim Rymerson faithfully tested his stuff to
match the 3270 specs.

And as another poster said they were doing, I had my PC emulating
a 3270 Keyboard so that when I went to a real one, I wasn't
having to look at the keys (I'm a touch typist -- I hate
rodents!).  

Lastly, you do not have to get out of ISPF to do data xfer.  I
found that if you did your xfers using =6 (TSO Command panel),
that ISPF would graciously get out of the way and resume control
when things were done.
