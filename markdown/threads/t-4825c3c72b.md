```
Replacement is only done by 'Cobol word'. The prefix 'ws' is not a
'word'. It is necessary to construct the copybook with words.

 01 (ws)-data
      02  (ws)-name                 pic x(40)
      02  (ws)-addr                   pic x(200).
      02  (ws)-addr-parsed        redefines (ws)-addr.


       copy  addrpars       replacing ==(ws)== by ==xx==.

Note also that the replacing and by are swapped and the apostrophes
removed.
```

```
So what you _actually_ want is a nested COPY, a COPY within a
copybook. No, you probably can't do that. Some compilers may allow it,
but it is not standard.

replacing =='xx'== by ==ws==.

You wouldn't want 'ws'-addr-line.. in the output, just ws-addr-line..
```

```
Nested COPYs ae allowed by the 2002 Standard, but only when REPLACING is not used.

7.1.2.3.14
If the REPLACING phrase is not specified, the library text may contain a COPY statement
that does not include a REPLACING phrase. The implementation shall support nesting of at
least 5 levels, including the first COPY statement in the sequence. The library text being
copied shall not cause the processing of a COPY statement that directly or indirectly
copies itself.

eowyn: 

01 ws-data
     02  ws-name                 pic x(40)
     02  ws-addr.
          03  ws-addr-line occurs 5 pic x(40).
```
