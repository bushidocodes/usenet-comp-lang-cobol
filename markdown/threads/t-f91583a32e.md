Most will probably know this fix I discovered yesterday. But for those that 
don't, well. . .

The problem is found in column widths in ListView and other boxes under 
PowerCobol. When the user punches the "full screen" button, the column 
widths get all wonky and do not enlarge proportionally.

Here's the fix.
A. Find the width of the entire form at normal screen mode (say, 10,000)
B. Find the width of each column (say 1000, 2000, and 4000).
C. Compute the ratio/percentage of each column's width compared to the total 
width of the form. (0.10, 0.20, and 0.40)
D. At the form event "Resized" drop in the following code:

   COMPUTE WS-WIDTH = POW-CLIENTWIDTH * 0.10.
   MOVE WS-WIDTH TO 'WIDTH' OF COLUMNS(1) OF ListView-POS.

   COMPUTE WS-WIDTH = POW-CLIENTWIDTH * 0.20.
   MOVE WS-WIDTH TO 'WIDTH' OF COLUMNS(2) OF ListView-POS.

    etc.

My users are so deliriously happy they want to have my babies! (Regrettably, 
by IV fertilization.)

After almost 11 years using this product, I'm still learning/discovering new 
things.

Meanwhile, I discovered how to actually DO multi-select in a listview box. 
Here's the trick (of course the LV box has to be flagged as multi-select) 
showing how to delete multiple, selected, items:

   MOVE "Count" OF LV-BOX TO WSCOUNT.
   PERFORM VARYING WSINDX FROM 1 BY 1 UNTIL WSINDX > WSCOUNT
    IF 'Selected' OF "ListItems"(WSINDX) OF LV-BOX = POW-TRUE
     INVOKE LV-BOX 'Delete' USING WSINDX
     SUBTRACT 1 FROM WSINDX
     MOVE 'Count' OF LV-BOX TO WSCOUNT
    END-IF
   END-PERFORM.
   IF WSCOUNT > 0
     INVOKE 'ListItems'(1) OF LV-BOX "EnsureVisible"
  END-IF.

The "IF" statement above contains the technique you won't find explicitly in 
any of the manuals, help files, or sample programs.

Ah, you have come to the same conclusion I did; don't ask them for help, do

I haven't personally used PowerCOBOL for quite a while now but many years 
ago I wrote a paper on using the ListView with it (several people had 
written to me saying they were having problems with it). This paper was 
later translated into Spanish and Portuguese (not by me :-)) and I believe 
it is still available on some South American websites.

I'll have a look across some old laptops and see if I can find the original 
article.

As I recall, I tumbled to the fact that the PowerCOBOL ListView control uses 
the POW-NODE structure to populate itself. Rather than addressing the 
ListView directly, I did all the manipulation using the underlying Node 
structure (which is a collection with certain useful functions built into 
it.) Consequently I never had problems with adding, changing, or deleting, 
single or multi selected items.

I realise now (after using C# for a few years) that a decent control has 
separate collections for what is selected and what is not. This just makes 
everything really simple without the need to look at or maintain counts or 
indexes. (You just iterate over the collection you want with a simple 
"foreach")

I still receive a few requests each month for help with PowerCOBOL from 
people who share your frustration with Alchemy's non-support of it. 
(Although their web site shows it as a proud member of their product set, I 
think their focus is elsewhere... ) The fact is that people who are 
committed to PowerCOBOL are probably going down a dead end through no fault 
of their own. I used it for years and was very impressed with it, but I 
wouldn't go back to it after using .Net.

I have a client at the moment whose entire business is based on PowerCOBOL. 
Currently they are moving all their indexed data to SQL Server and have used 
the PRIMA Migration Toolset to do this.  You can imagine what a job it is 
with hundreds of PowerCOBOL projects to go through and Transform every 
scriptlet that uses ISAM, into using invokes of the Data Access Layer. 
Nevertheless the Toolset identifies and strips out the scriptlets, changes 
the code and then places the changed scriptlets into a schedule for pasting 
back in the project. (The whole transformation process for them takes a 
couple of hours.)

I am really annoyed that I can't just update the project files. It is a 
proprietary format and not documented. The PowerCOB program accepts certain 
parameters for building and rebuilding projects, but not for editing. You 
can't call PowerCOB and update a scriptlet or even look at a scriptlet. (As 
far as I can tell; if anybody reading this knows differently PLEASE let us 
know...)

I'm too busy at the moment to investigate this further and we can live with 
pasting the scriptlets back, but I haven't given up on it yet...

PowerCOBOL is a perfect example of good software that got overtaken by 
events and is no longer of interest to the people who sell it.
