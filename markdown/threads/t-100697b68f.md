Yes, I've seen a number of these stories. I'm not suggesting that it is OK
for us to pollute the planet with plastic :-)

But there is a resilience in Nature which we sometimes overlook.

In the last 100 years the entire biosphere (including ourselves) has been
subject to such rapid changes that evolution is kind of gasping to keep up.
Changes to the environment which might normally take hundreds, even
thousands, of years to happen have happened "overnight".

I haven't yet quite reached the stage of making a tinfoil hat, but the fact
remains that we are bombarded every second by electromagnetic radiation
(cell phones, radio, tv, WiFi, etc) and we really don't know if there is any
long term harmful effect from this. Given that our bodies are fundamentally
made of "electricity" it isn't such a far reach to believe there could be an
effect.
I know people who sold their house because it was close to a major overhead
electricity supply line and they told me that compasses went crazy in the
vicinity of their house due to the induced fields from the flow.

Given time, I believe everything can adapt.

The trouble is that the current rate of change is so fast that it is
overwhelming our capacity to absorb it.

It's no wonder we are not feeling so good... :-)

Pete.
