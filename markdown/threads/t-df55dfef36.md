doesn't always happen proves nothing.

I know several that it did happen to, and that it happens proves it happens.

I also know one person who has a relative in the USA on an H1B for computers
that does not have a 4 year degree in a computer related field, and the
relative did not do any falsification.  So in at least one case, the
educational credentials were falsified, presumably by the agency.  I have
read that this is not infrequent.

This doesn't mean we should or should not have H1Bs.  I have no opinion on
that.

It means *some* agencies and/or sponsors are commiting fraud and they should
be stopped.

I can tell you that the popular press in India is unhappy with that
countries top brains being drained off.  India is not a wealthy country and
it cannot afford to subsidize the educations of so many people who are just
going to leave.  It is a popular topic in their papers apparently.

But the way things stand, many of those top brains are going to get good
training and experience, and then return home.

I'd like to see everyone, American, Indian and anything else, treated fairly
and with respect.
