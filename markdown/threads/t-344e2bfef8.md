Thank you, Bill, that was fast action.

When I got the same message, I sent the following to NCITS and the
appropriate mailing lists:

---------------------------------------------
Ever since this issue of ISO/IEC "copyright" of new standards came up,
the J4 committee has resisted.  Thus far, we have always gotten drafts
available to the public, but now  the only copy is password protected.
If this restriction is not removed promptly, I intend to file criminal
charges of Theft of Intellectual Property with the United States
Department of Justice against ISO/IEC and NCITS.
This charge will allege:
1. Parts of the standard are based on already copyrighted material, as
indicated in the draft standard, itself.  This copyrighted material
has been made available to the industry. Without releases from these
copyright holders, an attempt to copyright the draft standard
infringes on these existing copyrights.
2. I, and many (all?) other current and previous members of the J4
Committee have contributed our work product with the understanding
that it was for the benefit of our profession, not for the
remuneration of the standards bodies.  Not only did we not receive
payment, but in fact we paid dues and meeting fees and documentation
fees for the "privelege" of being members.  In fact, we paid these
dues and fees, and contributed our time and expertise,  as our
contribution to the well being of our profession.  The attempt to
copyright the draft standard, and to restrict access to it from the
general public, is theft of our work product, which is intellectual
property as defined by the statute and applicable treaties.
3. The J4 Committee has never acquiesced to the plans to copyright the
COBOL standard.  We have proceeded on the hope (understanding?) that
"things would be worked out".  I, myself, have repeatedly stated my
intention to file these charges if "things did not work out".

I hope that my fellow members of the committee, both individually and
corporately, as well as former members and the general public, will
join me in this action.
