Maybe I am being over analytical but starting with the very first
question the survey is ambiguous. What is the context of this survey?
Are you trying to find out how programmers approach business programming and work?
Are you trying to find out how programmers approach general life decisions?
Are you trying to find out if programmers approach life decisions differently
than non-programmers?

In my experience, rational thought is the only realistic basis for making decisions.

In a business programming development work context the answer is yes.
In life decisions such as who should I marry or what church should I go to the answer is no.
In a general decision like what car should I buy the answer is maybe.

You need to frame your survey better.

You are correct. Current research is looking at how cognitive style may
differ depending on the task at hand. The instrument that I'm using is
well-known, well-tested, and mature. However, its developers view
cognitive style as a unitary dimension, whereas other researchers
believe that it is multi-dimensional, and requires a much more complex
framework (and more complex and lengthy tests). I chose to use a
unitary instrument in this study for simplicity's sake. I couldn't
imagine that someone would spend more than a few minutes to complete a
test for someone they don't even know. Maybe I was wrong?
