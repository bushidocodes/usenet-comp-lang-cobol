Arno,

Much of what you need to know will be dependent on the specific flavor
of COBOL and mainframe platform you are targeting for. As someone else
pointed out, one of the main stumbling block is EBCDIC vs UniCode or
ASCII. Another is Little endian versus big endian numeric
representations.

The easiest thing you could do, would be to output an ASCII text file,
with fixed length fields and records. No field delimiters are necessary
(like no hex 00 at the end of a string as in C). Then have someone on
the mainframe side write a batch program to read the ASCII data and
properly convert it to what the mainframe apps expect.

The first thing that I think of:
COBOL compilers and mainframes generally work with EBCDIC characters.
PC-based processors use ASCII or UNICODE (maybe others, I don't know
much about this side). Our site uses a SYBASE product that makes the
translation for us. I would guess there are a number of products that do
this, either as a small portion of the product or as the main function
of the product.

Just roughly, comparing COBOL and Java, PIC X(..) is like 'String' or
'Char', PIC 9(..) is like 'int', and all other stuff I don't know much
about.

John
