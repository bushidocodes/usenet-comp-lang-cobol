### Try New Things From Time to Time

- **From:** "Jerry P" <jerryp@bisusa.com>
- **Date:** 2001-07-22T23:45:29+00:00
- **Newsgroups:** comp.lang.cobol
- **Message-ID:** `<tAJ67.8270$Xn.949932@newsread1.prod.itd.earthlink.net>`

```
I tried turning on the "COUNT" option in Fujitsu's compiler. This
creates a list of every verb and statement in the program along with
hit counters and percentages. The resulting tabulation purports to
show the internal activity of the program with an eye toward
fine-tuning - that is, the report shows the programmer where the
program spends its time.

Compiled the program (a large DLL), began execution, and exited right
away just to see what I'd get.

The resulting text file was 1.2 megabytes in size; over 500 printed
pages (of fine print, I might add). While I haven't studied the report
in depth (I'm having trouble lifting it), the report may very well
tell me more than I want to know.

After several hours of debugging another problem, I discovered that
turning on this COUNT feature caused an exception violation in the
upstream calling program.

But what's art without suffering, eh?

The "TRACE" option I'll leave for anon.
```
