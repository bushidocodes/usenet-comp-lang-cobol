```
I'm in a professional dilemma....can I ask the citizens of this NG for their
input.
I have a choice of two contract assignments when my contract with my present
employer terminates at the end of March '98. My basic skillset is COBOL2,
DB2, IDMS, MVS etc etc, I'm almost 34, and have worked in the
telecommunictions IT sector since 1983, and live just outside London, UK

1 - Stay with my current employer (telecommunications industry) for another
12 month contract - where I am a fairly senior developer in a 20 strong
team. Get retraining and development experience in TI's COMPOSER CASE-tool
(now actually Sterling Software COOL:GEN), with which we are doing some
process re-engineering / new development work. Pay rates are below
prevalent market rate but are more than adequate to live on (wife also works
part time, small baby too). Job is local - 6 miles from home (car or cycle
to work).
OR
2 - Take a new analyst/programmer contract with a reknowned European bank in
the city of London. Use the same skillset as I presently have. Do some Y2K
work, some EMU (for our non UK friends = European Monetary Union -
effectively a new parallel currency to UK pounds), and gain
financial/banking experience. Most importantly, pay rate is 30% *more* than
I currently get. Would need to commute - using around 5% of that 30%
increase.

Which one should I take. which one would you take ?? Your votes / advice
please.
```

```
Having done the long commute bit for quite a while. I would not recommend the
commute. I was spending approx 15 to 20 hours per week driving to/from work. I
don't have any small children, but if I did I would not want to trade missing
significant parts of their early years for time on the road. If you are
commuting long distances, you will miss school and sport functions. You may be
leaving for work while the baby is asleep and returning from work after the baby
has gone back to bed.

The only type of commute I can recommend is a temporary relocation. I did this.
The client put me up in a nice aprtment, gave me a rental car, my wife came with
me and found a good job (as an accountant) . I did this for two years. Made a
lotta dough, had a short commute and got to see another part of the country.

Maybe you can hit your present client up for another 10% to 15% and narrow the
gap. Since you have another offer, you could hold the client up and if they
don't come through, then you leave.

Good luck!!
```
