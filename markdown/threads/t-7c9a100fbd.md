>From the information I have received and what I am aware of, what you see
in the CA-Realia Version 5.x (Cobol Plus and Workbench) is the direction
of this line of products, like most other vendors. For better or worse,
the Windows band-wagon marches on. Version 4.2 was probably the last of
the familiar DOS-based Realia Cobol compilers. Of course, 5.x doesn't
prevent you from creating DOS applications. However, the DOS
applications created with version 5.x require a 286 or higher processor
to run, with a minimum of 1meg of memory. This is because the .exe
requires binding the extended memory manager handler into it.

As for pricing, I don't know if Cobol Plus is more than 4.2. I would
suspect the pricing has not changed (more costly).

mike dodas
