Hi Daniel:

There is a product (hardware) that we use, it is called "KWIK-BAR". This
device is in-line with the printer (includes both serial and parallel ports).
 You send text strings, no escape codes, to select printer type, barcode
type, barcode data, along with your normal data and it will "translate" the
commands to produce your barcodes.

This method works for us because, many times, we don't know what type of
environment (Operating system, COBOL Vendor, printers....) our application
will be running in and this gives us the most flexibility without having to
write "printer drivers."  You can actually program it through your print
jobs.  We just setup parameters that the user can modify (the type of
barcodes, the type of printer....).

It sends the appropriate codes for several different printer types (IBM,
EPSON, HP, Oki).

The name of the company that makes the KWIK-BAR is Intelligent Alternatives,
they are somewhere in Alabama, and the phone number escapes me at this moment.

Not sure if this will work for what you need, but I hope it helps.

Chris Loy
Maxom Holdings, Inc.
