parser and Cobol code generator. That's a lot of work that was already
done for you by Siber Systems.

We just released version 1.2.2 of the CobolTransformer --
C++ library that does all the Cobol parsing and code generation
work for you.

The 1.2.2 (as well as 1.2.1) is mostly a bug fix release.

More info is available at http://www.siber.com/sct

Frequently Asked Questions file is enclosed:


CobolTransformer: Frequently Asked Questions
============================================


Copyright 1996-1997 by Siber Systems Inc.


Q. What the CobolTransformer is?

A. Siber System CobolTransformer (SCT) is a C++ library
that you can use in an application that needs
to parse, transform and generate Cobol code.

The major components of the CobolTransformer library are:

- High quality Cobol Parser capable of parsing the following Cobol
dialects: ANSI-74, ANSI-85, OSVS, VS II, SAA, X/Open, Microsoft,
Microfocus (partial support), Ryan McFarland, DOSVS, UNIVAC.

- Internal Representation for Cobol programs (expression trees) and
C++ library to manipulate this representation.
Header file expr.h that declares Expression Tree is included
with Cbl-Beau distribution.

- PrettyPrint library that transforms our internal representation back into
beautifully indented COBOL program with fully preserved comments.
What's most important, pretty-printing is totally customizable, that is,
you can directly encode your COBOL formatting rules into our
table-driven pretty-printer.

That is, you get a complete and well-designed Cobol compiler toolkit
written in C++ that lets you focus on particulars of your Cobol
reengineering project and not worry about handling complexity of
quality Cobol lexing, parsing, and generating.



Q. What kind of applications you expect to use the CobolTransformer?

A. Any application that needs to parse, transform and generate Cobol.

Examples:

- Year 2000 conversion tool.
It parses and analyzes Cobol program, transforms it, and
generates new (or partially new) program.

- Syntax-aware Cobol editor. It needs a Cobol parser.
Tokens produced by the SCT lexer know their position in the source file,
which is very conveninet for editors and environments.

- Statistics and analysis tools.
Say, the tool that analyzes IO profile of the Cobol application.

- Various DB and screen migration tools.
For instance, Cobol with IMS DB to Cobol with DB/2 converion tool
will need all capabilities of the CobolTransformer.

- Cobol compilers. Yes, CobolTransformer may serve as a frontend
to the Cobol compiler you develop.

- Higher level programming environments that generate Cobol code.
For instance, a version of Delphi that generates Cobol code.
Here CobolTransformer code generation capabilities will come in handy.



Evaluating and Licensing CobolTransformer
=========================================


Q. How do I justify licensing of the CobolTransformer to the accounting?

A. Unlike other more modern languages, Cobol is a huge and complicated language.
Therefore parsing and lexing Cobol is much more complicated than
any other modern language.
Compare: C grammar is about 500 lines, Cobol grammar is about 5,000 lines.

By our estimates, developing Cobol parser on your own will take
anywhere from $150,000 to $300,000 depending on organization.

Licensing CobolTransformer is much cheaper.



Q. Do you have Free Evaluation Copy for CobolTransformer?

A. Yes, we do. The Free Evaluation Program is called Cbl-Beau.
It is not really a copy of CobolTransformer distribution, because
the distribution includes full source of the CobolTransformer.

It is an executable program that beautifies your Cobol program,
that is, pretty-prints it with beautiful indentation.

Using Cbl-Beau, you can test CobolTransformer parser technology
with you Cobol codes. The reasoning is simple: if CobolTransformer
can beautify your codes, it can parse them. Also it demonstrates quality
of another major component of the CobolTransformer: Cobol code generator.

Cbl-Beau is available as an executable for Win32 (Windows 95, Windows NT),
Linux, SunOS and Solaris operating systems from http://www.siber.com/sct/

Q. What are the licensing terms?

The terms of the license are subject to change but currently
they are as follows:

- You can use CobolTransformer (SCT) to develop your converters
and other products, as long as one developer works with SCT.

That is, if you sell application based on SCT, it should provide

Important Techical Details
==========================

Q. Tell me more about the CobolTransformer Cobol parser.

A. It is written in BackTracking Yacc and C++.
BackTracking Yacc is freely avaliable from http://www.siber.com/sct
It is a regular Berkeley Yacc with modifications
by Chris Dodd and by Siber Systems .

The most important improvenebt for us is that BtYacc may backtrack
and thus parse languages that require lookahead of more than 1.
Cobol is one of these languages.

Our Cobol grammar covers 12 Cobol dialects.
It is 5,500 lines long.

The other major component of the parser is the Lexical analyzer.
It has 2,400 lines of C++.




Q. Do you fully implement COPY, REPLACE and -INC statements?

A. Yes. Unlike some other Cobol parsers out there, CobolTransformer
fully implements COPY, REPLACE and -INC statements exactly at
they are desribed in the standards.

Specifically:
- You can have multiple levels of COPY, REPLACE and -INC statements.
- Word-part matching as well as whole word matching works to the spec.



Q. What if I don't have copylibs/copybooks? Will you still parse my program?

A. Yes. From the formal point of view, not having copylib file breaks the
pasring process and most parsers just refuse to do anything useful if this
happens.

The CobolTransformer parser can recover from the missing copybook situation.
Moreover, COPY statements are masqueraded into real statements and thus
they can be carried thru the transformation and code generation process.



Q. What about parsing embedded languages such as CICS, SQL?

A. All the contents of EXEC(UTE) ... END-EXEC statement are converted
to space-compressed string that can be submitted to the embedded language
parser.

Currently there are no embedded language parsers avaliable, but
we have plans for SQL and CICS parser.

To compensate for temporary absence of embedded language parsers
(and therefore for temporary inability to transform embedded languages),
we have an option of not preserving original formatting of the embedded language
statement. That is, we do not space compress embedded statements and let them
go thru unchanged.



Q. How CobolTransformer is tested and validated?

A. CobolTransformer correctly parses all the programs in CCVS --
Federal Cobol Compiler test suite by National Institute
of Standards and Technology (NIST).

Note: CobolTransformer was not formally submitted to NIST for testing, because
it is not a full compiler. But we made sure that all the Cobol sources
in CCVS are parsed and beautified correctly.

Also it is regression-tested on more than a million lines of Cobol code.



Q. Is it possible to add support for my favorite Cobol dialect?
You support 12 Cobol dialects, but there are more dialects
than that out there.

A. Most likely, yes. It all depends on how close your dialect is
to what we already support.
