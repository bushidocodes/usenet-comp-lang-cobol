folks:

background:  my colleagues "converted" from VSAM to DB2 by
simply changing meta-names; file->table, record->row, field->
column.  and moving the data, of course (well, sort of; it's
390, and i'm told that storage is really still VSAM).

the issue:  they use "copybooks" which still contain things like
widget001...widget025 in working-storage.  the referenced tables
still have the repeating columns; a referential no-no.  i have
been rebuffed in my attempts to split these situations into
multiple tables by the assertion that there is no way to handle
the resulting variable number of widgets (which, it seems to 
me from my relational perspective is a Good Thing) in a working-
storage declaration.

i find it hard to believe that this situation still persists after
20 years of DB2/COBOL.  i just think i work with a bunch on numbskulls.

is there a reference that describes how to handle this?  rooting
around in the COBOL books at my neighborhood B&N wasn't much help.
the current VSAM book is 1986!!!!!!!!!  the COBOL books only dealt
with DB2 as a source of flat files.  

please, it can't be this stupid.

toodles,
robert
