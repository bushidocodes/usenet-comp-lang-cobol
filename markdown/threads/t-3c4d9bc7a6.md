Quite a few years ago, Kernighan and Plauger wrote a book
entitled "Software Tools." You might find a copy in a
library somewhere.

The book was about programming and the examples were written
in "RatFor" (Rational Fortran). RatFor was "structured"
whereas Fortran wasn't.

Included was the text for a RatFor translator written in
RatFor. The translator would translate RatFor into Fortran.
I wrote MetaCOBOL macros that translated the RatFor
translator source into COBOL, which I then compiled into an
executable which would translate the RatFor translator
source into Fortran which I compiled and linked into an
Executable and then proceeded to translate the examples in
the book into Fortran.

Most of a summer's spare time was devoted to this activity,
which says more about how well the book was written than how
well I program, I think. It was a little like the person
that speaks German and Spanish who hears a story in German
and tells the same story in Spanish to a person that knows
Spanish and English who tells the story to you, with the
exception that computers are a little more rigorous.
