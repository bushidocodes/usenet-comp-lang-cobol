```
We began readying our users for Y2K in October 1998.

We sent out our final, Y2K-checked, version of our software in August
1999.

We sent two newsletters to all our users, put notices in all
correspondence, and e-mailed those of our customers for which we had
e-mail addresses. In all these communications, we warned them that
they had been sent a Y2K-fix release of our software and must install
the it before 1/1/2000.

Fully 20% of our user base has contacted us in the last two days
regarding Y2K issues. If the whole scene was not so tragic, it would
be comical. Some of the more common laments:

"I never got a software update!"
"I didn't think it was important."
"The person who does that sort of thing has been gone for four
months."
"Everything was working fine and I didn't see the need."
"No, we have not done a backup for at least a year."
"We tried to install the update, but it didn't work. We've been
meaning to call."
"Actually, we used the diskettes you sent for something else."
"We forgot."
"We planned to do it after the Christmas rush."
"If it ain't broke, don't fix it." (honest-to-God, that's what he
said)
(and my favorite)
"When our machine boots up, it says the date is January 1, 1980. What
does that mean?"

But it has not been all bad:
We've heard from three users who (allegedly) quit using our software
as much as FIVE years ago (and who have not been remitting the annual
maintenance). They demand to know why they should pay anything for a
Y2K fix. (Answer: Because car thieves don't automatically get a full
tank of gas.) We may actually convert one or two of these to the paths
of righteousness.

Around here, it's been 'all hands on deck' in the technical support
department - we've fielded twice the number of calls we normally get
each day since the 1st. And these calls are not the kind that can be
answered with a quick "Yes," or "No." We feel like we've be "'et by a
coyote and shit off a cliff."

Just remember, folks, you can lead a horse to water but you'll get
blamed if it dies of thirst.

Best wishes for a happy and prosperous New Year to all. As for me, I'm
gonna open a pig farm.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
"I opened my Windows 95 manual and fell into a magical place."
```
