Looking at your program, you have three hurdles to cross:  1) sorting your
input data, 2) accumulating totals, and 3) establishing a control break.

1) First, you need to sort your input into order by sales person.  Spend
some quality time studying SORT.  Sorting is necessary to allow you to
process the records for each sales person as a group.

2) Establish a working storage variable to hold the last key, something like
this:

01  last-salesperson    pic x(20) value spaces.

On your first read, move the sales person to last-salesperson.  Then, much
like you interrogated the line count, you can check if the current sales
person matches the last salesperson.  If it doesn't, perform your control
break processing, move the sales person to last-salesperson, then continue
processing.

Control break processing consists of writing a totals record, then invoking
your header routine.

3) For totals, establish two amount fields in working storage, one for the
sales person and one for the grand total.  Add the amount to each of them
with each record processed.  Whenever you do control break processing, use
the sales person amount for your total, then zero it.

When you run out of records, perform your control break processing, then
write the grand total you've accumulated.

You should be able to find this in your text under "break processing" or
something like that.

Sooo... sort on key, accumulate, break on change in key... (cha cha cha)
