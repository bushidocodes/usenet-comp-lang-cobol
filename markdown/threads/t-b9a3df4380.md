Ok, the answer is:

Robert Ludlum, in his novel, "The Icarus Agenda."

I just went looking for my paperback copy so I could give a verbatim quote,
but it looks like that is one of the books which found its way to the Racine
Rescue Mission when I was getting rid of dead weight prior to moving last
year.

Working only from memory, it is the "Paddy" character who says, "but if it
looks like a duck and sounds like a duck and walks like a duck, it's usually
a duck."

While Richard Darman was not the source of the phrase, the reason I
remembered this arcana is because of him. About a month after Mr. Darman got
a few minutes in the limelight for his "Darman Duck" comments (which, for
the record, he was applying to government-imposed  "user fees" in some
Federal budget proposal, trying to make the point that there is no
difference between a "user fee" and a tax), I read Mr. Ludlum's "Icarus
Agenda". The "duck phrase" struck me as incredibly timely - until I realized
that I was reading a paperback, and there was no way that Mr. Ludlum could
have "borrowed" from Mr. Darman's comments. So I checked the copyright page,
and yup, the hardcover version of "Icarus Agenda" had been published the
year before.

So that's the story.

No winners. Maybe you'll all do better when we return after this message for
"Double Jeopardy."
