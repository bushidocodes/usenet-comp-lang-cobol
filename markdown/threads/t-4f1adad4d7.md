This kind of snottiness always amazes me ... RPG has probably
processed more data every month of the last twenty five years than the
supposedly righteous languages like Ada have done in their entire life
cycle ... to me, the measure of a language is not what it can do (which is
often unrealized & empty theory), but what it does do ... and COBOL and RPG
are both splendid transaction processors ... which is what the world lives
on ... like airline reservations ... or credit card processing ... where
you're running very small numbers of daily transactions (relatively , for
it may be hundreds of thousands) against an immensely larger base ... but
where that base also requires daily updating (i.e., interest due) ... now a
language like APL would be helpless in the face of that ... but very good
at a problem with thousands of inputs and only one solution ... folks !!!
they're all good for something ... just know a few and pick the right one
for your problem ...
