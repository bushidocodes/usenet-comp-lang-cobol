If I had a relatively small mainframe application to develop, I would
probably want to go with VSAM first, simply because I am more familiar
with it, and I wouldn't need a lot of help from the DBA's.  It's nice
not to have to mess with DB/2 binds, and to always have full control
over your data without going to another department.  That may simply
reflect the organization structure where I am.

If I had a system that required a lot of alternate indexes, then I
think DB/2 would probably be a better solution.  I don't think
alternate indexes are very easy to maintain in VSAM.  If I were
working with a manufacturing inventory system, bills of material, MRP,
then some kind of database becomes almost mandatory just to manage all
the relationships.

> 
> As both VSAM and DB2 are IO bound, and the DB2 physical files are based on
…[10 more quoted lines elided]…
> Pete.

Ten years ago you could probably say that VSAM was faster than DB/2. 
Not now.  I'm a believer.  DocDwarf will probably remember something I
posted here a few months ago.  We converted a CICS application with
multiple alternate indexes over base ESDS VSAM to DB/2, and got a big
increase in performance.

Still, for the typical programmer, if there's no need for alternate
indexes, I think VSAM is easier to set up.

There's an awful lot of basic backoffice batch processing that works
perfectly well with sequential files and sorts.
