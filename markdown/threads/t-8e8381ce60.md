(B) OO Background :

Not important to you at this stage, but for future reference, gives you
background on other OOPLs :-

ISBN 0-201-13688-0 Smalltalk 80 - the Language. 1989 - Adele Goldberg & 
David Robson. (Got mine from amazon.com). Smalltalk treats everything as
an object whereas we talk pic x and pic 9 etc..., turning them into
objects for GUIs. Adele was on the original Xerox PARC team. Interesting
for treatment of collections/dictionaries which COBOL 2000 has copied.

ISBN 1-57870-009-4  Handbook of Programming Languages - Volume I -
Object-Oriented Programming Languages - Series Editor-in-Chief - Peter
H.Salus.

Background on OO and the OO languages - Smalltalk, C++, Eiffel, Ada 95,
Modula-3, Java - no mention of COBOL (??????). The academic world can be
just as vitriolic as the rest of us - Adele Goldberg relates how she got
shanghaied (no pun intended!) into giving a lecture on Smalltalk to
Steve Jobs and the early Apple crowd - so that they could walk away with
the keys to the Xerox vault. A couple of Smalltalk people take a knock
at Java. An interesting chapter heading, "C++ Traps and Pitfalls".

Enjoy ! - Jimmy, Calgary AB
