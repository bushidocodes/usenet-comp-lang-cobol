```
For those interested in IBM's position on the reaffirmation of the current
ANSI COBOL Standard and its two amendments, please see:

 http://people.ne.mediaone.net/doncobol/01-0251.doc

It states (in part),

"Reaffirmation of ANSI X3.23:1985 and its amendments sends a positive
message to businesses:  they can rely on continued existence of stable
compilers meeting a common specification.  Perception is important. Failure
to reaffirm creates a perception that COBOL vendors lack commitment to
provide such compilers.  That perception could lead organizations to make
decisions detrimental to their information processing investments, and could
have negative impact on COBOL vendors, tool makers, consultants, educators,
application providers, and programmers. "

Notice their statement about the "perception that COBOL Vendors lack
commitment to provide such compilers" *if* the Standard and amendments are
not reaffirmed.  Notice also that they do not EXPLICITLY state that this
perception is "false" (at least as far as IBM goes).  My *personal* guess is
that IBM will continue to provide such compilers (and prove the "perception
wrong") but if you are a current IBM customer, you might want to verify this
thru your "normal" IBM marketing and support channels.
```
