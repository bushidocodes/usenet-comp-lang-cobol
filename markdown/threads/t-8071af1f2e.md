```
Just as a follow-on to another thread.  If you have a file with variable length 
records and you do a read of a LONG record followed by a read of a short 
record - without doing anything to the 01 level item (or items) under the FD 
between the two reads, there is NO guarantee that the "end" of the record area 
will retain the original data from the long record after the read of the short 
record.

This certainly MAY happen with some implementations, but the ANSI Standard (past 
and present) are pretty clear that ONLY that part of the record area that was 
"filled in" by the most recently successful READ statement is defined.  ALL 
other "bytes" in the record area are UNDEFINED.

P.S.  There are some specific rules if you have a SAME RECORD AREA clause, but 
as I think that is sufficiently unusual that I won't go into those rules.
```

```
It makes sense because then the compiler can be lazy and not initialize 
the field between reads, or the compuler can be thorough and clear the 
field and be compliant with the standard in either case.  When cycles 
were expensive you didn't want to waste them doing unnecessary garbage 
collection or cleanup, but now it probably should clear the field before 
doing a read so all reads are consistent.
```
