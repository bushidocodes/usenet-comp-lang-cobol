```
I know that there has already been at least one question about exactly what
"macros" are in HP-COBOL II.  I have received the following information from
one of my "usually reliable sources".  Hopefully, this will help others to
answer the original question.

(My personal guess is that Micro Focus "Vocabularies" may well do exactly
what is desired.  What I don't know is whether that facility is or is not
available in their HP-UX product.)

"Bill,
Thanks for noticing this. I used to monitor comp.lang.cobol, but just
haven't had the time recently.

Our COBOL II/iX compiler implements a macro facility. For sites that have
used it extensively (and many of our users do seem to be enamored with it),
it will be one of the major obstacles to migration off of the HP e3000.

Here's a simple example of what you can do with it.

Define a macro:



000100$DEFINE %PERFORM=

000200 PERFORM !1

000300 VARYING !2 FROM !3 BY !4

000400 UNTIL !5.#


Call the macro:



001200 %PERFORM(CHEK-PARA#,CTROL#,INIT#,OFFSET#,A = B#)


The compiler expands the macro:



000200 PERFORM CHEK-PARA

000300 VARYING CTROL FROM INIT BY OFFSET

000400 UNTIL A = B.


Some of our users have developed hundreds of macros to handle common code
sequences. Some of their macros are dozens, if not hundreds, of lines.

If you want the full documentation, try this link:

http://docs.hp.com/cgi-bin/doc3k/B3150090013.11820/199

Other vendors are adding support for HP COBOL-style macros in their COBOL
compilers. You might point the poster to Acucorp, http://www.acucorp.com/ ,
or LegacyJ, http://www.legacyj.com/ . Both of them discuss their support for
HP COBOL extensions and mention macros in particular.

Feel free to post any or all of this to comp.lang.cobol or to forward it to
the original poster.
```
