It's a 1/4 century since I worked with mainframes :-) If I was doing it
now, this is a broad outline of what I would do :-

- New applications - cost them per man-hour - fairly easy because Joe,
Bill etc. work on a new program for x days, weeks or months etc.

- Existing application maintenance - and here I'm assuming you have two
teams, Development and Maintenance - the Maintenance people would need
to keep more rigorous records, time recorded per program
enhanced/altered. Possibly another factor included here would be
Operations re-runs because of foul-ups.

This Maintenance log is going to tell you several things - poorly
programmed, badly (systems) designed, ambiguous operating instructions
etc... You can then start using these statistics to justify
're-inventing' the wheel for new applications.

Of course you do say the numbers aren't available. Well perhaps the
first thing is that a start should be made to ensure they are available
from now on ! Other than a gut feeling about 'replacement' you can't
justify too much without numbers.
