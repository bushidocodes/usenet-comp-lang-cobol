Bill,
Your on the right track concerning the OBJ's being close to executables.
But unfortunately it's still NOT a "stand-alone" executable. That's the
correction I am speaking of. You have to purchase and use a runtime on all
supported operating systems. Their p-code is similar to Java's VM model.
You must keep in mind that compiling p-code makes it independent of the
operating system and dependant on a VM or runtime module for operating
system specific functionality. Seems to me a native code compiler with a
linker, builder and make file usage could be done.
Not too long ago, I asked Drake Coker, chief scientist at Acucobol,
about a native code compiler and he said it's only been tinkered with,
hasn't been done, and he's not sure it ever will. They may have something
"top secret" or "in the works", but a native compiler is not for sale yet.
Someday, hopefully. I'd sure want it. At least for Windows. It would go
great with their new AcuBench product.
Good talking with you.

Dan Maltes
Development Director
Applied Systems Technology
