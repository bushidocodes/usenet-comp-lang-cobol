Dennis:

While you have received some workaround information, you need to
understand the reason for the result obtained. Your client's mainframe
compiler should have the same result, but may just be ignoring the
slightly incorrect zone bits.

When you involve a *group item* (WS-FIELDA) in a MOVE, special rules
apply. Look it up: "Any move that is not an elementary move is treated
as if it were an alphanumeric to alphanumeric elementary move, except
that no conversion of data from one form of internal representation to
another takes place." (MF COBOL Language Reference, and also the
ANSI/ISO Standard).

Do it thus:

01 CURRENT-JULIAN PIC S9(5).

01 WS-FIELDA PIC 9(5).
01 REDEFINES WS-FIELDA.
05 WS-FIELDA-YEAR PIC 99.
05 WS-FIELDA-DAYS PIC 999.
...
MOVE CURRENT-JULIAN TO WS-FIELDA
is now a numeric elementary move, which will cause the sign to be
removed. I think the result thus obtained is what you desire. Standard
COBOL, no special compiler option needed (which, IMO, is just a future
bug waiting to happen, i.e. when the module gets compiled without the
option set).
