was uncompetitive, and down the tubes.  Now, of course, the
investors have moved their investments to Asia, mostly, and
the American industrial jobs are pretty much gone.

That didn't happen overnight, it took about fifteen years.
The Lima Accords were signed in 1974, signifying the US's
intention to ship manufacturing jobs overseas.  In the 80's,
the US Dept of Commerce was actively helping American
businesses set up shop overseas.  The rationale was that the
tariffs on the goods coming in would be greater than the
taxes lost through loss wages.  Of course, as soon as the
facilitites were up and running, GATT was signed, and the
tariffs removed.


I've seen lots of posters on this ng making fun of H1-b
workers, but they are missing the point.  When the Japanese
first starting manufacturing, they weren't very good at it.
"Made in Japan" was synonamous with poor qualifty for many
years.  But eventually they learned, and almost put the US
auto industry out of business.  Right now, China makes toys
and crappy electronic goods.  But the money and know-how is
being poured into China, and soon they will become a world
powerhouse of high-tech manufacturing.

Right now, the US is in the same position vis-a-vis the
information economy as it was in the 80s vis-a-vis the
manufacturing economy.  The same thing is now happening with
the high-tech sector that you seem to feel that only
Americans are capable of handling.  The whole global
corporate culture has been moving for years now to a labor
model of outsourced technical help. (Where do you think that

high-tech materials.    from http://www.iiit.net/:

freedom of an academic institution with the strength of the
corporate sector. IIIT is an autonomous, self-supporting
institution started in 1998 with seed support from the
Government of Andhra Pradesh and the participation of major
national and international IT companies. "

(There are several campuses, use any search engine).  They
graduate hundreds of thousands of graduates a year, and they
are learning the latest materal that is taught only at very
few American schools.

Twenty years ago, you could go to a local community college
and learn to do computer programming.  Try to go now, and
learn the latest technology.  It's not being taught to
Americans.
