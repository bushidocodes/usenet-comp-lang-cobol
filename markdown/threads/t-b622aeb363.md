Depending on blocking factors and paging characteristics of your system,
some large portion of that "half of my ISAM files" may already in fact be
*virtual* arrays (i.e., already held in memory)!  Programmers should remain
aware that a read or write is quite often a "logical" operation rather than
a "physical" one when evaluating relative efficiency considerations.  The
memory potentially available for buffering pages of data in modern computers
now routinely exceeds the maximum capacity of the hard disk storage devices
that were generally available just 20 years ago.
