Actually, that's meetings, PLURAL, Bill.  This topic was discussed at some
length at meeting #231 in June, as evidenced in document #01-0530, the final
version of the minutes of that meeting (see agenda entry 16.26, item 16)
available on the same web site, as well as at meeting #232 that ended on
Friday last.

The agenda item that precipitated this lengthy discussion (in both meetings)
was your Public Review Comment #26 (document 01-0215).  I have to admit
surprise that you weren't interested enough in J4's discussions on your
paper to be aware of (much less read and digest) the actions taken by the
committee in response to it (or for that matter to be present at either of
the meetings at which it was discussed).

I think having a web site and an "open source" body of examples of
conformant COBOL programs on a user-accessible repository is a *fantastic*
idea, but I also think managing the repository as well as managing the
editing and verification of the validity and relevance of the examples
themselves is way outside the scope of what the standards committee ought to
be doing or has time and resources to do.    It seems to me this is an
*excellent* opportunity for a user-driven body of conformant examples,
managed entirely outside the purview of the standards committees, perhaps
with some hands-off corporate sponsorship to provide the needed resources.

The formal disposition of document 01-0540 will be reflected in the draft
minutes for meeting #232; those draft minutes will be available (as document
01-0481), probably late Friday afternoon.    In all honesty, I think the J4
chair has enough to do without having to answer a bunch of queries on this
topic, since at least a "semi-official" response will be published in 48
hours or less!

    -Chuck Stevens
