It's been years and years. Dyl260 and 380 are report generators and can do
some other functions. They provide the ability to provide Ad Hoc inquiries
to VSAM files and other mainframe data. They run batch. The system was
last sold by Panasofic, which was aquired by CA, along with Realia COBOL

products any more. I used them in a DOS/VSE environment.

Speaking of which. There are a LOT of Mainframe MVS jobs out there. I had
OS back in school, but there was no MVS at that time. I learned DOS/VSE
and OS/VS. My employment has been all VSE. I can't see the lack of MVS
experience being that big a deal. I can learn the JCL pretty quickly.
However, I have been eliminated from consideration from several jobs
because I lack real MVS experience.

How would not KNOWING MVS (All I can claim is a vague familiarity with DD
statements and some JES2), really affect my job performance. Anything I
can do to improve my employability due to my lack of MVS. Anyone with
experience with both want to comment?

Thanks!

MVS JCL, TSO, ISPF is a lot of stuff to pick up but you should be able
to sell yourself as a VSAM, DYL-280, COBOL, whatever mechanic. Don't
say, "I'm a VSE junkie." Say, "I've got lots of applications
experience, IDCAMS, S/390, whatever." If they press you, tell them
honestly that you are more familiar with VSE but you can work
effectively in MVS. When you say OS/VS do you mean OS/VS1, OS/VS2 R1 or
OS/VS2 R2? R2 is MVS but all three systems shared JCL.

When you say JES2, do you mean the /* route and print cards? That's
about all most programmers know; few people dig down into JES2 source,
JESMODs.

These Y2K jobs require people who can dig deep, it's not all about
DD-cards, it's about bit-level work in S/390. Get yourself a copy of an
MVS JCL book and bone up. You'll do fine.
