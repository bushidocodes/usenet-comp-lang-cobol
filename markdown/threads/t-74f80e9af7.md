```
Dear CLC'ers,

Hmmmm... I think we've been looking at this _Does CoBOL Have A Future Thing_ all wrong.

For a fresh perspective, please see:
http://www.hastac.org/blogs/ari-schlesinger/2013/11/26/feminism-and-programming-languages?goback=.gde_70143_member_5830715163640102914#!

An excerpt:
"...a feminist programming language is to be built around a non-normative paradigm that represents alternative ways of abstracting. The intent is to encourage and allow new ways of thinking about problems such that we can code using a feminist ideology."

Reading that, I was at first taken aback, for fear that I had spent my entire career programming in a Male Chauvinist Programming Language, MCPL, (a variant, or better yet, a "perversion" of APL), until I realized that CoBOL, both because it is widely criticized as "verbose", and because of the Role of Admiral Grace Murray Hopper, was, and is, the archetypal feminist programming language.

I think I can understand better if maybe someone can explain to me what "post-structuralist" and "para-consistent" mean? Hmmm... is it the case that "para-consistent" is to "consistent" as "para-normal" is to "normal"?

I am aware of the subject matter Domain Specific Languages (DSLs), designed from the beginning to solve a particular "domain" set of problems, and some, including Robert Glass, have contended that CoBOL and FORTRAN were early DSLs for business and scientific applications respectively. But I guess I have been spending too much time in my man-cave, watching football and 'rasslin', as I missed the conversation on the origin of "gender specific languages."

Does that conversation have anything to do with left-brain vs. right-brain kinda stuff? Or maybe it is based upon that other esoteric branch of computer science called "multi-valued logic", where there is more than 0 or 1, more than left or right, more than black or white... there is room for "maybe." Even "50 Shades of Maybe". :-)

Or is it really all about some kind of "nouveau-deconstructionism?"

Please, CLCers, don't let CLC die. I posted this thread on the LinkedIn CoBOL discussion group, and if I get thrown out, I won't have anything to do during football half-times.


Ken
```
