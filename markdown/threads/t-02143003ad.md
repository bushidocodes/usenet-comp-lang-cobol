```
Although it is now about a decade since I worked for Micro Focus (and even more 
since I "owned" mainframe compatibility), I can respond partially (and 
historically) to what you (Kelly) say:

1) Micro Focus did not (and I doubt will EVER) "guarantee" 100% identical 
results on any platform with the behavior on z/OS, VSE, or elsewhere.  I 
(personally) would SERIOUSLY doubt any vendor (including IBM) that made that 
claim.

2) When I worked for Micro Focus (and I suspect still true) they were ALWAYS 
willing to accept a "problem report" (and try to provide a "fix") for any 
incompatibility between their compile-time and/or run-time behavior and that 
which  was DOCUMENTED as valid on the mainframe (for a currently supported 
environment).

3) In many cases, Micro Focus even created "code fixes" (often new directives) 
for cases where users wanted workstation behavior that matched mainframe 
behavior for UNDOCUMENTED and UNSUPPORTED mainframe behavior.

4) The mainframe compiler (and transaction processors) and run-times are "moving 
targets".  One of the biggest problems for Micro Focus (and Fujitsu and 
CA-Realia, et al) is that when IBM changes behavior (via APAR/PTF - not just 
release time), some customers want the "old" behavior and some want the new - 
and NO ONE wants yet another "directive" to allow user-control over the 
behavior.

   ***

I do think it is true that Micro Focus is likely to provide reference sites that 
were "successful" not necessarily that "exactly match" your environment. 
However, if you are considering ANY Micro Focus product or set of products, if 
you can't get useful information from "your sales rep" - then escalate it.
```
