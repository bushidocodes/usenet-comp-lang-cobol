Steve,

The ERASE verb in RMCOBOL means clear the screen from the
end of the last item displayed all the way to row 24 column 80.

One simple way to get a completely blank screen is to:
DISPLAY " " LINE 1 COLUMN 1. (That's a space 'tween to quotes).

At least that's the way RMCOBOL worked on the Unix system
we had a few years ago. I'm a PeeCeeWeeNee now, MFCOBOL
handles it slightly different.

DISPLAY "just a text" ERASE EOS. --> clear the screen till

› Just ERASE means the same as ERASE EOS I think.

ERASE EOS means to erase (clear) the screen from the current cursor
position (as defined by the LINE & POSITION attributes) to the end of
the screen (typically 80 & 24).

ERASE EOL is the same as above, but only to the end of the defined
line.

If now LINE and POSITION is defined, then the command procedes from the
current cursor position, as defined by the previous successful DISPLAY
command.

ERASE by itself means to completely clear the screen and reset LINE &
POSITION to 1,1.
