```
Twenty years ago when I started programming, I was advised by several
seemingly well-informed people in the DP (as it was called then) business to
stay away from COBOL because it was a dying language.  "It's too wordy.. and
besides we're all going to be replaced by code generators anyway".

Over the years, the "value" of this advice has become obvious to all of us
who, perversely, like to use a business oriented programming language to
solve business problems.

Obviously, COBOL has an image problem.  One solution is to follow the lead
of the university that, once they renamed a course from "Requirements
Analysis for Information Systems" to "Requirements Analysis for e-Business",
found that the course went from moribund to over-subscribed.

I therefore propose that we rename our favorite language to COmmon
e-Business Oriented Language:  COeBOL.

;-)
```
