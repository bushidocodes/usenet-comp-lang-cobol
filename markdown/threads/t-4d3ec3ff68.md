Since this newsgroup is the repository of all that is true and
beautiful, please permit a clarification. Texas does NOT permit the
use of deadly force, per se, to protect property.
Specifically, deadly force is permitted
a) to prevent arson, burglary, robbery, theft at night, or criminal
mischief at night
b) to prevent the dobad from fleeing WITH the property after a
burglary, robbery, or theft at night
AND
a) the land or property cannot be protected by any other means, or
b) the use of force less than deadly would expose one to substantial
risk of death or serious bodily injury.

So, you can't shoot someone in Texas just for breaking out your
windshield, pulling up your lettuce, kicking your dog, or not serving
your steak rare.

We do, however, have the "But you honor, he needed killin'" defense.
