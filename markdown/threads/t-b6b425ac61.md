"... while there may be some esoteric things, such as a few obsolete things

I will (personally) be VERY interested in a comparison chart of what COBOL
language supported in Fujitsu COBOL V6.0 and NetExpress 4.0 (including
extensions, ANSI'85 *and* *ISO 2002* syntax) will be supported in each of
the two .NET products.  (It will be particularly interesting to see which of
the "dialect" things that Micro Focus currently supports are retained -
whehter TRANSFORM statment or COMP-6 or Argument-Value or whatever)

Certainly, I hope (expect?) Micro Focus won't have the "basic" problems of
not supporting writing to printers and disallowing internal SORT's and
nested programs, that Fujitsu has - while NOT introducing any "new"
ommissions that Fujitsu already supports.

Suggestion to those in Micro Focus - when using the phrase "obsolete things
in the COBOL standards" - you may want to make it clear whether you are
talking about those items designated obsolete in the '85 Standard or in the
2002 Standard.

Speaking as an individual and a representative of Fujitsu....

The Fujitsu implementation seems to be geared toward taking advantage
of the interlanguage object inheritence of the .NET platform.  With
Fujitsu COBOL .NET you can inherit from objects of other languages,
and other languages can inherit from COBOL objects.  This is one of
the most interesting aspects of .NET.

The MicroFocus press releases I have read - I have not seen or
reviewed the product - seem to have a mapping aspect where you can
compile your COBOL under .NET, but with no mention of the cross
language inheritence.  As I understand it - one can create ILC from a
single language.  This is similar to the creation of Java from COBOL
that PerCOBOL does.  A full COBOL implementation that runs in the JVM
via conversion to Java.

What I am interested in learning is whether or not MicroFocus is
sacrificing the Interlanguage inheritence offered by .NET in order to
implement their compiler for .NET.
