>times.  Or zero times.  Should your royalty be the same in either case?

No, they do not.  The author/publisher of the music gets a per time royalty,
but the inventor of the language does not.  Nor do any of the musicians that
invented the scale, developed harmony, wrote the word processor that was
used for the script, or manufactured the paper that it is written on.

A compiler is specified as being a language processor.  If someone chooses
to implement that language by means of a called library rather than a
complete translation to binary, that is simple technique.

If I write a book, then I am entitled to royalties on it.  If someone
translates that book into another language, then I can negotiate any kind of
deal I want to with the translator. When, after I finish the book, someone
claims to be the "owner" of the language, and demands royalties thirty times
the price of the book, I get a tad pissed off.  Particularly when I spent a
good deal of my time as a youth contributing to that language.  Are MF
paying royalties to Warren?  Or Don?  Or Bill?

If MF feel they have a moral right to royalties on my Cobol code, then they
should feel an obligation to pay royalties to every programmer that ever sat
on an ANSII committee or submitted a bug fix.

invested $400,000 in Cobol code (the only reason that I bought it in the
first place), they tell me that I cannot use the code I wrote or recoup my
investment unless I pay them another $87,000?

Let's say that you, as an employee, work for five years on the basis of
getting paid $400,000 at the end.  The day you finish, and put your hand out
to collect your cheque, they tell you that you owe them another twenty years
of work? If they tell you that you can take or leave it, does that make it
better?

has bought the compiler, and tied themselves into it. If they only imposed
the fee on new users, it would be quite a different thing.  That is not the
case.

makes money.  If all the financial rules change every two years, then the
software business becomes a monopoly, and the only people allowed to write
software are the ones that work for the compiler writers.  That is precisely
what MS tried to do, by different methods, and precisely why they will be
bankrupt in three to four years.  I said that a year ago in this echo, and
was poo pooed.  Now, it is starting to become more obvious.

Get enough people pissed off, and the biggest company in the world can go
downhill like a toboggan.
