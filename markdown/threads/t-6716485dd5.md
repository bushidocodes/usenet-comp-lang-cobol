Sorry I can't offer you code to do this, but wanted to exend a couple
of extra cents I've collected in doing this.

Shifting from upper to proper case is a pretty good idea, but is not
as easy as it would seem.  Ed pointed out case exceptions, like in
McDonald that make this difficult.

The special capitalization cases depend intimately upon the nature
of the data being converted.  Names have a number of specific
conditions, as do addresses.  110 NW Congress Ave, for instance
would have the NW capitalized--a considerating that's not very
reasonable for name conversion.

Descriptive fields are even worse.  I recently converted a number
of medical procedure descriptions.  It was so jammed full of
acronyms, that the resultant text was barely better than the
original.  I ended up having to build a lookup list to properly adjust
these terms, but even that had it's limit.  (ie, is CAT an acronym,
abbreviation or domesticated pet?  You just can't tell from the
text alone.)
