```
The editor on the Fujitsu Cobol Version 4 is driving me nuts.
It is completely unworkable on any program over two pages
long (the search does not work) and I am attempting to work
on a 4000 line program. It is completely impossible, to say
the least.  To complicate the problem, the compiler is as fussy
as hell about tabs Vs blanks in columns 1-7. You cannot tell
from most editors whether you are looking at legal code or
not, as <tab7><space> will not compile, while <space*8>
will.  <At least that is what I *think* is happening, as I cannot
really tell without editing my source in fucking hex. You get
these mysterious errors that disappear when you go in
and make sure there are 8 or 12 leading spaces>.

BTW, Thane, they corrected that problem in version 3 where
you could trick it by using quotes.  Now, a quote is illegal. You
apparently cannot search for anything but a string of letters. No
dashes, no tabs, no asterisks, no spaces nothing but strings
of pure unadulterated letters.  Whoever figured that out had
to be brain dead.  I don't know why they even bothered to write
it.

Now that I have gotten THAT off my chest, can anybody give me
a pointer to a downloadable editor?  I have heard several
mentioned in the last few weeks.  I am quite desperate to get
something NOW, even if it only works for thirty days while
I get a cheque cut and mailed.
```

I'm partial to kedit (http://www.kedit.com). It uses a REXX-based macro
language. Visual SlickEdit is also a fine editor with a C-like macro language
including a GUI painter for customizing the editor (http://www.microedge.com).
Both have language-sensitive editing features including colorization,
auto-typing (type 'If' and get the matching 'END-IF'. The macro languages of
both allow for complete customization of the editor so it can become and IDE and
program launcher.

For the ultimate 'free' editor you could try GNU Emacs from
(http://www.cs.washington.edu/homes/voelker/ntemacs.html). It's a little too
scary for my blood but others swear by it (or was that 'swear at it').
