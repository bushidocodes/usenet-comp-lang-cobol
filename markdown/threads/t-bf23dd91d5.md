Today (September 6, 1997) in San Francisco, the U.S. Navy commissioned
the USS Hopper, a guided missile destroyer of the Arleigh Burke class.
Its home port will be Pearl Harbor.

The Hopper was named after Rear Admiral Grace Murray Hopper, an early
computer pioneer and a co-inventor of COBOL. It is the second U.S.
fighting ship to be named after a woman, and probably the first to be
named after a computer programmer.

For more information, point your browser at:

http://www.navsea.navy.mil/hopper/

Official Navy press releases did not indicate whether the Hopper's
Aegis electronic warfare system was programmed in COBOL.
