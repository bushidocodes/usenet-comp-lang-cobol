Well, I feel privileged to have met Grace Hopper and to have heard her
speak on several occasions.... she was a special person seeking to
share knowledge and understanding of COBOL. a major issue she pushed
was for standards, regardless of language used. but is she the source?
no, she is not. look to your acknowledgement pages in various COBOL
books and you'll see the origins... well worth the read. the
department of defense (USA) was a major sponsor, as was sperry-rand.
check out your local libraries to see if they have some old COBOL
books... can be quite entertaining (relatively speaking, of course...)


however, if I were to identify where Grace was a 'source' related to
COBOL, it would have been the pressure to make it an ANSI standard -
prior to her initiatives, ANSI standards were mostly on mechanical
items. ANSI 65 set the foundation for a cobol used by all
mainframes. we're now at ansi 85 (COBOL II and up...) and it's a major
item..... so, Grace, wherever you are, we have fond respect and
memories of you.....
