I wouldn't recommend using it.  Its probably OK for the basics but
there are *many* differences with Cobol on the AS/400, such as:

1)	Database/Sequential/ICF Files 
2)	Transaction Files (display screens, subfiles)
3)	Activation Groups (goback, stop run, cancel, etc.)

Instead, use the CD-ROM manuals -- you'll be much better off.
My 2 cents......

Good Luck
Terry

Sorry to say, the days of "SAA COBOL" are long gone.  The differences between
IBM COBOL for this-and-that as opposed to ILE COBOL (current OS/400 product)
are bigger than ever.  (Oh, the Standard stuff is still there - but the
extensions are "barely overlapping" sets).

To get to the online ILE COBOL pubs, see

  http://publib.boulder.ibm.com/pubs/html/as400/online/v4r4eng.htm

(and search for ILE COBOL)
