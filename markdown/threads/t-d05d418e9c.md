```
I am working for a large company that is struggling with the question
separating
business logic code from data access code. As anyone who has struggled
with this question can guess to camps are forming or the idea. On one side
you have the architecture group who argues for complete separation of the
two. On the other side you have the support group who argues that when it
comes to things like DB2 SQL there are no real benefits to separating the
two. The main reasons for this discussion are portability, reuse, and
maintenance. In almost every case the two sides can argue thier point,
very well. I would list out the points for each but I think most poeple
would not want to read them all. I have heard enough opinion and theroy, I
would like to here real world experience from poeple who have dealt with
this issue.
I am looking for how other companies have resolved this problem. We are
working in a C/S environment with OS/2 on the workstation using APPC to
communicate with ANSI'85 COBOL programs running under IMS/DC accessing DB2
tables and IMS DB's.

- What guidlines does your company have?
- What are your rules of thumb?
- What are your thoughts on these questions?

Thank,
```
