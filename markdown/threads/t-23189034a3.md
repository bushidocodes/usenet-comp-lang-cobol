I certainly suggest the implementor *provide* the (standard) feature;
without it, in fact, their compiler is not compliant with the current
standard.  The implementor will provide the *extension* -- or not -- as his
marketplace dictates.

As to *discouragement*:  I once ran across a fully-conformant COBOL(68)
program that had clearly been written by someone who was fluent in ForTran,
and who equally-clearly thought the ALTER verb was the Bee's Knees.  Clearly
the least-maintainable program I have ever seen.

An INSPECT...REPLACING on an edited-numeric data item can switch the sign.
I would discourage users from taking that approach.

As a friend of mine was fond of saying "You may not get what you want.  You
may not even get what you need.  But one thing's for sure:  You get what you
get."
