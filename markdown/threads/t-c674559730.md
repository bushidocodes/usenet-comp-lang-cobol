Dave,

If you like MFE/Workbench, then you should check out the C-Cubed tools
available for both MFE and Workbench.  They allow you to transparently
access MVS subroutines directly from the debug environment.  What I mean
by this is that when you "Step" a "CALL PROGRAM USING ..." statement,
the animator/debugger interfaces with C-Cubed to call the subroutine on
MVS and return the results.  It's just like the subroutine was on the
local workstation!  You can also mix-and-match which subroutines get
executed on what platform - MVS or PC.  No more downloading subroutines
to the local PC to test with - just use the existing ones on MVS.

We also provide the same sort of access relating to MVS datasets - VSAM,
QSAM, PDS, Librarian, etc.

Check out the web-site if you are interested - www.c-cubed.net

Thanks - TL

If you are used to Micro Focus Workbench, then I certainly would NOT argue
against this.  HOWEVER, I am a little "curious" about your mainframe
environment.

If
 - you don't use any Assembler routines
 - you don't still have any OS/VS COBOL routines
 - you want to debug IMS "locally" on the PC

then you MIGHT also want to look at IBM's VisualAge COBOL.  The one
advantage that this has over MERANT's product(s) is that it does use the
EXACT same "front-end" for COBOL as the mainframe.  Therefore, when IBM
introduces new releases/features on the mainframe you may (probably?) will
see them faster in IBM VisualAge COBOL than in MERANT Mainframe Express.  ON
THE OTHER HAND, if you do need to access "asm" routines or PC IMS, I think
that MFE is your best (only?) real option.
