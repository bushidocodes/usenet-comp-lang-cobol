This points out the "opinion" nature of the original question. When you ask
for a "good COBOL compiler" the question needs to be asked of you - What
type of editor are you used to today? If you are a current IBM MVS user,
then SPF/PC is probably a good suggestion; on the other hand, if you are an
IBM VM user, then probably one of the PC XEDIT products would be better. If
you are NOT an IBM mainframe programmer but a UNIX person, then one of the
PC "VI" products might be what you are looking for. If, on the other hand,
you are really a PC person used to WORD or WordPerfect as your major
"editor", then there are other editors that would probably meet your needs.
Finally, the "big" (usually expensive" PC COBOL compilers almost all have
"built-in" editors or editor interfaces - one of these may well be the best
product to meet your real application programming needs.

Bottom-line: Tell us what you are used to today (and what you like about
it) and we can probably give you a better suggestion.

We are old users of SPF/PC (since version 2), and we love it. We recently
upgraded to the SPFPro version, in order to support long file names on Unix
and NT, and found mixed reactions. However, when we called Command
Technology (the publishers of SPFPro and SPF/PC) they made a real effort to
fix the problems we had, including adding back in some features from the
character version which had been removed in the GUI version (e.g., hex
display). While it is taking some time to adjust to the GUI version, there
is still a character based version available, which is supposed to be more
like the mainframe SPF. Version 4 is the last character version we had, and
it was great, but it is no longer supported.

If you want to see them you can go to www.command-technology.com.

Another of our offices ordered Slick Edit, after reviewing SPF/PC, Slick
Edit and Multi Edit. Interestingly, the office which bought SPFPro had
