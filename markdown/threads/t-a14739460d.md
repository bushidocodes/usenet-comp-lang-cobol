Many people have hyped or over simplify(sic) the problem. Below, you

your problem, and help you to relax!

The computer and O/S

People got tired, LONG ago, of retyping the date when a computer booted
up. Because of this, many computers, including most IBM PC compatibles,
have clocks built into them. The BIOS may have a problem with dates
higher than 1999. To check it, simply:

1. Change the computers time to 1/1/2001.
2. Shutdown windows, and turn the computer off.
3. Boot to DOS(hit F8 Just before the booting windows screen).
4. Type "SET DATE" (without the quotes).

If the date is 1/1/2001, your BIOS and DOS are FINE! If not, then you
should probably get a new BIOS. On some of the newer systems(with
"FLASH BIOS"), this is simple. On the others, it may be more
difficult. If your BIOS is fine, which you can check by going into
setup, you should get a new copy of the O/S!
1. Boot windows, and check the time.


If the date is 1/1/2001, windows is FINE! Sadly, this isn't the whole
problem.

The PROGRAMS!

Each and every program may have a problem with YTK compliance! Do they
allow four digit
years? Do they REMEMBER them? Do all fields store them? Do all
calculations work with 4 digit years? If the answer to any of these
questions is no, you probably have a problem. If so, check with the
manufacturer of the software! They may have an update!

-- End Quote --

You don't seem to think Y2K is unimportant by these comments. I'm
