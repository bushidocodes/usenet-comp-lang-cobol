I want to know which city has ten thousand video stores.

What's more, I want to know which city has ten thousand video stores all
without any sort of computer automation already in place.

Our local store chain has it's own full video system now, BTW.

It's going away though, in the merger process the new company will implement
one of a number of possible existing third-party packages to replace our
home-grown one.  So such systems do exist.

I've got to admit, writing an entire system sounds a bit ambitious, but it
could be done.  Our video systems handles 200 stores (each about 1/4 the
size of a Blockbuster), and is one of the smallest systems we have.

However, it does incorporate a batch system on the mainframe; an online
system on the mainframe; plus data transfer jobs to push data back and forth
to the Unix servers (where the data is collected from the stores), plus the
online system on the server, plus the system in the store's In Store
Processor.  This involves, JCL, COBOL, COBOL-CICS, COBOL-DB2, FTP, MVS,
Unix, Informix SQL, Informix 4GL, plus knowledge of proprietary third party
Point of Sale software system.

Although I can guess that you mean a single PC system, self-contained, I
think that the majority of outlets are really a part of chains.

You've got you're work cut out for you.
