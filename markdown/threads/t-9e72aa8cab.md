There is one COBOL standard and it is the ANSI/ISO 1985 standard with
addenda for intrinsic functions and corrections. The standard is
published by ANSI and by ISO. I doubt if you would want one, because
it is not a user-friendly document.

The standard does not support pointers. Many implementations do,
however. The down side of the non-standard implementations is that
none of them support the method planned for the next standard (1998?)
and most of them differ in how they do it. Therefore, is you use
pointers you have lost portability. Many people use relative files
for fancy lists and whatnot. It may be a bit inefficient, but it
works.
