```
to: comp.lang.cobol & OpenCobol lists

In the past, there have been several reports of difficulty in "participating" in 
the international COBOL Standardization process from people in Canada.  I just 
received an email that looks as if Canada is now (again) "active" in the WG4 
process. If you are a Canadian and are interested in participating in this, then 
I suggest you contact BOTH the designated "Head of Delegation" and "Standards 
Council of Canada".  Each National Standards Body has its own rules for 
"delegation participation", so I can't tell you how easy (or hard) - much less 
how costly (or free) such participation will be in Canada.

The "Head of Delegation" for Canada was listed as Chris Tandy.  You can get 
contact information for him from either:
   http://www.cobolstandard.info/j4/files/09-0060.doc
or
  http://www.ibm.com/contact/employees/servlets/lookup

Contact information for SCC is available at:
  http://www.iso.org/iso/about/iso_members/iso_member_body.htm?member_id=1619

* * * * *

Of particular interest might be the fact that  WG4 is *considering* doing some 
"work" via teleconference.  For those who are interested in participating in the 
process, this may make it both easier and more cost effective.

As usual, I am not telling you that COBOL Standardization work is fun or even 
productive.  However, this "news" is something that might get some additional 
input (and resources) into the process.
```
