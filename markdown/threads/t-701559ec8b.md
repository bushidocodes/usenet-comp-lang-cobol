Since I missed the original post...

I am a contractor, but I don't think that all companies are evil... I
think that MOST are short-sighted. They continue to neglect their most
valuable asset; their employees. As soon as they have one bad quarter,
they decide that they can save money by shit-canning a bunch of mostly
hard-working people. (To be fair ... some dead wood also gets
eliminated) To top it all off, the idiot who thought this idea up takes
a huge bonus and hires consultants to fill the gaps... How does that
make sense? From a long view, I see this as a huge failing in the
American way of business. Though I agree with Gordon Gecco (From Wall
St, The Movie) that you produce or "get eliminated", it is hardly
appropriate to blame the employees when the people running the company
are so often inept and, more often than not, responsible for the
company's situation.

Even though I have been fairly successful at consulting, I would be
perfectly willing to be a loyal employee, if the company would
reciprocate. If they would pay me what I was worth, if I could be sure
that they would not axe me the first time they had a bad quarter. In the
consulting world we all accept the fact that certain job risks are
inevitable. The client can fire you any time, with little or no warning.
Thats the risk for the reward of more money, greater freedom, better
assignments. Why take a job as an employee that includes all of the
risks and none of the benefits?

IMO, contractors are a product of corporate philosophy.

Many years ago, when I was an employee at a Fortune 500 company, all
of the company's managers were required to read "In Search of
Excellence". It was considered the corporate bible. And being an
aspiring employee, I decided to read the book too.

I soon figured out why it wasn't required reading for the staff. To
sum the book up in a nutshell, all solutions are short-term. Go to
work for a company, make your mark, and move on. If the solution
doesn't help this quarter's bottom line, or more importantly, you
won't get credit for it, it's not a worthy solution.

What are contractors? Short-term solutions. Contractors also help
managers make their marks on a corporation. If a project is a
success, it's the manager who takes the credit. If a project is a
failure, it's the contractor who takes most of the blame.

But, more that just that. Many contractors are former employees who
lost their jobs, because the company "downsized" or "rightsized".
Again, what is that? Short-term solutions. Trying to help that
quarter's bottom line.

Reading "In Search of Excellence" actually convinced me to go into
contracting. After reading it, I knew the only real security was in
your own abilities.

Come to think about it. We have this Year 2000 problem. I think we
can attribute quite a few of those problems to short-term solutions.
