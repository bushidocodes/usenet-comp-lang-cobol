I have  used COBOL on ICL, IBM, HP3000 and now DEC (oops, sorry
COMPAQ) platforms since 1972 (although I also spent some years in
Systems Analysis, Computer operations techical support and supervisory
posts).

I would like to give my opinion on what I think should be priority
objectives in any new cobol standard.

The strengths of cobol have always been in its ease of use in
commercial data processing, in backroom batch processing, its very
simple English-like non-cryptic commands, its inherent
self-documention (especially if written in a structured manner),
strong portability between many platforms, comparative easier to
understand error messages, clear division into Data Division and
Procedure Division (which in turn is structured into sections,
paragraphs)

It is the above strengths that have persuaded users to continue making
use of the language for the last 30 years despite the many 'newer'
languages that have come and go during the same period.

I feel that in the formulation of any new standard, whether a new
feature is to be added or not should be based on whether it contribues
or enhances to the above strengths, and not on the fact that it is the
flavour of the month. If a new feature, which happens to be the
flavour of the month, contrbutes to the above strengths, then it
shoild be added, otherwise it should not. Only in this way, by
focusing, improving and extending the strengths of COBOL can we ensure
that new users will have the same reasons as the earlier users (of the
last 30 years) to continue making use of the language.
   
===================================================
