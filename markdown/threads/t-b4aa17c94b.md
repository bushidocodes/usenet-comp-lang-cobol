"Working as a 1099" refers to a independent contractor-contractee
relationship, rather than an employee-employer relationship. Usually,
a 1099 contractor has more than one contractee (client), and can be
thought of as an independent business negotiating with other
businesses to provide programming services in exchange for fees.

The United States Internal Revenue Service (IRS) has a list of 28 - 30
questions that a contractor must be able to answer yes to to qualify
as an independent business. The 1099 is the IRS form that the
contractee uses to report the money paid to the contractor to the
government for income tax purposes.

Also, if you are an employee (W2) you pay 7.5% of your pay for social
security/medicare (like it's going to be around) along with your
income tax. The company pays an additional 7.5% to match your
contribution to social security/medicare.

If you are 1099, you pay the full 15% for social security/medicare.
Also, a 1099 is a contract person and therefore considered a "temp"
worker so a company does not have to give you advance notice of a
layoff nor do they have to justify why you are being fired.

Most W2's have benefits (health insurance, etc) where a 1099 must pay
for their own if desired.
