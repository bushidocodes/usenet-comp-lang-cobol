Rome with an army and as punishment ordered John's eyes be put out as

excommunicated. Should he possibly object, his lips, teeth and tongue 
were removed next, and while still alive his mutilated body was shipped 
to a monastery.

somebody the 265th pontiff, but they'll note he is the 262nd successor 
to Peter. The glitch is due to the unbridled corruption in Rome in the 
11th century. One well connected Roman layman was put on the papal 
throne three times as Benedict IX. Official Vatican lists credit him 
with three pontificates - causing a bit of a problem for catholic 
students who aren't too good at math.

Evil influences rose and fell with the fortune of powerful European 
clans. 1492-1503 Alexander VI, a member of the notorious Borgia family. 
Ruled like a secular prince, fathering too many illegitimate children 
for historians to count. Brutal partnership with his son Cesare 
murdering their way across central Italy.

Political corruption wasn't quashed until early 20th century. Some 
political leaders slipped their cardinals a note as to who should be 
elected. Last time this happened, Franz Joseph of Austria in 1903,
