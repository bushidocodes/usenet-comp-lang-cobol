Lucky,
I'm currently taking Cobol on the home computer through Dakota State
University. We're using the text Comprehensive Structured Cobol by Horn
and Gleason which has available a microsoft compiler. You could learn
on your own through the book or through the microsoft How-To Section
included with the compiler. I've found the DSU class very good though
online classes don't give the opportunity for immediate feedback so you
have to work through rough spots on your own, proff is excellent, DSU
did wrack us on fees between first and second semester (doubled them!)
so I won't be going through them again.
Don't know about obsolete language - the want ads are looking for Cobol
programmers right and left though it may be 2000 conversion. Good luck.
