   I carried this for long with me to write something about the  
Vindication of SECTIONs in the PROCEDURE DIVISION.

   All divisions are divided up in SECTIONs (exept the IDENTIFICATION  
DIVISION), so the most natural first division of the PROCEDURE  
DIVISION is the SECTION, and within a SECTION maybe one or more labels  
as paragraph headers.

   SECTIONs are also necessary when DECLARATIVES for event and  
contingency processing are written. So, using SECTIONs in the first  
place, one does not have to rewrite the program after the introduction  
of DECLARATIVES (I would have always DECLARATIVES when files are read  
or written in the program, in order to handle unrecoverable file- 
errors).

   Besides, for those programs which still have GOTOs, I recommend the  
rule which I layed out for the COBOL development back in the early  
1980ies, when COBOL-85 with its (late) introduction of proper  
structuring possibilities, namely to use only paragraph labels within  
the section itself as destination of a GOTO, should a GOTO ever be  
unavoidable.

   One case where this was explicitly allowed was the use of a GOTO  
DEPENDING ON, which is a primitive form of a CASE statement, or  
EVALUATE in COBOLese.

   So, one could change a PERFORM para-name-1 THRU para-name-2 by a  
simple PERFORM section-name-1 and introduce a SECTION header for that  
sequence of paragraphs. A first and simple step to gradually reform  
the program.
