Take a look at the screen section.  Essentially, the
data to be displayed and accepted is laid out record
style, just like working storage, but with from and to
statements.  You then accept and display the record
names, and all else is automatic.

The console is really very primitive methodology, originally
intended for things like getting tapes mounted by a
console operator.

Peter,

If Netview can detect your scrollable and disparate messages (which aren't
shown on consecutive lines of the syslog), then it can echo them back to the
system console as a fully contiguous, unscrollable, and white or red
highlighted lines.

Whenever Netview at our site detects an abend code/message in the syslog, it
is immediately redisplayed as an unscrollable  while-highlighted message
(accompanied by a loud beep).  The operator must manually delete the message
to deal with it.

If you don't want operators to actually see the message, then I'm unsure as
to why you need them all on consecutive lines.  If you ever need them after
the fact, just have each line prefixed or suffixed by a unique combination
of characters, and use one of the available utilities to strip your messages
out of the syslog.

As two years at a console as an operator showed me, if a message is
important enough to put in the syslog and further important enough to
cluster together, then it had better be geared to alert the operator
immediately.

Of course, sites elsewhere might not operate in this manner.  So I apologize
if these suggestions don't help.
