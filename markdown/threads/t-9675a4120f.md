Does anyone know the whereabouts of Ellis Computing. They produced
several software products for quite a number of years, one of them
being a small inexpensive Cobol compiler. It was first brought out
to run on a Z80 platform running CPM. ALA Kaypro-4 style machines.
They later brought out a version that runs on a DOS machine. For the
low price of $59.95 it can't be beat. The last I heard from them was
in late 1959. I would like to find out if there is still support for
the excellent compiler that Roy Ellis wrote. The last address I have
for them, was in Reno Nevada. Of course the phone number no longer
works. I really hate to put the compiler I have back in the box and
put it on the shelve, as I have really gotten a lot of mileage from it.
I can't really afford to buy any of the latest Cobol compilers and
really don't have enough disk space even if I could afford them. The
Utah Cobol compiler although inexpensive, has 85% of Cobol 1985
compatability. A few things are not implemented but I can easyly
work around them.Any information will be greatly appreciated.
Thanks,
