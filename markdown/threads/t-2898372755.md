Did anybody else notice the way Micro Focus's business is described in this
release?

It's not described as a COBOL Compiler company, or a development tools
company.  It's described as a: "legacy development, transformation and
integration business."

That sounds like a services business to me, not a tools company.

Could be just the word smithing of a novice PR person at Golden Gate trying
to throw in all the latest buzz words, but quite frankly, that whole
description bothers me.  If they focus on services and not tools, they will
be competing with the very people they normally sell compilers to and
through.
With the exception of Microsoft (who is so arrogant they can get away with
it), very few companies can be both tools vendors and service companies.  It
divides their focus, but more importantly it alienates their strongest
evangelist - the contract developers.
