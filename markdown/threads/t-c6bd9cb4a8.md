```
As you know, I delight in responding to job posts in the
programming and Y2K newsgroups. I'm immediately attracted to
their *jobs* *jobs* *jobs* titles, and give them some serious
lip. Others do this too. I've had some interesting offline
discussions with several recruiters, Steve-the-recruiter, and now
Lloyd from Volt.

Lloyd has taken the time to share with me some insight from the
recruiter's side of the fence.

-------------------------email from Lloyd...

I saw your posting in the y2k group. Do u really run into recruiters
that don't have the openings, but want to "market" someone into a
company? We have more y2k jobs than we know what to do with.

If a contractor tells me he wants $xx/hr. I submit....regardless what
the client says he/she wants to pay. Simple economics. SUPPLY AND
DEMAND. If the client doesn't want to pay that rate.....okay.
Because there is another one that will! And if that client doesn't
want to pay market price, then they'll have to make do!

Y2k= the "new" SAP. Meaning....great rates, but is an absolute MUST!
No one HAS to have SAP....but EVERYONE HAS to deal with Y2k issues!

Lloyd D. Songne, Jr.
Volt Services Group

Visit our interactive employment web site at http://www.volt-tech.com

P.S. I posted in here too. Until I was asked not to by more than one
"reader." Anita is right, the job newsgroups are worthless.

--------------------------------My comments.
I believe what he says, more Y2K jobs than he knows what to do
with, Y2K is the new SAP. I asked Lloyd if he minded if I
posted his email to the newsgroup.

This is not a job spam, it's insight into the mind of the
recruiter.

-------------------------------- More email from Lloyd...
Cory,

Thanks for the response. By all means, please feel free to post my message
to you.

I agree with you....why pay good money for positions that don't exist? I
know we won't. If someone wants to throw money away like that, please,
throw it my way. We try and ask the hiring managers that contact
us with needs if they have gone through their internal process to
get the positions "approved." If they say "No", then that order
isn't as high a priority. We're not hurting for positions. We
are trying to avoid the "window shoppers."

Plus, when a manager tells us that a rate is "open" we just can't buy it.
If the position was approved, then he/she had to submit the requision for
approval with budgeted costs. Too many times the employers feel they will
tell us open, and then express an interest in someone that was submitted
too high. They believe that by doing this the applicant will lower his/her
rate because the employer expressed interest. Not anymore! This no longer
an "employers market!" Now an "employee's market". If you aren't willing to
pay what the applicant wants, then you won't get that applicant. I won't
even approach an applicant of mine and ask them to take less unless I have
been given some other incentive to present.

Thanks again. Have a great day.


Lloyd D. Songn, Jr.
Volt Services Group

Visit our interactive employment web site at
http://www.volt-tech.com

------------------------------More of my comments.

"Salary: OPEN" is one of my hot buttons. I know what kind of
hoops HR made me jump through to justify my department's budget.
There's no such thing as Salary: OPEN, unless you're Bill Gates.

There is no secret stash of good jobs. The jobs, and contract
gigs, are out there. There is a wide range of salaries and
rates.

Look at it from Lloyd's position, what does he care if you ask
$35, $55, or $155? If the deal goes through, he makes money. In
this market, the recruiters have to ratchet the rates *up* to
make the deal work. They've run out of geeks wearing bib
overalls, stalk of hay in their mouth, and "born to code CICS"
tattooed on their arm.

Even if they swing the watch in front of you, side ... to ...
side, watch ... the ... watch, and you take a $35/hour
assignment feeling refreshed and relaxed. How long before you
open a newspaper, or Time magazine decides to outdo Newsweek?

Hey... wait a minute, the guy who mows my lawn, charges me
$35/hour! A cup of coffee is $1.35. A movie is $7.50. Dinner
for two is $40.00.

Cory Hamasaki
```
