```
I don't think Judson McClendon got a responses from anyone in the
government on the thread he started about the status of Y2K.  (Maybe
because he asked for "direct knowledge?")  I don't have direct knowledge
either, but . . .

The contractors we hired almost three years ago to make our VSE programs
work in MVS are still hard at work.  The absolute deadline of March 1
has been changed to March 23, but they won't be ready then either. 
(There is a tie-in to Y2K here, bear with me.)

The programs they are working with are maybe 75 programs which are, say
steps 1 through 100 of our Claims run, which is run nightly.  All the
tapes we generated are now made into disk files.  All this is done in
Washington.  Just this week, someone here was looking at one of their
output files and noticed that instead of a control record followed by
data records, it is a control record, a data record, control record,
data record, and so on.  The contractors were aware of another problem,
the program was supposed to create (in our case) three files but was
only creating one.  They weren't aware of how the file looked - control,
data, control, data.

Since this program is one I inherited ten years ago, I looked at our
code and found that a FIRST-RECORD-SW is set to determine when a control
record is written for each file, and is also used to close and open the
files.  Then I looked at their code.  Unbelievable.  They didn't replace
the switch, it was just commented out in two places so the switch is
always set to '1'.  There are four places in the program that tests the
switch for '0'; if it's '0' the file is closed.  In their version the
instruction 'move '0' to first-record-sw is commented out, replaced by
their own test; if-the-file-is-closed, open it, then set it to
file-is-open.  They never use their test to close the file, so we have
one file instead of three.  The first-record-sw, when set to '0'
bypasses the creation of the control record, but it's never set to zero,
hence the control record for every data record.

My point is this:  They've been testing these programs for over a year. 
All the output tapes, maybe a hundred of them are now put on disk.  It
never once occurred to them TO LOOK AT THE OUTPUT???  In the case of
'my' program, they changed the logic by messing with the switch, and
even then NEVER CHECKED THE OUTPUT??  And apparrently never had any
intention of looking at it, because we've been told several times "the
programs are ready, we're just having a little problem with the
panels."  "The panels are working, but one of the programs blah blah
blah."

Now for the Y2K tie-in:  this same company of buffoons and idiots are
also our Y2K contractors.  And the Y2K group and the MVS group are
working on the same programs at the same time.
```
