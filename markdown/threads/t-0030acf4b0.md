We are writing a new COBOL course for the University of Regina. As
part of the course we are looking at adding a new COBOL compiler
to our system.
Our current compiler exists on a VAX/VMS system.
We are looking for an inexpensive alternative to using the VAX/VMS
version as the macine is getting older and is also very busy.
we have several other types of machine on our campus and
we are wondering what the best machine/software combination would be.
the types of machine available are:
Intel 386SX w/windows 3.11
Intel 486dx 33 w/os2
X-windows workstations (DEC 5000/25)
Unix terminals (IRIX)

we are interested in finding an inexpensive/free UNIX version of
COBOL but are open to other options.
Any ideas would be very helpful.

Your best bet is probably Micro Focus Personal COBOL for Windows. Not only
is it affordable for your students, it also allows them to develop Windows
based COBOL applications.

By the way, scrapping COBOL from your curriculum or teaching people how to
get away from COBOL would be a major error in judgement in my opinion. You
should be teaching other languages and tools *in addition to* COBOL, not in
place of COBOL.

Despite what you read in the media today, COBOL is alive and well and doing
just fine. There are so many programming tools available today that quite
often, re-writing an application in some other language results in:

projects which are more expensive than incorporating new tools in the
existing code

projects which ultimately fail

applications which are considerably harder to maintain than the original
code

massive expenditures in employee retraining
