One problem I can see in your code up front. You have a bit of recursion
in there that may baffle your compiler. Inside the "menu" paragraph, you
perform an error routine (if an invalid choice is selected) that then
performs the initial paragraph again. That's a no no. (A simple GO TO
AFTER performing the error routine - not it it - would fix it !!!!! JUST
KIDDING!!!! (Although it would work)). Try FLOW Charting that again, and
eliminate the recursion and see what happens.

I might do something like this:

Perform with test after until answer = 1 or answer = 2
display "make a choice"
accept answer
if answer not = 1 and answer not = 2
display "please choose 1 or 2"
end-if
end-perform.

Optionally, you may put the initial choice display before the perform.

Oh, and those NOT's on answer, and the way I coded the "and" and "or"
statements can be done differently and more efficiently, but this is easier
to understand.

"Thane Hubbell" wrote:
› One problem I can see in your code up front.  You have a bit of recursion
› in there that may baffle your compiler.  Inside the "menu" paragraph, you
› perform an error routine (if an invalid choice is selected) that then

Since when is paragraph recursion a no-no? That shouldn't bother any
conforming Cobol85 compiler. Of course, without a proper exit, it will cause
some stack-space problems at *run* time, but that's another story.

Possibly you are confusing paragraph recursion with program recursion.
Every version of Cobol that I've ever used prohibits recursive CALLs.

Cobol85 compilers should permit recursive PERFORMs.
