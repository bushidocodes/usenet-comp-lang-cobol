I have MF Personal COBOL at home, and I use VS COBOL II at work. MF
COBOL has a verb "EXIT PARAGRAPH" that I find useful. Apparently in
both MF and COBOL II the verb "EXIT" is a dummy verb that doesn't
actually do anything. Why on earth is this? Anyway, my point is that
in MF COBOL you can do the following:

procedure division.
0000-main-para.
perform do-something
stop run.

1000-do-something.
if the-number = 0
exit paragraph
end-if
* do large amount of code here
* and here
* .
* .
* .
* to here
exit.

I like this because of a few things. One, you could check
'the-number' for a value of 0 before performing it, but if you
performed it from many different places I don't feel it's a very good
idea. You might forget to check at some point, so why not just have
the check from inside the paragraph.

The only other alternatives I can think of in COBOL II is, 1) put a
dummy 1000-exit paragraph right after the final statement in
1000-do-something and then go to it where I have the "exit paragraph"
statement. Or 2) Put the entire remainder of 1000-do-something as
part of an "else," and make the "exit paragraph" statement a
"continue" statement instead, which would make it continue to the
bottom of the paragraph.

Both of these ideas are, in my opinion, terrible. Any other
suggestions? (Hope I wasn't too unclear...)

The MF construct is an extension to standard (85) COBOL that they chose to
implement.

IBM did not implement this particular extension in VS COBOL II.

The construct may be in the proposed 9x (97?) COBOL, I have not yet
received my "official" draft standard.

Even if it is in the standard, it will likely never appear in VS COBOL II,
since that product is "stabilized". It could appear in COBOL for MVS and
VM, or more likely in yet another product which might be available a year
of so after the next standard is finally adopted.

You can write code that achieves the same thing and will compile on both
platforms like this:

1000-do-something.
if the-number = 0
go to 1000-do-something-x
end-if
* remainder of paragraph
.
1000-do-something-x.
exit.

PS This helps explain what EXIT is for; it's just a no-op statement
like CONTINUE, but in this context that would be somewhat misleading.

PPS Many, myself included, would argue that 1000-do-something would be
better as a section with paragraph headers only used for the targets of
GO TO statements.
