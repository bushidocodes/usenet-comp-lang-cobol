Agreed. Also, the 'economic climate' plays a big part in how much the
government makes it easy for domestic employers to hire foreign workers.
Typically, during booms they increase the foreign employment markets by
increasing the cap on H-1B visas and L-1 visas (is it L-1?? I cannot
recall...) for companies. So, right now, it is more expensive and costly
in terms of dollars and time for a company to bring in and hire a
foreign worker due to the domestic economic climate of the United
States. However, it IS not impossible, and depending on your skillset
and experience and whatever else that can set you apart from your peers
there as WELL as labor competition that exists in the US already, you
may STILL be able to find an employer here that would work through this
process with you. Still, there is no free lunch, so you'd have to work
pretty hard and do your own homework on this issue. If you've got any
kind of international networking you could rely on, I would use your
time right now to rely on it if at all possible. Also, all of this is
contigent on WHAT skills are in demand, and as has been said, the demand
for skilled COBOL programmers is rather stagnant, but stable, so there
isn't a rigorous need to bring in foreign hands. But, as I just said, it
isn't impossible. Foreign COBOL programmers are being hired right as I
speak...
