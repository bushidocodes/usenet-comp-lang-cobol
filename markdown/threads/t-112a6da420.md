hmmm,,, well, I don't mean ever to advertise on usenet, but I would
be remiss if I didn't advise you of my book,
"CICS, A How-TO for COBOL Programmers", published by John Wiley. Price
is in low $50's, and is a full intro to interactive dialogue with CICS
and a thorough documentation on BMS and every CICS and COBOLII option.
ISBN: 0-89435-428-0. (Features related to COBOL/370 are in my book on
COBOL/370...)
Regarding doug lowe's book, this is also excellent, in 2 volumes, and
is from Murach Books. I consider myself a friend of the Murachs and
they have/are building a website at www.murach.com

Both books, mine and Murach's, assume you know COBOL.

The problem: Neither book focuses on where CICS is going, focusing
instead on where CICS has been. So... the big question is "What CICS
functions do you need to know?" With CICS 4, applications can run in
batch, can run SNA transactions, and can also do TCP/IP transactions.
I've also developed CICS transactions to run IMS transactions in
separate regions. This continual expansion of CICS into multiple
platforms creates a challenge for publishers and authors. No single
book can document it. IBM needs approx 10 linear feet of manuals to
explain it -- and well justified.

My suggestion is that you will need many manuals to ever feel you have
a sufficient library for CICS. Most of us write for the beginner, not
the expert. Why? The market isn't there. Few programmers will shell
out $50 apiece for 2-3 books on a topic. (BTW, even the few remaining
CICS books are slowly disappearing. Why? Programmers aren't buying
them and publishers don't know how to reach them -- it's not one or
the other -- it's both.) Good luck in your book search.
