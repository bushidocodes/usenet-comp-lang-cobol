>Do somebody know any articles
>about future directions in Mainframe-Cobol
>field ?

Well, outside of Y2K, Mainframe COBOL, not being sexy, and despised by most
academics as being too pragmatic, useful, and effective, to really be worth
their time (not that I have an opinion <G>), there haven't been a lot of
articles about Mainframe COBOL recently.  

However, in past postings to this group you will see references by one of our
leading lights, William Klein, to several standards groups, such as J4, and the
various things they are doing to "improve" Cobol.  

You could also check the IBM web site, WWW.IBM.COM, where some hints as to
future direction can be gained - the trick there is to know that COBOL is in
the Visual AGE family of products.

And, you could cruise back through the threads of this newsgroup.  Lots of
folks opinions here, and some may actually be worth the time to read.

However, please, please, PLEASE, do not start a thread on the future of COBOL! 
We've had them, they get very long and tedious, and encourage many of us to say
silly things.  If you are asking if COBOL has a future, I would refer you to
the Y2K postings, where some of us worked on code originated in the 70's or
earlier, and allow you to draw your own conclusions.

Good luck


Asimov, Heinlein, and Zappa
Still the best
