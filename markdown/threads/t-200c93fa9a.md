Frederico,

I understand your points but I have to balance them against the following:

1. The Internet in general is one of the last refuges for Free Speech in our
society.

2. You Don't HAVE to access it or participate in it if you don't like what
goes on in it. (Can't stand the heat? Don't go in the kitchen...)

3. Unmoderated Newsgroups provide a valuable "steam valve" for people to say
exactly what they think without having to modify it or tone it down. For the
most part, the participants are self-moderating and this has to be a better
solution than imposing Moderation. This forum has run effectively for over 5
years now and the current problem has only arisen because one individual has
set out to disrupt and destroy it for reasons of his own that are
incomprehensible to me.

4. I for one, cannot tolerate the idea that some other individual can "vet"
my posts and match them against a standard I may have no agreement with. It
is simply censorship where we allow another to decide what is acceptable to
us. I can't go along with that. (I resent it with movies but have little
choice; here on the Internet I do at least get to vote...)

Moderation in this forum (assuming you can get someone to do it...) would be
anathema to me and I would probably no longer post here. (Some will no doubt
see that as an excellent reason for getting Moderation as quickly as
possible...<G> Nevertheless, without closing my options, my feeling is that
I would not want to be part of a group were everything is censored for
"correctness".)

My vote therefore, has to be "NO" to Moderation.

As to what we do about Robert, well, maybe he'll change his mind and decide
there is no great kudos for him in destroying a group that has done him no
harm. (Yes, I do tend to see the bottle as half full, rather than half
empty...<G>). If that doesn't happen, it will be down to the particpants in
this forum to decide wheher they will let him simply ruin it, or they will
do what it takes to prevent him from doing so. In either of the above
scenarios, the group comes out better off.

Pete.
