We purchased Merant NetExpress and annual maintenance agreements starting at
Version 2.0. The licence agreement allowed distribution of compiled programs
without any additional runtime licence costs. (By the way, there were some
facilities such as FileShare which did involve additional licensing issues,
but we did not use any of these).

Have just received (as part of our maintenance agreement) the latest version
NetExpress 3.1. The new licence agreement now requires that EACH user site
that uses ANY programs compiled from NetExpress 3.1 must purchase a minimum
of 10 runtime licences for over $AUD2000! This was confirmed by the local
Merant office.

So for example, one our programs is a Payroll program which we sell for
approx. $900. If we want to use the latest version of the NetExpress
compiler, we would have to sell this for $900 PLUS well over $2000 (which
goes to Merant).

Merant has made it clear it does not want to do business with small software
development companies such as ourselves.

Regards, Robert Kovacic.
```

As a former Micro Focus (never MERANT) employee, let me state that
"concentrating" on the larger enterprise shops is NOT a new "MERANT" policy -
but goes back to - well let's say the "upgrade" of "VisOC" to "NetExpress".
(If not earlier - say "Workbench" enhancements as opposed to "Toolbox" much
less "Toolset").

As I have often stated in this newsgroup, there is no evidence that ANY
vendor is making money out of the "single license" users of COBOL (as a
development tool).  The extension of this "rule of thumb" to the fact that
"smaller" shops bring in less money than larger shops is not too far fetched.

As for run-time licensing fees, to the best of my knowledge MERATNT/Micro
Focus hasn't kept any consistent policy for this for over 18 months in a row
since at least the mid-80s.  I won't say that the next one will be any better
than the current one, but I will bet that whatever you have today - won't be
what you have in 18 months from now.

Bottom-Line:
  *EITHER* get a bunch of small and single license users to "band together"
to *VOCALLY* express their opinions to the MERANT board (and management) *or*
look for a vendor (and probably a programming language) that targets "small"
developers.  (P.S. Simply buying another product will NOT send a message to
MERANT - because they aren't counting on your "single developer" sales any
way.)
```
