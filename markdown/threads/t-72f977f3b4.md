Kenneth Morrison wrote:
>I'm guessing COBOL is easier to learn than C++ (what I'm learning now)
>because its older. Am I right? I'm not finding C++ hard at all.

Learning COBOL is comparable in difficulty to learning QBasic.  Learning
C++ is, by comparison, like learning Sanskrit.  Looks pretty much the same,
too. ;-)  The reason you're finding C++ 'not hard at all' is, I suspect,
because you haven't really gotten into the subtleties (and the difficulties
which arise from that) of it as yet.  COBOL is very simple and has very few
subtleties, by comparison.  With the addition of OO and pointers to COBOL,
that is changing some.

        Many companies are using windowing as a stop-gap measure,

        where they receive non-compliant input from other companies.
        Let's say Company A adopted 20 as their window.
        They will write some windowing code, for example:
                 MOVE EDI-DT-INPUT-YYMMDD TO WS-EDI-DT-INPUT-YYMMDD.
                 IF EDI-DT-INPUT-YY < 20
                     MOVE  20 TO WS-EDI-DT-INPUT-CC
                 ELSE
                     MOVE  19 TO WS-EDI-DT-INPUT-CC.

         However, Company A books Sales Orders up to 2 years ahead of
delivery.
         That means in the year 2018, the windowing routine will be
processing
         input dates for the year 2020.  However, the logic as written will
set the
         Century to 19.  Which means the window will have to be moved.
Again and again.
