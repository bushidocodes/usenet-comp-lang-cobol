In COBOL 85 you can MOVE an edited field to a numeric field.
De-editing occurs. The standard specifies that the recipient of ACCEPT
is more or less implementor-defined. If you read the rules (in the
standard) for ACCEPT it gives you the idea that the data is plunked
into the reciever. However, it does say the implementor specifies any
conversion. What I suspect is that the compiler is warning you that
they just plunk the value in, no editing or whatnot. That is, the
implementor specified that you might get garbage. I would make the
recipient alphanumeric and then do MOVE FUNCTION NUMVAL
(the-recipient) TO the-value.

This is a case of the better compiler knows...
You get what you pay for and Micro Focus costs much more.
change the pic of 'entry-field' to s9(9) (if you trust your customer)
or x(9) (if you think they may enter non-numerics). you're accepting
to an edited field; the compiler uses the '-' and Z's in a pic as
editing masks. based on the mask, I assume you need the value to be
negative; if so, put an 'if' statement after the accept, and if a
positive value is entered, loop back to the display and accept, and
tell the customer to enter it again; if the value is negative,
continue. if you use the x(9), you'll need to check for numeric with
another if, and then mutiply by .01 to get the decimal portion.
