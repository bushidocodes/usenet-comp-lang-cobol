```
New Year's Eve passed off fairly quietly here in Tauranga.

I declined a couple of parties and opted instead for a quiet evening with a 
couple of friends at my place. (I'm definitely getting old :-))

The weather has been glorious (27 degrees C yesterday) and the evenings are 
balmy, so it was good for barbequing. We had a good assortment of local 
produce:  a couple of rock lobsters (caught by a friend), beef and pork 
fillets, lamb chops, spiced venison sausages (home made by another friend), 
mixed salads (provided by the guests), some excellent local wine, and fresh 
fruits of the forest. At midnight there was not the usual hooting of ships 
from the port (or, if there was, we didn't hear it) and a few fireworks left 
over from Guy Fawkes went off in a neighbours yard. We drank a toast to the 
new year, sang 'Auld Lang Syne' and that was it.

My house guest has now departed back to the U.K. and I need some time to 
recover from the hectic full-on schedule of sightseeing and having fun...:-)

Extended my holiday until the 9th so I can get some rest before heading back 
to Auckland and the hurly burly of the project. After the 9th, my posts here 
will be less frequent due to pressure of work, but in the interim I'd like 
to wish you all a Happy New Year and good things coming your way in 2006.

Pete
```
