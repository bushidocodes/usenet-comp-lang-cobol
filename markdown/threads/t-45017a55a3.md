Windowing is where you set a "window" period for dealing with a 2 digit
year as representing one century or another. For example, in my company we
have been offering health coverage since the seventies. So until the year
2000, everyone that starts coverage will start in a year greater than 70.
When examining someones year of health coverage activation, we can check if
the year is greater than 70. If it is then the century it represents is
1900. If it was less than 70, then it would be the century 2000 (obvisouly
no such instances of this exist yet).

exist, such as 95 for 1995. If you're data does not have any dates
prior to 1970, then you might use "70" as your window. Any year
greater than 70 is assumed to be 19xx, any year less than 70 is

Windowing is a technique for interpreting a 2-digit year as a 4-digit year.

Most COBOL programs have traditionally been written with the assumption that
the century window is 1900-1999.

A fixed window uses an unchanging "pivot" year. For example, if your pivot
year is 50 (or 1950), any 2-digit year less that 50 is interpreted as 20xx
(or 2000-2049). Otherwise, any 2-digit year greater than or equal to 50 is
assumed to be 19xx (1950-1999). Thus you've changed your century window
from 1900-1999 (with pivot 00 or 1900) to be 1950-2049.

A sliding window technique requires knowledge of the current 4-digit year.
Let us assume that you want to support a sliding window of 70 years in the
past and 30 years into the future. The current year is 1997. Subtract 70
and you get your pivot year of 1927. Now you can interpret all your 2-digit
years as falling within a century window of 1927-2026. In 1998, your pivot
year becomes 1928 and your sliding window automatically shifts to 1928-2027,
without any reprogramming.

When choosing a pivot year, you need to know what range of dates your
application must support.

Windowing will almost certainly be necessary if you need to convert dates in
history files. Windowing is not a good solution if the dates to be windowed
could span more than 100 years (birthdates, for example, if used to
calculate a patient's age).
