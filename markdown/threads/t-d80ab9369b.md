Your picture clause does not agree with your data. the V in the pic means an
implied decimal, but your example is a numeric-edited field with  an explicit
decimal point. Try defining transaction-amount as PIC Z(10).99. Then you can
MOVE  transaction-amount  to ws-trans-amount.  As a side note, it is more
efficient to store/manipulate numbers that contain a V in the picture clause as
COMP-3.  COMP forces the compiler to do a lot of extra work.

You are correct that a numeric-edited item CAN contain spaces (if it is
correctly defined) but your field is numeric, not numeric edited.

Although there are some other possibilities, what it SOUNDS like you want is

  10 dec-transaction-amount redefines transacton-amount
                                               pic 9(11)v99
                       BLANK WHEN ZERO.

Or you want to change 9(11)v99 to Z(11).ZZ (which is one byte longer and
assumes that the input has a period "." rather than an implied decimal
point.

Note: There are some odd rules concerning when, if, and what it means to
have a BLANK WHEN ZERO clause with a PICTURE with "v" (and even funnier when
you have an "S") - but my guess is that this is what you really want.
