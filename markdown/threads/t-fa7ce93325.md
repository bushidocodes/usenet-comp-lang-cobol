```
some of the other replies to this note seem pretty frightening to me.
Instead try,

01 MONEY-OUT PIC 999.99.
* The following is NOT a redefines
01 Money-Implicit-Decimal Pic 999V99.
....
Move Money-Out to Money-implicit-decimal
* You could use the following - but it seems over-kill to me
Compute Money-implicit-Decimal = Money-out

Bottom-line, you cannot "redefine" a 6-byte field (with an explicit/display
decimal point) into a 5-byte field (with an implicit decimal point) - you
need to do a move from one storage area to another one.

P.S. At least as I read your last column - you have "left justified" your
price (with a space surpresed leading zero). If this is true, you need to
"undo" this before converting it to a numeric field with leading zeros.
```

```
When you put a "." in your picture, then the data is
no longer numeric, but edited for display purposes.

If you use the implied decimal point instead, then
your move will work.

Try defining your data as 999v99. That is a numeric
field.

01 MONEY-OUT REDEFINES XAMOUNT PIC 999.99.
› 01  DOLLARS-N-CENTS REDEFINES XAMOUNT.
›   05 DOLLARS               PIC 999.
›   05 FILLER                   PIC X.
›   05 CENTS                   PIC V99.

In the above example, MONEY-OUT, XAMOUNT, and
DOLLAR-N-CENTS are all alphabetic fields and will produce
errors when moved to a numeric field as they have a dot
in the middle. DOLLARS and CENTS are both numeric.
If you did this:

01 DECIMAL-AMOUNT-OF-DOLLARS.
02 PRE-DECIMAL PICTURE 999.
02 POST-DECIMAL PICTURE 99.
01 ENTIRE-AMOUNT REDEFINES DECIMAL-AMOUNT-OF-DOLLARS.
01 ENTIRE-AMOUNT PICTURE 999V99.

MOVE DOLLARS TO PRE-DECIMAL.
MOVE CENTS TO POST-DECIMAL.
MOVE ENTIRE-AMOUNT TO any-other-numeric-field.

then you will find that the amount gets moved and aligned
into the "any-other-numeric-field" correctly.
```
