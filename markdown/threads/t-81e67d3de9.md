Following up on my recent sceptical posting, GH does seem to have
some real achievements to her name

1) According to her, the first compiler. One of the first for
sure.
2) The idea of cobol (an english like language that non-engineers
can program). 
3) flow-matic 
4) the idea of code reuse (still encountering stiff resistance
among programmers) and some practical steps towards making it
possible.

Still following up GHs exact role in these things...

But she has been credited with so much she did not do, and to
have received so many honours, you have to wonder, why? Perhaps
because she was a woman in a male dominated field, perhaps the
force of her personality, or charisma, who knows.
