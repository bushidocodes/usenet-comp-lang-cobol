In answer to a thread in IBM-MAIN about IBM dropping support for its
("almost ancient" by now) ESA "architecture" (replaced first by OS/390 and
then z/OS), there was a reference to a PDF file that I found FASCINATING.
Although probably somewhere between "unintelligible" and totally
uninteresting to NON-IBM "mainframers" (of the "MVS-ish" persuasion), I
would hope those who are in or interested in this environment would look
at - and share within their organization, the PDF presentation at:

     http://docs.share.org/proceedings/sh100/s2412.pdf

The quote (related to the original IBM-MAIN issue) is on page 15 where it
says,

"In the release planned for September 2004, z/OS will no longer support the
ESA/390 architecture."

P.S.  Some of the presentation is fairly "nitty-gritty" for the "average
application programmer" - if such exists, but it still is worth going thru.
```

I found the ENTIRE presentation to be fascinating - not the "drop" of ESA
support.  (Things like the percentage of z/900 machines running z/OS and the
future of VSAM and DB2 enhancements and 64-bit virtual addressing.  All of
this has been "public" - but not something that I have seen "put together"
as in this presentation.)

As far as what the ESA drop means, I believe (but won't swear to it) that it
means that applications that REQUIRE a specific (earlier) ARCHLVL will have
problems.  I think that only impacts specific (authorized) instructions and
various control blocks.  However, as I have never "played" with such, I
don't know its full implications.

I *do* believe that "older" COBOL programs (even those compiled with OS/VS
COBOL) will continue to "run" and be supported if using the LE run-time
library.  OTOH, I might be mistaken on even this.
```
