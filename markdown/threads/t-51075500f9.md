Cobol is a long standing language,standardised by international agreement, and not  owned by any company. (like Delphi or C++) There are dozens of Cobol's by various vendors, for all different platforms. I would suspect that a course would be taught on PC's, but you could be learning on a mainframe.  Regardless, all the Cobol that you learn will be transportable from your PC to a mainframe.  The differences lie mainly in the screen-interfacing methods.

One place to start is www.adtools.com    , Fujitsu's webpage.  The sell one of the bettor PC Cobol Compiler's.  Another would be     www.flexus.com  .  Flexus makes a generic Cobol/OS screen product that allows many different compilers to interface to many different
operating systems (including the net) from standardized screen definitions. The web page has links to many Cobol vendors.
