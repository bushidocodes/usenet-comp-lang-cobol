Darius Blasband wrote:

› My concern is pretty theoretical. Given "X(9)", is there a lexical way
› to distinguish a picture clause from the extraction of an element out
› of an array. By lexical way, I mean without referring to any syntactical
› information, whether X is defined or not, whether we are in the procedure
› division or not, etc...

A picture character-string can appear only after the word PICTURE (or
PIC). Most compilers use this information to figure out what a
picture character-string is. If you had to figure out whether or not
it was an array reference you could not do so out of context. What is
worse is reference modification, which can look just like subscripting
when you hit it (the colon gives it away, but it requires scanning
ahead to find it because the leftmost character position can be a
really complicated arithmetic expression with thousands of terms).
Anyhow, the spaces before and after parentheses don't really matter.
Almost all compilers allow you to omit such spaces (some give a
warning that you are not conforming to the standard). In the proposed
standard the rule requiring spaces was deleted so no spaces are
required.
