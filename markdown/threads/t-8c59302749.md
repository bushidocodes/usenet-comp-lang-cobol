That may be the case for other vendors, but I think I can state without too
much fear of contradiction that that is *not* the semantic intent of a
"compiler error" in Unisys A Series COBOL compilers.  If a programmer
attempts something that was recognized as beyond the scope of the
implementation, the syntax error would indicate something along the lines of
"feature not implemented" or "unrecognized construct".

The intent of the "compiler error" message is to inform the user that there
is a *problem with the compiler* and that he should contact his support
organization.   The A Series COBOL74 version of this message, in fact, reads
"A compiler error has occurred.  Please contact your support organization."

    -Chuck Stevens

Your syntax is correct by the reference manual that I have handy (circa 1985).

My preferred variation of your request would be something along the lines of 

       FIND  FIRST KDS-N AT
             (( KDS-TEXT-A >= C-TEXT-A(C-ST)) AND
              ( KDS-TEXT-A <= C-TEXT-B(C-ST)))

I prefer to re-word queries to avoid any NOT logic.
Parenthetical grouping helps to clarify my understanding of what 
each component of a compound condition is operating upon.

Hope that this helps you get past the compiler error you are experiencing.
