```
To complement the "Ugly If" thread, I submit this:

This is, by the way, NOT incremental code - it was all done under a single
change control.

This person loves EVALUATE.  Makes me think of the old saying - "when you
have a hammer, everything looks like a nail".

EVALUATE TRUE ALSO TRUE ALSO TRUE ALSO TRUE ALSO TRUE
  WHEN ADD-ACTION
   ALSO W-DRM-ORIG-PRODUCT EQUAL CC-DDA
   ALSO W-DRM-DRIVER-TYPE EQUAL CC-TB
   ALSO W-DRM-REL-PRODUCT EQUAL CC-DDA
   ALSO W-DRM-INSTRUCTION-CD EQUAL CC-A
    MOVE P-REL-ACCESS-ACTY TO TCB-ACTIVITY
    PERFORM CA000-CALL-PEM
    EVALUATE TRUE
      WHEN M-RESULT-T50000D NOT EQUAL DGR-OK  OR
           M-RESULT-U50810D NOT EQUAL DGR-OK
        CONTINUE
      WHEN OTHER
        EVALUATE TRUE
          WHEN M-DRC-TB-NO-REL-ACTS EQUAL CP-999
            MOVE ZERO TO W-DRM-REL-EXEC-SEQ-NO
            MOVE COND-CODE-REL-DRC TO TCB-USER-CC
            PERFORM EX000-CALL-CCP
            GO TO ZZ000-END-OF-PROCESSING
          WHEN OTHER
            MOVE M-DRC-TB-NO-REL-ACTS TO M-REL-EXEC-SEQ-NO
            PERFORM UNTIL M-RESULT-U50812D EQUAL DGR-NO-FIND
                       OR M-RESULT-U50812D EQUAL DGR-END-DATA
              ADD +1 TO M-REL-EXEC-SEQ-NO
              MOVE DGA-READ-KEY-EQ TO M-ACTION-U50812D
              MOVE P-REL-ACCESS-ACTY TO TCB-ACTIVITY
              PERFORM CA000-CALL-PEM
            END-PERFORM
            MOVE M-REL-EXEC-SEQ-NO TO W-DRM-REL-EXEC-SEQ-NO
        END-EVALUATE
    END-EVALUATE
  WHEN OTHER
    CONTINUE
END-EVALUATE
```
