```
I was at a meeting with some Tourism people here and one of them referred to 
the Bay of Plenty (I live in Tauranga, in the Western end of the Bay) as 
"New Zealand's Florida".

I honestly don't know whether I should be pleased or saddened by that.

I've never been to Florida.

But I absolutely love living where I do.

They have put a new promotional video onto YouTube and the opening (and 
closing) sequences are of Mount Maunganui and the entrance to Tauranga 
Harbour. (I walk around the Mount base track - around 5 kilometres - 
whenever I need to think quietly, and a number of programming (and 
management) problems have been solved on that walk... :-) Besides, the 
exercise and getting out of the house does me no harm either...)

I'd like for as many of you as possible to take a look at the video (it 
would gladden the hearts of a very hard-working and under-acknowledged 
committee here), but in particular, for people who've been to or live in 
Florida, I'd be very interested to hear whether you think it is a fair 
comparison.

(Please note: we are not trying to compete with Florida and it isn't a zero 
sum game (everyone who visits NZ is one less for Florida, and vice versa...) 
really, would just like to know whether this is a fair comparison.)

Cheers,

Pete.
```

```
Pete, I've never lived in Florida, but I have travelled there a lot. I live in
Alabama, which borders NW Florida, and it's a quick weekend hop to
NE Florida from here. Except for the Keys, and the lower SW part of
the state, I've been just about all over Florida many times.

Beaches: As far as the beaches themselves, Florida, especially the Gulf
Coast, has beaches that make any California beach look like trash, and
I've seen both. They are very white, very fine sand. The beaches in the
video looked more grayish.

Manmade tourist attractions: Florida has manmade tourist attractions that
rank among the very best. Disney World, Universal Studios, and gobs
more, particularly around Orlando, but all over the state. I've spent time
in Southern California too, and it's possible they may edge Florida just a
bit, but it would very close. The video didn't show a lot about this, so I
can't really compare.

Other natural scenery: In this, there is no competition: NZ blows Florida
away. Florida is flat, sandy, mostly pine trees and palmetto bushes. The
scenery, other than the stunningly beautiful beaches, is boooorrriiinnngg!
There are no mountains in Florida, and only a few small hills (the highest
point in Florida is Britton Hill at 345 ft.). They do have some natural
wonders, like Silver Springs and some deep underwater caves, but
nothing like the stunning scenery in NZ.
```
