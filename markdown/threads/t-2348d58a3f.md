A couple of things that I can say (before others jump in)

1) Do you use OS/VS COBOL or CMPR2 on the mainframe?  If so, you need Micro
Focus to emulate these (IBM doesn't support these with VA).

2) Do you need to interface with Assembler (S/370 type) in your applications
on the PC? If so, you need Micro Focus (IBM doesn't support this).

3) Do you need a (fairly full) set of LE callable services?  If so, you need
Micro Focus - IBM doesn't support these on the PC.

4) Do you want a JCL emulator on the PC? If so, go with Micro Focus.

5) Do you want the SAME compiler (front end) on the PC as on the Mainframe
(i.e. messages will be IDENTICAL) between PC and mainframe?  Then use IBM
(Micro Focus "emulates" stuff on the mainframe, IBM uses the same front end).

5)Do you want a choice between IEEE and S/390 floating-point storage types?
If so, go with IBM.  Micro Focus *only* supports the S/390 format (when in
mainframe emulator mode).

6) If you are interested in IMS, CICS, DB2 or PL/I "interfaces", then both
products supply these, but you will need to look at the pros and cons of how
they do it.  (Again, in MOST cases, Micro Focus uses "emulators" while IBM
uses the "real thing").

7) Both products have "remote file access" (i.e. your PC product can access
"real" mainframe files and databases, not down-loaded copies) but the ease of
doing this and the resources on the mainframe will vary.

8) If you are interested in mainframe compatible OO syntax, then go with IBM.
Micro Focus does NOT provide syntax support for IBM's current OO syntax.

9) Are you trying to produce a "production" executable on the PC?  If so,
look strongly at IBM.  Micro Focus' current "mainframe development product"
does NOT produce executables (although their older products did).  Therefore,
you might need BOTH NetExpress and Mainframe Express if you want to do BOTH
mainframe development and PC development with COBOL.

   ***

Bottom-line is that both have PROs and CONs.
  You may ALSO want to look at CA-Realia if you are thinking about products
in this area.  It has its own PROs and CONs.

There are other "details" that you will need to look at, but I thought this
would get the discussion going.

A few more PROs and CONs that I have thought about:

A) If you use the MLE (Millennium Language Extensions) that are "native" in
IBM Mainframe COBOLs, then you will need to use the IBM product on the PC.
(Micro Focus doesn't provide emulation for them.)

B) The "professional" (?) version of the IBM product includes a PC version of
the mainframe "CCCA" product can provide automated source code conversion
from the "older" IBM dialects to the newer ones.

C) The Micro Focus product includes (or at least used to include) both WFL
(the product for converting files from various formats to others - including
EBCDIC/ASCII "intelligent" conversions) and the data-file editor (lets you
edit data files based on their record descriptions).  I don't know for sure,
but I don't think that the IBM products include such facilities.

D) It may show my background (Xpediter and Micro Focus) but in my opinion the
IBM debugger verges on the "user-hostile" while the Micro Focus Animator
(both the older character mode one and newer GUI one) is QUITE user-friendly
and intuitive.  On the other hand, the IBM product provides "remote"
debugging of the actual mainframe object code (no "emulation" of machine
instructions) and does provide better inter-language capabilities (the same
interface for PL/I, C, *and* COBOL).

   ***
