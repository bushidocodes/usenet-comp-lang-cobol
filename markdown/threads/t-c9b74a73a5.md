In answer to "why you need to know"  - the answer is that about 1/3 of the
COBOL syntax in the Language Reference Manual will cause compile-time errors
if you are using the CMPR2 compiler option.   I assume that you are supped
to be able to figure out what syntax is valid and what is not.

P.S.  If you are moving from DOS/VS COBOL to "COBOL LE" (more properly
called "COBOL for OS/390 & VM" in its latest version) then you are really
going to need some training on what syntax (and other features) are allowed
and not allowed.  Where you are coming from a VSE environment, am I correct
in guessing that you have a lot of CICS programs?  If so, the ENTIRE
programming interface will change dramatically.  (No more BLL-cells and lots
of new stuff).  Good luck!

If your IBMer would like to talk to me (and I am not now - nor was I ever -
an IBMer), please feel free to send me private email and I will be happy to
give you my phone number.
