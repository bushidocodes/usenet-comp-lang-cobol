It depends on what you work on during that year, Kennard. But now is a
great time to get into COBOL, because most companies who aren't desperate
for COBOL programmers now, will be shortly. Y2k is not going to wait, and
there simply aren't enough warm bodies out there to get the job done. Keep
plugging away, and ask companies if they will take you on for a trial
period on contract, so that they can get rid of you instantly and
painlessly if you don't measure up. My contracts explicitly state that my
clients can terminate the contract on the spot with no notice. This may
help you get your foot in the door, and get some experience. Once you get
some experience, you can look for something better. Most companies have
been burned so many times by lawsuits, when trying to fire bad employees,
that they are reluctant to put someone on the payroll they don't have
confidence in.
