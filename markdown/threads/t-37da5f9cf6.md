NobleNet is looking for a technology/business partner to create,
market and support a COBOL language mapping for our third generation,
rapid-prototyping RPC (middleware) compiler for OS9.

NobleNet RPC 3.0 allows ordinary application coders, to build
sophisticated, multi-tier, client-server applications. The tools
automatically generate fully-commented, open, network communications
code.

Supported features include: asynchronous, nowait, callback,
synchronous, broadcast, batch, and forking calls, arguably the highest
level External Data Representation (XDR) in the industry, naming, load
balancing, threading, and security with a clip in filter SDK.

Currently, the product is supported in more than 40 environments.

We also have a database independent, universal ODBC driver, suitable
for OEMing, that would make it possible for embedded systems/devices
to access ODBC databases.

We are open to proposals including the private branding of a product
under the partner's label with the proviso that it remains compatible
with the balance of NobleNet's RPC compilers. Moreover, in this role
as the supplier and supporter of NobleNet RPC in particular
environments, NobleNet would be willing to make the partner the master
distributor to all other NobleNet resellers for particular platforms.

More than 300 companies and organizations enjoy the benefits of
NobleNet client-server middleware development tools. These include:
Atria, Digital, Federal Reserve Bank of N.Y., IBM, Object Systems,
JC Penney, Picturetel, Motorola, SetPoint, Southwestern Bell,
State Street Bank & Trust, Sun Microsystems, Thomson Financial, The
Mitre Corporation, TRW, Walmart, and Xerox Systems.

please contact me.
