Years on back I read something that compared writing COBOL to writing 
music in one of the formalised 'high counterpoint' styles common before 
Bach changed the way many folks dealt with music... an unyielding set of 
rules must be followed but inside those boundaries creativity can be 
exercised and demonstrated; many canons have been written but only one is 
Pachelbel's in D.

(yes, I know it is not, strictly speaking, a 'canon'... but perhaps this 
discussion might be left for another time)

Anyhow... I was catching up on a previous issue of The Economist (22 - 28 
Apr 2006) and came across an article about Jordi Savall, a Catalan who is 
leading a resurgence in the playing of the viola da gamba.  One paragraph 
describes a Master's Class Mr Savall was teaching at the Metropolitan 
Museum in New York.  From the article:

--begin quoted text:

The masterclass seemed surprised to hear they should perform with the 
score.  Bur for Mr Savall memorisation is the same as playing on 
autopilot. and stifles a necessary element of unpredictability.  'The only 
contact we have with the composer is the music,' he explains.  'It is 
important to have this relationship, but it's a balance between discipline 
and freedom.'

--end quoted text

There have been discussions here about matters of balance... readibility 
versus efficiency, for example.  It gave me a smile to see another matter 
of balance - technique versus improvisation - mentioned in an article 
about a field (music) which, long ago, I saw being related to COBOL...

... but, of course, I'se jes' easily amused.

DD
