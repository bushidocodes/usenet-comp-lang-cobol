```
COBOL.Standards-Commitee.Proposal 
vs. 
Proposal Of Standards-Commitee Of COBOL?

30 posts and counting in the ongoing debate for a feature that's been available
in PL/I for several decades, but one that the COBOL Standards Commitee feels
can't be attained, at least in this Millennium...

Hey, Standards Dudes (And Dudettes):

1. In France I might go to la maison de Jim (pardon my poor recollection of
high school French), but in America, I go to Jim's house, NOT to the house of
Jim (OTOH, the House of Blues is another story).

2. The lack of this feature has given rise to tens of millions of lines of
COBOL in unnecessary copybooks and record descriptions embedded in COBOL
programs that are essentially duplicates of "real" copybooks, with the addition
of a prefix (e.g., OLD-, NEW-) to employ prefix notation, needlessly
complicating coding and Y2K remediation efforts. Some shops have even taken to
writing compiler exits to allow a prefixing convention for COPY statements.

3. It might be instructive to pick up another programming language's manual
from time to time to see what you might learn - PL/I, SQL, REXX, VB, VBA etc.
all permit this type of prefixing notation.

4. As far as legacy code goes, backwards compatibility could be provided. Also,
it wouldn't take a compiler design genius to write code to replace ' OF '
notation with '.' notation (or vice versa with a preprocessor approach).

My two cents...
```

```
1) To the best of my knowledge no one has yet come up with a final proposal
to MAKE to the Standards committee. (So I don't know how or why you think
this has already be rejected)

2) Yes, the Standards committee has said that they do not think they
can/will add ANY new features into the next draft at this time. However, it
has been "open for input" for over 7 years now - and they have over 150
major or semi-major corrections that still need to be made to what is
already done.  Do you really want to wait for a Standard until they not only
fix these but add and fix MORE new features?

3) There is a "volunteer" list available from the chair of J4 for work to be
done on the existing draft.  If you think you want to get the Standard out
more quickly or get more features in sooner *AND* you are willing to follow
(and understand) the Standard's drafting rules, I am positive that any help
you might want to offer would be accepted quite happily!
```

```
To clarify something, it is POSSIBLE to contribute (both comments and
proposals) withOUT being a member of the committee.  Normally, this is done
by those of us who have either been on the committee in the past or at least
worked with it in some detail.  HOWEVER, there are also those who submit
papers without ever seeing the committee or going to a meeting.  (Often a
committee person will "take over" the paper once it is discussed and needs
updating - so it is very helpful if you can provide it electronically - but
I assume most readers of this NG can do that.)

IMHO, there probably are NOT many members of this NG who could contribute
significantly to the draft at the stage it is at now (because of the
"bureaucracy" involved in proposal writing), but I do think that offers
might well be accepted.
```
