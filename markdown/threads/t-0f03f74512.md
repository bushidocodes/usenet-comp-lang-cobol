It is possible that shine98 doesn't quite understand the 'jargon' :)  So to clarify if that is the case:

When refering to an alphanumeric field regardless of the length programmers usually say the field is PIC X.  When refering to a numeric field regardless of the length programmers usually say PIC 9.  Of corse some will say the field is DISPLAY which (at least to me - a mainframer) means the field is PIC X also, or a group name which by definition defaults to PIC X even if the data stored in the sub fields below it are PIC 9 .  A packed field is always PIC 9 Comp-3, and a binary field is always PIC 9 COMP (again at least to me and my collegues).

An example:
Employee Name could be say 25 positions long - in your program PIC X(25).  When talking about the field one would just say is is a PIC X or DISPLAY field.

Social Security Number (assuming it is not stored with hyphens) is in your program PIC 9(9) but one would say it is a PIC 9 field.  One could also say it was a packed field if it was defined as PIC 9(9) COMP-3.

Webmaster is trying to center an Employee Name type field (more than 1 position)  that is usually stored left justified by reason of its PIC.  I've seen some interesting answers to that question.
