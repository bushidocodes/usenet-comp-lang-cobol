Back to searching the Web and knowing young people are impressionable, I was in my
generation, so are you any different. You have to differentiate between the 'factual' as
per measurements above and 'opinion'.

Factual :

This is an interesting one - HMS Victory - Horatio Nelson's flagship at the Battle of
Trafalgar, 1805 Oct 21 - defeated combined French and Spanish fleets. Nelson was mortally
wounded by a French sniper.

Back when my son was about five, I was assembling one of those plastic model kits; I had
the hull built. "Mr. Curious" reached up, dropped it on the floor and broke it.
Over here he had a pang of conscience about the age of 12-15 and bought me a new Airfix kit
- haven't built it yet. Perhaps one day......

Go googling and come up with about a dozen sites dedicated just to HMS Victory. You'll
appreciate the model kits are a bit limited on describing colour schemes. Well I find a
site which gives me the whole shebang on the colour scheme. Primarily black, and what I
knew to be Yellow or Gold is referred to as Yellow Ochre. But get this - it then lists
parts of the model which weren't kosher in 1805. (Don't ask me what the terminology is) :-

Mizzen Royal Stay and Mizzen Royal Backstay - to be omitted: not introduced until 1810.
Mizzen Preventer Stay - To be omitted : not introduced until circa 1815.

My home town was Portsmouth so I've been over the Victory some 3 or 4 times. Magnificent.
Without checking, built about 1791 and was still floating until 1922.
Now dry docked because the massive oak timbers were being gnawed to death by the Death
Watch Beetle.  They still commemorate Nelson's  death on October 21 each year on board the
Victory. It also serves as  the flagship of the C-in-C Portsmouth.

Opinion :

Really got to watch for this one. After all, anybody can post an 'essay' on a Website - so
you have to use your judgement. Example, a while back I mentioned Operation Sea Lion which
was a German plan during WW2 to invade UK about 1940. Richard (in NZ) refuted that it could
happen. His comment was backed up by an 'essay' on the Web.

The Web authors main contention was that the Royal Navy would not have let it happen - i.e.
the Germans making it the 21 miles from Calais to Dover. The RN would have sent in
battleships, and God knows what else from Plymouth and Portsmouth. Plausible. But prior to
this time the RN had already lost some capital ships to bombing or U-boats.

Now let's move on some two years later to Dieppe - Wednesday 19 August 1942. A one day raid
by Canadians, supported by two British commando units on the flanks and a handful of US
Rangers. (Plus the air battle overhead was the biggest since the Battle of Britain).

The Canadians had more casualties, (dead, wounded and POWs) during the period 06:00 to
18:00 that one day than they had in  their slog up through Italy along with British, Poles,
Indians and Mark Clark's US divisions.

Dieppe is a sleepy little seaside town - big pebbles on the beach, (which proved disastrous
for the Calgary Tank Regiment), plus either side, headlands which had artillery
emplacements.

Now back to the RN using battleships in 1940 - Lord Louis Mountbatten (cousin to George
VI), and head of Combined Operations, approached the First Sea Lord asking for battleship
support. The reply he got, "You must be mad Dicky !". I don't need to spell it out -
exactly the same reason that they wouldn't have used them in 1940.

All sorts of things went wrong, but without doubt, had a battleship's guns crushed those
artillery pieces on the headlands, it would have been a slightly different outcome. As it
was they had to rely on RN support with 4-inch guns from destroyers - just like using pea
shooters.

OK you can debate the issue with "what if....". The whole venture didn't happen because the
RAF won the Battle of Britain. There are certainly photographs of the Wermacht training on
French beaches, plus with Germanic thoroughness there was a meticulous plan.  And part of
that plan, not available until documentation was captured at the end of WW2 - was a plan to
take yours truly and other kids between about the ages of 5-12, (providing we looked
'Aryan'), back to Germany to be inculcated into Nazism.

You wouldn't now being asking if I was from Quebec. In JFK style I could reply "Ich bin ein
Berliner".

Caveat emptor. Let the buyer beware.

Websites - Let the reader beware ! Make your own judgements.
