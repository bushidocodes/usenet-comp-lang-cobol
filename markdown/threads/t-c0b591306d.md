mais oui

Several years back, I did s contract for a major US company
that had been bought out by a major French company. It seems
that the new parent had decided that all code would be replaced
by the French systems. Unfortunately, all documentation was in
French.

In their wisdom, the American counterparts (in a fit of pique actually)
realized that they had not a person that understood a word of French
so they sent it all up to Brampton, Ontario. They knew, you see, that
everybody in Canada spoke French.

In addition, it was the worst designed system I have ever seen. By the
time I got there, they had not cut an invoice in seven months, and had
lost about 90 million dollars worth on inventory(shipped, but who knew
where? only Honeywell Bull, and it was not telling).

After spending three weeks commuting between Toronto and the US
(both side sabotaging everything I did, in full cover your ass mode, like
if the 600 of us cannot get it working nobody can), I finally scrounged
a backup tape, and managed to dump it to cards. I sorted them in
order on a card sorter I found ware-housed (They promised me
computer time in 8-10 weeks), I printed the cards on unit record equipment
and highlighted the appropriate columns, and set a dozen typists to work.

Three weeks later, I placed 87 million dollars worth of manually typed
invoices on the president's desk, and told him to fired the entire data
processing department, both countries. Their budget was in the millions,
I did it with typists for under twenty grand.

I have always kept in mind since that politics is more important than code
in systems design.
