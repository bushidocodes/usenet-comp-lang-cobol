This seems to have spawned a few comments so let me clarify, if I can.  
There are several ways of making payments via the bank account here.

1.  Cheque - simple and readily understandable, even if spellings differ 
on different sides of the Pond.  Oddly enough, I caused a stir among 
some Swiss friends who were most impressed by my having a chequebook as 
they didn't use them at all.  Apparently you needed to be really rich to 
warrant a chequebook but maybe this has all changed since then.

2.  Standing Order.  You instruct the bank to pay a regular amount 
into someone else's bank account at a regular time such as 1st day of 
the month or 17th June or whatever.  You are able to cancel this at any 
time.

3.  Bank Giro Credit.  Giro is a throwback, it's the original name for 
the Bankers Aautomated Clearing System, or BACS, as it's now known.  
This is used for one-off transmission of funds to someone else's 
account.  The key here is that you instigate payment as and when you 
want.  This is what is used for salary payments and the like.  I don't 
think that reversals can be effected but I'm not a real expert and I 
don't have a manual to hand.

4.  Direct Debit.  This is one that might cause you concern.  It's a 
common enough method over here and is usually used for regular bill 
payments like utility bills, insurance premiums and the like.  You can 
specify a fixed sum or you can leave it open.  You specifically sign an 
authorisation for your bank to pay it.

For instance, my phone bill is paid by DD (Direct Debit, not you Doc, 
though any contributions would be gratefully received and genuine 
recognition of your munificence given to anyone who might slight you), 
and this is paid quarterly.  The phone company sends me their bill about 
ten days before they apply for payment.  This gives me the opportunity 
to accuse them of being cheats and liars, and I generally do.

In the final analysis, I could get really caned but the element of trust 
has been hard to establish - thirty years ago, there were plenty of 
mistakes but nowadays the system is much more reliable.  I'd be careful 
about who I signed a DD mandate for though, even now.  The banks prefer 
DD as a payment method, presumably because it's cheapest for them, and 
so there's a lot of effort put into making sure that it works.

Added to these basic methods, there's all sorts of fast payment schemes 
both inter-bank and intra-bank.  I have a dial-up to the bank, as I 
mentioned and can instigate any of these depending on the situation.  It 
would have made my paying Network Solutions for a domain name a whole
