I've used Brief for over 10 years, since well before Borland acquired it.
You are correct, it is an outstanding programmer's editor, often the
standard by which other editors are compared.  Any decent editor today
will provide Brief emulation of some sort.  As far as I know, it is still
available, but Borland has not improved it since acquiring it.  The only
problem with Brief is that it is still a DOS editor, and the world is
moving to Windows, whether we like it or not.  I have already bought
Multi-Edit, and am in the process of learning it.  It's traumatic, like
getting divorced and remarried! :-)

For programmers, editors can be a subject of passion rivaling that for
their loved ones. ;-)   But a good, relatively inexpensive editor is
Multi-Edit.  You can buy it on the web pretty cheap ($129 US).  My only
gripe is that you *cannot* get a printed manual, and there is no complete
manuel, even for download.  The online help is good though, and Multi-Edit
is very powerful and flexible.  You can also download the 97 page book
that comes with the retail version as a PDF file.
