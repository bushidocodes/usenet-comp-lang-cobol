Try: 
  01  ws-digit    PIC s9(4) Comp Value 0.
  01  redefines ws-digit.
      03          PIC X.
      03 ws-byte  PIC X.

  MOVE your-input-byte TO ws-byte.

And voila! Ws-digit contains the number. Now your number is in
numeric format and you can move it to other numeric fields (any
number of digits) or to an edit field for display/printing. 

Or you can use it as it is in an IF or EVALUATE.

(I am assuming the first 2 bits of your input byte are zeros, if they
are not you should divide ws-digit by 64, and the remainder will be
your "lucky number").
