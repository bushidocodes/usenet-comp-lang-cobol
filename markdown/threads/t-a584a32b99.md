You're right.  It does vary between COBOL implementors.

On WANG VS and IBM AS400, COMP is packed decimal.  In MF COBOL on unix, COMP
is binary.  I believe that MF COBOL equates COMP with COMP-4 but it may be
COMP-5.

Any way you have to know how it is implemented in your COBOL.

If the implementation is binary then the form PIC S9(n) COMP would give:
  BIN(15) if n=1 - 4
  BIN(31) if n=5 - 9.
If I remember my PL/1 correctly.
