Amortization is the process you use to calculate a fixed loan payment
given the principle, interest rate and term of the loan.  Present
value relates to money accruing interest or money that is held in an
annuity.  The definition and formula for present value can be
expressed as:

The total present amount (P) which will supply an annuity (N) at the
end of each year for (n) years beginning one year hence (assuming that
in successive years the amount not yet paid out earns interest at rate
(r), compounded annually) is,

1)  Calculate simple annuity (sn) at rate (r) = ((1+r)**n-1)/r
2)  Calculate annuity (an) at r = ((1-(1+r)**-n)/r
3)  Calculate Present Value  P =( N(sn))/(1 + r)**n 
      which should equal N(an)

As you can see, this is much more complex then amortization.
