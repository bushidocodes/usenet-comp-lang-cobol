About 30 years ...

If I remember correctly, RPG was designed in the 1960s as a Report
Generator for the 1401 to help installations make the transition from
plugwired control panels (tabulators, mainly) to stored programs. In the
early 1970s, RPG II came along with enhanced capabilities on S/360 DOS,
System/3 (then S/36) and was ported to OS/VS (i.e. VS1 and MVS) in the
early 1980s. RPG II was further enhanced as RPG III for the S/38 and its
successor, the AS/400. I recently heard that there is an RPG IV now,
perhaps is it object-oriented? I vaguely remember RPG compilers on
non-IBM mainframes, such as Univac and Burroughs, and perhaps also, on
PCs. After some hesitation, IBM added RPG to the list of SAA languages.

Today, RPG II is quite popular on VSE and RPG III on the AS/400. Last
year, we converted from VSE to MVS one of your clients which had over 4000
RPG II programs; they have even more now.

RPG a odd-looking language? yes, definitely! A bad language? probably
not, it's quite powerful and predictable - and it's the first language I
learned, many moons ago!
