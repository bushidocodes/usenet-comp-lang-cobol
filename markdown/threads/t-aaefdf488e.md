Bruce Scheirer recently posted the observation that Intel may have
introduced this feature as much to attack the problem of counterfeit or
stolen Pentium chips than for programmatic use.  Programs can never be
certain that, when querying to find out what the ID# is, they have been
told the truth.  Software could have intercepted the request and
substituted a forged ID# instead of the actual one.  (Software to do
this has already been published.)

"Cookies" and other readily-obtainable information are already widely
distributed, in clear, on the Internet to allow web-sites to recognize
users who have visited them before.  A built-in ID# would not really
make the situation any worse than it already is.  (You are already
embarrassingly "identifiable" on the net.)

All that a built-in ID# would really do is make the =chip=
identifiable.  Mainframe computers have had CPUIDs since time
immemorial, and they are used chiefly for copy protection purposes.  How
successful they would be for this purpose in the rough-and-tumble world
of PCs is ... doubtful at best.
