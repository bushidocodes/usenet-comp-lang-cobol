This is an annoyance with MicroSoft updates also. It is VERY difficult to 
get an earlier version of something installed over a later one. I have 
learned to do it but it is ugly and requires messing with the Registry. Best 
avoided.

Sometimes, because .DLLs are shared it can be problematic to re-install a 
product then find your action has impacts on other products. Fortunately, 
I've only ever experienced this once.

I think what is annoying is when any vendor refuses to allow you to do what 
you WANT to do, assuming that you will want support and that they will only 
provide it for the latest incarnation of their product. They should give you 
fair warning then allow you to do whatever you want. It is YOUR system, not 
theirs...
