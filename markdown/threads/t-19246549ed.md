Having an ODO (Occurs Depending ON) under another OCCURS clause *or* having
an OCCURS clause under an ODO is an "old" IBM extension. (I think it may have
been legal in the '68 Standard - but its rules were contradictory).  It is
explicitly DIS-allowed in the '85 Standard (and the draft of the next
Standard).  Some vendors still support it - IBM calls it a "complex ODO" -
and documents it as an extension.

As for undeliverable mail, many pieces of software used for reading and
posting to newsgroups have a place to put "reply to id".  (I know that all
the variations on Outlook do - and I think others do as well).

If you put some "spam" rejecter in this field, you will cut down on
"automatically generated" spam that comes from those developing mailing lists
from NG posts.   I have done this - as have several others.  I also include a
"spelled out" version of my ID that can be used in my signature line.   I
hope that not too many people are bothered by needing to "forward" or CC
notes to me - rather than using REPLY - but it HAS cut down on the spam that
I get.  (My ID in the FAQ still causes me to get more than I like, but that
seems worth it to me)

Another "issue" is that some ISPs are set up so that you can either default
to REJECTING mail from specific other ISPs (e.g. hotmail) - or where you have
to explicitly ALLOW a user to contact you.  Again, this is to prevent SPAM
but is a step that at least I haven't taken yet.
