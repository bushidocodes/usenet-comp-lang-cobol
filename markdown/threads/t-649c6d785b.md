Apparently, Thane Hubbell posted a message about INSPECT a while back
that I didn't see because of our flakey server. It was something like
this:


›
› I was trying out some things I read in your book, Don. specifically
› about
› inspect with the ab-phrase.

My book is a little misleading when I said you can bracket text. You
can bracket it as long as the delimiters differ.

› Here is the "example" settled on.  There is some ambiguity here, and I
› get
…[20 more quoted lines elided]…
› 
IDENTIFICATION DIVISION.
PROGRAM-ID. TEST2.
AUTHOR. THANE HUBBELL.
REMARKS. CHECK INSPECT ACTION.
WORKING-STORAGE SECTION.
01 WORK-FIELD PIC X(20) VALUE "aaa/aaaaa/aaa".
PROCEDURE DIVISION.
A-0000-MAINLINE.
DISPLAY "Before=" WORK-FIELD.
INSPECT WORK-FIELD CONVERTING "a" TO "A" AFTER "/"
BEFORE "/".
DISPLAY "After=" WORK-FIELD.
STOP RUN.


This is rather subtle. According to the standard, the item is checked
for the first occurrence of the slash (the word INITIAL is implied for
BEFORE and AFTER). Then, because BEFORE is present, we say only those
characters in front of it are eligible. However, because AFTER is
also present, we say that only those characters after it are eligible.
Because these two eliminate everything, no characters are eligible.
CA-Realia is wrong and Micro Focus and Tandem are right.

I will attempt to get a clarifying statement into the next standard.
Hopefully, this will make it into the vendor's reference manuals
