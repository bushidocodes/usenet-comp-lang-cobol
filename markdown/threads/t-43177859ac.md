```
Page 334, 13.16.41.3, PICTURE clause, general rule 11a,
requires that "at least one symbol '9' and ...".

This appears to be a substantive change potentially affecting
existing programs; but is not shown, as such, in Appendix D.

The PICTURE clause -(16)V-- shows as valid when checked
with Micro Focus 3.2 and ANS85 flagging. It also appears to
be valid based upon text in both Micro Focus and IBM LRMs.
However, not having a copy of the ANS 85 standard, I cannot
compare against the previous standard.

We know from a previous thread, "Picture Clause Format -
Redefinition Problem", that at least one program uses a similar
PICTURE clause and, therefore, will be affected.

Under the draft standard, PICTURE -(16)V--, would no longer
be valid because it does not have "at least one symbol '9' and ..."
in the PICTURE clause.

Could others, in the newsgroup, verify that -(16)V-- is valid in
other compilers with ANS85 standard flagging.

Do others agree with my conclusion that this constitutes a
"substantive change potentially affecting existing programs"
and that it should be noted in Appendix D of the draft?

A copy of this post was NOT sent to the J4 chair.
------------------
Rick Smith
```
