so what). Is COBOL going to stay for another 20 years ?

Do companies develop new application in COBOL?
Can COBOL survive outside the mainframe ?
Should I learn more about OO COBOL ?
Should I drop out of my school and learn C/C++ UNIX in another school ?
(I like COBOL more then C)

You will find cobol on just about all platforms. This alone means
that the elimination of cobol is a ways off (who would spend the time
developing a compiler for something thats going away). The latest mainframe
cobol -cobol for mvs - uses the same run time library as C and pl1, it has
function calls and pointers (I beleive local variables is coming shortly).
Just a few years ago the word was that mainframes were dead, you can find
some really funny articles in the Wall Street Journal on why mainframes were
a thing of the past. Yet IBM sales of manframes keep increasing (see last 2
years ok IBM financial reports). Your school has a pretty good selection of
coarses, though it is a little more weighted towards mainframe/mvs. You'll
be able to apply what you've learned on many platforms and find that cobol is
on most of them. Always look at what you're doing though - no single
language does everything best. You'll find uses for C and C++ at points - I
wish more places would teach assembler as there are still a lot of little
routines that use it and it make reading a dump so much easier.
