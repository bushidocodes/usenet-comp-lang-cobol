This is reference modification.  General format is identifier(m:n) where
identifier has usage DISPLAY.  It means the substring of identifier from
position m for a length of n.  The special case where n is omitted means
the substring from position m to the end.

This has nothing to do with tables, but everything to do with reference
modification (COBOL's way of saying 'substring').

MOVE SPACES TO naam(k :)

means move spaces to the field naam, begining at the k'th position, continuing
to the end of the field.

Reference modification can be applied to any DISPLAY item; the syntax is:

data_item_name(start:[length])

'start' must be specified

'length' is optional (hence the brackets, but do not actually put brackets in
your code)

the colon (:) is required; this is what tells COBOL you are talking reference
modification, not subscripts or indexes.

'start' and 'length' may be specified as hard-coded integers, numeric data
items, or numeric expressions; if an expression resolves to have a fractional
part, the fractional part is discarded

hope this helps.

Nick check out REFERENCE MODIFICATION :-

01 myname     pic x(12).
01 outfield   Pic x(????)

- move myname       to outfield (move whole shebang)
- move myname(1:12) to outfield (move whole shebang)
- move myname(1: )  to outfield (no second digit, so means move 'all') 
- move myname(1:3)  to outfield (you're moving 1, 2 and 3)
- move myname(4: )  to outfield (you're moving 4 through 12) 
- move myname(4:3)  to outfield (you're moving 4, 5 and 6)
