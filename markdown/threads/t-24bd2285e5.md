```
For the obvious reason ('to buy stuff') I recently visited a branch of the 
Ross Stores.  Those unfamiliar with this chain might research them at 
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ross_Dress_for_Less and those outside of the 
USA might find local near-equivalents by consulting 
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TJX_Companies .

While wandering through this local Church of Consumerism I overheard a 
trio chatting in what sounded like some Eastern European language; to my 
untutored ear it all came across as repetitions of 'Brzezinski'.  It 
chanced that all of us left the emporium nearly simultaneously.

The store uses multiple doors and an entry/exit area to keep down electric 
costs.  I reached the inner door, opened it and stood back, saying 
'Please, go ahead, it is my great pleasure.'

They went on to the second door, which one member of the trio held open 
for me, saying 'Now it is *our* great pleasure.'

I returned with 'Wonderful!  Now everyone gets a chance!'

All laughed.  Somehow, we all just felt... better.

DD
```

```
better qualified than him to do that and she certainly kept both of us in

"Because it reminds us we are civilized, it reminds us to think of and 
respect other people, but much more importantly, it makes people feel
```
