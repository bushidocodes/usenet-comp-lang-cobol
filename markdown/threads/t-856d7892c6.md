What you say is as I am sure you know not new.  This prediction has been 
the objective of users for decades.  Objective, make programming like 
Engineering.  Learn to read the books, keep up with the new materials and 
methods, and "it will be like something we can't get rid off anyway."  Quote 
is my thought as I can't seem to verbalize my point.

My only point in this whole discussion is that each branch of engineering 
has a continuing growth in facilities, methods, and demands.  The pieces 
of yesterday have proven that what is done by the book today will not 
stand up in the future.  Change is inevitable.  In each area of development 
of the tools there will be a need to refine, and invent, and throw away.  New 
insights will cause some of the difficult things of today to become simple 
in the guide book of tomorrow.  Currently, not everyone involved is working 
on the book in which they have the most knowledge .   A lot of refinement 
will occur, and still there will be new things that make much of it obsolete.

In my lingo, BFD.  I won't see it, and I feel most of the current speculation 
is miss directed as most of what we know is coming will not be here in our 
lifetime. Our effort might be better made in the area of the refinement of 
the tools we are now using.

Just my opinion.
