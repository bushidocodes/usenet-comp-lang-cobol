I'm not saying that you should choose a totally inefficient algoritm
just because it looks nice.  But no shortcuts.  Never ever thinking
about machine-spesific details.  Because the machine WILL be changed.
No optimizing for this or that compiler, or the quirks of any OS or
network. Indeed, I think it is better not to know such things at all,
as they just provide temptations and warp our unconscious efforts.
And I do hold the opinion that a good compiler should be able to do
such things as relocate one-off subroutines.  The programmer should
be free to concentrate on the structure.  But even if not, the costs
of extra bugs and the hair-tearing by subsequent programmers will
probably cost more than a few seconds lost to overhead.

And even monkey code WILL be executed at lightning speed five years
from now.  In fact, some of the most popular software in the world
would have been regarded as monkey code a few years ago.
