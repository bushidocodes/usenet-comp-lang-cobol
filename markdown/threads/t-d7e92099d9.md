Just a nickel plug's worth of unsolicited comment, but
irregardless ;) of chapter and verse, the group as it's presently
composed is an assembly of experience, wit, young'uns questions,
comaraderie, diatribe, opinion, hearty debate, advice, knowledge, all
there to be taken or left. Gosh, it's ... a party, with types of
characters I would like to know ... if physical reality were as one's
anticipation, which it usually isn't.

I've learned more here in a few months than all the college classes
I nodded through.

Most of the threads here follow a predictable pattern. They begin with
a substantive question regarding COBOL programming practice, technique or
issues of profession, employment or business practice. This question
is addressed and responded to and answered by *very* knowledgeable
professionals who offer diverse opinions that dovetail one another.

Then, after the substantive issues are clarified, refined and settled,
a period of joshing and reminiscing begins, which is often filled
with historical anecdote from which I, personally, learn much, filling
in the substantial gaps left from my (disappointing) formal education
experience. And, DD offers gems of ancient Greek and insights to
the range of logic and (pardon the word) philosophy --- which means,
I believe, love of learning.

This group's threads nearly always begin with COBOL questions, and
turn into knowledgeable conversation. True, some then degenerate
into shouts and mudslinging, but ... well, don't stay that long
at the party. Don't most newsreaders have a thread kill feature?

Well, talk is cheap; but, I guess I've rambled more than a nickel's worth.

God's grace to all.

Mark Twenhafel
