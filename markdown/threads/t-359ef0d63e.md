I find the concept expressed in the demonstration algorithm
useful in many pattern matching situations which require finding
a cluster of similar characters. Use the INSPECT...CONVERTING to
map all the similar characters to a single code point (zero in the
above example) then use INSPECT...TALLYING to scan quickly the
converted string.

James,

I'd like to help you but first I need to know more about the text string.
When you get the string, do you know how the phone numbers are kept in
the string? In other words, let's pretend your string looks like this
567-9010777-6478. In this string of 16 characters you know that every 8
characters you have a complete phone number. So you could move your hold
area into another working storage area to break it up. Such as:

01 New-hold-area.
05 Phone-1 PIC X(08).
05 Phone-2 PIC X(08).
.
.
.
05 Phone-10 PIC X(08).

Or you could use a table. For instance:

01 New-hold-area-table.
05 Phone-number occurs 10 PIC X(08).

Then you would move the HOLD-AREA into the New-hold-area or the
New-hold-area-table. After you do that, you should be able to refer to
each phone number individually Phone-1, Phone-2, etc. or
Phone-number(sub), where sub varies from 1 to 10, depending on which one
you use.

If you don't store the phone numbers in this manner in the string, then
you should consider other alternatives.
