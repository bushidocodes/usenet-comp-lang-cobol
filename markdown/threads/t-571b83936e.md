Tim Josling quotes Bill Klein:

<<It looks (to me) as if Australia is not (currently) a "P" member of SC22.
However, it should have (???) been possible for "O" countries to submit
comments, but not vote.>>

According to the ISO web page, NSAI (Ireland) was neither a member nor an
observer on SC22, yet their vote was considered, so the reverse of this
assertion may well be true -- it may be possible to submit a *vote* as a
non-member, and the convener may elect to include that standards body's
input in the determination of consensus.

Whether it "should have been possible for "O" countries to submit comments,
but not vote" is a red herring.  SAI (Australia) is NOT an "O" member of
SC22.  For that matter, neither is NSAI (Ireland).

Moreover, what's being established is whether to move from FCD to DIS, the
issue is *consensus* on that subject, and comments are an integral part of
*votes* (which themselves are guides to the convener, "straw votes", to
judge whether consensus has been reached).   This is a fundamentally YES/NO
point in the process.

There are comment stages before and after it, and comments may accompany
those YES/NO indications, but comments *without* a vote aren't allowed for
*here*, nor should they be procedurally, whether those "no-vote" comments
come from "P"-members, from "O"-members, or from sources outside the
participatory membership altogether.

Tim also opined:

<<I suspected as much. I am starting to come around to your view that the
process is broken. Standards Australia never sent one word of feedback on my
comments. >>

That's a shame; I am personally convinced that what you have to say on these
issues is worth careful consideration.  But I must point out that, so far as
I know, neither ISO, IEC, JTC1, SC22, WG4, ANSI, NCITS, J4 nor J4-TAG have
anything at all to say about what procedures SAI (or any other national
standards body) uses to decide a national position -- or in fact whether SAI
(or any other national standards body) chooses to participate in the
development of that position at all.   That's between you and SAI, and maybe
the best way to address those procedures -- or the lack of response from
SAI -- is to get off the sidelines and work actively to help the process
along.

Bill wrote:

<<*NONE* of your comments are included in the international comments -
> viewable at:
>
>  http://people.ne.mediaone.net/doncobol/01-0373.pdf

I think that's understandable given SAI's non-membership in SC22, and that
comments are only one part of a ballot in this context.  SAI doesn't seem to
have chosen to be eligible to submit a response to this request that would
have included such comments.

Tim, Don't get me wrong, I'm not saying that your comments shouldn't have
been responded to, or included in an SAI response submitted as part of their
activities as members of JTC1 and/or SC22.  But I'm not at all convinced the
problem has its origins anywhere beyond the borders of Australia in this
particular case, or that the solution to that problem needs to be addressed
at the international level.    Neither ISO nor IEC nor ANSI nor NCITS has
any say over whether SAI chooses to participate in this process, or to what
degree.

I am convinced that there's a body of people in Australia who are truly
concerned about the future of COBOL (and of "Programming languages, their
environments and system software interfaces" in general), but if there's
nobody willing to participate with time and resources, that's not a problem
with the *processes*.  Your comments might as well have been thrown as paper
airplanes onto the field during a football game for all the effectiveness
they would have in influencing SAI, if SAI isn't participating in SC22 in
the first place!

Have you investigated what would be involved in having *formal* input into
SAI's decisions as to what committees of JTC1 they wish to be involved in
(specifically, their participation in SC22 and WG4), and what positions they
should take on behalf of the Australian programming community as part of
their participation in those committees?   Have you thought about "suiting
up and showing up" yourself, and encouraging others to do the same, as a way
of improving the responsiveness of the national standards body of your
country to these issues?

Also, I think there's at least one more formal opportunity for *public*
input coming up, so the fact that your comments didn't get included in the
process of moving from FCD to DIS does not mean they can't be considered in
the DIS-to-FDIS or even the FDIS-to-IS steps.

    -Chuck Stevens

Anyone can send comments to any national body in these public
comments.  ANSI received quite a few from non-USA people.  Of course,
it ultimately has more significance if we get the same information
from a national body than an individual.  All comments received from
any individual anywhere are considered  equally in J4, but national
body comments will get more attention in WG4, which provides
direction.  In fact, the "US J4 Technical Advisory Group" uses the
comments received by ANSI to determine the US position (but foreign
comments ARE accepted with equal weight).
