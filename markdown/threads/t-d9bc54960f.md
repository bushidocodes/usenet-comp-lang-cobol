```
Suddenly, I might have a need for the "official" ISO Standard, which is
priced at a stunning $215, as Bill Klein pointed out a couple of weeks
ago.  Thane Hubbell wrote that he thought the total market for the
standard document would essentially be the companies developing COBOL
compilers to comply with it.  I hope it would be somewhat wider,
including other people like me, who are concerned with good usage of the
compilers.

I did download the Final Committee Draft a couple of months back.  Does
anyone think I need the "official" document, or could I work from that
Final Draft?

Also, I wondered if any of you would care to share your organization's
COBOL Standard?  I would be happy to share what we use now, and what we
come up with.  My organization implements our standard in a program that
is attached to the IBM mainframe COBOL compiler, so that diagnosis is
carried out on every compile (including when programs are put into
production).  Perhaps I could provide at least general information about
doing that, if anyone is interested.

Please reply to the Newsgroup, or feel free to e-mail me.  It's my home
e-mail address, but I'll see it, and be able to get back to you.
Thanks,
Colin Campbell
```
