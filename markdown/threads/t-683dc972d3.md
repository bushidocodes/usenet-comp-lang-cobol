Alistair Maclean wrote:
> Pete - how was the aftershock?

Thanks for asking.

It is simply a tragedy.

For the last month the people of Christchurch have been subject to 
aftershocks on a regular basis. It is quite terrifying and I have friends 
there who are not easily rattled, but the last time I Skyped them I could 
see that they are visibly disturbed by it.

Just when people think it is settling down and a degree of normalcy is 
restored, another series of aftershocks hits them.

The one you are referring to 2 days ago was over 5 on the Richter scale and 
it was situated at a depth of 9 kilometres directly under the city. Being 
that close to the surface (usually they are more than 30 KM deep) makes the 
effect like a magnitude 7.  More buildings were damaged, but worst of all, 
recently restored services and buildings were hit again and this has a 
devastating effect on people's morale.

So far, the estimate of cost for these earthquakes is over $3 billion and 
rising. (We have a total population of just over 4 million).

The people of Christchurch (about 350,000 of them...) 
http://www.christchurch.org.nz/About/ have had a huge amount to endure and 
are doing so with courage and dignity. The stories of people helping each 
other, the heroism amidst carnage, and the untiring efforts to restore vital 
services like water, electricity, phones, and waste disposal are really 
heartwarming and quite humbling. Reconstruction has already started and so 
it is even more painful when these efforts are damaged by further tremors.

That there have been no deaths is simply miraculous and a tribute to the 
systems we have in place for dealing with emergencies like this, the 
readiness and willingness of our Civil Defence, the proper construction of 
buildings, and the general attitude of Kiwis in crisis. Fund raising 
benefits and concerts are going on all over the country, donations from 
businesses and individuals are raising millions but it is a drop in the 
bucket compared to what is needed.

The predictions are that there will be continuing tremors for some months, 
but nobody expected the depth and location of the most recent one.

Life in Christchurch goes on. Schools are open, people are back at work and 
cleaning up is a background task.

But the emotional scars will take much longer to heal.

Pete.
