```
Mainly a salutary message for you younger whiz kids who think you've got
a handle on web applications.

My ISP concerned about home.com financial situation have sent three
messages suggesting their users sign up for a shaw.ca e-mail address. On
the third message, thought "Better get to it". Besides which the latest
message said it would only take 2 minutes !

Bring up their linked screen - Oops wants an ID (the one on the
bar-coded label on the Motorola box I have sat on the floor under my
L-shaped desk,  for direct cable connection). So get out of that screen,
get my aging body on my knees, and coupled with eye-glasses and
MAGNIFYING glass figure out the Serial # under the bar code.

Back to their screen :-

1 - enter your e-mail - Label next to it says "If your e-mail is
jjsmith.'@home.com', just enter 'jjsmith' - no big deal.

2. Enter that serial number above - OK

3. Enter your telephone number - OK - 403-271-2507

4. Enter password, repeat password. OK.

Press submit

Oops - doesn't like telephone number. OK, so Shaw is located in Calgary,
so they don't want my area code, so resubmit  with :-

        271-2507

Oops - doesn't like that. so resubmit with -

        271 2507

Oops - doesn't like that either, so resubmit with :-

    2712507

This time it accepted the Telephone #. Would have been real nice if
there had been a label "Enter your 7 digit telephone number without
spaces, NOT the Area code !"

OK - so I submit - no errors - but jumps me back to an empty submission
screen !

Contact Tech Support by phone - have you done "this' or 'that",  let's
check.......
All is kosher. "Well, I'll have to transfer you to a higher grade
tecchie.......". He does and I leave a message.

She gets back to me - "What did you key in ? What password did you use
?". Tell her. "Ah,  I see she says", but doesn't explain problem. "Let's
go to your Control Panel and enter it that route".

All honky dory. But the two minutes turned into 6 HOURS !

Apart from that lack of how to enter the Telephone Number - FAR MORE
IMPORTANT - how come this particular piece of software was released for
use without being thoroughly tested !!!!!!!!

Now of course, I've got to mess around and set myself up for CLC and
others under my new Shaw connection.

Jimmy, Calgary AB
```
