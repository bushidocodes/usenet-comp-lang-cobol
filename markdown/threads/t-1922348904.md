```
"COBOL II" is the name of a particular compiler product from IBM. It is
IBM's (now superceded by COBOL for MVS & VM, formerly "COBOL/370")
implementation (as of its latest release) of the ANSI/ISO 1985 Cobol
Standard.

It is exclusively for mainframes.

[COBOL for MVS & VM adds support for the 1989 Addendum in which Intrinsic
Functions are implemented. In addition, if you have other IBM language
products such as C and/or PL/I, it can be cheaper than COBOL II.]
```

```
I am working on a Year2000 project for my company, and my understanding
is that IBM VS-COBOL II does NOT have any provision for obtaining the
4-digit year or the century from the system. We've been told we will
need to upgrade to COBOL for MVS to get that. We will also need to
upgrade our release of MVS in order to have to support this as well.

I have heard a rumor that there is an IBM patch to provide the century
for VS-COBOL II, but I don't know if it's true.

We've also been told that we will need to upgrade to CICS release 4.1 to
get century date support in CICS. Currently, most of our CICS regions
are at Release 2.1.2, and the rest are at Release 3.3.
```
