I have been 'on call' for most of 20 years in one capacity or another.  I get 'overtime' pay from the moment I pick up the phone (or get beeped - depending on who is contacting me) to the time I say 'that's a wrap'.  We have a two tier 'on call' system.  There is one helpdesk person on call for a week.  They fix space problems, bad jcl, power outages restarts, stuff like that.  If the job documentation indicates that programming is called is when I get involved or they run into something that makes no sense and they need confirmation of the fix selected I'll get called.  The helpdesk person works from home with a remote hookup to the network.  I also have software to allow me remote hookup should I be called.  

Way back when the pay was time and a half, then it was straight time for any time over 44 hrs (yeah - they got their first 4 over 40 free), and now it is just straight time (oh the joys of being salaried). I do get reimbursed any long distance charges and travel if necessary.  Since most of the people who would be calling me are long distance and my desk is about 56 miles from my house this is nice.  Having the remote hookup allows me to fix abend is relative comfort  (various nocternal four footed furry creatures think it is play time when you're up at 3am) instead of driving those 56 miles at some horrendous time of the night but in the case of a total disaster they want you on site.

If there is one thing you can be certain of if you're a mainframer is that the systems will not have a problem during normal business hours - babies and computers - both puke at 2am - something not taught in today's programming classes.
