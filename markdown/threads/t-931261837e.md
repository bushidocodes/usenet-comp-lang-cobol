but left Wang before it was released (was it released?).
What I remember (note based on the prerelease):
  1. created a decent VS environment on AIX
  2. Even though on AIX, had 1 level libraries
     just like the VS.  Talk about changing that
     but for the first release only 1 level planned.
  3. You needed to recompile your programs.
  4. no COBOL 74, but I had no issues recompiling
  5. heard some screens may need tweeking, *but* I had
     some rather complex screens and they compiled fine
     and worked ok
  6. Not sure about PACE support, but I think it was either

'programmers' machines'; the utilities and interfaces made mundane tasks 
easily accomplished and the green-text-on-black-background terminals were 
what any decent-thinking coder *knew* it was what a computer was supposed 
to be.

On the other hand... if you're dealing with a real, functioning 
organisation that thinks so highly of its data-processing needs that it 
relies on a VS system nowadays then it might also consider an ancient and 
time-honored strategy called 'going out of business'.

(if things have been allowed to reach a point where the company is making 
so little money that it cannot afford to replace decades-old equipment 
(hardware or software) which allow profitable functions then it might be
