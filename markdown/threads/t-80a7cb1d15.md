General response to all follows...

SPAM always sucks. That's why you filter it on your personal system.

Here you can't (well, you CAN, but it is much more difficult and depends on 
which news client you are using to read the group...)

How hard is it to decide if a topic is about COBOL before opening it?

This is an unmoderated newsgroup as Kerry already observed.

In my opinion, that is such a valuable thing that I wouldn't want to see it 
changed.

Newsgroups such as this are fast becoming the last bastions of free speech 
on the planet. There are no vested interests who can censor what goes here, 
no particular political persuasion, no pressure groups, just an open 
platform where people can say anything they like.

It doesn't have to be palatable or politically correct, and on many 
occasions, it isn't. It will offend some and irritate some, and bore some 
and interest some, but that is what makes it valuable.

Like all Freedoms, the Freedom of Speech does not come free. Most of us have 
lived in Democratic societies so long we take it for granted and forget that 
people actually died to get us the right to speak our minds and not have our 
thoughts governed by the "thought Police"

The price is twofold:

1. You need to be mature enough to realise that everything you find here 
won't be what you want to see.
2.  You need to be tolerant enough NOT to complain about what happens in an 
open, unmoderated forum. (Don't like it? Don't come here. Pretty simple 
really...)

Tony's complaint could lead to Google dropping the group from their relays 
and that means that anyone using Google to post here or read it, will no 
longer be able to. It will NOT cause the content of the group to change in 
any way, and that includes SPAM.

The nature opf this particular forum is that it is about things connected 
to, or involving, COBOL. (A "shared interest" of the people who frequent the 
group. Anything else should be marked OT (Off Topic) as indeed, this thread 
should have been when it was originated.)

Most people who come here regularly observe common courtesy and generally 
follow the OT rule so that people who are here ONLY for the COBOL-related 
stuff can skip threads that are not likely to be of interest to them. 
(Personally, I find the OT posts usually much more interesting than the 
COBOL ones, but that's just me.)

NONE of us enjoy or want to wade through SPAM.

BUT, if you are going to have an unmoderated, open forum, then there will be 
people with no interest in COBOL (or anything much else, really) who will do 
blanket posts to ALL USENET forums, and that will include this one.

Deal with it.

It really isn't hard to see immediately what is SPAM and ignore it.

Pete.
