distinction between singular and plural second-person pronouns was retained
in the remoter areas of Appalachia (thou/thy-thine/thee vs.
you/your-yours/you) in some areas.  The anecdote was of a conversation
between a researcher and a "local" housewife, which didn't seem to involve
anything unusual, until the housewife looked up at the clock, said "Just a
moment", ran to the front door, opened it, and called out to her husband in
the field, "Marry, John!  Come get thy suppah!", complete with a trilled R
in the initial exclamation!

This is particularly interesting in that the *reconstruction* of a
distinction between the singular and the plural forms (you vs. y'all) occurs
not so very many leagues away ...
