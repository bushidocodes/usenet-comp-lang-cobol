Hi everyone,

Sorry to drop my e-mail on you like this. My name is Luis Espinal.
Currently I'm a senior student majoring in the Computer Sciences Department
at Florida
International University.

I'm working on a term paper for my Software Engineering class, and the
topic I'm planning to work on is about computer literacy, what it is, what
people think it is, and how it relates to people's jobs. Basically what I'm
doing is a survey in which I interview people with diverse occupational
and/or educational background. Through e-mail, I think I can reach more
people that I could possibly hope by using other means. Plus, e-mail may be
less intrusive than a phone call, and people can answer it, if they choose
to, at
their own leisure. What I want is to ask you a few questions if it is not
an
inconvenience. It is my goal that through this survey, I and other
soon-to-be
computer scientists and M.I.S. attain a better understanding of people's
needs
and help us serve them better as programmers, analysts, managers and
educators.
The questions are simple and the answers can be brief. The questions are
the following:

A) What is your current occupation?
B) What do you think computer literacy is?
C) How has computer technology affected/modified your job?
D) What did you expect of computers when you were first introduced to them?
E) What do you expect of computers now at work or at home?

If you wish to add your opinion or new ideas and suggestions, I would be
more than glad to
hear them.

Please, reply to my e-mail address rather than to this newsgroup since it
is there where I'll be
picking up your replies. Also, by doing so, we do not "clog" the newsgroup
with the replies.

Thanks in advance.

Luis Espinal

Nietzsche.
