Hi Mike.

You could convert the COBOL to C, but my experience with high-level language
conversions is that they do work (to a point), but what you're left with is
difficult to maintain. Primarily because the resulting code conforms to some
"language conversion" methodology, and not to any "structure programming"
practice best suited for the target language.

Difficult to tell for sure, but I expect your application will migrate
relatively easily. You can target a COBOL/ISAM platform on AIX before moving
the data over to a relational db.

These links should be useful (if only to move your thoughts forward):

http://www.microfocus.com/solutions/migrate/ibm/
http://www.microfocus.com/products/serverexpress/datasheet.asp
http://supportline.microfocus.com/productreleaselevels/unix.asp

Gary.

Depends on what you mean by 'advantage'.  The translator won't give
you a 'C program', but merely a 'Cobol program in an unreadable form
that gcc can deal with'.  The C code is not designed to be used by
humans.

I also doubt that OpenCOBOL and TinyCOBOL are robust and complete
enough, yet, for production work.
 
> The possible project is converting off our VSE mainframe and VSAM

If you are spending such huge amounts on the machine and database (not
compared to VSE of course) then it would seem poor economics to save a
couple of thousand to use a free compiler.
