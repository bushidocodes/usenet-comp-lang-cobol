The problem depends on what you're trying to measure.  And it's not a
Y2K problem.  When you're trying to see how old a school kid is, two
digits are sufficient.  When you're trying to find out how many people
in town are of school age you get the famous cases of the 105 year old
getting truancy notices.

But since there are so many cross referenced databases, it pays to put 4
digit years in everyplace.  

I tried to send my taxes in electronically.  It wouldn't take my taxes
because Social Security somehow has my wrong birth date.  I don't see
why there should be any relationship.  So I paid $20 to a service to
send me a certified birth certificate, and I will take a couple of hours
off work at the local social security office, then wait 3 weeks before I
can send in my tax forms again.

Just because there is no logical reason for a date to be compared
doesn't mean it won't be compared.  Since storage is cheap now, go to
the 4 digit year.
