Well, first of all, I would read a good COBOL book. There are several
ways to define variable length files and it will explain the
difference. I will assume that the file you want to define can
contain 1000-2200 bytes. You can take from my example to get what you
really need.

In you example above, you SELECT statement is fine. The write is the
same as any other write.

In your FD, you need to change the recording mode to V. Your record
contains clause would be: RECORD CONTAINS 1000 TO 2200 CHARACTERS.
You could then define your record in several ways:

a) 01 TAB-A-POST-MIN PIC X(1000).
01 TAB-A-POST-MAX PIC X(2200).

or

b) 01 TAB-A-POST.
05 FILLER PIC X(999).
05 TAB-A-REST OCCURS 1 TO 1201 TIMES DEPENDING ON TAB-LENGTH.

or

c) 01 TAB-A-POST.
05 TAB-A-BYTE OCCURS 1 TO 2200 TIMES DEPENDING ON TAB-LENGTH.

There are other ways, but these are the main ones.

In your JCL, change the RECFM from FB to VB and the LRECL to 2204.
You also shouldn't need the blksize parameter. In the disposition,
new is the default so you can eliminate that also.

Something tells me that you probably aren't sure what is going on now.
I again refer you back to the book (or your instructor). I would be
curious as to why you would like to format your data into a variable
length file when you know so little about them....
Dave
