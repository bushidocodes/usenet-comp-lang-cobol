I suggest you to try Grid Tools from www.dbi-tech.com. This activex is
very useful and easy to use. It can replace the "table" control supplied
with Powercobol, without any limitation about the number ofrows/cols.

Another great comtrol is Spread from Farpoint Inc. (www.fpoint.com). It
can be used as a Excel grid with many advanced features. Try it also.

Other suggestion: go to download.cnet.com Activex section. You certainly
will find a lot of grid controls (some free and other as shareware).

Hi Gianni,

Thanks for your reply.

I have looked at a lot of the grids available, and most of them are far too
complex for what is required.  BTW, Grid Tools from Dbi was one of
the best documented, and most flexible.  After that Janus looked like
a good choice, and value for money at the bottom end, Xpress Quantum
 grid.  (kudos for them, as they replied to our email, and gave us some
sample code in Cobol!  They are about $US134.00).

The real problem is that I only want to do a few things that the Fujitsu
table cannot.  These are:
1.    Limit of 2000 rows.
2.    Drag and drop easily.
3.    Better and more flexible cell editing.
4.    Table sorting capability.
5.    Display more than 1 line of text in a cell easily on reports.
6.    The Fujitsu table is very difficult to get it to print sometimes
       if there is more than 1 table on a form.  Can lose a row off the
       print occassionally.
7.    Split columns.

I will have a look around in download.com

Thanks



Rob.
