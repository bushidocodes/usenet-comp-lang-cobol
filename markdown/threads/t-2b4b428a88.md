         MOVE Debtor-Key  TO transaction-Key
         START Transaction-File
             KEY NOT < Transaction-Key
             INVALID KEY

         PERFORM

             READ Transaction-File
                 AT END
                     MOVE HIGH-VALUES TO Transaction-Key
              END-READ

the problem where the debtor just happens to be the

Although I do not have a copy of the ANSI / ISO standard, the text is
frequently quoted and noted in the implementors LRMs.

What I find under the READ statement in my LRMs is the phase "the
content of the associated record area is undefined." Under the OPEN
statement, a successful OPEN "makes the associated record area
available to the program." Under the CLOSE statement, after a successful
CLOSE "the record area associated with [the file] is no longer available.

This states that an "associated record area" must be available, from
successful OPEN to successful CLOSE.

The applicable wording from CD 1.2 appears under the READ statement.

19) If a record operation conflict condition occurs during the execution
      of a sequential format READ statement, the content of the associated
      record area is undefined, and the key of reference for indexed files
      and the file position indicator remain unchanged. For any other
      unsuccessful execution of a READ statement, the content of the
      associated record area is undefined and the file position indicator is
     set to indicate that no valid record position has been established.

The wording for the OPEN statement.

2) The successful execution of an OPEN statement makes the associated
     record area available to the program. If the file connector associated
     with file-name is an external file connector, there is only one record
     area associated with the file connector for the run unit.

The wording for the CLOSE statement.

7) Following the successful execution of a CLOSE statement without the
    UNIT phrase, the record area associated with a file-name-1 is no
    longer available. The unsuccessful execution of such a CLOSE
    statement leaves the availability of the record area undefined.

This wording is almost identical to the wording in the LRMs.

Furthermore, the notes in the LRMs indicate that requirements for
availability of the record area apply to ANSI 74, but not to ANSI 68.

I think what is "confusing" you (or where you differ from what many
implementors do - and what ANSI has upheld in interpretation requests) is
that you think that having an "undefined record area" means that you don't
know what is in it - but that you can still move things into it or from it.

What J4 has determined in the past (but it would take me a while to find it)
is that "undefined" means that it is not even guaranteed that it has
"addressability" or any other attribute.  Hence, if you try to test a value
in it or move any item to or from it, results are explicitly undefined
(which means the program can die or can do any other strange thing that the
vendor comes up with).  "Undefined" in the Standard (normally? always?
usually?) really means "without any known attributes or behavior".
Therefore, if you hit an "undefined" situation in the Standard - all "bets
are off".
