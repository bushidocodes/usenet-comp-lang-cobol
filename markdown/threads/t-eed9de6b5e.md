Interstingly (YMMV) some of the differences in the terms have sensible
origins.

When cars first started being used in England they were for short trips
to or through the countryside, the roads were unmade so a good pair of
wellies was required equipment. Not wanting to clutter up the insides
with dirty footwear the manufacturers added a 'boot locker'.

When cars first started being used in America they were for longer
trips, usually overnight, and so they required something to take the
clothes.  Often a cabin trunk was strapped to the back, then
manufacturers added one as a fixure.

Hence boot and trunk.

will tend to use brand-names for generic items while the latter tends 
towards using the name of the inventor or a well-known user.

canvas shoes (sneakers) (after the resemblance of their mudguards to the

'What is all this muck? Who's responsible for all that mess in the
