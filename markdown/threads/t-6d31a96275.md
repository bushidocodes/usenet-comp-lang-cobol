OK, we know it's not toll free. Now you are still showing numbers
in US format as if all your respondents lived in the USA.

The international telephone number format is like this -
Starts with the plus sign '+'
which denotes the relevant international access code
(00 in Europe, 011 in USA etc)
to dial out of whatever country you happen to be in
then the international country code to dial in to
in this case it is 1 for the USA (44 for Britain)
then a space as a separator
then the national phone number, without brackets.

So you should be presenting the numbers in this format:
Fax to +1 810 358-0890
Voice +1 800 238-0890 (toll free in the USA)

A minor problem is that many North American residents of the USA,
like you, think all phone numbers must be: (area code) nnn-nnnn so
they might have some trouble comprehending the international format.
