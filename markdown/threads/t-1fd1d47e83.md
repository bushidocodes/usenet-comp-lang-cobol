Try putting a '.' (period) after the MOVE 'NO' imperative (side note -
old fogies will tell you that flags like that should be a single
character because a CLI is three times faster than a CLC) or put an
END-READ in the same place. Without delimiting the scope by either the
'.' or END-READ your subsequent imperatives fall under the conditional
established by AT END.
