I went last year (in Atlanta). I found it to be a worthwhile experience
from a few perspectives:

1) It was interesting to find out where MF is headed with their
products, and learn a few new tricks on existing products.

2) Networking. Comparing what I am doing with what others using the
same tools are doing was priceless. Just bouncing ideas around durin
gthe "down time" at the conference provided me with several interesting
ideas I've been toying with.

3) Third party presentations. Lots of folks are offering up third party
solutions that are very interesting (if not always applicable to my
particualr situation).

4) You really get the ear of the developers there. I was surprised at
how much direct access there was beteween the folks that actually
develop MF's products and their end-users (namely us).


My only  "complaint" was that the conference at times tended to be less
technical than I would have liked. At times it seemed to be more of a
tech-oriented sales pitch than anything else. But MF has promised that
this year's event will be even further geared toward the techies - so
we'll see.

I'll be attending again, and I'm sure it'll prove to be worthwhile.


Chris
