```
FYI - there is now just ONE WEEK left to get your comments in (if any) on
the draft of the next COBOL Standard (if you want to submit them as US
comments - comments in other countries may have different "due" dates).

The deadline in the US is April 9, 2001.

Given past experience/input, it would be my best *guess* that:

 A) This draft *will* be approved as it is at the end of the review (and
response) period.
 B) That there will be a MINIMUM of 5 (and more likely 10 or more) years
until another "Full revision" is done.  (Although "individual"
features/functions may WELL be added before that)
 C) When/if any "corrections" to problems that are missed in this review
period MIGHT be fixed, is totally unknown (but highly unlikely before
2004/2005 - at the EARLIEST).
 D) Which vendors will actually implement this standard (in part or whole)
and when is unknown at this time; similarly it is unknown when (if ever)
such vendors would STOP supporting the current "standard" or
version/releases of their COBOL products.

If you are interested in how to "see" the process for reviewing and making
comments, I would like to point you to an earlier "article" that I did that
can still be seen at:

 http://www.cobolreport.com/columnists/bill/01222001.htm

As with the "reaffirmation" of the current COBOL Standard, it does NOT hurt
to send in a comment just saying that you want the whole thing approved or
that you don't.  It is MOST useful if you can give "specific" comments (on
problems especially) - but that isn't a requirement. Comments on "new"
features that you would like added are HIGHLY unlikely to be accepted for
this revision - but may be added to a "candidate" list for the next
revision.  Items that you want REMOVED from this revision are also medium
unlikely to be removed, but that is still a THEORETICAL possibility.  Things
that are optional (Processor Dependent) would probably NOT be added to the
"required" list; things that are currently required *might* (but probably
would NOT) be put in the optional (processor dependent) list.

Bottom-Line:
  What you see is what you will get
     or more accurately
  What you don't look at now, is what you will STILL be getting (so why not
look now).
```
