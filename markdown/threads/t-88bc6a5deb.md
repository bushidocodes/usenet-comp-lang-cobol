Announcement: Requirements Based Testing Seminars

The Requirements Based Testing methods and SoftTest Requirements Processing
and Test Case Design tool are used in working with complex issues related to
the specification and testing of development and maintenance projects.
These methods and tools may be used on any projects where it may be
desired to remove ambiguities from requirements especially those
where the requirements have intensive logical relationships.

Two courses are planned to take place during the week of March 16, near
London, England. The first is a one day course that will introduce the
concepts below and demonstrate the tool. The other is a 3 day course for
those who wish to learn these disciplines for processing and validating
requirements and who wish to learn how to use SoftTest in support of them.

The Methods and Tool

* Ambiguity Analysis Method:
Ambiguity reviews are a method of analysing requirements specifications in
order to find and correct a number of types of ambiguities. Ambiguities
include, for example, logical inconsistencies, contradictory requirements,
missing dependencies and incomplete requirements. The methodology permits
the analysis of requirements in a variety of forms, such as functional
specifications, use cases, state transition diagrams and process flow
diagrams.

* Cause-Effect Diagramming Method:
Cause Effect diagrams are a method of condensing requirements into their
fundamental cause and effect components. They provide a method of
diagrammatically clarifying the relationship between causes and effects,
where these relationships are often clouded by convoluted or complex
document, paragraph and sentence structures. This method also exposes
aliasing (synonym) and precedence problems. In effect these powerful
capabilities result in integration testing commencing at the specification
stage.

* SoftTest Requirements Processing Tool:
In the process of creating SoftTest input, the requirements are converted
into clear, concise unambiguous elements. The SoftTest mathematical engine
then analyses requirements to produce a variety of powerful reports for
analysing and correcting requirements. These reports include:
Test Cases (in clear unambiguous language) created from the requirements
themselves. As a result of this and the power of its mathematical engine,
SoftTest produces the optimal set of tests necessary to facilitate high
confidence, cost efficient requirements verification.
Functional Requirements Specification (in clear unambiguous language).
Functional Variations (logical process paths through the requirements) and
inconsistencies.

Functional coverage and test case definition tables (providing a quick view
of the functional requirements and their interdependencies and of how the
test cases address these).
