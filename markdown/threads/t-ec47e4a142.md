The book I used in college and at a private business school to take
"COBOL programming" and "Advanced COBOL programming" was called:

Understanding Structured COBOL: Second Edition
Author: Michel Boillot (Pensacola Junior College)
ISBN: 0-314-93155-4
Publisher: West Publishing Company, 50 West Kellogg Boulevard, P.O.
Box 64526, St. Paul, MN 55164-1003 (USA)
Copyright 1986

My opinion is that is was the best book I used to learn COBOL on both
a IBM/360 and IBM PC. I took my first class in 1986 having to use
JCL, then took it a few years afterward working with MicroSoft's
COBOL 4.5 on the PC platform. It seems to cover COBOL 65, since COBOL
85 wasn't tried and true around when I first took it (it wasn't
popular).

Today, I would pick MicroFocus' Visual COBOL and some newer books. I
picked up on COBOL II when I was learning about DB2 and CICS.

I hear many students asking about COBOL programming and I feel like
you should just learn COBOL from a book by reading it chapter by
chapter - working out the examples. If you feel like that is the
language you are sticking with for your career then try to get an "A"
in those courses (I don't remember having to be a superior MATH
student to pass COBOL classes). Take time to find out what platforms
use COBOL in your area and other cities and learn it by on-the-job or
refresher courses for that platform.
