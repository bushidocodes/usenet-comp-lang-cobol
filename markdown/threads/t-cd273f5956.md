```
If your student compiler comes with an editor, you're probably
ahead using it. Many times compiler vendors bundle an
editor that interacts with their compiler (editor jumps right to
errors detected from last compile). Further, vendor-supplied
editors know you are dealing with COBOL and can take
language-specific features to heart (indentation, highlighting
reserved words, etc.) then automatically invoke the proper
compiler, linker, and so forth.
```

```
I studied COBOL in 1992, with VAX, and in 1993, I got a PC COBOL editor.
I am a mainframe COBOL now. I have been helping students in college with
COBOL, and their instructors encourage them to use Text editor.

However, my PC COBOL editor is more convenient to students than any Text
editors, Notepad, WordPad, Words, WordPerfect, DOS, Boland C/C++ editors
or MS Visual C++ editor. It is totaly free.

The problem is that I do not have manual book to give you the instruction.
It is very easy and simple to students of 1980s or early 1990s when we use
DOS screen without mouse and menu bar.

It is RM-COBOL editor and compiler, in a single floppy disk. Using this
editor, you cannot copy, cut or paste a selection. You cannot move the
cursor to the beginning or to the end of line as in MS environment.
You can copy, cut or paste a block of lines. The Tab key helps you to
move to the position 7 and 12, which are the critical position for the
coding, and it keeps each position as one byte. It means that when you
apply the Tab key, the code does not have a real tab, but 7 spaces, or
1 space, or 4 spaces instead. None of other text editors helps you to
type and jump at COBOL critical points like that.

Compare with MS editors as Notepad, WordPad and Words, RM-COBOL editor
is equally fast. It takes more time to save the change, and the skills
you learn from it cannot be applied in the MS environment. It helps you
get familiar with VAX and mainframe. That is the reason you may want to
try it, but not necessary to learn it. It take me few hours to use RM-
COBOL editor. It may take you few days or longer to figure it out. Please,
do not think that I under-estimate you. I am a student of early 1990s,
and you are students of Mouse and Menu bar, who never plays DOS games.

If you have the interest of exploring the old technology, write me.
```
