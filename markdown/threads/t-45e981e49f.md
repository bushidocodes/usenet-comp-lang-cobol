There is a virus around called MTX and my system is currently infected with
it.

I cannot establish beyond doubt where it came from but I have reason to
believe it may have been from private posts out of this Newsgroup.

DO NOT OPEN ANY ATTACHMENTS TO E-MAIL WHERE THERE IS NO MESSAGE, AND
ESPECIALLY, IF YOU HAVE RECEIVED MAIL FROM ME WITH ATTACHMENTS IN THE LAST
FEW DAYS IT MAY HAVE BEEN INFECTED.

I can only say sorry, it wasn't done deliberately...

The virus manifests itself by attaching to outgoing e-mails. If you open the
attachment, you are likely to become infected.

DO NOT OPEN any attachments to this message! Simply delete the second
message.

This is a particularly nasty virus as it actually has three components: the
virus, a worm, and a Trojan Horse. Although it is not especially malicious,
it opens a port on your system from which it can update itself from the
Internet. It has another nasty aspect in that it monitors what sites you are
accessing on the net and will not allow you to access any virus protection
sites which may have an anti virus.

It is difficult to remove (although, believe me I am working on it...!)
because it installs into and corrupts certain system .dlls.

If you are sure your system is clean, you would be well advised to update
your anti-virus software (mine was 41 days out of date...) and treat any
e-mails you are not expecting (even from trusted sources) with suspicion if
they have attachments.

Sadly,

Pete.

patient. I am not sending any e-mails until this problem is sorted

I have noticed a growing smugness amongst UNIX (and UNIX-flavoured) users.

There are viruses which can attack UNIX systems just as easily; in fact UNIX
was more famous in its infancy for the holes in its security than for its
processing power.

Mainframes may be less prone to viruses (I believe this is because, in
general, they are less open to attack from the Internet by not being
connected to it, and also the majority of people working on them are
computer professionals who have nothing to prove, as well as the
architecture being more secure), but mischievious programs have been written
for the Mainframe since I was a junior programmer (and that is a VERY long
time ago...<G>).

I well remember being carpeted because I wrote a program which emulated
mainframe DOS system messages and caused an Engineering call to be made to
IBM...My defence that it was April the First cut very little ice and I kept
my job by the skin of my teeth and the fact that in those days programmers
were in very short supply... I never wound up the Operations people again...

The point is that we should all be on our toes regarding malevolent idiots
who derive some pleasure in their sad lives from  this kind of endeavour.

In fact it took me 6 hours to research, isolate, and remove the virus, worm,
and Trojan and clean up the infected system .dlls. This is not so bad, and I
learned a great deal in the exercise... I also found out how it slipped
through the protection I had in place which should have trapped it.  (this
was caused by taking a default in a configuration when I should've thought
about it...)

So, on the positive side it was a learning experience, but not one I would
want to have every day...

If anyone else has this virus and needs help, mail me. (yes, infected mail
is OK...no problem...<G>)

Pete.
