Thane and some others indicated that the free version 3 compiler comes 
with "Teach Yourself COBOL in 24 Hours" and "COBOL Unleashed".
Version 3 can also be downloaded off the web at www.adtools.com -or-
as part of ETK's development platform at www.etk.com.
There is also a book out "Mastering COBOL" by Carol Baroudi (I think)
that has the version 4 compiler included.

I have used the Fujitsu COBOL compiler for batch type programs over 
the last 3 years and found it to be an excellent tool.  The majoritiy of 
my work with the compiler is for off-loading development from the mainframe
and ocassionally having to debug a program.   The ancillary tools (Data
Convertor,
Layout Definition, ...) have been very helpful in translating files from EBCDIC
to ASCII for testing of mainframe files on the PC.

Hope that this info gets you on your way.
