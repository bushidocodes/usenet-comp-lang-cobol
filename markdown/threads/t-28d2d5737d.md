Hello, Christian ! I currently work for Keane and find it a terrific company
to work for with ample oppurtunity. The ASDP program is an excellent program
to learn the basics of programming. You will have to pay your dues and
program for awhile but as your career progresses there are many
oppurtunities. There are oppurtunities for UNIX Admin in the New York area.
There is also Project Mgmt, People Mgmt and Sales. They give you a true sense
of belonging to a family. There is a mentoring program and a lot of support.
They also sponsorin in house education, CDROM education, Certified Learning
Center education and reimburse up to $4,000 per your for college credit
courses towards a degree. What more of an investment can you ask a company to
make in you? As well, they also pay a productivity bonus for working above 80
hours in a two week period. Very few companies offer this. Keane also
dedicated a Personnel Development Manager to monitor your growth and further
your career. We are not your typical body shop connsulting companies as many
others are. Keane takes an interest in the individual employee. I have worked
for several consulting companies and find Keane to be the best. If you do
some research you will find that they have been voted one of the top 25
companies to work for. When you finish with an assignment you are not laid
off. You go on the bench until they find you another assignment. In the
meantime, you can use their classroom to study new technologies via CDROM or
classes. Keane is rated very highly by the industry in general and is a
preferred vendor of IBM and many other companies. Take a look at the stock
and see how well its done over the last couple of years.
