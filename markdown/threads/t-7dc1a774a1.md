using this command in the redefines field defined as alphanumeric of the
numeric field and then your numeric field would be able to calculate.

Another way that works just like the inspect command would be to create a
perform routine to look at the field byte by byte using FIELD-A (X1:1) and
replace any space found with a zero.  X1 is numeric starting at 1 and
incrementing it by 1 until hitting the length of the numeric field OR until
you hit a numeric byte.

You didn't give the format of LIST-PRI, but if it isn't numeric, compare it to
spaces as well.  Also note that when numeric variables or literals are
compared, generated code will pay attention to where the decimal point and what
the sign is while alphanumerics are compared left to right with trailing spaces
assumed on the shorter one if need be.  99.00 and 100.00 will compare
differently if they are alphanumeric for example (with no leading space).
