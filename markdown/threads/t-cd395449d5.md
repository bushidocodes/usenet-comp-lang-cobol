a training course the company I was working for offered to learn it.

courses that enhance your abilities.  Going off to learn CICS would
qualify.

Learn all you can in school.  Just don't stop there.

My opinion:
I don't think it matters what language a person learned. If they're
just out of school, they are not going to be whiz-bang at the technical
aspects of the job. They can't out-code the incumbents. (In fact,
declaring themselves as such probably nixes the job prospect.)

So why would any employer hire a newbie for anything?
Maybe because:
A. They could work on some 'simpler' aspects of the systems, which are
now assigned to more-experienced people. The more-experienced people
could be freed up for assignment to more difficult tasks.
B. They are probably less expensive than oldtimers.
C. They don't mind doing the 'mundane'work that the oldbies are tired
of.
D. They aren't burned out (yet) so will apply the extra effort.
E. They are more likely (than some oldbies)to feel they need to perform
well to keep their jobs.
F. They enjoy the challenge of new tasks and the rapid change of this
profession.

Maybe a good interview strategy is to convince the hirer that you are a
guy who likes to get his hands dirty on the code, who enjoys solving
problems, who has an old-fashioned work ethic, and also who gets along
with all kinds of personalities. One real bad thing - whining about
whatever.
