I too have started looking into this *FREE* compiler and the thing
that is beginning to annoy me is the PowerCOBOL manual; huge,
unwieldy, unsearchable, difficult-to-read images instead of nice
searchable text and practically unprintable (in this lifetime).

Try looking, at random, at page 218 of COB_UGW.DP COBOL85 User
Guide (on CD or disk). Zoomed to 200% it is nearly readable but
it needs the new Intel 400 MHz processor to scan across or down.
What on earth is it doing with all that Pentium power?

How about a manual with real text, characters as bytes instead of
pixels. There are signs that the manual was originally composed
with Microsoft Word 7.0 so why not just release that version - or
even a Rich Text Format edition?

Or is it just me?

It is searchable. You can set up the search function and create
indexes that give you full text searches. Admitedly the images are
not perfect and we are looking at ways to improve them in the future
manuals

› Try looking, at random, at page 218 of COB_UGW.DP COBOL85 User 
› Guide (on CD or disk).  Zoomed to 200% it is nearly readable but 
› it needs the new Intel 400 MHz processor to scan across or down.

Are you using the Pro-viewer or the mini-viewer? I am using a P133
with a 2 MB graphics card and that page, as all pages with graphics on
them, flies by. I am able to scroll quickly and efficiently. I am
not sure why you have encountered this error. Make sure that you are
using the ProViewer.

› How about a manual with real text, characters as bytes instead of 
› pixels.  There are signs that the manual was originally composed 
› with Microsoft Word 7.0 so why not just release that version - or 
› even a Rich Text Format edition?

All the manuals in .DP format take up about 22 MB's. Just the Windows
User's Guide in Word format takes up 52 MB's and is unstable. The
total for all the manuals in Word 7.0 format was well over 200 MB. We
thought that most users would prefer to take up less space on their
hard drive. We were not very pleased with Word's performance on big
documents and are currently looking for another tool.

We are currently getting all the manuals bound so that user's can buy
the hard-copies if they prefer.
