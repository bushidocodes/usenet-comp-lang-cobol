```
This morning, I realized that I have replied to several "linguistics-only"
threads withOUT prefixing the subject line with "OT". (Thanks, Pete - for
your note to "Newbies" that reminded me of this.)

I thought that I would post this note for a few reasons.

- My undergraduate degree (too many years ago to think about) was in
Linguistics and French.  Therefore, my "passion" for linguistics pre-dates
my "passion" for COBOL.

- When I see notes with "false" or "misleading" or "misinformed" statements
about *NATURAL* languages, I usually want to "jump in" and provide
corrections or clarifications (just as I do with such statements about
COBOL).

- Anyone with a modicum of knowledge of linguistics knows that there are
SIGNIFICANT differences between:
     - "natural" and "artificial" languages
     - "spoken" and "written" languages
     - "structural" and "historical" linguistics
     - "semantics" and "syntax"

- The "modern" theories of linguistics (of "natural languages") has been
significantly impacted by computers (e.g. attempts for computerized
translations of both written and spoken languages, statistics, etc).
Similarly, modern "artificial languages" (such as computer programming
languages) have been influenced by theoretical linguistics.  The fact that
the two disciplines have impacted each other does NOT (IMHO) mean that they
should be "confused". What is true for one is not necessarily true (nor is
it necessarily false) for the other.

    ***

Again, sorry for not indicating "OT" for linguistic-only posts to the NG.  I
will probably continue to "jump in" on linguistic threads (as such really DO
interest me) - but I will try and remember to make certain that they are
noted as Off-Topic for those who want to skip them.
```
