First of all, let me * THANK* each one of you. I didn't think I get this kind of
response from such a innocent question. There are quite a few in this group,
who I will call the Foundation ( I'm an Asimov nut ). These people, and you
all know who you are, give their time and experience, to help a great deal.
I posed this question just to find out a little more about who I was dealing
with. I think we all found out a little bit. There were a few, I thought you
were younger, or older's. I ended my part of this Thursday, August 20,1998
at around 12 midnight est. If you chimed in after this, it just means your
not included in the averages given, but thanks anyway. It started as just
an age question, but most of you felt the need to give your years of experience
also. So, I included these numbers also. It seems an inordinate amount of us
are also multi-linguistic, and progressed to COBOL from Assembler. In my
personal opinion, this seems to be a very logical progression,
as this is the route I was taken at the programming school I attended.
Also quite evident is the fact that, we have some very highly educated people
among us. We have all sorts of degree's, and a few Doctorate's. I feel dwarfed,
and even a little intimidated, with just a programming certificate, 2 yrs.
Community College, and a couple of semesters at Penn State. Gives me more
incentive to return. Well here, finally, is what I found.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

AGE RANGE 20 - 74 (so we cover the whole range of so-called Adulthood)

In the time allotted, we received 36 responses. They are still trickling in.
Of these, there were 34 MEN, and only 2 WOMEN (come on girls speak up)
I did notice a few more after I stopped. Maybe Bill Lynch has a point about
the question having to do with age.-- LOL

around 20 or so. (logical)
