```
Are you certain that your input (historic) times do include some that are
"daylight savings time" - and others that are not?

If this is true, then you are going to have a MONSTROUS time trying to
"un-do" this.  Remember that DST does *not* take place in all time-zones at
the same time.  (For example, UK usually is about a week "off" from when the
US changes.

Also, are all the input times from the same location (not just same
time-zone)?  If not, you will run into problems like

  US - Arizona and parts of Indiana vs other places in the same timezone
  International - British "double daylight savings time" for certain years.

    ***

If you DO know for certain that
  A) all your input times are from the same location
  B) that they really DID use DST time for some entries

Then, I think your best bet is going to be to get a "table" of dates for
years on exactly when they changed in that specific location - and NO -
there is no general service (that I know of) to do this.
```
