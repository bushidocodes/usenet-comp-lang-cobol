I received the following from one of my "usually reliable sources" at MERANT
concerning several of the issues that have appeared recently in
comp.lang.cobol.  This information does reply to some "specific" comments in
previous notes that I sent on - and which I am NOT repeating here.  However,
I think this note can be read (and understand) without seeing exactly which
note(s) are being responded to.

Note:  I do *not* "vouch" for any of this - but do consider the source as
someone who would know about this - if anyone does.

"Hi, Bill.

Thanks for passing on the note.  I have asked <other managers> to respond
regarding the Net Express v3.1 amnesty program and the stated availability
notification that was to be made.  I will try to address the issues related
to AnswerLab and other issues related to Support in the note.  Can I ask you
to pass them along to the original forum in answer to the message.

In preface, this year has seen many changes to the SupportNet area of our
web site.  Part of these changes were the rationalization of the different
web services originally provided by Micro Focus and Intersolv as we merged
them together and restructured ourselves into the type of Support operation
we wanted to be to, in our view, provide the best services to our customers.
Another part was so we could offer new and expanded services to make our web
support services more useful to our customers.  In May of this year, we
brought up that new view of those Support web services in the new SupportNet
Online site (www.merant.com/SupportNet).  This new site merged several of
the past functions that we had into fewer and, hopefully, easier to use
features and introduced a number of new services (such as the ability to
report problems to us and to be able to view the records of all of the new
problems that you, or your company, had reported, the new SupportNet
Knowlegebase, etc.).

One of the new services planned was a revamped AnswerLab that would not only
use a later version of the underlying software and serve it from a much more
reliable server, but would also open that service up to users of all of the
MERANT products, not just the Micro Focus branded products.  In revamping
the AnswerLab, we had a problem because of the different login mechanisms
for the (old) AnswerLab and the new SupportNet site, which had it's own
integrated login process and which tied the user login registrations with
our Call Tracking system and, ultimately, with our master customer
information system.  The previous AnswerLab login process used a standalone
database which could not be connected to these other, central, login
services.  So, we had to make a choice as to how to move forward.  We did
what we felt was the obvious solution, which was to shift the AnswerLab
registration to the, now, standard SupportNet Login registration process, so
only a single login would be needed for all SupportNet web services.  This
means that all of the past AnswerLab users would need to re-register for
using the SupportNet Registration process to be able to get to the new
AnswerLab.  Since the old AnswerLab had too much valuable information to
just take it away, we have made it a read-only resource for our customers.
Yes, that does mean that you can't post messages to it any longer (those
need to be posted to the new AnswerLab service), but we have opened up the
access to the old AnswerLab to anyone who has an old AnswerLab login ID, to
anyone accessing it through their SupportNet login ID, or to anyone who uses
an (old AnswerLab) ID and Password of GUEST.

The writer of the <one of the original> messages was right in that he was
given access to the AnswerLab for the past year, even though he let his
product maintenance
lapse.  Until now, we did not have the ability to link the two systems
together.  With the new structure, the registration to access all of the
SupportNet services are tied to the central customer information and
maintenance status is checked automatically.  Yes, this means that should
your maintenance lapse (after a small grace period), your access to the
SupportNet services will also lapse until the maintenance is restored.  When
the writer's original maintenance lapsed, we were transitioning our
maintenance renewal process from one group to another within the company.
For a time, during that transition, some renewal notifications were not sent
out.  However, since the completion of that transition (to a dedicated
maintenance sales and renewal staff), we have been much better at getting
renewal notices out early enough to prevent maintenance lapses.  Any
questions on maintenance prices, renewals, consolidations, etc. can be
directed to our SupportNet Sales staff at 1-800-443-1601, voice prompt 6 or
to supportnet.sales@merant.com .

Prior to the new AnswerLab going live and the old AnswerLab being made a
read-only resource, we did put out a notice on the page path to the
AnswerLab noting the changes that were coming.  Could we have had the notice
available for a longer time frame, perhaps.  However, since this change was
coming about 3 to 4 months after the new SupportNet site, with it's
requirement for having a SupportNet login registration to get to most of the
other services, we figured (rightly or wrongly) that most of the users would
already have registered for their SupportNet login and would be able to
access the new AnswerLab immediately as a result of that.  For those who
used previously saved bookmarks to the old AnswerLab, they would run into
the read-only message if they didn't understand what had happened.  Going
into the AnswerLab via the SupportNet page link would eliminate the problem,
obviously.

Over the past few months, we have made the SupportNet Informer the main
notification mechanism for our SupportNet services.  This is a successor to
the older Web Informer and now makes use of both the web newsletter format
along with E-Mail notification of both new issues of the SupportNet Informer
and, separately, for notifications of new releases, important patches, etc.
The notification of new upgrades and such are made here and in the last
issue, there was the notification regarding the availability of the new Net
Express v3.1 release.  I can't say if this was the official notification
that Tony <Hill> mentioned in his previous note, or not, but it was a
notification.
That notification included the E-Mail address to use to request your v3.1
upgrade.  You can, also, request an upgrade for any Micro Focus branded
product by using the form provided (under the Product Update link on the
SupportNet page) at
http://support.merant.com/websupport/upgstore/mfcdupgrade.asp .  This form
prefills in your company and personal information from your SupportNet
registration profile, thereby reducing the information that you have to fill
in to complete an upgrade request.

In relation to the writer's item regarding his Net Express v3.0 serial
number not working, many of the early Net Express serial numbers had leading
zeros.  In my experience, those serial numbers are only accepted when the
leading zeros are left off - ie only the significant digits are entered.  An
additional problem is that, since the maintenance on his product had lapsed,
his SupportNet registration would not have been automatically approved.
SupportNet registrations are automatically approved when the company that
you register under and the product serial number that you enter match in our
database (ie. the serial number that you enter matches in our records for
the company that you enter).  Any exception to this puts your registration
into a work queue to have one of our people manually look at the
registration and try to resolve the issues (which may be a mis-keyed serial
number, a serial number for a product that was replaced by a later product
update, a change in company name, etc.).  In those cases where the
maintenance on the input serial number has lapsed, the registration is
referred to the SupportNet Sales group so they can contact the customer and
resolve the maintenance contract issue.

Lastly, we will be constantly striving to improve our electronic services
that we make available via our SupportNet Online web site.  Some will
require changes from previous procedures.  We will try to make notifications
of these changes and additions where and when we can.  However, since not
everyone follows the same path to all of the individual services, we cannot
guarantee that every possible user will see the notifications we do make
available. "
