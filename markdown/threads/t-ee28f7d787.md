COMP-6 usually represents unsigned (as opposed to always positive) packed
decimal; two decimal digits per byte, with NO sign nibble. I believe that
COMP-6 first showed up on NCR machines. It was also available on TI 990
COBOL, probably as a means to attract existing NCR applications.
