```
I am extremely pleased to announce that the COBDATA site will be back on the 
air from Monday, officially, but bits of it will be appearing over the 
weekend.

The site has been completely revised and extended to cover more areas of 
COBOL interest. You will find:

1. Free downloads of tools, components, source code, scripts, and tutorials.
2. Information about how COBOL investment can be recovered. Full examples of 
doing it.
3. A portal to Open COBOL.
4. Background and conceptual stuff on OO, components, and mixing languages.
5. Using Relational databases with COBOL. Converting flat files to 
Relational Database, the traps and pitfalls and how to avoid and overcome 
them.
6. A complete technical strategy for migrating procedural COBOL to the .NET 
or Mono framework. Details on how to do it manually; overview of tools 
available from PRIMA if you want help.
7. There is a bit of nostalgia and some stuff that may bring a tear to the 
eye of old time mainframers... :-) There is stuff you can mess with, 
interact with, and play with.

I have gone to considerable trouble to ensure the site renders properly in 
Firefox and IE browsers, but Bill Klein pointed out an important thing that 
is lacking:  DPI values for the vision impaired. If your display is set to 
values other than 96 DPI, pages will not render correctly. I can only 
apologise for this. It is simply too hard to fix (properly) at the moment. 
However, I am investigating the arrival of VSG fonts and when I can do 
something about it, I will.

If you want to contribute to the site, have code samples or experiences you 
want to share, please contact me. I have deliberately NOT  put a forum on 
the site because I'd like the forum for it to be this one. The site is 
intended to be a resource for COBOL people (and programmers from other 
disciplines who have to deal with COBOL legacy), and guides site visitors 
back to this forum. Issues and questions arising from material on the site 
can be discussed here for the benefit of all, and discussions here can lead 
to things being posted on the site (this has already happened...)

There is no advertising on the site and there is unlikely to be. PRIMA 
Computing (my company) sponsors the site and covers the server costs, but I 
have insisted that the PRIMA presence be very low key (there is one indirect 
page that describes the PRIMA toolset and it is a technical page, not a 
marketing one). You will not be assaulted with special offers, or demands to 
try PRIMA products. Neither is your registration added to a marketing 
database. If you register, you can remove yourself any time you want to. 
Registered users have full access (including delete) to their DB entries. (I 
think ALL web sites should be like this...)

The site is safe and I promise you it is safe to enable cookies and flash 
for this site. Cookies expire after 10 minutes or 24 hours, depending on the 
context they are raised in. NOTHING is ever placed on your computer unless 
you request a download. Downloads are thoroughly malware checked
 and I wouldn't offer them unless I was satisfied they are clean.

Enjoy the site and post any comments or questions back here.

http://primacomputing.co.nz/cobdata

Pete.
```
