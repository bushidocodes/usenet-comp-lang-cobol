```
A recent report in the UK Computer Press reported:-

Jobs for program languages culled from adverts over last 12 months as:-

1st Cobol.
2nd Unix.
3rd C
4th C++

I know nothing about Cobol, am a C/C++ programmer with an International
Meteorological data analysis project behind me, 6000 hours work, full
graphics, map drawing in colour and import of a full range of satellite
images. High speed processing of multi-megabyte encoded data files.Etc.

Now 53, unemployed last three years and not even an interview!

Should I learn COBOL?

Does COBOL allow generation of 100 percent graphics screens with input
and processing of data? My limited and ancient knowledge seems to
suggest mono screens. Mind you I do date back to triode valve computers
with 40 acres of power giving at least 10k of processing ability!

I associate COBOL with punched cards, guess it is a bit better now!

Guess the chances of a 53 year old getting a COBOL job with only a
self learnt background?

The article suggests huge potential in mere data changing for 2000
and vast earning for long boring days!
Maybe potential for a self employed capacity as a COBOLDATE Corp?

Any comments?


John
```
