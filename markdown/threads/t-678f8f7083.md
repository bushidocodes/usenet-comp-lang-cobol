There may be a language problem, but I don't understand your question.

You can use EXEC CICS LINK (or the COBOL CALL statement) to go between COBOL
and C under CICS.

However, I don't understand your reference to
  "c  program on tso/ispf"

Are you trying to run a program under ISPF instead of under CICS?  Are you
trying to find a CICS emulator under ISPF (similar to BTS for IMS)? Are you
interested in batch?   What environment are your calling and called really
programs in?

In general, most of your questions about ILC (inter-language communications)
between COBOL and C should be answered at:

 http://publibz.boulder.ibm.com/cgi-bin/bookmgr_OS390/BOOKS/ceea4110/4.0

which is,
 "Chapter 4. Communicating between C and COBOL"

in the current LE ILC manual.
