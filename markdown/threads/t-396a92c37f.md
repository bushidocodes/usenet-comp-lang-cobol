```
2000-12-11 22:00:00 MEZ

And yet another text on quantum comptation which I may
recommend:

http://www.sees.bangor.ac.uk/~schmuel/comp/comp.html

This one shows nicely how reversible computing and quantum
computing fits together. No programmer is required to think
about the management of destroying information - a
compiler can do that easily. Just as I guessed. That
way, we can keep reversible computation / quantum
computation out of any language at the language-specific
level.

(No USAGE QBOOL in the next COBOL-Standard necessary -
although the text gives hints about a dedicated
programming language for a quantum computer.)

What this text does not show is how quantum computation
fits into the many worlds interpretation - no idea
why this facet of the subject is omitted. The
many-dimensional Hilbert-Space is not quite as good
for imagination.

If you feel intimidated by the notation of quantum
mechanics, just read over it and keep to the easy parts.
So did I.

Herwig
```
