A few months ago, my church helped organize a large group of volunteers who 
spent a rainy Saturday weatherstripping, winterizing, and making general 
repairs to the homes of needy people in the inner city -- exactly *none* of 
whom are members of our church, or possibly of any church. We did this simply 
because it's the right thing to do, in accordance with Christ's admonition 
that "in as much as you did it for the least of my brethren, you did it for 
me." This is what Christianity is really about. 

Please remember that that hypocrites abound, and try to judge the value of any 
teaching (religious or otherwise) by the behavior of those who truly follow 
it, not those who merely give lip service to its ideals while living something 
entirely different. I once knew a young woman who carefully followed the 
guidelines of the latest weight-loss diet book -- except for a pint of ice 
cream every night -- and managed to gain weight anyway. Because the diet was 
worthless? No, because she didn't follow its teaching. The same holds true for 
religious teachings. We should no more judge Christianity by the behavior of 
those pseudo-Christians you describe, than we should judge Islam by the 
behavior of a handful of terrorists -- or a diet book by the actions of 
someone who eats a pint of ice cream every night.
