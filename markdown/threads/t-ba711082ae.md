Promise us! When you "get rid of CICS" and convert all your
COBOL to Java that you will come back and tell us how well
the conversion went, how long it took and well your system
runs.

You know, Java does run on the mainframe and you CAN interface
it with CICS. Heck, you can even interface COBOL with Java.
You can 'EXEC CICS WEB' and put your transactions in a browser.

My prediction if you're putting this on some server - based
on my experience of these projects: "YOU'LL BE SORRY".
It'll cost a fortune and it'll run like crap.

My advice: Keep your system where it is. Upgrade your knowledge
base to current MAINFRAME technology. It'll cost you much less
in the long run in development, maintenance and down-time. The
mainframe is far superior in performance, reliability, security
and overall cost to any server.

Just because something is the newest hot-shot whoop-de-doo
technology doesn't mean you should just jump on it. Remember
when 8-track tapes and BetaMax were latest technology? You
have pretty poor reasons for completely re-doing your systems.
You're going to shoot yourself in the foot.
