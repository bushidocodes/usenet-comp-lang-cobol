Remember most of the managers are men and since we are all chauvinist pigs
it makes it hard for a woman to get a job.

People are also more likely to hire someone they know rather than Joe Blow
off the street, regardless of gender or experience. I as a former consultant
worked with several "company men" who had no formal computer education but
had worked for the company for many years. Sometimes lack of education can
be good incentive to keep a person from moving on to a better location.

In the end you don't necessarily have to convince the interviewer of the
company, just the interviewer at the consulting agency. Allot of the
consulting agencies are hiring people with little experience to fill
positions they are not quite qualified for just to fill the positions which
they can receive their all-important percentage of the hourly rate which
they are charging the client for. Although I was qualified for all the
positions I took as a consultant, many I took without an interview. My
account manager was very good at pimping me to the clients. My former
consulting agency was getting paid almost twice what I was receiving for my
services. Their incentive to place a consultant in a position far
overshadows the consultants abilities to perform the job. To give the
consultants credit, most people don't know what they can do until they are
given assignments they don't think they can accomplish. If you just shut up
and do the best you can, most of the time that is enough.

I spent 9 months sending resumes to regular companies looking for jobs.
Literally hundreds of resumes. I sent just 7 to consulting agencies and my
answering machine was filled when I got home the next day.

No matter how disappointing the job search gets, remember you can always
sell your soul to a consulting agency.

>Why is this selling your soul?

As a consultant I found that most managers don't like hearing suggestions
from their consultants. They don't want consultants, they want
contractors/indentured servants. They want you to do what you are told and
stop finding errors in their suggestions. Sometimes this is due to just
plain incompetence, but most times this is just due to the amount of money
they have to spend on your hourly rate as a contractor. Thus a
consultant/contractor/indentured servant will do whatever it takes to get it
done, and get it done quickly. Regardless of how wrong, inefficient, or what
it will affect in the long run. Thus giving up your beliefs and just a
little piece of your soul.

If you aren't being paid as much managers are more willing to allow you to
spend the time to do something right, rather than quick. The risk is lower
and almost always saves trouble in the long run.

Doing quality work should never mean they have to pay you allot of money.
The incentive should be just to do a good job, not to make a ridiculous
amount of money.
