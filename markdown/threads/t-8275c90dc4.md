We apologize for the confusion generated by dual license agreements in
our free CD package. The "No-Charge 60 Day Trial License Agreement"
refers to the HP-UX version of our product which is also on the CD.
The"No-Charge 60 Day Trial License Agreement" should be ignored by
Windows users. The license agreement that pertains to all users is the
"Fujitsu Software Corporation End User Software License Agreement."

To quote from the cover letter for Windows users:

"FREE PRODUCT-
Includes 60 Day Technical Support

You have the right to use and keep the Fujitsu COBOL Family products

and will receive Technical Support for 60 days free of charge from
the date of receipt. After that period of time you must be a
customer of record and hold an authorized subscription to continue to

receive Technical Support on the Fujitsu COBOL family suite."

You may keep the product and use it for as long as you desire. We
STRONGLY recommend that those users planning to develop and distribute
applications with our tools become subscribers. Subscription will
also allow you to receive new releases, updates, patches and continued
support. (after the initial 60 days) The COBOL Run Time System is
freely distributable with applications that you create.

If you have any further questions, please do not hesitate to e-mail
co··.@adt··s.com

Thank you,

Fujitsu Software

This is typical marketroid doublespeak, a runon sentence with a dangling
conclusion. How does it parse? One way you get the whole product for 60 days,
one way you get it forever depending on how the sentence is parenthesized.

Version 1: dangling "for" belongs to both halves of the and.

: (You have the right to use and keep the Fujitsu COBOL Family products
: and will receive Technical Support) for 60 days free of charge from
: the date of receipt.

Version 2: dangling "for" belongs to second part of the and.

: (You have the right to use and keep the Fujitsu COBOL Family products)
: and (will receive Technical Support for 60 days free of charge from
: the date of receipt.)
