The purpose of a formfeed is to position the printer so that:
1. The current job can be separated from the next, and
2. The next print job starts at a pre-defined, standardized place.

Unless this standard is rigorously followed at a shop you'll end up
with:

xxxx
xxxx
THE END
THE BEGINNING
yyy
yyy

in the middle of a sheet of paper.

As to your actual question: Your idea, WRITE [blank line] AFTER PAGE,
is a correct answer to the job's requirements.

Going beyond the assignment:

Your solution, however, puts a blank line at the top of the next page.
The next page belongs, presumably, to the next job and a blank line
may throw off the next program's line counter.

An alternate solution is

WRITE [blank line] BEFORE PAGE.

This is one instance where you can violate the policy of not mixing
WRITE BEFORE and WRITE AFTER in a program.

BUT, if you've already reached the maximum printable area allowed by
the output device (as you write a blank line BEFORE PAGE), the output
device may issue a formfeed on its own and not tell you about it. This
'automatic' formfeed result is a blank sheet being ejected from the
printer. Also, some operating systems or spoolers (Windows, for
example) automatically start the next job at the top of form anyway,
so doing anything involving PAGE results in a blank sheet of paper.
Unless, of course, the printer itself knows not to eject a blank sheet
(e.g., PC page printers).

So, then, to avoid the hideous problem of a blank sheet of paper, your
program:
a) Should keep track of where it is on the printed page at all times,
and
b) Know what steps the hardware takes when the job terminates.
