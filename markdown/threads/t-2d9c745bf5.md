### Y2K empire building

- **From:** "greg" <ua-author-13260@usenetarchives.gap>
- **Date:** 1998-07-16T14:16:01+00:00
- **Newsgroups:** comp.lang.cobol
- **Message-ID:** `<35AE4538.4811B1B2@mrfax.com>`

```
Every week, I receive 3 or 4 Y2K compliance surveys from our customers,
and this number is growing. I've completed and returned no less than 50
of these so far. One of them was ten pages long (!), and did not even
have a box you could check stating "fully Y2K compatible" (no, that
would be too easy).

A lot of companies have set up "Y2K task forces" and "Year 2000
committees". I don't know what else they do, but these surveys take time
and a ton of money to design, print and mail. When the dust settles, I
bet only a fraction of the cost of retooling will have been spent on
real work -- the bulk of the expense is in executive salaries,
conferences, publications, and fruitless Y2K busywork.

COBOL programmers may find it harder to get good jobs 3 years from now,
but what about all these new departments, these empires built on
fin-de-ciecle paranoia? Typically, once a department is created, it's
very hard to dismantle it.

They'll have to invent a Year-2001 crisis.

-Greg
```
