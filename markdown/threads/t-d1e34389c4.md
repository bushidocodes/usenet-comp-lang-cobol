```
As Jimmy indicated, I am not a Micro Focus employee (and haven't been for over a 
decade).  I do have "contacts" still at MF and I am glad that this lead to some 
contact.

Now, for what I *think* you are asking for, it sounds like you ONLY want to run 
an "existing" application on your machine.  If that is the case, then the 
"owner" (assuming they are current on support payments) of the existing 
application should be able to purchase a "run-time license" to port the existing 
application to your system.  It doesn't sound as if you need to actually do ANY 
"development" on your system.

It would seem to me that the company whose application you are "migrating" 
should be able to bare most (if not all) of the cost of such a run-time license. 
(However, this may depend on your contract with them.)

As others have pointed out, it is unlikely that MF would offer you a "deal" on a 
system to migrate AWAY one of their existing customers from their product.  On 
the other hand, a purchase of a "run-time license" for copying an existing MF 
application to a new machine should be a REASONABLY priced option.

  * * * * *

It might be of interest to find out if the existing MF customer only runs the 
existing application on one machine.  If not, they SHOULD have multiple run-time 
licenses already and SHOULD know how to "port" it to another (similarly O/S and 
environment) machine.

Obviously (???) if you are actually providing a "migration" to another 
environment (Operating system, file-system, whatever), then you will need to 
"rebuild" the existing MF application in order to do a "true" parallel test. 
This will certainly be more expensive (for you and/or your customer)  and may 
require expertise AND software that you don't currently have.
```
