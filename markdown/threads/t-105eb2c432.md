```
Ok. Here goes a strange post.

I'm a COBOL and CICS programmer with 12 years of experience in the IBM
Mainframe arena. Recently (3 years ago) I started my own consulting
company, and I've been kept busy hopping from here to there doing the
consulting thing for several large banks.

Sometimes, during a project I find myself getting bored, especially in the
early, information gathering phase of the project. I spend my time in
meetings, and writing up reports, and doing data mapping, etc...etc...etc.
I find that during these times, which can last several months (depending
on the size of the project), I have the time to do some coding, but not
the available work.

So, here is my question. Is there a market out there for a very good
programmer to do some "free-lance" programming? What I'm referring to is
this:

You send me a good, clear spec
You send me input files and their layouts
You send me what reports you want to see

I send you back a unit tested COBOL program.

I have Micro Focus Workbench, and I use it extensively to do mainframe
development for my current clients. I can promise the highest quality,
and very easily maintainable code (if you need a sample, just ask).

Anyway, this may just be pie in the sky, but I thought I'd ask.

And, for all of you out there who are thinking about giving some sort of
"smart" or "sarcastic" reply......don't bother. It really bores me! :-)

Thanks

Dave
```

```
Dave, I don't think that your desire is unreasonable. I do exactly the same
thing forall of my clients now, although it's for other languages (C, C++,
BASIC, etc).

There is always a certain amount of face-to-face work involved with any
project. This is unavoidable, since you need to have the job requirements
crystal clear before commencing any off-site projects. I've been working this
way since 1980 and except for quick on-site bug fixes, I do ALL my programming
off-site.

It's not pie in the sky, it's the way of the future! And good luck in your
endeavors.

By the way, the upcoming 2000 crunch has me learning COBOL now, and I wouldn't
be doing so if I didn't think there was a future need for it, and much work
available. I expect that the date problem will be fertile ground,
particularly for off-site work, from now until the milennium...
```

```
Dave - I've been an indie for about 10 years now and the core of your
request seems to represent the core of the problem. I have never worked
inside what I would call a "healthy" shop; without going into what I mean
by that (send email if you want details) I will say that even though we
deal in "knowledge work" the atmosphere at most of my clients is heavily
imbued with the Industrial Ethic of "Be At Your Station At A Given Time So
We Can Keep Track Of You; A Typing Consultant Is A Consultant Earning His
Keep".

Couple that with the ever-present desire for the DWIM command ("Do What I
Mean") and the situation gets stickier... how many times have we all
followed a specification (or, more likely, a speculification) only to be
told "Well, that's almost it... but can you sort it in descednding
barometric-pressure order and give me another column with last year's YTD
totals, too?"

If you ever *do* find folks who have the structures and discipline in
place to provide you with workable specs then you'll have succeded in
fulfilling a dream of mine, as well... I still have a copy of MicroFocus
COBOL version 1.7 that I "borrowed" from my last full-time employer nigh a
decade ago when I switched to consulting for just such an eventuality...
bona fortuna!

LJS
```
