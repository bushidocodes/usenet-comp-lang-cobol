Actually it is unfortunate for compilers to casually permit extensions
because it is a source of many portability problems. Often COBOL
programmers (or programmers in other languages) are confused as to
what is in the standard and what is implemented by their compiler, or
they don't even stop to think that there might be a difference.

COBOL is a *remarkably* portable language *if* you stick to what is
defined in the standard, and also avoid the use of implementation
dependent features (like computational data or the COMPUTE statement).
