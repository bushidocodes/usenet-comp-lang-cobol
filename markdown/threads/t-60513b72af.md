I'm curious about the numbers.  IIRC at the height of the cold war the
US and the USSR each had somewhere between 5,000 and 8,000 warheads. 
The UK had a few dozen, Israel had a few dozen, China got a few from
the USSR and France had one to give to whichever country they
surrendered to next.  My (admittedly imperfect) recollection would put
the number at about 20,000ish, not 70,000ish.

If the 70,000 number is correct that puts the plutonium needed for a
single bomb at a bit over a kilogram.  That seems rather smallish.  I
would suspect the plutonium from the missing 30,000 found itself
recycled into larger bombs in the existing inventory.  There really is
no way to destroy it short of waiting a few billion years.

Still, it sound like a hoax thing.  Either that or propaganda from
some anti-nuke group. 30,000 is no small number to be ignored, it
would have received wider press.
