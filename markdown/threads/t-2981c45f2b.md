```
This is a follow-up to a conversation we had last spring about the issue
of compatibility of Fujitsu COBOL and MS Win 2000.

I will be teaching my annual section of "File Processing Using COBOL"
this coming Fall.  My plan is to continue Fujitsu COBOL (v. 3) in the
classroom.  We have enough copies of this product to allow its
installation in the classroom and students will receive personal copies
with their textbook (Thane's "TY COBOL in 24 Hours").  I have ordered
one copy of Fujitsu COBOL (v. 5) (thanks to Roger Johnson) for my own
use.

If my use of v. 5 goes well (I expect it will), we will either adopt
Fujitsu v. 5 (Standard or Professional) or NetExpress (University
Edition) for use in the Fall 2001 semester.  My experience with COBOL is
primary with the Fujitsu product, though Will Price did provide a
week-long introduction last summer to NetExpress' OO and web programming
features.  A final decision is still six months away.

As best I remember from our earlier conversation, both Fujitsu version 3
and version 5 of the compiler functioned satisfactorily in the WIN 2000
environment.  I think I remember some mention of there being
compatibility problems with Fujitsu version 4.

Given that we have now gone 4 to 5 months since that early advice, does
anyone have additional facts or anecdotes that would help with this
summer's upgrade?

Thank you for any feedback and advice.  Your help is always appreciated.

Floyd Johnson
```
