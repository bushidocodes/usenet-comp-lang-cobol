Um, no, you aren't.

This is Usenet. It may look the same on your computer screen in your
office as the Internet, but it isn't the same at all. You have just
broadcast your message (at someone else's expense!) to six hundred
thousand-odd sites all over the world, millions of people in toto, the
overwhelming majority of whom have not the slightest interest in Florida
jobs.

You wouldn't pay to advertise your jobs in German newspapers, because you
wouldn't get any response, but through a historical accident you are able
to plague everyone in Germany with your job advertisment at someone
else's expense.

If I can't persuade you to stop advertising Florida jobs in Finland, at
least follow the rules in the Job Posting FAQ. Put the country first in
the Subject: header, e.g., US-FL-Naples, so that people in Singapore (Hi,
Peter!) can skip your article more easily.

Better still, post job advertisements in the job newsgroups! Usenet is
like a library, with different sections for different kinds of books, so
you can find what you want more easily. You can be as specific as Dade
County or as general as the world.

But posting job advertisements in _this_ newsgroup is like filing the
works of James Clavell under history instead of under fiction. While it
makes a certain perverted sort of sense, it decreases the overall
usability of the library. Suppose you really want to read "Shogun". You
go to the fiction section and ... it's not there. Conversely, suppose
you have a paper on Japanese history due tomorrow. You go to the
Japanese history section and ... it's full of historical novels that may
be very good in their place but are just in your way now.

Of course, a library has a librarian, who can put the books back in their
places. Usenet has no defense except persuasion, which is useless
against commercial incursions. However, if you follow this newsgroup,
you'll see that, while new headhunters pop up every day, they don't stay
long, even though no one can stop them. That's because there's just no
profit advertising Florida jobs in New Zealand, even at someone else's
expense, and sooner or later they realize this for themselves.
