```
Don't know the implementation / platform but an error here is not at all
unreasonable.

Sequence numbers in fixed-form reference format format* COBOL are in Columns
1 - 6.  In that format Column 7 may contain a space, a hyphen, a slash, a
"D" or a "*".  Column 7 here contains "1", which is not on that list.

It's sort of tough to figure out what the author meant by the sequence
numbers here; one possibility is
    000100 01 CSCSCD.
    000150 10  ...
although I'm not a personal fan of the relaxation of "area A / Area B"
restrictions when using fixed-form reference format, so I would prefer
something more like
    000100 01 CSCSCD.
    000150     10  ...
if that's what was meant in the first place.

Also,
    000100 01 CSCSCD.
    001510 ...
is a viable possibility in that format.

    -Chuck Stevens
```

```
If the "00015000" actually occurs beginning in Column 31, the only way I can
think of offhand that this would be valid syntax in  fixed-form reference
format is if your implementation defined Margin R (which must be fixed at a
specific location in any given implementation) as occurring between Columns
30 and 31, which I think is extremely unlikely.  There's also the
theoretical possibility that the implementation allows the location of
Margin R explicitly to be varied by the user, and somehow that location has
gotten set to an incorrect or inappropriate value.  Barring these
possibilities, what this fragment is supposed to mean or accomplish I
haven't a clue.
```

```
It looks to me as if in your second source line the "1" from "10" is in
column 7.  The only valid characters for column 7 (in the ANSI'85 Standard)
are

   " "  (space)
  "D"  (or 'd' for debugging line)
  "*"  (for a comment)
  "/"  (for a comment and a "page throw")

My guess is that your compiler ALSO allows a "$" - probably indicating a
compiler directive or option.

    ***

NOTE:
  In addition, it also looks as if you have found out about the A-margin and
the B-margin in ANSI'85 Standard "fixed" format.  I suggest that you look
these up in your text book or language reference.   Certain parts of the
COBOL ('85 Standard) language must begin in columns 8-11 - other parts of
the language must begin in columns 12 - thru the R-margin (which may be
after Column 72, Column 80 - or wherever your compiler thinks it is)
```
