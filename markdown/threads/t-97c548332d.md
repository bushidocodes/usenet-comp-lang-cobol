After my first semester of Law School, I was ranked 19th out of 180
freshmen. After the 2nd semester, I had moved up to 18th.

I visited with my advisor and asked what was going on? I skipped many
classes and didn't really study until the month before finals (although I
didn't admit my failings to my advisor!). He asked my undergraduate
background.

"I have  a Masters in Math" said I.

"Oh, you won't have any trouble with law school," he said. "The purpose of
law school is not to teach law - laws change every day, no one can keep up.
The purpose of law school is to teach you to THINK like a lawyer: logic,
deduction, comprehensive."

He went on: "Students who come to us from math, the physical sciences,
philosophy, engineering... they all do quite well. Those from business,
accounting, finance... those are your average law students. Those from the
liberal arts: English, music, history... they have the toughest time and
most don't make it."
