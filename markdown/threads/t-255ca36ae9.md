```
Hello all,

I've been reading this newsgroup for a few years now and I have come to
respect the opinions of a good number of people here. I have been
working in a large mainframe shop for the last 3 years or so doing a lot
of new batch COBOL development and a good deal of batch maint. I have
very little CICS background (less than 10 programs). I was recently
selected to join a team building a corporate wide data warehouse for my
employer. My question is this: I will get to learn a lot of new
technology on this project, but I will not get to do a lot of mainframe
work. The project is scheduled for at least a year and a half through
the first 3 phases of iterative implementation. I'm concerned that I
will not get to grow more in my mainframe skillset during this project
and I'm not sure if the skills I will gain will compensate for this. The
project is being implemented on a non-mainframe platform as well. Does
anyone have any opinions? Will a year away from every day cobol
programming be held against me by a future employer? Should I look for
something else that allows me to do more programming (what I really love
to do) or learn a lot about a relatively hot area of the field - data
warehousing (and do less programming)?

Sorry if this post is not appropriate for this newsgroup, I'm just sort
of stuck and there aren't many experienced folks left in my shop to talk
with! Thanks for your opinions!

Steve
```

```
The greater the skill set you can bring to a job the better. In the past with
tech moving a bit slower, peple became experts in one specific thing and
remained in that. Nowdays, programmers are constanly being asked to deal with
new technologies and ways of doing things. Having experience in other areas
only enriches your potential in the eyes of employers, it indicates that you
are able to learn new things(not a sure thing with some people).

Just make sure that whatever you do has some connection with what your long
term career goals are. Data warehousing is still an enterprise area(even if
NT is being used) for most companies. If you were thinking about doing a
project desiging video games or programming embedded processors, then it would
create confusion in people's minds as to your long-term goals
```
