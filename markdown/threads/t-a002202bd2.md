According to David Shelby Kirk's excellent book "MVS COBOL II Power
Programmer's Desk Reference", ISBN 0-89435-305-5, copyright 1991 QED
Information Sciences, page 26:

"OS/VS COBOL supported both the ANSI 1974 standard and the IBM extension
to the ANSI 1968 standard. COBOL II supports the ANSI 1974 standard and
the ANSI 1985 standard. The format of the COPY statement that met the
ANSI 1968 standard is no longer allowed. That format was

01 data-name COPY membername.

whereby data-name replaced the associated data-name from the copy
member."

So it looks like your example was allowed in the 1968 standard and
dropped in the 1985 standard.

David Kirk posts in comp.lang.cobol from time to time. I recommend his
book.

The workaround we used a lot when bulk converting from "old" COBOL to
COBOL II was to change the statement to the following:

01 COPY-BOOK COPY COPYBOOK REPLACING ==01 COPYBOOK== BY ====

where the 01 level in the == == is the 01 level in the copybook. This
effectively the same as your statement.

Chris
