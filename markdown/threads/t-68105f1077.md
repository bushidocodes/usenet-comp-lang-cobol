As your friendly "non-moderator," I wanted to make several statements about
vendor input into the COBOL newsgroups (the preferred, comp.lang.cobol as
well as the less used alt.cobol).

First and foremost, both of these newsgroups are NOT moderated.  Therefore,
anyone can post whatever they want with whatever "hiding" of their
affiliations and return email addresses that they want.   Having said that,
any and ALL input that "clearly" identifies the background knowledge and
motivation of the poster - as well as ways to contact the poster "off-line"
is much, MUCH preferred.

I think that everyone in the newsgroups really appreciates the "technical"
support that various vendor participants provide about THEIR OWN products.
When a question comes in from a Fujitsu user and gets a Fujitsu employee's
response (even if it is made clear that this is not an "official" reply), I
think that all the readers find this useful, beneficial, and desirable.
Similarly for all the other COBOL and tool vendors *and* distributors (e.g.
Micro Focus/MERANT, IBM, Flexus, Norcom, AcuCORP, etc ...)

However, (and I have commented on this in the past - via private and public
statements), when a question comes in from a user saying "How do I do this
with product XYZ" or "I am having the following specific problem with product
XYZ" - if we get a reply (either admitting or not that the poster is a vendor
or distributor),  "I can't help you with product XYZ, but with OUR product it
is easier/better/simpler/whatever - and doesn't have that problem".   Then we
usually find it less useful.  When the reply turns into a "basing" of product
XYZ by a vendor or distributor of product ABC, then many of us REALLY resent
it. (Occasionally, there are times when an employee of vendor ABC may be able
to explain some OTHER interface such as Windows and USEFULLY say - "I am not
certain that this is how it works with XYZ, but it does work with ABC and you
might want to try it with XYZ."

It is also HIGHLY appreciated when those "associated" with a product,
distributor, etc "identify" themselves when commenting on another product
(and usually even when they comment on their own).  Now most of us who are
regulars in comp.lang.cobol KNOW that Bob Wolfe is from Flexus and Paddy
Coleman is from MERANT.   Therefore, if either  of them forgot on one or two
messages to include their usual "signature line," I don't think anyone would
complain.  I don't know if all the regulars know that "gazaloo" is associated
with MERANT (and his return email doesn't make this clear - although he has
said this in some past posts), on the other hand,  Paul Barnett's return
email DOES make it clear that he is with MERANT.

Certainly the "recent" apparent "flames wars" seem to include
vendors/distributors who don't seem to be making their company
affiliations/backgrounds clear.  I won't say that this is "wrong" - but it is
certainly not what I like to see and when I find out about such "hidden
agendas," it tends to make me discount ALL posts from such posters.

General Hints to vendors and distributors:

1) Any and all comments about your OWN products are greatly appreciated.

2) Whenever  possible, please identify your company "affiliation" (or
background) - especially when you are commenting on ANOTHER company's
product.

3) The default (AFAIK) is to assume that any post from company employees is
"not the official statement" of the company, however, it never hurts to make
this clear when posting "personal input/opinions".  On the other hand,
(compare the recent statements from Tony Hill of MERANT), when you ARE giving
us "official policy," PLEASE do make this clear (and preferably, let us know
where/how we can get this "confirmed" via one of your official channels - not
just a newsgroup).

4) "Bashing" of other vendors' products reflects poorly on you and your
company.  You are free to do it (because this is a non-moderated NG), but
please consider the consequences before doing so.  I know that I have some
new names to add to my "killfile" and I suspect other NG readers may be doing
the same.
