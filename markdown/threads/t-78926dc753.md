```
>COBOL is COBOL.  It really doesn't matter what the platform is.

Yeah, right.
 

Wang VS Cobol (74 & 85) has many nice features that make
moving to another dialect difficult.  For instance, I've been 
writing Wang Cobol for 15 years and have never needed to write
a DECLARATIVE.  But the biggest difference is screen definitions.

Wang probably has the most powerful screen abilities of any
Cobol I've found (I think I'm up to six different dialects now).
Things like controlling what data can be accepted into a screen
field (upper only, uplow, numeric), codeless validation (attach 
a validation table or a RANGE statement to a screen field), and
<drum roll> repeating fields (define it once; repeat horizontally,
repeat vertically, or both).

It's all described in Chapter 4 of the Cobol manual (with samples).


If there's anything about Wang you want to know, just ask me.
```
