Sometimes getting parts from machines intended for scrap can be very viable, 
(but it takes time and phone calls to local vendors. I found that if they 
know it is for charity they will sometimes be very generous...). I built 
several PCs for disabled people from parts nobody wanted (got a 200GB hard 
drive for $10...) ending up with some great value. I built a really good one 
for a lady with MS, so she could do a writing course by correspondence. She 
had it for years and passed it to her grandson who is still using it. (It 
cost a total of $150, loaded with Office 2003, Win XP, and 56KB modem. The 
software was OEM or second-hand.)

If your charity is likely to be ongoing for the duration, leasingcould be an 
attractive option. A leasing company can get a tax break on leasing to a 
charity and some of them may be sympathetic. Try and get them cover in a 
local newspaper for example...
