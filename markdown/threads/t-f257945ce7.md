```
"Rebuilding ?", probably wrong terminology, but bear with me. Plus I'm
talking small datasets on PCs.

- COBOL files - for either crashed indices or to get rid of waste space,
(deleted records) - I can use Micro Focus REBUILD.

- MS Access Tables -  I'm guessing I will have wasted space due to
deleted rows (?),  and although haven't  hit it *yet*,  for sure,
Murphy's Law will prevail and inevitably I will get a glitch in the MS
myapp.mdb.

Wasted space is not that critical, given the small datasets, and any
prior reason for doing this was associated with RM/COBOL using Radio
Shack 8/10 inch platters with very limited storage.

I should add, and applies to both Files/Tables, datasets are  active for
no more than a six-week cycle while data is being setup, inspection
occurs at oil plant, reports produced - finito. The ms.mdb can then be
zipped to a diskette. Small users, so no fancy backup systems which
would just cost them more than they can afford.

As yet, haven't addressed the 'rebuild/cleanup" features of MS Access,
which in theory, a reasonably computer literate user should be able to
handle.

Any guidelines on addressing the problem - particularly with reference
to where the DBMS becomes inaccessible through a hard disk glitch ?.

Jimmy
```
