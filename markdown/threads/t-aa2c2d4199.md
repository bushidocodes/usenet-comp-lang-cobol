```
The suggestions you will receive cluster into two basic themes:

1. Move the input to the output a byte at a time, making a decision on
whether to move for each byte, or
2. Use STRING, UNSTRING, FUNCTIONS, REFERENCE MODIFICATIONS, or other
(more advanced) language constructs.

Simpler is usually better.
```

```
You can't get rid of blanks by just inspecting/replacing. To do that you
are going to have to use inspect counts (tallying etc..) and then use

	initialize mysecondnanme
	move myname ( 1:8) to mysecondname (1: ) 	*> or (1:8)
	move myname (10:5) to mysecondname (9: ) 	*> or (9:5)
```

```
To produce this output:
LAST-NAME ="JOHN-JACOB-JINGLEHEIMER-SMITH           "
LAST-NAME ="JOHNJACOBJINGLEHEIMERSMITH              "

Run this code:

    DISPLAY 'LAST-NAME ="' LAST-NAME '"'.
    PERFORM VARYING COUNTER FROM 1 BY 1 UNTIL COUNTER > 40
       IF LAST-NAME (COUNTER:1) = '-'
           MOVE LAST-NAME(COUNTER + 1:) TO LAST-NAME (COUNTER:)
       END-IF
    END-PERFORM.
    DISPLAY 'LAST-NAME ="' LAST-NAME '"'.
```

```
You cannot make a byte in the middle of a fixed length field disappear!
Inspecting it and replacing it with anything is going to leave the
"anything" right where it is.  Try unstringing it delimited by the dash,
then restringing it back without.
```
