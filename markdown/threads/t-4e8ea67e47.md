```

I am curious. WHY do the COBOL newsgroups allow/encourage job postings?
Rather than commenting on "give us rates" should we actually be telling
recruiters to use the "appropriate" newsgroups to place their job postings?

I was still fairly new to the newsgroups when I took over the FAQ and wanted
to reflect what others did - not my personal opinions. Therefore, I
expressed the view that job openings are encouraged - but need postings.
Yet, I really do question this. On the other hand, I am *not* looking for a
job - so it doesn't really impact me. (I can ignore the ones we get as
easily as anyone. And I don't mind reading them to see if they have rates
or not.)

I know that if we tried to "discourage" job postings in the newsgroups and
asked recruiters (and/or headhunters) to use the jobs newsgroups, that we
would still get postings - but I would be willing to try this - if there
seemed to be newsgroup consensus to do this.

Can anyone tell me the PROs for encouraging these posts in the COBOL
newsgroups?
```
