Programing requires knowledge, but programing is not itself knowledge.

It is a skill, like carpentry or plumbing, and you learn it by practice,
not by reading or by watching videos.

In the past, you learned e.g. history from textbooks, but what worked for
you to learn knowledge isn't working for you now when you are trying to
learn a skill, so you think the book sucks. There are lots of books out
there, but you won't like them any better than the one you have until you
understand the difference between learning knowledge and practicing a
skill.

Imagine that for your final exam you are going to run a mile. The faster
you run the mile, the better your grade. The book is like a coach who
gives you your workouts: today we do wind sprints, tomorrow we do
Nautilus, and so on. Working out with a coach is a more efficient use of
your time than working out alone; on the other hand, different coaches
have different theories of which exercises are best.

Your instructor doesn't teach you to run, either. He's just there to
show you how to use the Nautilus in case you don't understand it from the
book. It isn't possible for someone else to use the Nautilus _for_ you.
You've got to do it for yourself.

The best part is that once you have learned a skill from a book, you are
well on your way to another valuable skill: how to learn a skill from a
book.
