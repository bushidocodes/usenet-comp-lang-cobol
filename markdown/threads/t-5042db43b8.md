> They were claiming that it worked fine on previous releases but was
>failing since we went to the combination of COBOL/MVS and LE.

Sounds like you were calling a proc to run the COBOL which did the exec. The
PROC had the SYSIN defined within it. Now someone has changed the PROC. If
your ACCEPT is failing it is because the SYSIN is not there.Doing an ACCEPT
without a SYSIN DD on purpose is foolhardly and definitely unpredictable
depending on how the compiler is designed to handle it. If you changed the
compiler then reran a previously functioning JCL with an undefined SYSIN
then the JCL was written incorrectly erroneously and incompletely.

Just because something worked in once doesn't mean we should do it.

To prove your co-workers claim, recompile the program under your old
compiler and re-run the JCL. Recompile with new compiler and rerun. Compare
results.
