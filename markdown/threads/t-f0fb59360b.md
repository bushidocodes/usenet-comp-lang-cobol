Here's a quick refresher, then...

1) Stay calm.

2) Do a quick recompile using the NOOBJ and PMAP options. (for IGYCRCTL
substitute LIST for PMAP)

3) Check the dump for the PSW.

4) In SDSF H (no need to print the fool thing!) get into the listing
generated in 2) and do a(n) F ' EQU '; this will bring you to the top of
the ASM.

5) Do a(n) F on the PSW.

6) Back up one instruction.

7) Mull over what's there, get the 'smell' of it... and I'll bet dollars
to navy beans that if you've gotten a S0C7 you're at a ZAP.

Note to the newbies: this may not make sense. Such is Life.

I can find some - the best place for this inquiry is the
comp.lang.asm370 or the bit.listserv.ibm-main NGs. Abend-Aid, and other
products like it, use Assembler services to sift through the Address
Space Control Block (ASCB), Task Control Block (TCB), Contents Directory

in Cobol.
