Having learned both common IBM m/f online interfaces, CICS
and IMS/DC, within about a year of each other 10 years ago, I
can unequivocally state that CICS is much easier to learn because
the commands make more sense. SEND/RECEIVE of CICS vs
GU/ISRT to IO-PCB in IMS. Also, built-in HANDLE CONDITION
commands in CICS make for better readablility than checking the
PCB status in IMS.

Of course, once you leave the m/f world and get into the PC geek
arena, online development is trivial. Drag-and-drop the control
on to the form. OOOOoooh, how difficult. Hit an F-key to invoke
the debugger--a real challenge (not) compared to Xpediter, Intertest
or even CEDF.

BTW, CEDF is a pretty cool feature of CICS for debuging. I'm not
sure if something comparable exists for IMS/DC. I know there is
BTS, but it may be that BTS is a seperate piece of software? Any
IMS DC gurus can comment--does IMS/DC come with anything
analagous to CEDF? Does BTS come with it?
