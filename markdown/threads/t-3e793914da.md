```
A newbie, although seemingly competent programmer showed me some code that
he didn't like.  He commented about it having long paragraphs (they were
about 1 page long).

I bit my tongue and didn't want to say anything.  But I felt like saying
something like "Well, as long as the code is readable"  or  "I don't know
what it's doing, so I can't comment."  Instead, I shrugged not knowing what
to say.

I like to write nice little easy to read programs.  But sometimes, you get a
real humdinger, that is all over the place, and hard to understand or read.
I have coded one and one/half page paragraphs, sometimes 2 pages long.  I
could care less.

To me, sometimes, a nice, sequential listing of instructions is very clear.
GO TO's or not.  If I have a paragraph which represents a logical unit then
I don't really care how long it is.  As long as it's flow is clear and
correct.

Well, anyways, I had a little fun tonight.  I met up with a friend at the
Baltimore Inner Harbor (just beautiful on a nice night, Doc you've been
there I'm sure).  It was a guy friend ( I'm not gay, trust me on that ) but
we were just chillin, ya know?  I felt like I was in Mexico or something.
Everyone was partying and drinking and just having a great time.  Old,
young, colored, white, tall, short, pretty, ugly, all types of people.  It's
nice to see black and white people operating in perfect harmony, dancing and
just being happy.  What a better world this would be if people just got
along, and also if they didn't tailgate you while driving about 5 feet away
at 60 or 70 miles per hour.

It was the Operation Sail event where huge, almost unbelievable sailboats
sail into the harbor and stay a week or so.  Next week, all the boats go to
NYC for Independence Day.  Very cool.  Businesses were raking in the dough.

Anyways,  I guess the moral of the story is, don't say anything if you can't
say something nice.  And that is what I learned today.

Stoned,
Jeff
```
