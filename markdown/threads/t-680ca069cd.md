Please, folks... Let's keep age out of this.  At my company we routinely
hire contractors over 55 to write code.  I'm no kid myself...  :-)  Programming
is a learned skill and I've yet to see any age level that can't master it.  
For background I strongly recommend Weinberg's "Psychology of Computer 
Programming", written in mid 70's (I think).  Beautiful essay on what programmers
are, how they think, and who they are.  Best programmers, it turns out, are
*NOT* comp sci grads.   Housewives, english lit grads, music majors, all
tend to do better.   (ain't life grand...)
