```
I hate (almost <G>) to start another thread on REAFFIRMATION, but there is
one issue that is actually "up in the air".  It has now been confirmed by
ANSI and NCITS there is ABSOLUTELY no way to get ANSI X3.23-1985 (The '85
Standard) and X3.23a-1989 (the intrinsic function amendment) reaffirmed or
reinstated (because of the administrative snafu - and my taking advantage of
it).

HOWEVER, there is a real question of what will happen with X3.23b-1993 (The
"Correction Amendment).  I believe (but am not positive) that there is the
possibility to reaffirm it - even though it is written as a "change
document" to a no-longer approved/valid Standard.  (E.g. it has lines like

Page I-12/X3.23-1985 (A-4/X3.23a-1989)
 Change the entry for "Reference-modifier" to "Reference modification".

*NOWHERE* in the amendment (that I have found) does include a statement that
parts of the earlier standards that are *not* changed remain in effect.

I plan to suggest (in a public review comment) that IF (more accurately
WHEN) the other Standards are withdrawn, then this should be withdrawn as
well - as it simply makes no sense as a Standard by itself.

At the risk of seeing lots of comments that disagree with me (again), I
would be interested in what this group thinks about this issue.

Again, you may send comments in (even if you have already sent in another
"general" comment) indicating whether you do or do NOT want this Standard
reaffirmed - when the others are withdrawn.
```
