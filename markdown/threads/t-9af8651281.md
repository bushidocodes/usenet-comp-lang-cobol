Your Opinion, Please

The state of Utah maintains job descriptions for all types of jobs
filled within the state. Recently,
the Programmer/Analyst titles were rewritten to reflect new job
requirements and skills. Typically,
the Programmer/Analyst positions have been changed over the years to
reflect main-stream,
mission-critical skills found in larger organizations. The pay scales
for these series of job titles
have also been adjusted (somewhat) in an attempt to make them more
competitive with the
private sector, although rather unsuccessfully. These titles carry a
series of Programmer/Analyst
I, II, III and IV, where a level I is a newer individual entering this
field, a III being an xperienced
individual and a IV carrying a supervisory/lead function.

We are very much interested in your comments concerning the discussion
below. We would like
to hear from individuals who have been in this profession many years, as
well as from people who
have just started their careers. We would also like to hear from
individuals with management and
hiring responsibilities, and even employment recruiters. If possible,
please indicate your current
title and the state in which you live. Names are not important.

The difference in the starting pay between a Programmer/Analyst III and
a Programmer/Analyst IV
is only $2.31 per hour. The P/A III starting wage is $15.85 per hour.
The P/A IV starting wage is
$18.16 per hour. The P/A III & IV job and skills requirements are
listed at the end of this
document.

In our opinion, the P/A IV requirements are unrealistic, a step
backward, and will contribute to
making it more difficult to find and fill positions. The position
description is very poorly written and sounds like garbly-goop. It
almost sounds like something written by someone with little or no
background in ITS. Typically, these P/A titles have been designed for
large-scale development.
Now, they seem to be blinded and unconcerned about attracting (and
keeping) the qualified
people for specific skills where they are needed most, such as COBOL,
CICS, DB2, etc. We
believe this because an individual at the state personnel office
indicated that he thought that the
Year 2000 problem wasn't a big deal and that we (you and me joe-Cobol
programmer) were hi-
balling for higher wages. This, of course, is not true.

Please keep in mind when forming your opinions that the P/A IV position
not only requires an
individual to perform typical functions of design, analysis and coding,
it may also require an
individual to carry a full supervisory workload function as well. As we
read in-between-the-lines
and based on our experience in government, this appears to be an
attempt to trivialize the work
of the people who keep the large-scale, mission critical systems running
the state's most
important functions and promote people using the "trendy" (FoxPro,
Visual Basic) individuals. We
are *NOT* attacking people who use FoxPro or Visual Basic. It is
simply, for the most part, that
these individuals do not have anywhere near the responsibility,
accountability and stress of
keeping multi-million, it-better-be-right systems. The typical Cobol
programmer may be required
to not only develop and maintain mainframe systems, but also software
for the PC and other
platforms. Typically, a PC programmer will never be required to
develop and maintain mainframe
systems.

Tell me what you think. Please be as direct as you wish. We intend to
compile all remarks
received and share them with the appropriate individuals here in Utah.
Listed below are the
requirements for the Programmer/Analyst IV and the Programmer/Analyst
III. We believe the
state of Utah must do a better job in their recruitment, hiring and
promotional practices.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

State of Utah Job Description for * * Programmer/Analyst IV **

Incumbents perform supervisory or project lead tasks while performing
user data processing
systems needs analysis and creating data processing program solutions to
the needs of end
users by coding computer instructions in computer languages applicable
to resident hardware to
fulfill the purpose of the data processing system. Incumbents
lead/supervise work on a system
projects from inception to coding, testing and maintenance on a full
system requiring resolution of
unique and complex problems. Work level is that expected of a college
graduate in data
processing and five years of experience.

Job Requirements/Skills:

A full range of system development techniques including resolution of
complex technical
problems.
Current data processing languages.
Application of system development techniques.
Hardware configurations.
Data communications.
Must demonstrate leadership/supervisory skills.
Application of information systems.
Applications of IS languages.
Use of IS languages.
Use of IS terms.
Application of data processing techniques and theory.
Application of computer technology.
Use of system flow charting techniques.
Application of distributed data techniques.
Use of programming languages.
Use of system analysis techniques.
Use of software standards.
Application of unique data processing problem solving skills.
Supervise staff or continually lead projects.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

State of Utah Job Description for ** Programmer/Analyst III **

Incumbent performs expert level and end user data processing systems
needs analysis and
creates data processing program solutions to the needs of end users by
coding computer
instructions in computer languages applicable to resident hardware to
fulfill the purpose of the
data processing system. Incumbents work on system projects from
inception to coding, testing
and maintenance of a full system requiring resolution of unique and
complex problems.


Job Requirements/Skills:

Applications of data processing techniques and theory.
Independent and expert use of multiple programming languages.
Independent application of system analysis techniques.
Lead data processing projects or programmer analysis.
