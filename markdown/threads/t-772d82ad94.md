```
Dear CLC'ers,

Having recently  been "cashiered out" of my most recent contract
position of six years (a nice tidy duration, thank you very much),
once again I am faced with the proverbial, "What do I do next?"

Hmmmmm.... tried DICE, a fair number of inquiries, but nothing yet
seems to quite jell, for any number of reasons or no reason at all, as
we have veterans have learned to concede may be the  real painful
truth.

So what to do next? More attempts at the same? Maybe open up the
geographic preference radius beyond the 50-mile from Indianapolis or
Detroit epicenters, take early social security and start that
synthesizer band, a temptation that I have diddled for a few years, to
the point of buying several thousand dollars worth of equipment. It is
quite fun making killer sounds, izn't it?

On the other hand, at the tender age of 62, I just don't think I
could, no, I _can't_, go further into my misty twilight years without
at least of giving something bigger a try.

It may be time to call it quits, at least for a while, of trying to
work for the COI, and instead, go into competition with him at that
proverbial "next higher level" organization-wise. Ahh, yes, a path
rife with risks, pitfalls, doubtlessy at least a little frustration in
and of itself, and yet, I  keep coming back to "I just couldn't live
myself a few years from now knowing that I at least didn't *TRY*."

So here's what I'm interested in, and my pitch is to the un- or under-
employed among you, the professional contractors, the bored, the
already retired and already bored...

I want to go after some "pockets" of significant sized mainframe Z/OS
CICS (with batch) systems still in need of significant web-enablement.
And yes, I know specifically that there are still some of them, more
than one. And I also know that some of the competition is looking at
the situation as a mountain of gold, like $50-$100 million plus $25
million a year in support after launch, cause they're gonna
essentially do a rewrite from scratch. All to achieve a GUI interface,
but maybe not that much more.

Technical approach is to limit ourselves to legacy
pseudoconversational systems, and replace the SEND and RECEIVE with
screen images placed in the COMMAREA, thus turning the installed base
of CICS transactions into the proverbial "service oriented
architecture" (SOA), by any number of techniques that strap into IBM's
External Call Interface (ECI). See
http://publib.boulder.ibm.com/infocenter/cicsts/v2r2/index.jsp?topic=/com.ibm.cicsuc600.doc/ccllao0214.htm
.

While this may seem obvious to some, or most of you, please keep in
mind that the purchase decisions are being made by the COI who, once
again, for any number of reasons, or no reason at all, may be
perfectly happy to spend $100M instead of say, $3-$5M. Well, someone
should call him to task, and I say it should be US. Just don't quite
know who US is yet. :-)

I have some support vendors lined up, and some contract houses who
maybe could share some cash flow burden with us, but not all, so all
should expect to bear some of the interim pain. Technical Architecture
needs to be vetted, because after all, I have been known to be wrong
at least once or twice :-). Proposals need to be  written, customers,
at some level, need to be stroked, and a "resume package" needs to be
accumulated for presentation. All very tentative, of necessity.
Possibility for remote telecomputing? I don't know, I just don't know.
Usually presents a problem, but maybe can be worked out with part-time
onsite presence, teleconferencing, fix-pricing portions, or
combinations of all of the above.

Exploratory posts welcome here on CLC, but of course, please keep in
mind there will be a limit on what I am willing to discuss publicly.
Those wishing to start a private dialogue, please conrtact me at -
klshafer(-at-)att.net.

What say you CLC wimps, are any of you ready for one last Hurrah?

Ken Shafer
```
