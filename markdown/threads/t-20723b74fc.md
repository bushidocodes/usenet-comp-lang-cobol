definition would look like

01 RECORD-OUT.
03 RECORD-LEN PIC S9(4) COMP.
03 RECORD-TEXT PIC X OCCURS 1 TO 2000
DEPENDING ON RECORD-LEN.

I guess since this is wednesday and you posted on monday that on one has
come up with a way of doing this with without looking at every byte.

I have to make some assumptions here since I don't know much about your
data. I don't know if it can include embedded spaces or other special
characters. I will assume that your data does not include null
(low-value).

If you are populating this field from some other data source (another
file ? input screen? ), maybe that facility can tell you the length. If
not then you can kind of short circuit the loop to determine the length
of a field by starting at the end of the field and work backwards look
for the first non-null.

If your data does not include embedded spaces instead of coding your own
algorithm to determine the length, why not use an INSPECT that positions
a PTR to the first space in your field. That position should be equal
to the length of your field. I know that under the covers the INSPECT is
probably checking each byte, but lets face it no matter which method you
do, it has to resort to a byte by byte inspection.
