Hello fellow COBOLists,

When giving a COBOL class I usually recommend a few books etc that the students should get
their hands on.  Among others I mentioned COBOL for DUMMIES and the COBOL Starter Kit,
which includes the book Teach Yourself COBOL in 21 days.  

Yesterday one of my students came to me and complained bitterly - The TYC in 21 days were
hopelessely outdated, as it wouldn't mention the structured programming elements of COBOL
85, like explicit scope terminators, evaluate, and others.  

I guessed that maybe he has picked up an older version of the book, and went checking in
my library - I have the COBOL starter kit, the copyright notice says 1997, and he is
right:  All the IFs are terminated by dots, no mentioning of END-IF, no EVALUATE, none of
all the other goodies

My question now ; did we both pick up some old copy?  Or should the book be just thrown
away, and he should just keep the ACCUCOBOL compiler?


with kind regards

Volker Bandke
(BSP GmbH)

I would definitely keep the compiler, as for the book I guess that is up to you.
I have no experience with Teach Yourself Cobol in 21 days but I have
only heard positive comments about it. As for Cobol for Dummies, keep the
disk and chuck the book.

<My 2 Cents>

When I learned Cobol I used Carl Feingold's text and I thought it was a very
good book. I don't know if he has a updated version or not. The book
I have been using lately is Advanced ANSI Cobol with Structured Programming
by Gary DeWard Brown and I have found it to be quite good. In addition I
like his JCL books and use them all of the time and I find them to be very
useful. So, I would tend to use his book for instruction purposes because
combined they are good to learn how to program Cobol for IBM environments.

The other book I have found to be very good is Cobol Unleashed. It covers
quite a bit and it is written very well but it is not intended for beginners.

</My 2 cents>
