```
7/19/99

I have written a six-page paper on "converting COBOL-produced data to IEEE
data
for use with BASIC/C/C++ programs and how ASCII and EBCDIC can affect the
conversion"  (The actual working title is, "ASCII, EBCDIC, COBOL, and IEEE"
but
that doesn't really tell you what it is).

In plain English, your boss just told you, "Please get the data off the
mainframe or AS/400 onto my PC so I can load it into Access/Visual
BASIC/Delphi, etc."  OR,  "Please move the data from the mainframe COBOL
system to the PC/Unix COBOL system."

Before the paper is finalized, I would appreciate a couple of volunteers to
read/edit it and make suggestions. Detailed (non-technical) instructions for
"editing" will be supplied, along with a brief (less than ten questions)
questionnaire. You would have about two weeks to read, review and comment.

The basic audiences for this will be intermediate/advanced BASIC/C/C++
programmers with little or no experience working with COBOL data; AND,
any-level COBOL programmers who need to learn about moving data between
EBCDIC and ASCII COBOL systems.

I am looking for :
- At least one person who can program in BASIC and/or C/C++, but NOT in
COBOL
- At least one person who can program in COBOL, but NOT BASIC/C/C++
- At least one person who can program in COBOL and BASIC and/or  C/C++
- Someone who either has before or is now in the process of doing one of
these
conversions; BASIC and/or C/C++ programmer, COBOL skills irrelevant.
- At least one person who qualifies for the above, plus, when I say "EBCDIC
character set" your eyes glaze over. (That's one of the purposes of this
paper: to help people who do not have a clue and give them the info they
need to get the job done).
- Can handle Microsoft Word 97 documents.

This paper, along with some software I am developing, will be posted on
the Internet when complete, and will be free for non-commercial use. I am
not offering any cash or merchandise for the editing and suggestions;
however, those who help me out will
get a credit in the document.

That's right: no money, no free software, no beer or pizza, but Mom and Dad
will be proud of you.

While bad "netiquette,"  I would prefer responses be emailed to me; please
don't forget to include your programming languages -  or lack of them where
requested.

Regards,
Michael Mattias
Tal Systems
Racine WI USA
michael.mattias@gte.net
```
