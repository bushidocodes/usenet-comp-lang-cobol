Visual Object Cobol was a worthy attempt by Micro Focus to have a go at the
VB / Visual C++ market, which didn't sell mainly due to marketing problems;
traditional COBOL people couldn't take seriously a COBOL product costing
400 quid (UKP). VisOC has evolved into NetExpress, now shipping version
2.0. This will cost you something like 2 to 3 thousand quid (not sure of
the exact price), but I have to tell you, it is the business as far as
COBOL goes. The IDE and class libraries are very similar in style and
functionality to the aforementioned VB/C++ offerings, and although they are
flogging it mainly for its WWW capabilities (which are damn good) it will
also do all your usual COBOL things, procedural code, client-server,
databases, windows GUI, and OO if you're feeling brave. And other stuff
I'm too thick to understand.

By the way, congratulations on choosing a proper man's language, it's sure
to put hair on your chest. (PC note to women COBOL'ers, this will only
affect the hair under your armpits, which we don't mind here in England,
not sure about the yanks.)

Just my opinions, etc., and weak jokes.

I disagree with your comment about a COBOL product not being taken
seriously because it was priced reasonably (400 quid). That would be
the same thing as saying none of the other development products are
taken seriously because of their much more reasonable prices than the
COBOL products, which is not true.

Most of the COBOL vendors cater to companies who have deep pockets.
CA-Realia has turned to the same tactics as Micro Focus has--to hell
with the independent developer who only wants and needs the basics, such
as the compiler, debugger and some utilities.

The best overall COBOL vendor for price and product capability today is
Fujitsu. From what I have seen, the prices for their products are
extremely reasonable in comparison to most of the mainstream COBOL
vendors. Even IBM's Visual Age product is reasonable in comparison to
most, except for the potential costs of support and upgrades. As far as
I'm concerned, owning the CA-Realia product, I was left in a learch when
they changed their pricing structure from $995 to $4,000+. I refuse to
pay that kind of money and the associated maintenance fees for
components I do not want or need. Right now, I believe certain COBOL
vendors are targeting their PC COBOL products to cash in on Y2k
remediation at the sake of development. In the long run, if this is
true, I don't think that this to be a wise marketing decision.

I congratulate Fujitsu for their entry in the market and hope that they
do not reverse their marketing and pricing methods like CA-Realia and
Micro Focus. The PC COBOL market have, in my opinion, become too
unpredictable. I think this is very sad because I have always been a
very big supporter of the PC COBOL products. I've produced some very
terrific projects with them.
