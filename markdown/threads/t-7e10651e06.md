First off, try using any contacts you have. Obviously, since you are starting
out you won't know many people in IT, but 'networking' as they call it has the
highest success rate in obtaining a job. Try friends/friends of friends/the
bloke you met down the pub the other week. You'll be surprised how many times
Uncle Bob just happens to know of a job for you in the company he works for.

Then get yourself a good resume, and then send a whole load out to all the
big companies you can think of. Companies like to be contacted directly
because it means that they don't have to pay recruitment agency fees - so you
might get an interview at least as it doesn't cost them anything. You
probably won't get a job at a small company because they cannot generally
afford to take a chance on staff without experience. A few companies run
graduate training schemes - so if you have a degree (in any subject) then you
should try to get yourself on one of these. I'm afraid that if you haven't
got a degree then you'll have a hard time finding a job with no experience -
but with y2k looming it'll be a bit easier.

You can also try a computer recruitment agency (details will be in your local
computer press) or replying to ads in the computer press. Once you sign up
with an agency make sure they don't send your details to anyone you have
contacted directly. There is nothing more likely to kill a job offer than 2
resumes arriving for the same person.

If all that fails then try to get yourself any job it an IT department, or in
the business side of a large corporation. Once you get in a company & they
can see how good you are then it is much easier to move across to a job in
programming. Again you'll find this much easier in a large company where
there are more vacancies. Where I work there is a graduate training scheme,
but quite a few people have joined it from the business side and they don't
have a degree. Also one guy started five years ago as an Administrative
Assistant (or glorified filing clerk) for the IT department, he moved to
programming after 6 months & and now he is a Project Team Leader. Computer
operations is also a good place to start - there a quite a few people in my
department who started as operators.

Good luck.
