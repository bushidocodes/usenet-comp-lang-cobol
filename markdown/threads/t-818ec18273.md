A standard numerical approximation is given in chapter 26 of
Abromowitz & Stegun, "Handbook of Mathematical Functions", equations
26.2.22 and 26.2.23.

A Google serach on "Inverse Normal CDF" pulled up several examples,
the first one returned is in JavaScript, courtesy of

Jan Deleeuw (deleeuw@galton.math.ucla.edu)

p = form.prob.value
t = (p > .5) ? (1 - p) : p
s = Math.sqrt(-2.0 * Math.log(t))
a = 2.515517 + (0.802853 * s) + (0.010328 * s * s)
b = 1 + (1.432788 * s) + (0.189269 * s * s) + (0.001308 * s * s * s)
u = s - (a / b)
form.zval.value = (p < .5) ? (- u) : u

Of course you need to scale the returned Z value. Good luck with the
log and square root functions. The ? & : is a "C" construction for
if/then/else. Also note that the range of s is quite large, so fixed
point may not be a good representation.
