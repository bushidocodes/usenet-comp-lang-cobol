I am only finishing my first semester of an intro to
mainframe class that is in fact a programming COBOL
class, our instructor is from another college, and
he says it is more important to learn the COBOL.
We got very little mainframe training, it
s mostly learn as you go. Although I am ahead of
the rest in the class (thanks to the advice
I get in this newsgroup), I am far from what I
would consider a COBOL programmer, but last evening
the instructor came to me as soon as he entered
the room and started talking to me about a
programmer 2 (?) job at the one state college.
I told him I could never, get it, I don't have any
degrees, he said it didn't matter, they can't find
people that want to program COBOL and the only
thing they are concerned about is if the person
can do the job. After he talked with me a while
he told the entire class that anyone that completes
the second semester could qualify for this type of
job. He said they need COBOL programmers now,
mostly for the year 2000 change. BTW: he told me not
to worry about them no longer needing COBOL programmers
because the college pays for you to take other
programming courses.

To add to this, the Harrisburg Patriot News had
an article about a local company called AMP, they
said they can't find local people to program
and they must recruit via the internet because they
need people that can program COBOL, but most colleges
won't even teach it, the fellow quoted said that
every one is more excited about the more popular
languages like C++ but most don't realize just
how much COBOL code is still being used.

Anyhow, I realize that all of you know this. I was just
a little surprised that my instructor would talk to
me about checking into a job as a COBOL programmer,
when I can't even program yet . It did make me
feel good though, not bad for a 46 year old
"dumb dutchman".


Greetings,
joe
