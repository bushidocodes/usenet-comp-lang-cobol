part-time telecommuters. With the supposedly huge demand for cobol people, I
can't understand this.

Some companies do hire part-time telecommuters. Most are
ex-programmers that "retired' to raise a family. Here in St. Louis,
Edward Jones (http://www.edwardjones.com) hires part-time
telecommuters. 30hrs/wk, no benefits. I know of both mainframe COBOL
and Unix/C programmers doing this work, but they are all locally
recruited.

I do full time telecommuting, for a company in another state, and
their clients that are in other states. But my specialized
programming AND business skills is what got me into telecommuting (ie.
specialized skillset).

The problem is from a management perspective. Part-time AND
telecommuting are the least desirable. Since Jones hires direct and
they don't pay benefits, it becomes worthwhile to them.

I'd call all the HR departments in your city, and ask if they are open
to such an arrangement or currently have such a program.

7 or 8 years ago, this wasn't possible. Now, I have the power to
access client/server, mainframe, and Unix applications and directories
all from my home office PC. Heck, I can even work while I'm on
vacation, using my laptop. (But, no I didn't!!).
