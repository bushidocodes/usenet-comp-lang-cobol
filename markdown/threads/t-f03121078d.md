Well we all know they've got a good sharp sense of humour Down Under. But
universally we know that Customs/Immigration officers are not imbued with a
'funny bone'.

Ivor my former DPM at Debenhams in Taunton, stayed on for several years after I
left UK. As a result of a merger/takeover he got the heave-ho and of all things
started up a company making computer circuit boards or some such down there in
beautiful but sleepy Somerset. (A definition of UK West Country folk - they have
two speeds, SLOW and STOP!). Don't know the specifics but appears he made a
bundle of money, enough to let him and his wife go gallivanting around the world
some three times each year. (Came here about three years ago - Seattle, across
WA and Montana, up into Alberta, across to Prince Rupert in BC, boat down to
Vancouver Island, back to Vancouver, Seattle, air to Toronto/Niagra Falls and
back to UK. Phew ! )

Anyway, as a result of his endeavours he latched on to one of those UK Board of
Trade selling 'missions. So they head for parts East, first stopping over in
Hong Kong where they get the grand treatment as befits a government marketing
party.
Next stop - Aussieland.

Hot, and very tired from the flight, he gets off and dutifully fills in the
temporary immigration form. A female immigration officer says, "You haven't
completed it - you missed this". He looks at the missed question :-

    Do you have a criminal record  ? - Yes/No plus details

Tired Ivor responds, "I didn't know you still needed one after all this time".
She would have liked to have nailed him there and then - but from an 'official
party' ? He related the event to his Australian hosts over drinks and they
roared with laughter.

He was always a quiet bugger. Back to his grammar school days at Exeter in
Devon. They had to fill in a form of subjects they wanted to take and they were
given an additional optional subject. Something piqued his interest, so he
entered it.

Having read his application, his tutor with a look of disgust said, "Carpentry
?" "C-A-R-P-E-N-T-R-Y ?".  Ivor gave the only response he thought was
appropriate. "Well, it was good enough for Jesus Christ". <G>

Jimmy
