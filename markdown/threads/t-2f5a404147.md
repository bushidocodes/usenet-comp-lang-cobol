I can't answer this really.  I have tried over several years to "pin down"
my "usually reliable sources" at IBM about who/where OO COBOL is actually
being used by mainframe sites.  The best that I have been able to
UN-officially been able to determine is that there are more than 5 and less
than 100 (probably less than 20) "real" customers doing (significant?) OO
COBOL development on OS/390.  I believe that there have been previous posts
in comp.lang.cobol indicating that at least one shop is a "large bank" in
Germany.

I have the feeling (and this could be wrong) that this is a "chicken and
egg" situation.  IBM is NOT stressing or enhancing its OO COBOL support
because no (few?) customers are using it - and no customers are using it
because IBM doesn't seem to be "pushing" it.
```

"William M. Klein" wrote:

> I have the feeling (and this could be wrong) that this is a "chicken and
> egg" situation.  IBM is NOT stressing or enhancing its OO COBOL support
> because no (few?) customers are using it - and no customers are using it

I wonder if there are a couple of other reasons:

1.  Few shops are starting up mainframe CoBOL environments from scratch.   If
you already have a large body of non-OO programs, the advantages of OO are less
obvious.

2.  Enterprise environments are finding integrated systems are more vulnerable
than when their components are more isolated.   They concentrate their
integration in the data side, purposefully keeping programs not integrated.

If either of these are important, they are important to the future of CoBOL,
whether or not we are interested in OO.   Are people starting up new CoBOL
mainframe shops from scratch?  If not, why not?   Are new enterprise shops
designed around data warehouses, data marts, and database tools, with
specialized data entry, data retrieval, and data communications tools?
```
