```
Subject: more-or-less says it all... a GSD500G-01AW, filled and powered-up 
the usual way... but something at the back of my neck, that low, 
murmuring, leftover-lizard part of the brain that deals with machinery 
('Whack it... *there*.' 'I said... what it... *thudthere*' 'chug chug chug 
chug chug'... 'how does he *do* that?)

... something at the back of my head said 'not tonight, Josephine... let's 
just watch the machinery work.' The pipe hummed... and water went in... 
and more water went in... and at the point where it should have gone 
*chunk* and the innards started spinning...

... nothing happened.  In a few moments foam decided the dishwasher was 
too crowded and should be exited; I turned the cycle-switch to the Drain 
point for that section and the water exited where it should have.

Being the kind of fellow I am I let everything sit for long enough so that 
one could have had a cigarette... and did the routine again.  Clik to 
fill, pipe, she go pppssssssshhhhh... and foam, speaking some rather gooey 
diphthongs, began to make its way to liberty again.

My guess is that DeForrest Kelley would have said 'I'm a doctor, not a 
physician... Jim, he's dead.'

But... tonight Mr Insomnia decided to stop by so I figured that I'd tell 
some folks a story.  Some folks, having particularly configured killfiles, 
would not know that happened, other folks, having other configurations, 
would skip on to the next thread... and still others might say 'Yep, 
r'minds me'a th' time that ol' Cousin Rutheford flooded out his 
mother-in-law's kitchen, newly remodelled in lime-green... if you thought 
they weren't the best of friends before, well, let me tell *you*...'

... and maybe... jest maybe... someone out there would say 'I've seen 
something like that, all ya gotta do is pop open the side panel and you'll 
see this little box labelled 'Gnihtyreve Seod' (they didn't want to make 
it *too* easy) and snag one off eBay... last time I got one it was three 
tuppence, a groat and since they'd just had Garlic Stew they threw in, for 
free, a great farthing... worked fine for another year.

'Rutherford did the same thing and it was DOA, had to start all over... 
hey, you got a bunch o' decades out of machine, it's not like it owes you 
all that much.'

DD
```

```
The defroster coils (or associated thermostat) are probably the #1
cause of refrigerators failing.  If you see ice or frost on the back
(or sometimes sides) of the freezer compartment, that's almost always
the problem - the evaporator coils have frozen up, and are encased in
a nice insulating block of ice.

It's also a highly independent fault, and bad defrosters are basically
no indication of any other issues with the device.

On most refrigerators replacing the coils (typically there is more
than one coil, and they should *always* be replaced as a set) and
thermostat is pretty trivial, and well within the capability of anyone
semi-competent with a screwdriver.  Usually the service manuals are
easy to find (if only for ease of finding the part number, or there
are plenty of parts vendors on the Internet with decent search
functions, or if you live near a big city, there's usually several
decently stocked parts stores).  There is sometimes an exercise in
hidden screw removal, but it's usually not too bad.

Usually a new set of defroster coils is on the order of $50 (I
replaced a coil/thermostat set for $51 on a 17 year old GE fridge a
couple of months ago - oddly, that's the second time I've done it on
that fridge, the first was a couple of months after the warranty
expired).  Given the cost of new refrigerators, that's a reasonable
investment.  And once you defrost the evaporator coils, it'll work for
a week or two without issues (even without the defroster coils
working, it'll take that long to freeze up again), so you can spend a
few days farting around without too much hassle.

Of course when my mom's died last year (I'm pretty sure the defrosters
were only one of the problems, I know one of the fans was out too),
she decided she "needed" a stainless steel one.

What's all this got to do with Cobol?
```
