1- Actually, pow-self and pow-true are 2 totally diferent things. While
pow-true refers to a pre-defined constant (probably a 88 level on a bit
field), pow-self refers to an object reference, in the case, the form
itself. So, the answer from Jerry P is correct, but you will have to dig a
little to find all references to pow-??? as they are documented on diferent
places.
2- If you are working with 4.2, the best way is to rigth-click on the
(Script) item bellow the form in the PowerCobol development tool, and then
select (Print procedures...). It will print a (nice?) listing of all your
modules; since there isn't any way of configuring the paper size,
orientation, etc. the result won't be to good, but any way it is a program
listing. I think Jerry P was refering to print the generated cobol when he
said you wouldn't like it. That code is so far way from what we write, that
it is (almost)  useless for documentation purposes.

Finally, it's nice to know that there are other portuguese people using
PowerCobol. Feel free to address me privatly (mailto:pvieira@emporsoft.pt)
about any issue using PowerCobol. I know it's not simple to plunge into such
a tool, but after learning your way arround, you won't want any thing else.

See you

Paulo Vieira , Emporsoft
