I don't know if it is the ONLY problem, but I do see one thing (possibly not
clear when you read ahead a chapter),

Look at:


>
>  01 DETAIL-TABLE.

>

How big is the original item (DETAIL-TABLE)?  Now, how big is the redefined
(01-FILLER)?

There is a GENERAL rule in redefines (with some exceptions - and some
exceptions for specific compilers - but you are always safer if you follow
this rule)

NEVER redefine a smaller field by a larger field.

When you (eventually) reference
    Detail-Desc - with a subscript of 3,
what storage will it get?

Basically, the "results are unpredictable".  (If you weren't doing 01-level
redefines, things can get even messier).

Some (many? most?) compilers give warning messages against doing this - but
if yours doesn't, you still shouldn't do it.
