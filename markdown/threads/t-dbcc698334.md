Alister,

Don't you just love MF sales people and the BS that they can throw at you?
Don't get me started down that trail...  :-)

Anyway, I have been using WB4 combined with Visual Basic (v5/6) front-ends
now for a long while.  We used to have everything in Dialog System, but only
due to cross-platform concerns - when we dumped support for OS/2, we
converted everything from Dialog to VB and called our Cobol DLL's from
there.  It works great, is stable, and is pretty fast.

NetExpress has only one advantage that I can see - no more paying for
distributed run-time system (unless you are developing web-based apps).
Other than that, it's not worth paying the $$$ to upgrade.  WB4 works great,
can seamlessly invoke Win32API's, and is pretty stable - we use it for our
applications that we resell to customers.  The only drawback to WB4 is that
your customers (internal or external) must have the WB4 installed, or you
must ship the RTS with your apps.

I've never worked with Linux (or Unix) before, so I can't tell you how it
works with it.  Hope this helps...
