G'day George.

Canary Wharf is somewhat off the beaten track in London and will
probably require you to commute from somewhere, especially if you want
to have some fun on your trip as well. The only effective transport at
the moment is the Docklands Light Railway and that doesn't really go
anywhere interesting but will connect you with the tube in one or two
places. Of course, I could be out of date now since it's now well over
a year since I worked down there and there is a promised tube extension
to somewhere nearby.

London is a big place, lots of people, lots of everything. Some people
might recommend living outside and commuting in but I would get over
here and get somewhere relatively central and enjoy the experience. For
some unknown reason there seem to be a lot of Australians in the Earls
Court area, and the commute from there could take you up to an hour,
door to door. You'll also find a couple of free ANZAC-oriented
newspapers to make you feel right at home but I just know that you'll
want to try our warm beer. :-)

Fifty quid an hour seems OK for six months, that'll come up to Â£1,750 a
week minimum and should see you right. It won't get you the West Winf
of Buckingham Palace but you'll be able to get somewhere decent enough
and still have tons of cash left over for the finer things in life.
Once the first six months is over there'll probably be more money in the
deal as well. You may find that the overtime rate will be no different
from the standard rate, though, and this is not unusual in the UK.

I see you mentioned that it's a bank, so be prepared to have to wear a
suit - lots of these places are a bit old-fashioned like that. In fact
bring loads of mates over and then maybe I can get a contract in OZ for
an obscene sum, not that I think it'll be much fun after the rugby
result the other day :-)
