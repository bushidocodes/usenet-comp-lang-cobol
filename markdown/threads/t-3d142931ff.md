Hmmm... interesting situation here...

I just did a scan of all newsgroups on IBM (my service provider), and, guess
what? The only ones that reference RPG are those focused on Role Playing
Games... absolutely nothing for them poor Report Program Generator people
who have to struggle with S/34, S/36, S/38, and AS/400 machines.

This is really strange... and worrying... :o

There are several newsgroups that focus on COBOL (besides alt.cobol and
comp.lang.cobol), a whole bunch dedicated to Visual BASIC (most sponsored by
Microsoft), and more newsgroups than I'd care to think about devoted to
coding in C/C++ (I know the language is cryptic, but... sheesh... does it
require THAT many newsgroups?!?)... but absolutely nothing on RPG!

Now... I *know* that RPG is supposed to be so easy to understand, anyone can
pick it up in a matter of hours (YeahRightSure...), but, still... how come
no one has ever proposed a small message area in the deep darkness of
cyberspace where the poor, hapless, little RPG coders can gather and discuss
the latest in op-codes? The ramifications of RPG/Free?? The fact that
Microsoft itself uses AS/400s (and probably RPG/400) to track its financials
(so much for Windows/NT)???

Even *more* distressing... now that my shop is actually purchasing a whole
bunch of AS/400s, and I'm gonna have to help support an application system
written in RPG/400... which newsgroup will I turn to?!!!!!?

Yikes!!!!!

Any RPG people out there who wanna talk Factor1?... Factor2??... resulting
indicators??? Anyone at all!??!
