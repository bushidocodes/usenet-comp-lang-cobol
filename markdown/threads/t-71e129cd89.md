```
Okay, I've gotten very use to wonderful FORMS processing
available in Fujitsu COBOL. (If they would just wake up and
more it to a bunch of platforms, there would be little competition
in the COBOL world anymore.... :)

However, for cross platform stuff I use AcuCOBOL, which is a
great compiler, and for NT/Windows/OS/2 stuff that HAS to be
compatible with "IBM standards" I use Visual Age COBOL, which
is the best quality compiler of the lot.

The problem is that I need to write out a BUNCH of forms in
postscript, and darn if I can figure a way to do it that works.
(This will have to work for PCL5 as well I suppose.) I tried
wirting the form in a separate file, opening it in sequential
mode and writing it to the printer, but line endings or some
other never-to-be-sufficiently-cursed factor intervents and
causes grief. Not to mention it is difficult to fit the real
text into where it belongs. This is primarly for a very
patient customer, but it is really torqueing me off that
either I have to pay a fortune to AcuCorp for a forms
generator, or I have to figure out something that should be
relatively simple.


Anyone have any recommendations or better yet, anyone else
solved this problem before and willing to share their solution?

Thanks
-Paul
```
