There are at least a couple of ways.

1) Redefine your record as follows ....

01 DUMMY REDEFINES record-of-50-chars.
03 PIC X(24).
03 USEFUL-BIT PIC X(10).
03 PIC X(16).

DISPLAY USEFUL-BIT

2) Use reference modification ....

DISPLAY record-of-50-chars(25:10)

Cheers,
Kev.

PS. I`m not quite sure what you mean by "between the 25th and 35th chars", so
those 10`s might want to be 11's or whatever.

If you are using a COBOL-85 compiler, there is a language construct called "Reference Modification". This
allows you to extract and modify parts of a string.

For example:

01 DATA PIC X(50).

DISPLAY 'POSITIONS 25 THRU 35 OF DATA = " DATA(25:10). 25=Start of substring 10= length of substring

Also reference modifiation can be used on the receiving side of a move statement.

MOVE 'XXXX' TO DATA(5:4).

This replaces positions 5 through 8 with 'XXXX' .

I hope this information is helpful to you.

Ron Voyles -- AT&T Consumer Billing Systems
