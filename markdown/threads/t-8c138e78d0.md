```
There have been a couple of psychology experiments which I have read
about - where having extra tools hurts the problem solving.

One wanted people to cross a room without touching the floor as quickly
as possible.  If they had two planks of wood with two ropes among their
tools, they tied one piece of wood to each foot and crossed the room.

If they only had one plank of wood with one rope, they tied the rope to
the plank and skidded across in half the time.

When I moved to Colorado, my parents already lived here.  A couple of
months earlier my father had a heart attack and a bunch of his friends
moved a large dining room table to his recently finished basement.  It
was hard work with a bunch of crushed fingers going up the narrow
staircase.  With my family up there, my mother mentioned in passing that
it was too bad her big table was down stairs.  So I went down stairs,
got under the table, and carried it up stairs.  The tool of one person
worked better than the tool of a bunch of people.

Which indicates that we should be careful in how we encourage
programmers to use or not use various COBOL tools.  KISS should be at
the forefront of our recommendations and policies.  Maybe dynamic memory
management will allow us to do a rare task - but make sure it isn't
being used promiscuously.
```
