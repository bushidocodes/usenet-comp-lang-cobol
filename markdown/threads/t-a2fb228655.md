One was a recursive Quicksort which is a slightly modified version of
JAZQSORT that Joe Z. posted here recently. I changed it to work with a 30
character key.  The other is Leif Svalgaard's optimized version of
Quicksort from the CobolReport web site. The times below are the total
times for 10 sorts of 50,000 records with 30 byte keys:

RW's Radix Sort:  1.27 secs
JAZQSORT:         4.58 secs
Leif's Quicksort:   1.03 secs

All are pretty decent results but the difference is much smaller than
Robert claimed.

good way to pass parameters to a generalized sort to specify where the sort
