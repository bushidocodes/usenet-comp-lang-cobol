--- Earlier post snipped for brevity --------------------
There are a number of editors on the market which address COBOL. The major
workbenches (e.g., Realia, MicroFocus, etc.) include thier own editors.
The shop I am in uses SPF/PC, from Command Technology, and we love it.
Another location in our company has just selected a product called Slick
Edit, which they say is even better than SPF/PC, but I have not yet seen
it, so I cannot say (it could be it's just cheaper...).

There are also a number of editors which are on the public download sites
which can do COBOL. The basic requirement is that they are 'column
position' aware, and that you can set tab stops in them. The old IBM
Personal Editor II for DOS could do this. An updated verson of this (I
think) is the TEDIT editor which comes with OS/2 (used to edit config.sys
during install, etc.) I don't know whether this editor is distributed with
the IBM DOS line, nor whether the OS/2 distributed version will work on DOS
or Windows.

On the UNIX side, there are lots of older text-mode editors which could do
column aware. The main thing is to look for something old, as the newer
GUI based editors do not seem to be as likely to notice column positioning.
