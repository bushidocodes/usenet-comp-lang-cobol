If you are looking for heuristics for *when* (e.g., what circumstances) to
refactor, I have only a little bit to offer.
We've found that many COBOL programs (well, in fact, most programs
regardless
of language) are 10-20%, i.e., contains removable cloned code.    Given that
it costs about $1/line/year to have code around, finding and removing
typically
15% of a 100K SLOC application can make a visible dent in your budget every
year.
You can find out about clones at our website.

Others can probably offer other advice on when to refactor.

If you are looking for *how* to refactor, you can
1) do it by hand with an editor
2) do it with automated tools.
We offer the capability to do automated refactoring in the form of the
DMS Software Reengineering Toolkit.
DMS is generalized compiler technology, and can be obtained
with a COBOL front, and can parse/analyze/transform/prettyprint.
It is the basic machinery needed to carry out refactoring.
See http://www.semdesigns.com/Products/DMS/DMSToolkit.html.
