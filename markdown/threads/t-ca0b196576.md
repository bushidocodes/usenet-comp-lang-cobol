### Bull DPS7000 & Y2K

- **From:** "G. van Vlimmeren" <gvlimmer@hoogenbosch.nl>
- **Date:** 1998-09-01T00:00:00
- **Newsgroups:** comp.lang.cobol
- **Message-ID:** `<01bdd57b$52f00fc0$2c050380@s70>`

```
Hi,

For all you Cobolprogrammers working on Bull DPS7000 systems:
We've tested a complete administrative environment on a DPS with critical
dates in the near future.
(like 1999, 29 feb 2000, etc...). The system itself worked like a dream.
Only problems we found were some date-related conversions in our own
printing programs, that were fixed within 1 - 2 hours max.

The Mainframe itself, working with a 'non-2000-proof' version of GCOS7
performed outstandingly. The only glitch we encountered involved changing
the systemdate back to the past, while rebooting. This problem was solved
easily and will be removed from the current '2000-proof' version of GCOS7. 

We understood from the testing department at Bull that not many mainframes
had undertaken this physical step into the future yet,

So, rest assured. It'll get the job done.
```
