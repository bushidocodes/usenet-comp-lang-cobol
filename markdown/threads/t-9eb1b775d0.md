I am not sure whether it works for Windows 95, but you could try
re-installing Windows without reformatting your hard disk.  Under
Windows 98SE this is a way of repairing problems with Windows without
having to reinstall all your programs and data.  Your help system and
documentation should indicate if this is allowable, if not try asking
Microsoft or a Windows forum.  You might still have quite a bit of
work to do to re-establish program settings, defaults etc.

Another way that should definitely work is to reinstall Windows from a
re-formatted hard disk, obviously back up everything first.  This
would have the advantage of also thoroughly checking your hard disk
for incipient failure, which might anyway be the underlying cause of
the problem in the first place.  While long-winded, the process of
reinstalling anew might clear out a lot of clutter that can accumulate
from discarded programs that never uninstalled properly as was often
the case.  If you went this far, you might think it worthwhile trying
to get Windows 98SE, to upgrade your system at the same time, but
Microsoft no longer sells it, so it might be hard to come by.  The
problem with this route, is that you still need a fresh copy of the IE
5.5 installation files.

Another way, if it is available under Windows 95, could be to use the
repair facility for IE, there is such a feature in Windows 98 under
system tools, but it might be somewhere else on W95.

Another way, try to beg/borrow a copy of IE 5.5 from a friend with old
computer magazine freebie disk.  I can't remember when IE 5.5 was
superceded, but you might even be able to order an appropriate back
issue from a Computer Magazine company.

Another possibility is to try another browser, such as a more recent
(but not necessarily bang up-to-date) version of Netscape, Mozilla,
etc.  Netscape has/had lots of old versions on its website when last I
looked a few months ago.

Hope this helps,

Robert

Yep. The Houston tower and approach control folk are a pretty laid back
bunch.

You'll appreciate this (at 3 o'clock in the afternoon):

Pilot: "Houston Hobby, Cessna 324 inbound from Shreveport with Charlie."
Tower: "Cessna 324, squawk 300 and ident."
Pilot: "Squawking"
(pause)
Tower: "Cessna 324 I have you. You are 110 miles SSE of Houston. Recommend
you do a 180 and come to 045 degrees."
(pause)
Pilot: "You mean I missed the whole fuckin' city?"

And my moment:

Me: "Hobby tower, Cessna 77 turning final for 10."
Tower: "Cessna 77, traffic is a 737 at 3 o'clock on short final for 80. Step
it up."
Me (looking right) (aw, shit!) "Cessna 77 roger."

quarter-million pounds, fill up the entire window, and do, oh, 200mph.)

school near Galveston Bay after work to drill a few holes in the sky. After
about an hour, she headed home. Oops! Fog.

She radioed her instructor who told her to head for the practice area and
he'd be up to get her. About 20 minutes later, he appeared (in another
plane, obviously) and told her to follow him, they were going to Hobby.
Thirty minutes later, as they approached Hobby, it too, was below VFR
minimums.

"Ok," said the instructor, "we're going to Houston Intercontinental." Twenty
minutes later (after sunset by now):

Instructor: "Approach Control, Cessna ABC, flight of two request approach."
TAC: "Cessna ABC, this field is commercial only."
