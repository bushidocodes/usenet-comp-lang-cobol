```
In order to use the EXAMINE verb, your compiler must be set to support a
dialect with it.  (I think that with MS COBOL 5.0 - this would be either
OSVS or MF.)  Have you chosen one of those dialects?  If not, it won't
understand the EXAMINE verb at all.

The TALLY special register is also an extension that is only available with
certain dialects set. You should just be able to use it as a sending field
after the EXAMINE statement.

NOTE: You may well want to look at the INSPECT verb instead of the EXAMINE
verb - so you can get more "portable" COBOL code.
```

```
Should start in Area B (column 12).  Because the sentence starts in
Area A, the compiler thinks 'EXAMINE' is a paragraph (procedure name)
and is complaining that you left out the period.  Note the message:

>    12 EXAMINE a TALLYING ALL "E".
>* 641-S************* **
>** '.' missing following procedure name declaration, or unrecognized verb
>* Checking complete - errors found

If 'EXAMINE' starts to the left of Area A, you could get into a whole
host of other possible error messages.  When I cut and pasted your
program and compiled it with MF COBOL 3.2 (a close relative of MS COBOL
5.0), I got a lot of errors, but the first one flagged 'EXAMINE' with
"Missing Period".
```
