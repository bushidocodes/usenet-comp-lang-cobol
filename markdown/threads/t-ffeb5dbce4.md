I went to Chubb years ago. Paid my own way I didn't go through the Top Gun Class.
(Our class was the first class that DID NOT use card readers for program source)
Was one of the smartest things I've ever done. I don't know if the current
curriculum lives up to the same rigorous standards that were imposed when I went,
but if it does and you make it through, you will have your pick of jobs. I have
heard from some recent grads that the curriculum and standards are not as tough.

(I hope I don't sound like anyone's grandfather... but in my day..: ) ) I
remember the first day of class. The Dean came in and told us to look left and
right, half of us would be gone or asked to leave in six weeks. She wasn't far
off. We started with 30 people in our class. After 6 weeks we were 18!!!

My wife was accepted to the Client Server Top Gun Program, but hasn't started yet.
The topics covered looked pretty good. They pay for the training which they
estimate at approx $14,500 for the Client Server track. (Sounds high to me too for
a three month course! I paid about $5000 for a six month course when I went. )
After graduating they pay you approx $28,000 per year , although you are only
required to stay with them for eight months after graduating. Considering the
value they place on their training plus the $21,000 they will pay for the first
eight months... I guess this isn't too bad of a starting salary. To make it better
I don't think you pay taxes on the value of the education.

Go for it, good luck, and as I am sure you have read in this forum before. Do
your own homework : )
