The presidents of micro focus user groups meet monthly by telephone to
discuss issues related to mugs.  We were told months ago about this
decision.  We immediately put together a position paper for Merant to
take to management.  We essentially said that the most important things
about the annual user conference were the excellent training and the
people networking that goes on.  We doubted that Merant could provide
any altenate solution that would include these.

Merant responded to us in one of the telephone meetings.  They said that
the cost of the annual conference was too much for what they perceived
the compnay gained.  They have decided to increase web based conferences
and training sessions, and to increase support to regional meetings.

So far there has been one announced web based session, and a monthly
'chat' session set up.  If they are increasing support to region
meetings (MUGS) I haven't seen it (Don't misunderstand me, Merant
support of the mugs has been excellent.  The problem is that interest in
mugs and mug activities has declined drastically over the years. 
Membeship is down and mugs are dying in several places around the
country.

The bottom line for now is that there will be no conference in 2000. 
Some of us are trying to spark interest in a cobol conference, sponsered
by one of the independant groups, or perhaps by several vendors.  So
far, there has been interest, but no one who has stepped in to cary the
torch...
