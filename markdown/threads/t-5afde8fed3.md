As you noted, Bill, this is a recurring problem in CLC.

I got to thinking about it.

I have to admit that over the years, I have enjoyed the off-topic threads 
much more than the on-topic ones.

However, I realise that isn't true for everybody.

Here's what I think the problem stems from:

You have a group of lively, intelligent people (for the most part... :-)) 
and they are just tired of talking about COBOL.

What more is there to say about COBOL? It simply isn't an interesting or 
exciting topic any more. Sometimes it was very interesting to see people 
with problems in COBOL programming and look at and discuss alternative 
solutions, but as the use of the language declines, we get very few of those 
posts any more. Instead it comes down to very basic "kiddie stuff" that 
prompts Doc's message about homework.

There is no point in arguing "religious wars" about OO COBOL vs procedural 
because nobody is really interested, and the OO COBOL people have, for the 
most part, moved on to better tools and languages.

The internicine wranglings of the Standards committees and the inherent 
inability to get things done has made that a pointless topic too. Nobody 
believes it any more, and nobody cares. Few people are serious about a 
future for COBOL, so why expend energy  making suggestions or arguing with a 
remote and ineffectual body about what needs to be included in it?

The remaining hard core of COBOL users (largely IBM mainframers) can talk 
about COBOL in their environment on IBM-listserv. Few of them actually need 
or want help with programming and if they did, they wouldn't post here.

I believe that, as a COBOL forum, this group has become moribund.

But that is just one man's opinion, and  even if it is true, it doesn't make 
the forum worthless. Although I don't come here as often as I used to, I 
still check it regularly. It is still a bastion of free speech and there is 
still wit and humour here which makes it worth my effort.

Like I said, I value the differing opinions on things in general and, for me 
at least, it is much more interesting to debate controversial issues from 
daily life, to explore ideas, philosophy, literature, and get insight into 
the way other people see things that may bother me personally, than it is to 
argue about the "proper" testing of VSAM reply codes, or the relative merits 
of SECTIONs in COBOL.

Having said all of that, I completely agree that it is good to flag 
off-topic posts with OT in the subject and I generally try to do that.

The fact that I haven't done it with this topic indicates I think it is 
pertinent.

Pete.
