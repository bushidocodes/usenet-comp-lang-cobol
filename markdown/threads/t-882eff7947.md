Syria is indeed a tragedy.

We are witnessing people coming out of the dark ages of fear and ignorance 
and demanding a better deal. Normally this process would take place over 
several hundred years but because of modern communication technology and 
wider educational opportunities it is being compressed into a decade or so.

The message is becoming clear to tyrants everywhere. Ideas have power and 
simply will not be suppressed by ruthless regimes. The world is moving 
forward and the legacy of centuries of despotic rule in some places is over. 
The emerging Democracies will be shaky and unstable, for they are not used 
to governing themselves and it will take time for them to get the experience 
they need. People who have clung to a blind faith for centuries and been 
controlled by their religions and a series of ruthless rulers who enforced 
their will are not going to be able to cope with sudden freedom very well. 
But they will get the hang of it, and the realization is growing that it is 
better to live free in a disorganised State than to be enslaved in an 
organised one.

I see the current unrest as a legacy of the last century. By the end of this 
one the world should be a better place for most of its inhabitants.

Education and communication will see to the mental requirements and 
technology will see to the physical.

Sadly, because it is being "rushed", and because of the nature of modern 
weaponry, the price is terrible and tragic.

Pete.
