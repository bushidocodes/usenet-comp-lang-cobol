Iconoclast that I am, a few thousand years of anything seems in need of
violation.   Also, in my educational tradition, violating earlier accepted
meanings for things is encouraged.    Ideas continue to move ahead, evolve
and get changed.   Words change from benign meanings to politically
offensive or politically correct.   Even technical terms are not sacrosanct.
So I have no problem with extending the scope of a given terminology
to better reflect its reality in practice, beyond its theoretical
boundaries. 

My original, somewhat outrageous, remark was that true structured 
programming was not possible in COBOL prior to the ANSI-85
standard.   The replies were as I expected.   No surprises there.  Your
reply did take me by surprise, at least a little bit.  

Those of us with some enduring affection [perhaps misguided] affection
for COBOL should be pleased that it continues to evolve and improve.  I
suspect those who frequent this forum are pleased.   However, we should
not be unwilling to acknowledge the shortcomings of prior versions of 
the language.   It is important that COBOL continue to get better with each 
well-reasoned discovery in software practice.  Yes, we need to avoid 
jumping on each fad, but some new ideas simply seem right and are.
