Not entirely correct, old son. They are reserved words used in the
COBOL Report Writer. In any decent shop, report output has been
device-independent for decades though you will see unit-record as
as a device in the ENVIRONMENT DIVISION in many programs.

› But MVS COBOL Compiler does not support these verbs ! Can any body tell me
› Why these verbs are not supported in MVS COBOL and
› suggest some equivalent verbs/ logic.

Sadly, Ganesh, some people decided that they didn't like Report
Writer for differing reasons, and it seems to have been knocked
out of the language.

INITIATE would cause the report to commence, with suitable header
pages and the like.

GENERATE caused the program to iterate through the detail, putting
in page headers and footers at appropriate moments.

TERMINATE would get report footer pages printed and close the file.

REPORTS ARE was for naming the reports in a report dataset, so that
you could have multiple reports in one big file.

As to suggesting equivalent verbs/logic, we just don't often use
COBOL at mainframe sites these days for pure reporting programs,
we tend to use things like Selcopy or EasyTrieve and similar types
of product.

The thing with Report Writer was that you defined your report in the
DATA DIVISION, REPORT SECTION, which is where most of the procedure
was generated from. The PROCEDURE DIVISION in a pure reporting
program was very short, comprising mainly GENERATE statements inside
the INITIATE/TERMINATE pair. The advantage was speed of development,
a competent programmer could wrap up his Report Writer program in
about half to two-thirds of the time it would take him using COBOL
procedure statements.

Do I miss it? Kind of. I don't undertand exactly why it had to go.
It was widely used; it was useful. Maybe that's why.

Charles

Report Writer was an IBM extension to ANSI COBOL. It was a very nice
feature, if somewhat obscure and a nightmare to debug if you weren't
very comfortable with it. There are no equivalent features in any of
the follow on COBOL products.

I was part of the original VS-COBOL-II beta program and we had a
number of discussions with IBM about this. For ANSI compatibility,
one of their original design decisions had been to drop most of the
old VS-COBOL language extentions. Also they had a number of 'new'
extensions they needed to add and felt that including the old ones as
well would dirty the water too much.

They also were depending on products like the Cobol Conversion Aid and
another product, the name of which escapes me now, that would handle
report writer to COBOL-II conversion. I remember looking at it but
rejecting it as it had a licensed run-time component for converted
programs that was quite expensive. We decided to leave any programs in
report-writer in VS-COBOL until they got picked up in the normal
development/maintenance cycle.

Another reason I heard, as an aside, was that one programmer had been
responsible for the report writer extentions. No one else really knew
this feature and it was written in very 'inspired' code. He was long
gone, no one else really understood it so they were glad to drop it.

Scott Peterson
