### Draft of the next COBOL Standard - *not* Available on-line

- **From:** "William M. Klein" <wmklein@ix.netcom.com>
- **Date:** 1998-10-27T00:00:00
- **Newsgroups:** comp.lang.cobol,alt.cobol
- **Message-ID:** `<7158ft$67e@dfw-ixnews3.ix.netcom.com>`

```
(Sent to comp.lang.cobol and alt.cobol - with CC to J4 and WG4 - from Bill
Klein)

FYI -
  The draft of the next COBOL Standard which was previously available
on-line (in either ASCII or PDF format) is currently NOT available.  The
reason being - that the version that was most recently posted was CD 1.2
which was wildly out-of-date.

The J4 (ANSI-ish) and WG4 (ISO-ish) Standards groups are currently doing an
"internal quality review" of CD 1.4.  For various processing reasons, they
have decided NOT to open this up to the public and, hence, have not made an
electronic version of this draft generally available.

Their schedule for processing this review has been extended at least once
and I do not now know when they hope to have all comments processed and the
next publicly available version of the draft Standard available for viewing
(much less comments).  However, as CD 1.2 was so out-of-date and will be
even more out-of-date by the time all outstanding comments are processed, it
was felt (I think) that having no version available was better than having
this misleading one out there.

If you are interested in what the current draft looks like - or in what is
happening or when you will be able to see a more current copy, please
contact

  For J4 - Don Schricker (J4 Chair) at
      doncobol@mediaone.net
            or
  For WG4 - Ann Wallace (WG4 convenor) at
       nwallace@us.ibm.com

NOTE: If you are working with any Vendor's product that claims to "match"
the next COBOL Standard (for anything - most commonly for OO), please
remember that each and every draft of the next Standard IS a draft and is
very much a "moving target".  There are many features that have not changed
for many drafts while there are other features that seem to change with each
draft.  Just don't believe a vendor when they tell you that they KNOW what
the next draft will look like (or when the final Standard will be
official/approved).
```
