"The TIOBE Programming Community index is an indicator of the popularity of
programming languages."

The simple explanation might be that there was an increase in activity in December 2014 that met the criteria that TIOBE used to calculate the index causing the rating to rise for January 2015. That activity has since decreased back to more normal levels.

What I found remarkable (hence my post) was the increase to 13, which as I
recall was the highest rating in about 10 years. The only article I saw that
might account for some of the rise concerned regulatory changes in banking
in the UK, or parts thereof. The article mentioned that the regulatory changes would require changes to COBOL programs and that COBOL salaries had
increase by about 11%. Though I cannot grasp how that one item would have
caused the near doubling of activity that moved the index rating so high.

Other causes for the increase might have included other regulatory changes,
hiring of replacement programmers for those retiring, budget approvals
for replacing COBOL systems, etc.
