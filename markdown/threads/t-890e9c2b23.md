The major values that I see in allowing the specific that data areas are
constant are two fold.

1. It makes it easier for the compiler to determine which data areas can
be included in the executable module, something of importance for
re-entrant programs to cut down on the acquired storage and
initialization overhead.

2. It allows the programmer to guard against inadvertant change to a
data area that was intended to be read only.

If this capability is implemented by a USAGE CONSTANT, I would like it
both at the 01 level and the 77 level. At the 01 level it allows the
building of title and heading lines easily rather than having to create
one large field. The 77 level allows the compiler to reuse constants for
its own needs. A CONSTANT SECTION would also be acceptable.

I consider the addition of this facility a useful one but if there are
complications, it should not hold up the publication of the standard but
rather be carried over to the next standard.

My proposal on constants passed at the last meeting and will probably
be in the standard. The syntax is "CONSTANT constant-name [IS GLOBAL]
IS literal". There is no level-number. You can specify one anywhere
an 01 or 77 can be specified. There is no constant section, so it is
easy to bring them into the source along with records and such in
which you need them. One of the earlier versions allowed a structure
that would let you build your headers, but that was shot down in favor
of this simpler method. You might mention in your comments that you
would like the feature. Every little bit helps.

In addition to the constant entry, you can use a constant directive to
define constants (>>CONSTANT literal), but it is not as safe as the
constant entry. Also, the directive does not work well with COPY.
