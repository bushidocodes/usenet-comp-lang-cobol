No, no, no, no, no! Have you got it all wrong, guy!

Let's say your program is called MYPROG.EXE and the data file is DATA:TEST.DAT

In DCL you'd do...

$DEFINE/USER W_TESTE DATA:TEST.DAT
$R MYPROG

In your COBOL program (MYPROG.COB), you'd code...

SELECT TESTE ASSIGN TO W_TESTE
ORGANIZATION IS... (either INDEXED or SEQUENTIAL)
ACCESS MODE IS... (RANDOM, SEQUENTIAL, or RELATIVE)
etc.

Then, in the PROCEDURE DIVISION...

OPEN INPUT TESTE.
...or...
OPEN I-O TESTE.
...or... whatever (OUTPUT, EXTEND, etc.)

W_TESTE is the logical name of the file, connecting the internal reference to
the external object. VMS automatically makes the association for you when you
execute the OPEN. You never actually 'move' anything to the logical reference,
since it's a system managed pointer, of sorts.

Comprende? Good! I knew you'd understand... :D
