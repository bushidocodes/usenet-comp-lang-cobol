I have used MF COBOL for 6 or 7 years... haven't used Realia
extensively, but I do have some experience with it. MF seems to have
more of the bases covered in terms of platforms supported. So if you are
looking for a tool that can work on OS/2, NT, Windows 3.1, Unix (AIX),
then I think MF is a good choice. Realia by comparison has no UNIX
version available. This could be important in the future because you may
decide to create some "middle layer" and move some of your code from the
PC back to a UNIX server.

The ugly part of MF is the tech support. They have a great product
(IMHO)... but their first line tech support people are refugees from
McDonalds.
