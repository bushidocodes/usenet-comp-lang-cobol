That's exactly the wrong way to look at it. First of all it's not 5
instructions *per* record. It's 5 instructions per PERFORM in a program that
processes millions of records. Everything you can do to reduce the overhead
of processing *per* record is a big savings because everything is multiplied
by the number of records you have to process. Removing as many instructions
as possible from any looping path while still doing what you have to is a
win, no matter what fraction of time they require. That is probably the 1st
optimization technique anybody ever discovered since the dawn of coding.

Take a medium size program that does 100 PERFORMs to process every record.
100 PERFORMs * 5 excess instructions is 500 excess instructions. Like you
say, not much. Now consider a 10 million record batch run. 500 x 10 million
is 5 billion. Are you saying 5 billion extra instructions aren't significant?
As you know we are paying for MIPS or CPU utilization in most cases so
it's time *and* money. Of course the biggest savings would be to process
less records but you can't do that after you blocked your file maximally and
buffered all you can. Next best things is to cut down everything you can in
*per* record processing because that's your biggest opportunity for saving
time overall. Every statement counts. And all of this is in addition to the
benefits of writing code that's easier to understand and work on and has
less impact on paging etc.
