IMHO, you should go to a fully accredited four college and get a B.S.
degree. I would suggest Purdue only because I am an alum and am
famaliar with their program. You should take a course of study that
fits your interests and skills. You could focus on PC programming by
learning BASIC, C++, PASCAL etc. You could focus on the busniess
world by learning COBOL, FORTRAN, RPG etc. You could focus on systems
programming by learning ASSEMBLER and taking logic and computer
science courses. The trend in business applications appears to headed
toward client/server applications. This would require PC programming
skills, a thorough knowledge of networks and networking
(telecommunications) and some mainframe programming as well. There
are still plenty of CICS, DB2 and IMS jobs out there as well, so
knowledge of databases and their structure are important to learn as
well. Any good engineering school, such as Purdue, will have programs
to fit all of what I have just listed.

Good Luck.

Steve
