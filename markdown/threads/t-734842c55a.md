The currency is the 'Euro' and the conversion is only a small part of the
problem. The major one is that you have to conduct business and report figures
in dual currencies. eg. UKP and Euro.

This is common to all nation states, even those that aren't joining the first
wave and using the 'Euro' in there own markets as a currency, if they wish do
do business in Europe.

I think that tourists are also supposed to use Euro's anywhere in the EC and
they are supposed to accepted as legal tender even if the country they are in
doesn't use Euro's themselves. That means Point-of-Sale equipment has to be
changed etc.

It's one of those seemingly small problems that turns out quite large. I've
done a bit of EMU work myself already with POS and a banking application. Both
weren't a problem as the POS application already coped with two currencies for
use in the US/Mexico, US/Canada and the banking application was for currency
conversion anyway ;-)
