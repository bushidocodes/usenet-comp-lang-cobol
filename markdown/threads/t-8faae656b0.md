I assume from the way you worded it that you are going into these sites
as a contractor ? Unfortunately if you are a salaried employee as part
of the beast, regrettably, sometimes you just can't ignore the politics.
EDP should be a 'pure science', (I prefer 'art').

I'm not telling tales out of school - because it was such a long time
ago (around VAT '73). Debenhams bought out a family supermarket group in
SE. Gave the family directors a whole bunch of money - and get this,
then gracefully allowed them to stay on as directors. (Portents of the
future - you can see where this is heading ?). Their thinking just
didn't jive with ours - they wanted to do it their way. We already had a
successful system for A/P (Bought Ledger) that told the merchandisers
what they were buying. It was agreed they (the subsidiary), would run
the Food A/P for the group. 

Initially I did it, but then I had a lead Systems Analyst who used to
travel weekly from the S.W up to London. He was coming back saying, "But
it isn't going to work". I understood perfectly what he was saying but I
had no hard proof facts on which to base anything, just his feelings and
mine.

What I should have done was follow my instincts, write a letter DIRECTLY
to the chairman, copies to others, and let the proverbial hit the fan. I
didn't - guess what, it was a complete cock-up. The external auditors
warned that if the mess wasn't cleaned up for the following year - they
would qualify Debenhams accounts. (To those not appreciative of that
last little bit - that's like seeing 'this company's accounts are
questionable' - your shares take a real nose-dive on the stock
exchange). And to cap it all, the Financial Director, one of the
instigators of the take-over, announced 'it was because we had a
computer boo-boo'. That comment together with all the navel-gazing that
used to go on by decision makers - is the reason I said to hell with it
and made a fresh start here in the Great White North. (I could have
happily seen out my time there, but they got taken over by Burtons the
tailors anyway).

I love it here, but funny thing is I get the distinct impression that
down in the zippity-zippty oil patch they don't navel-gaze, but actually
try to look in their bloody navels.

Jimmy
