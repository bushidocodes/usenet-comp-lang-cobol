It doesn't mean either ! Check your manuals/text book for moving
numerics.

	9v99 is the same as 9(01)v9(02) and the 'v' is an implied 	decimal
point.

move 1234567.12 to 9v99 gives you 7.12 . If you want to get your 
123..etc then :-

	move 1234567.12 to 9(07)v9(02) or 9999999v99

 - looking at the latter (9999999v99) you can see why we put the number
of digits in brackets to avoid confusion.

Note - if you display/print 9(07)v9(02) you will get "123456712" - which
isn't what you want. For display/printing move to NUMERIC EDITED :-

  move 9(07)v9(02) to pic z(07).9(02) which is zzzzzzz.99
