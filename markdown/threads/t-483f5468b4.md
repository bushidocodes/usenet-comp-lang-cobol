I'm not aware of any "canned" tool, but surely it can't be too hard to
create one.

I wish you had told us the O/S and brand of database, but...

If you can hit the database though an ODBC interface, you can use SQLTables
to get a list of all the tables, and SqlColumns to get a list of all the
columns. (You can also get all the 'relational integrity' info - e.g.,
foreign keys - this way). The column function returns the size, decimals ,
NUMERIC, VARCHAR, etc, etc etc. for everything in the destination database.

Auto-generating the COBOL  "IF X is NUMERIC"  or whatever is trivial once
you have the destination specs.

But I think you are going to spend a lot more time 'mapping' source fields
to destination table-columns.

Feel free to contact me direct if interested in pursuing this on a
professional basis.
