```
I thought I'd share my Win98 Upgrade experience with my fellow COBOL
professionals.

To say the least, it was stressful and just plain did not go well.  But in
the end, successful.

Installation took about 45 minutes on a 200MMX PC with 64MB RAM, and at the
very end(you guessed it), the installation crashed.  Everything locked up
and died.  Aargh!!  "How could this be?", I thought.  Windows 95 ran fine
with all my drivers and peripherals and now the latest and greatest Windows
98 is crapping out!  I'm sure you could understand how hot under the collar
I was.

So, I hit the old reboot button on my PC and waited for the bad news to
appear on the screen.  Sure enough, "Windows protection error... Your system
must be rebooted."  Yikes!  What the heck did that mean.  No specifics, no
details, nada, nunca, zip, nothing!!  So, I rebooted again.  Same damn
message.

Well, by now it's midnight and I'm seeing red.  Do I go to bed and start in
the morning?  Heck no, I'm a stupid guy and I have to make this work.  I
paid $90 bucks for this ridiculous software, and it has to work!.

Next, I rebooted into safe mode.  It said some strange message about my
video settings and how it would have to use standard VGA mode.  "Fine.  Just
get on with it", I thought.  Finally, I get a desktop.  So where do I go
from here I thought.  I decided to check my c:\bootlog.txt file.  Nothing
revealing there.  So now what?

At this point, I figured it must be some older OEM peripheral driver that's
not compatible with Win98.  So I went into my control panel system settings
and removed a few things.  I removed my STB video driver, my joystick
driver, my 3Dfx video accelerator driver, and my gateway monitor driver.  I
figured I could reinstall newer drivers after I got Win98 up and running and
I was back on the internet.  I noticed I was getting sound in Win98, so I
left my sound card driver alone.

Then, I figured I better remove a few pieces of software that run when my
system starts.  They weren't in my start group, so I realized I'd better
remove them in the control panel add/remove programs option.  I got rid of
Norton Antivirus, and my Gravis Grip joystick software.

Well, time to reboot and cross my fingers.  Sure enough, Win98's
installation screen came up and the rest of the install continued.  The
install had crashed where it was trying to detect my hardware, so that's
where it picked up.  This time, it detected everything, and low and behold,
actually dialed the internet and downloaded the latest drivers for my video
card, 3Dfx accelerator, and plug and play monitor.  That sort of made up for
my aggravation, but not quite.

Anyway, I have a few tips to follow BEFORE running the upgrade:
1. Uninstall or turn off any software that runs when you boot-up Win95.
2. Remove your virus detection software.
3. Change your video settings to standard vga, and a standard monitor.
4. Remove drivers for video accelerators and joysticks.
5. Start early in the day so your not up all night!

Good luck! :)
```
