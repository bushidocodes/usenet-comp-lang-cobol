Probably like most, I'm a bit jaundiced by all the hype about
e-commerce, and grin when I see all those yuppie dot coms going down the
tubes. However e-commerce does appear to have some merit - catalogue
your products on the Net and HOPE somebody will buy.

But here's an interesting twist happening in Calgary. Husky Oil has been
around for quite some time, most notably known for its gas stations but
recently took over Renaissance Energy one of the local 'darlings',
medium sized oil/gas exploration. The latter misstepped, so having grown
to a certain size, became a juicy candidate for takeover. (The takeover
sharks are in a frenzy in the oil patch and any 'loose change' available
in the States is also being used).

Anyway back to Husky - following (paraphrased) written by a computer
consultant (yuck!) in today's paper. Husky uses the SAP suite of
e-business applications to do admin and handle materials (inventory).
Husky implemented wireless PDA(Personal Digital Assistant) to record
accurately consumption of inventory at each warehouse.
They formerly used the traditional , purchase order, receive delivery,
write cheque etc. Under new approach each supplier is responsible for
ensuring products are always on hand at all Husky warehouses. Each
supplier issues a MONTHLY invoice based on CONSUMPTION - not shipments,
(so the latter bit means it is the supplier's problem if he tries to
shovel unwanted product into your warehouse).

System produces a DAILY receipt and consumption status for each
supplier, sent to suppliers via the Net who then decide what to ship.
(means Husky is not concerned with admin effort associated with managing
low value items). Husky uses UNIX on servers, and Windows CE 3.0 on its
PDAs. SAP uses Oracle DBMS and Visual Basic used to construct and
maintain application to run on PDA components.

Now of course the above doesn't cover all the specifics, and like any
application there is a downside. The writer of the article may well have
had a hand in the design - but he doesn't comment. Nor is there a
mention of the language used with SAP - could it be C or C++ ? (Don has
had dealings with SAP back East - any ideas on language ?).

Just so it doesn't appear as one 'glowing' story. On the downside
Renaissance shares were at one time around $50 but new Husky shares
stand at about $13. Husky is some 75% controlled by Li Kai Shang (not
correct spelling, but close), 5th richest gent after Billy Gates. Reason
for low shares - the investment community aren't too thrilled at 75% of
the action being controlled by one shareholder.

Anyway, anybody got any other interesting e-commerce stories, or neat
approaches to using the Net ?

Jimmy, Calgary AB
