```
I thought I had made my PERSONAL position clear a while ago, but thought
that starting a new thread and explaining it again MIGHT be useful -
especially when at least one person that I respect (yes, I mean you, Pete)
seems to have missed it.

1) This is an unmoderated newsgroup.  This means, BY DEFINITION, anyone may
post he/she wants - on whatever topic she/he wants - and using any tone
desired.  (I won't get into laws on slander or other legal issues which
apply to any "public" speech.  I assume anyone tending to such posts either
already knows them or doesn't care about them.)

2) I rarely use a "killfile" technique in this newsgroup, but do when a
single person gets to the stage that he/she does many/most/all of the
following
  - posts frequently
  - makes erroneous statements frequently
  - states opinions as fact frequently
 - misrepresents others in his/her replies
 - does not acknowledge her/his previous errors
 - attacks others opinions as "wrong" while refusing to accept her/his
opinions as "views" not "truths"
 - seems to "turn off" the majority of experienced participants within this
newsgroup (whose opinions and knowledge I respect)

3) Before I use a "killfile" technique, I will ALWAYS try to explain my
personal opinions on productive participation in this newsgroup - both
publicly and privately to the person in question.  If this seems to be
ignored and the person continues to post notes that I see as
counter-productive, non-responsive, and/or confusing opinion with fact, then
I will use KILLFILE (or similar) approaches to avoid reading the posts
directly.  I will continue, however, to read replies to that person and when
necessary add additional information to explain  either what facts are in
error or what opinions I disagree with.

4)  As much as I enjoy and profit from this newsgroup, my PERSONAL opinion
is that its active participants represent only a *tiny* portion of those
using COBOL to earn their livings or produce "production" applications.
Therefore, I use my "contacts" and experience outside this newsgroup AS WELL
AS communications within the newsgroup to further my opinions of what is and
is not important to the "COBOL community". I do *not* limit my view of where
and how COBOL is currently used to any single platform or type of program.
Furthermore, I use my contacts with a variety of COBOL vendors to gain as
much knowledge as I can of what is and is not happening with COBOL vendors
and their users.  When any individual posts a note in this newsgroup that
runs counter to MY experience or opinions, I often reply with my OPINION or
evaluation - but hopefully "mark" it as based on personal experience NOT as
"truth from on high".

  ***

I don't expect this note to change anyone's posting habits.   I don't even
think that I will get those who * IMHO * abuse and misinform within the
newsgroup to change their ways.  I did, however, think that it was important
for me to restate WHY I react as I do.
```
