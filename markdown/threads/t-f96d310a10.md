```
To all of you involved in the International Standards of COBOL effort

The recent scramble to keep the spec public touched my heart.

Thanks to all of you.

This same kind of thing happened with the first ANSI Standard spec. At that
time
the logic of ANSI was (as I remember it) unless they owned the content of
the specification of
the language standard, they could not recover their cost of operation.

Users have had the most cost of the development and success of the COBOL
standard,
and vendors certainly deserve a close second place.

The Goverment Printing Office published the first specification at TAX Payer
Expense.
At least one and maybe more were published by the Canadian equivalent.

By the continued publication of anything that includes COBOL language
specification
as part of any document does not remove it from public ownership.

How or why would anyone want to enforce such a restriction?

Thanks to all of you for your efforts.

Warren G. Simmons
also at:
warren@tinycobol.org
```
