It has been brought to my attention that some people are having trouble
getting mail to PRIMA from these web sites and registrations are also not
getting done.

Ths is largely due to the sites not being currently maintained as all
available resource is being channelled into preparing the new combined site.

The new site is late and represents an investment of hundreds of hours of
web programming (I did most of it so I know...) Had I realised the extent of
what was involved, I would not have undertaken it. As an experienced project
manager I can only say I badly undercalled this and it has been a sobering
experience.

Never mind.

We are now in the Home straight and I expect to launch the new site within
the next few weeks.

The new site will be a COBOL-friendly place for people with an interest in
COBOL and will also be a showcase for new PRIMA products and services.

I intend to provide code samples, tutorials, background and concepts on a
number of aspects of both modern and traditional COBOL as well as some fun
things in a new lounge area. (You will be invited to contribute if you want
to, once the site is launched.)

Because of the problems being reported (and my simply not having the
capacity currently to fix them) I have decided to let people in CLC (that is
YOU, gentle reader) use the new unreleased site in "preview" mode.

Before I give you the URL, a few words of warning:

1.It is not a "traditional" web site with static pages being served in HTML.
Rather, pages are generated on the fly in HTML5. All main pages have C#
code-behinds that update numbers of visits and provide "smarts". Most pages
include client side javascript for theming and dropdown selections. One of
the benefits of this is that you can change settings and themes for pages
using the settings control provided at the top of the main pages. Some pages
look better in the "dark" theme; some are better in the "light"...

2.If you really hate Windows 8 you probably won't like this. It uses a touch
enabled Metro style interface. I have viewed some of the pages on tablet and
smart phone and it all worked fine. (Just think... while you're stuck on the
train or bus on the way home you can browse the new PRIMA site on your smart
phone... :-))

3. It can be viewed in most browsers but the more modern your browser is,
the more features you will see. (IE8 does not support theming or full screen
videos, for example. IE9+ does. The best browser overall was found to be
Chrome.)

Anyone wishing to join the PRIMA registration base SHOULD be able to, using
the logon facilties on the new site. You can email us from the "terms of
use" at the bottom of each main page.

here's the URL: http://primacomputing.co.nz/PRIMAMetro

It is "unofficial" and still in development.

Please post here if you have any comments or suggestions on the new site.

Thanks,

Pete.
"I used to write COBOL...now I can do anything."
