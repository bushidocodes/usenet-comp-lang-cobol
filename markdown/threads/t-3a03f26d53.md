Assuming the text contains no embedded spaces, you could do:

    MOVE SPACES TO TEMP-VAR-RIGHT-JUST
    UNSTRING VAR-RIGHT-JUST DELIMITED BY SPACE
        INTO TEMP-VAR-RIGHT-JUST
    MOVE TEMP-VAR-RIGHT-JUST TO VAR-RIGHT-JUST

Or, skip the last move and use it directly.

The UNSTRING will skip the leading delimiters (SPACEs) copying
the remaining characters.

A similar question appeared in the newsgroup several months ago.
In that case, a database was being used and the DB function LTRIM
was determined to be the best approach for that particular problem.
If you are working with a DB, you might also consider LTRIM.

Determine the position of the first non-blank character an do a MOVE like

MOVE VAR-RIGHT (POSITION:FUNCTION LENGTH (VAR-RIGHT) - POSITION + 1)
     TO VAR-RIGHT.

The statement

UNSTRING VAR-RIGHT
         DELIMITED BY ALL SPACES
         INTO DUMMY ANOTHER-DUMMY

MOVE ANOTHER-DUMMY TO VAR-RIGHT

should do more or less the same.

If the number in VAR-RIGHT were not longer than 18 digits, you could  
redefine it with PIC Z

01 VAR-RIGHT                    PIC X(40).
01 FILLER REDEFINES VAR-RIGHT.
   05 FILLER                    PIC X(22).
   05 VAR-RIGHT-Z               PIC Z(18).

and MOVE VAR-RIGHT-Z into a numeric field.
