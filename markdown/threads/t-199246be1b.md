```
As Mr Klein so often reminds us, COBOL COMP-1 and COMP-2 data definitions
are 'implementor-defined" and I am stuck on one implementor's version of
COMP-1 and COMP-2 data. (It's a BIG implementor, though!)

I have been searching the my manuals, IBM's online bookshelf, the whole
internet, and anyhthing else I can think of and cannot find the
"description" of the data format for COMP-1 and COMP-2 when using FLOAT(HEX)
in IBM COBOL for S/390.

Here are comparable descriptions for FLOAT(IEEE) if anyone needs them (also
to show an example of what I'm looking for?)

COMP-2,  IEEE single precision: 32 bits total. Approx  Range +/- 8.43 E37
to +/-. 3.37E-38
  Bit   31:         Sign Bit (0=positive, 1=negative)
  Bits 30-23      8 bit exponent, positive bias 'x7F'
  Bits 0-22      23 bit normalized mantissa

COMP-1,  IEEE double precision: 64 bits total.  Approx Range +/- 1.67 E 308
to +/- 4.19E-307
  Bit   63:         Sign Bit (0=positive, 1=negative)
  Bits 62-52      11 bit exponent, positive bias 'x3FF'
  Bits 0-51        52  bit normalized mantissa

I have some data I am working with, and for integers I can almost 'deduce'
the FLOAT(HEX) format, but I just cannot seem to figure out how much of the
mantissa is valid when there are decimals in the value. It seems as though
the binary values terminate partway though the length of the four or eight
bytes (depending on exponent?).

Can anyone point me to a reference work on this? On-line, paper (I have a
couple of clients with paper manuals, but finding the correct manual in a
stack 25 feet high.......), type it out..whatever.

NOTE: this format "appears" to be the same as used in VS/COBOL II (MVS) for
COMP-1 and COMP-2. (Can't find that, either).


Regards,
Michael Mattias
Tal Systems
Racine WI USA
michael.mattias@gte.net
```

```
of operations".
```
