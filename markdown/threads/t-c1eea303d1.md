Although as a recruiter I certainly know that some of my colleagues troll
for resumes, I can't imagine why someone would run a blind ad to establish a
salary-rate in today's job market. This isn't my ad, but you're right:
"competitive salary" is almost a cliche anymore and doesn't really mean
much. That being said, the reason why I'm personally reluctant to post
salary info is twofold: the range is often so broad and so fluid that to
post one would be meaningless; and I want to begin discussions from the
point of you being interested in the project/job rather than the money only.
The fact is, salaries are negotiable and the process of negotiating is a
give and take process. You're motivated to get as much as possible and the
employer is motivated to hold down costs as much as possible. Why put limits
on the end result before we've each established what it is we want from each
other on the skills/position side?
