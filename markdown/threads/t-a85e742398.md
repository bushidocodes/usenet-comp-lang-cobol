if your application doesn't do anything "nasty" (like calling
16-bit OS/2 PM api) then your migration should be simple. the
32-bit compiler and run-time has a very high level of compatability
with the 16-bit products (including run-time behaviour).

performance? well, it depends. during our beta, some customers
with large amounts of data and/or calculation intensive apps,
typically reported app performance gains of 2-5x faster. some even
gained an order of magnitude increase in app performance.

if you app is *very* i/o bound, you may not see a noticeable
increase.

basically, 32-bit apps will run faster for the simple fact that
they operate in a flat address space. 16-bit code frequently
jumps across segment boundries causing a level of indirection.
