   MOVE  "Invalid selection," WS-KEY-UNPACKED   TO  WS-ERR-MSG

I've done more Easytrieve than I care to admit.....

In your example, you use:  MOVE  "Invalid selection," WS-KEY-UNPACKED   TO
WS-ERR-MSG.

The syntax of the MOVE statement is:
MOVE <send-field> <send-field-length>
TO <receive-field> <receive-field-length>.

The field-lengths can be numeric literals or numeric fields.  So, your
WS-KEY-UNPACKED is just setting the length of the sending field, which is
why you're getting "....." (hex zeroes) for trailing characters.

By the way, a numeric field is unsigned if no decimal positions are
specified.  The numeric field is signed if decimals are specified (from 0 to
18).  So, in your example, both WS-KEY fields are unsigned.  Doesn't make a
difference in this example, but it's good to know.

Try this:

WS-ERR-MSG             W             40     A     value ' '
  WS-ERR1  WS-ERR-MSG        18  A
  WS-ERR2  WS-ERR-MSG +18   4  A

..giving you a redefinition of WS-ERR-MSG into two useable areas.

then....
WS-ERR1 = "Invalid selection,"
WS-ERR2 = WS-KEY-PACK

When you move the packed field WS-KEY-PACK to the alphanumeric WS-ERR2,
according to the book, "The resulting value of (the receiving field) is the
(unsigned) zoned decimal equivalent of (the sending field) with padding or
truncation on the left as necessary".

Cliff
