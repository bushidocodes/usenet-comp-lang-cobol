I am considering licensing AcuCOBOL-GT, mainly because of its multiplatform
support including DOS.

However, I am still quite undecided how to best cope with the issue of how to
drive the serial ports (and sometimes the parallel port) directly from within
my Cobol apps. As there is no native support for COM1, COM2 and LPTn from
within AcuCOBOL, I will have to use a comms library from a third party, such
as Greeleaf, etc.

I have a need to get 'down and dirty' with the UARTs for special printers,
modems, and other serial devices as used in apps for the retailing industry
and others. There are times when I need to read and write to LPT ports as
well for special devices.

AcuCORP marketing very kindly sent me an evaluation copy of an embryonic
library module developed by AcuCORP's Italian office, which pacakges serial
comms primitives into nicely worded calls, but the problems there are: (1) it
only runs on Windows and (2) it's unsupported.

So, I'm interested in hearing experiences and suggestions from anyone who uses
comms libraries to drive serial and/or parallel ports from their COBOL
apps, on both DOS and Windows, especially AcuCOBOL apps.

TIA.

Graeme

I have doing that sort of thing for years in assembler, and
just converted to the Marshalsoft subroutine library.  It contains
all the low level stuff in DLL's, and enabled me to do it all in
Cobol quite nicely.

The library is quite cheap (under $100). I am using it with
Fujitsu, but do not see that making a huge difference. Most
of the examples are not Cobol, but that was not a huge problem.
There was enough to figure it out.  If you do get them, and
want samples, yell out and I will post some stuff.

The company that I work for sells a serial communications program
called the Software Wedge that you may find to be a good tool for
adding serial communications capabilities to your application. The
Software Wedge is an executable program that can pass serial data back
and forth to other programs using either DDE (Dynamic Data Exchange)
or by converting incoming serial data to keystrokes (i.e. it stuffs
the keyboard buffer with the incoming serial data).
We also have a DOS version that works with any DOS program.
The program is extremely easy to use and is designed to have you up
and running sending and receiving serial data directly from within
your application in just a few minutes.
Please visit http://www.taltech.com for more information.
