Jeanette,

It is not a matter of OO *versus* Structured. Perhaps you mean OO versus
procedural. But in that case"even in a so-called "pure" OO language like
Smalltalk, one programs procedural sequences of messages in methods.

Why not taking the best of both worlds and learn COBOL conforming the next
standard, which includes full Object Orientation. If thats an option, you'll
get enormous opportunities in the near *and* far future, as COBOL has a well
installed base and OO will alse have the future ...

Personally I'm whole-heartedly devoted to COBOL *and* OO, but if I had to
choose between current (procedural) COBOL and Java, I would chose COBOL and
learn the OO part later, after the standard is out. Or pick up another
language, in which case Java feels a good choice.

The Frog
