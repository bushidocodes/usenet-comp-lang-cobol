Are you familiar at all with the work of Brian Henderson-Sellers - university
based Down Under ? His topic appears to be Components.

As regards Cope, I noted it claimed that you could dramatically reduce your
COBOL source. Have you used it effectively with COBOL ? My reaction was,
although in theory it is supposed to be possible, to view a business problem as
a totally mathematical model - it raises some doubts in my mind as to the
complexity it may cause. Inevitably, if you use an extra tool, it is going to
'push' your mind down an established path, just so your design fits the tool.
(In that respect, COBOL is so open ended, allowing you to travel many paths to
achieve the same solution).

I'll accept that discrete modules in a program can be preprocessed as they
claim, but a whole program or application - Oooohhhh ! (And as somebody here is
want to quote from time to time, "It's the artist, not the paintbrush". And I
don't consider that flip, it truly applies to a skilled COBOLer).

As an example - somebody quite proficient in OO COBOL, 'wrapped' his COBOL file
handling classes within the tool Rational Rose. For the life of me, can't
understand why he did that - it is dead easy just using OO and standard COBOL
syntax.

Jimmy, Calgary AB
