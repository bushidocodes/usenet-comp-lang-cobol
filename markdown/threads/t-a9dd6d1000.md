If you have FileAid from CompuWare, the ISPF portion can tell you probably
all you want to know. It will tell you the link-edit date and the compile
date of all the included programs. It will also tell you the compiler
used (assembler, PL/1, COBOL).

Also, I've heard that Serena has an even better product. 

If you are sysprog-less, you may not know of a really great web site for
downloading a LOT of MVS type programs. It is at http://www.cbttape.org .
Also, if you know mind a fairly active list, you might want to subscribe
to the IBM-MAIN list. This is sort-of like a newsgroup, except that you
get each article emailed to you, or you can get a daily digest. Or you
can just look at the archives at http://bama.ua.edu/archives/ibm-main.html
to see if you like it. If you do not subscribe (it's free!), then you can
read the archives, but you cannot ask questions. The archives are created
monthly. What I'd suggest that you do is go to 

http://bama.ua.edu/archives/ibm-main.html

Now click on "Join or leave the list". From there you can join the list and
set your options (such as digest mode to only get 1 email per day). When
you do join, an email will be sent to you and you must reply to it in order
to be subscribed. I don't know if you really want to join this list. It does
generate a lot of traffic. And sometime we "old-times" go off on weird 
tangents <grin>. But you get people from all over. There are people from
the various vendors, including IBM. Australians, Brits, French, Germans,
Israeli, South Americans (Brazil mainly as I recall), and others.

Just a thought. Hope it is of some use to you. Oh, this really is for
sysprog type questions. There are very few application type questions. 

John
