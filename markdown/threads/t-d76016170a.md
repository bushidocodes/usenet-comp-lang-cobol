A wise man, whose observations proved to be a gift to those around him, 
was walking through some wild country.  He met some hill-folk and the 
hill-folk, eager to demonstrate their hospitality, held a festival dinner 
where the wise man sat at the chief's right hand.

During the meal a beautiful widow approached and knelt, crying loudly 
'Honored Visitor, my son is gravely ill!  My husband died in battle and I 
have no man to care for me, give me money that I might buy him medicine!'

The wise man closed his eyes and said 'Either you have no son or he is not 
ill.'

All laughed, as they knew the widow and her trick.  The chief asked 'How 
did you know?' and the wise man said 'It is simple.  The people here are 
too noble to permit a wido to go begging; if she truly had an ill son she 
would have offered herself or her daughter to me for money.'

The chief was outraged.  'You have offended the honor of my people and for 
that you shall die!'

The wise man said 'Death comes to us all.  If you kill me you will deny 
yourselves and all others of a gift.'

Now, think long and hard: was this during the summer?

Pay attention to what is important!

Doug Miller wrote:
> 
> Makes no difference. Northern or southern hemisphere, summer is the time between the 
> summer solstice and the autumnal equinox, hence a time of decreasing daylight.

False pedantry. There is no single definition for "summer" (or for any 
of the season terms in English). Calendar-makers and the like may 
claim that summer begins on the solstice, but many meteorologists take 
the far more sensible position of letting it start on 1 June (northern 
hemisphere) or 1 December (southern), which is both a more convenient 
division of the year into seasons and a better correspondence with 
average temperatures.

Other people, who recognize that "official" seasons are somewhat less 
  useful than, say, "official" color names or "official" flavors of 
jam, declare that summer begins when they decide it's summer.

http://www.straightdope.com/columns/read/161/is-it-true-summer-in-ireland-starts-may-1
