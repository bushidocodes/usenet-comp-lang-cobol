```
This isn't a troll for jobs; that'll come when I figure out what to fish for
and what lure to use.

How do I convince recruiters and employers that lotsa years of batch cobol on
an obsolete platform can benefit them?

My former employer was in financial difficulties for a number of years before
finally biting the dust. We never moved into the Windows/Client Server arena.
We had our hands full just keeping our heads above water long enough to be
sold. To make matters worse, we were an NCR shop [RIP], so I don't have the
IBM buzzwords on my resume. And my windows knowledge comes from home
tinkering, we couldn't afford to upgrade all the workstations. Hell, we had to
get approval to buy a $129 modem.

Anybody here had experience in this type of platform switch?
Whats a true timeframe for achieving productivity in the new environment?

My experience with IBM mainframe sites vs. NCR has been that the NCR shops
require more diversity (and work) from their programmers. IBM shops paid a lot
more. In my area AS400 Cobol programmers with 2 years experience were earning
what I earned with 10 years experience. Of course I should have jumped ship
earlier, but the financial difficulties of the company actually made for a
really interesting work environment. If we didn't have it (we usually didn't)
we wrote it. We instituted an 18 month ROI requirement (po'd the users). For
the first time, the EDP department was viewed as an asset.

Bottom Line: In an age of specialization and buzzwords, how do I market
myself? How do I relate TOICAM to VSAM? CDT to CICS? VRX to MVS? Do they
relate?

I'd like to hear from anyone who has overcome this hurdle. I personally would
like to move into a UNIX/MF Cobol environment, I took a UNIX course towards
that end. Can I move my DOS/MF batch cobol skills to that environment? I
haven't used DIALOGUE, only the command line compiler, Forms and Animator.

Again, this is NOT a troll for job offers. I need help focusing my job search.
I also have to figure out how to convince people that a snow on the roof
programmer with 20 years in edp will accept an entry level position and stay.
Interviewers take one look at my white hair and get that look in their eyes.

Anyway; any advice, anecdotes, warnings, flames (except IBM vs ???) emailed or
otherwise are appreciated.

mikem
Michael R. McGuire -- mmc··.@ep··x.net
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
   No User Servicable Parts Inside
```
