I am unfamiliar with the Unisys 2200 and I thought that excess-three
was last seen, in hardware at least, in the Univac Three.  In any
case it's a system of encoding decimal digits in four-bit "nybbles"
in a way that's more convenient for computation.  The XS3 number is
formed simply by adding three to each digit.

	12345 (dec) + 33333 = 45678 (XS3)

This makes the number symmetrical within the range of the four-bit
field with three unused values at the bottom of the range and three
unused values at the top.  When two XS3 numbers are added, the digit
sums which produced carries are again natural decimal digits and
those that did not are six greater than natural decimal digits.
There are well known methods for correcting such sums.

Signed arithmetic, in either nines complement or tens complement is
also possible in the system.

I have no idea what packed-binary is.  Is it related to XS3?
