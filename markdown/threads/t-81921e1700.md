You may also effect the working storage by use of levels (based on version
of COBOL)

An "01" level forces COBOL to allign on a full-word boundary. Thus comes
the really sticky question of "SLACK BYTES".

An example:
01 WS-example1.
05 ws-ex1-data1 Pic X(1).
01 WS-example2.
05 ws-ex2-data1 pic x(2).
01 ........and so on

In this example the two 01 levels detailed take up 8 bytes, not 3 because
each "01" will allign on the next fullword. This will not be noted in the
compile however unless you look at the starting address of the fields.
