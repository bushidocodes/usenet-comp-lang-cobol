Binyamin Dissen opined:

<< I thought a V would be required for the implied decimal point.>>

The "V" characters in PICTURE VPPP9999 and in PICTURE 999PPPPV are
redundant.   And in a PICTURE with both "P" and "V" characters, these are
the only locations in which the "V" characters are meaningful (before "P"
characters that precede digits, or after "P" characters that follow digits).

From the last three standards:

ISO/IEC 1989:2002

13.16.38, PICTURE clause:

Syntax rule 15:  When the symbol 'V' and one or more symbols 'P' are used in
character-string-1, the symbol 'V' shall either immediately precede the
first symbol 'P' or immediately follow the last symbol 'P'.

General Rule 13:  P  The symbol 'P' specifies the location of an assumed
decimal point when that point is not within the number that appears in the
data item. ... [The] assumed decimal point is either to the left of the
string of 'P's if they indicate the leftmost digit positions ... or to the
right of the string of 'P's if they indicate the rightmost ...

General Rule 13:  V"  The symbol 'V' indicates the position of the assumed
decimal point for data alignment purposes. ...


ANSI X3.23-1985 (ISO 1989:2002)

5.9, THE PICTURE CLAUSE

General Rule 8:  ... The scaling position character 'P' can appear only as a
continuous string of 'P's in the leftmost or rightmost digit positions
within a PICTURE character-string; since the scaling position character 'P'
implies an assumed decimal point (to the left of 'P's if 'P's are leftmost
PICTURE symbols and to the right if 'P's are rightmost PICTURE symbols), the
assumed decimal point symbol 'V" is redundant as either the leftmost or
rightmost character within such a PICTURE description.  ...

ANSI X3.23-1974 (ISO 1989 -- 1978)

4.7, THE PICTURE CLAUSE, wording is identical to the '85 standard.

MOVE 25 TO N1

yields 00000025 internally and the right-most 6 digits are truncated. The
result, after truncation, is stored in the 2-digit field: "00"

On the other hand, had you said:

MOVE 25000000 TO N1

the result would have been "25" (with 6 implied zeros following).

As a check, what do you suppose would be stored in N1 after the following?

MOVE 12345678 TO N1

"12" or "78"?




If you have a COBOL compiler, almost everything will treated exactly the
same way - and yield identical results - on another  COBOL compiler.

If the results are not what you expect, look to your implementation first.
The notion that different compilers could/would generate different answers
is way, way down on the list of possibilities.

P.S.
In my experience the "P" scaling position symbol is extremely rare.
Originally conceived (I assume) to save space, I've only seen it used in a
production program once in 35 years.
