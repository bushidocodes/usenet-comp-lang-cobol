It is interesting to continue to read the stories of COBOL's imminent
death. PL/1 was supposed to kill it. 4GLs were supposed to kill it,
C is supposed to kill it. And so on. The first forecast of death was
back in 1959 when it was being put together. Two of the main
designers (Charlie Philips and Howard Bromberg) had a disagreement
over some point or other. One said doing it this way or that way
would kill COBOL. The other disagreed. Howard went to a cemetary and
purchased a tombstone with a little lamb on top. It is a little over
a foot high and a couple of feet long. He had COBOL enscribed on it,
and shipped it COD to Charlie at the Pentagon. The pentagon accepted
the charges, amazingly enough. Needless to say, COBOL still lives
(and will probably live for a long time) and the tombstone can be seen
today at the Boston Computer museum.

Howard wrote a great article about this a while back. If anyone
recalls where and when, I would appreciate knowing where I might find
it.
