I had never heard the story that started this, but here is
one that we studied as part of one of my psychology (not CS)
courses.

IBM was attempting to design a questionnaire that would discover
who would become a top-flight programmer with the appropriate
training. They spent a lot of money developing a test that tested
everything from IQ to blood pressure, and tested several hundred
candidates. They ten put all candidates through the programming
course, gave them jobs, and followed their careers for two years.

At the end of the two years, they asked their supervisors to rate
them as to programming qualifications, and attempted to co-relate
the evaluations with the tests given back at the start of the study.

There was almost no meaningful co-relations to be found, with the
exception of a single question. That particular question had an
extremely high co-relation --- in nearly every case the top programmers
had given the same answer.

The question was "Do you like programming computers?".
