A newsgroup with one moderator "controls" access to the
newsgroup and, therefore, may "effectively own ...".

However, a newsgroup with several moderators "controls"
access to the newsgroup; but cannot "effectively own ..." unless
they consort.

Moderators can be removed democratically or by fiat.

Democratically, this may be accomplished by a discussion and
Call For Vote (CFV) that is administered by usenet. Should a
different moderator be chosen, the previous moderator is out.

Should a moderator attempt to prevent a CFV or control the
discussion by refusing to post negative comments, usenet may
instruct ISPs to "unmoderate" the group. This allows the CFV
and discussions to take place without interference. Once the
vote is taken, the group can be "re-moderated" based upon the
vote. I do not know if the foregoing has ever been necessary; but
it is feasible.
