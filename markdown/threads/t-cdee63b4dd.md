```

Hey everybody,

I've got to admit that about a 2 years ago when I stumbled across this
newsgroup, I was naive enough to think this was a untapped source of
programmers. I was flamed (deservedly so) for posting job ads here.
Since that time, I've followed the postings, interjected some of my own
observations, and grown to enjoy the banter on this newsgroup.

Unfortunately, there's a whole new wave of newbies and robots posting
here who think they've discovered a gold mine. They fail to realize that
we are all well aware of the job newsgroups.

The "classified newsgroups" have turned into weed gardens (some would
say cess pools) of spam and I've taken upon myself the job of
'educating" these folk. I flamed a guy named Adam Koopermen from
Chicago who posted over 250 job listings on mi.jobs last night.

Bottom line. I'd like to police this group and "educate" those blatant
offenders...maybe publish a "blacklist" of jerks, I know where the're
comming from....What do you think?

Regards,
Steve the ex-recruiter - "Success is knowing the difference between
cornering people and getting them in your corner."
```
