Thanks for this Marty.

COBOL was in use before CRT screens came along and that was what I was
talking about. We didn't see 3270 screens in the 60s so if you are
correct, all I can say is that it wasn't "generally available".
We were running Tape operating system (TOS) because disk drives hadn't
been invented yet, or perhaps more correctly, they existed in
laboratories and were not "generally available"

Here's a piece I wrote about Disks in the 80s at around the time that
Pentium 3 was introduced:

http://www.thecorememory.com/Tales_from_the_Bit-Bucket.pdf

Before CICS we used IMS/DC or Shadow. Both of these were "good"
Teleprocessing monitors but there was an even better one, developed in
Germany, called "Taskmaster". Unfortunately, it had trouble competing
against IBM and eventually they went broke.

All of the above were actually better than CICS.

I would agree that CICS (originally "Customer Input Control
System"...not intended or designed to be a true teleprocessing monitor)
is OK now, but it certainly wasn't in the early days.

But the real reason behind my statement was that the whole paradigm of
expecting a centralized processor running COBOL to be BETTER for online
realtime transaction processing, than a distributed network with
multiple processing nodes and the ability to bring more processors
online as loads increase, just doesn't stack up.

The reason that CICS even gets the throughput that it does, is because
the processors it is running on today are exponentially more powerful
than they were in the 1960s.

I haven't used CICS on the Web as you say but I'll take your word that
it runs fine.

Unfortunately, as is so often the case, these things come down to cost.

For the price of owning and running a modern IBM mainframe, you could
buy dozens of blade servers, each with a CPU that is close in power to
your mainframe.

The comparative cost per transaction would be strongly in favour of the
Network, rather than the mainframe.

But none of this has anything to do with COBOL as a language.

And it doesn't mean that mainframes have no use. (even though there is
no arguing the declining use of them world wide.)

You CAN do most things in COBOL. I used to delight in writing PC COBOL
to do things that my Java oriented colleagues thought was not possible.
"Hey Pete, I didn't know you could do THAT in COBOL..."

But when it is all boiled down the OO paradigm has swept the world and
the procedural paradigm that COBOL was developed for is being replaced.

OO COBOL is excellent and we use it extensively as many of the PRIMA
tools were written in it originally. But even this code is being
replaced by C# and we are more productive with it than we ever were in
COBOL. (Partly because tools like Visual Studio are a quantum leap ahead
of SPUFI.)

I do explain the position I took in the interview much better on our web
site and I give the background and reasons why I believe that using the
Network is better for transaction processing.

https://primacomputing.co.nz/PRIMAMetro/ObjectsAndLayers.aspx

But, I will finish by agreeing that it is arguable and if you prefer
CICS, then I have no problem with that.

Pete.
