Now that brings back memories.

I took an APL course (1978) before I graduated high school (1980).  It was
the "coolest thing" out there at the time.  All the funky characters
probably doomed it as a general purpose language.  It was amazing how much
you could do in one line of code.

The professor at the University had custom-built a portable computer with a
built-in APL interpreter with a 3-line plasma display.  Naturally it was in
a hand-made aluminum case.  Got pretty warm, too.  I think it ran at 1Mhz or
a bit less.  This guy was way ahead of his time.
