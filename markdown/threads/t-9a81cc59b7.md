A textbooks I found the best and still use as references are
"Structured COBOL 74/85: Fundamentals and Style, 3rd ed." by Tyler
Welburn and Wilson Price; ISBN 0-07-069166-5 and "Advanced Structured
COBOL, Batch, On-line and Data-base Concepts" by Tyler Welburn; ISBN
0-87484-558-0. For the new student the two books gives a very strong
foundation in coding style in addition to concepts. The first job I
had after college gave a review in COBOL using the same books I used
in college!

Unfortunally, the books' weakness is in the area on programming for
interactive services, such as CICS and ISPF, focusing more on batch
programming (I had to learn these two extensions on my own; you may
consider providing a primer on the various extensions out there like
SQL, IMS, CICS, ISPF,etc.). But that is understandable giving the
books were originally written just before the PC and online revolution
really took off. The 3rd edition book does attempt to address it with
a chapter on "Screen Output and Keyboard Input" that describes what is
needed to make a PC COBOL program work.

Here's the the ads on the back of the books:

"Structured COBOL 74/85, Fundamentals and Sytle, 3rd edition" by Tyler
Welburn and Wilson Price.

Now available in a revised 1990 edition, this book retains and expands
on those features making it the industry leader.

1) COBOL-85 is taught parallel with COBOL-74 throughout the text.

2) This book may be purchased with an educational version of the
Ryan-McFarland RM COBOL-85 compiler (with student data disk) for those
who teach COBOL in a microcomputer environment.

3) Twenty-two new and twenty-two expanded programming assignments are
added to the many exercises in the previous edition.

4) Nine unique "concept modules" explain fundamental data processing
concepts in depth.

5) Sixteen complete programs emphasize program design documentation
with structure charts.

6) Emphasis on programming style conventions enables the beginning
programmer to code quickly.

7) A wide range of challenging programming assignments asks the
student to practice and apply programming concepts.

8) An extraordinary group of ancillary materials is available to
adopters of this text.

"Advanced Structured COBOL: Batch, On-line, and Database Concepts" by
Tyler Welburn.

This book is coordinated with Structured COBOL 3rd edition, to provide
comprehensive coverage of advanced topics including:

1) Complete file maintanance treatment and program models for
SEQUENTIAL, INDEXED, and RELATIVE files through coverage of
SEQUENTIAL, RANDOM, and DYNAMIC ACCESS retrievals.

2) Subprograms usage and linkage.

3) On-line and database processing applied to COBOL.

Hope this helps,
Boyce Williams
