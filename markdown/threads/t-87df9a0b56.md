A few things, Jimmy.
1. Most members of J4, and other standards committees, are NOT unpaid
volunteers.  They are mostly paid by their employers to do the work.
We now have 8 "institutional" members, and 3 independent comsultants.
2. ANSI/ISO has the "machinery" in place to distribute the standards,
and the money to stock them.  Some standards are done by IEEE/ISO, or
IEEE itself, hmmm, might be something to investigate.  The IEEE
Journal devoted a whole issue to COBOL, itself.
The only other "standards" group with the resources to be responsible
(that I know of) (in the United States) is the World Wide Web
Consortium, and my understanding is that membership there is
EXPENSIVE.
3.  If J4 has only 8 "institutional" members now; from where will the
support come?  Three of these people are nearly full time working for
J4.
4.  The technical committees are almost always sponsored by a single
national organization (with membership open to anyone). J4 is a
"Technical Committee".  But, unless they have international input (in
this case, WG4), they are working in a commercial vacuum.

How are these newsgroups set up? Maybe we could have a
"comp.lang.cobol.j4", but moderated, for worldwide discussion of j4
issues? Somebody, help!?

Stephen J Spiro
Member, J4 COBOL Standards Committee
