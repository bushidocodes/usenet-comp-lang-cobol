UCLA Extension Winter Programs 1999 

Y2K Cobol: Hands-On 
818.20 Management 2.40 CEU $525
(Includes course material.) 
This course examines the Cobol programming language from the perspective of the
Year 2000 (Y2K) computer problem. The goal of the course is to provide
sufficient knowledge to allow programmers to fix Y2K "bugs" in Cobol programs
to ensure Year 2000 compliance. Program managers also can benefit from this
course. Instruction emphasizes the parts of Cobol specific to Y2K; assignments
are Y2K- or date-related. Course material was developed around a score of Cobol
routines that perform Y2K-related tasks, arranged pedagogically, with lectures
on Cobol to explain the statements needed to write the programs. Topics include
Y2K remediation theory, pros and cons, when to use it, and the syntax of Cobol
statements. Students have the opportunity to test in-class solutions by
exchanging and running their programs. Prerequisite: Experience in computer
programming. Previous experience with Cobol is recommended. Enrollment prior to
the first class required. No refund after January 25.
