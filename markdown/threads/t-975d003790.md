    As far as C++ being less forgiving, I believe that to be true. The COBOL
compiler will flag potential error situations (not all) whereas I don't
believe C++ will flag as many. If C++ is anything like ASSEMBLER (which most

even knowing it.

    Having the knowledge and experience using C++ and COBOL will definitely
put food on the table in the years to come. COBOL is NOT going away as many
Pollyanna's would predict. IMHO, get some ASSEMBLER knowledge as well and
you can round out the categories.

I believe that all programmers should know the ASSEMBLER basics. It would
give them a greater appreciation for high-level languages as well as the
people who designed them (Grace Hopper to name one). Afterall, high-level
language design requires a very thorough knowledge of ASSEMBLER. Without
PMAP's (Mainframe speak), some pesky problems would not be as easy to find in
a dump without some ASSEMBLER knowledge. I'm sure you've probably seen a
single COMPUTE statement that generates dozens of PACK's, UNPKS's, ZAP's,
AP's, SRP's, etc. If you bomb on the third ZAP, it all that easier to find
the offending data-area.

Many programmers rely on third-party debugger's to find their abend. I can
tell you (from personal experience) that these debuggers are NOT found in
shops located in developing countries or 3rd-world nations. If you have a
problem, you must dig through the dump until you find the bad boy.

I'm not here advocating the resurrection of ASSEMBLER. I would like to see
more emphasis on COBOL in colleges and universities. I believe a little dose
of ASSEMBLER would serve students well.

Cheers,

WOB
