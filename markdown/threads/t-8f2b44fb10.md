If you want a "practical" book that has working examples. I would recommend
Elments of Object-Oriented COBOL by Wilson Prices. I have all of the current
books on the market and while some of them have good theory and some even
have code snips. Will's book takes you A-Z thru a sample problem (A Hotel
Registration Application) and explains it very well. It is written to work
with MicroFocus Personal COBOL but would work in the WorkBench or NetExpress
versions. It would probably work in most OO-COBOL compilers with a little
work. I attended a class that Will gave in early January and I have been
writing OO COBOL code since that time. I had a firm background in PC COBOL
and in OO before this time. It was an easy transition for me in large part
because of Will's book. You can order the book from Amazon.com I believe or
directly from Will. I would be happy to answer any questions via email that
anyone might have.
