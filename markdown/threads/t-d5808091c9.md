Erroneous bills were sent to Visa customers as reported here

http://www.cnn.com/2009/US/07/15/quadrillion.dollar.glitch/index.html.

The faulty amount was  $23,148,855,308,184,500.

When multiplied by 100 and converted to hex, it becomes 20 20 20 20 20
20 12 50. [almost reported that way on Slashdot.]

Of course hex 20 is an ASCII space not an EBCDIC space (40) but it is
plausible to see how this could have happened in COBOL, or in some
other language through a buffer over-run or an array access out of
bounds.

Ouch.

Customers were also charged over-limit fees. Eventually these charges
were rescinded by the bank.

-- e
