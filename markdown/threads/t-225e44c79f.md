The worse case I have seen was a job we bid on in Beaufort, SC where the
winning bid was $9.50/hr on a 6 month contract for a MicroFocus COBOL

COBOL experience, plus a BS in Computer Science! We can't event cover
our overhead on such a rate!

I guess the situation is, if you get outside of a good size town (pop.

will work for 'nothing' just to say in their current location. I guess
that is why companies often locate major manufacturing facilities in
rural areas -- cheap labor rates!

As far as going labor rates, for contract work in medium-sized towns,
the typical rate seems to be $35-$50/hr, DOE. In bigger cities,
especially in the N.E. and CA, the rate is often $45-$65/hr, DOE. In the
WDC area, $45-$80/hr, DOE, seems to be the going rate. In NYC, I see
rates typically between $55-$100/hr, DOE. HOWEVER, these are the going
rates THIS MONTH. As Y2K approaches, expect to see labor rates for COBOL
programs grow, at least, exponentially! (As I tell companies that are
procrastinating on starting their Y2K work -- and that seems to be the
majority of medium to small size companies -- its either pay us now, or
pay us MUCH more later!)
