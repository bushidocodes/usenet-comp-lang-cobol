I don't know if you got any e-mail replies, but I'll take a stab at
this. The relationship of CICS to COBOL is approximately that of
Windows 3.1 to C. CICS is an operating system extension that provides a
cooperative multi-tasking environment. The big difference is that CICS
supports hundreds or even thousands of concurrent terminal sessions
(generally character-mode only). In CICS, COBOL programs execute as
re-entrant subprograms of the CICS system, and obtain CICS services via
API calls.

I work in an MVS mainframe environment, and COBOL is the primary
programming language for both online and batch processing. We do a lot
of new development. The other two most commonly used languages are
assembler and EasyTrieve, but COBOL is by far the number one choice of
our mainframe geeks. I understand the PEE CEE WEE NEE's here are trying
to standardize on Visual Basic, but they've experimented with C and
Powerbuilder.
