Gary, go to The COBOL Center at http://www.infogoal.com/cbd/cbdhome.htm,
click on "COBOL Example Projects and Code", then download DATE.ZIP. It
contains a number of my COBOL date manipulation routines.

To convert from your Julian Day Number, take the equivalent Gregorian date
for your Julian Day #1 (or some date a fixed number of days after Day 1),
and use the 'Add Days to Date' routine to obtain the equivalent Gregorian
date.

To convert from Gregorian date to Julian Day Number, use the routine Date
Day Number which returns a number similar to your Julian Day Number. Then
simply add or subtract a fixed offset to obtain Julian Day Number. To find
the fixed offset, calculate both numbers on the same date and subtract.
