A lot depends on your approach to studying. Are you the sort of person
who is happier listening to an instructor in a class, or (like me), do
you pick stuff up by reading the theory and following through on the
examples.

If in the first category then you're stuck if COBOL isn't being taught
locally. Whether you do an online/correspondence course or read text
books amounts to the same exercise - you are self-pacing yourself.

For on-line - try objectz.com - I believe they offer some training.

For self-teaching texts :-

- Thane Hubbell's 'COBOL in 24 hours;' (that's before Thane makes his
sales pitch <G>)

- COBOL in 21 Days

- COBOL Unleashed

Whichever approach you take, having gone through the course or
material/texts - get your hands on other people's coding to get
examples. Go to Google.com and check sites using "COBOL". The name of
the game is 'steal' other peoples coding.

Good luck

Jimmy, Calgary AB
