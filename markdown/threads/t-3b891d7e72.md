```
Hi all.
Our team at work was recently assigned a project whereby we will be writing
system and data files to diskettes, mass-distributing the same to approximately
2000 agents, then they will upload the same files to their laptop hard
drives. Sounds pretty straight-forward, huh? Only trouble is that the original
system was written in WANG COBOL, WISP'd and compilied for Micro Focus
on the AIX/560 (RS6000) platform. The same source is then compiled a second
time under ACUCOBOL and sent to laptops wherein it is interpreted by an
ACUCOBOL runtime module. As I said, pretty straight-forward, huh???
Management's intent is to do away with the ACUCOBOL runtime module and
lay the same files on the hard drive as executables (dynamically-linked load
modules). Now, my question: one of our recently hired contract programmers
has had considerable previous experience in Micro Focus Workbench, 3.2 (I
believe). He seems to think that the programs can be linked to a DOS or
Windows format. Sorry, I failed to mention that the OS on the laptop machines
is WIN 95. He knows that this is done via issuing directive statements directly
to the segmented linkage editor. Three of us poured over Micro Focus manuals
(fascinating reading, that) for 2 hours attempting to find the correct directives
and the proper syntax for entering them. So far, to no avail. Would anyone
out there in Cybol land happen to know the proper directives to enter for the
OS/2 version of Workbench to link to either DOS or Windows? ANY help would
certainly be appreciated!!

TIA,
Keith Osterman
```
