The following is an actual correspondence between the project lead of
our y2k project and the corporate y2k inspectors of our parent
conglomerate/corporation:

Message #1 from corporate people:

"I recently came across a date test which Jane and I felt should be
incorporated into the sample list of test cases.

The date is 10/10/2000 which is the first 8-digit date in 2000.

This addition has been incorporated into the Year 2000 Compliancy
Testing Strategy framework and the Definition of Year 2000 compliancy,
both of which are attached."

Reply from y2k project lead:

"I really don't quite see your point. We do not keep January through
September as 1, 2, etc. we store them as 01, 02, etc. In addition, we
are storing most of our dates in a 6 digit format anyway. Is there
something I'm missing?"

Response from corporate people:

"Possible truncation. Programmers have been known to get quite creative
from time to time. For example, changing the data definition of a date
field from YYMMDD to YYYYMD."



I have 2 questions for the group:
1. Doesn't 01/01/2000 have 8 digits?
2. Does the corporate y2k person have any clue what they are talking
about?

Shawn.
