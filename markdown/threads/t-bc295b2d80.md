Hi Eric,

It's a bit difficult to know whether you need a 3 dimensional table
from the example you put on the NG.

Do you have 3 different data-items that occurs a number of times
together ?

Or do you really have 3 different plans. For each of the 3 plans you
have 4 different options and for each option you have 4 different
unions ?

If you need only 1 dimension you code the second part :

01 table-red redefines table-payroll.
05 table-entry occurs x times indexed by st-index.
10 plan pic 9(??).
10 option pic 9(??).
10 union pic 9(??).

If you need 3 dimensions you code the second part :
(ie. your table consists of unions only )
01 table-red redefines table-payroll.
05 plan occurs 3 times indexed by plan-index.
10 option occurs 4 times indexed by option-index.
15 union occurs 4 times index by union-index.
20 data-item pic 9(??).

On the other hand if what you need is a table with a number of
'records' with each containing : 3 plans, 4 options and 4 unions,
you should code the following :

01 table-red redefines table-payroll.
05 table-entry occurs x times indexed by st-index.
10 plan pic 9(??) occurs 3 times indexed by plan-ix.
10 option pic 9(??) occurs 4 times indexed by option-ix.
10 union pic 9(??) occurs 4 times indexed by union-ix.

Confused ?
