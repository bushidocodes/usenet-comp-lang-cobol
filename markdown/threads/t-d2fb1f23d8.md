Your best bet is to get a hold of the book 'Cobol for Dummies' by Arthur
Griffith. It covers the basics of cobol programming and includes a gem of a
CD containing dos/windows/unix compilers from Acucobol, Fujitsu and
Desaware. You can read reviews and/or purchase the book online at
http://www.amazon.com/exec/obidos/ISBN=0764502980/2603-4654092-889137

You can find out more about Fujitsu PowerCobol for Windows at
http://www.adtools.com/products/powercobol.htm

You can find out more about Acucobol-GT for Windows and download the
latest eval kit at http://www.acucobol.com/Services/Downloads/gteval.html

And last, but not least, check out the IDG website(publishers of Cobol
for Dummies) to read the table of contents, and sample exerpts from the book
at
http://www.dummies.com/cgi/fill_out_template.pl?book:0-7645-0298-0:book-Dumm
ies+Press::u905

Actually, the entire chapter 9 of the book is on this site
http://www.dummies.com/cgi/fill_out_template.pl?book:0-7645-0298-0:book.chap
ter::u905

You can read through it, print it out, try the examples and learn a
little bit of Cobol for free!
Go for it!

Regards,
