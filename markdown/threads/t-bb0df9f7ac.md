```
Bottom-line (in advance): Let's spend our time in this newsgroup talking
about COBOL, but those who don't want to are as free to do otherwise as we
are to ignore or respond to them.

As stated in another note stream, I would like to bring the newsgroup up to
speed on what I have found out (or think I understand) from the input of the
news.admin people about our recent not-so-friendly discussions.

This note may be a little long, so anyone who doesn't care should skip it
now.

The following are my understandings only; anyone who understands differently
is not just allowed but encouraged to correct me (via the newsgroup, private
email, or any other method that you think will be effective).

1) There is a charter for the comp.lang.cobol newsgroup. I have posted its
location in the FAQ (for future historical interest).

2) In general, charters for a newsgroup reflect an initial blueprint of what
is envisaged; they are not intended or expected to rule all future posts
within the newsgroup.

3) This newsgroup was created for discussing issues that relate to COBOL.
Although time may have changed the scope of the initial intent, most people
who read it today WANT to know about, ask about, comment about, or work with
COBOL. Therefore, the more COBOL-related your posts are, the wider your
audience and the more they are likely to be appreciated.

A) The only thing specifically prohibited in the charter is
cross-language flaming. However, when we have our COBOL vs C (or Java or
anything else) fights, no one can/should yell "FOUL - that's against the
charter - you're out of the newsgroup"

B) The original charter gave a list of "for examples" that didn't
include either asking homework questions or posting ANY job openings. I
think we all know now, that such posts are here to stay - and each of us
needs to process them as we think best.

4) If you want to express your own opinions about COBOL or *anything* else,
you can. As this is an UNmodereated newsgroup, unless you "break" the
newsgroup, you can't be stopped. You can, however, be ignored (via filters)
or responded to (privately or publicly) concerning your detrimental
behavior.

5) If someone claims in a post that their view and topics are the "official"
topics of the newsgroup and you can't (not shouldn't, but can't) post what
you want - those people are lying.

6) "SPAM" has an interesting definition in the news.admin world. It doesn't
matter how relevant or irrelevant your posts are - what matters is how often
and how many places you post it.

For example, when I send a "canned" reply to every newbie who asks is there
a free COBOL compiler - my reply can be considered "spam" - because I do it
too often. Similarly, if someone posts "Do your own homework" over and
over, this can be considered "spam" - not because people can or cannot ask
homework questions - but because this is the same post put out too
frequently.

I am still working on finding a way that we can get frequently asked
questions a "frequently given answer" without being "spam" but with letting
the rest of the newsgroup know that the answer has been given. (No solution
on this has been figured out yet.)

7) If your post is changing the topic of an existing thread, it would be
nice (but still not required),

A) If it is still about COBOL, you change the subject to a new COBOL
related one before putting out your new post.

B) If it is NOT related to COBOL, that you put out a new subject and
start it with "OT" to give those with filters against such a thing a chance
to avoid your comments.

As stated above, the bottom line is that most of us reading this newsgroup
expect the majority of the posts and the majority of replies to be
(relatively) polite and (relatively) on-topic, i.e. COBOL-related. However,
there are no rules to "enforce" this and what you don't like, you really
should ignore. Other methods are available for dealing with off-topic posts
and posters but the behavior must be "breaking the newsgroup" before
official sanctions will be appropriate to apply. (See the
news.admin.net-abuse newsgroups for hints and tips on what can/should be
done before that.)

I wanted to end with the section of the net-abuse FAQ that relates to our
current skirmishes, but the copyright on the FAQ says that I can only copy
it, if I copy the whole thing. Therefore, for those who are interested, I
would point you to:

http://www.cybernothing.org/faqs/net-abuse-faq.html

and look for the topic of:

"3.7) My group is full of crap. Why isn't it being cancelled?"
```
