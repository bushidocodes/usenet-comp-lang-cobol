We may be able to give you more meaningful interaction if you could state what
you mean by " it don't work like it should.".

In passing, it is easy to see that you have no error checking after the open.
This may not be the source of your difficulty. But you may wish to know that if
a system has trouble opening your file then it may never return EOF to the
READ. You could read infinitely, or the read may abend (crash, halt, stop or
otherwise seem misbehaved).

Recommend that you check the FILE STATUS after the open, and don't do the read,
AND don't do the close if the open fails.  

Also, IMHO, your read paragraph needs more than AT END / NOT AT END.  There
could be errors within 'NOT AT END' that you should interrogate with FILE
STATUS.

You do not reveal the definition of the variable end-of-file, nor any
initialization of same.  One can not assume there is a problem there, but
uninitialized control variable are certainly a classic source of program
weakness. I say weakness, because some days they can be okay, somedays they
malfunction.  If you do not initilize the varaible in question to a value other
than 1, the read paragraph might never execute, it will depend entirely on what
was left randomly in memory in that position by previous programs or data.


COBOL PERFORM is a test-first-then-execute construct. A good prescription for
such ailments is;
 
 1) define all control variables with explicit initial values (which usually
allow loops to spin, but sometimes prevent them from spinning unless enabled,
... but always explicit initialization in working storage).
 2) have a major program initialization routine that inits your control
variables at the begining of your program (which might be executed once in your
program),
 
 AND/OR

 3) get control variables under the control of major page-break procedures,
(which might be executed in outter loops repeated).

Stated otherwise - every component referenced in an UNTIL clause necessitates
atleast one initialization.
Leave nothing to chance.

Your posted code shows a comparison of " end-of-file = 1 ", a numeric test. If
the field is numeric that might work just fine.  If the fields is PIC X, you
could get into some difficulties in conversion of the MOVE 1 and/or UNTIL ... =
1.  

Frequently, control variables are made alphabetic to avoid this (but that is
discretionary). Furthermore if you do not define the control variable as 
alphabetic, then the PERFORM's UNTIL clause can crash on an uninitialized
numeric field. 

As you will see in many other posts, some programmers prefer 88 levels for
conditional control of iterations. (Some do not). Use of 88s under a
conventional definition of a FILE STATUS variable may be just fine for your
needs.

Recommend that you look up FILE STATUS.  Make certain you initialize it.
