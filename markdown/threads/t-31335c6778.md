If you are currently working, see if you're employer has positions for 
junior programmers.  Many companies try to hire "from within" when they can
and may have oppurtunities for people with little or no experience.

This is how I started in the "real world", about 2 years ago.  I had worked
for my employer (a large grocery store chain) for about 4 years when they
sent out a bulletin expressing interest in hiring from within about 10 new
programmers.

My company employs 27,000 people and it only makes sense that out of that
many people, there is bound to be 10 people who can program.  Companies
sometimes benefit from doing this kind of thing because they hire people who
have a better feel for how the company does business.

Getting a programming job from "off the street" with nothing on your resume
will be difficult.  You may have to start off somewhat lower on the
corporate rung.  Maybe a 'help desk' (or as we call it, the call
re-direction center) associate or a customer service rep or something like
that.  If the employer is worth anything, they will see your talents and put
you somewhere that you can do more good.

Good luck,
Jeff
(OS/390, COBOLII, IBM 4680/4690 O/S Point Of Sale system and SAV2
Supermarket Application)
