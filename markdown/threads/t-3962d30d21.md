wjl_xyz wrote:
> How do I get a posting removed?

As Allodoxaphobia astutely pointed out, once your post has been propagated 
by the news server, you have very little chance of removing it from the 
cyber space. Even if you get it off the Google server, it will still remain 
on hundreds, possibly thousands, of other servers...

Theoretically it would be possible to trace the server logs and remove 
something from everywhere it was posted but I know of no software that does 
that (probably in the CIA domain :-)), and I'm almost certain that Google 
would accept no responsibility for servers other than their own. (Why should 
they? It was YOU who posted...:-))

The feature of message propagation is what makes these forums so useful.

Efforts have been directed over years to getting messages propagated, not to 
recalling them.

Sometimes it is spooky... I have found things I posted in all innocence 
years ago, popping up on web sites I never heard of and certainly never 
posted to. Investigation showed they had picked up stuff I wrote from a 
Usenet feed (once its on Usenet it is, in reality, in the Public Domain, and 
it's no good bleating about your intellectual property and the fact that 
what you said was not intended for circulation...)

BOTTOM LINE:

If you post to Usenet, whatever you post will be propagated into cyberspace 
within a few hours. Your only chance is to remove it IMMEDIATELY  before it 
has a chance to propagate, and, of course, you can only do that if the 
server permits it (as Google apparently does...)

Given that what you say is out there for ever, it is probably wise to think 
BEFORE posting :-)

I can't say I have always followed this rule because sometimes emotion can 
get the better of you, but nowadays I try to postpone clicking SEND if I 
know I am upset. Sleep on it... THEN send it :-)

Pete.
