[...]
>The ATC system runs some (maybe most?) of it's non time critical
>software on COBOL based systems,  but the embedded systems are
>not COBOL; they are a mixture of various HOL and assembler.

What other HOLs? At the time the system was built, COBOL
was the only computer language approved for use by the
US government.

It seems you may be misinterpreting time critical as meaning
fast. In real-time programming, that is not the case. Different
parts of the system have different time requirements. One
routine may need to execute in about 5 ms; but the requirement
may be soft. Another routine may need to execute in 150 ms;
but the requirement may be hard. A COBOL routine might
meet the hard requirement of 150 ms by executing consistently
within 120 ms. An assembler routine might meet the soft 5ms
requirement by averaging less than 5 ms; but occasionally
using 5.5 ms. The routine with the hard real-time requirement
is the time critical routine despite its longer execution time.

I am not sure what you mean by "the embedded systems are
not COBOL." There is a mainframe computer connected to
radar, radio, displays, keyboards, etc. That computer is
dedicated to the task of controlling air traffic. It runs 24 hours
per day, 7 days per week, doing one thing. That computer
is embedded and the software running on that computer is
embedded even if it is written in COBOL.

Rick Smith

displays and such are not the IBM hunkers; they are embedded
