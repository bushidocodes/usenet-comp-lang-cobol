conducting his recruiting campaign. 'comp.lang.cobol'  is not
'business.technical.recruiting.'  There no doubt are some participants in
'comp.lang.cobol' who happen to know about such things, but I sure wouldn't
look for advice on preparing a gourmet chicken dish in 'alt.hardware.'

Offering unsolicited advice like this seems to me an exact parallel to a
COBOL programmer having a need/desire to learn something about the
recruiting business posting a request in 'business.technical.recruiting,'
only to receive a reply which says, "use PERFORM,  not PERFORM THROUGH".
That is, the rudeness of unsolicited advice works both ways.

Whose business is it but the poster's if he is trolling for resumes or
trying to come up with current market rates?  These are perfectly valid and
accepted techniques for those in the recruiting business.

Let's take this a step further: Suppose a 'real user' is in 'real need' of a
'real COBOL consultant' to solve some 'real problem'  and decides to 'go
direct' by posting a request for help in a 'promising-sounding' newsgroup..
e.g., 'comp.lang.cobol.'  Anyone who publically denigrates the way Mr. Real
User asks his question (and since Mr. User is a widget manufacturer, not a
COBOL programmer or a recruiter, he may well forget 'something' of
importance in his request)  does a disservice to us all: he makes us all
look unprofessional.

If the tone, technique, spelling, grammar or syntax of a solicitation does
not appeal to you, you can always exercise your ultimate right: ignore it.

Or, you can lobby your Congressman for the establishment of no-shit for-real
Newsgroup Police. But please "include me out" of that venture: I like
newsgroups just the way they are, thank you.
