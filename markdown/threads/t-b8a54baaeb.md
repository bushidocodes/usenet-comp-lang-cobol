The perception of COBOL in many North American companies is that it is
obsolete and will be replaced.  In addition, the long time that it has
taken to produce a new standard has alienated many of us practitioners.
My firm belief is that the whole process must change.  If I understand
correctly, for an internet standard to become effective, two separate
implementations must be produced and shown to interoperate.  Thus the
most important criteria of any further changes must be that they are
developed AND TESTED in a timely (1-2 year timeframe).  ISO and ANSI
should study the IETF and modify current processes so that we see tested
and testable code.  The various open source COBOL projects may be a
valuable source of effort in this regard.
