```
One of the longtime members of Reborn has written to ask about my
health.  Rather than answer just this one person, I thought it might be
helpful to let the entire group in on what is going on.

It has now been about two months since I started my medication.  As I
have written previously, there are three choices concerning the
medication available for the type of MS that I have been diagnosed
with.  I chose to take COPAXONE which involves a daily, relatively
painless  injection with minimal side effects.  The medication is not
designed to make one better - but to prevent the disease from
progressing.  So, even if there are further exacerbation from the MS,
once can only guess that they come less frequently or with less
severity.  There are times that I feel like skipping the shot - even
after two months it feels like a drag!

At this point I am doing much better than when I was earlier this school
year (i.e. mid October).  At that point both legs were numb from my feet
to my knees and my waist also had significant numbness.  Most of the
numbness is gone - at this point my feet feel very raw, as if someone
has scraped them with a large wood file.  Most of this improvement had
occurred before I began medication.  

I continue my daily job.  Also, since being diagnosed last December I
have made significant trips to Illinois to attend the Urbana Missionary
Convention and Charlotte, NC, to attend a Computer Science Education
conference.  I have had no further attacks since the major problems last
fall.  Probably the biggest lost is the ability to drive.  I will try
again once this snow is gone and I am convinced that I can do so safely.

On a more important note, my spiritual life continues to grow.  Urbana
had a major spiritual impact on my (and my wife's!) life.  In fact,
Sandra, who has long struggled with baptism (she was baptized at 12
years of age, before she understood what it was to be a Christian), has
decided to be baptized next summer when our pastor will have a baptismal
service.  

I do appreciate your prayers - both past, present, and future.  God has
been good.  I hope that sort of brings you up to date.  Feel free to
ask  for more if there are questions.  I expect for some, I have given
too much information; though others will want more details.

Yours because His,

Floyd Johnson
```
