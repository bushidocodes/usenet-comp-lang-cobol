I am interested in putting together a proposal for the implementation of a
COBOL compiler for UNIX / DOS / MS Windows and/or OS/2, and possibly other
systems. I need information that would show that it would be worth it to
my company to spend the time and money developing something like this.

I am interested in comments from people who either would purchase or
influence purchase of such a program, or who would use this if it was
available. Specifically I am interested in what is the most important
issues to you as far as what would make you buy or ask your employer to
buy it.

What features are most critical, what would you want in the package, what
is it you find missing in other compilers? What kind of price range you
think is appropriate. Even if you think it can't be sold for that price
(like a rate for students), write anyway, it will give me some idea of
what price range people think a program like this should sell for.

What I am interested in are the issues that are most important to you,
if you use the language:

1. Target generated language: Is only a "pure compiler" acceptable, or
would one that translates COBOL into something else like Assembler,
C, FORTRAN, Pascal, Mumps or (Visual) Basic/CA-Realizer source code,
be acceptable? Or would you _rather_ see something that does that
either instead of machine code only or as an additional compilation
option?

2. Is only an executable image creator acceptable, or one that generates
a module that uses a run-time system which runs the executable image
be acceptable?

3. Target machine and environment: What is it you would want a COBOL
compiler for? Unix Boxes (which?), Windows, OS/2, DOS? Other? More?

4. If you are considering moving legacy applications from older
mainframe computers:

4a. What environments are most important that the compiler be able
to accept code written for those machines without rewriting?
Honeywell, Burroughs, Univac, CDC, DEC? Which O/S and environment?
IBM DOS/VSE? OS/VS1? Some other IBM?
Other? What O/S and environment?

4b. What application environments are critical to be emulated
or give equivalent performance by the compiler or environment?
CICS? IMS? OS/VS JCL?

4c. What database management and/or file systems are critical
to be supported or emulated by the compiler or environment?
DB2? ISAM? VSAM? Other?

5. Is running the same application in multiple environments (DOS, Windows,
OS/2, Unix Boxes, Mainframes) important? Which ones?

6. What do you think is a price that a compiler that does what you need
is something you would be willing to pay? How many copies would you
think you would purchase? If you think that there would be more
interest in a site license, what would be the approximate amount of
use and how much?

7. Are you using a COBOL compiler for PC or Windows or UNIX? Which one?
Do you like it? What is good about it? Bad? What did it cost?
Would you buy it again? Would you buy something else also if it did
more of what you needed?

8. Tell me what you wish you had in the way of tools to make creating/
writing/developing/designing applications in COBOL? What do you find
the most frustrating problem that you wish could be automated or
made easier?

9. Are there other questions that you wish I had asked, and if so, what
is the answer to them?

These answers will be confidential, once the answers to the questions are
collected all identifying information will be destroyed. The information
is simply to show me if there is interest in this sort of thing. Please
feel free to pass this questionaire on to others who might be interested.

E-Mail me, Paul Robinson

General Manager

"An Employee Owned Company"

Or write to me at:

Paul,

I would suggest caution - I believe I am one of a very few people who have
actually done this and understand *exactly* what is involved. The COBOL
language is probably one of the most complex languages on the market - this
is why there is relatively so few compilers for the language. Working on a
COBOL compiler makes Pascal, C, C++, et al seem like a vacation. If you are
serious and think you can afford the manpower (you will need at least
12 man years of effort with access to some *real* experts) then I am willing
to have a chat about it. Otherwise I would look for something else to do.
When I was working on this two years ago we used the latest compiler tools
and the job was still massive.

Regards,

Mike
