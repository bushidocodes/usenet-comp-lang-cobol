```
We have just passed the 100th download of the  free COBDATA Tool on 
http://primacomputing.co.nz/cobol21

I remember when it was first discussed here people saying things like:

1. My compiler listing shows me offsets and lengths... why would I need a 
separate tool?
2. I can easily write a script to call my compiler and get this information.

And yet, over 100 people have found it useful.

I use it "under the covers" every day, and specifically from the desktop 
quite frequently. Being able to simply select with the mouse the areas of a 
record layout I am interested in, then get what I need with one or two mouse 
clicks is really convenient, and I don't have to interrupt the flow of what 
I am doing in order to run a compilation.

People have found some uses for it which it was certainly not designed for 
and they are nothing short of ingenious. At least one database interface 
uses it as a "COBOL data dictionary" for translating between a COBOL record 
buffer and the Tables on a RDB.

I have a fair bit on my plate at the moment, but the next free component 
will probably be DECLGEN for Networked COBOL.There is a full DECLGEN 
facility included in the Migration Toolset but, several people have asked if 
they can have a standalone one without having to buy the Toolset.

Many years ago, in an early paper on using RDB with COBOL I mentioned I had 
written such a facility and would give it to people for free, if they wrote 
and asked me for it. Unfortunately, I can't do this at the moment because it 
is integrated into the Toolset and there is work involved in decoupling and 
rewrapping it so it can be a standalone tool. DECLGEN (Declaration 
Generator) analyses tables on a relational DB and generates either Host 
Variables or a COBOL record layout for them, so they can be easily 
interfaced to from COBOL.

Watch this space... (but don't hold your breath... :-))

Pete.
```
