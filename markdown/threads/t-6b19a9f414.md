I had a go at this and gave up by the third screen...

The questions are stupid and pointless. Expecting people to quantify in 
hours how long it takes to learn something complex, where learning is an 
ongoing experience, and might extend over months or years is simply 
unrealistic. (But the page accepts decimal points so let's get it right... 
:-))

Non-quantified questions of the type: "How hard was this, on a scale of 1 to 
5" are more "doable" but the quality of the data obtained is totally 
subjective and not much use for serious research.

I would equate this survey with the kind of thing you might find in 
Cosmopolitan, to see if you are sexually compatible with your camel...

I don't do those (I don't need a survey to tell me my camel loves me...), so 
I won't be doing this.

It might be important and urgent to someone, but I would strongly suggest a 
little less urgency and some more serious thought in formulating  the 
questions.

Overall, I thought the whole tenor of the survey reflected naivete, and I 
would expect more from an institution of learning.

Pete.
