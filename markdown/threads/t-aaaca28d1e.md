Rhapsody is a new version of OpenStep ported to the PowerPC and Intel
x86 processors/clones. Openstep was developed as a replacement for
NextStep to make the software portable to Windows NT platforms, and
the Windows NT component is now called Yellow Box for NT.

Rhapsody runs on a Mach kernel and will the Unix BSD 4.4 operating
system.

PowerPC.

The reduction in sales for the Mac platform has been due primarily to
corporations and government agencies wanting to standardize on a single
platform to reduce costs. Sort of like the VHS/Beta VCR competition.

If COBOL is available for AIX, it may be easy to port it to Rhapsody
for PowerPC.
