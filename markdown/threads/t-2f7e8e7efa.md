Paul:

You're not only going to need a compiler, you'll also need to learn
the COBOL language and get a bit of experience if you want to get top
dollar doing Y2K work. Anyway, here are some compilers:

Micro Focus has a couple of cheap student compilers. One for Dos and
one for Windows. The Windows version is actually a 16 bit compiler
but it will run in 16 or 32 bit Windows environments. They're called
"Personal COBOL" These compilers are for educational purposes which
means that you cannot create a stand alone executable.
http://www.mfltd.co.uk

Fujitsu has a free compiler for Windows. I think that they have both
16 and 32 bit versions.
http://www.adtools.com

You can also buy a book called COBOL for Dummies by Arthur Griffith.
The ISBN number is : 0-7645-0298-0
It contains the Fujitsu compiler as well as demo versions of the
Acucobol and Micro Focus compilers. Contact IDG Books at:

http://www.dummies.com/

and search for the word COBOL.

You can get a compiler in a book called, "The Revolutionary Guide to
COBOL." I believe that it's a DOS compiler.
http://www.wrox.com.

You can also get a copy of the Realia COBOL compiler in a book called,
"COBOL: from Micro to Mainframe" by Robert T. Grauer & Carol Vazquez.
The ISBN number is 0-13-140179-3 and it's available from Prentice
Hall. http://www.prenhall.com/divisions/ecs/cscat.html The
toll-free number in the United States is 1-800-223-1360.

As a "public service" we keep a couple of copies of the shareware
COBOL compiler (COB650 and COBOL650) on our ftp site. Please keep in
mind that we just keep it there for downloads. We didn't write it.
We don't support it.
http://www.flexus.com

The book, entitled, Modern COBOL Programming by Wilson Price & Jack
Olson includes a copy of the Ryan McFarland compiler. I suspect that
the compiler is limited in some fashion. The Publisher is Mitchell
McGraw-Hill and the ISBN number is 0-07-837527-4.

Acucobol suggests that you download their eval version which
eventually times out. http//www.acucobol.com. I also believe that
Acucobol has provided a limited version of their compiler with the
Teach Yourself COBOL in 21 Days book. Acucobol operates a special
program for bona-fide educational sites. Contact your local Acucobol
office for details. In the USA call 1-800-COBOL-85, or (619)-689-7220

If I forgot anyone, I apologize and encourage you to "enlighten" me
via e-mail.
