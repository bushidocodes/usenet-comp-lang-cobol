I have just completed an exercise assisting a Software House to convert
their very successful package from ISAM to Relational Database.

Tools were developed to assist in this conversion. Procedures were developed
and much experience was gained. The conversion went very successfully in far
less time than any of us could have hoped for.
(60 ISAM files were converted to a 3NF RDB with appropriate indexes,
referential constraints, and the removal of repeating groups and key
dependencies) and the newly created tables were loaded with existing live
data within 2.5 elapsed days (using 3 people and tools for both the
conversion and the loading which I wrote especially for this job). This has
allowed the existing code to be carefully examined and converted according
to a successful strategy we devised.

Many lessons were learned during this exercise which took 7 working days in
all.

I am happy to make both the tools and the strategy available to anyone
considering such an exercise. If you want to try it for credibility, send me
the source COBOL SELECT and the FD for up to three of your ISAM files (no
data required) and I'll return within 24 hours an Access Database with the
appropriate matching structure. OCCURS and REDEFINES in the COBOL source are
handled, along with COBOL data structure (levels), none of which is directly
"translatable" to RDB.

This offer is not indefinite and may be curtailed if there is heavy demand.

While I would not pretend that this is an ideal way to develop Relational
Databases, it is a VERY good way to convert existing Indexed structures.

Please draw on our experience if you are considering this kind of
conversion.

The process applies across PC and Mainframe platforms.

Pete.
