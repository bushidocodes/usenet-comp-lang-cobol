Last weekend I bought the "COBOL Programming Starter Kit" published by
Macmillan, at CompUSA for about $40. I haven't gotten around to
installing it yet, but the small manual that comes with it clearly
explains that the compiler is ACUCOBOL, and the entire contents of
"Teach Yourself COBOL in 21 days" is included on the CD-Rom, with an
Adobe Acrobat reader. I haven't used Adobe before, so you may know
better than I would how convenient that would be.

This is the first time I've ever seen a shrink-wrapped COBOL compiler in
a PC Computer store at ANY price. I don't have any previous experience
with ACUCOBOL, but it appears to compile to p-code, and then requires a
runtime interpreter (one for DOS and one for Windows). The starter kit
manual says it contains a "complete and fully functional version of
ACUCOBOL-85". It also says to contact ACUCOBOL for runtime licenses.

If you have problems with the starter kit, you're supposed to call
MacMillan. They will post updates and bug fixes at their website:

http://www.macmillansoftware.com

I hope that helps.
