These guys really do offer great benefits. Over the past couple of years
they have given multiple substantial across the board raises to their IS
staff just to prevent leakage. They are known in this area for one of the
best benefits packages around. They even do the weird things, like buying
several parking ramps in downtown St. Paul so their employees could be
guaranteed secured indoor parking. I have known a number of people there
who absolutely love the place.

stuff, SAS, and Syncsort routines for various systems. My guess is that
they are bringing in the shorter experience people so that they can give
the better development assignments to their senior people (based on the
questions floating at their job fair). This is not really a bad idea, of
course, as it shows some respect for the people who have done their time.

Another interesting sign is that they tend to use the more reputable
head-hunters in the area first when they have a new job opening (for what
it's worth...)
