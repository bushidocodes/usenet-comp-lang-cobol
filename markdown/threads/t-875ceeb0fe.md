There *WAS* such a document (or it may have actually been two documents).  I 
can't remember the "colors" of the various versions of the draft '85 Standard  - 
but between the various (major) drafts of the '85 Standard, the changes were 
summarized in the "cover letter" sent out to those who commented on the various 
documents.  (I suspect that I have a copy of the "pink" version of the draft and 
its cover letter somewhere, but don't ask me where.)

Certainly the MAJOR difference was the entire introduction of the "Obsolete" 
category (and the items put into it).  "Removal" of such items and their 
eventual retention is what (according to most authorities) delayed the '85 
Standard. The last significant (???) change that I remember was adding "ALSO" to 
EVALUATE (with multiple selection subjects/objects).

The "paper" archives of J4 were just recently "donated" to University of 
Minnesota (I think) but you might be able to contact J4 and ask them if someone 
has those cover letters.  I *doubt* that they were produced electronically (or 
that anyone would have the original electronic text) but they *might* have been 
scanned by someone.
