As we enter the home straight for Xmas/New Year, I'd just like to take this 
opportunity of wishng everyone here a great festive season and a fruitful 
and happy New Year.

The local paper here asked me for a small piece to run on January 1st so I 
thought I'd share my effort with you. Consider it another worthless stocking 
filler :-)

NEW YEAR 2011
================
January.

Named after the Roman God, Janus; God of doors and new beginnings.

Janus is depicted as having two faces, one coming, one going.

I'm a bit like that with New Year Resolutions. Part of me knows at the time 
I make them that there is a very good chance I won't get to keep them, but 
the pragmatic side of my nature says: "Hey, if you even manage to do this 
for a few weeks, it will be a Good Thing."

So, I avoid the pastry counter, bite my tongue when there is an acerbic 
remark on it, suffer fools gladly or leave the room, am kind and sweet to 
relatives who make me want to scream,  politely decline when telemarketing 
people phone me at dinner time, and amass hundreds (maybe even thousands.) 
of Karmic Brownie points. for a few weeks.

And it IS a Good Thing.

For everyone except me.

My stress levels increase, I have unaccountable sweats, I start grinding my 
teeth in my fitful sleep, and my normally cheerful disposition becomes one 
of suppressed anger and tension and the certain knowledge that Doom is 
impending and there will be tears before bedtime.

I'm just not designed to be all things to all people.

Most of us are not.

It violates the natural condition for us to be what we are not in the 
interests of turning over a new leaf because it is a New Year.  Why should 
some arbitrary calendar date trigger a guilt-based rash of self flagellation 
and resolving to be better? Why don't we resolve to be better all through 
the year, whenever we realise there is some room for improvement?

When the tiny voice of conscience says: "Well, you could have done that 
better. I mean. telling her you don't want to see her any more because every 
time she opens her mouth it is to torture and kill the English Language, and 
you can't have a relationship with someone whose only adjective is "awesome" 
or something still considered obscene in the eyes of the Law, with a 
superlative being both of her two adjectives used together. " .

Perhaps THAT is the time to resolve to be gentler, rather than leaving it 
'til New Year.

Anyway, I wish you all luck with your New Year Resolutions.

This year I have resolved not to make any. (Except this one, of course.)

I think it is an achievable goal. It will be nice to succeed with 
Resolutions for a change.

Now excuse me while I give these �clairs the attention they deserve.

=====================================================================================

Sincere Best Wishes for the Holidays to all in CLC and let's hope 2011 is 
great for everyone.

Pete.
