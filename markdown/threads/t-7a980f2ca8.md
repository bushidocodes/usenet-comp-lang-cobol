Carol, we teach a variety of COBOL courses that might meet your needs (see
below). We would be happy to teach them in DC if you want to work with us
in setting them up.

By the way, COBOL/370 is the name of Release 1 of the newest IBM COBOL
compiler; Release 2 is called "COBOL for MVS & VM".

Related courses we offer:

Converting to COBOL/370 - 2 days;
for people going from OS/VS COBOL to COBOL/370

COBOL for MVS & VM, COBOL II differences - 1 day
for people going from COBOL II to COBOL for MVS & VM

Structured COBOL Workshop - 5 days
for people new to COBOL (but with background in some programming
language) or a refresh for people who have been away from COBOL
for
a while; supports COBOL II, COBOL/370, COBOL for MVS & VM

[by the way, we are offering this in Portland, OR 4/28 and we
have room for a few more students]

Advanced Topics in COBOL - 3 days
as its name suggests; supports COBOL II, COBOL/370, COBOL for
MVS & VM

COBOL II and COBOL/370 Debugging and Mainentance - 2 days
despite its title this course supports COBOL II, COBOL/370, and
COBOL for MVS & VM; deugging and MVS dump reading as well
as external subroutine linking and binding

VSAM For COBOL Programmers - 3 days
VSAM concepts and utilities as well as how to access VSAM files

from COBOL; supports COBOL II, COBOL/370, COBOL for
MVS & VM

And more: DB2, CICS, IMS, Language Environment (the run-time environment
for COBOL/370 and COBOL for MVS & VM), etc.

If you host a class and allow outside students you can get free seats,
depending on the size of the class.
