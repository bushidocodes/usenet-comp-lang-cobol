Actually, the best "articles" on this are the IBM-supplied Migration guides. 
They are available on BookManager (check the systems programmer for the site to
find out how to access - and then look for bookshelf CEE1BK02, and manual
IGYMG102 - Compiler and Run-Time Migration Guide).  These are also available
from the IBM Web Support Site
www.ibm.com/software/s390/le/ and select the library button on the left.  There
may also be a downloadable PDF.
  I suggest these, rather than something written for Entrprise Journal or such,
because they contains almost everything the IBM Labs knows about doing the
conversion, and include some issues you might not have considered, such as the
different ways the programs will fail in the new environment (you may not get
an abend when you expect to!).
Be aware that the major difference is that you are moving from a MVS/ESA
environment to a Language Environment under OS/390 (I presume).  This has large
implications to link-edit and runtime language support.
The differences between COBOL/370 and COBOL II are less significant, from a
source code standpoint, since both are ANSI 85 implementations.  The major
differences are the way the operating system supports the language.
If you have a lot of OS/VS COBOL to convert, you may want to investigate an
IBM-supplied conversion tool to do that.  It is called CCCA (COBOL and
Command-level CICS Conversion Aid).  It automates much of the tedious work of
replacing reserved words (all EXHIBITs become DISPLAYs, and so on), and it does
a pretty good job of converting BLL Cells, should you have any CICS programs to
do.  I've used it on a couple of conversion projects, and it paid for itself
very quickly.  Its nicest feature, aside from being very customizable (you can
build a conversion for any text string, if you are in an acquistion or merged
environment, for example), is that it explains very clearly when if finds a
conversion it can not do!
You can find the manual for it in the COBOL language bookshelf (sorry, I don't
have the name handy).
.  Also, be aware that Visual Age Services will, for a fee, send an expert to
advise, or even a team to do it, if requested.
Good luck, and send me an e-mail if you have specific questions these books
don't appear to answer
