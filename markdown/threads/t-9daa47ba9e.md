I am currently Vice President of the Research and Development Department

So, here's my question................

If you were in the position where you needed some work to be done, would

data, to a programmer who would code and test your program using

this programmer a previously agreed on hourly rate.

Do you think there is any interest in that sort of thing in the
programming world??

I've the same experience set as you do. My choice however, is CA-Realia
COBOL. I do some of the things you say. It's not a lot of work, but there

some who are friends, some who have heard of me through word of mouth. I
have written things like a system to handle archery shoots, and another to
convert on CNC programs to a Pega 2000 laser punch. My rates started out
very cheap, and I still keep them low. I make an agreement up front that I
owne the system, and have the rights to re-sell it, but I still charge an
hourly rate. I have sold one system with full rights to the buyer, but
with the understanding that I get a commission off of everything he sells.
I do fast quality work. I give an estimate up front, with the explanation
that if I come in under that time, they pay the lowest price, and if I am
going to go over, I talk to them first. No suprises. I work mostly with
very small businesses, that can't afford, but do want, some quality
applications. I was developing for DOS only, but now am moving existing
applications to windows and writing for windows, using CA-Realia COBOL and
the very good product from Flexus, SP2.

So, the answer is yes, there is a demand. But you have to search it out.
