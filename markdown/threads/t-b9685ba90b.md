```
Assuming that you mean "OS/VS COBOL" when you say "COBOL I" and assuming
that you mean "VS COBOL II" when you say "COBOL II" (there are some other
possibilities, but this is the usual mis-nomer), the one thing they both
have in common is that they are
   BOTH obsolete (non-strategic) IBM products

VS COBOL II, R4 is still supported (and will be until March 2001) but it was
LONG ago superceded by COBOL/370 which in turn was superceded by IBM COBOL
for MVS & VM which in turn was superceded by IBM COBOL for OS/390 & VM.
(Either of the last 2 products *can* be supplemented by MLE - Millennium
Language Extensions)

Having said all of that, the best place to see an introduction to the
difference between OS/VS COBOL and VS COBOL II (R4) is in the IBM
publication that can be seen on the web at:

    http://www.s390.ibm.com/bookmgr-cgi/bookmgr.cmd/BOOKS/IGYM1101/CCONTENTS

which (For the web-less) is:
  Title: VS COBOL II Migration Guide for MVS and CMS
  Document Number: GC26-3151-00

If you want something that is more detailed, then several people (myself
included) have written entire books on this.  If you REALLY want to know the
differences between OS/VS COBOL and one of IBM's *strategic* COBOL products,
check out the web for the IBM COBOL for OS/390 & VM Migration Guide.
```
