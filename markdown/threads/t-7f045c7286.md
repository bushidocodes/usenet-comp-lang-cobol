Treat the tables as a grid of graph paper. The first dimension is the
first index in the perform varying and the second dimension is the
second index, etc. There is no easy way and it can get really confusing
when you add extra levels.

Most tables have a structure and if you consider a table composed of
motor vehicle details (date of manufacture, model, trim level..) the
first dimension is the record of motor details. The second dimension is
the field type (date of manu....). Therefore name the index according to
it's use - first dimension index could be WS-VEHICLE-INDEX and the
second dimension could be WS-FIELD-INDEX.

Wait till you have multiple multi-dimensional tables on the go, and then
somebody re-uses an index/subscript.
