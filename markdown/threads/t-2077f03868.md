The compiler vendors are LIMITING options for people. It's an all or none
kind of deal. We have to either purchase the entire enterprise driving
development system, for a lot of money, or nothing.

People who dearly want to stay with COBOL are now seriously examining these
other options. Visual Basic. Visual C++, JAVA. Heck, Borland sent me a
card offering me thier top of the line, brand new C++ development SUITE for
$259.00.

If the COBOL vendors will unbundle this stuff and let us purchase our own
tools from good third party companies, or purchase them directly from the
comiler vendors - IF WE SO CHOOSE - they will survive. Otherwise, they are
doomed to die. Visual Basic, and C++ will retain control of the PC
desktop and COBOL - although SUPERIOR for developing desktop business
applications - will fall futher and further by the wayside.
