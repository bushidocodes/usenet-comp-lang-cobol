Yes, It's possible. With Micro Focus COBOL, at least, just treat the
COBOL programs as C functions (ie, declare prototypes with extern "C"
etc).

› Also I know there will be a lot of data conversion involved.

No more than if you were calling COBOL from C (ie, you need to worry
about space-padded buffers of fixed length vs nul-terminated strings,
and the internal representation of numeric quantities (Byte ordering,
COMP vs DISPLAY vs PACKED DECIMAL etc)).

There are various enhancements to Micro Focus COBOL which make inter-
language communications easier (USAGE POINTER, PROCEDURE-POINTER,
COMP-5 and COMP-X, support for recursion etc).

Cheers,
Kev.
