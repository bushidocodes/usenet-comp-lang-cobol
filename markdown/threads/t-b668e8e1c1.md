Eileen,

I have heavily invested in PC based COBOL/PLI/C/CICS/DB2 development, because I cannot
afford to carry a mainframe around on my travels.  I am an instructor fo rmainframe
topics, (mainly sytem programming stuff, but quite a lot of application programming as
well), and I prepare all the samples, exercises, demonstrations and such on my laptop.  So
far I still have to find a feature that is present on the mainframe, but not on the PC

(ok - There ain't no ADABAS, DATACOM, IDEAL, et. al. on the PC, but these are things I can
do without) 
As long as it stays in the applcation development part, the PC is great (but then, the
compilers I use are not exceptionally cheap:  Visual Age for COBOL enterprise edition,
Visual Age for PL/I enterprise edition, Visual Age for JAVA, enterprise edtion each cost
about $3,000 here, Visual Age C++ is somewhat cheaper.  I don't know what DB/2 for NT
costs, as I got it as part of the Solution Developer Program, but I would guess it isn't
cheap either.  CICS for NT (and OS/2) is included with the COBOL and the PLI compiler

When it comes to system programming stuff, I have to try to get access to an IBM mainframe
(I wouldn't be able to afford one of those....)

I've been programming in COBOL on the PC for about 15 years now.  I've
programmed for a variety of companies/clients.  For proprietary reasons I
won't match the projects with the companies but the companies have included:
        The US Government (USAF)
        JC Penney
        National Group Life
        CSC Logic
        Excel Telecommunications
        United Insurance Companies
        Blockbuster Video
        Chancellor Media
        DeVry Institute

The projects I have worked on are Agent Commission Programs, Group Health
Care programs, Front-end Policy submission programs, Payroll, Benefits,
Customer File Maintenance, National Flood Insurance Program, Real Property
programs, Work Information Programs, warehouse programs, Material
acquisition programs.

So as you can see, there are a wide variety of PC flavored programs out
there.  Don't look for much of a difference in the COBOL - COBOL is COBOL.
The major difference is in the environments.  And there are at least as many
in the PC arena as there are in the Mainframe world.

We've got DOS, Windows, Windows 95/98, Windows NT, Novell, UNIX, XENIX,
OS/2, etc.

customers like CNN, Headline News and CNNSI...Election Coverage software,
Sportsticker software...Most of this software has to interface directly with
the special computers used to air television pictures and text (character
generator computers or graphics equipment) so it has to be relatively
fast...Seems to work pretty good...IO with the external devices is much
faster than could be achieved with a mainframe and a CICS application per
say...

I know there are some accounting packages out there that use cobol...Look at
all that mainframe code that can be converted to a PC
application...Client/server apps and GUI interfaces on the PC is the name of
the game today...

I have a very successful system for tracking Archery shoots - all in
PC COBOL.

I have a pedigree system.

I have a bowling center management system.

The company I used to work for has an entire retail store unit control
system, from order entry to POS, including layaway and in house charge
accounts.  All in COBOL.

Any business activity can be serviced by PC based COBOL provided the
transaction volume is not so large as to require the services of a
mainframe.

On this group you will find MANY of a rare breed of COBOL programmer.
Those who know "both" worlds.  Daily I program on an OS/390 mainframe,
an OpenVMS system and on the PC.  All in COBOL.  There are not a lot
of us that have experience in all these platforms - I've met more here
in this group than anywhere else.  I think you will find some
interesting answers.

Oh, and I have a simple invoicing system that I wrote for my wife, and
her small business -- Written in COBOL to run under Windows.
