```
Over the times, in this NG there has been some "Fujitsu-bashing",
partly about late deliveries of software, partly about their support.

I have now used the 4.X Cobol compiler for aprox. one year. I only use
the GUI-builder Power-Cobol, and uses MF for all other works.

During that period, the need for support has come up several times.
The first time we didn't even have a support agreement, since I was
just testing the product. But they did a great job in helping me, and
they even put me in direct contact with the guy who wrote the "faulty"
part of their product, so we could solve the problem as fast as
possible.

At all times, the support has been very professional, and _very_
prompt and fast. It is my clear impression, that they are doing a
great job, once you need them. Our main problem is the time-zones;
when we go home, they come in.

I should however note, that every support-call I've made, was because
of a bug i their RunTime-System. So on the other hand, if they didn't
help me, hell would break loose.

As for their sales : After testing 4.0, we wanted to by an upgrade to
4.2 and maintenance. After sending several e-mails to their sales
department and not becoming an answer, I had to send an e-mail to one
of their support-people, I order to get her to get their sales-people
to answer me!

Just my two cents; when we have somthing to complain about, we always
make sure to make a post - so when we are satisfied costumers, we
should post as well.

Best regards

Steffen Arnecke
```

```
I haven't seen any "Fujitsu bashing" and would be very interested to see
what was said.

I have been using Fujitsu since V3.00, over the last year, converted
successfully all our existing MicroFocus COBOL/GUI DIALOG applications, have
now standardised my company on Fujitsu COBOL, and have persuaded a major
corporate client to run a pilot project using Fujitsu PowerCOBOL to leverage
programmers' legacy skills. (I certainly wouldn't recommend them to do this
if I had any doubts about the Fujitsu Product...and I am hoping to persuade
a couple more companies to go the same route.)

I have found version 4.2 of PowerCOBOL to be stable and useful. Support from
Fujitsu (on the very rare occasions it has been needed) has been exemplary.
I have even had a personal phone call from one of their consultants in
Colorado to help me solve a particular problem which resulted in a new
addition to their product (the point being, that they do take on board user
feedback...). By e-mail I have never had to wait more than 24 hours for an
effective response (bear in mind that I am being supported from the USA
while I am situated in England).

No, I am not on commission, neither am I accepting any favours or
backhanders from Fujitsu (they haven't offered...he said ,ruefully :-)). I
just think it is an EXCELLENT COBOL implementation.

Half a dozen reasons why I like it:

1. Total support for COM and ActiveX with a development environment which
easily allows custom controls from different languages.

2. Extremely easy integration with C++ and VB. (you can step through COBOL
and VB source code in the same debugging session...)

3. Event processing written in COBOL (no need for separate DIALOG language)

4. External sharing of variables allows tight modular on-line code.

5. Simple switching between .dll and .exe structures without requirement to
know complex compiler/linker switches and commands. (Select whether you want
DEBUG or RELEASE and .EXE or .DLL, it is very simple, and you can build
either or both and switch between them.)

6. Comprehensive on-line documentation on CD-ROM, with the software.

If other people have had a different experience it's a pity.

Pete.
```
