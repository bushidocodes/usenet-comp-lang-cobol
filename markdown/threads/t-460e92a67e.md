Tomba,

Confusion reigns in your mind. Let's see if we can clarify it for you.

There are three elements, and the programs I already sent you from the M/F
examples, cover #2 and #3 below. Plus further programs show you how to
manipulate colours etc. If you look at the various examples, whilst not
necessarily related to accept/display, they give you some tips on screen layout.

1. Screen layout - which allows you to (a) display myScreen or display this
field, or
    (b) accept MyScreen or accept this field - you are dealing with numerics or
text.

2. The keyboard routines testing for characters or function keys.

3. The Mouse routines for checking where the Mouse is or even placing the Mouse.

You are obviously a beginner, and appear to be trying to do it all in one BIG
BANG. You wont succeed. Do it in order by the numbers above. Get a handle on
simple display screen or accept screen before you move on to #2, then #3.

Now you've already been through #2 where you were testing for Function keys -
but it is in no way the same as # 1.

#1 Screen Section.
-------------------

Keep it simple. Don't use files, just a record in Working Storage.

01 ws-record.
    05 ws-Name            pic x(30).
    05 ws-Balance         pic 9(06)v9(02).

Screen Section.

01 AcceptScreen.
    05 line 6 col 10  value "Customer Name".
    05 line 6 col 30 A-name pic x(30) to ws-name.
    05 line 8 col 10  value "Balance".
    05 line 8 col 30 A-balance pic zzz,zzz.99 to ws-balance..

01 DisplayScreen.
    05 line 6 col 10  value "Customer Name".
    05 line 6 col 30 D-name pic x(30) from ws-name.
    05 line 8 col 10  value "Balance".
    05 line 8 col 30 D-balance pic zzz,zzz.99 from ws-balance..

Procedure Division.

initialize ws-record
move "Joe Blow" to ws-name
move 123.45" to ws-balance
display DisplayScreen

initialize ws-record
accept AcceptScreen

now accept a new balance for the record we have just accepted
accept D-balance

NOW - it is such a long time since I used Screens that the above may not be
totally correct. I'm sure you must be able to use one screen that both accepts
and displays. (one screen line indicating both "To" and "From".  You will just
have to look at your language references and try it out. Note particularly,
don't confuse accepting/displaying with testing for Hex values.

When you have mastered Steps 1, 2 and 3, individually - NOW - you can put them
all together in one program.

Jimmy, Calgary AB
