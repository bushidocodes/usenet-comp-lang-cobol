Of the 20 or so computer languages I have had to become proficient in over
the years, I have had formal classes in only two or three. My education
was in physics and math. I will share with you my method of learning to
program or learning in a new programming language:

1. Obtain books on programming (language, theory, technique, etc.)

2. Study diligently

3. Program, program, program (you have to do it to learn it)

4. Repeat steps 1-3 as long as you continue to program

I have nothing against formal training; it's great if you have the time,
money and opportunity, but it isn't required. But programming is like
playing a musical instrument; you must study, study, study and practice,
practice, practice. You can't be 'taught' to do it, you must 'learn' to do
it. Teaching is only a useful aid. I know people who wanted someone to
'teach' them how to program, and none of them made it.

Programming will force you to develop some less-used parts of your brain,
and may surprise you in how much it affects your everyday problem solving
ability.
