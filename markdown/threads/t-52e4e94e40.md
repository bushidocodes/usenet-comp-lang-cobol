Jean,
    First of all, AcuServer won't work with SQL.  You'll need to either use
Acu4GL or AcuODBC to manipulate the data.  The systems engineers at AcuCorp
told me that both are slow and that we would be better off using AcuServer
with Vision files.
    Not sure what sort of warehousing you intend to do, but we decided
against SQL with AcuCOBOL just for this reason and have stayed with our
indexed file scheme.
    There's a Nigel (don't recall the last name) who is a system engineer
for AcuCorp who lurks around this newsgroup.  Maybe he'll see your post and
give you more information that I can.

    Regards,

    Jason Bailey

I'm a little concerned by the systems engineer who told you that
Acu4GL would be slow. Slow'ER' than Vision I'll buy (for most
applications), but 'slow' is a relative term. Looks like it's time
break out the bright lights and sharp spikes again!  :^)

AcuODBC allows you to access Vision from ODBC compliant
apps, so doesn't really figure here. As Jason says AcuServer isn;t
appropriate here either (it's a Vision only product).

Your options for SQL Server are Acu4GL which will allow you to
use standard Cobol I/O verbs on your SQL Server database, or
AcuSQL which will let you embed SQL in your programs.

Which one? Depends....... For ease of use, speed of deployment
and simplicity Acu4GL is your answer. For ultimate performance
AcuSQL may be the way to go. Sometimes a combination of the
two technologies gets best results.

The simple answer is to try it and see what works. We (Acucorp)
will be very happy to work with you to figure out a workable,
affordable solution to your problem. Contact your local office, or
