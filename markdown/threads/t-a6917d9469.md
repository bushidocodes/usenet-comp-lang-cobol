Michael,

The answer to your question is "No".

It IS possible to derive what the last Verb executed was, but NOT via
standard ODBC (at least as far as I know; different ODBC drivers may have
different features, but I've never seen one with what you are asking for. I
spend a lot of time dealing with RDBs on PCs and Mainframes and have done
since they were invented (1983).

There is no need for you to have "code which gets the results of each", just
use dynamic SQL.

This will allow you to process ANY statement input by your users and you
will always know what it was because it will be in the PREPARE area.

Check out dynamic SQL, PREPARE and EXECUTE.

Early implementations were pretty inefficient, but this is no longer true.

Another possibility would be to use EXPLAIN, but this has different results
in different environments and I wouldn't recommend it.

Pete.
