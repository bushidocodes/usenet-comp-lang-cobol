The COBOL language is currently undergoing some major changes.
Although ANSI 85/ISO 89 is still the current standard, a new
standard will be approved (hopefully!) in the year 2000 or so. Many
of the proposed changes have been implemented in current compilers.

As a starting point, you may wish to obtain a compiler and some
reference books.

Read the FAQ < http://www.netcom.com/~wmklein/cobolfaq.htm >
for a list of compiler vendors, reference books, etc.

For the compiler, you may wish to download the Fujitsu V3.0
starter set from < www.adtools.com >. It may be downloaded for
free. This is not the most recent version, but is ANSI 85 and
should help you to re-learn the language.

Any text or reference books that you may still have and that were
based on COBOL 85 should be useable. Should you wish to
consider some more recent texts, the authors/contributors to
"COBOL Unleashed" and Teach Yourself COBOL in 24 Hours"
are regular contributors to this newgroup.

Finally, once you have, again, experienced the "joy of COBOL",
you may decide whether taking COBOL classes again is more
appropriate for you.
