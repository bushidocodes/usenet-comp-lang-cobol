>I code as much Assembler as I do Cobol, but I do not use the
>structured macros and I'm not even sure I know what they are.


I don't know how much assembler you have written, but I
am assuming that you understand macros.  If not, say
so and I will explain.

"Structured macros" refer to a technique of coding assembler
using libraried macro's that mimic the typical topdown structure
of high level languages.  Using them, you can write top down
gotoless assembler.  It is not that difficult to write such a set
of macros for any decent macro assembler.

Although I tend to use macros extensively when writing in
assembler (a colleague once accused me of inventing my
own language after reading my assembler code) I have
only dabbled in a full structured set.  I do have a CASE
macro, though, that basically uses a two tables.  One is
of literals that go on the screen as a menu, and the other
is a jump table.  As long as the two are the same size
it is pretty well idiot proof.
