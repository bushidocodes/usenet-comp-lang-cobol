ei = ((1 + (ni/f)) ** f) - 1  *> f = number of periods per year

If cash flows are irregular or bi-directional, you must converge on the answer:

compute precision = .0000005 / frequency
compute rate = 1 + (.1 / frequency)  *> initial extimate is 10%
move 1 to d, d-absolute
move zero to iterations
perform until d-absolute less than precision
    add 1 to iterations
    move present-value to b, c
    perform varying n from 1 by 1 until n > n-limit
        compute b = (b * rate) - payment (n)
        if  n less than n-limit
            compute c = b + (c * rate)
        end-if
    end-perform
    if  c not equal to zero
        compute d = b / c
    end-if
    subtract d from rate
    move d to d-absolute
    if  iterations greater than 9 and d-absolute not less than previous-d
        display 'does not compute'
        move zero to rate
        goback
    end-if
    move d-absolute to previous-d
end-perform

How about something simpler than the other offering:

Taken from here: http://www.rwent.com/NominalvsEffective.html

There is a formula for finding the Effective Rate...  f = ( 1 + i )m - 1

 

where f is Effective Rate and m = number of compounds per year.

 

EXAMPLE

Find the effective rate for 18% compounded quarterly

 

i = .18 / 4 = 0.045

m = 4

 

f = ( 1 + i )m - 1

 

f = ( 1.045 )4 - 1

f = 1.192518601 - 1

f = 0.192518601

As a percent, f = 19.25%.
