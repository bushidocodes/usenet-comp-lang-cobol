I would recommend reading Structured Analysis and System Specification by
Tom DeMarco.
You may find a copy at your local library.

You don't have to follow the book rigorously, but you may be able to adapt
some of the conceps to your situation.

Some Genral rules.

Hold regular meeting with ALL participants present. Do not work exclusively
thru a chain of command.
If the project falls behind schedule, you may need to cut resources. You
will have to know who the key players are.

Insist on System Design and Test Plan walk-throughs. Present the method in
one meeting, briefly discuss it, have a follow-up meeting after people have
had time to consider other options.

State your Goals and Objectives in writing.

GOAL 1: The system must be 100% accurate.
GOAL 2: The system should be easily maintainable.
GOAL 3: The system should be reasonably efficient.

Do not allow pepple to be distracted by side issues. i.e. Addressing
performance issues where no evidence of performance degradation is present.
If a Batch JOB runs in 3 minutes, don't waste time trying to get it to run
in 2 Minutes and 50 seconds.

Stay focused on your goals.

Avoid huge monolithic programs. The make maintence more difficult, and you
are stuck with 1 person doing all the coding. Modular programming can speed
up the development process.

Automate testing processes where you can. The machine does a more accurate
JOB.

Public praise, Private criticism.
