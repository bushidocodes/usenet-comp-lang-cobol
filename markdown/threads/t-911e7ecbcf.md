mean that short term memory understanding is good enough !!! In fact,
you will need an understanding of everything that text says ! That is

what your instructors are basing their course on and you can bet that
not only are they sure of Their Own understanding of Cobol but in
fact they are sure they could help any of their students understand
the material.

My suggestion is talk to your instructor until he/she knows where you
stand. I don't mean to send you into their arena and have you act
like an idiot, BUt if and when you understand what the text means to
you, then that instructor can open doors for you.

Don't think I'm being a smartass, I'm not trying to be. I need you
to understand that the instructor can help, if you can explain where
you are in understanding the material.

Didn't you ever have to have a math instructor help you? This is
exactly the same thing. Get help from BOTH the book and your
instructor.

As for a different class, check into community colleges. I take Cobol
at one near me and our Cobol is broken down into 2 semesters. Not
only are community colleges cheaper, there are usually not as many
students and you might have a more-helping instructor. Credit hours
are probably the same as the college or look around for a comm.
college that has the right hours.

You are right in coming to this group. There might be a Cobol
email newsgroup too, but as of this time, I'm not sure.
