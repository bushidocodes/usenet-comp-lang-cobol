```
Throughout my lengthy career in IT I have been privileged to work with
developers and be one.

I have seen really sensible suggestions and ideas dismissed because this
group had little credibility with Management.

Of course, if they pay large sums of money to a consultant to give them the
same advice, it is then credible.

(I pointed this out to a Director once when I had been retained to
independently investigate and advise on some IT problems and directions in a
big corporation. Their own staff had been telling them for months pretty
much what was in my final report but they ignored it. Once I completed and
delivered my report they started doing the things recommended.)

Fortunately, it appears as if this trend may be changing and the new breed
of developers are not standing for it.

http://adtmag.com/articles/2014/04/16/developer-survey.aspx

So, if you have a lifetime of development under your belt and are about to
retire, might be time to acquire a few new skills and hang in there for a
bit... Maybe you'll get the respect (and money) you should have had years
ago...:-)

Pete.
"I used to write COBOL...now I can do anything."
```
