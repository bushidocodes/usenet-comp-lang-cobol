could be used as the basis of their User Documentation? Who would be the
person in the vendor Company to approach on these issues?

In other words try and find out what is needed and wanted with regard to a
Standard document. This has to  be a better approach than simply sitting
down writing a document that is aimed at no-one in particular.

ALL feedback in this area must be valuable, whether the Alternative process
proceeds or not. I guess I'm saying that some "Market Research" should be
done, irrespective of what process we use to produce a Standard.

It would also help to publicise the fact that the whole process of Standard
production is being reviewed (whether you agree with what I'm proposing or
not). Make sure that everyone who has an interest in COBOL is aware of the
debate going on here and is encouraged to contribute to it.

Finally, ask yourself whether you are honestly prepared to accept what the
results turn up. If there really is no need for a standard can you accept
that?  If the majority of the User Base have been excluded so long that they
no longer care about it, can you accept that?

I can.

If the majority feel that J4/Alphabet soup is doing a good job, the problems
have been resolved, the new Standard is just what the Doctor ordered, I can
also accept that. (To do otherwise would be a bit like me trying to free
people who LIKE being enslaved...<G>) But it needs to be a clear message
from people who are NOT involved in producing it...<G>

Said it before and I say  it again: "It is NOT WHO's right, it is WHAT's
right...".

3. There is currently enough indication of support for an open, empowered,
moderated forum, using timeboxed, interactive iteration as the management
process, to encourage me to take the necessary remaining actions to bring it
into being. However, the final decision on it cannot (and will not) be taken
until the end of the current timebox.

Meanwhile, anyone who is genuinely interested in helping this effort should
indicate that to me by private mail. Moral support is very acceptable, as
well as tangible help...<G>
