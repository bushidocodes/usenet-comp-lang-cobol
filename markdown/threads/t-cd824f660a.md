One of them is the idea that you should not use the COMPUTE statement,
using ADD, SUBTRACT ect instead. The only argrument I have heard for this
is
that it is more CPU intensive to use COMPUTE.

I personally don't. Simple steps are always easier to read for me
especially when figuring out what things do. Stepping through with a
debugger querying each step in the calculation to see whats going wrong.

Then again, I keep to a very efficient almost machine language like
subset of COBOL anyway and almost every arithmetic statement is two
parameter. This may be force of habit after 8 years of Micro Focus COBOL
where I know what's most effecient.

›

can simplify? Not everyone can nest 3 levels of parenthesis in their
heads.

working-storage anyway. Blanket INITIALIZE's just get converted into
multiple move statements anyway.
