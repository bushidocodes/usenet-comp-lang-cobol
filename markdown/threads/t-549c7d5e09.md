It all depends on whether you are trying to learn COBOL, already know COBOL
and are trying to learn the differences in COBOL 85, or are trying to learn
how to develop GUI programs for Win95.

If just trying to learn COBOL is your objective, then I would suggest (and
I am serious here, not joking) the simplest environment you can put
together. DOS and a character interface is enough to learn COBOL. If you
know COBOL and are trying to learn the differences in COBOL 85, then
spending an afternoon with the manual is more than enough. And if you are
trying to learn to do GUI's, then the GUI workbench is OK, although it is
more complicated than it might be.

Of course, if you don't know COBOL at all, but you have to get jump started
on doing GUI's in it immediately, then you are in deep doo-doo. Trying to
learn COBOL from the GUI COBOL Workbench is kind of like trying to learn to
drive in the space shuttle.
