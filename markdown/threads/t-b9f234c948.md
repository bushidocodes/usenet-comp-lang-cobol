I am currently evaluating erp systems for my company. Two in particular
stand out as a good fit for our organization of 30 employees. These are
Made2Manage and Syspro Impact. Syspro, as many of you may know is written in
Cobol, while Made2Manage is largely in C++ and FoxPro. Both will do the job,
both look good, both use MS SQL Server as backbone, both are approximately
the same cost.

While Made2Manage may be customized with VB / VBA, Syspro is cusomized with
Cobol using Merant's NetWare.

Would anyone like to share their thoughts on the merits of either, given
this (extremely) limited glimpse.

The *only* considerations vis-a-vis the source language(s) of an ERP system
are:

1. Are there differences in one-time/annual license fees for run-time
support / required compilers or interpreters for selecting erp system brand
"A" written in language "A" versus system brand "B" written in language "B?"

2. Will there exist people to maintain the software?

For item one, you proably already have the numbers; they are probably buried
in a vendor proposal, but it's not worth the effort to break them out.
(Because you don't purchase the run-time support without purchasing the
basic system).

For item two, you ask around.

In your case, there are many qualified people available for C++, FoxPro and
COBOL. If the maintenance skills you need must come from existing staff,
that complicates this a bit; but in the abstract your two systems are, in my
view anyway, dead-nuts even on 'maintainability.'

Don't get hung up on 'source language' except for the above.

MCM
