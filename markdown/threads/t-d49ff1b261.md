Douglas, your comparison between Pascal and COBOL is totally bogus, it is
not interesting to know whether COBOL is useful or concise for writing
one liners (your Pascal incidentally is indeed rusty, it bears no
relationship whatsoever to standard Pascal, which lacks a string data
type of the kind you imagine).

What is interesting is to take a typical fiscal application of reasonable
length and code it in Pascal and COBOL. I think you will find the COBOL
MUCH easier to read. Just think for a moment:

Pascal lacks arithmetic that meets the needs of fiscal applications (hih
precision decimal scaled arithmetic)

Pascal lacks user defined operators

The combination is pretty deadly, it means that you have to use function
calls for all arithmetic operations in the Pascal case, and that you have
no literals for your arithmetic types. UGH!

C fares no better in this comparison. You can at least dress up the syntax
a bit better in C++, although literals are still a sticking point. Ada 95
has the necessary dictions, but its new and relatively untested for fiscal
applications (I am an Ada 95 fan and very involved with Ada 95, but it has
to prove itself in this arena). In short, COBOL is ideally suited still for
many applications, both from an efficiency point of view AND a readability
point of view.

Yes there are weaknesses in COBOL, but for the domain in which it shows its
strengths, the weaknesses of obvious competitors are much more significant.

MOVE has two problems -
(1) beginners expect it to MOVE a data item from one place
to another, ie copy & delete original, and
(2) assembly language programmers, who are not beginners,

in the DOS batch language. The single "=" is ambiguous.
