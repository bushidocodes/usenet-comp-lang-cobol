There are compiler certifications made by government agency that result
from tests using "standard" COBOL. Vendors should be able to tell you
which level of the standard they meet. American National Standards
Institute should be able to provide you with a copy of the standard, and
it will tell you which features fall into what level.

I am unaware of certification of programmers in the use of COBOL except
of course the installation of a program that has been compiled by a
certified compiler. Different authors, and schools of thought may have
their view of what makes a certified COBOL programmer, but if you read
the comments in the thread 'you may be a COBOL programmer if", you will
see that many a "running" program should not be considered as written by
a certified programmer. In olden days, it was thought that a program
that ran was a good program vs. of course the one that says it's good,
but frequently fails or doesn't run at all.

: I heard that there are certifications in Cobol. What are they?

If you are asking about certification of COBOL programmers, I have
first-hand experience with several certification programs that have
been available over the past 30 years.

1. In the early '70s there was a designation called the Registered
Business Programmer (RBP). If I remember right, it was granted
by the Data Processing Management Association. One of the
requirements was to pass a written exam on COBOL programming.

2. A few years later the Institute for Certification of Computer
Professionals (ICCP) offered the Certificate in Computer Programming
(CCP). As I recall, there were three flavors: business,
scientific, and systems. Earning the CCP as a business
programmer required passing a test on COBOL. The Certificate
in Computer Programming, per se, is no longer available, and
the initials CCP have been recycled to mean Certified Computing
Professional, a credential that requires much broader knowledge
and experience.

3. Check with the ICCP (< http://www.iccp.org/ >) to see what might
be available now. According to their Web site, they do offer a
Proficiency Certificate in COBOL. Let us know what you find out.

Walter Murray, RBP, CCP, CDP, CCP
