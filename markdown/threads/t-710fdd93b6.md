Dear White Sparrow:
If you can afford it, buy a 486 or pentium computer with enough memory,
then buy some language compilers.

You may consider taking a programming class a junior college.
Various languages have their merits. You can buy Turbo Pascal compiler
for a reasonable price. And then there is Turbo C/C++.

I suggest you take a class in BASIC or Pascal, and use your computer
to do the homework.

You can obtain Personal COBOL from Micro Focus which is reasonably
priced for students.

The alternative to the above approach would be to install the linux
operating system on your computer and perhaps use gcc and g++ to
learn C++ and C. This is more unix oriented.

If you get a large hard disk and cdrom, you can have the best of both
worlds by partitioning your hard disk to have both linux and Dos/Windows.


New languages come from different sources. COBOL was a result of
a Industry/Government committee and turned out pretty good. C was
a by-product of Unix development. C++ was the addition of object
oriented Stuff to C.
