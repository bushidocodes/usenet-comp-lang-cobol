You're lucky. You are choosing between two good options.

IMS experience makes you valuable. DB2 experience makes you valuable.
Additional IMS experience makes you more valuable then initial DB2 experience,
IMHO. But its a close call, because a mix of experience makes you more valuable
then anything else. (It's just that you really need to get solid in each area,
and the second choice does not solidify your current forte).

Longevity of service at a single company also makes you more valuable. So don't
jump unless there is a quantitative basis.

Something is missing here though. You have said nothing about an increase in
pay. That does not make sense. Programmers increase in value very rapidly
during the first several years of experience.

Almost all companies are entirely unable to match this value increase with pay
increase. So the way to get your fair share early is to move.

It is not unusual for companies to loose folks at the 12-month or 18-month
point (newbies eventually wake-up). 

But you have not mentioned the pay factor. So there is perhaps a problem here.
This is a guess, but I am thinking you are not shopping well enough.

You should move when the new pay matches the skill level you have acquired
already. And if it is a concern to you that you have IMS and DB2, then make
that your goal. Move only to a situation where you can continue to build the
IMS experience and add DB2.

In other words, just to play devil's advocate, a weakness here is that you are
making a choice with OR logic. You should use AND logic. You are the one with
the talent.  You can call the tune.  You just have to believe in strength of
your position.

But I do not mean to be presumptuous, nor do I invite you to comment on the pay
issue, since you have not elected to do so.  Infact, if the pay is better at
the new spot, then that plus the additional DB2 skills would be heavy
indicators.

Early in your DP career the only way to get fair increases is to jump. But you
do not want to jump too often (that will hurt you).  So jump when the
remuneration warrants it, and jump to skill combinations (not just to new
skills).

Best Wishes for a good decision.
