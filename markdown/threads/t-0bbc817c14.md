Another tack is to start sending out spec' letters & CV's to everyone you
know that uses COBOL, (the Software Yearbook is a good place to start as
well as all the major Banks, Insurance Companies etc.).  I did the
Computeach course, sent out about 200 letters got replies from about 10 of
them, and managed to get work with Barclays.

Something to remember (I'm sure Computeach will have stressed this) is to
highlight reasons why they should give you a trainee position rather than
wait for someone with experience e.g. lower costs, enthusiasm, commitment to
learning etc.

Also check out www.stepstone.co.uk and search for Programmers.  Try to avoid
the agencies, look for ones where the companies are advertising themselves.

Good Luck

Kippers
