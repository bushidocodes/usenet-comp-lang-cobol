### Fujitsu COBOL slow down RUMOR is not true. Read about it...

- **From:** "Gary Roush" <gkr@adtools.com>
- **Date:** 1999-06-07T00:00:00
- **Newsgroups:** comp.lang.cobol
- **Message-ID:** `<ucW63.16$H73.702@typ42b.nn.bcandid.com>`

```
We were recently informed about a rumor suggesting that "Fujitsu is slowing
down its support for its COBOL product". As there is absolutely no truth to
this rumor, we thought we should send you some facts to reassure you that
Fujitsu is very strongly committed to its COBOL product - so you won't have
to waste time thinking about such rumors in the future.

1. We believe that Fujitsu has one of the largest, if not the largest,
COBOL compiler and tools development teams in the world.

2. Fujitsu started working with COBOL in 1965 and now delivers COBOL on
platforms from PCs to mainframes in U.S. and international markets.
Fujitsu's commitment to COBOL is long term and strategic.

3. Version 4.2 of Fujitsu COBOL was released in March 1999 and we are
developing Version 5 for release later this year. It will contain
enhancements such as support for the Windows 2000 Application Specification
and further tuning of our already superior run-time performance.

4. Fujitsu is the only COBOL vendor to have announced support for Intel's
IA-64 hardware. By building what is likely to be the only COBOL-specific
code generator for IA-64, we are continuing our tradition of being the
highest performing COBOL on all platforms that we support.

5. The Fujitsu COBOL group is actively working on the next COBOL standard
and has the closest OO COBOL implementation of any COBOL vendor.

6. Fujitsu has built the COBOL Cartridge for Oracle's Web Application
Server that allows COBOL programs to exploit all Oracle web server
technology

7. Fujitsu Software Corporation has put substantial effort into delivering
high quality code converters for such dialects as IBM COBOL, Micro Focus
COBOL, Microsoft COBOL and ICOBOL. This indicates our increasing support
for those moving to Fujitsu COBOL.

From these facts, we hope that you will agree with us that the message
should be: "No one is as committed to COBOL as is Fujitsu!"

If you hear someone passing on rumors that down play Fujitsu's commitment
to COBOL, please suggest to them that they contact us - as some of you
have - so that they can get the facts first-hand. (Email:
cobol@adtools.com; Phone: 408-428-0500)

With our best regards and thanks to all of you who wait to check out the
facts before acting on rumors!

The COBOL Team at Fujitsu Software Corporation.
```
