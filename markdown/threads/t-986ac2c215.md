I am currently contracting through a body shop to a small manufacturing
company. My boss inquired Friday whether I'd like a permanent
position. I replied that I felt this company was small and their data
processing wasn't in bad shape, so I didn't see how they could afford me
when the crucnch comes. I was asked to give a number. I responded that
I would have to think about how to structure a deal, and he said go
ahead.

I figured I was writing my own ticket, so I did. I wrote some very
specific terms to allow me to pursue outside part time work at large
hourly rates while keeping this permanent position. But eventually I
had to specify how much this company should pay me.

Based on the first rule of negotiation (you don't ask, you don't get) I
specified the absolute highest amount I could imagine them swallowing,
then I upped it by 20%. I emailed it to my boss at 3:30 this afternoon.

Just after 5:00 he went home. He came by my desk and just mumbled
"good-bye". It was as though he wanted to say something (like "let's
talk tomorrow") but couldn't. He was clearly in shock.

I'm glad I didn't give him a heart attack. I never could have forgiven
myself.
