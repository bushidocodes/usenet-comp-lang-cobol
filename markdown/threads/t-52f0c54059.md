"Anthropos", human being, as distinct from "andros", male human being.  Luke 
2:14 has the former in all versions of the GNT of which I'm aware.  *Here* 
the text is clearly and unambiguously referring to "all humans".

On the other hand, the last word in the verse appears in early Greek texts 
(and in translation into Latin in the Vulgate and other even earlier Latin 
translations as well) as "eudokias" (genitive case) rather than the 
"eudokia" (nominative case) as found in the TR.  The omission of the final 
sigma in the TR leads to the KJV "on earth peace, good will toward men" of 
the KJV as contrasted with "pacem in terris hominibus bonae voluntatis" of 
the Vulgate and "and on earth peace to men of good will" of the Douay-Rheims 
(and many other more recent translations as well, as I recall).
