It sounds like you have a statement to the effect of "MOVE Q556AA-REC TO 
Q556-FPRNT1-REC" somewhere and they are really redefinations of the same 
storage.

There is a trick to clearing storage on an S3* by moving a space to the 
first byte of a field, then moving the field to itself.  This is 
self-evident when writing in assembler.  But I've heard tail of it being 
done in early Cobol as well, back in the dark ages before well defined 
padding rules or initialize verbs.

E.g.

   01 Q556AA-REC.
      05 CLEAR-BYTE       PIC X.
      05 Q556-FPRNT1-REC.
        ....

  * Generate  "MVI CLEAR-BYTE,C' '"
  * Generate  "MVC Q556AA_REC(L'Q556AA_REC-1),Q556AA-REC+1"
      MOVE Q556AA-REC TO Q556-FPRNT1-REC

  * Generate a message, maybe an error, I can't remember
      MOVE Q556-FPRNT1-REC to Q556AA-REC

I cannot really recall the details, as it has been most of ten years 
since my last VS Cobol II conversion to Cobol for *, but there was 
something about this trick that caused a message.

Was this ever compiled clean on this version of the compiler?

If the code compiled clean previously the question becomes, did you use the
same compiler and version? If so, then you must be compiling with a different
option. 

Your best bet is to deal with the problem, and fix it. Accept your fate. Take
responsibility. Get it done. The compiler is your friend.

But if you are not up to a mission that is not related to your original plan,
then look at the parms on the production compiles versus your test compile.

That message, some how, looks a little more severe (being an E level) than one
usually get with IGYCRCTL on overlaps. The compiler may well be seeing
something about the index that
previous compilers were not able to discern.

I would face it and fix it. Let the compiler help you.

There is another scenerios here, which is that a copybook has changed, and so
actually the program did not previously compile successfully, ... because the
previous compile might have seen a different data layout. 

That subscript looks important. (is there any ODO above the occurs that the
subscript is dealing with? ... is there any redefines of a group level above
the occurs that the subscript is dealing with?)
