Everyone that is looking for a compiler for COBOL:

I purchased a book at a local computer retailer call "Revolutionary
Cobol" written by a Russian that includes a compiler and example
programs. While the text of the book has not impressed me, the
compiler has handled every problem I have had to code for my Cobol I
and Cobol II classes. It creates executable modules, has a text editor
from which you compile directly, and it gives you feedback on error
messages and directs you to the exact line where the compiler detects
the error. This is a DOS based product but runs fine in a DOS window
in Win3.11 and Win95. The cost was around $50 and well worth it to me.
I can write, compile and run programs and produce output. The programs
are just text files that I can easily upload to the AS400 we are using
for class. After modifying a few lines (Source computer, object
computer and "select/assign") I can run these on the AS400 without a
hitch.

If anyone is interested, I will look at the author and publisher and
email it to you. The book is at my office at the moment.
