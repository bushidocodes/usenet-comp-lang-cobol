```

A short while back... '03 or so... a Cabinet-level Federal agency of the
government of the United States of America contracted with me to makesome
modifications to their decades-old, rock-solid COBOL payroll and personnel
system (IBM mainframe-based). It was the start of a beautiful friendship
and a worthwhile chunk of billable hours.

In '03 they asked if I could train someone to take over from me and I
replied 'Sure... but to make bricks I need clay and this clay must have at
least five years' experience coding COBOL for IBM mainframes.'

Over the years they've repeated the request and received the same
answer... and never showed me a candidate. The beautiful friendship and
billable hours continues.

Two months back they brought in another consultant/contractor/hired gun
for... something or other and tossed him a '... and can you get us off
this mainframe?'

'Sure, no problem, right away!', says Mr Newguy.

(never mind the difficulties imposed by things like the COVID-19
restrictions on shoulder-surfing... right away!)

Yesterday Mr Newguy sends out his second Monthly Status Report which
includes a line about '... and Payroll processed to completion without
incident.'

... and... just because I'm who I am... I did a Reply All: with the
following (edited for reasons of client confidentiality):

I was informed that on/about 27 Jul (federal agency) will be altering its
linklist employed at IPL so that the concatenation sequence establishes
primacy of the COBOL 6.2 library over the currently-sole COBOL (earlier
version) library. This will synchronize the agency's Test and Prod system
configurations.

... and now I wait. I envision something like:

Mr Newguy: 'Hey, I'm just trying to prioritize our synergistic efforts in
order to get maximum returns... can you give me some insight into this?'

Me: 'I thought it was pretty clear, each word means exactly what it
says... please, sit down and we'll go over it so I can understand what you
don't understand and tailor this learning experience exactly.'

Mr Newguy: 'OK... what's a 'linklist'?'

DD: 'I think I'm beginning to get a better picture. This is going to take
a while, is your schedule clear? Do you like tea? I've a lovely
Darjeeling, a Japanese green - bi luo chun, it tastes like springtime -
and a Chinese white. Give me a moment to brew a pot...'

'There is no royal road to geometry.' - attrib. var.

DD
```
