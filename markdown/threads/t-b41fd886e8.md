I have just learned that the charter for this newsgroup encourages
users to give info on new COBOL book. At this risk of being accused of
sounding my own trumpet, may I menion my book?

"COBOL by Command" by Bernard V Liengme is publised by Arnold (UK) and
sold in USA by Micro Focus and by Chapman Hall. In Canada, Oxford
University Press carries it. Ths ISBN is 0 340 64552 0.

I wrote the book because I could not find anything suitable for my
students. Most of them have training in some other language before
they take COBOL. We didn't need a text that took 5 pages to explain
why one needs an IF statement, for example. Futhermore, most books
give long sample programs. Students should not be expected to work
thru a 500 line program to see an example of how to use SORT. It is a
compact book (300 pages) but gives full coverage to COBOL other than
Report Writer.

ANSI COBOL-85 is used with some Micro Focus and some Ryan-McFarland
extension - screens for example.
