Thanks to everybody who responded - my original post appears below.

The general consensus is:

1)We should have a "front company" that would handle payroll.
Apparently, the IRS has BURNED many employer companies (mine included -
$79,000/1995) over the Independent Contractor issue. Marketing you
guys(gals) to "Y2K Panicked Companies" would be much more successful if
they could avoid the liability of taking on a 1099 workers. I talked to
the owners of my company (ASG Renaissance http://www.asgren.com) today
and we reached an agreement to run this whole thing through our payroll,
bumping up your rates about $10/hr to cover FICA, MEDI, FUTA, SUTA,
WorkersComp, ADP processing fee.

2)We need to separate the "chaff" from the legitimate guys/gals who can
provide Y2K solutions. Is a phone interview sufficient or should they
have to pass a proficiency test?

3)If the Y2K problem is real (some would say NOT!) and if wages for US
programmers are spiraling, then let's apply some principles from ECON
101 and see what the free market will pay.

Let me know what YOU think.

Thanks,
