Yeah, those of us that use to work there who tried to get
management to attack the up and coming markets were laid off
years ago...  As an example, I just wonder what would have
happened to Amdahl's market share had the Firebird (Intel 386
based S/370 emulation system -- 1989/90) been put on the market? 
Or, how about certifying the 5890 & 5990 boxes for DOS/VSE in
preparation for IBM having to make VSE use the XA I/O
instructions (since we all knew that the S/370 base
architecture's days were numbered -- 1988/90)?  What about Key
Computer -- what ever happened to that Unix Super computer
system?

When I use to work there, Amdahl had IBM worried.  Then came the
layoffs (began in 1989, 30 days after the big quake in the SF Bay
area).  Finally Fujitsu bought out the company and quietly (IMHO)
removed the top managers -- the ones that caused Amdahl to be so
indecisive -- no one that I knew there in 1995-6 could tell me
what direction the company would take.

Don't get me wrong -- I'm gonna miss those Red Boxes and folders
that use to get you a 10% discount from IBM (as soon as the IBM
sales person saw them, you got 10% off without saying a word
according to all the DP managers I've talked to).  I truly
enjoyed working there, and still miss it to this day.
