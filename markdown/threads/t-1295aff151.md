When the box comes up, click on the "environment setup" menu item.
Click "keyword".  Scroll to @EnvSetWindow, and click on "selection". The
parameter will jump to the upper window of the background screen, and the
front window will close. Cursor over to after the "=" sign, and type "UNUSE"
(caps on).  Click "set" then "save". You will have to answer "Ok" to all the
stupid "are you sure, I am doing it, I am the compiler and smarter than you"
messages, but it should solve your problem.

If your program does not need any pointers to files or limited use environment
variables, you canset @ENVSETWINDOW=UNUSE.
In the case of internal sorts or external file connections, you use this window
to set the environment variables that will be used by the program.

BSORT-TMPDIR=<some big drive space>
INFILE=<drive:path\filename or \\domain\path\filename>[,BSAM]
OUTFILE=<drive:path\filename>

This method is used when the Select/Assign is done to implementor names
versus hardcoded internal file names or working-storage names.

Select input-file assign to infile.
Select output-file assign to outfile.

To help reduce the amount of re-entry, you can click on the SAVE button
to store the information into COBOL85.CBR.  This will help on the next
execution
of the same program.
