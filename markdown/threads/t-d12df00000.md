Actually,  my guess is that this is not a problem with "PKZIP" - as such -
but rather a problem with the fact that PKZIP on the mainframe and on the PC
are not the same product (and/or same release).  In other words, they are
using (slightly) different "compression" algorithms and what gets compressed
on the mainframe (via one algorithm) is NOT getting expanded on the PC with
the same algorithm.

My guess is that DD *might* agree with this (and isn't saying something
particularly different).  However, some people may not be aware of the
distinction of multiple "products" called PKZIP - and changes in compression
routines between (among?) them.

Turns out you are correct on the Hex translation.  We erroneously chose 09.  I
changed it to 05 and got the correct translation for tab.  Before this I had to
specifiy -TEXT to force this conversion.  Turns out that PKZIP interrogates the
file to determine the type of compression to use.  The presence of
non-displayable characters tripped it up.  Forcing the TEXT option got the
desired results.  Thanks for your help on this.
