Hello everyone,

The following is a warning to everyone in trying to do business
over the Internet, and specifically to anyone who might have dealings
with the following person:

Andrew Laverne Freeman
2094 Ledge Rock Road
Grovespring, MO 65662

(417) 462-7736 (voice/data)
afr··.@ser··o.us

A couple of months ago, I posted the following in the Usenet
Usenet newsgroups comp.lang.cobol and alt.cobol:

›› I have the following PC software package for sale:
›› 
…[24 more quoted lines elided]…
›› BEST OFFER OVER $20 TAKES THE ENTIRE THING!

The package originally cost me over $300, but I no longer work
with COBOL, and I wanted to get it to someone who would have a use
for it. Andrew Freeman E-mailed me a high bid of $85. I shipped the
software package to him COD. Unfortunately, I neglected to check the box
marked "Cash Only" in the UPS shipping form. Andrew Freeman paid for the
software with a personal check. When I tried to deposit the check, it was
returned to me marked "Insufficient funds". I telephoned Andrew Freeman,
and he apologized for the problem, saying that he would send me a cashier's
check for $97, $85 + $12 for the fee for the bad check. In the several
weeks since that conversation, I have not received the cashier's check,
and attempts to contact Andrew Freeman by telephone or E-mail have been
unsuccessful.

Now, this is definately my problem, I was foolish enough to
accept a personal check, even if by oversight. I am certainly not
rich and will miss the $97. What is almost as upsetting though is the
abuse of the Internet that Andrew Freeman has perpetrated here. It's
stuff like this that may kill the Usenet newsgroups and the Internet.

Lessons that I have learned, and hopefully everyone can benefit
from:

1) When buying or selling something on the Internet, structure
the transaction to protect the buyer and the seller. COD
(Cash On Delivery) is the preferred method. If you are
purchasing something, and are afraid to pay COD, only to
then open a box filled with shredded newspaper, specify that
the item be shipped via the US Postal Service. If you do
get stuck, you can complain to your local Postmaster. The
US Postal Service does not take kindly to mail fraud. I may
still have some slim chance of recourse this way, since the
check from Andrew Freeman was delivered from UPS to me by the
Post Office.

2) United Parcel Service will NOT help you at all in this matter.

If anyone out there is selling something to Andrew Laverne
Freeman, take cash ONLY! He did mention to me in one E-mail message that
he wanted to use my COBOL package to upgrade to the current MICROFOCUS
COBOL package, so if any software vendor or someone from MICROFOCUS is
reading this, BEWARE!

>From what I gathered from the E-mail messages that he and I
exchanged before the sale, Andrew Freeman may be some kind of consultant.
If anyone out there may be a client of his, BEWARE! Obviously, this
person is dishonest. If he would be working at your place of business,
he probably would have no moral problem with walking away with anything
that he could get his hands on. I would also be extremely wary of any
products that he would deliver. In my opinion, if he would cheat someone
on a relatively trivial matter such as this, he would probably have no
moral qualms with sticking someone else with a substandard product.
If he works for you, or you are or will be considering employing him for
something, BE FORWARNED!! Personally, I could never have a person like
this working for me.

As a final note, based on the one telephone conversation that I
did have with him, Andrew Freeman is not some 15-year old kid, up to
some hijinks. He's an adult, who knows and understands what he is doing.

To everyone else who bid on the MS-COBOL package, my apologies.
I feel bad that it didn't go to someone who deserved it.

I hope that this experience is somehow helpful to anyone out there
buying or selling items on the Internet. Good luck.

Lou Avrami ( attmail!lavrami )
