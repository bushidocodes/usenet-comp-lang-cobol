Idcams is a JCL utility (JCL = Job Control Language, the underlying
embedding of your Cobol programs on MVS). It is the utility you need
to create and handle VSAM files.

There are hardly any books on JCL. The best ones are:
Gary DeWard Brown - System 390 JCL; 4th edition; Wiley (www.wiley.
  com/compbooks/) and
Doug Lowe - MVS JCL; 2d edition; Murach.
Of course you can find details about both books at www.amazon.com.

The manual you probably want is "Access Method Services for ICF
Catalogs", not the one for VSAM catalogs.  VSAM Catalogs are obsolete
(they are non-Y2K compliant and are designed to refuse to work after
1999-12-31).  VSAM Catalogs have been functionally stabilized for years
with all enhancements only going to ICF (Integrated Catalog Facility)
catalog structures.  If you are at a shop that still uses any VSAM
Catalogs, they have barely 9 months to convert.

The best resource I know of for the beginner is VSAM: Access Method
Services and Application Programming, by Doug Lowe (the same author
that wrote the JCL book mentioned in another post on this thread),
published by Mike Murach & Associates (www.murach.com)
This book covers the majority of what you will use in VSAM.  IBM's
manual, also already mentioned, will get you the rest of the
information you need.

There is also another book, also by Murach, and recently revised, VSAM
for the COBOL Programmer.  This book is shorter than the first, and
leaves out the section on creating MVS catalogs and using VSAM with
MVS Assembler, and has been re-written in a more modern style, and
appears to concentrate solely on interaction with COBOL programs; but
I don't own it and haven't read it, so I can't vouch for it.

(When I say re-written, I mean in the last ten years, some of the
Murach books date back to the middle eighties, although they're still
good; they've just released new editions of their first DB2 book, and
another is coming in a couple of months.  Hopefully, this will signal
a complete mainframe book line revision.)
