Ah- an advocate of the tyranny of the majority. The simple answer is "no."
The more complex answer requires a rather long ethical review of what
one considers "rights" vs. "privileges."

When a government is protecting a person (*a* person, as in singular!) in regard to their
rights, it is doing its job.

When a government is granting groups of people, or even individuals "privileges", it pretty much has grown into some kind of
oppressive monster, whether on the left end or the right end of the scale. Individuals, and their rights, fall smack dab right into
the middle of that scale, and that is
exactly where a government is least effective in controlling the everyday lives of a person.

As to states and their rights, I personally think that states are too large too, and those rights
should be held at a very local level. That way people can always move to another locality more to their liking.  Remember our
states, at least many of them, have larger budgets and more
politicians than many countries!

-Paul
