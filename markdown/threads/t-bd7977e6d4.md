(personal notes follow - there is NOTHING (except quoted text) official in
these)

NOTE WELL:
   It is my understanding that the U.S. (via ANSI) will be required to
submit its ballot to ISO *before* the Sept 9, 2002 deadline for comments
from this U.S. (ANSI) Public Review.  Therefore, if you want your comments
to be received before the U.S. votes, then I suggest you send any comments
as SOON AS POSSIBLE.  In fact (and I could be totally mistaken on this), I
believe that at least an "initial" U.S. position has already been
considered.  Therefore, I don't know if "public review" comments received
immediately will or will not have any impact on the ultimate disposition of
this document as either an ANSI or ISO Standard.

NOTE 2:
 Although I *think* that this document is available for purchase (18USD)
from anywhere, the information on Public Review comments *only* applies to
ANSI (the U.S.  national standards body).  If you want to participate in the
"review process" of some other country, I suggest you contact your own
national body.  How to do this can be found at:
  http://std.dkuug.dk/JTC1/SC22/

by selecting the appropriate link from the line that reads
  "To obtain the international standards from SC22, please contact your
national member body."
In the past, ANSI has accepted "public review comments" from those outside
the US, so you MAY want to send any comments to BOTH your own national
standards body *and* to ANSI (per the instructions above).
