Hi Daniel,
I hope that this helps:

COMPUTATIONAL-2

Specifies a four-byte binary integer. Bits 1-8 of four successive bytes 
are treated as a contiguous 32-bit binary item. Bit 0 of each byte is 
ignored.

examples:

Decimal -> Octal

1234   ->  000 000 002 322

-1234   ->   377 377 373 056

=-=-=-=-=

 From this, taking what I know about COMP in the environment with which 
I'm most familiar, I believe you could infer that Pic S9(09) COMP would 
be 4 characters (which, I believe, is two words - 32 bits).

S9(09) COMPUTATIONAL

The item is byte-aligned (occupying an integral number of bytes) and 
right-justified. If the length of the item including the sign is odd, 
the COBOL compiler generates a halfbyte filler in the leading byte. If 
the item is signed, a minus (trailing) sign is represented by octal 15, 
a plus sign by octal 13.

examples:

COMPUTATIONAL  PICTURE   S9(5)

1234   ->   001 043 113

-1234   ->  001 043 115
COMPUTATIONAL  PICTURE   S9(6)

01234   ->  000 001 043 113

-1234   ->   000 001 043 115

  S9(9) COMP on MVS :
    1234=X'000004D2' 
   -1234=X'FFFFFB2E'
 
  COMP-2 on MVS :
     1234=X'434D200000000000'

COBOL Data description 
BULL                nearest equivalent
