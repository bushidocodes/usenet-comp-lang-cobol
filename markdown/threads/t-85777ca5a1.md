ISPF is Interactive Structured Programming Facility. It's first
incarnation was simply called SPF and did not have a full-screen editor,
only a line editor. Many installations used a third-party add-on editor
called FSE (Full-Screen Edit). The I was added to SPF (along with it's
present editor) sometime in the mid-1980's.

Things get pretty confusing when you try explaining the difference between
PDF and ISPF to non-IBM mainframe people - especially since historically
they have been together, been apart, and are now back together again.

Historically, PDF was the development tool/environment in which option 2 was
the editor. (Other options allowed foreground and background compiles,
library utilities - and a host of other things).

Again historically, ISPF provided the "dialog" services with which PDF was
written - and which most mainframe products provided interfaces for a common
"look and feel" - long before this became an issue on the PC. In fact,
although PDF was almost universal on MVS, many VM sites got ISPF but not PDF
(and stuck to XEDIT and its macros for editing and tool development).

Today, the products have come together and are both part of what you get
when you get OS/390 (the current MVS operating system).
