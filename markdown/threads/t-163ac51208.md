Suppose you had a field that contained six bytes of alphanumeric data,
On every line the field can be begin with one or more letters
followed by either 1) one or more blanks or 2) one or more zeros
followed by at least one digit 1-9 followed by none, one or more spaces.

123456

CR10
CR   1
C    1
L1
L 2
L 20
L  21
VR 100
VR  10
VR   9

Suppose you wanted to compact these values, squeezing out all blanks
and *leading* zeros. Blanks padding out to the right are OK. Resulting
in:

CR10
CR1
C1
L1
L2
L20
L21
VR100
VR10
VR9

This would be pretty easy in some other languages but text-processing is
not COBOL's strong suite. So, any ideas on how to accomplish this?

I would create two fields which are redefined as array of characters. 
Move your input field to one of these and spaces to the other.

Then do the following perform loops:
Move character from one array to the other array incrementing pointers,
stop when no longer alpha.
Increment the input pointer until numeric & > 0
Move character from one array to the other, incrementing pointers.
Either stop with space or stop at end.

Here's my reply:

01  work-fields.
      05  in-field.
            10  in-char pic x occurs 6 indexed by in-position.
      05  out-field.
             10  out-char pic x occurs 6 indexed by out-position.
      05  digit-found pic x.
.
.
*    assuming that in-field contains the string and out-field is what you
want
     move 'n' to digit-found.
     move spaces to out-field.
     set out-position to 1.
     perform varying in-position from 1 by 1 until in-position > 6
           if  in-char (in-position) = space
           or in-char (in-position) = zero and digit-found = 'n'
                 continue
           else
                 move in-char (in-position) to out-char (out-position)
                 set out-position up by 1
                 if in-char (in-position) numeric
                     move 'y' to digit-found
                 end-if
            end-if
       end-perform.

this should effectively skip all spaces and zeroes in advance of other
digits.  I don't think this is particularly difficult in COBOL either.

With in-line performs and reference modification, text processing in cobol is
just as easy, and very similar to, doing it in Basic.

MOVE ZERO TO SUB2
MOVE SPACES TO RESULT-STRING
PERFORM VARYING SUB1 FROM 1 BY 1
        UNTIL SUB1 > LENGTH OF RESULT-STRING
   IF SOURCE-STRING(SUB1:1) > ZERO
      MOVE SOURCE-STRING(SUB1:1) TO RESULT-STRING(SUB2:1)
   END-IF
END-PERFORM

That squeezes out your spaces and shows how easy string processing is in
cobol.  I'll leave it to you to flesh out how to drop the leading zeros.
