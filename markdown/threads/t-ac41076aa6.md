Mr. Lewis,
It looks to me like you're facing the addition of ^M (carriage
return) characters at the end of each line.... if that IS the problem,
you can easily remove them in vi with the following command-mode
command:
first go to the command mode(colon) prompt by entering a colon (:)
then enter
%s/^V^M//g
The % indicates that all lines will be processed and the s is the
substitute command. The control-V indicates to vi to take the next
character as a literal control-M, (which is a carriage return).
The final result is that all ^M characters are replaced by nothing.

(I have used a variety of simple text editors (and some not so simple)
on PCs and transferred the text up to UNIX. UNIX always sees the ^M
at the end of every text line....)

Good luck
