After seeing the term "corporate investment in cobol" a few times, it seems
a good idea to extend the point. Most applications in some companies  are
expensed not taken as assets. Should the management become fully aware of
the full investment in IT projects, it might find a new way to book the
efforts of application development and maintenance. In that case, the
"picture of the advisability of using a newer compiler or language" might be
better understood. This might also support more planning, and wiser use of
facilities. Our book keeping for a period  proved that there were
measureable savings on each project. The sum total over time showed a very
large increase in the bottom line. Though an accounting major with some
experience, I was never sure the book keeping was not wallpaper to get the
job done.

I have no doubt that with the use of a standard language there were
substantial savings. How to measure them well seems to
excape us.  I did not have full access to the books.

Warren Simmons
