A) Most IBM mainframe shops used "static" calls with older COBOL compilers.
(Various performance and other reasons - that I won't go into)

B) When VS COBOL II first was introduced ("replacing" both DOS/VS COBOL
(VSE) and OS/VS COBOL (MVS & VM)) - one of the MAJOR complaints was
"performance" - particularly performance on CALLs (dynamic and static) - but
DYNAMIC Calls were a "disaster".  By about release 3.1, this began to be
improved - but it really took until LE before even vaguely comparable
dynamic CALL performance was reported.

The major "design" flaw (as I recall) with the earlier versions was how much
of the COBOL environment was "built" with each CALL.  This was particularly
true when CALLing from OS/VS COBOL to VS COBOL II or vice versa.  There were
"performance APARs" throughout the history of VS COBOL II but I don't think
the newsgroup really cares about this (now historical) trivia.
