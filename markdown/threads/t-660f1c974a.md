Then Micro Focus is violating the 1985 ANSI (i.e. current) standard,
section 4.2.2.1.3, viz:

"Reserved words satisfy the following conditions:
(1)  ....[does not apply]
(2)  ....[does not apply]
(3)  Reserved words do not start with 1 or 2 characters followed by '-'
except for the reserved words I-O, I-O-CONTROL, and reserved words which
begin with 'B-' or 'DB-'.
(4)  Reserved words do not contain two or more contiguous hyphens."

Are these rules being 'discontinued' in the new standard?  The 4.2.2.1.3
rule exists so that COBOL programmers may design sets of user-words that are
guaranteed not to be reserved words.

Yes, this is true when the compiler implementor is implementing a
system-name, a COBOL word used to communicate with the operating
environment.  The standard is silent about the implementor inventing new
reserved words (which all COBOL vendors have and will continue to do).  My
personal opinion is that a compiler implementor, when inventing a reserved
word (which by definition is not a system-name), should observe the cited
rules in the ANSI standard.

way, as do other vendors.
