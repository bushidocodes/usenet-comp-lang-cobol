Here's a guess. Perhaps your COBOLII compiles are defaulting to
AMODE(31), whereas the old compiles were AMODE(24). This is labelled
'running above the line', and relates to the core addresses your
programs can use. When we first started doing COBOLII we ran into a
problem calling Assembler subroutines (which run AMODE(24) - below the
line). Programs would compile nicely but then abend at runtime (I
forget the abend code). The DL/I interface modules are assembler, hence
my guess.
One stab at a solution is to add a compile option AMODE(24) to your
COBOL II compiles and try it again. There are some efficiency aspects of
such a decision, beyond my meager knowledge.
If this turns out to be the problem, there may be a workaround provided
by IBM to let you use DL/I above the line - contact your SE.

The AMODE stuff came into the limelight when machines got big enough to
handle more than 6-byte addresses in their memory. I'm sure some real
expert here can explain that whole thing if anyone is curious.

Then again, the above may be totally unrelated to your problem.

You might want to list the abend code, and what sort of statement it
abends on (i.e., at the beginning of the Procedure division, or at some
particular DL/I statement) if you do a subsequent post on this.

John
