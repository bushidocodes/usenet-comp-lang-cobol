The following program demonstrates a problem I encountered while
converting old IBM code to ANSI 85 for a computer that uses ASCII.
Of particular interest are lines 18 thru 21.

=====
      $set charset "ebcdic" badsigns apost
     1 identification division.
     2 program-id. hex-demo.
     3*Demonstrates 'abuse' of COBOL language on IBM <g>
     4 data division.
     5 working-storage section.
     6 01  hex-table value '0123456789ABCDEF'.
     7     03  hex-char pic x occurs 16.
     8 01  hex-value pic 99.
     9 01  cnt pic 9(4) comp.
    10 procedure division.
    11 begin.
    12     perform display-table
    13     varying cnt from 1 by 1
    14     until cnt > 16
    15     goback
    16     .
    17 display-table.
    18     move 0 to hex-value
    19     move hex-char (cnt) to hex-value (2:1)
    20     if hex-char (cnt) not numeric
    21         add 9 to hex-value
    22     end-if
    23     display quote hex-char (cnt) quote space hex-value
    24     .
===== [results for EBCDIC]
['0' thru '7' deleted as clear]
'8' 08
'9' 09
'A' 10
'B' 11
'C' 12
'D' 13
'E' 14
'F' 15
=====

Now, assuming that 'hex-value' (line 8) must be maintained
as is, what remediation is required to use ASCII characters?

I added the following similar code before line 21.
=====
               move function char (function ord (hex-value (2:1))
                   - function ord ("A") + 1 + function ord ("0"))
                   to hex-value (2:1)
===== [results for ASCII]
["0" thru "7" deleted as clear]
"8" 08
"9" 09
"A" 10
"B" 11
"C" 12
"D" 13
"E" 14
"F" 15
=====

The question then is: What are 'good', platform-independent
ways for converting hexadecimal characters to numeric values?

The language did not allow it. Neither did the language
specifically prohibit it. But it does violate the allowable
content for unsigned numeric display; which for IBM is X"F0"
thru X"F9".

Here is the actual code:
=====
             05  WK-NUMBER             PICTURE 99.
             05  FILLER                REFEFINES WK-NUMBER.
               07  FILLER              PICTURE X.
               07  WK-NUM-UNIT         PICTURE X.
       ...
      1    MOVE ZERO TO WK-NUMBER.
      2    MOVE DWR-TEXT-COL (TX-NEXT) TO WK-NUM-UNIT.
      3    IF X5 IS EQUAL TO 16,
      4        AND WK-NUM-UNIT IS NOT LESS THAN 'A',
      5        AND IS NOT GREATER THAN THAN 'F',
      6        ADD 9 TO WK-NUMBER.
=====
Line 1 places valid values in 'wk-number', i.e., x"F0F0".
Line 2 indirectly moves a potentially invalid character into 'wk-
number',
       specifically a character in the range x"C1" thru x"C6".
Line 6 gives an undefined result.

The undefined result is valid on IBM and can be made valid
elsewhere. However, with the 2002 standard, line 6 will give an
"EC-DATA-INCOMPATIBLE exception". So, the language now provides
a specific exception condition for what was once undefined.

    evaluate hex-char

=====
       identification division.
       program-id. hex-sub.
       environment division.
       configuration section.
       object-computer. cobol-85
           sequence hexadecimal-alphabet.
       special-names.
           alphabet hexadecimal-alphabet "0" thru "9"
               "A" also "a" "B" also "b" "C" also "c"

       data division.
       linkage section.
       01  hex-char pic x.
       01  hex-value pic 99.
       procedure division using hex-char hex-value.
       begin.
           compute hex-value = function ord (hex-char) - 1
           exit program
           .
