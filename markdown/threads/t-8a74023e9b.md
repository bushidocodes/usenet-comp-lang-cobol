IBM introduced some '85 standard features with VS COBOL II R1.0 back in 84 or so. R1 and R2 of VS COBOL II WERE not ANSI 85 compilers, but were 74 compliant products with many elements of what became the 85 standard included as IBM extensions.

With R3.0 of VS COBOL II full compliance to the '85 standard was in the product (so long as the NOCMPR2 option was specified at compile time).

I beleive that reference modification was provided in R1.0 or R1.1 - both of which had very limited distribution. R2.0 had much more distribution, but has disappeared from the marketplace.

R3.x had lots of presence in the marketplace, some of which are still in use (unsupported products). The only release of VS COBOL II that has support is 4.0.

All of these features are also available in COBOL/370 and COBOL for MVS and VM.

Rex Widmer
