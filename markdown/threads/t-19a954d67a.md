I disagree with part of your assessment. While it is true that people in
this forum are biased towards COBOL, asking for their honest opinions does
not hurt.

The point about personal development is more important than your career is
also somewhat misstated but correct. Your personal development (i.e. keeping
up in the field of programming) is important for your career. Is there a
future in COBOL, for now yes there is. Will there always be is up for
speculation. Is it good to know it, yes but you should diversify your
skillset to other languages. BASIC, COBOL, C++ etc. are all languages that
you should learn and how they interact with database programs such as DB2,
Oracle and Access. At some point in your career you may end up being a
Database Administrator, Systems Analyst or something else entirely. Think of
programming as a starting point, not an ending point. COBOL as a language is
still strong in the market, but limited to Mainframes for the most part. C++
and Java tend to be more cross platform and BASIC is just downright fun (and
still used for various applications in the market as well).

The person that wrote the response below is correct in that you must take
responsibility for your own decisions and actions. How you chart your future
depends upon the decisions you make. But it does not hurt to know COBOL
since it is still used.
