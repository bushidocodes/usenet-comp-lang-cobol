```
I am using NetExpress as my interface, trying to get a fairly simple
program to work correctly.  One of the problems I have is with the
following code:

01 STUDENT-RECORD.
    05 SR-RCD                   PIC 9(2).
    05 FILLER                   PIC X(3)  VALUE SPACES.
    05 SR-STUDENT-NAME.
        10 SR-FIRST-LETTER      PIC X(1).
        10 SR-NAME-REMAIN       PIC X(19).
    05 FILLER                   PIC X(2)  VALUE SPACES.
    05 SR-CORR-ANS              PIC 9(3).
    05 FILLER                   PIC X(11) VALUE SPACES.
    05 SR-CTRL-NUMBER.
        10 SR-CTRL-NON-CHECK.
            15 SR-CTRL-1        PIC 9(1).
            15 SR-CTRL-2        PIC 9(1).
            15 SR-CTRL-3        PIC 9(1).
            15 SR-CTRL-4        PIC 9(1).
            15 SR-CTRL-5        PIC 9(1).
        10 SR-CTRL-CHECK        PIC X(1).
    05 FILLER                   PIC X(32) VALUE SPACES.
           
I show the whole variable for completeness sake, but I'm having a
problem with the level 15 variables.  In my test case, SR-CTRL-NUMBER
is equal to 654321, SR-CTRL-NON-CHECK is equal to 65432, SR-CTRL-1 is
equal to 6, and SR-CTRL-CHECK is equal to 1, all of which is correct. 
However, none of the other SR-CTRL-# numbers have the correct value. 
They have the following values:

2 = >
3 = 9
4 = 0
5 = 5

I'm having problems with other variables in the program as well.  Has
anyone seen or heard of something like this?  I'm fairly new to the
language, so I could be missing something relatively obvious.  Oh, I'm
using the compiler's run-time value displaying ability to look at the
variable's values, in case that makes any difference.

Thanks for any pointers.

Mason
```

```
My guess is that the problem is that you have a line sequential input file,
but have allowed the Select/Assign to default to "record sequential".  Try
putting  "Organization is Line Sequential" in your input file's
Select/Assign clause and see if that fixes your problem.
```
