Docdwarf, in a later post did get it correct.  CSP stands for Cross
Systems Product.  It is an IBM SAA specific language it runs on all of
their platforms from PC-DOS to MVS and everything in the middle.

I'm going to guess that you *DO* know what COBOL is and you *do not*
know what CSP is.

CSP is a rather simple language to learn, provided you know another
programming language.  I never heard of it until I took a contract with
a company that used it.  Within a week or so, I was productive in it.

It has COBOL-like syntax, however with some C overtones, as most of it
has been written in C (from what I have been told).  Here in the USA it
did not do to well but apparently in Europe it is much more popular.

If you can do a DejaNews search, do one for this newsgroup as I
answered this question just a few weeks ago.
