CCI is not suitable for this. The CCI protocol is layered on top of
TCP (or IPX/whatever) just like FTP. You can't make them talk to each
other.

The session ID is (as I recall) assigned by the ccitcp2 daemon --
it's internal to the CCI implementation.

If you want to talk to a terminal emulator using TCP/IP, the telnet
protocol is probably a better bet, btw.

Have a look at the RFC that describes the telnet protocol -- I don't
have the number handy at the moment, but it shouldn't be too hard to
find. I think it's probably more appropriate to consider telnet as a
sort of educated data transfer protocol rather than as something that
allows you to log in to other systems -- that's just what the telnet
daemon does if you specifically connect to it. I quite often use the
telnet program to connect to the mail transfer or USENET daemons for
debugging purposes when there's no concept of logging on. If you can get
your NT application to send data in a format compatible with telnet then
you'll probably have everything else come out in the wash.
