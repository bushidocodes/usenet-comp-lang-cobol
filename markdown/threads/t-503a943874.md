I used Fujitsu COBOL for my finals at college this year. COBOL dead? Normal
courses use an old Compiler (IBM 370 and Digital VAX) but our group got the
permission to experiment with Windows and COBOL.
We used PowerCOBOL 3.0 as supplied with COBOL for Dummies, ISDG Books
(sorry, but this way the compiler was affordable). There is a lot of
difference in legacy COBOL and we wasted a lot of time experimenting and
searching because we were completely on our own.
Now Windows COBOL is a major attraction on my curriculum. I want to help the
other students by combining some Fujitsu COBOL tips in a small written
course. Perhaps even some unofficial training if interest is high enough.
I ask for the readers to know regular COBOL. Most have no experience in
Visual Basic or anything.

*     If anyone has suggestions about the contents, please send them to my
email (I am not on-line that often to follow newsgroups)
*    The course will be in Dutch but if someone wants to read it (still in
very early stages)
*    Job offers in Belgium are also welcome :)

All languages are dead, just as we all are.  (obviously I mean this in
the same way as you are asking if Cobol is dead - we & Cobol are
productive today, but someday will die).  Sure Cobol will die out.  I
would be hard pressed to pick a language that will last longer than
Cobol will though.  It wasn't too long ago when colleges taught Pascal
as the language of the future.  The environments which Visual Basic and
Java have been designed to fit into are changing rapidly.  If the
environments are changing, then it seems likely that the tools won't fit
as well in the future.

Learn to like whatever tool (programming language) you can get.  If you
learn to be a good Cobol programmer, your biggest challenge may be to
get your employer to allow you to work on the Java project coming up as
well.  You want to be skilled in a variety of tools - and more
importantly, have a good picture at what you want to accomplish with
those tools (solutions which help your organization achieve its
objectives)
