### Standard, Portable, and calling a "rose a rose"

(Sent by Bill Klein to the comp.lang.cobol newsgroup with CC's to the J4 and
WG4 distribution lists.)

This is a fairly LONG note, but if you are interested in what is in the next
COBOL Standard - and most particularly what is in it on every conforming
implementation, I would really ask you to read this note and respond to it
(to the email IDs listed at the bottom.)

I thought that I would post this to the newsgroup to get "user reaction" to
a rather interesting situation with the draft Standard.

At the last WG4 (ISO working group for COBOL) meeting there was reported (I
wasn't there) significant discussion about "optional" vs "system dependent"
vs "required" vs "other" items. For members of the newsgroup who don't know
about this, the current draft (unlike the '85 and earlier Standards) has
*no* "optional" features - except for items that are also OBSOLETE and
intended to be removed from the next Standard (for example, what used to be
called the "Communications Module"). This was decided on early in the
development process and was intended to enhance "portability" of COBOL code
written to conform to the Standard. It was also based on the assumption
that in today's environment that "size and complexity" issues that used to
make it possible for a mainframe compiler to support things that wouldn't
fit on a PC really are non-issues today.

Another piece of background you need to know is that what used to be called
"hardware dependent" is now called "system dependent".

According to J4 document, J4/98-0337 - "Internationalization and
miscellaneous fixes", the following discussion point is made

"The conformance for processor-dependent items needs to be updated to
reflect the consensus at the Monterey WG4 meeting that it is up to the
implementor to declare which processor dependent items are supported for
COBOL. There was consensus that an implementor could use marketing
considerations as criteria for deciding which processor-dependent items to
support"

It then goes on to propose (this paper hasn't been approved yet - but this
certainly does match what I have heard WG4 requested),

"1. Page 4, 3.1.5, Processor-dependent language elements,

a. add the following as the 3rd sentence (after the sentence ending "...
claims support."):

"The decision of whether to claim support support for a processor-dependent
component is entirely within an implementor's discretion. Factors that may
be considered include, but are not limited to, hardware capability, software
capability, and market positioning."

**************

OK - now to what I want feedback on.

It seems to me that this new definition of what "processor dependent" means
is simply making anything in the Standard that is now called "processor
dependent" into what used to be called OPTIONAL. This has NOTHING to do
with the processor and everything to do with what the implementor WANTS to
include in their compiler.

To me, what users expect from "processor dependent" is something that would
say (for example - and not worded in "Standardize") -

"An implementor shall document which features of the COBOL language
designated as 'processor dependent" are available in their implementation
and this designation is entirely up to each implementor. HOWEVER, should
any implementor provide support for a feature on the same hardware with the
same non-COBOL software and operating system, then this feature must be
supported by all implementors and cannot be claimed to be "processor
dependent"."

In other words, to be processor dependent, it must NOT be dependent upon
what the COBOL implementor wants to do - but what their hardware and
operating system/software actually CAN support.

If the Standards committees really want to add back the ability for
implementors to decide whether or not to support something based on
"marketing forces" - then they need to add back the OPTIONAL feature and
admit that portability is not as important as writing a Standard that
pleases the implementors' interpretation of their users desire.

It should be noted that it is never made clear WHICH implementor gets to
decide what the market place wants. What this means (for example),

If you are running a COBOL compiler produced by vendorA on an operating
system provided by vendorB using a file-system provided by vendorC accessing
files on hardware provided by vendorD, then (apparently) ANY of those
"implementors" can decide that some feature of file handling doesn't have
"market demand" and can say not to implement it as "processor dependent".
As far as I can tell, this means that almost EVERY "processor dependent"
feature will be pretty unportable.

,,,,,,,

What can you do about this?

It really is NOT J4's "fault" that this change is being made to the draft
Standard. They are following the WG4 direction. Therefore, if you think
that this is a bad idea, you need to communicate this to your "national body
representative" and not just to J4.

If you are in the US, then you can send your comments (pro or con) to the US
head of delegation at
d.··.@mic··s.com

If you are not in the US, then contact the WG4 convenor at
nwa··.@us.··m.com
to find out how to reach your national body.
