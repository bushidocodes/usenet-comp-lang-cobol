We are assisting in the preparation and re-writing of a university level
cobol course. The current goal of the current course is the instruction
of COBOL to undergraduates. The paper we are writing looks at possible
new alternatives that the course may take.

The two major changes that are being considered are:
a) teaching the conversion of COBOL to another language,
such as C++, Visual Basic or other 4GLs.

b) Teching the maintenance, repair and upgrading of COBOL
to COBOL, perhaps porting the new code to a workstation
environment.

What we are looking for on the internet is information about
what is currently in use in the workplace
with respect to:
1. COBOL to COBOL conversion
2. COBOL to other language conversion
- what languages are popular
for this type of conversion?
3. Since there is only one spot on our
calander for this course, would it
be better to be looking towards
conversion or maintenance (or a 50/50
split)?

Any ideas along any of these lines would be appreciated.
Thanks for your time and ideas.
Alan Dedman
Kevin Waddell
Computer Science
University of Regina
