Hello J

I would suggest you consider the Fujitsu version 3 student edition
which was downloadable from their website last time I looked, it is
free, but comes with some licensing restrictions that stop you from
distributing executable programs for profit, you would have to look at
them in detail to be sure exactly.  This compiler runs under Windows
98SE and other Windows variants, though there may be some problems with
XP, which may be surmountable with help from the manual and this group.
 As is to be expected for a freebie, it is not as up to date as their
more recent offerings.

It is a quite comprehensive implementation of the ANSI 85 standard with
lots of extensions with a bias towards compatibility with IBM mainframe
compilers, though you would have to check the details and options
available to be sure that what you write is directly transferable to
the AS400 compilers.

There are some other free compilers, Bill Klein's FAQ is a
comprehensive though somewhat out of date source of information and
amongst other items lists some or all of the various compiler
suppliers.

I would suggest that whichever compiler you choose to use, you try to
avoid the non-standard extensions since the ability to transfer
programs and data between platforms is much more important these days.

I am afraid to say that I don't know much about the LE and XLE
environments, but the manuals are on IBM's website and are free for
downloading.  Others in this group could probably provide further
advice.

With regards

Robert
