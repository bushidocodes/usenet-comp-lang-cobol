I like the analogy to building a lift/elevator - it takes time. Those
who have been at COBOL a long time have learned to capture 'repetitive'
routines to be used as copyfiles or called sub-programs.

In this respect OO COBOL is no different BUT, for overall success it
does DEMAND lots of prior planning, which involves clearing the building
site, engineers/draughtsmen designing/drawing the building/elevator
specification - all this before the 'grunts' get down to the actual
construction. A lot of prior work - but the benefits show up in spades
later from this initial planning.

Pete Dashwood has illustrated the efficacy of Components. Same applies
with OO generally; arrive at a common design structure, say for editing,
printing, file/DB access, (the 'repetitives') and it makes the magic
word 'reusability' a truism.
