MAGNA X CLIENT/SERVER APPLICATION DEVELOPMENT
ENVIRONMENT NOW SUPPORTS TRANSARCUS ENCINA

Transarc to Distribute MAGNA X


La Jolla, CA, DECORUM -- February 7, 1995 -- Magna Software and Transarc
Corporation today announced a version of MAGNA X that enables the simplified
development of Encina-based applications. The two companies have also signed an agreement
whereby Transarc will distribute the MAGNA X second generation client/server
application development environment. An Early Entry Program for Encina users
will begin on April 1, 1995.

According to Dr. Alfred Spector, president and CEO of Transarc, MAGNA X fills a
critical need for the traditional MIS manager looking to minimize the risks
involved in migrating to Encina. It can be easily learned by experienced COBOL
programmers, enabling developers to quickly generate Encina applications. This means
that a familiar development path now exists to create mission-critical
applications for client/server environments.

Kent Lawson, president of Magna, added, Mainframe customers can now shorten the
learning curve for building robust Encina applications by using a familiar
programming style. And, they can leverage their recent investments in Visual
Basic and PowerBuilder to extend those products for development of large-scale transactional
systems.S He concludes, with MAGNA X for Encina, weve brought together the
lowest risk method of developing high performance transaction processing
systems, with the leading standards-based open TP monitor.

Pricing and Availability
Transarc will immediately start marketing MAGNA X to its Encina customers
through its Transarc Sales Group. MAGNA X for Encina has a base price of
$40,000. This price includes a development server license and five developer
workstation licenses. For the Early Entry Program, the price is $20,000.
What does MAGNA X offer?
MAGNA X is the only application development tool that allows IS to immediately
leverage the skills and knowledge of mainframe developers to create
business-critical, high volume client/server applications. By taking advantage
of the investment that has been made in proven technologies such as
PowerBuilder, Visual Basic, COBOL and CICS, MAGNA X shortens the time to market
for powerful second generation client/server systems that integrate PCs, UNIX
servers and the mainframe.



What does Encina provide?
Encina is the industry leader for DCE-based, large-scale, client/server
computing and OLTP solutions. It provides platform-independent transactional
integrity, record-oriented file storage, X/Open standard database integration
and secure mainframe connectivity.

About the companies
Magna Software Corporation, of New York City, has 14 years experience as a
supplier of application development tools to MIS developers. Magna has an
additional office in Washington D.C. and a European affiliate company in Paris.
MAGNA X is the third major application development product produced by Magna
Software. Its first product was a fourth generation language for Control Data
mainframes that dominated its market in the early 1980s. The second product was
an application generator for the Groupe Bull mainframe series that has been licensed
by 45% of Groupe Bull mainframe sites in the U.S. and 25% of the Groupe Bull
sites worldwide.

Transarc Corporation is a leader in client/server computing solutions for the
enterprise, and a major DCE developer and distributor. Transarc, a wholly-owned
subsidiary of IBM, is based in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. Transarc distributes
its products through its Transarc Sales Group, with offices throughout North
America, and through a network of international resellers. Encina products are
available worldwide through most leading systems vendors.
