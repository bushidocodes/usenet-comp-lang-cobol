I have a question for those programmers that are NOT located
in Georgia.  

It's been my experience here in the metro-Atlanta area that 
companies go through programmers like water through
a seive.  The company hires a programmer then within
90-120 days fires them.  That was not my experience while
I was in Kansas City, Missouri.

I myself have been fired more than just several times since
moving to Atlanta..  I recommened to one employer that during 
the time we re-wrote the code, why not take care of the Y2K 
problem that everyone's going to face in a few years anyway.  He
called me in the next morning and I was out looking for a job 10
minutes later.  Georgia is not a "right-to-work" state so getting
a straight and honest answer from an employer hasn't worked
either for me or others.  It appears that being either laid-off
or being fired frequently goes along with the job title!!

It's just not me, I've talked to other COBOL programmers
and my experience has been the same as theirs.  Just 
curious about what is going through these employers
minds when they hire someone?  It would seem to me
that once you have a programmer on staff, then get him/her
trained you would want to keep them.  Not here in Atlanta!?!

I don't honestly know.  I spent well over 20 years working
for the Government, then retired and went out in the
private market.  So far, it's been a real and total bitch
both to find a job, as well as keep one over 120 days.


Roger
