```
The company from which I am retired contracted me in 1998 to get their
systems ready for Y2K.

Although the company had an IBM S-390 they decided against migrating to the
new COBOL at that time because they did not want to add complexities to
getting ready for Y2K.

The company now wants to contract me to migrate their DOS COBOL programs to
COBOL/VSE.  The old compiler was the '74 standard but the company used the
LANGLVL(!) CBL option for all compiles so that programs written during the
sixties and early seventies would not have to be changed.

I have been reading the IBM migration manuals and have not seen anything
that looks overwhelming.

I would appreciate hearing about any unexpected problems encountered by
anyone who migrated from DOS COBOL to COBOL/VSE.

Thanks,
Leon
```

```
None of the problems are particularly overwhelming, especially if the shop
had some reasonable standards and adhered reasonably well to them.   Getting
the LE and COBOL default parameters established properly early on is
important (e.g. the way you specify parms on the execute card is, by
default, NOT the way you're used to doing it but the default can be
changed).  Many programs should simply require a recompile (YMMV).

You may wish to consider the CCCA product from IBM if you find you have a
lot of code to convert which requires manual intervention.
```

```
"show stoppers".  I did find IBM COBOL for VSE/ESA Migration Guide

browsing a lot of lines of code. More grunt work than thought inducing though.
```
