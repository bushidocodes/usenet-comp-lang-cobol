Although not specifically with any brand compiler, I've actually seen a fair
amount of this kind of thing. Many "old" MS-DOS programs .. especially
games... have had problems when the CPU speed gets to numbers significantly
greater than the those on the systems available when the software was first
written.

Because "games" seem to be the lion's share of these 'CPU too fast'
problems, and many games were created by 'one-timer-programmers,'  I
attribute this to sloppy programming.. not performing a 'wait' when one
should (e.g., video refresh) ... which did not cause a problem on slower
machines which simply could not get to the next instruction fast enough to
make a difference.

My empirical evidence for this view is precisely ZERO.  I believe it
nontheless.

MCM
