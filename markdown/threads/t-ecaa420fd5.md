Randy Graff wrote:
>Who invented COBOL???

Nobody knows for sure, the information was lost in WWI.  But
we are pretty sure it was one of three guys: Charles, Bob or
Larry.  So the original name of the language was the acronym
for Charles-Bob-Larry, or CBL.  However, this was difficult
to pronounce, and because of the uncertainty in origin, they
changed it to an acronym of Charles-Or-Bob-Or-Larry: COBOL.
The only problem is that people sometimes confuse COBOL with
a substance invented much earlier by God.  You can keep it
straight by remembering that COBOL is not radioactive, and is
therefore rarely used in cancer treatment. :-J
