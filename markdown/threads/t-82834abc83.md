Yes, it's quite true you don't need to know COBOL to learn C.  The idea of the
Moving from COBOL to C was to take the way a COBOL programmer thinks and
explainhow he should be thinking in "C."  So it's written from the approach of
explaining how to write C to someone who knows COBOL.  

And it's always a good idea to read more than one book on a subject. Moving
from COBOL to C would be a good starting point for a COBOL programmer.  Just
like "Teach Yourself COBOL in 21 Days" is a good starting point for a beginning
programmer :)

Been there, done that.... I was a 10 Year COBOL veteran who bought into a C
language software house. Now I switch back and forth daily (sometimes hourly
when the fires come).
 
Anyway, I'd also suggest a good data structures book. C stores things and
represents data in pretty much very different manners than COBOL does. And
knowing how the machine is handling your data and what it is going to do next
is important. A base knowledge of non-COBOL data structures and representation
will make your C experience a lot nicer.
 
Don't worry about C++. Just another layer of stuff on top of C. C++ is really
cute, and makes life a lot nicer. By don't worry, I don't mean forget about it,
C++ is here to stay and a career programer shoud not ignore it. But learn C at
a basic level first, then branch out.
 
And don't worry about "object oriented programming" - an 01 level (record, for
example) is an "object" when you read past the fluff. You've been using objects
since you first read a file.... just have to adjust your thinking....
 
And remember, these machines only count to 1. So you are still in charge
