```
FYI, (if you are in or care about "IBM mainframe" environments),

There were new announcements today for the next releases of z/OS and z/OS.e

See:

IBM z/OS V1.4: Enabling and Protecting Your e-business and Preview: z/OS
V1.5 (129.8KB)
    http://isource.ibm.com/cgi-bin/goto?it=usa_annred&on=202-190

IBM z/OS.e V1.4 and z/OS.e 1.5 -- Brings Affordability to Enterprise and
e-business Applications (53.3KB)
    http://isource.ibm.com/cgi-bin/goto?it=usa_annred&on=202-191

***

Of most interest (to me) is the fact that V1.4 will be available this Sept;
there will be NO releases in 2003; there will be two releases in 2004; then
IBM plans on going to an annual (rather than the older semi-annual) release
schedule.

Second (interesting to me) was the lack of any ANNOUNCED enhancements to LE.

Final (not so interesting) point.  The z/OS.e announcement maintains the
same "odd" support for COBOL compiled applications, (i.e. they are supported
*if* you precompile them on a z/OS machine *and* include specific calls to
CEEPIPI for LE initialization).  It is still unsupported (violation of the
license) to run any COBOL compiler under z/OS.e (you MUST use z/OS for this)
```
