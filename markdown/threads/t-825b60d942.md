My own approach to file-status - Back aeons ago the old 8.5 floppies and
even the 5.25's were volatile so I had a standard copy file for
Declaratives included in each program - nice catch-all. And Michael M.
referred to problems of running out of disk(ette) space, plus the old
Radio Shack O/S (TRSDOS) only allowed you to do 16 'extensions' to an
indexed file before you had to rebuild, (nice to get a specific error
message when you hit this situation).

I don't do status checks as you do, but it is comparable; my
file-handling is separate classes(programs) and each always tests :-

        if	file-status <> "00"
		invoke os-FileErrors using ws-file-status
					   ws-filename

Most often I've goofed in design, like reading an indexed sequentially,
and I haven't opened the bloody file. File-status checking is a small
price to pay and gives me peace of mind, (and in your case, saves that
56 mile journey, leaving only the kid to contend with who pukes at 3 am
<G>).
