```
Alright, I chickened out on using IBM VisualAge and the AIX COBOL
Set, and instead decided, based on commentary here and otherplaces,
to use the AccuCOBOL compiler.



For $1300, I would have kind of expected to at least get a context
sensitive editor with it, but alas, such is not to be. :) Anyone
have recommendation on 3rd party tools for things like a decent
editor (preferably syntax sensitive) and perhaps, a screen painter?

Or should I call AccuCOBOL up, tell 'em I'm disappointed in this
development system, and look at another COBOL compiler?

The end result of this is that the product needs to run seamlessly
under AIX 4.3 and under SCO UNIX (Intel), but allow development
under Windows NT. I'm fairly certain that any of the major vendors
products will do this fine. I wish Fujitsu would, I like thier
stuff. :)

(Lessee, that leaves MicroFocus and AccuCOBOL, since R/M seems to
be dealing itself a death blow with their salespeople. I may be old
fashioned, but damn if I like calling up a sales person and having
them act like they are doing *me* a favor! Or getting sulky if I ask
them to dig out information or provide me a rationale why
their compiler is better. This is, in part, why we are looking at
AccuCOBOL so hard. They are enthusiastic about their stuff. So is
MF.)

-Paul
```

```
Paul,
I use Acucobol-GT and do exactly what your doing. I develop on Windows
95 and distribute programs on Win 3.x, 95 NT, AIX, and SCO and Sun Unix.
Works fine. You'll run into a few limitations in user interface
translation, but nothing major. After all, you can't expect Window GUI
screens to translate 100% over to 80x24 character based screens. Anyway, we
use Multi-edit for Windows from American
Cybernetics(http://www.amcyber.com). I looked at Visual Slick-Edit, but
found that Multi-Edit was easier to use and the extra money for Slick-Edit
just wasn't worth it. Multi-edit comes with language support and templates
for Acucobol. If you get Multi-Edit and you need a more complete keyword
list, I'd be glad to e-mail it to you. I also have Acucobol's on-line
documentation help file that I use with Multi-Edit's context sensitive help
feature. Works great. I just press Ctrl+F1 on any keyword and Acucobol's
on-line manual pops up.
A couple of very positive things I must mention about Acucobol that
should not be looked upon lightly:
1. Support - I've dealt with many different support departments and
Acucobol's so far has been the best I've seen. They are eager and go out of
their way to help you. They are of course not perfect, but they are
extremely willing to help you. Eduardo, Kate and Pete are my favorites.
2. Sales - The sales team at Acucobol are angels. They are always
friendly and happy to talk to you. They make an extra effort and are proud
of their products. I for one find that refreshing. Kim, Janice, Julie,
etc, etc. They're good people.
3. Development - Drake Coker, chief scientist and vice president, is no
less than a genius. And believe it or not, can actually be e-mailed and
you'll get a response. For a company their size, that's a surprise to me.
As smart as he is, he never talks down to you and is always polite and
genuine.
4. New Products - I have beta copies of some wonderful new products from
Acucobol that will be very useful to Acucobol developers and users.
AcuBench - This is the new IDE for Acucobol-GT for Windows 95 and
NT. It includes a drag and drop screen painter, and a full-blown editor
with project management, on the fly compilation, keyword support, on-line
help, etc, etc.
Acucobol Web-browser plug-in - This version of the runtime will run
as a Netscape and Explorer plug-in so that Acucobol's object files can run
right inside your browser. This is very exciting because it allows for
full-blown Acucobol applications to be run on a corporate intranet or the
internet. True client/server.
AcuConnect - Allows for distributed applications where objects can
be run from multiple servers and mixed with partial local objects and server
objects. This can be useful in speeding up some client server applications
between Windows clients and Unix servers. You could design your
applications where the user interface programs are on the local windows
client or server and the file i/o and other business logic resides on the
Unix server.

Anyway, check out http://www.acucobol.com and find out more. If you
need any help with Acucobol-GT, feel free to contact me.

Dan Maltes
http://www.frontiernet.net/~dwm
Development Director
Applied Systems Technology
http://www.astusa.com
```
