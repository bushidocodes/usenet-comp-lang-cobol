Tony, Newbies, and others -
  It has been a little while since I reposted what the COBOL FAQ says about
homework questions in the NG.  I hope this helps understand the "majority"
view on such posts,

"18.1 Can I get help with homework via the newsgroups?

Yes, you can get help with your homework via these newsgroups. HOWEVER, that
does mean that you will receive help - you will NOT find many participants
who will be very happy if you ask them to do your homework FOR YOU. Some
hints that you should consider when drafting your post for assistance with
homework include:

 * Make it clear from the beginning that you are asking for homework help.
(Most of the newsgroup participants are very good at sniffing out those who
try and pose homework questions as "business need" questions - and they are
not very polite in replying to such questions).

 * Make certain that you specify what compiler and operating system your
homework is targeted at. Solutions may vary significantly based on this. (You
might also want to include what text book you are using.)

 * The more information that you can give that demonstrates that you really
have tried to solve the problem yourself (using your text book, class
material/presentations, lab assistance, etc), the more likely it is that you
will get useful and friendly responses. If you let us know what you have
found on your own and why you are still uncertain or confused, you will
usually get helpful responses; if it looks like you are asking us questions
without trying to solve it yourself, you are likely to get very pointed
replies.

NOTE: if you are looking for a COBOL tutor, (and don't want to see the "Do
your own homework" notes that occur in the COBOL newsgroups), you might want
to try,

    http://www.tutoraid.org/
"

Yes, it may "look" rude of Docdwarf to go around telling people to "Do your
own homework."  But really, you're not looking at it in the correct context.

Usenet newsgroups are discussions places filled with many different people.
They cluster here in a virtual manner, checking in every so often according
to their own cycles.

In real world terms, you can think of it as a continuously occupied lounge
where people are restricted (most of the time) to discussing one thing.
However, unlike the real world, every single word uttered in the lounge can
be heard by every other person without distortion; or in some cases where
grammar is ignored, with too much distortion.

(Whispering in a corner of the lounge is accomplished by email, but in this
case, no one can see you do it; however, sometimes from the context of
discussions and the way a thread develops it is possible to tell if emailing
is going on, and really, the other poeple here come here to participate, and
emailing individuals prevents some parties from participating)

In any event, such a place is often viewed as the source of a "easy and
quick answer".  Yes, everyone who has been here awhile knows all the easy
answers, but as the years roll by (and no, I haven't been here that long,
though others have), you get to see that people are stopping by and asking
the their questions simply because they know they can get an instant reponse
from a living person without having to spend their own time on it; and
instead of taking a couple of hours out and actually reading the material,
they rely on getting it from those who already know.  After doing this over
an over for a *long* time, it tends to be very easy to look upon this as
other people unwittingly using up your time for the most basic of purposes
just because they find it easier than spending their own time.  Overall,
it's a net loss.  The regular attendees here, if they answered such
questions would wind up literally spending hours working on their newsgroup
reader posting answers to such questions.

How do we know that people are doing this?  Stick around, read the newsgroup
consistently, and you'll know that people really are stopping by for just a
moment to get the quick and easy answer to whatever is troubling them.  You
can tell the difference from a real question and a quick and easy question
after only a week or two of reading the newsgroup.

Docdwarf has taken it upon himself to stop this at the beginning with the
simple eye opener "Do your own homework."

This is not rude.  Really, It's not.  It's just the simplest and shortest
way of saying, "Please, if you spent a little of your own time looking at
it, you'd have figured it out.  Try again, and then if you still can't get
it, show us what you did, and we'll see what we can do with you."


On top of all of this, I learned COBOL plugging away at a COBOL manual for
about 6 months.  Every compiler error, every runtime error, every
misassumption about what the book was saying about how the code worked, and
figuring out the parts it left out.  I did it without any help in a shop
surrounded by other COBOL programmers.  Believe me when I say that this was
a huge bonus to me.  I can't tell you the number of other people my former
company sent through their COBOL training program who came out with only the
faintest glimmer of how to really program, much less what all the compiler
and runtime errors meant, much less how to solve them.

Because I had gone to class before these others, and because I actually knew
the answers and management wanted results now, I spent a lot of time
"handing out answers" to my colleagues.  I regret it now, despite the
popularity it gained me, as I certainly wasn't doing their programming
skills any good.

I could go on, there is literally a mountain of evidence for why Docdwarf's
statement is perfectly justified.

assignment to figure out how to construct the solution.
