```
Hi,

Should you learn COBOL? Yes. Actually, it depends. You have to understand
that COBOL is geared
towards MIS and computer science people. It is meant to be easy to program
and read (do to its structured
programming style and pseudocode nature). It works very well in transaction
processing fields since its
meant to be styled after certain concepts (get input fields, processes
transactions, format output).

Learn COBOL if thats what you like and it comes easy to you. I learned COBOL
and Advanced COBOL
over five years ago. COBOL is a easy and fun language, but you have to like
being in the field. Most
programmers feel like they need to learn C/C++ to feel honorable by their
peers. This is an incorrect thought.

There are many Ada, Smalltalk, PeopleSoft, SAP, PERL and other types of
programmers out there. Just
because businesses pay more for a certain type of skill doesn't mean they
won't pay for a talented COBOL
programmer that is willing to learn new things. Also, you have to realize
that a competent manager will pay
someone who has been knee-deep in COBOL (also have above-average talent) for
over three years at
least $20-$25/hr for salaried pay and $35/hr for contract pay (if not more).
So don't feel that just because you
didn't learn Java in school that you won't get a job in ten years.

Focus on if you can do the job within a limited time frame with the skills
you have at the moment. How much
you are getting paid or not being paid isn't an issue if you can't do the
work. If you can do the work then consider the area you live in and if they
pay as much as large cities like Atlanta or San Diego. You may have to
relocate to get the pay you are reaching for but do what it takes.

Nuff said.

Ken
```
