How about a teeny bit of Banking DDA 101?<G>

Point #1, Debit Cards whether at an ATM or at a merchant work pretty much just
like a check. The big difference is between "Online Merchants", those that
actually process directly thru your bank, and the vast majority of smaller
merchants that process your debit card exactly as if it were a Visa or
Mastercard. However, once the transaction gets to your bank its usually
processed like it was a check. The big difference happens during the day. The
true online transactions are "memo posted" against your balance. The real DDA
(checking) systems are all batch. Not for the least of the reasons because of
those pesky pieces of paper called checks:)

As to what order checks are handled and what will bounce or not bounce, the
answer varies depending on the specific software the bank uses.

ALL DDA systems process by chronological order within trancode so that deposits
are posted first, followed by internal transactions (real withdrawals from
either the bank in person or ATMs or online debits) and finally checks. If a
large check is processed first, the balance is reduced as long as there are
funds to cover it. If the next check causes an overdraft, it is rejected and
the program continues trying each subsequent check.

Some (a very very few) DDA systems once tried to minimize the number of checks
bounced by sorting by value. But....with the fee income that can be generated I
doubt most do now. All that I know of DO keep trying to process the remaining
checks however.

Since I've been playing with banking systems for the better part of 30 years, I
think I do have a few insights:)

BTW...NONE of what I said above applies to anything the bank considers a
"critical" account. If you just happen to be Microsoft or GM, you'll probably
NEVER have a check bounce<G>

David Olson
