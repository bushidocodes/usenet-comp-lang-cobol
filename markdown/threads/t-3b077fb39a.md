every military member of the Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps,
Coast Guard, all the civilians for those branches, and for the DOD

of which I coldnot find at that moment.  That's a payroll of well
over 6 million, almost 5% of the population.  Care to try doing that
while maintaining a functioning system with no disruptions in service?
Taxes, payrools, insurance, credit cards, mortgages, like it or not,
COBOL is not going away.  Not in my lifetime, not in your lifetime
and probably not in our offsprings lifetimes either.  Isn't it time
people accepted this fact and took it back into the IT fold?  I have
never said that COBOL is the only thing that should be taught in
schools, but by the same token it is a grave dis-service to both the
students and the industry to pretend it is insignificant and that it
will just go away.
