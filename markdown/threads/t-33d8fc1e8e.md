Incorrecto! Russia isn't ever likely to be a huge outsourcing location
either. COBOL and mainframe technologies are western alphabetic languages,
obviously, and english at that. Not much chance of a mandarin compiler being
developed - communication in general would be far too much of an obstacle,
never mind the lack of a skills base.

That's why India is really the only place this is going to in any quantity
that effects us. English is actually the Indian first language, and
companies are exploiting that left right and centre - such a vast swathe of
English speakers, educated and cheap. That's why call centres, support
centres, financial houses and all other forms of business support services
that aren't dependent on the transfer of manufactured goods are being
outsourced there. It's not just us techies who are suffering - many other
business support functions are feeling the pinch also.

The demand for our skills has actually gone through the roof in the last few
years. Yes, displacement has happened due to outsourcing most certainly to
cut costs, but also to meet ever spiralling demand across the globe. How do
you think that India is ever going to modernise it's economy if it hasn't
got some kind of central mainframe technology to hold health, crime,
education, financial etc etc records on it's 1 billion inhabitants? Via
mainframes. India has always long adopted western technology that has
matured but is still wholly useful.
