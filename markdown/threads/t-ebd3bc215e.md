This is a summary of various recent threads on the subject of
jobs ads in this newsgroup. I think it fairly reflects the
opinions of the majority of those people who posted. I send
this text to jobs posters who don't try to follow the simple
guidelines.

I'll post this every month or so.
Pieter A. Hintjens

----------------------------------------------------------------
››› Is it O.K. to advertise job openings in this newsgroup?

Not really. According to the charter of comp.lang.cobol (maybe
others are similar) it is for the discussion of all aspects of the
use of the programming language COBOL (eg Object-oriented COBOL).
The charter does not mention job ads.

But here are some informal guidelines to avoid irritating too many
of us:
Mark the Subject line with JOB: and say WHERE the job is
eg Subject: JOB: USA Ca AS400 Cobol A/P
Keep it brief (20 lines + 4 sig)
Don't post it more than once
NO APPLICATION FORMS or MISSION STATEMENTS
For multiple positions, post 1 ad + pointer to others
Agencies restricted to 1 ad per week.
----------------------------------------------------------------

››› What newsgroups can I post COBOL job ads to?

biz.jobs.offered
misc.jobs.contract
misc.jobs.fields.chemistry
misc.jobs.misc
misc.jobs.offered
misc.jobs.offered.entry
uk.jobs.offered
uk.jobs.contract
... etc

››› What is the charter for comp.lang.cobol?
 
› Subject: RESULT: comp.lang.cobol 468:39
› Date: 3 Nov 1994 15:44:58 -0500
› Organization: Usenet Volunteer Votetakers
› Message-ID: 

RESULT unmoderated group comp.lang.cobol passes 468:39
There were 468 YES votes and 39 NO votes, for a total of 507 valid
votes. There were 2 abstains and 4 invalid ballots...

CHARTER

This newsgroup is for discussion of all aspects of the use of the COBOL
programming language. Examples of relevant topics are :

o Good books to learn from
o Requests for algorithms
o Discussions of coding problems
o Comparisons of different COBOL products
o The COBOL standards, past, present and future
o Object Oriented COBOL
o Portability between COBOL on mainframes, UNIX and DOS

The group should not be used for discussions or flame wars of the ``my
language is better than your language because...'' variety which more
properly belong in /dev/null.

› end of charter
