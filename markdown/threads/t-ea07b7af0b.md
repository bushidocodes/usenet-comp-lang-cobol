That's the way going on here.
Companies can neither pay for the AS/400(and other
mainframe) maintenance nor for outsourcing contracts
so they simply switch to PC server of Sun boxes and
write totally new in C++. This trend happens with
BANKS.
Day after day companies once large get sliced up or
go R.I.P. and the Y2K issue is accelerating Cobol->C++.
Sun box+C is much cheaper than mainframe+cobol..
Young gyus will simply refuse programming in Cobol but
argue to rewrite totally in Java/Delphi/C++. Here another
problem is that firing is very very difficult.
While I am no more with technology, my question is:
I myself had bad experience with the reliability of C programs.
Of course, I know that the companies are compelled into
this trend(many many workers have not gotten payment for
3-6 months:in some cases,programmers only get $200 or so
a month is not fired;Ph.D's,MBAs,CPAs,programmers and
financial analysts are the ones mostly to get fired while
those with huge muscles get richer day by day)




But wouldn't this cost much more in the future?
I am not talking about simple system crashes but about
systematic reliability problems of the "small systems".
That is,very cheap today but very expensive later.
I know that there are a bunch of nontechnical reasons
to continue usage of mainframe/COBOL.
But I couldn't find any relevant (comparative) case studies
about this reliability issue betw. Unix/NT box+C(Java,Delphi)
and mainframe(AS400)+Cobol.
Any related experiences?

I don't want to start a language war but...

Most of the new 'business' applications I'm doing these days are in Delphi.
Whereas I think it's a very good technology for GUI work and interfacing with
SQL, I find it a royal pain in the ass to maintain other peoples code and the
performance of typical applications is knowhere near that of COBOL (Micro
Focus) in my experience. The overheads of file handling via SQL are largely to
blame. In COBOL I use my own custom non-sql database handler. Raw file
handling is just so much quicker in COBOL.

Delphi's threading sucks too. MF COBOL's knocks seven bells out of it.

Where it does score well is in it's framework paradigm. I can re-use a lot of
code very easily. I have an asset retrevial system with some 1500+ assets to
call on, most of which are Delphi. I think I put one COBOL asset in there! OO
COBOL is not as good and few people understand or use it although I must say
it's a much fuller OO language than Delphi. Why Borland stuck with Pascal
instead of Modula (2/3) or Oberon is somewhat puzzling.

Delphi is also superb at prototyping GUI interfaces and doesn't have arcane
languages behind the app like MF's Dialog system. Mix OO COBOL with Delphi's
GUI creation ability and I'd be more than happy.
