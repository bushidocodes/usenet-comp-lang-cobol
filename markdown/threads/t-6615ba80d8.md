My email and the reply related to this earlier thread:

"Bill,

Thanks for your thoughts on clarifying Fujitsu's position on run-time
licenses. Yes, Fujitsu does charge for traditional run-time licenses in
Japan. But we have distinctly chosen NOT to implement a similar model in the
U.S. We stand behind our belief that COBOL programmers deserve a reasonably
priced COBOL compiler with royalty free run-times.

At the same time, we have implemented some valuable technology in Version 5
and feel it is appropriate to charge a small fee for these new innovations.
Users may OPTIONALLY purchase the multi-threaded run-time license for
enterprise applications that require higher performance. The Fujitsu COBOL
development system will ship with both the single (FREE) and multi-threaded
run-time systems to allow users to develop and test under either run-time
with no additional run-time requirements. Developers can easily invoke the
multi-threaded run-time system for development and testing with a simple
run-time switch - no additional configuration is required to switch between
the single threaded and multi-threaded run-time systems. If warranted, users
can then decide if they want to purchase the multi-threaded run-time.

Hope this helps!

Ron Langer
