For the past decade I've relied upon Microsoft COBOL v3.0, which was
actually a repackaged version of Microfocus COBOL.  It is relatively
simple yet powerful and absolutely reliable.  It creates stand-alone
sexcutable files, and best of all, the entire compiler and all its
libraries, plus several invaluable tools (text editor, sort/merge etc)
all fit easily on a single floppy disk.  

Most of my work was done on 386's, but I still keep a copy installed on
my newer laptop for throwing together an occasional quick & dirty app.  
I've tried newer versions of Microfocus but instead now use Fujitsu's
COBOL suite to develop commercial apps.  It has a nice database
interface and people like the radio buttons and scroll bars and such.

Which COBOL compiler you choose really depends on just what you intend
to develop with it.  Wishing you the best of luck...

If you are  just going to dial-up and use the PC as a 3270 terminal with
all the work being done on the employers computer, the get the fastest
modem/connection you can.  There are many 3270 emulators for the PC.  One I
have used is Citrix.  Don't know the cost as y company paid for it.  At
home will need a dedicated telephone line so you can receive telephone
calls while on your computer.

If you are going to code, compile, and test on your PC  then the isssue is
whitch Cobol vendors product is most compatible with your employers
version.  Each vendor has their own extensions, but you should be ok if you
code within the Standard. Then again you employer may want to use the
extensions,  Many Cobol compilers are available for the PC and most any new
computer has the necessary resources.  See the Cobol FAQ at:
http://cobolreport.com/faqs/cobolfaq.htm.for a list.

Thane Hubbells Cobol in 24 hours book comes with a Fujitsu compiler

http://www.murach.com/books/cobol/microfocus.htm 
book comes with MF Personal Cobol

http://www.murach.com/books/eooc/index.htm
book come with MF NetExpress, University Edition

http://www.murach.com/books/ecwp/index.htm
book come with MF NetExpress, University Edition

These are just some that I know of. Shop around for best price.  Of those
above Thanes book was the cheapest.  Be sure to check out the limitations
as there might be some you cannot accept.

Other full feature compilers will cost more.

PERCobol compile to the Java Virtual Machine (JVM) and will work on many
platforms.  I have no idea how much it costs.

Good luck!
