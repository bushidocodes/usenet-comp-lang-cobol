```
Joey, believe me, no one has been trying to dump on you, but give
you good advice from many years of experience.  Personally, I have
been gainfully employed writing software for over 31 years, the last
20 years owning my own company.  My comment about the crayon face
was just an attempt to illustrate by reductio ad absurdum, that
frivolity has no place in a business *introduction*.  Like you, I
would hate (and probably refuse) to work at a place that would not
tolerate reasonable humor.  But that is not the issue here.  The
issue is 'First Impression'.  A typical manager has 10,000 things
begging his attention.  If you have ever managed people, you will
know that it is an environment of constant change of focus as your
attention is diverted over and over again by interruptions upon
interruptions.  A manager is constantly filtering out tons and tons
of noise, trying to use his mental energy on what is important.  If
his first impression of your post is not very good *immediately*,
then it's just noise, as far as he is concerned, because he has
probably wasted his time on a thousand zeros who weren't worth the
effort, and is not eager to do so again.  If this happens, you will
not *get* that chance to impress him with your ability.  You would
have blown it for that manager.  You can complain and boost
yourself until you are blue in the face, talking about how the
company is the loser, but when you remain unemployed, you are the
loser too, no? :-)

My suggestion is simply this, keep your humor and cutesy names to
yourself, until you have had an opportunity to present your case
fairly to a prospective employer.  From the dawn of time, when
people sent out resumes, they work very hard making them as perfect
as possible, using expensive paper, professional printing, etc.,
just to make that First Impression a good one.  And after all, if
you are unable to restrain yourself even that little bit, then what
does that say about your ability to be serious when needed. ;-)

And Joey, I really hope you get that job. :-)
```
