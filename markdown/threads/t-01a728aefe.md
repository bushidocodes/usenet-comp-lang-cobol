Thanks first for buying the book.  The whole debate seems a bit silly,
but here's the deal.

I *INSISTED* that the book be packaged with a compiler.  Originally
the book was to come sans CD.  I felt and do feel strongly that if you
want someone to really learn the language they had better be provided
with a compiler.   I personally secured the Fujitsu version because
they were giving away the CD.  At the TIME it was not available for
download.  In addition to the full CD (the download has no manuals)
you get some bonus material in the evaluation version of sp2 (So I
could do GUI in a single chapter, not to mention the fact that I loke
the tool), and also some Lotus Screen Cam movies I creates showing how
to do different things with the compiler in case you had trouble.
Also, on the CD is content that would not fit in the book without
making it too large.  

The RETAIL price of the book is $24.99.  If the CD had NOT been
included the price would have been 19.99.  So you ARE paying for the
CD.  The price would have been the same if there were a CD with NO
compiler on it, so you are TECHNICALLY not paying for the compiler.
However, it IS true that the CD increased the final price of the book.
