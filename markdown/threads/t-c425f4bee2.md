I'm afraid that I have to agree with DD here.  If a job is available, then
providing details about the position (title, location, length of contract,
responsibilities, pay range) should be easy to provide.  It will save time,
both for the employer and the applicants.

As for pay, why, as an employer, would you want to underpay an employee.  Do
you really believe that the employee won't figure it out?  It is pretty easy
to get a good idea of what the pay range should be, so most applicants are
going to come in with that information.  Even if you do get them, how long
do you think it will take for them to figure it out.  When he/she does, do
you think that they will want to continue to work for you?

Why, as a potential employee, should I respond to an ad for a company that
is unwilling to release this information?  I can tell you that *I* won't.
Ever.  Failure to provide this information will simply eliminate many
potential applicants.

Any time that I see a post that doesn't provide employment information, I
assume it is a troll (and I know that I am not the only one).  If an
employer really wants his opening to be considered, they should provide the
information...
