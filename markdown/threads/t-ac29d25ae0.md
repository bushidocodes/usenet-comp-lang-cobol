```
I thought I was about to pass out, I saw a reply from someone at Liant!

I work for a company that uses RM/COS as a small system OS that works
extremely well. I was always amazed what someone could do in such a
small/inexpensive environment.

For "political" reasons, when we also included AIX systems in our Mix
(RS6000), we moved to MF. Being a 'C' programmer who dabbles in COBOL
from the system point of view (%^$%^^^%$ RUNTIMES!), I want you folks to
know that the RM stuff is head and shoulders more reliable, less
exasperating, and higher Quality than anything that we've gotten from MF.

To be blunt, we'd change to RM in a New York minute if we could get a
reasonable way to swap out 2000 customers using the MF stuff w/o cost to
our clients and us not taking a bath. Like everything else, the
technically competent don't know how to market and the technically
incompetent can sell you anything (see IBM OS/2 vs Microsoft Winxxx).

Also, our other platform is TI/HP 1500 Unix System 5 3.3.1 so not sure if
RM supports that either.

As for the person who complained about the high prices and license fees
forcing small businesses to compromise, isn't America Grand?

I too went the C route since I could afford the tools even to the point
of using xBase structures even though ISAM would've been just as fine, if
not better.

John...
```
