### CA-Realia Abandons the PC Development Market

- **From:** "red..." <ua-author-9624@usenetarchives.gap>
- **Date:** 1997-11-06T19:00:01+00:00
- **Newsgroups:** comp.lang.cobol
- **Message-ID:** `<346277c3.243260408@news1.ibm.net>`

```

Hopefully the title got your attention. Many CA-Realia users have
been waiting for the 32 bit version of this very fast, very reliable
compiler. Well, if you develop PC applications with this compiler
your wait will be a long one.

I have had several contacts with CA over the issue. One particularly
irks me. I called about getting a 32 bit version of what is now
called COBOL +. I was informed that they would not be taking COBOL +
forward and that their only forward going product would be Workbench
II. Well, another Internet correspondent has the latest WorkBench II
release and has the following from the manual:

I just got a copy of CA-Realia II Workbench 3.0/GA-Candidate. (final
beta?)

Page 1-1 of Chapter 1/Introduction of each manual has the following:

/quote
Note: Ca-Realia II Workbench is intended for the purpose of offloading
development and / or maintenance of mainframe Cobol applications. PC
routines are included to provide interfaces to facilities outside the
scope
of the product. For example, you can use these low-level routines to
emulate mainframe API's that would not otherwise be available on the
PC

Usage of the product to create PC applications is possible but is
beyond the
scope of the product....Computer Associates technical support will
only be
able to provide limited support for creating PC-based application.
/end
quote

CA tried to sell me this product KNOWING I was doing PC based ONLY
development with it. I'm beyond discusted, as are several other
Realia users I know.

Realia COBOL was the BEST COBOL compiler for the PC. In it's 16 bit
version it remains the overall fastest, especially in the areas of
tight compact code, fast - lightning fast - file I/O and sorts and in
general reliability and lack of bugs in the compiler. However, CA,
true to their record, has not completely and utterly destroyed a
masterful product. I really wanted to stay with CA for my 32 bit PC
development. My remaining choices are not nearly as fast, compact or
reliable. This is a SAD SAD thing. If you are a CA Realia user and
you target the PC with your development, I ENCOURAGE you STRONGLY to
call your sales rep and tell them that you are ANGRY, and UPSET about
this turn of events.

The other COBOL options for the PC, and my objections/reasons for not
wanting to use them:

RM - Runtime requirement and general overhead.
Micro Focus - Completly unreliable file system. Slow. Potential
Runtime Fees.
IBM Visual Object Cobol - for windows, in it's infancy. No native
Indexed file structure. Need a database.
ACCU COBOL - Runtime Fees.
Fujitsu - only potential solution. File access still much slower than
CA-Realia, but computes are very fast. Seems reliable but requires a
LOT of overhead in Runtime DLL's. Bears watching.

I would prefer to stay with CA and not have to change all my code for
yet another compiler. I am ticked about this CA. Listen up. You are
abandoning a large market. Many of these shops use or could be
customers for other CA products. They will think twice before
purchasing any based on your actions with your COBOL compiler.
```

products. I personally know people laid off from software companies
acquired by CA. I heard from a reliable source that Computer Associates

have no other place to go.
