RM/COBOL may not have an easy-to-use debugger, or a debugger like MF's,
but it does have one.

You invoke it through the run time:

RUNCOBOL D

The debugger starts out in step-through mode, stops at the first line of
executable code, displays the module name and line number, and waits for
a command. You can look at and change variables, set breakpoints, step
through single or multiple lines -- everything you need. Unfortunately,
it's about as easy to use as EDLIN used to be -- but ya do what ya gotta
do. You'll need a listing of your compilation to keep track of line
numbers and variable names, plus the RM/COBOL user's guide for a list of
DEBUG commands.

Good luck.

--Greg
