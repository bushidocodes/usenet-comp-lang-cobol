Matter of taste and style, I'd say... having been taught that GO TO is 
allowed under three conditions:

to the immediately preceding paragraph-label
to the -EXIT paragraph for the currently performed paragraph
to the ABEND routine

... and that the first (for READ-loops) was permitted, but slightly... 
distasteful...

... then seeing a GO TO 9999-BLOW-EM-UP-GOOD served as a flag that 
Something Really Important was in process and its failure was, as The 
Professionals say 'really bad news'.

The ABEND paragraph can be stuffed with counters, DISPLAYs and other 
debugging information in the same matter as a paragraph invoked by a 
PERFORM... but that GO TO was a 'jump out and scream'.

(of course... that is how I was taught; getting out into shops where it 
was used otherwise, eg.

PARSE-FIRST-NAME

    (instructions)
    IF MIDDLE-NAME = SPACES OR LOW-VALUES
        GO TO PARSE-LAST-NAME.

PARSE-MIDDLE-NAME.

    (instructions)

PARSE-LAST-NAME.

    (instructions)

... quickly taught me that other folks had learned under different 
regimens)
