programmer, but I don't think it would do any harm. I started out working on

solved... :-)

paper. In my experience, a track record trumps a PhD. (I was contending for 
a particular job against a guy who had one and I got it. They gave him 
another position in IT in the same company and he proved to be a disaster 
when it came to the coal face and actually getting a result. Eventually he 
left in disgrace... So, then people get into arguments about whether a PhD 
from Yale is better than a PhD from Harvard, or Oxford or Cambridge and so 
on... It really doesn't matter. What matters are results.

Almost everything I know about IT I learned on the job and in my spare time, 
teaching myself. It is an ongoing process and I'm still learning. I don't 
believe a comp. sci. background is worthless because I don't really believe 
that ANY formal education is worthless. At the very least, it imposes a 
discipline and teaches young people to enquire and dig for information which 
is pretty important as part of long term career and personal growth. But my 
advice would be not to get obsessed with the paper chase. Start producing 
results and get known for that. It won't matter if you don't have a PhD.

"Computer professional" eh?  Anyone who makes a living from providing 
computer based goods and services would have a perfect right to call 
themselves that. It's just pompous and silly to decide that if you're not a

out of testing other people's software. They are every bit as serious and 
professional in their job as the person who wrote the software they are 
testing.

Just like with certificates, people can get too obsessed over titles. If you 
make a living at it, apply ethics and integrity in your daily dealings, act 
honestly and responsibly, who's to say you are NOT a professional?

When I was working as a contract programmer I liked it when people asked me 
what my job title was. I invariably replied: "Cyberwhore."  It was something

the ladies of the night if you are honest...

The bottom line is: Don't be obsessed by titles and bits of paper and 
whether you are a Member of the Professional Society... It is all bollocks 
if you can't produce results.

And don't get too pompous about what you do. I was at a party back in the 
60s where a guy was sucking on a pipe trying to impress some girls. I 
cringed when I heard him say: "Well, speaking as a computer programmer..."

Another guy standing nearby jumped in immediately and said:"Computer 
programmer?!!! That's just a Space Age clerk..!" The computer guy slunk away
