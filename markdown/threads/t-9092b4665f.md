I am currently bewildered and would appreciate any opinions on the
following:

1. If you go to a web page and it has a button on it to show you something,
would you press it?

The reason I ask is because:

1. I went to considerable trouble to open up the current project PRIMA is
working on, so (interested) people can see it. There is a complete schematic
of the design and a table showing progress towards achieving the design. (I
update this table every so often and have just done so...). 102 people have
accessed the page so far.
(http://primacomputing.co.nz/primametro/pwrCOBMigration.aspx)


2. Recently, the project reached a stage where it was possible to actually
run the tool being built and get some meaningful output from it. As this is
a major milestone, I posted a screen shot and linked it to the page via a
button. Unfortunately, I took the line of least resistance (easiest for me)
and coded it as a pop-up. Most Browsers have pop-ups disabled, so I guess
some people may have clicked the button and nothing happened. I couldn't
understand why only a few people had "clicked through" to see the actual
tool. After some discussion with Web Development people whose opinions I
respect, I grasped the nettle and changed it from being a pop-up to being a
link to a new page.

RESULT: A deafening silence :-) Currently, the page has been visited 102
times and 7 have clicked through.

The pundits are as perplexed as I am. No current explanation.

People accessing the site must know it is safe, and the page with the link
on is safe, and the page linked to is safe. Yet less than 1 in 10 of the
visitors follow the link.

So, is it just that there is no interest in the product itself?

Are people really terrified of clicking links?

I have to assume that if someone takes the trouble to go to the page, they
probably have SOME interest in the product.

What could I do to encourage people to click-through?

I guess it doesn't really matter (we'll develop the tool anyway) but it is
interesting to me to try and understand how people browse the web.

All comments, theories, ideas welcome.

Pete.
"I used to write COBOL...now I can do anything."
