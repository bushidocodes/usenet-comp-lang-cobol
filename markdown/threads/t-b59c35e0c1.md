```
As someone who saw some (not much) of the "internal" IBM vs Microsoft inability 
to work together during the first two "joint" releases of OS/2 (while working on 
COBOL/2), I am curious if there are any GOOD "summary" sites (or books) that 
actually detail the demise of the relationship which (ultimately) led to the 
demise of OS/2?

The "simplistic" answer that I have often heard was that IBM thought that 
customers (their existing large corporate customers) would "settle" for a 
user-UNfriendly interface for several releases - to get the "structure" for this 
"multiprocessing scalable OS" working.  While Microsoft thought that their 
extensive (individual / "home" / "small business") customers cared MUCH more 
about the friendliness of the interface and were willing to "wait" for 
extended/enhanced OS capabilities.

Neither corporation would accept that the either management/marketing teams knew 
what they were talking about.

  ***

However, I would be interested in reading more "informed" post mortems.
```
