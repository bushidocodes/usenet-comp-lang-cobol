Here is something that you all can debate/discuss:

A few years back I wrote a Telephoen Billing program. Very simple in
code, used a raw data file that was guaranteed to have the correct data, etc.

Anyways, for the past two years I've always been searching for a bug that
happened when applying rate discounts. It seems that customers bills
would not always come out to the correct amount. Off by a few cents usually.

Well, after reading discussions of COMPUTE on here I went back to the
code and changed the COMPUTE to ADD, MULTIPLY, etc. Guess what? Fixed
the problem!

I was using decimals (0.15) for example for discount rates and the
COMPUTE statement was not giving the "exact" answer.

I have purchased a very detailed book on COBOL and I am now investigating
why this is so. I'll post what I find.
