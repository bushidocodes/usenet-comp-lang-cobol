Your comp-3 solution will not work - it ISNT 2 bytes that are packed, it is
2 packed digits.
Other solutions: mmm. I work for a Y2K company in Australia, and we believe
there are really only 2 solutions: Expansion (your 1st solution) and
windowing (either fixed or rolling), so I suppose that's 3 (not 2.5).

Windowing, is where you compare the year to a fixed value (say 50) to
determine the century, or to the
current year (rolling window). I don't know how much experience you have
so I'll give eggs..

fixed window -

if YEAR-IN > 50
move 19 to CENTURY-IN
else
move 20 to CENTURY-IN
end-if

rolling window

accept ws-date from date (yymmdd format)

if YEAR-IN > yy (of ws-date)
move 19 to CENTURY-IN
else
move 20 to CENTURY-IN
end-if


Both of these solutions have the limitation that all dates are in a 100
year period. It is not possible to have a date that is OVER 100 years old.


On the other hand, expansion can, but requires that all stored dates be
changed.


2 more solutions:

4 - kill application (I've seen it done - it was not worth the effort of
making y2k compliant)

5 - can't think of one.


Steve.
