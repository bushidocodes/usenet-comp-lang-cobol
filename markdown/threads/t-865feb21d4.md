My previous COBOL job finished on Oct 1.  The company, a multi-national, 
had placed an embargo on all system changes from Nov 1 until January 31 
and so, we all were finished at the end of our terms and even a few were 
finished early.  Since October, there were few, if any, opportunities in 
the UK and the EU - even to the point where the web sites that provide e-
mail notifications weren't even sending me e-mails for days at a time.  
This changed during February with more opportunities coming available but 
the competition has been hottish.

Tax administration changes for contractors are going to have a 
detrimental effect where, for the same client outgoings, the contractor 
will actually be receiving the same as the permies without all the 
benefits associated with being a wage slave.  Thus, many contractors are 
considering going back into the permie market because of the red tape 
hassles.  Others will be making sure that they don't fall foul of the new 
regulations and yet others will be screwed for about 25% of their income 
over and above their traditional (to date) tax liability.  So, it's all a 
bit dire here as well.

I've been doing some web work in the meantime, and a couple of small 
private projects as investment for the future - with some friends I am 
getting into becoming part of an ISP.  Now that we've started to get 
serious about it, I am now up for a contract with a client who actually 
wants old farts like me to do Assembler applications development and 
maintenance, with some COBOL thrown in alongside.  I should hear within a 
couple of days about this so my only problem should be how to divide my 
time between "real work" and the Internet stuff.  But, hey, this is 
almost six months on from the previous "real work".

I think most people, not just in IT, found the Christmas effect to have 
extended for somewhat longer than usual this time around because a couple 
of neighbours of mine are civil engineers and they've both been out of 
work altogether for about six months.  Also, they're finding that their 
marketplace is picking up again.
