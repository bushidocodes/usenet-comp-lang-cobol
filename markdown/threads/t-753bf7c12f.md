Nice to see your signature again and I am wish for you, that you get the
job.

As many other good men (no women) has stated, the gap analysis (in our
world) shows the difference between two software products.

Normally you will make a list of all the functions that matters to your
company (add customer, change customer, remove customer, receive orders from
internet etc. etc). You can even put values on the list (10 is extreemly
important, 1 is 'nice to have').

Then you look at your options using your list as a 'shopping list'. After
that you eliminate equals. Now you have the 'gap' left. Now sort the list in
descending priority, and it is normally obvious, what you should give a
closer look.

Sales persons are using the same method to control their presentation. Have
you ever seen a presentation with focus at the week spots - I'll bet that
you have not. However, looking at a presentation, you will often see much
time spent on nice things, that the competitor cannot do. The reason is a
gap analysis with coustomer values multiplied with a 'Us / them' -value.

Well that's about it - good luck

Ib

years ago.

use:

Produce a gap analysis report.

where the new software does not meet user requirements/current processing.

If you read my statement about Gap-analysis as an anouncement for an
exciting new animal in the business, you are wrong. As I stated elsewhere,
elderly people like me would love re-use, but why are they renaming their
pets all the time.

When I first saw a Gap-analysis in 1982, I found it interesting - though it
was called an Argument-analysis. The needs for a true compare of
EDP-services made that kind of work very popular. The difficulties we found
were that it was almost impossible to find equal terms for evaluating
products.

I am using my own dialect of the analysis now. It's shorter, It's simple,
not only rational and it is based upon my very own 'good-enough-principle'.

When I suddenly saw the Gap-analysis introduced as a new born baby, I looked
into it. Still, I cannot tell the diference between our work in the eighties
and what engineers are taught nowadays, except for a few improvements we
also had to make back then.

Yes, I was trying to give a very simlifyed explanation and I added a few
improvements used in real life. Yes, I agree - I will even raise the bet.
97% is common sence. The problem with this stuff is that values are
impossible to decide upon from a rational point of view.

Try to use it at home. Compare 2 food-processors. Find 25 focus points to
evaluate the quality. You can use spread sheets and whatever - You will buy
the red one, and that's life.

I do think that the Gap-analysis is worth something. The process itself -
finding out what to compare - will improve your product knowledge, it will
give you a good background - before you buy the red one.

Regards
Ib
