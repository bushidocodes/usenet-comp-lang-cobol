Coming out of retirement was a story invented by magazine/newspaper
reporters. 

Y2K programmers were hired as contractors, not permanent employees.
They left when projects finished, in 98 through early 99. 

Y2K caused an oversupply of low-paid contractors from outside the US,
and it made contracting companies greedy. Before Y2K, they kept 30% of
the bill rate; now, some of them try for more than 50%. Also, it made
management more receptive to using contractors and outsourcing
systems.
