As pointed out by others, the use of the word 'productive' in this context
is at best subjective.

However....

Since productivity is the ratio of output to input, and in this case input
is dollars, dollars cannot be part of your question unless the dollar value
of output is included in that question.

That is, since productivity = Output per dollar of input, the absolute cost
is immaterial, as the value of output is required to find the ratio.

Alternately, you may simply choose to believe Mr. Ittycheria is actually a
closet politician: he answered a question other than that which was asked.
He also receives ten (10) politician's bonus points for coming up with
verbiage with violates arithmetic grammar:  a "difference" is never
expressed as a ratio ("six to one").. a "difference" is an absolute value.
