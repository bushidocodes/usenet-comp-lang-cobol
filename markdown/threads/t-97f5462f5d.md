>I was hoping for a cookie "Spoofer," - one that deliberately returns

Um...  No need.  The cookie creator is the one who creates ALL of the
information in the cookies.  Your system is used to store the cookie and
send the cookie, but it plays NO role in the creation of the cookie.  So
the only person who knows how to edit the cookie is the person who created
it, the person on the other side.

A lot of people seem to think that cookies contain info about them.  They
don't and can't (unless you told the other site something about yourself).
The closest they can come is recording the number of times you visit a
site, or (more annoyingly) the locations of the sites from which you view
an advertisement service (doubleclick.net is notorious for this).

If you insist on changing cookies, though, you're free to hand-edit them
-- netscape stores them in "cookies.txt".  I have no clue how to get at
IE's cookies; it does terrible things to make its cookies as hard to deal
with as possible.
