These are all IBM mainframe terms - and actually have nothing to do with
COBOL - or rather are not limited to COBOL.

MVS is an operating system (like DOS or UNIX)
VSAM is a file system (when not modified, usually for indexed files)
IMS is made up of two parts
A) a hierarchical database system (used to be known as IMS/DB)
B) an online transaction system (used to be known as IMS/DC)

Note: to original poster. Although the terms you mention are still known to
most IBM mainframers, there are fine points to what they actually mean and
changes in names (or modifiers) for different versions/releases.

Note: to mainframers - please do not flame me for over simplification - this
seemed the level of explanation that was appropriate for the question.
