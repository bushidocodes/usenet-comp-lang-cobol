Institute. One book I recommend is based our the course material for
the course I did. It is written by the head of the college, Rui
D'Oliviera and is available from the college. Their phone number is

Technology Users Council. If there is something similar in the USA,
you could ask them about courses. Otherwise I suggest you contact a
hiring agency or a manager (IT or personnel) at a company using COBOL
to find out where their successful candidates trained.

Hope this helps!

I have said before (sorry to those who have already seen it...) that some
of the best programming courses are in local vo-techs/technical
colleges/whatever your area calls them. These schools are strictly
interested in teaching you to do the job, and often the staff are working
professionals who part-time instruct. This may not sound good, but in fact
you get a perspective that is lacking in some (not all) four year programs.
Also, it is generally cheaper, and the programs are geared to working
people (e.g., night and weekend classes available), so even if you are a
full-time student you have more flexibility in scheduling.

I have taken programming at graduate, undergraduate four year, two year and
tech schools, and in general I prefer to take mine at the vo-tech now.
