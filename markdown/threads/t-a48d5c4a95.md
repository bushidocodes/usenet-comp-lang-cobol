NEVER rested on my laurels and thought I had enough. Learned Web

commercial computing.

NEVER waited for someone else to pay for my training; acquired the skills

that is UAC on Windows 8; nevertheless, it is still the best OS MS have

them and making progress.) And, even in spite of all this, I LOVE Windows 
8.)

I'm far too busy to be unemployed and haven't been for around 45 years.

(I'm currently fighting to retire; people keep arousing my interest with 
problems and I just can't sit back and do nothing. However, these days I 
tend to work around 4 hours a day and will cut it down to zero in the next 
few years.)

In 10 years as an employee, programming corporate computers, I was out of 
work (through no fault of my own) for around 3 months. In a 35 year career 
as an independent programmer, analyst, project manager and consultant, I 
have been out of work for a total of around 10 days, when I didn't want to 
be.

It has been my policy to think about, discuss, and discover what was needed 
by people, then produce it. Sometimes I have had to do really tedious, 
boring jobs with computers and I looked for ways to let the machines do it. 
(That was how the PRIMA Migration Toolset came to be written. Anyone who has 
ever done a serious Migration knows how time consuming and error-prone it 
can be; automating it was a dream which is now a reality.)

A chance conversation where someone said: "It would be really cool if we 
could do..." led me to design and produce a major new security product that 
will be sold by PRIMA Computing next year. (It is currently in production on 
some selected pilot sites and has exceeded my expectations.)

I learned many years ago that relying on other people to provide you with a 
wage is very high risk. Even very well run companies can go broke, and the 
days of "jobs for life" are long gone. The computer industry hasn't been 
immune... where are NCR, Burroughs, Honeywell and Univac?

If you don't believe the world owes you a living and are prepared to invest 
time, energy, and money into learning, then even if there are no job 
openings, you'll still be busy and eventually, you will sell what you 
produced.

Find out what is needed and wanted. Listen to people and see if you can use 
your skills to improve their workplaces. Always be prepared to help people; 
it comes back in the most remarkable ways...

 I've covered a fair bit of this in other forums and discussions but here is 
a summary:

1. The single most expressed "boo-hoo" has been the removal of the START 
button. I find this particular reaction (and it is usually from people who 
should know better) pathetic. The whole START screen replaces it and you can

button does it without any need for touch or mouse movement.

phone, your tablet, your touch device, or your home computer. You may say 
you don't want the same user interface for your bicycle as you do for your

all the devices I am likely to use, makes sense to me.
