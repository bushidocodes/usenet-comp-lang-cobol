```
My apologies for the lack of knowledge and experience I'll exhibit in asking
these questions.  I'm primarly a Win/Web programmer that has been asked to
assist in dissolving a complication in upgrading a mbr/vcobol to 32-bit.

Our requirement is that we have a routine that will execute a batch file,
wait for its completion, and continue the program flow.  Not a spectacular
requirement by any means, but one that has presented problems.

Using the "native" CHAINR command and calling the winapi
CreateProcess/WaitForSingleObject routines has yielded the same result: The
calling and called program deadlocks.  If you kill either process, the other
continues as intended.

CreateProcess is called in a pedestrian manner that has worked well for me
in other places.  CHAINR has been called with various arguments.  Some of
the flailing attempts have been:

CMD.EXE /c d:\bob.bat
CMD.EXE /c START /wait CMD.EXE /c d:\bob.bat

Are we doing something wrong, or does mbr have a specific difficulty with NT
4.0?  Also, what is the best way to do this?
```
