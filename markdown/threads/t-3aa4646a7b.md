I think the assumption is much simpler than that. The VAST majority of
the spam comes from @gmail.com addresses. Ergo many believe it's
simpler to just filter @gmail.com.

I have had fairly good luck filtering things on my personal / private
news server. I have INN configured to use Cleanfeed and Cleanfeed
configured to reject messages from known problematic posters and replies
to known problematic messages. This filters messages from known
problematic posters, including unknown threads. This also filters
replies to problematic threads, including unknown posters. Both
unknowns get logged and become a new source of data to grow the list of
posters and reply messages to filter.

A few hours worth of work has made Usenet quite usable for me.

I recognize that not everybody can do what I'm doing. But I think that
a quite similar effect could be done in Thunderbird by ignoring
(sub)threads and filtering any new threads from problematic posters.
The only thing that I don't know how to do well is use this filtered
data as a source of new information. Perhaps that's a simple matter of
having the filter copy messages to a folder for harvesting data.

My algorithm (if you'll allow the use of the term) is as follows:

1) Is the message from a known bad posting account?
2) Is the message a reply to a known bad message?
3) If #1 and #2 are both true, mark as read.
4) If #1 but not #2 mark as read and copy to .
5) If #2 but not #1 mark as read and copy to .

Then I periodically take the lists 1 and 2 from tests #4 & #5 and
augment the tests in #2 and #1 (respectively).

Ultimately, once things are on both lists, they get filtered by #3 and I
never see or bother with them again.

I choose to do this on my server where I think it's easier and it has
the benefit of making sure that these messages never come into my server
or client.
