```
How to dynalloc a dsn from within a MVS/COBOL application?

No native COBOL facility exists to accomplish this task, however....

MVS (actually all the way back to MVT in the 1970's) provides an interface
called SVC 99, also known as DYNALLOC. This was originally constructed to
support TSO, and was later "opened up" and documented.

Unfortunately, the interface to SVC 99 resembles Swahili (hope I spelled
that correctly). The documentation for the interface makes the interface
itself look good. In addition, only assembler language can access the
interface.

All is not lost, however.... some years ago a set of routines to provide
high level language (such as COBOL) access to SVC 99 services were
written. More than one set of these routines were written.

These routines were all accessable with COBOL CALLs.

Multiple sets of these routines exist on a repository known as the "CBT
Tape". This tape is available to members of the SHARE organization, and
(I beleive) the GUIDE organization (both IBM mainframe user groups). In
additioin, I have seen the CBT files (including one I wrote back in the
1970's) distributed on a CD issued by NASP (National System Programmer's
Association).

I am not sure what the copyright and distribution terms are on these
routines. I beleive that any shop which is a GUIDE or SHARE member has
access to them.

In many cases, I suspect, the tape may have been obtained years ago by the
MVS system programmer, who picked some files that interested them, and
ignored the rest. You may want to inquire around your installation. By
the way these routines do not require "APF Authorization" (known as very
dangerous to MVS sysprogs).
```
