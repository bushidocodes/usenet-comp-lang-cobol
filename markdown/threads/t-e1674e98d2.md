I always keep two non-IBM COBOL books at hand.  One is "VS COBOL II:
highlights and Techniques" by James G. Janossy (ISBN 0-471-57205-5)
and "Advanced ANSI COBOL with Structured Programming: For VS COBOL II
and Microsoft/Micro focus COBOL" by Gary DeWard Brown (ISBN
0-471-54786-7) which is, of course, colored blue (inside joke for
those who tell the FNG's in one's shop to "get the Brown book").  I
keep two IBM COBOL books handy:  the Language Reference and
Programming Guide.  I refer to the IBM books first, but if I need a
more non-jargoneze description on the purpose of the syntax then I
refer to the others.

The Janossy is good for transistion to COBOL-85 from COBOL-74 and even
tells you what verbs are new or dropped.  Both Janossy and Brown has
good examples and code you can use to get started in the brave new
COBOL.

Enjoy,
