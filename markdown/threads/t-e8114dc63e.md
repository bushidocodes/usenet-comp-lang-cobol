It occurred to me, after reading several answering posts,
that you may actually be asking a different question (or
need to).

How did your job get ABENDed? 

Does the computing environment you are running in handle
data set enqueuing for JOBs (by recognizing that JOB ???? on
a given system will conflict with another "task" on a second
system) and control JOB initiation? (I guess I'm asking if
you are in a JES3 environment?)

Let us assume that you are in a JES3 environment. If you
know in advance the name of the DSN to be dynamically
allocated, you can do the following in your JCL and stop the
enqueuing problem(s):


//xxxx JOB (whatever),your-id,.....
    .
    .
//STEP1  EXEC PGM=IEFBR14,COND=(0,GT)   <<<=== this step
will not get executed
//DD1    DD   DSN= <<<===dynamically allocated DSN 
//            DISP=   <<<=== see comments below
//DD2 .......
//* End of fake 'em out step

OK, what does this accomplish? Well, in a JES3 environment
it causes JES3 to see the DSN(s) in question for job
scheduling purposes.

AH, but if the DSN does not exist, you will fail during JCL
Parse/Scan under JES3, UNLESS you code the DD1 to give dummy
allocation information. Remember, this step will not
execute, so even if you are going to run IDCAMS and create a
VSAM D/S, there will be no conflict. The system cannot
assume this step won't execute, so during
converter/interpreter (JES3) the DSN is checked for
existence...

The idea here is to get the system to check your DISP
information and prevent what could be (or become) a deadly
embrace (two jobs running and they need the same data sets
but they ask for them in the reverse order...).

Why would I give you this idea as a solution to your
problem?  Well, as a software developer, in order to get
things to work in a JES3 environment, we had to find a way
around JES3 looking at JCL and seeing a non-creative DISP
(e.g., OLD, SHR, etc.) and failing the JOB because the DSN
does not exist (JES3 doesn't realize that the IDCAMS step(s)
may be creating the DSN). This little trick then allowed us
to submit jobs wherein we knew we would be touching certain
DSNs dynamically, BUT there is no reference in the JCL,
which can lead to the DEADLY EMBRACE problem. [And it
allowed us to write installation code without regard to JES2
or JES3 environments because this technique works for both.]

So, the non-executing STEP referencing the DSN the way we
will be using it (DISP=SHR if exists AND we won't need it
exclusive, DISP=MOD if it does/doesn't exist and we will
either create it or otherwise need it EXCLUSIVE) causes the
JOB to be handled automatically with out the submitter
having to manually check on the status of n DSNs.
