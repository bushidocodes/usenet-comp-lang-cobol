I was flamed (deservedly) for posting some job listings promising
BIG$$$$ for mainframe programmers/analysts on this newsgroup last week.
I originally wrote these listings a year ago when I was still in Finance
and anything over $15.38/hr sounded pretty good to me. Since then, I've
looked at a lot of print ads throughout the country promising high rates
for MVS/Cobol II/IMS DC/DB2/CICS/JCL....(you know the drill) and I think
that they are hype and BS.

So....I'm volunteering to compile the "1st Annual Unofficial
comp.lang.cobol Salary Survey" and publish the results March 1, 1997.
Here's the rules:

1)Please submit your gross earnings for 1996 (you know... w-2 or 1099)
2)you can be assured of complete confidentiality
3)Please indicate your skill level:
a)good b)better c)best
4)Please indicate your primary region of employment, eg. West, East,
Mideast, North...
5)Please indicate the nearest major city
6)Please don't exaggerate-there will be NO PRIZES AWARDED or friends
impressed

In return, I will promise not to use any fancy statistical methods or
mind-numbing regression analysis.
I'll just add all the numbers by catagory and divide by the number of
respondents - I think that this might be interesting, albeit somewhat
unscientific.

Please be economical in your responses - my email already overflows w/
get rich quick offers and MLM schemes.

Regards,
Steve Wray " Confucious says - Man with 2 watches, never knows what time
it is"

I'm preparing a longer analysis offline on this salary mess - what
sparked it was talking to two different consultant-programmers. Both
said they had recruiter-pals who were being squeezed and pounded on by
companies, "You call yourself a recuiter! Get people in here who are so
stupid that they will work for 1995 salaries! We don't care how hard
YOU have to work or what lies you have to tell!"

One of them is thinking about getting out of the recruiting business.

I believe that a kid just out of school with a BS degree in CS, IT, IS,
can get $50-60K today, 4 years experience on top of the BS gets you
$85K.

Of course, I would expect the BS degree to be from a good university
with good grades and the experience to be applicable to the job.
