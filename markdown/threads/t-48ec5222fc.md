I've done something like this at home on my PC. I don't know if this is
going to work in your environment but at least it's a suggestion.

My approach:
1) Copy the ACCESS-tables as sequential-files.
=> Use a VB-module in the ACCESS-database.
2) Copy the records of the sequential-file to the target-file.

Tips:
VB:
Beware of the single-,double- and memo-format. They can vary in size.
Check the length of every string you write. It makes sure the
process won't
be corrupted.
Open the output-file in mode binary. Then you are sure ACCESS doesn't
add junk to your output.
COBOL:
Check for error: EOR before EOF.

I hope you can use this,

Paul Heetebrij
