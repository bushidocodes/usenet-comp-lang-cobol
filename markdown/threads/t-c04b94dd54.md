Hello you COBOL people,

As a newbie to COBOL I have read with interest your recent postings.  I
joined a company last April in the UK to re-train in IT at age 38.  I
currently have half  a degree from study with the Open University.  I am
one of 150 new starters, half who are now placed with client companies.  I
myself have been Y2K testing with Xerox thougth only user acceptance
testing, but it is a good way to get to know the systems.  Training
consisted of 3 months writing COBOL programs using MicroFocus workbench (10
of us at a time, a new group every fortnight !!).  The programs were not
too taxing though the latter ones did use about 9 input/output files so
plenty to think about for a newcomer.  I was paid a full salary whilst
training comparable to my previous (corporate finance) role and was also
paid at full pay for 5 weeks whilst waiting for a placement.  On completion
of training we were tested using TeckChek.  Whilst hoping to get into a
mainstream role soon I agree with an earlier comment "take what you can". 
I am currently using Fujitsu V3 to convert my old Pascal progs to COBOL and
I am also working through Mastering COBOL Programming (ISBN 0-333-69343-4).
 This is an excellent intro to COBOL with lots of sample programs
(available via FTP) nearly all the guys on my course used it.  I hope my
story is of some interest to you all either experienced or like me a new
guy.
