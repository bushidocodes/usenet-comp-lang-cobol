I evaluated several Viasoft products for MasterCard a few years ago. We
have them, but I believe we don't use them to their full potential,
because they require TSO. We are much more of CICS shop than a TSO
shop, and our TSO is tuned so that it really runs slow.

Most of our mainframe application programmers use a CICS-hosted ISPF
lookalike, so SmartEdit cannot be used there. You need to be in TSO and
use the real ISPF. SmartTest was a good product, and has been around
for many years, but Viasoft now only supports a TSO connection thru VTAM
for CICS debugging. So we still use Xpeditor within CICS regions.

They are very capable tools. We also used Estimate 2000 for our Y2K
inventory, and it was pretty useful, but Viasoft's Y2K approach is to
expand all dates everywhere, which makes it less helpful if you would
prefer to use windowing in selected applications.

My experience is that you really need to be a TSO shop to take full
advantage of their tools, and it can take a long time to learn them.
The tools are reliable and the support is good.

Arnold Trembley
Software Engineer I (just a job title, still a programmer)
MasterCard International
St. Louis, Missouri
