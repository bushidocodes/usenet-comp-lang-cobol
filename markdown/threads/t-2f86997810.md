Richard Jackson wrote:
› 
› The shop I am currently at has Cobol II and CICS, vintage unknown
› but > 2.1 but not Y2K compliant.
› If they upgrade CICS to a Y2K compliant version, is it mandatory
› that anything else be upgraded?

In general, and assuming we're discussing your application programs, no.
We're running CICS/ESA 4.1.0 (our MVS level is 5.3.2, or something like
that) and are still executing OS/VS Cobol programs, some compiled
several years ago. As always, you'll have to check for any restrictions
on your then-current release (and the intervening ones, too) to see if
you have anything which is now unsupported/illegal.

BTW, be sure to save copies of CICS ver 3.x.x Cobol command translator &
stub - you'll need them if you want to recompile any OS/VS Cobol
programs. The ver. 4.x.x translator doesn't support OS/VS Cobol.

› Will Cobol II still run?
 
› Yes.
 
› They have many VSAM datasets, some old,old IMS modules.

I don't see the VSAM datasets as a problem, per se. Be sure to check the
keys, including AIXs, for dates - these will likely have an affect on
your processing. (g>

I know nothing about IMS, so that's what I'll say.

This is right off the top - feel free to ask for more info if needed,
