deal with terminals long before the advent of the PC.

When I go to the CICS Conferences (good number of COBOL programmers attend)
I think what I am seeing is not that the language is dying, but many of the
people who know it are.  COBOL programmers are not a renewable resource.
Most colleges in my area (Boston) don't even know that it still exists.

IBM is pushing Java, in order to go with the flow.  From now on you will
code a Bean via a series of Visual Age wizards, drag and drop it to a
container (deploy it to CICS) and fill out the Bean property sheet (really
an HTTP enabled BMS map).

In the meantime, I am watching COBOL programmers count their gray hairs.
But to answer your question, I don't think COBOL is dying.

Regards,
