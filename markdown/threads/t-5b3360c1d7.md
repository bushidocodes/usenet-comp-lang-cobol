```
Hi,

Can anyone tell me which newsgroup this should be posted to. I'm trying to 
contact mainframe (IBM, ICL, etc.) users with Cobol-based systems who would
like to migrate to Unix but can't.

My company offers a service through a proprietary toolset which automatically
converts mainframe Cobol hierarchical database or flat file systems to 
relational systems on Unix. In the process we remove the need for a monitor
(such as CICS) and the end product requires no further licencing other than 
the RDBMS. The process is automated, involves no rewriting or re-engineering
and is completed within three to six months. 

The customer gets a system, functionally identical to the old one (warts
all!) and places the customer in a position where they can further develop any
parts of the system they feel can make better use of the RDBMS, SQL, client
server, etc.

So far we've done a couple of migrations here in the UK and I'm looking to
cast the net further afield. If anyone is interested or knows the best forum
for this, please let me know.

Thanks
```
