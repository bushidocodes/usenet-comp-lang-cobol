```
I have only skimmed all the current notes, but was wondering if anyone had 
posted something that said that one of the structures actually ABSOLUTELY 
prevented some type of "bad coding" technique or whether one "guaranteed" easier 
to maintain code.

It has always been my position that there are "good" styles (sets of rules) that 
a programmer or shop could set up that would produce "easier to maintain and 
less error-prone" (non-spaghetti) code.  Once a programmer was familiar with 
such a style, they could pick up a "new" program and understand its structure 
and maintain it in a comparable way.

I can describe such "good" (IMHO) techniques using either Paragraphs-only or 
Sections (with or without paragraphs other than exit paragraphs).  I can also 
tell (for either type) how a "bad" programmer could mess up such a program.

I guess this note is just because it always surprises me that some of those 
posting on this (reoccurring) topic always SOUND as if they think they will 
"convince" someone who uses (and is used to) one approach to change their mind - 
and this certainly never happens.
```
