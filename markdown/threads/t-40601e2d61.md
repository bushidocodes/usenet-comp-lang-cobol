I'm not sure this is a wise thing to do right now. :) 

First, you *can* do it. You use the ACCESS ODBC driver
that comes with VB, and a COBOL that knows how to
talk ODBC. VisualAge does, and I think that AcuCOBOL and
MicroFocus do as well. I'm not absolutely positive
about them though. 

Should you do it that way is another animal, and
just free advice, I would say "no."

If you really want to use Access as your database,
then just use VB and Access. This will be much eaiser
and reliable for you than inserting COBOL in the middle.

Or better yet, use one of the COBOLS that can do windoze
front ends (VisualAge, AcuCOBOLE, MicroFocus, Fujitsu)
and just do it all with COBOL files.

If you *must* separate out the database part, you r
will be well advised to use SQL Server or another
similar database (DB2, Oracle, etc.) as your database,
and Cobol *or* VisualBasic as your front end. 

Trust me, what you are wanting to do doesn't make
a lot of sense. 

Free advice, which I hesitated to give, but
which I will stand by. :) 

-Paul
