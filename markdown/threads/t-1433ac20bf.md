As long as you haven't used MF specific items, such as called routines
"CBL_.. or "PC_.. etc then it should be OK to go to any compiler that
you can get SP2 for.  

There are a few MFisms such as DE-EDITing that are MF specific too.

> I believe they are discontinuing support for the 4.0 compiler.  
> When I finish the rewrite with the 16 bit, will I be able to
> recompile and go? 

Just about, or you could take it to Unix/Linux in a day or so.

>  How about data files, are they compatible with both
> 16 and 32 bit programs?  

Yes.

> I am used to compiling gnts and putting them in
> a lbr which makes them easy to handle.  I believe that is not possible
> with NE, everything is an exe, right?  I would appreciate comments on
> these and other obstacles I face.

If you built .EXEs under 3.4 (or 4.x) and checked the list in the manual
you can distribute free.  With Net Express there is no such mechanism -
you require licenced application servers for every distribution on a
per-user basis.  There is some concession for small developers if you
ask nicely.

If you work on window system, I don't think NetExpress is a good solution.
Fujitsu cobol is a much better choice.  I have been using Micro Focus cobol
from V2.2, to netexpress 3.0.   Before netexpress, it is protected by a
keypro,  so you can install on you desktop, notebook, and still get mobility
just by carrying keypro around.   NetExpress is protected by a softwarekey,
so everything is tied to one machine.  To change machine, you need to
uninstall and reinstall with the 'permission' from MicroFocus. Here is the
biggest problem, if you crash your system,  you got no chance to uninstall
first,  you will get a painful process with microfocus that make you feel
like a thief.  Anyway, my suggestion is that don't choose NetExpress, choose
Fujitsu cobol.
