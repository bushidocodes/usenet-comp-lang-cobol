	Can't help on the manuals, but the folks who developed Bull CP-6
	(a fine OS, never widely adopted, unfortunately) used a feature
	in that OS to define a console profile for one.  It was a "Device"
	console, and they configured it to use a specific "language" file
	to issue its messages.  Device consoles on CP-6 were used to report
	and administer devices like printers and tape drives -- so when
	a tape needed to be mounted or a form on a printer needed to be
	changed, their system would issue audible reports to the operations
	staff.
	
	One of the neat things about CP-6 was that error/console messages
	could be ESCALATED when the OS cycled around and repeated them
	due to inaction.  Thus the "requests" for operator action eminating
	from the Votrax console got more and more caustic and abusive as
	the operators ignored them ;-)  

	Gosh, how I miss that OS.  Now I'm stuck with boring OpenVMS ;-{
