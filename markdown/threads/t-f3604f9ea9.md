Thank you all for responding and for offering to help. Since I've had so
many responses, I'll take the off-topic bandwith to fill everybody in at
the same time. The original message is quoted at the bottom. Here's the
long and the short of it.

Short...DD names, step names, and other things that go at the left edge of JCL
cannot support symbols. I finally found it in our current IBM manuals.

Long...Our production control staff uses CA-7/11 to run our jobs. Our system
programmers have control of IMS and its JCL. A couple of years ago, we application
programmers took control of the IMS JCL so we could put it into procs and gain
safer control of our own JCL (more symbolic substitution and less JCL overall).
Now, with nested procs and includes, we are willing to give some control back to
the system programmers as long as we can retain the flexibility and safety we
already have.

I will recommend to our people that we try not to use nested procs and that we
limit ourselves to includes. This gives us the ability to nest the JCL and to
reduce the amount of it. It also means that we will continue to run with jobs
that have only one level of proc; so that our internal controls on JCL overrides
can continue to be used safely.

Thanks again.
