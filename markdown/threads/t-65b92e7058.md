```
I found my "logic" error (in my program using FUNCTION MOD and RANDOM) and I
am *not* a happy camper.  I don't know what other vendor's compilers will do
with this (or even what Fujitsu V4.2 would do - but it turns out that V4.0
is doing some "odd" things with their intermediate results and compute.
(All perfectly "legal" but still not pretty).  If you are interested (and
I'll let the Fujitsu-believers see it - to believe it), try compiling and
running the following (non-GUI) program, and I think you will see what is
(and is not) happening:

 @OPTIONS MAIN
 Identification Division.
  Program-ID. TestRand.
 Data Division.
 WORKING-STORAGE SECTION.
  01  Random-Num  Pic 9(05) Comp-5.
 01  Random-Num2  Pic 9(05)v9(05) Comp-5.
 01  Num-in-List  Pic 9(03).
 01 Table-of-Results.
   05  Each-approach
     occurs 4 times
     indexed by Ind1.
      10 Each-Num
         occurs 10 times
         indexed by Ind2
             Pic 9(03).
 PROCEDURE DIVISION.
* Setup RANDOM series
  Compute Random-Num = Function Random (1)
      Move 45 to Num-in-List
*
      Set Ind1 to 1
      Perform
        Varying Ind2 from 1 By 1
        until Ind2 > 10
         Compute Random-Num = Function Integer ((Function Random) * 10000)
         Compute Random-Num = (Function Mod (Random-Num Num-In-List)) + 1
          Move Random-Num to Each-Num (Ind1 Ind2)
      End-Perform
*
      Set Ind1 to 2
      Compute Random-Num = Function Random (1)
      Perform
        Varying Ind2 from 1 by 1
        until Ind2 > 10
         Compute Random-Num = Function Integer ((Function Random) *
10000.000)
         Compute Random-Num = (Function Mod (Random-Num Num-In-List)) + 1
         Move Random-Num to Each-Num (Ind1 Ind2)
      End-Perform
*
      Set Ind1 to 3
      Compute Random-Num = Function Random (1)
      Perform
        Varying Ind2 from 1 by 1
        until Ind2 > 10
          Compute Random-Num2 = ((Function Random) * 10000)
         Compute Random-Num = Function Integer (Random-Num2)
         Compute Random-Num = (Function Mod (Random-Num Num-In-List)) + 1
         Move Random-Num to Each-Num (Ind1 Ind2)
      End-Perform
     Perform
       Varying Ind2 from 1 by 1 until Ind2 > 10
           Display "1:" Each-Num (1 ind2)
                " 2:" Each-Num (2 Ind2)
                " 3:" Each-Num (3 Ind2)
     End-Perform
     .
```
