Absolutely not. The copyright is for the person purchasing the book. It is 
not a licence to distribute. Uploading to a share site is a blatant 
copyright violation.

What you COULD legally do is assign your copyright to ONE other person and 
relinquish the book and the software to that person (transferral of 
licence).

However, as I mentioned a long time ago in the original thread, it would be 
good if Alchemy/Fujitsu simply re-asserted their rights to it or just 
released it to the public domain, given they are not supporting or using it, 
and it is hardly in conflict with their current product offerings.

Unless or until they do that, it remains their copyright for 70, 90, or 125 
years, depending on various factors. See US Copyright Law Title 17, Chapter 
3, para 302 (http://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/17/302.html )

(Oddly enough, duration of copyright outside the USA is much less than the 
American law, typically being 25 years from date of publication, and not 
having any dependency on the lifespan of the author.)
