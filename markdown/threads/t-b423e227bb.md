I would have to agree with Donald Tees.
I would add that I've been informed of a group who's attempting to
come up with a free cobol compiler for Linux also.  I would
expect to be seeing this in the flesh within the next 2 years.

Micro Focus has always been king of the PC COBOL systems.
Fujitsu might just take a good chunk away from them for price alone.

I forget what Version 2.0 seats went for, think $3,200 or more.
While Fujitsu was around $800 a seat.

Then you examine Borland, and Microsoft products in the C++ world
and you see their professional development platforms with SQL type
data bases, custom IDE and the whole nine yards are around $1,800
a seat.

I think in the long term, if the Linux group comes up with a free version
of a COBOL compiler I will probably lean that way.  Their GNU C compiler
is exceptionally stable and very upward compatible.

Their lack of interest in marketing is allowing them to produce a code which
has remained stable for many versions now in the C world.

MF is also very stable within MF's scope.  It's NT which keeps flipping
them every so often with new toys.

Charlie Ebert
