```
Here's another way (Ansi 85 COBOL, with '89 extensions) to validate a
date.

The Working-Storage.

01  Date-Work-Area.
      03  Date-To-Check.
            05  D-Year Pic 9(4).
            05  D-Month Pic 99.
            05  D-Day Pic 99.
      03  Date-To-Check-N Redefines Date-To-Check Pic 9(8).
      03  Rem-1 Pic 9(4).
      03  Valid-Flag Pic X value Spaces.
            88  Date-Invalid Value "I".

The Procedure Division.

Move Space to Valid-Flag
Evaluate D-Month Also D-Day
   When 1 Also 1 thru 31
   When 3 Also 1 thru 31
   When 4 Also 1 thru 30
   When 5 Also 1 Thru 31
   When 6 Also 1 thru 30
   When 7 Thru 8 Also 1 Thru 31
   When 9 Also 1 thru 30
   When 10 Also 1 thru 31 
   When 11 Also 1 thru 30
   When 12 Also 1 thru 30
       Continue
   When 2 Also 1 thru 29
       If D-Day = 29
          Compute Rem-1 = Function Rem (Date-To-Check 4)
          If Rem-1 = Zeros 
             Compute Rem-1 = Function Rem (Date-To-Check 100)
             If Rem-1 = Zeros
                Compute Rem-1 = Function Rem (Date-To-Check 400)
                If Rem-1 = Zeros
                   Continue
                Else
                   Set Invalid-Date to True
                End-if
             End-if 
          Else
             Set Date-Invalid to True
          End-if
       End-if
       Continue
   When Other
       Set Date-Invalid To True
End-Evaluate
.

Short and sweet. <G>  (But you do need ANSI 85 COBOL with the 89
extensions).
```
