Haven't looked in a while, but I'm fairly certain both 3.0 and 3.1 came with  a
small starter handbook - and this was one of the things covered. There's more to
this in terms of distribution of the end product (Look at on-line help on
"Distributing your Application", support DLLs etc.),  and I'm assuming you are
using the IDE :-

1 - create project and add source files. The sub-directory holding your source
will have sub-directories \Debug and \Release
2 - Top Left it reads "Generic Debug Build" - from "Project" dropdown, selecting
Build or whatever gets your "ints" into the \Debug sub-directory
3 - to get your EXE(s) change the 'Generic Debug Build" to "Generic Release
Build" and then build again; results stored in \Release sub-directory.
