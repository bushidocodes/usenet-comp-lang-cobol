Getting back to COBOL <G>

 It should be noted that both ACCEPT FROM TIME and FUNCTION CURRENT-DATE
support "00:00" thru "23:59" values and *not* "00:01" thru "24:00" as a
valid times.  HOWEVER, J4 and WG4 have "discussed" the fact that the *new*
intrinsic function (in the draft Standard) "allows" for (but does not
REQUIRE) support for "24:00" as a returned value from the Locale-Time
function - in the case where a "locale" is defined allowing for 2400.

Jerry P wrote:

> Remember, to us purists, 1200 hours cannot be resolved to either AM or
> PM while 0000 hours is either AM or PM.

To language purists, our digital 12 hour watches are wrong twice a day.
They never say 12:00 M or 12:00 MM, or even 12 noon or midnight, but
instead have the oxymoron of 12:00 AM, which means 12:00 in the time
before noon. or 12:00 PM which means 12:00 in the afternoon.

While 00:00 hours doesn't mean either AM or PM, it unambiguously means
midnight, as does 24:00.

But even the term midnight is ambiguous when we have customers in
different time zones.  I watched a football game where they announced "60
minutes will immediately follow the game except on the west coast when it
will be seen on its regular time".  As a customer of the network, I
resented the fact that they forgot about my Rocky Mountain time, where it
also would be seen on its regular time. - Always be aware of the customer.

On a different note - with digital clocks, will the words widdershins and
deasil make a comeback?
