Serial communications is not my specialty, but a quick trip through the
MS-Windows Platform SDK indicates that the functions necessary to
open/read/write communications resources are included in the standard API.

This includes such mutlti-purpose functions as CreateFile, ReadFile and
WriteFile; and a series of specialty functons including SetupComm,
WaitCommEvent and TransmitCommChar.

A complete article on serial communications is in the "Techincal Articles"
section of the PSDK; the article is titled
"Serial Communications in Win32" by Allen Denver, Microsoft Windows
Developer Support, December 11, 1995.

I do not have an on-line URL direct to this (my PSDK is on CD), but you can
go to the Microsoft Developer's start page at
http://msdn.microsoft.com/library/default.asp  and do a search for "serial
communications" to find all kinds of info.

I also never heard of cobapi32.dll. Neither, it seems, has Microsoft's
'search' function.
