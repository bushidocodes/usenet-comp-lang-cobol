Assuming there is nothing extra in the line such as 'SIGN
LEADING/TRAILING SEPARATE' or a USAGE clause like COMP-3,  PIC S999V999
will occupy 6 bytes.  In EBCDIC, the sign will cause the zone part of
the last byte (first 4 bits) to change:  a positive number ending in 2
would be a 'B' while a negative 2 would be 'K'.  This dates from punched
card usage.

The 'V' tells the compiler this field contains three digits after the
decimal point.  Code generated when this field is used will account for
this.  HTH
