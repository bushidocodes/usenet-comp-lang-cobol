programmer finding the post (either in the current NG or in its archives)
and getting wrong information.

So the question is:

Do others think that I should simply add this person to my killfile and
"ignore" his posts - or should I keep an eye out for his latest error(s) and
post corrections?  Obviously, if he continues posting erroneous "facts" (not
opinions) and others post replies - I will continue to monitor the replies
(whatever the original post says).

What are others doing?  What do you recommend?

Normally I don't comment much on grammar... but the examples (some from
http://www.dictionary.com) that can be dredged up for this'un are too good

Small nit: 'smite' is a fine, old word, a 'strong' verb with irregular 
inflections; 'smote' is the past tense/participle and a future use 
('will...') should take the uninflected form.  Consider:

And David . . . took thence a stone, and slang it, and smote the 
Philistine in his forehead. --1 Sam. xvii. 49.

(wow... not only a 'smote' but a 'slang', too!)

Saul . . . smote the javelin into the wall. --1 Sam. xix. 10.

And if thou refuse to let them go, behold, I will smite all thy borders 
with frogs:  --Ex. viii. 2.

The LORD will smite thee with the botch of Egypt, and with the emerods, 
and with the scab, and with the itch, whereof thou canst not be healed.  
--Deut. xxviii. 27.

(note 'will smite')

This all was triggered, actually, by my remembering a bit of Chaucer which 
used an even more archaic form in 'The Miller's Tale':

And he was redy with his iren hoot,
And Nicholas amydde the ers he smoot.
