Hmmm. Well there are only three things I can think of at the moment:

1. Linking (binding) your modules: check module attributes, especially entry
point, reusability, AMODE and RMODE; no use of DLL linkages

2. There may well be a compiler bug (sounds most likely in the PL/I compiler or
PL/I runtime component of LE); these don't happen all that frequently, but they
do happen, and the circumstances you are describing seem unusual enough that
there may not have been testing to cover that case.

3. Perhaps there is some runtime setting that is getting in the way; I would
check these (and I don't have any particular reasons, they just seem to be ones
that might influence the behavior you are seeing): ALL31; ERRCOUNT - must be 0
for PL/I apps; PLITASKCOUNT(n) - try either 1 or 0 for 'n';

Just some guesses because I don't really have any more intuitions based on your
information. Good luck.
