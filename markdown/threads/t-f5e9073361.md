We're actually familiar with Acu4GL in general, because we've been using
Acu4GL for Oracle (A4O) for years. But with A4O we didn't have to install a
bunch of stored procedures and lock administration tables. This could be
because the Acu4GL product has evolved over time, or it could be that A4S
required some kludges to be workable.

Unfortunately, sometimes the real "gotcha"s don't become apparent until
you've purchased the product and are attempting to use it in a production
environment. I'm just wondering if there's any such item that made you
scream with rage and regret buying the product.

As to the row limit, that appears to be for 6.5 or 7.0 . v8.0 has a limit of
8K, and that doesn't necessarily include text fields, so I don't really
anticipate a problem there.

As to just install-and-run, I remember with A4O that there were a couple of
segmented secondary key setups that A4O simply couldn't handle, so we had to
kludge the file structures, but that was just an irritation. I do anticipate
some headaches. Just no migraines.
