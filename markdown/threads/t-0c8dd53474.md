```
For those of you (us) who have given up on the comp.software.year-2000
newsgroup, you might want to check out the new MODERATED newsgroup

    comp.software.year-2000.tech

I just read the following post that may help you

"It's been brought to my attention that valid posts are bouncing
somewhere between the point of origin and the moderation account
at stump.algebra.com.  These posts are NOT being rejected by the
modbot -- they aren't reaching it.

I have asked the people who maintain the list of moderation addresses
to redirect submissions directly to the moderation account here
at NIC.com.  That will probably take a few days.  In the meantime,
please be aware that you can send submissions via email directly
to the modbot, <csy2kt@nic.com>.

FYI:

* Anyone who voted to create the group is already on the preapproved
list.  Your post will appear without moderator intervention unless
it contains a term from our fairly short "bad words" list.  (This
assumes you post under the same address you used to vote.)  Using a
phrase from the "bad words" list does not automatically cause the
modbot to reject a post.  It only causes it to be held for review.

* The only posts rejected sight-unseen are crossposted to more than
two other groups, or include a binary.  If the human moderator rejects
a post, he should be able to cite a reason that appears on the group's
Web page.  (Posting in HTML is an example.  Posting job listings is
another.)

* You may appeal the moderator's decision by replying to the rejection
notice -- your reply automatically goes to the group proponent for a
second opinion.

* If you have a "nospam" address, any reply you get is a courtesy
extended by the moderator.  As traffic increases, we must rely on
the modbot more heavily.

Followups set to poster.  I'll summarize frequently-asked questions,
but please check the Web page at http://www.nic.com/~csy2kt/ before
sending mail.
```
