In the 1970s, Richard Nixon tried some things to reduce energy
consumption in the US.  One was the 55 mph speed limit which has since
been repealed.

Another was the imposition of year-round Daylight Savings Time.  The
theory possibly being that we'd save on business lighting costs if a
larger percentage of each workday occurred during daylight hours.  This
failed, among other reasons, because school children would have had to
travel to school before sunrise during some parts of the winter and
there was a lot of backlash from parents.  Also, many of the energy
consumption concerns were related to gasoline and heating oil,
consumption of which would not be affected much by such a policy.

One of my favorite cartoons of that time had three panels, each showing
Nixon with a blanket and a pair of scissors.  The captions read "Today 
I'm going to show how to make a blanket one foot longer", "by cutting a 
foot off of one end", "and sewing it onto the other end".
