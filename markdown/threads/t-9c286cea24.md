To any of you are "current" on your paid support for Fujitsu's COBOL product
and have used (or are currently using) their ServiceWise online support
service:

I submitted one problem on March 4th and another on March 5th.  By March
6th, ServiceWise support changed the status to "in development" (and I have
heard nothing to indicate that either US or Japan support/development have
had any problems recreating the software bugs).

On March 12th, I requested ANY update - even a status update or an estimate
on when I should EXPECT a status update.

Since my last ServiceWise update, it appears that NOTHING has happened - at
least I have seen nothing (even a request to "wait for 'x' amount of time
for a status update) from Fujitsu in my ServiceWise incident.

   ***

Is this type of "response" what others have found with ServiceWise reports
of software bugs?  If so, have you found any way to get more responsive
support and what was it?

Thank you in advance for any suggestions (other than switching vendors) that
you might have.

I have no experience with ServiceWise or Fujitsu support (except I almost went
to work for them, another thread). I write to describe IBM's similar evolution
20-30 years earlier. 

In the beginning, IBM sent fixes to everyone 'on support'. They found it didn't
work well for one of three reasons, depending on whom you believe. 

1.  It caused more problems than it fixed. 
2.  It gave the impression that IBM software was full of bugs. 
3.  Users complained about needing to install almost daily fixes. 

So IBM changed to 'as needed' mode. If you queried the database or called,
they'd send the fix; otherwise, not .. until the next release. 

Pragmatically, it is probably the best solution.
