```
If your compiler (that you don't know what it is) supports intrinsic functions,
use the one for getting the full date.

If your compiler supports the 2002 Standard (or as an extension to the '85
Standard) optional keywords on the ACCEPT statement, use that.

If your compiler doesn't support any of the above, but does support access to
the system date (via a systems call), use that.

If your compiler doesn't support any of the above, then you will probably have
to use logic to GUESS what century it is (possibly depending upon what year it
is).

  ***

Note:
  If this is for a COBOL class, I suggest attending your classes, reading the
books, and going to your labs. Any of these may help.
```

```
When you compile your program most compilers print a date on the listing.
Usually it is a 4 digit year that the compiler will "print out on a
cobol programm". Also, usually, the compiler will print its name which
will tell you what it is. So look at one of your compile listings you
should have your answers.

IBM OS/VS COBOL   January 16, 1904.
```

```
Let's break this down, shall we? Are you having trouble GETTING today's date
or are you having trouble PRINTING today's date?

If your difficulty is GETTING today's date, there are several solutions, not
all of which may work on your compiler:

I. ACCEPT TODAYS-DATE FROM DATE YYYYMMDD
   where TODAYS-DATE is defined as PIC X(8).

II. MOVE FUNCTION CURRENT-DATE TO TODAYS-DATE
   where TODAYS-DATE is defined as PIC X(21) the first 8 bytes are today's
date in yyyymmdd format
   the remaining are time of day. Actually, if you're not interest in the
time, you can define as PIC X(8).

III. Define:
   01  TODAYS-DATE.
     02  TODAY-DATE-CC  PIC X(2).
     02  TODAYS-DATE-YYMMDD PIC X(6).
then
  ACCEPT TODAYS-DATE-YYMMDD FROM DATE
  IF TODAYS-DATE-YYMMDD < '40'
      MOVE '20' TO TODAYS-DATE-CC
  ELSE
      MOVE '19' TO TODAYS-DATE-CC.

IV (For extra credit)
   DISPLAY 'What is todays date in YYYYMMDD format?' UPON CONSOLE
   ACCEPT TODAYS-DATE FROM CONSOLE.
   where TODAYS-DATE is defined as X(6).

If you're having trouble DISPLAYING today's date, that exercise is left to
the reader.
```
