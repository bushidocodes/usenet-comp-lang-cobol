Kind of depends on what you mean by "best".

I would be inclined to suggest that the old Burroughs Medium System
(B2x00/3x00/4x00) and its Unisys V Series successors -- a hardware platform
*specifically designed* to run COBOL programs efficiently -- was arguably
the most COBOL-friendly platforms ever built.   Unfortunately for the many
fans of this machine, the remaining examples are rapidly being retired ...

The Burroughs B1000 series was also an excellent COBOL machine for its price
class, but I always felt that the COBOL "s-machines" on the B1000 were
largely emulations of the Medium System architecture and thus somewhat
derivative of it.

    -Chuck Stevens
