If the COBOL know-how you want to pick up is for the mainframe
environment,
a good reference book (suitable for folks with *some* grounding in
COBOL)
might be the following:
"MVS COBOL II - Power Programmer's Desk Reference" by David Shelby Kirk,
QED Technical Publishing, 1991.

I would certainly recommend the book to anyone who has done a fair
amount
of OS/VS COBOL, and wants to become familiar with the new language
elements introduced with COBOL II.
I believe there is also an updated version of the book for COBOL/370.
