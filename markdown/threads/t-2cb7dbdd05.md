Darn right there's always a rate or salary range. By the time the job REQ

company says, hey recruiter or jobshop, we're looking for a COBOL-geek w/ 3.5

calculated down to the penny.

The reasons they hold back the number is sometimes, perhaps most times, the
recruiter doesn't have a job REQ in hand and they are trolling for resumes
which have a street value. Yes, your resume can be resold from one recruiter
to another. Another reason is, in the case of jobshops, it is to their
advantage to pay you low and pocket the difference. Sure, the job can pay a
max of $32.75/hour they'll say to you while telling the client, whew, we're
lucky to get anyone, this geek will cost you $107/hour but keep the number
confidential, here, sign this official looking document, rates are business
confidential...

Keep pounding on the recruiters and jobshops. They can pay, rates are going up
