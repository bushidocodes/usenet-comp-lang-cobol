Since you got your program working, I won't look very closely (in fact,
I am being very cursory, but address style questions:

1.  Lots of shops like to have field names with a prefix pointing to the
01 name, making  it obvious which records are being referenced.

2.  Depending on your compiler, you may wish to leave BLKSIZE out of the
code, and let your system or JCL determine it.

3.  If you do not have any input card, HEADER-ERROR will still be 'N'
and you will try to read an input card again.  What I would do is
(pseudocode):

Read first record at end set record-eof to true.
display error as you did,
perform main-routine until record-eof.

Then in main-routine, I check the valid header and continue.

4.  The standard of starting paragraph names with numbers is one I
like.  But leave room for new paragraphs inserted logically between
existing paragraphs.  And move the numbers of I/O down to 800- (or
8000-), plus error routines to 900-, etc, giving them a logical
separation from the normal flow.

5.  I like putting each open and close as a separate statement - when it
errors, I know which file errored.

6.  Some people prefer GOBACK to STOP RUN, but I haven't found it makes
any real difference.

End of my very quick scan and reply.
