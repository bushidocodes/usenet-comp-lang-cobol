All I can say is that I use MicroFocus COBOL NetExpress (not
ServerExpress) for a system with up to 150 users, running on an NT
Network, and we get good performance.  Some speed trials I ran, whilst
probably 60-80 users were connected, could read through MF COBOL
indexed files, with some rewrites, at over 300,000 transactions per
minute.  Other tests, again over a network but with a single user,
reached 766,000 sequential reads per minute.  I was impressed - and
surprised!  Normal performance with variety of different programs
running, accessing different bits of data is probably not anywhere
near as fast as this, but is adequate.

Any idea how many, and what sort of reads or other transactions you
are doing in the 70 minutes?  I would be interested.

Phil Hickling
