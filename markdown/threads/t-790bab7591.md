You are just about 180 degrees out there buddy - if you are 
doing work on an IBM Mainframe, you cannot get better than
VisualAge COBOL. Indeed, the manual even states it is for
VM/MVS, AIX, and VisualAge COBOL. 

Microfocus, on the other hand, can deal with screens in ways 
that VisualAge can not. Until you get to graphics, at which
point, VisualAge again excells because the display and the
code are 100% separate. It is only in that very small intermediate
area that Microfocus' products really take a lead. 

Oh yes, and when working with the Web. VA Cobol has a couple
problems dealing with the interaction between CGI and the 
stdin/stdout streams.
