### COBOL Standard - consider yourself DISENFRANCHISED

- **From:** "William M. Klein" <wmklein@ix.netcom.com>
- **Date:** 1998-10-30T00:00:00
- **Newsgroups:** comp.lang.cobol,alt.cobol
- **Message-ID:** `<71bqi7$ntj@dfw-ixnews6.ix.netcom.com>`

```
In way of prefaces, as many readers of the COBOL newsgroup know, I think
there are many really wonderful new features in the draft of the next COBOL
Standard.  There are, however, still some problems (IMHO) - some of which
the committees agree with and some they do not.

HOWEVER, in J4 document, 98-0544, "Selected CD 1.4 quality review comments
for WG4 consideration", (in response to a request for a review period where
the public could provide input - before the "final committee draft" - which
is supposed to have no significant changes made to it), it states,

"Note:   Regarding the request to open the document for comments from the
public, Ms. Wallace reported (as WG4 convener) that it is not proper
procedure for WG4 to bypass the ISO process and send a document to limited
public review between the CD ballot and the FCD ballot.  Another WG4
internal review is appropriate as a normal part of preparing the document
for FCD."

What that means (as far as I can read it) is that you (the COBOL "public")
will have NO chance to provide input or comment upon any version of the
draft Standard - except if you go thru your "national body" or "WG4
delegation".  In the US, this means that the next public review period will
be for a document that will be put out on a "take it or leave it" basis (or
delayed yet more years).

My suggestions are to:

FIRST: make absolutely certain that you are made aware of how to provide
input to your national body's WG4 delegation.  In the US, you can do this by
sending email (also requesting that you receive all J4 email notices) to the
J4-TAG via Don Schricker at

     doncobol@mediaone.net

If you are a citizen of another country (or work for a non-US domiciled
company), I suggest that you contact your national Standards group or the
WG4 convener to find out about your country's WG4 participation.  Ann
Wallace, the WG4 convenor can be reached at

     NWALLACE@us.ibm.com

SECOND: you might want to comment to either or both of the people listed
above if you think there is a need for further public input - before the
final committee draft - on the Standard.  (As noted in another
comp.lang.cobol postings, the draft is no longer even available on-line and
may or may not be made so at any time in the future before the final review
period.)

P.S. Most of us want to do almost anything that will speed rather than
hinder the delivery of the next official COBOL Standard.  On the other hand,
I hope that those of you who have been or are watching its development
insure that your input can and will still be heard.
```
