Well it is a matter of timing.  It is already 1999 and if a company is
on schedule with their Y2K remediation, they should have finished
coding in 1998 and be well into Enterprise Testing by now.  Hence they
may not need coders for Y2K at this time.  If a company is still doing
remediation, they are way behind schedule and I would certainly have
to think twice about working for such a company.

As the Y2K remediation work is or should be nearly complete, companies
are now looking at doing everything they put off to do Y2K work.  That
may be what you are seeing.  

Another thought occurred to me as I was typing this.  It could be that
those companies that are not on schedule with Y2K and do still need
coders may not be advertising in the want ads as this could give the
company a very negative image and, if picked up on by the general
public, could drive their stock price down or have other unwanted
effects.
