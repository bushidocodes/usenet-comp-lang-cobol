Walkthroughs are an exceptional tool *if* they are done at clean
compile time.  Often they are done far to late and become a rubber
stamp exercise.  Done correctly they pay themselves back 3 times
(1 hour in takes out three hours testing) on average.

Walkthroughs of a skilled programmers work for a junior programmer
should still happen.  The review by a peer is always useful and
second the junior will gain knowledge about why things are
attacked a certain way.  Ask lots of questions and get under the
skin of these reviews.  Spend at least two hours before the review
understanding what is going on and ask if you can't figure it
out.  The bottom line is that the others are probably not thinking
and you can really change that.

If you think that the guru's code was that well done tell him to
post it and see what happens :-)

Good luck,
Ken
