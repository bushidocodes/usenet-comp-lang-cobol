Bob Wolfe has already responded with a list of COBOL-related URLs. My
own
pages (which Bob included in his list) specifically discuss dynamic
memory
allocation, data structures, and pointers, among other things.

The syntax used is that of VS COBOL II. Other dialects differ in the
details,
but the principles are the same. In fact you don't even really need
pointers
or dynamically allocated memory -- you can allocate a static array and
use
subscripts in place of pointers to stitch your nodes together.
