*Very* briefly:

Strengths:
    Simple to learn and program, ergo less error prone
    Easiest language to maintain over time
    Well written COBOL is the clearest language to read
    Efficient in time and resources
    Best language for writing business applications
    Extremely stable over 40 years

Weaknesses:
    Somewhat verbose
    Not well suited for system level programming (compilers, OS, etc.)

Talking about using COBOL for systems style programming.  Perhaps I could
relate an experience of mine.

A few years ago, I was lead-developer on a project that implemented a
real-time customer information system in COBOL.  It provided train running
information to about twenty stations in the Welsh valleys.  I wrote the
kernel of the system: lots of pointer manipulation.  I found no problems
using COBOL in this way; in fact, I found it preferable to C - and I've
written quite a few lines of C in my time !

And why did we use COBOL?    Portability.    V1 of the system was written on
DOS using the MF multi-threading environment on DOS.  It worked, amazingly.
But only for small stations, with a couple of train info displays.  It ran
out of stack space really fast.  V2 was ported to OS/2, and that was OK.  V3
was ported to AIX and that was where it started to work really well :)
