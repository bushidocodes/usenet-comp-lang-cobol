But back to the main thrust of the discussion.  I stopped wearing a suit
at the end of 1991 and all of my contracts have been on site; until this
year when I went to do a six month sentence without parole for an
insurance company.

Then it was buy a couple of suits, formal shirts, ties, shiny shoes and
the rest of it which was a bit of a drag.  However, I decided that if
they were going to insist that I wear the clobber, I would wear it as
well as it could be worn.  Nobody was going be able to fault me on dress
sense and I remained the most formally dressed individual there for my
six months.  I occasionally mentioned that I hadn't worn a suit for work
since 1991 and most people didn't seem to believe me, especially when
they knew the sort of client companies that I had been with.

The only downside was that it added about half an hour onto my morning
personal preparation, and about quarter of an hour when I got home.  I
hope the next place prefers the "smart casual" look.
