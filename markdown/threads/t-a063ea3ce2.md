Sounds like your "year" hasn't been on IBM mainframes. These are mainframe
"terms".

1) I don't know "ISBF" - but would guests that you mean "ISPF". although
there are lots of better (technically correct) definitions, this term is
usually used to refer to the "application development environment" in which
IBM (MVS usually - rather than VM - these are 2 operating systems)
programmers usually work.

2) IMS usually refers to a hierarchical database product (although it may
also refer to the data communication product - as these used to be bundled
together - under MVS)

3) The product "VS COBOL II" (in Release 3 and later) was an ANS'85 COBOL
compiler - but it also includes many IBM extensions. There have been 3
products (COBOL compilers) since then. The most current one is "IBM COBOL
for OS/390 & VM" (and more recently still you can add on the "Millenium
Language Extensions" to get even more syntax support.

You may want to look at DejaNews for many different threads on IBM acronyms
and products. You didn't ask about CICS, DB2, JCL, or LOTS of other things
that IBM mainframe (MVS particularly) COBOL programmers "take for granted".
