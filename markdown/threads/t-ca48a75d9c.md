```
I see that a few people seem to think that decent job offers post the
salary with them.

I am still a "baby-programmer" in the real world and don't have too much
experience with the job market.  All job offers that I have seen rarely
told the salary right away.  What quite often happened was that during
the interview the potential employer asked me what salary I wanted.  At
the time I was looking for my internship I didn't know and that's what I
told them. 

When a friend applied several years ago, another company asked him how
much he wanted during the interview.  I guess he asked for too much
since they weren't interested in him after that.


I would like to know how everybody on this news group feels about jobs
that are advertised without salaries.  And also if you think that quite
a few people aren't paid as much as they are worth because they didn't
ask for enough money.

Thanks
  Claus
```

```
Yes, I've seen this time and again.  This was especially true of really
good, sharp programmers back in the mid-eighties.  They just wouldn't
ask for enough money when they were providing astounding benefits
for management.  This is back when DP became MIS.

Management was making all manner of sound business decisions based
on the new stuff coming out of the computer room.  And in most cases
we weren't making much more than a journeyman TV tech.

I've since learned that you can pretty much get what you ask for.  I'm
very pleased with my compensation.  If you're a competent programmer,
there's no reason not to be pleased.

JC Jones.
```
