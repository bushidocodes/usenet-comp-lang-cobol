I think I used READY TRACE once back in the early 1980's, and found it
not very helpful.

I liked EXHIBIT NAMED, and I used to see it in production programs
where it was used to display summary totals at end-of-job.  Obviously,
EXHIBIT NAMED was intended as a debugging tool, but like "ON 1" it was
an IBM extension that tended to get used in production programs for
non-testing purposes.  

It seems to me that IBM OS/VS COBOL also allowed an EXHIBIT (without
named) that did what DISPLAY could NOT do at that time, which was
convert binary and packed-decimal numerics into displayable format. 
The DISPLAY verb couldn't do that in OS/VS COBOL.  It wasn't until
COBOL II that DISPLAY actually worked the way it should.  

DISPLAY has not been eliminated.
