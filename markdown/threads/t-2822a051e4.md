Floyd,

You haven't indicated length of course, so I'm assuming you want to cover
COBOL basics and perhaps move on to OO (Will's book) - how long on OO ?

Non-OO - at this point in time I would go for Thane's book. I haven't noted
others that are current apart from TYCI21Days - don't care for it much.
When I say 'current'., I mean with 'extensions' that will be a part of
COBOL 2002. (Then of course there's a whole slough that we have lost
reference to. I wasn't aware Don Nelson had written on COBOL - and Will has
written some TWENTY books on COBOL that I wasn't aware of). One other
possible source, and I don't know whether he has written books - is Gary de
Ward Brown. Some useful articles by him in cobolreport.com.

Specifically OO - it's down to Will and Ed Arranga/Frank Coyle. The latter
book gives you a complete coded example of an application. Unfortunately
you would have to key in the source yourself - Ed has lost the damned
diskette containing his source code !

OO books NOT to bother with :-

Ned Chapin -"Standard OO COBOL" - What 'Standard' ? - it was written in '97
- Syntax too general and conveys more 'structured' that OO Classes.

Doke & Hardgrave "An Intro to O COBOL' - simplistic and a repetition of
Will's book, but without the guts.

Andrew Topper 'OO Development in COBOL". '95. - He just loves tables and
diagrams.

So back to teaching OO using Will's examples. If you get stuck I'm sure one
of the following F/J users will help - Don Tees, Pete or Thane. You should
be able to adapt his source from the diskette examples - your only major
stumbling block would be to put in the Repository syntax to suit the F/J
compiler; plus where Net Express uses Object-Storage Section you are
talking 'Working-Storage Section' in F/J. (I can't recall without
referencing, but Will probably has Working-Storage in some methods - this
is a No-No in the new Standard - you can only have Local-Storage).

New Standard - watch out for file definitions and Declaratives which are
part of Global - can't be in methods.

If you want to get more specific - just sing out.

Jimmy, Calgary AB
