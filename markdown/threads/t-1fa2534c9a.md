a similar methodology applies to other compilers.

I did not know you could do it that way, I always used it
on the command line.  It does make a big difference in Win95
with older DOS code.  Without the P switch, nothing gets
released to the spooler until the program exits.  With it,
it gets released when the print file is closed.  Since my
programs print stuff like bills of lading one page at a time,
without ever exiting back to the OS, the difference is fairly
crucial<G>.
