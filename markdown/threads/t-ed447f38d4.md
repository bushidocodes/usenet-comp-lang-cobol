There is NO doubt in my mind that the most misunderstood construct in COBOL
is the "CAME FROM" verb.

This was put forward at the last meeting of spaghetti standards, when the
boys realised that this new "structured" nonsense was going to prevent or
discourage the use of their favourite construct, "GO TO".

With the prospect of maintainable code (and the consequent diminution of job
security...) hanging over them, not to mention the demise of ALTER also on
the cards, the committee decided to get approval for "CAME FROM" as a "back
door" "GO TO"... It is buried in most compilers to this day but only the
most tenacious spaghetti coders can find it.

I reckon the second place goes to the "DONT DO THAT" clause, which can be
used both inside and outside complex PERFORM ...VARYING loops to ensure that
terminating conditions are ignored  and  processor cycles are consumed as
they were intended to be by the Great Hardware Vendor in the sky.

A tie for third place between the unconditional "SOMETIMES" clause (as in
"PERFORM this SOMETIMES") and the "STOP RUN" alternative, "HALT AND CATCH
FIRE".

Fourth place goes to the "SEARCH" enhancement clauses "HARD" and "REALLY
HARD", which are infinitely more efficient than the binary chop implemented
by "SEARCH ALL". ("SEARCH REALLY HARD" is implemented by a tertiary balanced
Fibonacci series, and is so dedicated that even when it finds what it is
looking for, it goes and does it again to be absolutely sure....)

Fifth on my list are the dyslexic arithmetic functions: DAD, SUBCART,
MILTIPLUY, DAVIDE, and COMPOST, with their exception detection "ON FULLUP".
Although they were intended to help a small minority of slightly disabled
programmers, they had to be withdrawn from the language because perfectly
normal programmers were using  them to get laughs and they were bringing
COBOL's reputation as a self-documenting language into disrepute.

Sixth would be the very rarely used "CLOSE DISK WITH REWIND". This is a
handy one when you need the afternoon off to attend a rock concert. It
causes an untraceable hardware error so the system is down for a few hours.
Of course, if it is overused they replace the hardware unit, so it is best
saved for times when Dire Straits, Oasis, or Pink Floyd are in town (Don't
waste it frivolously on Robbie Williams or Martine McClucheon...)

All of the above are very infrequently used because they are totally
misunderstood.

Pete.
