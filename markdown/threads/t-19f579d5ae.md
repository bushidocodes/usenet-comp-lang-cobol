Don't' want to stifle your enthusiasm Deek - but on and off I have been plugging
this one for about roughly two years.

What is truly required, is *somebody(bodies)* to co-ordinate, taking the existing
COBOL standard and the future COBOL 200X, and take every syntactical phrase and
illustrate it with numerous and varying examples. Then it becomes a case of getting
selective on 'traditional problem'. examples, e.g. :-

        - Write a program that reads in a file
        - produce totals for products
        - output a report or file etc....

Now to make the thing useful, it needs be, purely as an example :-

1    - Structured - Basics - the sort of examples you are looking for to help you
    getting acquainted with the language
2    - Structured - Advanced - the level I might be looking for to use a feature in

    COBOL that I haven't yet used, (e.g. the new VALIDATE verb to be introduced
    in COBOL 200X)
 3   - Structured - Performance - just as IMPORTANT - if you've been following
    some of the threads you will have seen messages from Judson about optimum
    coding  approaches for efficiency, like when you do/and do NOT open/close
files,
    and performance statistics resulting from different techniques
4 - Object Orientation - Basics - to get people off the ground
5 - Object Orientation - Advanced - melding different sub modules together,
    component technology, linking with other languages etc.
6 - etc...... (add you item......)

Now I have made the same sort of suggestion elsewhere and I did get a response from
two folks on J4 (that's 'our' committee that discusses and determines what COBOL
200X will be). My suggestion was it should be a supporting document to the standard
- their reaction, good idea but not produced by J4. One immediately got a glint in
his eye, "Here's a way to make a few bucks...."

I don't know the mechanics, but you'd have to have a heavy duty site - chances are
it will receive a lot of hits during the course of the day. Then there is the sheer
problem of selecting, co-ordinating and cataloguing finalized examples. Last but
not least, the money required to run the site.

Maybe it has to start with the academics (educators) - dunno.

Check the following site for some interesting examples - although I haven't
reviewed the quality :-

http://www.csis.ul.ie/COBOL/examples

PS: Picked up above reference here in the News group -  could have even been from
one of your messages <G>.

Jimmy, Calgary AB
