No, I did not.  I posted about the UNSTRING being used more frequently
on PC's than on mainframes, then gave a very simple example of UNSTRING
usage.  If you want to get into command-line parsing, then there have
been dozens of books written on the subject.

UNSTRING is by far the simplest (and most powerfull) Cobol verb
for attacking such problems ... the point of the post.  If you are going
to write a program that parses thousands of lines out of a file, there
may well be a more efficient way of doing it.  However, for the amount
of time involved in coding, for something as simple and infrequently
used as a command line in, then UNSTRING is by far the most
simple ... the point of my second post.

Now if you want to get into every type of command line that can
be analysed, then that is a completely different topic.
