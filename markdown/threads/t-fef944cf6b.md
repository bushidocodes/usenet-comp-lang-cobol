I won't travel anymore.

It is not hard to get a job where the company will promise that you will
get experience in new technologies in 6 months to a year.  But once you
get that job, they keep finding reasons to keep you where they need you
the most - in COBOL.  Ends up that they don't keep you as you don't
trust the company anymore.

Companies need to make plans and budgets designed around keeping skilled
workers.  This should include education and above else, tours of duty. 
Come June 1, even if your project is behind, you will be reassigned to
the Java team for 6 months.  Certainly it is better to loose you down
the hall, where they can find you, and where you will return in 6
months, than to lose you across town.

So many companies lie about their offers of new technology - or have
wishful thinking that COBOL programmers won't be as vital in 6 months
that they will be able to treat them honestly, that we have stopped
believing them.  Finally when we get near retirement, we stop caring,
but the companies are screwing themselves worse than they are screwing
us.

behaviour.
