All references are to WD 1.6.

Page 73, 8.3.1.1.2.4 Device-name,
"A device-name identifies a hardware or software
device in the operating environment."

Page 215, 12.2.6.1 SPECIAL-NAMES paragraph, GF,
"device-name-1 IS mnemonic-name-3".

Page 217, 12.2.6.2 SPECIAL-NAMES paragraph, SR 7,
"Mnemonic-name-3 may be specified only in the ACCEPT
and DISPLAY statements. The implementor may specify
additional restrictions on the use of mnemonic-names
that reference specific device-names."

Page 437, 14.8.1.1 ACCEPT statement, GF,
"Format 1 (device):"

Page 438, 14.8.1.2 ACCEPT statement, SR 2,
"Mnemonic-name-1 shall be specified in the
SPECIAL-NAMES paragraph of the environment
division and shall be associated with a hardware
or software device in the operating environment."

Page 464, 14.8.10 DISPLAY statement, first paragraph,
"The line format of the DISPLAY statement causes data
to be transferred to a hardware or software device in the
operating environment."

Page 464, 14.8.10.1 DISPLAY statement, GF,
"Format 1 (line):"

Page 464, 14.8.10.2 DISPLAY statement, SR 2,
"Mnemonic-name-1 shall be associated with a device
in the SPECIAL-NAMES paragraph in the environment
division."

I suggest changing the DISPLAY statement, Format 1,
from "line" to "device" to reflect the fact that rules are
for a "device" and not a "line", which word is only applicable
to those devices that are capable of positioning, and making
the same change to the second word of the first paragraph
so that it reads ""The device format of the DISPLAY
statement causes data to be transferred to a hardware
or software device in the operating environment."

Furthermore, I suggest providing the same wording for
both ACCEPT statement SR2 and DISPLAY statement
SR2, since ultimately the same requirement exists for
mnemonic-name-1 in both statements and suggest that
this change be accomplished by changing ACCEPT
statement SR2 to use the same words as DISPLAY
statement SR2, since the use of "device" resolves to
"device-name", which 8.3.1.1.2.4 states "identifies
a hardware or software device in the operating
environment". (This eliminates the redundancy between
ACCEPT statement SR2 and Device-name, which
redundancy is not present in DISPLAY statement SR2.)
```

The one thing I disagree with you on is the replacement of the wording in 
ACCEPT Syntax Rule 2 with that found in DISPLAY Syntax Rule 2; I personally 
prefer the longer, more explicit (and, yes, redundant) wording in ACCEPT be 
used in both places because the *previous* standard explicitly required that 
the mnemonic name "be associated with a hardware device" in both rules.  The 
draft *no longer* requires that the device be a *hardware* device; that's 
the point.

If this weren't something that changed, yes, I think we could allow the 
chain of references to take care of it, but when the basic idea changes in a 
rule, I think it's appropriate to highlight the change, and this changed 
(for both ACCEPT and DISPLAY).

My recommendation to J4 will be that DISPLAY SR2 be changed to read like 
ACCEPT SR2 rather than vice versa as you suggest.

As I indicated in private e-mail to Rick Smith, my suggestion to J4 goes a 
little bit beyond what I indicated in my previous posting.  Syntax Rule 2 
for both ACCEPT and DISPLAY need to be explicit that the mnemonic-name used 
there is associated with a device-name (as distinct from a feature-name or a 
switch-name) in SPECIAL-NAMES.  There are several reasons for this; to me 
the most important is that the *previous* standard merely referred to 
"hardware device" in these locations and we need to make it absolutely clear 
that this has changed, where it changed.  I don't feel comfortable relying 
on following a chain of references to discover that the standard no longer 
requires the implementor to provide a *hardware* device for ACCEPT and 
DISPLAY regardless of whether the *platform* exists as hardware or not! 
While sometimes we *do* rely on such "chain references", there's nothing 
wrong with explicitness even if it's not technically required it does help 
drive the point home.

The wording I am proposing for Syntax Rule 2 for ACCEPT is:

"Mnemonic-name-1 shall be specified in the SPECIAL-NAMES paragraph of the 
environment division and shall be associated with an implementor-defined 
device-name that is identified in the operating environment as a hardware or 
software device capable of providing data to the program."

The wording for DISPLAY SR2 is the same, except for the trailing phrase: 
"... capable of receiving data from the program."

 I realize this is clumsy, but I also believe it's more complete and precise 
than the existing rules for either ACCEPT or DISPLAY.

I agree with the change from "line format" to "device format" for DISPLAY. 
It was "line format" in the 2002 standard, but the change looks clearer to 
me, and as far as I can tell it doesn't break anything.

I'm planning to get my proposed response posted on the J4 website tommorow.

    -Chuck Stevens
```
