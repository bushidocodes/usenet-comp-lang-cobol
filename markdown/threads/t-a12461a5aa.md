Quentin,

The best method for you will be to create "flat" files, that have 
records 133 bytes long.  Ask your customer to provide you with the 
carriage control character that should be in the first byte.  The 
remainder of the print line follows this byte.  There is no specific 
"character positioning" - you have to print each and every line.  A 
print layout overlayed on one of their pre-printed forms would help.  
After you create this file, you transfer it to the system with the 
impact printer (likely a mainframe) and have them send it to the 
output queue.
