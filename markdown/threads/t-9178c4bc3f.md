```
http://arstechnica.com/business/2013/11/half-an-operating-system-the-triumph-and-tragedy-of-os2/

I thoroughly enjoyed this really outstanding article and I think most people
here may enjoy it too.

Most of us have been involved in IT a long time and some of us watched the
arrival of the "personal computer" and saw it rock our world.

As I read this I kept smiling as the article evoked happy memories...

Buying an Amiga with a bridgeboard so it could run PC software as well,
working at IBM and being given the latest release of OS/2 WARP as a Xmas
present for staff, gasping in awe as my 16 bit Micro Focus COBOL Workbench
compiled and ran mainframe code I had written some years before, on my 286
PC... waiting with eager anticipation for my first 386 processor, the
launch of Windows 95 with the Rolling Stones "Start Me Up"... (a perfect
piece of marketing, in my opinion)... the first time I connected a PC to a
modem and accessed remote Bulletin Boards at 1200 baud, the incredible rise
in processing power and OS capability culminating for me with Windows XP as
being the very first PC OS I was ever completely happy with (I liked the
reference to how it arrived, in this article).

Today, running ultrabroadband to connect me to the World and my own wireless
LAN at speeds that would have been unthinkable a few years back, being able
to converse with people half a world away and see them in high definition,
sharing my desktop with them in real-time and with total clarity, connecting
machines with widely varying hardware and software capability seamlessly and
easily, running virtualized machines to test software development with no
system crashes or freezes, it makes me aware how far we have come.

This article, carefully researched, unbiased, and very well written,
entertained me at a number of levels.

Hope you enjoy it.

Pete.
"I used to write COBOL...now I can do anything."
```

```
I did enjoy the article. The first "work" PC given was a PS/2 Model 70
with OS/2 (version 1.3?), around 1990. I did some mainframe development
in Realia COBOL on that box. My employer had a mix of OS/2, Windows and
Apple computers but a few years later standardized on Windows NT 3.51.
After that it was NT4, Win2000, XP, and they skipped Vista and went to
Win7. I haven't heard of any plans to upgrade to Win8 yet.

In the early 90's I used Wordperfect. By the mid-1990's we were using
Lotus ccmail, then lotus notes, and a couple of years ago they converted
MS Outlook and Exchange.

Of course I can remember the old-timers talking about programming with
patch cords until the 1401 came out. I actually wrote programs on an
IBM-029 keypunch in school around 1987, but never punched a card again
when I got my first programming job.

OS/2 is still available from ecomstation.biz.
```
