Dear Colleagues,

It's here!

The Data Management Journal (TDMJ), an Internet, subscription-free
magazine is now awaiting your visit. The first issue contains articles
on IBM's recently concluded Technical Interchange '96 held in
Nashville, TN. The next issue, on June 1, will carry even more
articles from TI'96.

TDMJ invites your own contribution. I've already written to several
of you to ask that you consider authoring an article for publication.
Our target readership in your area is the technical support or
application developer working with COBOL. As many of you upgrade to
'85 COBOL or Object-Oriented COBOL, or just keep things running, you've managed to
acquire experience that others would find valuable. Don't hide your
light under a basket!

TDMJ isn't a replacement for lists or USENET. On the
contrary. We aspire to pick-up where most "on-line" conversations
end and those involved exchange information "off-line." We want to
share those "off-line" exchanges with a larger readership.

You don't have to be a great writer. We'll help you out there. And,
your article can be as long (or as short) as you believe appropriate.

TDMJ also hosts blind help and position wanted advertising. For a
modest fee, your advertisement remains on-line for a full 30 days.
This service does require an e-mail address (which we don't
disclose) to which responses may be sent.

Please stop by:

http://www.thenumberco.com/TDMJ.htm
