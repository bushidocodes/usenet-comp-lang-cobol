We are looking at using VA COBOL for mainframe development. We have
heavy use of IMS and DB2 both online and batch. Many years ago we were
using MicroFocus COBOL workbench with the IMSVS86 IMS emulator and the
Stingray XDB DB2 emulator. We would like to use thsi same concept with
VA COBOL but are not aware of compatable IMS emulators like IMSVS86. We
know that we probably could use UDB for the DB2 part.

We are aware that VACOBOL can interface with a mainframe and IMS and
DB2. The idea here is to offload development and unit testing as much as
possible in order to reduce mainframe costs.

Does anyone have any suggestions?

I also once used Micro Focus Workbench with XDB and IMSVS86 at a UK bank, in
a department with 250 programmers.

Later on we upgraded to their Mainframe Express product which is far
superior. As well as IMS and DB2, it emulates CICS, Assembler (now includes
the new z/OS instructions), JCL, mainframe catalog, REXX, CLIST, and it can
be used with MQ Series. It also lets you view or download mainframe source
code and data from Windows, with access to various SCM systems (in our case,
Endevor).

We evaluated VisualAge COBOL a couple of years ago but it relied too heavily
on the mainframe for it to be of benefit to us. I don't know of any other
IMS emulation I'm afraid.
