It is not so much different than the issues are different. COBOL's strengths
are Database management, screen management, printer management, BCD math,
and a "fairly" easy way of understanding what is going on in the program. So
far, the only thing close to this are a number of 4GL RDMS, which do not have
the strength or versatility of COBOL.

Most of the other languages available on multiple platforms do not address
these issues to the extent they are useful in a business environment.

I don't recommend getting in a debate as to which language is better than
another, for that would be like comparing apples and oranges. They all have
their strengths, they all have their weaknesses.
