The *actual* next steps are:

1) Resolve international comments

2) Countries are allowed (but almost never do) change their "YES" votes to
"NO" because of "fixes" done for other countries' comments

3) This is EXPECTED to be completed around the end of 1st quarter or
beginning of 2nd quarter of 2002.

4) There is then an international ballot on "DIS" (Draft International
Standard).  Public Review comments ARE *not* appropriate at this time.  It
is usually just a "yes" or "no" vote.  *NO* real changes are allowed at this
time (except those MINOR editorial changes required by the ISO secretariat).
This ballot period is (again) a 4 month ballot (internationally - there will
probably be a US ballot during that time).

5) ASSUMING this ballot passes, then the DIS (Draft International Standard)
becomes an IS (International Standard) - currently expected around the end
of 2002.

6) The US (and probably other countries) then "adopt" it as a national
Standard.  (In the US this is "automatic" - so it will become an ANSI
Standard "roughly" as soon as it becomes an ISO Standard)

FYI - to see the "official" set of ISO comments (and ballots) go to:

 http://people.ne.mediaone.net/doncobol/01-0373.pdf

According to information available from http://www.iso.ch , there are twenty
national standards bodies that are participating members in the activities
of ISO/IEC JTC1/SC22, and twenty-five at the JTC1 level.

SAI (Australia) is one of several national standards bodies who are
participating members in JTC1, but apparently do not choose to be
participating members of (or even observers at) the SC22 subcommittee of
that organization.

Formal submission of ballots with comments regarding the transition from FCD
status to DIS status is invited from those national standards bodies who
have chosen to participate in the process; apparently, SAI (Australia) did
not choose to be a participating member of SC22 and thus would have had no
reason to submit a ballot on an issue being decided by that subcommittee.


For those who are interested in such matters, here's an expanded list of who
is and who is not on which committees:

SAI (Australia) has already been discussed; the other national standards
bodies who are full members of JTC1 but do not participate (either as
members or as observers) in SC22 are CSK (Democratic People's Republic of
Korea), NSAI (Ireland), SABS (South Africa) and SNV (Switzerland).

ASRO (Romania), DSTU (Ukraine),  EOS (Egypt), GOST R (Russian Federation)
and ON (Austria) are observers at the JTC1 level but full participating
members of SC22.

IPQ (Portugal), KATS (Republic of Korea), SIS (Sweden), SNZ (New Zealand)
and UNI (Italy) are listed as participating member organizations in JTC1 but
observers on SC22.

ABNT (Brazil), AFNOR (France), ANSI (United States), BSI (United Kingdom),
CSBTS (China), CSNI (Czech Republic), DIN (Germany), IBM (Belgium), JISC
(Japan), NEN (Netherlands), NSF (Norway), SCC (Canada), SFGS (Finland) and
SMIS (Slovenia) are all full participating members of both bodies.
