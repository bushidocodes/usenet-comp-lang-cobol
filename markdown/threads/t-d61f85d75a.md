Right now I am neck deep in Y2K remediation, and have been for about
a year.  But there is something I have noticed in all the fracas.  Not
one of the people I can remember crying wolf over Y2K was a programmer
directly involved in the Y2K effort.  I even started a thread recently
asking Y2K programmers in this newsgroup to say if their company/clients
were in good or bad shape, and every single response was that their
company/clients Y2K efforts were either complete or in good order on
conservative schedules.  I don't have a clue how the rest of the world
is doing, or how embedded systems are doing.  But for mainline business
in the U.S., the people who are making the noise are not the ones in
the best position to know.  I would be much more concerned if the ones
actually doing the work seemed worried.  My clients are in good shape.
If anybody here has *direct* knowledge of a different story, I would
like to hear it.

The higher ups (really high) in our company probably don't KNOW what's
been going on.  I have prevented some Y2K failures already.  As it
sits right now, I know of only one thing that must be done in the
systems that I have responsibility for, to prevent any Y2K problems.
There were a handfull of items that I already fixed.  Some of these
would have gone unnoticed until after the rollover, but would have
started happening in January this year.  We have some billing records
that are once a year.  When we bill, we roll them into the next year.
The very next step deleted any records from this recurring billing
file (It does monthly also) that are over 3 months old.  In January
the yearly items would have been rolled into year 00 (it's YYMM) and
then would have been quietly deleted.  I changed the delete to only
delete if YY > 90, so as not to roll off any of these items.  But very
few people even know it would have happened.  I suspect this type of
thing IS happening in some places, and they won't find out till they
wonder where those yearly billing records went!

 We have the misforture of having to incorportate some purchased
business into our own, and it was NOT compliant in the least.  I have
fixed that as I have moved it.  I do not think that Y2K will go over
for us without a hitch, but I don't see it being any worse than the
daily struggles I encounter just keeping all the disparate peices from
beating each other to death.
