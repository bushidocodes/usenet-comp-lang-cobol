I have programmed on both mainframes and PCs in COBOL. Three different
"versions" of the mainframe variety, and two of the PC style. On all the
platforms involved, the COBOL source was invariably so similar that on
the 2 PC styles and on 2 of the mainframe ones it was possible to
literally copy the source from one platform to the other and compile/run
without any modification at all. Only on the HP mini-computer did I find
a COBOL which would not compile on the other platforms without some
serious alterations in code.
But, in general, COBOL is COBOL is COBOL, no matter where you are.

Well, for the most part, COBOL is COBOL. Each platform will have some
hardware specific issues, but from platform to platform, you will see
a great deal of similarity. The basic logic, flow, and syntax is
statndard throughout all editions of COBOL. Your main differences are
going to be hardware specific (storage of packed fields in memory,
system devices, etc). You aren't wasting your time in pursuing any
knowledge. What you learn on the PC will give you some basis for what
you will need and learn for any other platform, and vice versa....
