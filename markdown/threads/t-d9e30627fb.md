I liked the COBOL->C discussion in this same newsgroup.
I think it's a good idea and I'll collaborate if I can.
But until then, I'll have to keep on with my work.
I use RM/COBOL-85 on SCO/UNIX, and I've found that you
can save a lot of work with very simple tools. I've got
a kind of editor (vi/ex) extension to create standard
routines, a SELECT, FD and PROCEDURE COPY generator for
files and their respective I-O operations. I also have
a program generator (makes about 70% of new programs),
that I would'nt want to show anybody , but it works
ok for me. Now I'm working on a report generator along
the lines of the Microsoft (MicroFocus?) REPORT SECTION.

(I'm new to Internet - through Compuserve - so I'll be
grateful to anybody helping me along with this!)
