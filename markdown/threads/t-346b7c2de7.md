Graham Hobbs wrote:
› On Mon, 9 Sep 2013 01:45:49 +1200, "Pete Dashwood"
›  wrote:
…[77 more quoted lines elided]…
› runtime licencing.

What you are describing sounds very much like the production of .INTs. (This
is intermediate code produced by Micro Focus and requiring their runtime.
Given that your "IBM compiler" is actually MicroFocus under the covers that
seems mostlikely the case.)

. INTs can run as if they were .EXEs, provided the runtime system is
available.

You should be able to statically link the .INTs with the Run Time system
(RTS), simply by changing the way you build your application. This will
produce .EXEs that will then run anywhere. (Well, NEARLY anywhere...)

">... assume this is similiar to Microfocus, Futitsu and their
› runtime licencing."

It is nothing like what Fujitsu do, and they DON'T have runtime licensing.

It is very much what Micro Focus USED to do...and they DO have runtime
licensing. (Then and now... although, as you are technically using an IBM
implementation they may not be able to charge you for the runtime. Read your
licence agreement.)

If you are determined not to learn anything new, then you have few options:

1. Find out how to statically link your existing COBOL using your existing
compiler. (I'm assuming you have the "IBM" docs...? There may be
examples... look for "static linking".)
(This option will not involve you spending money but you will have to do
some reading and experimenting. I think it is your best option.)
Nowadays, this is no longer an option with Micro Focus and the good old
days of deployable .EXEs have gone the way of all flesh. The modern RTS is
essential and, as far as I know there is no option in NetExpress to
statically link for deployment - could be wrong about that; I don't use
NetExpress unless I have to.

2. Get a Fujitsu compiler, recompile ABC and you can deploy DEF easily to
other platforms without needing to recompile, or pay RTS licensing.
