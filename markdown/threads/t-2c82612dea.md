I have used Netscape since v1.2 and have never paid for it.

The only reason Microsoft is even still around is the fact that they got
into the BUSINESS world early.  While everyone else concentrated on home use
and education.  If, for example, Apple Computer had concentrated more on
business than on education and home use, then I'm sure we'd all be using
Macs right now.  Instead, poorly-managed Apple innovated while others waited
and imitated.  The mouse, the cd-rom drive, the desktop, etc.  Now Steve
Jobs is back as (still) interim CEO and Apple continues to innovate but at a
much smaller market.  Meanwhile Billy is rich.

Businesses have demanded exponentially more and more stuff, compared to
other environments.  Schools can get any kind of computer they want (it's
the government's money).  Home users have a choice too (when one is
'obselete', most people just trash it and buy a new one), although they
usually end up buying the same kind.

It's not easy for a business to throw away millions of dollars of equipment,
software, and training to go to another platform.  Therefore, businesses,
over time, have essentially gotten stuck using Windows.  Simply because it
was there first, not because it's better.

Windows is about the worst operating system ever made.  Period.  It is less
intuitive in every way then even DOS.  When I saw Windows 3.1 the first
time, I nearly pissed myself laughing.  If our mainframe was 100 times more
reliable then our best Windows applications, our business would crumble is
less than a day.

After using many other operating systems over the years, I cannot understand
why people continue to think Windows is the best.  Almost every feature of
the Windows operating system (except maybe the Start menu) is a direct
rip-off of some predecessor, albeit executed at about 10% of the
effectiveness.

Ever wondered why those whiz-bang MCSE people make so much money.  Because
the software is that bad, and that hard to fix.  Supply is lower than
demand.  Stop kidding yourself.

I know very little about Linux, but I think this is the future.  It is
immature but with the help of many programmers could become a robust ticket
for the future.

I am using Outlook Express right now, and use Internet Explorer, by choice,
also.  Only because they are better products.  I am not an idiot; I know and
use a better package when I see it.

It is unfortunate that the computer industry is so stagnant and uninspiring
these days....

Jeff
