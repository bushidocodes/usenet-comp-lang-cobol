in Seattle.  Folks think it will pick up early in 2000 -- not only
to fix problems but also to do those enhancements everyone put
on hold while doing Y2K.

When I was looking last September (my contract was going to
end in December and I didn't want to get caught out of work
during that holiday dead zone) I was asking $50-60 an hour W2.  
This rate was possible although some of the contract agencies
were saying they'd try to get me $50.  I'm hearing from
folks looking that there are contracts up to $65 an hour W2 in 
the Seattle area but I couldn't tell you what the normal rate is.

: My contract is coming due up here soon, and I might have to 
: consider heading south for a while.

I worked with a fellow from Calgary a few years back who 
was working in the U.S. for similar reasons.
