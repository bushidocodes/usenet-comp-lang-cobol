    If a language is useful enough to replace Cobol as a business language,
then may the best language win.  I don't believe that Java is an assault on
Cobol because it isn't a business oriented language.  I do believe however
that some companies are trying to replace their Cobol with other languages
like Java, but I don't necessarily agree that that is an assault on Cobol.

Our company (www.synkronix.com) has a product called PERCobol which compiles
Cobol for use in a Java environment.  We ALWAYS recommend that companies
keep their business logic IN COBOL, but with our product they get all of the
benefits of Java.  Their Cobol is compiled to what we call a "Cobol Bean" (A
Java Bean that was written in Cobol) rather than to native code.  (They have
the option of compiling to native code as well, of course.)
