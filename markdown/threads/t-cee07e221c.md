> How does one write and compile COBOL programs on a Commodore 64? I

With a text editor and a Cobol compiler, just like on any other
platform.

The C64 had a 6502 (actually a 6510), and while it certainly had a
BASIC interpreter in ROM, you could program it in any language that
you could get a compiler or interpreter for.  Pascal, C, assembler and
Logo were all available (as well as others).

Now whether or not anyone ever built a Cobol compiler for the C64 is
another question.  The C64 Wiki appears to list a product from Abacus,
but I don't know anything about it.
