- **From:** "William Bub" <fathafluff@hotmail.com>

```
I just found this bug, and fixed it. I would like an explanation of WHY it
behaved as it did.

05 VAR-COMP   PIC S9(05) COMP VALUE 4.
05 VAR-X            PIC X(02).
05 VAR-N            PIC 9(02).

MOVE VAR-COMP TO VAR-N
                                         VAR-X

VAR-COMP actually resides in 2 bytes, and has a value of +4 stored
internally as x'0004'.
VAR-N successfully receives a value of 04 display, or x'F0F4'; but VAR-X
receives a value of 00 display, or x'F0F0'

I always thought that when moving to a PIC X field, no numeric conversion
took place. So I expected VAR-X to contain the non-display value of x'0004'.
Instead, it contained a value of 00 display, or x'F0F0'. Can anyone explain
this?

The fix was to MOVE VAR-N TO VAR-X.
```

```
Your MOVE comp to PIC X(2) display just does a conversion to a PIC X(4), and
_then_ does the move X(4) to X(2) with normal X to X rules.  Absent a JUSTIFY
clause to the contrary, the justification defaults to LEFT justification and
'0004' get chopped on the right side to '00'.  Piece of cake.

Very standard behavior!  Actually your fix is good (the move through a
explicated intermediary, instead of an intermediary field deployed by the
compiler) . A JUSTIFY RIGHT clause on VAR-X might work too! That would be less
work and more automagic.
```

```
IBM's mainframe compiler is ANSI/ISO ('85) Standard compliant and follows
the rules as documented at:

http://publibz.boulder.ibm.com/cgi-bin/bookmgr_OS390/BOOKS/igy3lr10/6.2.24.1

A) It is VALID to move a numeric (any usage) integer field to an
alphanumeric field

B) When the receiving field is Alphanumeric or Alphanumeric-Edited:
  If the sending item has an operational sign, the unsigned value is used.
If the operational sign occupies a separate character, that character is not
moved, and the size of the sending item is considered to be one less
character than the actual size.

C) The rules of WHAT values are "used" in binary items is usually impacted
by the TRUNC compiler option (however, COMP-5 items work slightly
differently).  To understand what values are used, read and understand the
information at:

http://publibz.boulder.ibm.com/cgi-bin/bookmgr_OS390/BOOKS/igy3pg10/2.4.52
and the examples that are included in this section.

  ***

If you find an example of the compiler NOT working according to these rules,
then you should contact IBM support (open a PMR) - assuming you are using a
SUPPORTED compiler.
```
