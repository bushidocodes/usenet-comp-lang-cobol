I did research some time ago because I had heard that the North
American System was running out of numbers.  I found a report on plans
for an expanded telephone numbering system for North America at
ftp://ftp.atis.org/pub/clc/inc/99121025.doc (WARNING this is a Word
document).  It appears that there is no agreement on the format. Some
of the proposals are 4+3+4, 4+4+4 and 1+3+3+4.  However, there seems to
be general agreement that it will contain no more than 12 digits.

For a global system, you would have to allow space for punctuation
(since it varies by country), so a single 14 character field should be
adequate.  This would handle any of the proposed new standards.  It
should also be adequate for other countries - at least until China or
India start to approach North American levels of phone use.

14 digits is enough? Australia from America
"+61-3-9557-6157x4321" (my old phone number) = 20 characters. 

How many m^dBillions of dollars have been wasted because someone
tried to optimize field size (eg Y2k)? How big is the database?
How much would the extra few bytes actually cost vs the cost of
fixing it later?

Given the cost of disk space vs the cost of changing all the
programs and databases later it is sensible to err on the side of
larger fields. IMHO.

Certainly one thing to avoid is any assumption that the phone
number is seven digits. Numerous US web sites reject my phone
number for this reason.
