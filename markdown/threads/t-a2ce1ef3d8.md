The year 2000 scenario raises issue that are unique and potentially will

trigger unqualifiable consequences.
Although the underlying problem is considered very basic, namely the
traditional method of entering and storing dates as 2 digits (98) rather

than 4 digits (1998) the issues are very profound:

   * The Problem is world-wide and universal across all computer
     manufacturers and system vendors.
   * The Impact will grow from now onwards reaching its peak on
     01.01.2000
   * The Deadline cannot be delayed
   * The Problem is compounded by affecting not only applications but
     the BIOS ( Basic Input/Output System ) Chips, operating systems as
     well as the application systems and there interfaces ( be it EDI as

     well as bespoke interfaces). This mixture generates almost an
     infinite number of scenarios, each with a potential unique
     consequence.

These consequences potentially may include

   * The Complete failure of systems
   * Inappropriate processing (i.e. reflecting all products as being
     "Out Of Date")
   * Rejection of valid information

Owing to the fact that system consequences are not easily predictable,
the business consequences are also unpredictable, (i.e. inability for
customers to order or pay; suppliers to deliver) therefor business
contingency also needs to be considered.

If you have not created a year 2000 task force, do so today with
properly qualified personnel. If you do have a year 2000 task force,
ensue an immediate audit of its procedures, activities and plans

If you want any help or assistance please contact Euro-Y2K, we will
respond immediately.

Euro Y2K can offer a In-house training, a full assessment of your
Business or task force, we can set up a test facility and potentially
save you รฏยฟยฝรฏยฟยฝรฏยฟยฝรฏยฟยฝ's with features such as our ability to test and correct an

infinite number of COBOL lines, very quickley
