The only luck you are likely to have is if Microsoft have some dusty
copies in their basement - they no longer offer technical support.
Bearing in mind this is their 're-badged' version of Micro Focus DOS V3
- the manuals will be very similar. The only major difference is that
Microsoft have their own Programmers' Workbench (what we would now call
an IDE (Integrated Development Environment). The PWB includes the Editor
and the compiling/linkage features specific to the Microsoft version of
the compiler..

> Or
>
> Is there an inexpensive (or free?) compiler
> that is fully functional?
>

Depends what you mean by the phrase 'fully functional' - could be COBOL
85 or are you after a compiler, which with extensions, is closer to the
newly announced COBOL 2002 Standard. I'm not sure but *think* Fujitsu's
V3 is still available as a freebie - but remember they are now up to
Verson 7.. Alternatively both Fujitsu and Micro Focus have 'student'
versions of their current compilers around $100. However, these are
'learning' tools and you can't distribute commercial applications.

?????
