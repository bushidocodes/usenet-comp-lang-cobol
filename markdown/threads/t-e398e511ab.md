>These are NOT MY rules, Daniel. It isn't even MY position. (My position is 
>that debates are most productive when played by the rules of debate.

If the participants are all playing by the same rules.    When we
assume that this is the case, but it isn't, then miscommunication will
result.

If one party thinks this is a formal debate following some set rules,
and the other party thinks this is a casual discussion following
standards of his local language, people can be frustrated, and people
are likely to infer stuff which were not implied.

In our jobs, we see this when working on programming requirements and
specs.    That is the hard part of our work - coding is easy.   So we
learn to give feedback about what we understand our customers to be
asking for, and learn to change words and listen, hoping to discover
early where miscommunications occur.
