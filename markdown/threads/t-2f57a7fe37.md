The *best* (alright only) incentive scheme I have ever seen was a
salary packaging solution. It cost about 10 very well paid staff to
1000 very well paid employees (Y2K salaries) and a very good tracking
system. The objective was to maximise the tax benefits to the
employees much the same way as contractors.

Example (mine): I had a company car worth $4,000. (a 'real' car
here starts at $30,000 for a basic sedan). The fringe benefits tax I
paid was $800 (in tax dollars). This immediately made my insurance,
petrol, running costs tax deductable. It cost me about $4,000 to run
the car so it saved me about $1,500 (in tax dollars).

If I did not want it then I could just take a salary like everyone
else.

This is an example of the company working with the employee to
maximise the cash in hand that they get. Due to this I added $5,000
onto my salary before I moved jobs, but I tend to move every 4 years
anyway.

If you have 40 staff and you can effectively put $4,000 back into
their pockets and it costs you a full time clerk at $30,000
(reasonably expensive) the effective return is $160,000.

My current company can ONLY see the $30,000 in extra costs not the
benefits. It is a tough sell.
