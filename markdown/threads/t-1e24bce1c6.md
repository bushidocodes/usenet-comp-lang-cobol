Hi Curt,

Grace Hopper is the person most often given the credit for COBOL.
This derives from her own Univac development efforts originally called
B0 (B zero). She also did an A zero for algebraic stuff. Her B 0 became
known as FLOW-MATIC, and Amico.

During the actual specification of COBOL by CODASYL'S Short Range
Committee, Flowmatic, Amico, and two other works in process were used.
They were a Honeywell language, and one invented under the guidence of
Bob Beamer (IBM) at that time. The short range commitee used ideas from
all four places. Grace and Bob had an ongoing unofficial exchange
program (says Bob Beamer), and they both can/could take a lot of credit
for COBOL. Members of the short range committee would take a little
umbrage, but mainly support this contention. It took the short range
committee a year to develope their first draft of COBOL. They had been
given a three month tarket for the job. This is "fact" in my mind, and
also lends some creadance to the idea that no one person could be named
the author of the specification. Since 1960 a large number of people
have contributed to the language in many ways. Some more than others.
Now days, I would pick Don Nelson as a major player as he has been with
the effort longer than anyone I know personally.
