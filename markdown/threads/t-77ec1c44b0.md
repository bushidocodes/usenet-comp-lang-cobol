I don't know about AIX-based COBOL, but, I can tell you that the IBM VA
COBOL is a port of the LE compiler. A rather direct port, too! If
you're planning on using text-based batch type programs then it will
probably work fine. Just be sure to get the latest release and be sure
you have a fast 'puter (especially if you're doing any GUI work; the
'workframe' is rather slow). I'm not wild about their debugger,
either. I'm not advocating MicroFocus' COBOL 4.0.26 but their debugger
is about a zillion times better right at the moment. Have you looked
into other COBOL compilers? Microsoft makes one, along with
RyanMcFarland and CA. I think MF sells a 'personal' COBOL for less
money than a full-fledged version, but don't take that as gospel.
RP

It sounds like you won the contract because you didn't do the necessary
homework ahead of time. You should have:
1. Selected a Cobol compiler with development/target platforms in mind.
2. Priced the compiler/runtimes ahead of time.
3. Limited the target platforms based on the compiler selected.

I would suggest you look for a compiler that you can use on a development
OS you currently use, with runtimes available for the RS/6000, or find a
compiler for the development platform that has compatibility with the cheap
compiler available for the RS/6000 (which may be the way to go). Also, if
the client is going to buy the equipment/compiler ahead of time, you could
always telnet in and work directly on the RS/6000.

I don't mean to sound critical Paul, but I know how seemingly simple
projects can turn into nightmares when adequate planning hasn't been done
ahead of time.

Good luck!

Mike

Paul,

You could take a look at NetExpress 2.0 from Micro Focus. This version has
a new feature called the UNIX Deployment Option. Basically, you can develop
and test your application on the PC under Windows 95/NT and then when you
are happy simply press a button to deploy to UNIX.

Normally our corresponding UNIX product runtime would be on the UNIX
machine. The advantage of this method is that you get to use a high
performance and intuative GUI development environment on the PC.

Hope this helps.

Paddy Coleman
Team Leader, UK Distributed Computing Support (WinTel)
Micro Focus.
