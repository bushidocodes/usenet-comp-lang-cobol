I am pretty sure that IBM will never revise the ACCEPT verb to receive a DATE
with the century. Doing that would not either not comply with the ANSI-85
standard or would break too many older programs.

Recently I saw a posting (either here or in an IBM mainframe newsgroup) about
an APAR (a patch) available for COBOL II which would give you the ability to
CALL 'IGZEDT4' USING MY-DATE. This would return an eight-character date in
the form CCYYMMDD. I don't know the APAR number, so you would have to call
IBM for that.

Otherwise your best bet is to upgrade to COBOL for MVS and use the intrinsic
function call to obtain the date with 4-digit year.

Not exactly. You have to add an additional word to get an 8 character
date. You say ACCEPT whatever FROM DATE YYYYMMDD or ACCEPT whatever
FROM DAY YYYYDDD. Leaving off the YYYYMMDD or YYYYDDD gets you the
regular two-digit year. I still recommend using the CURRENT-DATE
function.
