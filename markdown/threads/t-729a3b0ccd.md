Steve,

Try the IRS site at http://www.irs.ustreas.gov. You should be able to
download "Publication 15, Circular E, Employer's Tax Guide". This will
provide you with standard Federal (US) deductions. You will also need
the tax rules for any state/province you intend to support, and
county/city rules for any site at which employees work. You may even
need school district rules where employees reside. Good luck--you've
just touched the tip of a very large, complex, and illogical iceberg.
Payroll is a nightmare. Take it from one who developed our in-house
payroll package in 1985 and gratefully abandonded it 1997.

Canada _and_ the US? That covers a lot of ground!

The right direction is the Yellow Pages, under "Payroll Preparation
Service".

If management thinks payroll must physically be run at your site, then
purchase a payroll package -- and its updates.

Even if you amass all of the tables for all of the political subdivisions
in Canada and the US (and they are free for the asking from the relevant
taxing agencies) that only takes care of this month.

Every time any two-bit legislature in Canada or the US tinkers with
taxes, you'll have to make the corresponding change. Taking on payroll
is taking on a never-ending commitment.
