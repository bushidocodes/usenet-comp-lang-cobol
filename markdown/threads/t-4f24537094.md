```
Well, the short answer is: you can't.

But this should work:

01  BIG-FIELD    PIC 9(9) COMP-3.
01  FILLER REDEFINES BIG-FIELD.
   02  LITTLE-FIELD   PIC X(4).
   02  FILLER  PIC X VALUE X'0F'.

MOVE MYDATA TO LITTLE-FIELD.
COMPUTE MY-ANSWER = BIG-FIELD / 10
```

```
Here is one way to handle this situation (IBM COBOL for this-and-that):

05  WS-DATE-AREA.
 10  WS-DATE-AREA-X          PIC X(04).
 10  FILLER                               PIC X(01) VALUE X'0C'.

05  WS-DATE-9                      REDEFINES
      WS-DATE-AREA              PIC S9(09) COMP-3.

05 WS-DATE-NUM                PIC 9(08).

MOVE your date field to WS-DATE-AREA-X

COMPUTE WS-DATE-NUM = WS-DATE-9 / 10  (This last step gets rid of the X'0C'
in WS-DATE-AREA)

HTH
```

```
Define it like this:

03  WS-DATE-PACKED PIC  9(08)V9 PACKED DECIMAL.
03  WS-DATE-PACKED-X REDEFINES WS-DATE-PACKED
                                            PIC  X(05).
03  WS-DATE-DISPLAY PIC  9(08).
03  WS-DATE-BCD         PIC  X(04) VALUE X'20020517'.

MOVE WS-DATE-BCD TO WS-DATE-PACKED-X (1:4).
MOVE X'0F' WS-DATE-PACKED-X (5:).
MOVE WS-DATE-PACKED TO WS-DATE-DISPLAY.

At this point, WS-DISPLAY-DATE contains 20020517 in Display-Numeric format.

HTH....
```
