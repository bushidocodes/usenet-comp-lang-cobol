```
There may well be "costs" in upgrading to COBOL V4 (or any of the even NEWER
Unix Products from  MERANT) - but lose of "features" is simply not one of
them.  If you check TRANSFORM is still fully supported - you just need to
know which "language dialect" directive(s) to turn on.  I am not positive
whether that directive will work with the MF directive, but I will guarantee
that TRANSFORM is "fully" supported when the OSVS directive is turned on.

There are a number (MANY) other "changes in syntax and semantics" when you
upgrade releases of Micro Focus/MERANT products - however, with the
"correct" use of directives, it is almost always possible to get the "old"
support.  Furthermore, once you have upgraded compilers, you should be able
to "gradually" upgrade to the "latest and greatest" features by gradually
(program by program - when you are doing regular maintenance or development)
move the directives up to the "latest" variations.

I would caution  you, however, that going from COBOL/2 1.3 - to the latest
version of Unix compiler may be so large a "jump" that MERANT will not have
"easy" to document migration material - you may need to go thru ALL (many?
most?) of the documents for the intermediary releases/versions.  If you do
decide to go this route, check with your sales/support people for
documentation before you attempt (purchase) the upgrade.

AGAIN, (just so I am clear) I am not saying that upgrading doesn't have its
own problems and costs - I just didn't want you to think that "lose of
syntax/semantics" was one of them.
```
