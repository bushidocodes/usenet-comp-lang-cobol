Any binary data item in Cobol will contain a USAGE COMPUTATIONAL clause
in its description (the word USAGE may be omitted, and COMPUTATIONAL
may be abbreviated as COMP). This specifies the particular type of binary
data item, and may be COMP, COMP-1, COMP-2, ... COMP-6, etc. You must
know the usage in order to interpret the stored values correctly.

Storage of binary numbers, regardless of usage, also depends on whether the
hardware platform is big-endian or little-endian (words stored as high-order byte
first, then low-order byte, or low-order first, then high-order).
