```
(As a follow-up on a recent comp.lang.cobol thread or two)

Just for those who might be interested in the COBOL Standardization process,

The J4 "external" web-site is at:

   http://www.cobolstandards.com/

This site includes a link to where you can STILL get a free copy of the
final draft of the recently approved 2002 Standard.  (Other than a VERY few
formatting changes, there were no changes - that I know of - between this
and the approved version).

This site also includes information on what J4 is currently working on, what
its (open) meeting schedule is, and even what its upcoming meeting's agenda
is.

Although J4 is a part of the INCITS/ANSI "hierarchy" - it is also the group
that does most of the "grunt" work in the development and maintenance of
both the American (US)  and International COBOL Standards (which are
actually identical at this time).

    ***

The WG4 web site is at:

 http://www.cobolstandard.info/wg4/wg4.html

The WG4 (international COBOL Working Group) process differs significantly
from the J4 process.  "Membership" in ISO is at the "national standards
body" level and not by "individual person or company".  Therefore, to get
involved in this process you should contact your National Standards Body.
This web-site, however, does tell you what is happening at the "policy
level" of COBOL Standardization process.  It also has a number of useful
links for learning about ISO COBOL.

   ***

If you are interested in what is going to be happening (or influencing what
is going to be happening) in the next 5 years of the COBOL Standardization
process, check the comp.lang.cobol archives for a recent post on "CALL FOR
PAPERS"

If you are interested in influencing the particular direction of "your
compiler vendor of choice" - check with them for any COBOL-specific "user
groups" or methods for submitting "enhancement requests".   Contact
information for many of these vendors is in the COBOL FAQ (as is some of the
rest of the information in this note).
```
