Before you buy this "self study course" outlined by COBOLGYP, you should
read the review at Amazon.com.  In part: "This book teaches you ANSI 74
unstructured COBOL.  ANSI 85 COBOL 
(which is now 13 years old) is mentioned but not used in coding
examples. The ANSI 89 addition to the standard (instrinsic functions) 
is not covered at all.  Modularity and encapsulation can be done quite 
well even in '74 COBOL but the reader is completely uneducated
after finishing this book. And the reader would never guess that most 
modern COBOL compilers (including new IBM mainframes) support
complete object orientation and that it is fully included in the 
forthcoming ANSI standard. If you want to maintain old code on 
IBM mainframes, this book will get you by. Otherwise, even the most
diligent reader would be unfit to code at every COBOL installation 
I have ever seen."

While I agree to a great degree that the book is outdated - there has
been a second edition of the book.  Amazon.com has included reviews of
the FIRST edition with the reviews for the SECOND edition - a bad, but
wide spread practice at their site. (check out the revies for TY COBOL
in 21 days - the first edition and compare them to the second).  As an
author, I can tell you that these reviews DO have an impact, and it's
unfair the way they are attributed to the second edition.   With my
book, we made corrections to the first printing.  If someone got
really angry the could complain about the extra period in CHAPT04C
that makes the program not compile.  So far all that have written to
me or who have contacted the publisher have been friendly and
understanding.  But I still wait for that one individual to ROUTE the
book because of a single typo - one that, BTW was not included in ANY
of my manuscripts that went back to the publisher - but where someone
got a little crazy with cut and paste, or search and replace.  Anyway
these mistakes happen and books and authors get crucified for them.  I
know that TY COBOL in 21 Days has been updated in the second edition
with many "bonus" chapters and additional information.  I am
prejudiced and I like my book better (I never even looked at TY in 21
before I finished mine), but I understand the "review" issues at
Amazon.  You have to take them each with a grain of salt.

BTW:  My book is doing better in the foriegn market than it is in the
US.  Mainly, I think, because COBOL is still being taught in most
Universities abroad.  I am thrilled.   At the same time, I'm concerned
about the poor use of English in the most recent review (on the US
site).  It's interesting also that they cross post the US reviews on
their foriegn sites, but not on the US site - so my great review on
the Amazon.co.uk site doesn't get any play in the US!
