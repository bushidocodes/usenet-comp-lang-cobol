```
After a fairly long period without much happening, suddenly there is
renewed interest in PRIMA tools.

( I was beginning to think that our potential market place might have
moved on from COBOL or found other solutions- I'm sure some of them
have... but, there are still people who are looking for a good solution.
Fortunately, we managed to keep the company going through the rough
patches and there is always plenty to be done).

I am pleasantly surprised that there are COBOL users in places as widely
diverse as the USA, Spain, and Romania who are interested in getting
their indexed files replaced by a normalized, optimized Relational
Database. And there are an increasing number of people who are looking
at moving PowerCOBOL to .Net. There is one client currently who is doing
both (Moving to .Net from PowerCOBOL AND replacing flat files with RDB...)

Neither of these exercises are what you would call "trivial" (if they
are done properly...) but we have 2 toolsets that address them:

1. For migration of code and data from an indexed file base to a modern
database: MigTSet
(https://primacomputing.co.nz/PRIMAMetro/demosandtutorials.aspx - see
video 0)

2. For a solid conversion away from dependency on PowerCOBOL: PC2N
(PCOB2Net) (https://primacomputing.co.nz/PRIMAMetro/PCOBMigVideos.aspx
- see video "Quick video"

If you have trouble viewing any of the above, right click, then select
"Open in a new tab". You can also download the videos for local viewing
with whatever software you prefer. Please note that, although these
videos are a little out of date (we are on Release 3 of PC2N), the
principles they outline are still valid. I'm just too busy currently to
make new videos (a lot of time and effort goes into them...)

OK, so we are having a revival of interest... :-)

It has been brought to my attention that PRIMA does not use a company logo.

Before I race off to a graphic designer, I thought it might be fun for
some of you to have a go at designing us a logo.

I will pay $US150 if your design is selected, but it isn't about the
money. I want something eyecatching, that implies the underlying quality
of our company, and will provide brand recognition for us.

The search (it is not a contest...) will close on the 1st January 2019.

Please email your suggestion(s) to me personally, as an image file
attached to an email, with your contact details.

Cheers,

Pete.
I used to write COBOL; now I can do anything...
```
