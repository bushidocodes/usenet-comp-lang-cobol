```
Referring to ISO8601:2000, Data elements and
interchange formats - Information interchange -
Representation of dates and times,

-----begin quote
A calendar week is identified within a calendar year
by the calendar week number. This is its ordinal position
within the year, applying the rule that the first calendar
week of a year is the one that includes the first Thursday
of that year and that the last calendar week of a calendar
year is the week immediately preceding the first calendar
week of the next calendar year.

NOTE 1 These rules provide for a calendar year to have
52 or 53 calendar weeks;

NOTE 2 The first calendar week of a calendar year may
include up to three days from the previous calendar year;
the last calendar week of a calendar year may include up
to three days from the following calendar year;

NOTE 3 The time-interval formed by the week dates of
a calendar year is not the same as the time-interval formed
by the calendar dates or ordinal dates for the same year.
For instance:

- Sunday 1995 January 1 is the 7th day of the 52nd week
     of 1994, and
- Tuesday 1996 December 31 is the 2nd day of the 1st
     week 1997.

NOTE 4 The rule for determining the first calendar week
is equivalent with the rule "the first calendar week is the
week which includes January 4".
-----end quote

For calendar weeks, knowing the start dates of the prior,
current, and next years makes determining the correct
start date easier. Then given the correct start date, the
week number may be determined by subtracting the start
date from the given date, taking the integer of that difference
divided by 7, then adding 1.

The following program implements this description within
the context of ISO8601:2000.
-----
      $set ans85 flag"ans85" flagas"s"
       identification division.
       program-id. wk-of-yr.
      *implements ISO8601:2000 for calendar week
       data division.
       working-storage section.
       01 given-date pic 9(8) value 20051231.
       01 integer-of-given-date binary pic 9(8).
       01 work-table.
         02 work-item occurs 3.
           03 work-year binary pic 9(4).
           03 work-jan-4 binary pic 9(8).
           03 work-first-date binary pic 9(8).
       01 work-sub binary pic 9(4).
       01 week-for-given-date pic 9(2).
       procedure division.
       begin.
           perform with test after until given-date = 0
               display "Enter a date as CCYYMMDD: "
                   with no advancing
               accept given-date
               if given-date = 0
                   continue
               else
                   perform calculations
                   display "Calendar week for " given-date " is "
                       week-for-given-date " in " work-year (work-sub)
               end-if
           end-perform
           stop run
           .
       calculations.
           divide given-date by 10000 giving work-year (2)
           subtract 1 from work-year (2) giving work-year (1)
           add 1 to work-year (2) giving work-year (3)
           perform varying work-sub from 1 by 1
             until work-sub > 3
               compute work-jan-4 (work-sub) =
                   function integer-of-date
                   (work-year (work-sub) * 10000 + 0104)
               compute work-first-date (work-sub) =
                   (function integer
                    ((work-jan-4 (work-sub) - 1) / 7))
                       * 7 + 1
           end-perform
           compute integer-of-given-date =
               function integer-of-date (given-date)
           evaluate integer-of-given-date
             when work-first-date (1)
                   thru (work-first-date (2) - 1)
               move 1 to work-sub
               perform calculate-week
             when work-first-date (2)
                   thru (work-first-date (3) - 1)
               move 2 to work-sub
               perform calculate-week
             when other
               move 3 to work-sub
               perform calculate-week
           end-evaluate
           .
       calculate-week.
           compute week-for-given-date =
               (function integer (
                   (integer-of-given-date
                       - work-first-date (work-sub))
                   / 7)) + 1
           .
       end program wk-of-yr.
-----
```

```
Actually, December 20, 2005 falls in the 52nd week, because 2005-01-01 fell 
on Saturday of the first week, making the final full week of 2005 (Dec 25 - 
31) the 53rd week. I will assume you do not have intrinsic functions. If you 
do have intrinsic functions, the appropriate substitutions in the method 
below should be clear.

First, download DATES.ZIP from my website below. It contains COBOL date 
calculation and manipulation routines. I will refer to routines and data 
items within those routines in the text below. Directions on how to use the 
routines are clearly commented in the code.

Use routine 001100-DATE-DAYS to calculate the Date Day Number of January 1st 
of the year in question (you can construct the date YYYY0101 using the 
breakdown of data item DW-WORK-YYYYMMDD) and store it in (I'll call it) 
START-DAY.

Use routine 001100-DATE-DAYS to calculate the Date Day Numbers for the date 
in question (which I will call TargetDate) and store it in (I'll call it) 
TARGET-DAY.

Perform routine 001200-WEEKDAY, which will return the day of the week in 
data item DW-WEEKDAY. (Note that routine 001100-DATE-DAYS preserves the 
argument in data item DW-WORK-YYYYMMDD, so it still contains TargetDate).

Compute WEEK-NUMBER =
        (TARGET-DAY - START-DAY + (14 - DW-WEEKDAY)) / 7
```

```
First you have to define weeks of the year.  What is the first week of 
the year?  The day may start on Monday or any day.

Also on your calendar, what is the week? Is it Sunday through Saturday 
or is it Monday through Sunday? or what?

Ideally you could have a way to select this as an option or as a variable.

Here is the concept:  "If Saturday is the last day of the week, and the 
year starts on Saturday, is Saturday the first week all by itself?"

These things you have to decide first.

So you could divide the julian number by 7 and use the remainder for the 
day of the week.  Then use a spacial index to add to that for the real 
day of the week.  Then divide by 52 for the week.  You would have to 
play with it to get it to work.

You could get carries away with this and build a 2 level array to figure 
this out and work through the array to figure out the week with week 
occurs 52 or 53 times and day occurs 7 times.  Then you have 2 indexes 
and as you cycle through the array you have to figure out where you are.

That may be too much work compared to pure math based on the julian date.

Another way is to build your own calendar with fields like:
a numeric index i.e. 001-356, the date in another field i.e. yyyymmdd, 
whether it is a work day, and the day of the week as a number i.e. 1 
thru 7, then the week number i.e. 1-53.  Then search the table for the 
date and lookup the result.

We build our calendar in an indexed file so we can look it up.  If you 
had an indexed file you could use the date as 20051202 and just use a 
random lookup using the date as a possible index and have multiple 
indexes for the julian number, or your unique numbering system.
```
