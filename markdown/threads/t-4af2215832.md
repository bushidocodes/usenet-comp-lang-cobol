planing to use your TY COBOL book once again.  I do have a

Floyd, as Rich has already pointed out it is Will Price - close but any
confusion is because you are also thinking of Ed Arranga (and Frank
Coyle) who wrote another book on OO COBOL. Ed is Editor of
cobolreport.com/objectz.com and Will is Deputy Editor.

Should you cover OO in one semester - difficult - because of your time
constraints. If as you say they are already familiar with Java, make a
passing reference to OO COBOL with coding comparisons - briefly showing
how we are doing the same thing with COBOL classes/methods. Try and
avoid the GUI issue - it will only confuse in a short course. I'm
biased, but would suggest you stick in a plug for
collections/dictionaries (as yet an underdetermined feature in COBOL 
200x) copy-catted from Smalltalk. (Should you be interested in
background on this feature - check out Adele Goldberg "Smalltalk 80").

Is OO COBOL the wave of the future - I'd like to think so - who knows ?

Floyd

It's not Ed Price, it's Wilson (Will) Price.

I can respond better to your second question. Why teach web
programming to COBOL programmers? This depends, do your student
plan on writing business applications for the web? If there is a
remote possiblity, then yes. Over time, I will be taking an
application that I market for the PC and apply it to the web. I
will be able to create forms using MS Frontpage or NetExpress,
incorporate them with NetExpress, access data bases (SQL, ISAM,
etc.), update, remove and add records over the internet.

Using COBOL for web programming gives your students another tool
to develop web applications. Will Price's "Elements of COBOL Web
Programming" is an excellent resource, but it doesn't go into
much detail about publishing a COBOL web application to the
internet. Will and I have talked and he will be providing more
information on this topic.
