Bill:

Are they looking for the cost of both an "I" and an "O" or just one or
the other?  I believe that most "I-O shops" sell them as a package,
which means that you pay a premium when purchasing them separately.

You also have to take into consideration the condition of the "I-O."
For example, do they want to purchase new "I-O's" or are they willing
to accept used ones.  New ones generally cost more, but then they also
come with a warranty.  Used ones are cheaper unless you purchase
antique "I-O's" which can be very expensive (providing of course they
are in very good condition).

I prefer to purchase my "I-O's" at "I-O" auctions.  There is nothing
quite as exciting as a flurry of competitive bidding on a highly
desirable "I" or "O."

Be careful when buying "I-O's" outside of your home country and then
attempting to carry them onto a commercial flight.  If you attempt to
carry more than 10,000 I-O's through customs, they can be confiscated
in some countries.

I know that the VAT for I-O's in France are quite high because of the
high price/high quality of I-O's from the Bordeaux region.
