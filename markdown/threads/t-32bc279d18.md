This is a Usenet newsgroup.  Its existence doesn't really depend on
anyone's sponsorship, rather a simple entry in the list of such
groups*.  Periodically inactive groups are purged by the committee
(the "Big-8 Management Board") overseeing such things, but this groups
gets *vastly* more than enough traffic to avoid that fate.

Google Groups is just a very poor newsgroup reader.  Google Groups
also has their own private group/discussion board system, which its
user interface does not clearly distinguish from non-Google groups,
like this one.  But Google Groups could go away tomorrow, and other
than disconnecting a few people using it to access this group, it
would not impact comp.lang.cobol one bit.



*At least within the "Big 7" (comp.*, news.*, sci.*, rec.*, soc.*,
talk.*, misc.*).  Stuff in the alt.* groups plus any of the many
non-standard group hierarchies (for example, Australia's aus.*
hierarchy), follows different rules.
