Alright, gang... here's a doozy. I got a spam from an agency for some
manner of coding slot for which I was not qualified; I gave a simple
reply:

› (PIMPCO) has placed over 5000 technical people into new positions in Atlanta and
› the Southeast.

DocDwarf immediately discards *any* job-offer which does not include a
rate.

... simple enough, neh? They refer to themselves in the third person, I
refer to myself in the third person, everything is equitable... until I
get a reply of... (my comments, bracketed by... brackets, are
more-or-less what I replied to them... although I was a bit more civil)

› The reason we don't send out a rate is so candidates don't automatically
› disqualify themselves based on this criteria.

{so? companies disqualify candidates based on the criterion of rate
every day, why not have it work both ways? 'I don't care if he's
Charles Goddamned Babbage, we ain't paying *nobody* that much until he's
in a corner office!'}

› We find that very often clients will consider a higher rate than they originally >anticipated based on an individuals skill set and experience they have to offer.

{and I find that very often this is utter crap, see the 'Babbage'
comment above... not until it is too late to do the job does the rate go
up}

Next reply I expect to get from these dolts is 'We need someone smart
enought to solve the three-body problem but who will believe us when we
micturate upon her spine and inform her it is precipitation'

DD
