```
Okay, not being a mainframe type of guy, I got hit with a
really wierd thing day before yesterday. I'll tell you guys
what I wound up proposing, though I'll be danged if I can figure
out exactly *what* the customer was asking for.

One of my larger customers recently hired an "expert" to install
a small NT system for them. The - ah - person they hired told
them they could run a bunch of Wyse and 3151 terminals out on their
shop floor- with NT. That NT was a "multi-user" multi-tasking system,
and could drive terminals just fine. I think he proposed CITRIX or
something like that. (Which, can do some pretty amazing things,
but driving these terminals ain't one of them...)

Anyway, the long and short of it this guy gets fired, and I get
a call. (Note, that I joshingly told them they would be calling me
after they had enough "fooling around." I thought I was joking,
but they believe I was serious! :) The CEO of this little company
is so steamed I'd be a bit afraid to be around him. He reads off
the CIO's recommendation to me: Get an ALL IBM solution to this
mess!" The CEO says to me "Get an IBM Solution, get it in here fast,
and get it in here on the EXISTING equipment. FAST. We want 3270
terminals out there!"


Now they have a some IBM server running NT, 18 ascii terminals,
and a couple dozen PC workstations. Easy enough to hande the server,
replace NT with OS/2 or SCO. SCO will handle botht he terminals and
the workstations with ease. But it isn't from IBM.
(*sigh*)

Soo we look at OS/2. It absolutely cannot drive the terminals.

But guess what *can*? CICS. On OS/2. Now I know next to NOTHING
about CICS except it functions like a job monitor, communiations
controller, scheduler, and probably a dozen more things all at once.
But IBM says it will do it. And the IBM rep says - it's expensive.
(It isn't *that* bad- about $4K for the CICS, plus user licenses.)


Well, the long and short of it is - I called the CEO and told him
I could do a long involved IBM OS/2 process, and it would be expensive.
Especially since there appears to be exactly TWO COBOL compilers that
support CICS - IBM Visual Age and MicroFocus, neither of which I
own at this time. Or I could put SCO OpenServer on there, load an
AcuCOBOL program, and he would be running. He said mock it up and
let him see it, which I did. Amazingly enough, they just called and
said "GO!". I think it impressed them that I ran the demo on
a little IBM Valuepoint with a 486/33 in it, and it ran 3 terminals
and a workstation for the demo.

Then they asked me about 3270 terminals, and I had to admit to
total ignorance. I think they run over some kind of coaxial connection.
This was a pretty sticky point, and I said I would ask if 3270 terminals
can be used from UNIX workstations. (Well, there is a telnet 3270 -
wouldn't you just need a UNIX termcap entry for 3270??) Anyway,
I talked them into not deciding about the terminals right now,
sice they can use all those 3151 and Wy50 terminals out there.
Anyone have any pointers to what a 3270 terminal is and can it be used
with any UNIX stuff? I searched the IBM web site, but apparently
they are so ubiquitious, everyone is expected to know all about them. :)


Did I mention why this company choose the other vendor? I proposed
the project in COBOL. He proposed it in Visual Basic.

One more win for COBOL. :) :) :)

And a good start to the weekend for me! A double good start since
I picked up another job that I believe I will really enjoy as well.
:) :) Y'all have a good weekend!

-Paul
```

```
I don't know if CICS on OS/2 uses 3270 protocol, but there are a number
of Windows based 3270 terminal emulators. You can even find shareware
telnet 3270 emulators. I use QWS3270 by JGS Software in Canada.
There's an earlier version available as freeware from various download
sites, including TUCOWS. All it requires is a TCP/IP connection to the
host. Commercial 3270 emulators include Chameleon by NetManage and
Attachmate, and I'm sure there are lots of others.

IBM 3270 terminals originally required coaxial cable, but years ago
people learned you could safely use twisted pair and standard RJ11
telephone connectors (I may have that part number wrong). Ten years ago
we were running true-blue IBM 3278 terminals over telephone wires (but
not the telephone network) straight into the controllers. Now we run
Chameleon and QWS3270 over ethernet, and I know that QWS3270 works over
token-ring as well as dial-up. I don't know if that will work for you,
because we're connected to a TCP/IP stack on an Amdahl mainframe running
MVS/ESA 5.2.2.
```

```
AFAIR (not being an IBM mainframer) the 3270 terminal connects via
twin-ax (double co-ax) cable to a cluster controller which then
connects to an IBM system (AS400 or big mainframe) via either
twin-ax or RS232 (if remote).

I would suggest avoiding these if you are using Unix. It is
unlikely that you will be able to connect them, unless there is
some specialist (and probably expensive) piece of kit around.

Stick with the terminals that you have got and which work.

If the original 'consultant' had supplied the correct Wyse
WinTerms (connect via ethernet) with Citrix/NT that should have
worked.
```
