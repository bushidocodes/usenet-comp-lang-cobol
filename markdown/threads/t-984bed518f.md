Well, you do not provide very much input data, but I have had time where I had to be
content with even less.

The one clue that you give is that 

> ONLY HALF (LIKE EVERY OTHER ONE) OF THE DATA IS SENT TO THE OUTPUT

My guess (and I could be way off here

Your program reads Data from SYSIN using ACCEPT

Your program WRITES the ACCEPTed data to some output

If the declaration of the input record is larger than the implied record size for the
accept statement, COBOL will read a second record from input to fill the rest of the
record buffer
Obviously, you will never "see" this record, and thus loose half of your data
