that you really know whatever language(s) you use. Hardly a day will go by
that you wonder if your chosen language is the correct one for you..

guidance. Above all else, remember that business seldom changes because it
is fashionable. They only change when it is cheaper and they can make more

This may not be the right question to ask. It might be better addressed
at the application level, rather than at the hardware level. It becomes
much clearer that way that some programs would be vastly improved by a
client/server implementation, while others, like bibo processes will always
be accomplished best on something with blistering though-put like a
mainframe. I suspect the mainframe of the future may not be recognizable
as having evolved from mainframes of today (and may not be related at all).
They may be massively parallel arrays of individual PCs, or an evolution
of current SMP technology or even use room-temperature superconductors in a
box the size of your wallet.
I think COBOL won't die, although once again it may evolve into something
unrecognizable today. I'm looking forward to seeing development
environments based on COBOL97, and with any luck they will do for COBOL
what Delphi has done for Pascal or PowerBuilder has done for C.
