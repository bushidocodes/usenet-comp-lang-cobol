We have had some turnover reciently.  I don't think it has been
directly caused by salary or people problems.  I think the primary
cause has been opportunities opening up for people to get away from
dead-end, no-fun work positions.  Then we have to recruit from outside
for these jobs because no one in house wants them, and it becomes very
hard to find someone qualified for the unique skills required, so we
appear to have a staff shortage.

I think our industry has a shortage of fun jobs.  And people with the
desirable skills are shying away from the difficult jobs.

Another significant problem with our industry is too many  unique
skill requirements.  What new language or technology will we have to
manage in the next 6 months?  Give me a break!

Strictly one mans opinion :-)
Pete
