It was coloured by my personal experiences _while_ I was working with
them. I worked on the GTE Wireless/Verizon account, on a team that was
located on the customer site. What a team we were! Excellent project
management, technical support, your name it. Then we were relocated
with the rest of the account, and I got to see how they worked. Sloppy
management, cost-overruns, little attention to detail, "don't-care"
attitudes, and no real procedures for project management. Just take
someone who has been there a while and give them a project, regardless
of whether they have the skills for it.

I worked on a project at the end where the project manager
consistently cancelled meetings without regard as to whether those
working on the project required them. It was a project having to do
with archiving data for Verizon so they could access their billing and
point of sale data after our exit. How can you cancel such a meeting
without asking those needing to attend whether or not they need to
have the meeting? Not to mention he often failed to notify those
attending... But this is an example, not an isolated case.

I am at least proud of the fact that when I said goodbye to my
customers, they were not aware that I was a contractor...

And EDS was well known for being unable to retain their better
employees. And the last two years with them wasn't fun. First, we lost
our bonuses. Then, on our account we were told there would be
retention bonuses if we stayed on. Those never materialized. Soon
enough, we realized that the "retention bonus" was "you might still
have a job". Then, they froze our salaries. Then, the layoffs came.
Outsourcing started en-masse. I was let go after re-training for the
BellSouth account, while a junior employee I had helped hire was kept.
My skill-set far exceeded his. He was still dependent on me to do his
job. The only other difference was I am sure I made more money. So, I
could save EDS more money if I was let go.

Yes, I'd say I was bitter over that. But still, I realized that they
were all talk and no substance long before that.

Still looking for that better door... or hell, just a door!

Ben
