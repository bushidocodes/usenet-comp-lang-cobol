```
To start a new thread (in reply to a comment by Mr. Wagner in another
thread).  The following is my PERSONAL perception of what is (almost)
unanimous in replies to his posts:

1) When he argues that one STYLE is "superior" to another, most comments
will be negative (even from those who PERSONALLY prefer the same style as he
does). Failure to distinguish between "style" and non-subjective issues is a
reoccurring problem

2) When he states "as fact" something that is actually his opinion, replies
will cite counter-examples and ignore his "content".  (In other words his
implied "In my opinion" causes negative responses.)

3) When he "generalizes" from his experience to claim that what he has
experienced is "generally true" those with other experiences will argue with
him. (Particularly when he "jumps" from IBM mainframes to ALL mainframes and
when he fails to distinguish between experiences 20-30 years ago with those
of today.)

4) Actual "errors in fact" (e.g. what the Standard says, what Micro Focus
does in <implied> all environments, what is true in linguistics - as applied
to either natural or artificial languages, etc) will often get corrections
ALONG WITH comments on his "usual accuracy".

    ***

As my perception (and I have *not* quantified this to verify this) is that
the MAJORITY of his posts have one or more of the above, I probably agree
with HIS perception that whatever he posts many/most of this group will
"disagree" with.
```
