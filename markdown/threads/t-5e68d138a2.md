Aloha,

You didn't say what platform or compiler you're using, but I'll assume its a
IBM mainframe. My experience (from several years ago) was that before the
first I/O and after the AT END condition, the file section record was
"unavailable" (pointer undefined). Also, once a record is written, the file
record may not be available (memory a bit fuzzy here). So, WS records are
very useful for testing keys/data after EOF conditions, processing data after
it's actual written, etc.

It is certainly true that modern operating systems and diagnostic utilities
will make the file buffers visible in a dump. But the IBM 360/30 I learned
on had exactly 64K BYTES of memory. The operating system (DOS, not even

the remaining 54K.

Even today I would argue that READ INTO and WRITE FROM is not necessarily a
bad recommendation to make. It is frequently easier to locate
working-storage by the character display portion of a storage dump.

And as for teaching it, I don't know if that school even teaches COBOL
anymore. I wish more schools taught COBOL at all.
