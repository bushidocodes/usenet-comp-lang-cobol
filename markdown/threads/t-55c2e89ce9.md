end of file is reached. Like so ...

77 END-FLAG PIC X VALUE "N".

I'm not sure if this is homework for you, but since I am enjoying my lunch,
I'll take a stab at it. You need an accumulator that counts each time you
read the input file. Here is the syntax for the table:

05 REC-COUNT PIC S9(04) BINARY.
05 TABLE-MAX PIC S9(04) BINARY VALUE +50.
05 MY-TABLE
OCCURS 50 TIMES
DEPENDING ON REC-COUNT
INDEXED BY MY-TABLE-INDEX.
10 TABLE-ITEM-1.
10 TABLE-ITEM-2.

I always use indexes, but you may do as you like.

In the procedure division, you load the table:

BUILD-TABLE.
PERFORM READ-TABLE-FILE
PERFORM LOAD-TABLE
VARYING MY-TABLE-INDEX
FROM +1 BY +1 UNTIL TABLE-FILE-EOF
END-PERFORM
.

LOAD-TABLE.
IF REC-COUNT < TABLE-MAX
CONTINUE
ELSE
MOVE INPUT-DATA TO TABLE-ITEM-1(MY-TABLE-INDEX)
END-IF
PERFORM READ-TABLE-FILE
.

I am new to COBOL as well, so if I make any errors, I'm sure they will be
noted by others, and forgive my (perhaps) peculiar conventions. That's how
I learned it...

Good luck!

Homework is this ?

MOVE ZERO TO Table-Counter
PERFORM
UNTIL End-of-File = "Y"

READ Data-File
AT END
MOVE "Y" TO End-of-File
NOT AT END
ADD 1 TO Table-Counter
MOVE Data-Record TO Table-Entry(Table-Counter)
END-READ
END-PERFORM

then to process the table


PERFORM
VARYING Table-Index FROM 1 BY 1
UNTIL Table-Index > Table-Counter

process 1 item
END-PERFORM

This works even if there no records in the data file, and thus
a zero counter. You also need to check that Table-Counter
never exceeds the size of the table, and displays a warning
when it does. However, with BOUNDs checking you will get
told when the table overflows.
