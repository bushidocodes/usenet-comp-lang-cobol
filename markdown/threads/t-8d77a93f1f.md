```
The major tool which you require is EDUCATION.

The mechanics of the migration are relatively simple in the vast majority
of cases - we typically do source conversion "from clean compile on OS/VS
to clean compile on VS COBOL II, COBOL/370, COBOL for MVS and VM or COBOL
for OS/390" for less than one hour's billing for a typical COBOL cranker
contractor.

What is less clear is the more subtle changes. It compiles clean but does
not work the same anymore.

Also less clear is what to do with non-COBOL components such as assembler
routines mixed in the COBOL applications.

Even less clear is the configuration and certification of the LE/370 and
compiler environment. Lots of potential mis-behavior for the un-aware.

Tools that I recommend:

"Planning COBOL Migration" - 3 days of comprehensive training on the
process, the issues, and the solutions

RWS Predictability Test Suite for COBOL. A set of test cases to explore
all of those nasty mis-behaviors which await the unaware.

"Edge Portfolio Analyzer" - a tool to tell you exactly where you are
currently, and prepare work lists of what needs to be dealt with.

(of course those were both blatant commercial statements - see
http://www.edge-information.com for more details).

For source converters there are three players that seem competent
(additional players exist, but I can not speak to their competency).

IBM has CCCA - a converter that runs on the mainframe environment.

IBM has a version of CCCA that is part of the Visual Age COBOL
Professional edition - runs on OS/2, and maybe (by now) on 95 or NT.

Prince Software has a converter who's name escapes me at the moment -
another mainframe product. As I recall, more expensive than the IBM
offerings, but more functional in some areas, and with at least a claimed
performance advantage.

Rex Widmer
Builder of software archeology tools and other strange programs to help survive in a legacy based world.
```
