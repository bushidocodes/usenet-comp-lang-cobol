A background to a lot of your questions can be found if you go to
cobolreport.com and take a look at Bill Klein's COBOL FAQ - lists compiler
vendors, sites with examples and other useful links on COBOL.

Some short answers to specifics :-

1.  'fast and small programs ' - YES, plus 'fast and BIG programs'.

2.  DBs - COBOL handles, subject to compiler - I for instance currently use a
COBOL wizard which generates necessary COBOL fields from MS Access files, and
even better, the tool allows me to generate the queries and auto paste into my
COBOL source. (Nothing to stop you including SQL in your Business Logic
programs, but typically, using OO, I segregate reference to MS Access tables
into separate classes).

3. You mentioned both Windows and DOS. Any particular reason for DOS ? If so, in
a text environment, as a separate 'text window' within Windows, you can have
text applications - in this respect COBOL Screen Section would be appropriate,
though I confess I think it a  somewhat backward step for somebody already
conversant with VB and Delphi.

4. As is apparent from my comments, OO is available in COBOL - specifically
POWERCOBOL from Fujitsu, and Net Express from Micro Focus.

5. GUIs - GUI classes built into some of the compilers, plus there are GUI
add-on tools from Flexus and Norcom, specifically geared to COBOL programmers.
Nothing to stop COBOL invoking VB or vice versa. Don't know about Delphi, but
assuming it fits into the OLE 'glove', no reason why you can't do the same for
that.

6. COBOL obviously offers the :-

            if
            else if
            End-if ,        syntax, plus 'IFs" can be nested

One area where I think COBOL beats the other languages hands down is what you
call CASE:  Ours has a greater degree of flexibility - the EVALUATE :-

    Evaluate true also true also true also true etc...
        when A = B and C < ??? and D < ???
                        do this.....
        when A > B also any
                        do this
        when etc....
    End-Evaluate

Like "IFs", EVALUATEs can also be nested.

7. Assuming you still want to research, suggest your starting point should be
Bill Klein's COBOL FAQ, which will link you to the different vendors.

been the total price you have paid for VB and Delphi, including any packages to
support them ?

It almost leads me to wonder if your question is more:

 Do big central information systems still have a place in a world of PC's and distributed
network systems?

The answer to that is less than obvious, and is usually expressed as 'Does COBOL have ...'
etc. The answer to that is somewhat non-intuitive. When you narrow the scope of an
issue down, it usually appears that either one or the other is the best solution. That is, however,
nothing more than an illusion of scale. Every system needs some kind of central coordination
and some kind of distributed processing. In some systems, one is very much more important
than the other, while in some systems, they tend to balance much more closely.

COBOL fits easily into systems where the emphasis is closely balanced, or weighted towards
a centralized control. It also fits very well into the *parts* of a distributed system than
deal with the necessary centralized controls.

This is of course, no a comprehensive overview - it is just something that I and others have found to be
generally true. I am sure there are exceptions, but in truth, I haven't really *found* any exceptions,
in more than 20 years of doing this stuff...
