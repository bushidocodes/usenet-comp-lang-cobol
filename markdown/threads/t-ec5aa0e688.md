Well, this is how I did it.  I left college after gaining two A levels in
1991.  Wrote to as many companies as my career officer could give me, asking
for work as a trainee programmer.  I explained that I had an A level in
Computer Science, that I'd had a deep interest in computers for a long time,
stuff like that.

Eventually one of them liked my CV enough to give me an interview, and took
me on.  Once you get in, where you end up is up to you.

Any decent careers office will have a large number of contacts in your local
area which you can write too.  These are invaluable.  Local companies often
go for trainees, as they have a harder job recruiting against the big
cities.

Sell yourself as much as possible on the CV, and tailor your attached letter
to the company.  Do some research on each company on the Internet.  It shows
your interested in working for them.  This letter and CV needs to be good
enough to get you an interview, where you can really sell yourself.  Most
companies like trainees, as they can shape them to suit their systems, don't
have to pay them lots of money, and still get a decent return on the work
level.

Finally, one other hint is to get a book on interview techniques.  There are
a lot available, and can help coach you into really selling yourself,
answering the difficult questions, and making the company wonder how they
did without you.  Essential if your going for a trainee position.

So, find a local careers office, get local contacts, and sell yourself to
them as a trainee.  And make sure your up to speed with interview
techniques.

Well, hope things go well for you.
