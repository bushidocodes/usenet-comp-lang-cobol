done several times in the past) - a point to bear in mind if they try
the 'loyalty' card (eg whatever they say now, they'll still be looking for
someone cheaper when the heat's off).

I've heard that the world will end when the DOW goes over 9,999.

to their shiny new(ish) mainframe systems when they've been
polished a bit and start catching up with all the upgrades that
weren't done in the '90s.

Anyway, when everyone else has moved to the latest whizzy thing
we'll be even more scarce! (one can hope).

My company just recently (in the past 12 months) decided to take our current
character-based application to a gui environment.  After looking at some
options like Delphi and C++, they decided that it would be easier to have
the existing COBOL programmers learn how to use NetExpress and Dialog and
stick with COBOL than try to bring in a bunch of new programmers who don't
know how the current code works.  The result is we all got raises, we are
improving and enhancing the current product, and we have extended the lives
of the product (and our jobs) for many years to come.  Viva le COBOL!!!
