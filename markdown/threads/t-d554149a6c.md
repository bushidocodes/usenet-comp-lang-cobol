Ah, another old-timer.

robin wrote:
> 
> I think the F compiler ran under model 40, in 1966.
> It certainly ran with 128K main memory, and I think
> even smaller memories.

Sure, but a "typical" system around this time was around 32K.  64K would
have been a big machine. (I worked for several consultants, and got to
see a lot of installations.)
> 
> The DOS version ran on model 30, and there was even a
> version that ran on the 1100 (?)

Sure the DOS version ran, but very slooooowly.  People were complaining
about COBOL overhead.  The bigger PL/I compiler had to be broken down
into so many "phases" (overlays) that it was probably 50% or more slower
than COBOL.  

The other machine was an 1130 - a small FORTRAN-oriented machine.  They
typically came with 8K 16-bit words and a small removable disk.  
"SL/I" was an interpreter.  It parsed fast, but ran slowly.  I played
with it at one time, but it was basically a toy developed as EWS
(employee-written software).
