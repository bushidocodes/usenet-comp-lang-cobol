```
Given how/when people do their planning (and budgeting) for business trips,
I thought I would post some information here and now - but really about next
year.
   (Please pass this note on to anyone else that you think would be
interested - even if they don't frequent "comp.lang.cobol".)

Are you in Europe - or do you like beautiful Amsterdam in the Spring?
   Do you care about the future of COBOL?

Well ...

There will be a co-located J4 and WG4 meeting in Amsterdam next Spring
(April 2000) - currently scheduled as follows:

  J4  - Thursday - Saturday (April 6 - April  8 ) *and*
        - Thursday - Friday (April 13 - April 14
  WG4 - Monday - Wed (April 10 - April 12)

       ***

Please understand,
   J4 meetings are BY DEFINITION "open" to anyone who is interested in
attending.  Furthermore, there are (usually?) many votes where both an "all
present" and a "voting members" votes are taken.  All attendees are allowed
(encouraged?) to speak. If you think you might be attending JUST this part
of the Amsterdam meeting and want to be notified of how to "register" or to
get other planning information, I suggest that you contact the J4 chair at:
     doncobol <at> mediaone.net

  WG4 meetings are BY DEFINITION "closed."  You *MUST* be on a pre-submitted
national delegation list.  If you are interested in participating in this
portion of the Amsterdam meetings, you should contact your national
"Standards" body *now* to find out how to be on their delegation.
(Different countries have different rules and requirements.)  If you aren't
certain how to contact your national body, I suggest that you start at the
WG4 web-page and look either from there directly or via the SC22 page to
find out about how to reach your national body.  The WG4 home page is at:
    http://anubis.dkuug.dk/jtc1/sc22/wg4/

    ***

OK, why am I mentioning this specific meeting?

Unless something very unexpected happens between now and then, the "output"
of this meeting will be the "Final Committee Draft" of the next COBOL
Standard.  This is a document that will be sent to both ISO and ANSI Public
Reviews.  (At least for ISO - it is uncertain how ANSI will handle this),
this will be mostly a "take it or leave it" type review.  (Minor changes
will be possible - but not major technical changes.)  Therefore, if you want
to insure that what the committees send to the public actually meets what
you think the public wants/expects - this is your (final) chance.

Also, I am mentioning this meeting because it DOES provide a place/time for
European COBOL fans to participate in J4 (open) meetings with less expense
than the usual US meetings.

  ***

If you are interested in the current status of J4's work (on the next
revision),  I suggest that you check out the J4 "external" web-page at:
   http://merant.microfocus.com/Standards/

NOTE WELL:
   The end of that page tells you how to guarantee that you are notified
when the draft Standard goes out to it next (final?) public review.
```
