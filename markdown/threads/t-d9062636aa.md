Extensions that include "AT LINE ... COLUMN ..." are available in *some* but
not all COBOL compilers.

you should check out the LRM (Language Reference Manual).   You should also
check to see if your compiler supports the "Screen Section" - another
medium-common extension.

I'm not familiar with the COBOL/B compiler that you mention, but ACCEPT
and DISPLAY depend upon the actual compiler you will be using. There are
two forms :-

- Standard ANSI ACCEPT and DISPLAY :-

    accept anumber
    display NumericEditedNumber

Both those forms just appear on the next line on your DOS text screen.

- and what you've indicated in your message, some varying flavours of
SCREEN SECTION which allow you do do what you illustrated :-

    ACCEPT MYNUM LINE 12 COLUMN 20

and I think some compilers might have, as an alternative to the above :-

    ACCEPT MYNUM COLUMN 20 LINE 12, (perhaps even POSITION instead of
COLUMN)

For your given compiler, either from the Reference Manuals or on-line
help look up ACCEPT, DISPLAY and SCREEN SECTION (if it has the latter).

These verbs are used to accept & display low volumes of data.  In
IBMese, the ACCEPT will pickup data from the //SYSIN DD statement, and
the DISPLAY will write to the //SYSOUT DD statement.  There is no
end-of-file test available for ACCEPT, nor is there line spacing
available for the DISPLAY.  Also (apparently) relatively inefficient.
Upside is that a SELECT and FD are not required for the SYSIN & SYSOUT
files.

Usage is primarily to ACCEPT control information and to DISPLAY error
messages, in batch COBOL applications.

Newer (non-standard) COBOL implementations also use ACCEPT / DISPLAY
for screen manipulation.

Hope this helps...
