Hi
I read the discussions about cobol variale name length, and to my humble
opinion, most of you missed the main point. Adhering to the standard, does not
mean only portability but also compatability to newer versions.
I had recently to convert hundreds of old Cobol programs to Cobol II on an IBM
MF. most of the programs were written with complete disregard to the standard
or to IBM's own warnings. I had to rewrite many sections instead of automatic
conversion.
In my experience, I once wrote a system which used Cobol besides other
languages. That system lasted 10 years without change, despite many changes in
the environment only due to adherence to standards.

To add a point about "Is Cobol dead?". It is definitely not. We now
even have Object Oriented Cobol, which means that Cobol is here to stay.

Ilan Even-Or
