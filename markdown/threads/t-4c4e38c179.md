```
OK folks - it's time for an informal survey (which I may or may not pay
attention to):

The FAQ is STARTING to get "current."  (See the WEB version if you are
interested in what is there and/or what is new.)
     http://pweb.netcom.com/~wmklein/cobolfaq.htm

I have also "resurrected" my (COBOL) program to convert the HTML-ish version
to a relatively readable "text" version.

In the past, I put the *FULL* text version out in the "automated" FAQ
poster.  This meant that a fairly LONG (and often non-changing) news item
was posted to comp.lang.cobol and alt.cobol every 15 days.

In most of the US, "reading in" this article into one's NG reader isn't too
bad (or expensive) - and anyone who isn't interested can just skip it or
"filter it out."  HOWEVER,  in some other places in the world (and possibly
with some ISPs in the US), there are "charges" for connect time and
downloading all of this "stuff".

I *could* just post (every 15 days) a "pointer" to the web version of the
FAQ.  HOWEVER, when I last tried this (and according to the reports of some
of the other FAQ readers), this is a problem for those who (still) don't
have access to the WEB - but do access the internet.  Furthermore, it means
that the latest text of the FAQ isn't "available" to those who browse the
Standard FAQ archives.

I am NOT interested in (or willing to) tell people that they can send me
private email to get the text version.  I get enough of these anyway - and
just do NOT want to worry about "timely replies" to this type of request.

  So ...

What do the NG readers think about this?  Should I keep posting the full
text version? or can I switch to just posting a "pointer" to the web
version?  If I did the latter, would it help any if I posted the full
table-of-contents and the "latest changes" sections? (but if I did this,
wouldn't I still get requests for the text version?)
```
