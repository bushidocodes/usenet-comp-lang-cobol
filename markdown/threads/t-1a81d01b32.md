This is silly. If somebody hires me for 1 hour once a month, the rate is
very different than if somebody hires me for 40 hours a week for a month.
I doubt you can find a "lawn mower" in the world that makes $65 per
hour for 40 hours per week. Or a mechanic. If you want to compare rates,
compare them to the person hired for a season to cut lawns, or to what
a mechanic actually earns per hour for continuous eight hour days.

If you go from site to site, fixing problems in two hour sessions, and you
are not billing a minimum of $150 per hour, you will go bankrupt. On the
other hand, I would take $50 per hour as an hourly rate on a permanent
job, and consider it good pay for ANY programmer. That comes out to
$100,000 per year, which is reasonable if you can hack a steady job.

Ninety percent of my work is for four to twenty hours. I charge $125 per
hour and include travel time door to door. I also charge $0.30 per
kilometre car expense for the travel. That is not negotiable. Most larger
jobs, I either quote a fixed price, or drop my rate to the $90/hour rate.
Again, I charge door to door plus travel.

I would not consider below $90 unless the job had a minimum of 200 hours.
I would not take full-time contract as I would miss out on the higher pay
stuff.
On a long (200 hour and up) contract, I would only accept it if it were
spread
out to a minimum of 20 hours per week, with me doing the scheduling so that
I could do the other work.

In fact, the best money is fixed price, but you have to be good enough.
