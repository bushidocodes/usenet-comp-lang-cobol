Reformatting won't do it. There exist Un-Format utilities. Conversely,
there also exist "Wipe" utilities (Norton has one, for example).

Regarding PGP or other encryption routines (some are quite handy in
that they encrypt/decrypt on the fly without any user intervention),
there is still a downside:

The government can compel you to provide the password. If law
enforcement has a valid warrant, courts have held that passwords or
encryption keys are equivalent to the combination of a safe and the
owner may be compelled to provide access. Remember, if you are ever in
a disupte with the government (criminal or civil), your records will
convict you irrespective of whether you're guilty. The only safe
technique is to put someone else's name on the data.

Of course if it's a question of sitting in jail for contempt or
divulging the planning documents for bombing the World Trade Center,
the choice of whether to provide the key may be clear.

Further, if the drive is broken, you can't 'wipe' it or reformat it or
anything else. The FBI can, though. Your only choice is to physically
destroy the drive.
