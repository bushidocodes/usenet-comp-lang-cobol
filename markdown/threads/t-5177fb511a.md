Sorry about that.  It's "SPECIAL-NAMES.  ODT IS CONSOLE.".

"SPO" was, as I recall, "Supervisory Printer Output" or something similar.
"ODT" is "Operator Display Terminal". The former is a holdover from
TTY-based system control devices; the latter from when we moved away from
them toward CRT devices for that purpose.  The two terms are accepted
interchangeably in our '74 implementation; of the two, '68 accepted only
"SPO".      Note that the <x> in "<x> IS <y>" in Special-Names identifies
the *implementor's* name for the device, the <y> is what the programmer
calls it within the program.
