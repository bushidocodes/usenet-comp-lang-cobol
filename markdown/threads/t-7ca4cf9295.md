From my old days as an IBM customer and as a Micro Focus employee, the first
question that I would ask you is,

   What do you want this workstation environment for:
      Maintaining and developing applications that will only run on
production on the mainframe?
      Gradually moving from a mainframe-centric applications to a
workstation or "layered" environment?
      Moving all production off the mainframe?
      Some combination of the above?

The next question that I would ask is what release of COBOL, CICS, IMS, etc
are you running on the mainframe?  The IBM products are best if you are
ABSOLUTELY current on the mainframe (and may not provide non-IBM
interfaces - such as Oracle). Do your mainframe applications interface with
Assembler? Pl/I? or any other language?

Finally, give us an estimate of what you would LIKE to spend per
Workstation? How many you plan on providing for development? for run-time?
and what operating system and memory they will have?

All of the above questions can help people make better suggestions to meet
your specific shop needs.

I would say look *hard* at Visual Age COBOL. To start with,
you get built in support for both CICS and DB2, as well as 
OBDC from PCs'. In the mainframe area, you can do almost
anything with remote compile and edit, and I understand 
it cuts a load off the mainframes. It isn't just a "compatible"
compiler either, the manual for the PC product is *also* the manual
for OS/290, VM, and the COBOL set for AIX. I like it a LOT.
