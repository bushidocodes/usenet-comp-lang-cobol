```
Back in November, a flame war in another online forum consumed much of
my time.  It caused me to re-examine how I spend my time online, and I
decided that I needed to cut back on c.l.c.  By early December, I had
decided to drop c.l.c entirely.  I wrote a few sentences of a goodbye
to c.l.c, and saved it to finish the next day.  A few hours later, I
knocked my laptop computer off a table and cracked the screen.  I need
to plug in an old desktop monitor to use the computer.  I can't read
my email sitting on the couch, or sitting in the back yard, and the
experience has been rather depressing.  Now I find over 7000 unread
messages in Forte Agent, a third of those in comp.lang.cobol.  

So, I am writing this to tell you that I no longer read
comp.lang.cobol.  It's not that this newsgroup is bad, it's that it is
good.  I would like to reply to at least 5% of the messages posted
here.  It takes about two hours a day just to keep up with this
newsgroup when I'm not posting a lot.  I feel my COBOL time is better
spent with issues related to J4 and GNU COBOL.  Those of you who are
interested in Gnubol, the archive is at
http://www.lusars.net/maillists/gnu-cobol/threads.html.

And one final note:  The New York Times quoted me out of context.  The
article at
http://www.nytimes.com/library/tech/00/01/biztech/articles/03work.html
has me saying "I'm hoping for some big disaster somewhere to hit."  I
don't know if I said those exact words -- I recall saying "I'm hoping
for a bank somewhere to be bleeding a million dollars a day" -- but
even if the words are accurate, they are out of context.  I was
talking strictly about Y2K bugs.  I am not praying for war,
pestilence, plague, and famine, I am only expecting that accounting
data will be munged, and that I will be called upon to minimize the
losses.  I am sending an email to the NYTimes to that effect.
```
