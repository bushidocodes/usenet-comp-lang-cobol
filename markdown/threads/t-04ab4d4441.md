As someone who voted AGAINST having a separate newsgroup for
acucorp/acucobol, I was wondering what comp.lang.cobol would think about
having a:

   comp.lang.cobol.oo

for Object Oriented discussions.  This is JUST A THOUGHT and I am not certain
that even I would support it, but it does seem that a LOT of the people
reading this NG (the current one) are interested in "today's" (and
"yesterday's") COBOL issues.  (Migration from '74 to '85 Standard compilers;
how to do thinks with character-based screens, etc)  while others are
interested in the "bleeding edge" technology and philosophy.  I know that I
would watch BOTH groups, but wondered if others thought it would be "good" or
"bad" to separate them.  (I don't know if the "new" group should be "OO" and
"GUI" or not - the two are related but certainly not identical.)

Anyway, I just thought I would raise the question to see what others think
about it.

Let me throw in my pennyworth. The concept isn't bad at all, but as a
group we don't want a 'split camp' - we are all interested in the
general standards and techniques of 'OUR' language. How I SORT or SEARCH
is relevant whether or not I'm using a mainframe or PC. What we have to
watch is that we don't lose touch with one another as a community using
our COMMON-BOL.

Certainly, I have found some of the issues raised regarding mainframes,
such as handling multi-users, coping effectively with large volumes of
data of extreme interest. To use a WMK phrase, 'On the other hand', I
really have no interest in those technical issues which relate to using
mainframes tied in with JCL or their OS features which support file
handling, and topics like "LE". (And obviously, I don't give a damn
whether you use NEXT SENTENCE, or not, in an 'old' compiler which
doesn't have END-IF). It often means I have to peruse the first message
in a thread and then 'Mark Thread read' - Don't misread me; these are
IMPORTANT issues to mainframers.

I'll try and tread carefully here - should it be :-

(a) Mainframes
(b) Unix/Desktops/OO - this latter category suggested because, (I'm
treading gently again), the emphasis on using OO seems to be of more
immediate interest in this group.

I think it is problematic to try and split 'desktops' between OO and
non-OO.

Third choice, as Bill suggests - a separate OO NG - but obviously open
to all who wish to participate. It doesn't happen too often, but there
are the occasional 'guilty-parties' apart from moi, who admit to using
OO. Perhaps an NG focused to OO would draw them out more - that could be 
good for the language as a whole, with the hope that techniques and
ideas get swapped.

Something important to remember in 'splitting' - where does a Newbie go
for, "How do I do this....?".

Can't offer the ideal solution - but 'them's me thoughts' ????

Jimmy, Calgary AB

I am very much sympathetic to Mr Gavan's views that there is much in "the
world of COBOL" which is platform and/or dialect independent.

About two years ago (well, sometime in the previous millenium) one of my
compiler vendors (PowerBASIC Inc Carmel CA USA), started a web-based BBS for
peer-peer discussions. They originally set up the sections based on their
products: one for their Windows Console Compiler, on for their Windows
DLL/EXE compilers, one for the MS-DOS compiler.

The first change they made in response to comments was to add a section
simply titled, "Programming" - for discussion of algorithms, techniques and
tips which were not dependent on "which specific compiler was being used"
and,  "which compiler should I be thinking about for this application and
why?"  There is, in BASIC programming, simply so much more in common across
compilers than there is different.

So... while not opposed to the idea of niche forums,  I would just as soon
get "all the news" in one place, and filter out that in which I am not
interested. ( Confession:  I also subscribe to the "whole newspaper" even
though I never read it all).
