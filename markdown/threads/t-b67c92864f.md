Pleased to see you're enjoying Texas, Thane.

Some years ago I was in Dallas for a couple of months getting a pilot's
licence. I LOVED the place and the people. The figures of speech were
hilarious and they were very warm with a great sense of humour and able to
laugh at themselves. I still support the Cowboys ("How 'Bout Them Coboys?!")
in fond memory of my time in Texas.

One quick story...

I had to do a long solo cross country flight and after about 6 hours of
flying over pistachios, macadamias, and sagebrush and coyotes, I finally
spotted the little airstrip where I was supposed to re-fuel. (You have no
idea what a relief this is to the inexperienced aviator, flying on dead
reckoning and VFR.  (VOR and DMF are for wimps...and the radio's for
listening to TEXAS FM...) As time goes by and the airstrip fails to
materialise, your anxiety rises proportionately... By the time I turned onto
final I was a gibbering wreck and just wanted to be on the ground and
wrapping myself around a Texas steak...

I taxied up to the hangar and shop and a guy came running out in his
overalls (probably hadn't seen a plane (or even a human) for several
hours... He looked at my dust covered little Tomahawk and said: "Howdy!
Where you all from?" Not exactly being myself, without thinking, I replied:
"New Zealand."

A look of awe came over his face. he looked from me to the tiny, dust
streaked aircraft, then back to me again... Finally he summed it all up in
one word: "Hell!".

I didn't have the heart to tell him I had only come from Redbird, South
Dallas. Later he loaned me his car so I could go into "town" and get a
steak. I was amazed at this, because he didn't know me at all, but then of
course, he had my aeroplane...

You can't help but be affectionate towards people who are larger than life
and play stuff like this on the radio:

"When I die
I may not go to Heaven
Cos I don't know if they let cowboys in...
If they don't
Just let me go to TEXAS!
Cos Texas is as close as ah've bin..."

Yeeeehaaa! pardners!

Pete.
