application uses significant "Micro Focus" extensions and your easiest port
would be to stay with the same compiler vendor.

Jimmy,

Our choice might be curious but I can explain the reasons that lead us
to that conclusion.

We have been developping application for 30 years on various system
but always in Cobol. We develop for our own needs and not for resale.
We switch to Unix and MF at the beginning of the 80s. We are only 2
programmers, so the internal human resources are quite limited but we
have an experience of Cobol under Unix.

Due to historical reasons, we had to maintain programs (written in MF
Cobol) under different systems : Unixware 2, SCO 3.2. It meant the
cost of different compilers and, although all Unix, dealing with small
differences from machine to machine.

We arrived at a point where two of our main servers became old and
needed to be replaced (one in France, the other in Belgium) and where
we had to implement other programs in France and Canada and probably
in Germany and Spain. We decided it was time to rationalize and
standardize all our systems. Switching to NT was a step we didn't
wanted to do (cost and expertize). We evaluated the cost of upgrading
all our systems to SCO. But when you calculate the price of the
different licences, it became a budget we couldn't afford. 

Linux appeared to be a reasonable option : 
- it's a unix-like system so there no waste of time for us,
- it is known to be stable,
- the MF compiler is avalaible and we pay only one compiler, not for
different systems,
- the cost of licence is nihil and we can deploy as many systems as we
need with the compiled code and the run-time fee.

We have now began the process of changing from our old server to brand
new system. A Dell server is not expensive and comes with everything
installed. We can even install Linux on our old server and use it as a
backup system. The second server will be installed in the next days in
Paris and then Brussels, Montreal.

I hope it was not a too boring explanation of our choice...

Alain Reymond
