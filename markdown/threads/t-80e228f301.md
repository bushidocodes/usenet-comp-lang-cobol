can expect that many applications in that process will be 'right-sized'.

About eight months ago I started asking that same question: "What happens
when all the Y2K remediation is done?"

At that time, a heard a lot of "all the deferred development will need to be
done, so COBOL skills will remain in high demand."

That was music to my ears, but I thought I'd wait before counting my
chickens. Now I'm counting. The whole Y2k-remediation thing either sucked up
all the development resources or, as mentioned above, really aided in
identifying some major economies avvailable through re-engineering.

From a purely selfish point of view, the best news of all is that a lot of
this "delayed development"  work is not suitable for entry-level
programmers, and experienced programmers like me are simply going to have to
raise our rates to maintain any sort of supply-demand balance. Bummer, huh?.
