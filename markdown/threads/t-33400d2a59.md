```
After some some initial head-scratching, the decision was made last year
to go ahead with Y2K remediation of our existing COBOL code and add a
few enhancements at the same time.  That task fell on me.

This past October 4th the new system went into production with just a minor
glitch or two.  The boss is happy because we're compliant and the users
are happy because the system is much faster and easier to use.

So I got a nice fat bonus check last week.  Much bigger than I expected.

<sour grapes>  I was going over the manual check register with the bookkeeper
today and I discovered that on the same day I got my bonus, the two outside
salesmen both received bonuses exactly double what mine was. </sourgrapes>

Better to not ever have known, I guess.  But this is not the first time I've
seen 
employers treat technical staff, especially programmers, as inferior
step-children of sales & marketing.  I was a contract programmer for 6 years
in the mid-80's and they were the only form of life lower than the in-house DP
staff back then.

JC Jones.
```
