Gazaloo:

I think that you are probably correct that this is the best approach
for our Italian friend if self-study is desired, but he should be
forewarned that the Babelfish translation tool (as all translation
tools I have ever used) does a poor job of translating to a proper
grammatical format.

Language translation is very complex and Babelfish tends to translate
the text in a fairly "literal" fashion.  It usually translates well
written English into poor Italian or excellent Italian into poor
English.

That doesn't mean that it is useless.  As long as it is recognized
that the text may be a bit awkward, the translated text can generally
be understood.
