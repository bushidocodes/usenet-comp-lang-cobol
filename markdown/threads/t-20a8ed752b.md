COMP declares the field as BINARY. You should not have converted any COMP or
COMP-3 fields in your ASCII to EBCDIC translation. If you did, it won't
work very well.

MicroFocus has a product under their workbench which allows you to define
record layouts and then translate them to ASCII. It will selectively
convert character fields and leave COMP and COMP-3 fields alone. (BTW -
COMP-3 means it's packed decimal.)

Since you mentioned the data was in a database, do you know what kind of
database? From your post I take it that you're NOT talking about DB2.
Perhaps these databases are stored in VSAM, IMDS, or IMS?

If you do, by chance, have the data in DB2, check to see if your company
has a gateway (i.e. DDCS/2). If it does, you should be able to load
the proper drivers (under Windows) to connect via ODBC. If you're under
OS/2, you should be able to get a hold of DB2/2 and go from there. When
you do this, the translation will be made for you. What you get at the
client end will be whatever type of data the client requires (most likely
ASCII.) BTW - If the data is in DB2, dates should be stored in columns
which have the DATE data type. Makes it a heck of a lot easier to deal
with dates.

Sergio,

I have done some this often, the problem is "You can't translate comp data from
EBCDIC to ASCII"!

Your best bet is to create the extract or file in display form. So for example a
field that is S9(5)V99 COMP would become S9(5)V99. This gets rid of all the
problems of convertng EBCDIC comp data to ASCII. In fact the best way is DO NOT
convert the comp data to ASCII only the display fields.

COMP is COMP whether it is on an ASCII or EBCDIC machine. Normally the only
other problem with signed fields is the location and type of the sign character.

If you ever transfer files with COMP-3 or COMP fields from an IBM (EBCDIC) to
anything using ASCII (Unix, VAX, PC, whatever), you need to unpack your
numeric packed fields into display fields (i.e. change the 9(7) COMP or COMP-3
to just 9(7).)

I've had to just that exercise for migrating data from an IBM mainframe to
a Unix box.
