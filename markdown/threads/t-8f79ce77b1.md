```
A few decades back a college-aged lad I knew thought my musical horizons 
were too limited and tried to expand them.  My tastes ran mostly towards 
orchestral (then called 'classical') music and early American 
country/folk/'hillbilly' music; I thought this genre had begun to 
degenerate when Bill and Charlie Monroe broke up.

At any rate... this lad introduced me to New, Hep group with the 
improbable name of Hot Tuna... and lo, I was *very* impressed.  I had some 
initial trouble pronouncing Jorma Kaukonnen (and still, at times, confuse 
his name with Jarkko Oikarinen's, the inventor of IRC) but was amazed by 
the technical mastery and the sense of music the man demonstrated.

(The lad, emboldened by his success, then cued up a disc of more common 
taste and I did my best to listen... and the best I could say was 'The 
guitarist on this sounds a lot like the one doing the acoustic work 
earlier.'  That was my introduction to another improbably-named group, The 
Jefferson Airplane, whose lead guitarist was Jarkko Oikarinen... errrrr, 
Jorma Kaukonnen.)

And so... earlier I was using the YouTube site, finding odd bits here and 
there... and I ran across http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lUseW_yJkpg , a 
recording of Kaukonnen and Casady from 1986 (their first album also 
contained this song and was issued in 1970).

From there it gets strange.  I noticed a link to 
http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QjHl-57_I0g and a recording of the same 
song (if it can be called that) by its purported originator, a fellow I've 
perhaps mentioned, one Blind Willie Johnson.  Blind Willie's works were 
familiar to me long before Kaukonnen's and the two interpretations made 
for curious contrasts.

For those who truly enjoy the old Delta Blues 'Woke Up This Mornin', 
Gargled With Some Rusty Razor-blades' vocal style I'd recommend 
http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SW1SRJrNZZw ... be careful, though, as I 
have seen incidents where this song has driven people out of the room, 
screaming.

No accounting for taste, eh?

DD
```
