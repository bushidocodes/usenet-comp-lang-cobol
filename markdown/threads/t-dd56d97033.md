```
A (slightly) updated version of the currently recognized list of
"enhancement request" for some future COBOL Standard (after -hopefully -
there are Technical Reports on "FINALIZER" and "OO Classes") is available
out on the web at:

 http://www.cobolportal.com/j4/files/02-0065.doc

A few things that I would say before (or after <G>) you read this list.

1)  Whether or not you do or do not believe in the current "standardization
process" - I find it HARD to believe that the Standard after this one will
be developed in exactly the "same way"  - much less as SLOWLY as the one
that is due out the end of this year.  Whatever you think should/might be
added to this list, PLEASE separate it from whatever you think of the
current process.  (If you don't believe in the current process and/or its
future, then simply don't pay any attention to this list).

2) The description in this list are INCREDIBLY brief.  Some of them refer to
previous J4 documents that may or may not be "easy" to get a hold of.  If
you are "interested" in a topic that you want to see the referenced document
for, contact the J4 chair.  (but please do NOT flood him with "useless"
requests - if you aren't willing to WORK on something)

3) My latest "quick" glance seems to me to point out some items that DID
make it into the 2002 revision.  Just because something is on this list, do
NOT assume that it won't be in this revision.  On the other hand, I haven't
really studied this - so I could be wrong.

4) Just because something is on the CANDIDATE list, please do not think that
the majority of current J4/WG4 members think it is a "good idea" -  much
less that it is "guaranteed" to be in some future Standard. If you think it
would be a GREAT enhancement to COBOL, then it may still be useful for you
to submit input on WHY you want it - and how you would like to see it
"defined".

5) Is there is something on this list, that you think is AWFUL, please feel
free to send your reasons to the J4 Chair at
   Don Schricker [Don.Schricker <at> microfocus.com]
      or
  (preferably) via "normal" J4 paper submissions if you know how to do this.

6) If there is something that is NOT on this list (and not already
identified for a "planned" technical report) that you think really belongs
in "native COBOL" as soon as possible, then send a DETAILED note/paper to
the J4 chair at:
  Don Schricker [Don.Schricker <at> microfocus.com]
      or
  (preferably) via "normal" J4 paper submissions if you know how to do this.

Note: It is "useful" to point to existing implementor extensions for
something that "works" but isn't in the Standard.  On the other hand, you
still need to explain WHY this is useful if you want other J4/WG4 members to
agree with you.

   ****

PLEASE remember

A) that items on this (or any list) will NOT be part of any future Standard
"instantaneously" (i.e. before AT LEAST three-to-five more years - and I
personally think that is pretty "optimistic").

B) If *no* user takes the time/effort to actually SUBMIT "requirements" to
J4 (and/or internationally via WG4 or your national standards body), then
you really SHOULD assume that it will NEVER make it into the Standard.
```
