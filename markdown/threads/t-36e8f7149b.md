Jimmy, there are a number of ways in which things can start at start-up on 
your system (not just via "startup"on the start menu), and yes, you don't 
have to go to each application to enable or disable it.

MS provide a tool called msconfig which, as you might expect, allows you to 
configure your Windows system.

Click Start>Run ...  then type: msconfig

You will see a tabbed display. Click the "startup" tab to see every 
executable which starts when your system does. Untick boxes to prevent them 
from doing so. Some of them are system tasks and you won't know what they do 
or whether they are essential. Don't untick anything you are not sure about.

For more information I would strongly recommend downloading "HijackThis" and 
running it. It explains each of the areas it logs.

Running both Ad Aware and AVG is not a good idea, and you should default to 
one of them (as you are trying to do...). Both of them are good packages. I 
have a number of anti-virus systems which I can activate on demand, but only 
one which runs at startup.

Of the free ones I have found SuperAntivirus to be best, although none of 
them are actually bad (any of them is better than no protection at all).
