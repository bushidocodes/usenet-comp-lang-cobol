Several years ago I participated in my largest client's decision to
select Acucobol over Microfocus as their COBOL.

My client selected Acucobol for several reasons:

1. Their heritage was Data General COBOL (ICOBOL) and Acucobol appeared
to provide mariginally superior compatibility with ICOBOL.

2. At the time they were a very small software shop which nonetheless
supported about 200 independent systems running a variety of hardwares:
PC's, Vaxes, DG Aviions, and various unix boxes. They also needed to
support OS2,Citrix multi-user OS2, Novelle, VMS, DGUX, and various other
unixes. While both COBOLs could provide code for this range of object
computers, Acucobol's interpretive approach was ideal for them because

a. They only needed to maintain one set of object making release
building and archiving trivial,

b. They could do all development (and testing!) on PC's freeing them
from the requirement that the developers have access to all the
hardware/OS combinations,

3. While there was some initial very serious concern about the
performance loss in interpretive COBOL (Acu) versus native code (MF),
the few tests we made (in my client's most important environments)
revealed no significant differences in speed. We attributed this to the
superiority of the Acucobol proprietary Vision file system over the
C-ISAM system in the systems we tested. (File I-O is the biggest bottle
neck in my client's applications.)

4. MF required that the source be in 80 column format and my client's
source was in CRT format (using up to 255 columns). The source
conversion requirement was a big issue at the time.

On the MF side of the ledger:

1. MF did supply a WorkBench which AcuCobol did not. The client's
programmers seemed content with the combination of the "brief" text
editor and DOS utilities .BAT files for development and were not eager
to learn a "foreign" toolset.

2. The MF Animator was then (and may still be) regarded as the ultimate
in debugging tools for COBOL. Again the programmer's found the Acu
debugger to be such a huge improvement over their DG debugger that they
were more than happy to accept it.

Over time the decision to choose Acu over MF appears to have been the
correct one. My client has found Acucobol to be very responsive to tech
support issues as well as very forward thinking: Acu has recently
included GUI (not just Win95) support directly into their COBOL (no
CALLs). They also are producing a WorkBench with an integrated (GUI)
screen painter. Their most recent version of COBOL includes support for
multi-threading which is something I hadn't even imagined possible (or
useful) in COBOL a few years ago.
