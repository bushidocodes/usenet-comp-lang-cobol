You need to find out how CTG is viewed in the particular city that you
plan to work in. Brokers and consulting firms can vary in
respectability from city to city. In one city, they could be on top,
another city they might be shunned.

Plus, times change. People move around. One person might leave a
firm and take 80% of the business with them. In my market, if I'd
have to pick what I thought was the best firm in town in 1980, 1985,
1990, and 1995, I'd come up with 4 different winners for each year.

Find out what the "word" is out on your local streets. Getting a
nationwide endorsement won't be as accurate. Locally owned firms can
be better, because they aren't dictated by the national or regional
offices. But be careful, there's a local firm here that I wouldn't
come near. Again, find out what other IS professionals in your area
think is a good consulting company.

Also, before signing a contract, READ IT VERY CAREFULLY.

If they offer you training, great. But does it come with a price?
Will you have to pay them back if you leave within 1 or 2 years (could
be thousands of dollars). And in the meantime, can they force you to
transfer to another city.

What kind of "no compete" clause is in the contract. Is it just at
the shop they place you at? Or is it for the entire metropolitian
area? Is it 1 year? 2 years? 5 years?

Remember signing a contract is just business. Everything is
negociable, everything written is binding, everything stated verbally
"is a good as the paper it's written on".

I'm not trying to scare you Micheal, just trying to get your
attention, and to get you to keep your eyes open. I've been
contracting for 12yrs now and I do enjoy it.
