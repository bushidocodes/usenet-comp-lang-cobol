### Initialize verb & variable arrays

- **From:** "Russell Styles" <RWSTYLES@COMPUSERVE.COM>
- **Date:** 1999-10-30T00:00:00
- **Newsgroups:** comp.lang.cobol
- **Message-ID:** `<7vfm3e$bva$1@ssauraab-i-1.production.compuserve.com>`

```
    On the compilers that I have tried (MF 3.1 & 3.4 runtime, Netexpress 2.0
& 3.0) the Initialize verb initalizes the entire array, as if the occurs
depending on data name contained the maximum legal value.  Even the odoslide
compiler option does not seem to prevent this.

    When the array is really in WS, this seems to be harmless, except
possibly for the odoslide bit.  But when the array is in allocated memory
only just large enough to hold the occurs depending length of the array, it
blows up.  Badly in 16 bit products.

    Is this behaviour defined in the J4 specs, or is it implementor defined?
Will it change in the future?

    As it is, I have to manually initialize, although move space or
low-values to the group name works well enough.
```

```
We had a long thread on this only recently.  COBOL 85 standard says in
paragraph 6.17.3 paragraph (4), that the data item referenced in the
INITIALIZ statement, or any data item subordinate to it, may not contain
an OCCURS DEPENDING clause.  Sounds like the compiler failed to flag the
syntax error.
```
