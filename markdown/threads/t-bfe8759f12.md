```
If you are thinking of coming (even for a day or two) to EITHER the San Diego
meeting in January (that occurs both the week before and the week after the
COBOL 40th Anniversary Celebration - talked about recently in
comp.lang.cobol),
   OR
the "co-located" J4/WG4 meeting in Newbury England next May,  (or any other
J4 meeting - for that matter) please look at and consider the following note
that I just received from one J4 member.

"Bill,
If you are aware that there will be "drop ins" at the San Diego meeting,
you should advise them to inform the chair and host.   I recall this being
said at an early meeting I attended, but there have not been unannounced
guests in so long that it has not been said in years.  J4 meetings are
"open", but hosts have to be prepared so it's not unreasonable to request
this courtesy.

As you know, the host checks with the hotel to see who has registered for
the meeting.  As a host myself, I know you need people's names to get the
"right" information from the hotel.  Knowing the number of attendees helps
get the refreshment order and seating right.  When I am host, I am prepared
for no more than 2 unannounced guests, based on historical attendance.

I do not think a meeting should be stopped while documents are obtained for
unannounced guests, but I will bring more than the usual 5 copies if I am
notified by the chair a few days ahead that there will be a number of
guests."

If you do need/want to contact the J4 chair (who can give you information on
contacting the host(s) for specific meetings), he can be reached at:

   doncobol <at> mediaone.net
```
