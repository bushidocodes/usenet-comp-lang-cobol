For those of us who grew up here we do tend to take it for granted.

Returning after many years abroad (apart from the regular short visits) it
is like discovering it all again.

There are still occasions when I gasp at how beautiful it is. You come over
a hill or round a corner and have to stop the car...

Most of us are very aware that it is "special" and there is high
environmental awareness here, even (and especially) amongst the rising
generation.

Although LOTR is filmed in both islands (the Shire is 30 minutes from where
I live, in a fertile river valley where NZ's longest river, the Waikato
winds its way from Lake Taupo (in the middle of the North Island) to the
Tasman Sea, emerging just South of Auckland), the most spectacular scenery
is in the South Island. The mountain scenes were shot in the Southern Alps
and the city of Rohan was assembled and completely removed after shooting,
in one of the Southern National Parks. (It was a condition of the permit to
shoot that everything must be left exactly as they found it, and the
contract was honoured. Jackson (being a Kiwi) realised how important this
was, where another Director might not have.The logistics of building and
removing a city (even in set form) in an inaccessible part of the South
Island must have been quite formidable.

Mordor and Mount Doom are in the middle of the North Island (Mount Doom is
based around an actual active volcano) in an area of desert where I did my
military service 35 years ago...the army like this place because it is miles
from habitation and you can fire off high explosive without hurting
anything...<G>)

As for the sheep... we are no longer as reliant on sheep as we were.
Originally, we were simply an outpost of the British Empire, designed to
provide Mother England with ovine products. By the turn of the 20th Century
we were well geared up for this and were shipping frozen lamb (on sailing
ships in the 1880s!), wool, and, later, butter to the Old country. This
worked out well for all concerned, and we simply lived off the wool cheques
up until the 1960s, when Ted Heath shafted us in the name of EuroGreed.
(Personally, I don't blame him for entering Europe; I DO blame him for not
protecting the interests of his traditional allies and standing up to the
French when they demanded an end to imports of NZ lamb and butter. Thatcher
would have done it differently and we would have had time to re-gear our
economy.)

Our standard of living fell from 4th highest in the World to 38th. (today we
are somewhere around 16th, so we're coming back...<G>).

It took many years to recover from this and there is still much bitterness
over the way it was handled, but, in the long run, it was good for us. We
had toget off our arses and find other markets and other products. (Movie
making is just one of these areas of diversity which is starting to pay
off.)

Today we run around 35,000,000 sheep. If that sounds like a lot, remember
that in 1960 the figure was nearer 70,000,000.
(Most of it goes to the Middle East and we have slaughterhouses here that
are Halal and face East, specifically for this market...)
