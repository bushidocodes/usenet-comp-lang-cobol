... And like *all* of Anna Russell's humo(u)r, it's spot-on accurate.

Her other most famous piece is her analysis of "The Ring of the Nibelungs", 
and it's actually a pretty good synopsis of the plot (relating to a 
reference earlier in the parent thread).

Both it and the G&S plot sketch are on "The Anna Russell Album", available 
on CD, itself a compendium of the original vinyl "Anna Russell Sings?" (from 
her New York Town Hall concert in 1952) and "Anna Russell Sings! Again?" 
(ditto 1953).

I had the privilege of hearing her live in the late 1970's or early 1980's, 
but alas, by then her comedic timing was getting a little sluggish (more 
noticeable since I was so familiar with her material).

I don't know the later stuff ("Anna Russell Again?", "Anna Russell takes on 
... Nabucco & the Magic Flute" or "Anna Russell Encore"), all of which is 
also apparently still available.
