```
I think that you should wiegh some current market factors very heavily in your
determination.  Things have changed since 1986!

Today, if you have a pulse yor are hired.  You can get a COBOL job with any
verifiable training. You should be a lot smarter than this though. Don't just
get this job, get the next job.

All programmers increase in value during their first year or so, faster than
any organization will increase your pay. You must move to get what you are
worth, come twelve or eighteen months. Make that move create the maximum
benefit for you and your family by getting a job that does more than solidify
your COBOL knowledge.

Get a COBOL job where you will learnd SQL (DB2/Oracle).

Or get a COBOL job where you will learn Java / Html / Perl.

Or get a COBOL job where you will learn CICS or IMS.

Combined skills are worth much more eighteen month down the road. Don't ask
for, instead, demand training.

It may seem completely impossible to you, but this is not 1986 in one very
important sense. As of right now you are in the driver's seat.

FYI, there are COBOL compilers with full tutorial documentation available on
the net for free to help you re-familiarize your self with the syntax.

Demand is very high. You should be able to pick the one that will leave you
with two or more marketable skills in the near future. Development or
maintenance are both okay for beginners. The key is to get more and more
training. Never quit that strategy. 
More and more training leading to more and more training.

Best Wishes,

Robert Rayhawk
RKRayhawk@aol.com
```
