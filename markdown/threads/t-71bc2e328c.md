### Correction/Clarification on my comment on inline PERFORMs

```
In response to Colin's recent post, I stated that the '85 Standard does
*not* allow the use of the "AFTER" phrase for inline PERFORMs (which is
true) but I indicated that the "only" way to get around this if you want to
PERFORM code that "Varies" 2 or more items is with an out-of-line PERFORM.

There is, in the '85 Standard, another solution to this problem - while
still using inline performs - and that is to "nest" the inline performs,
e.g.

  Perform
     Varying Ind1 from 1 by 1 until Ind1 > 10
            Perform
                 Varying Ind2 from 1 by 1 until Ind2 > 100
                     Move Whatever (Ind1 Ind2) to someplace
            End-Perform
   End-Perform

With the 2002 Standard, the restriction is removed, so this can (with a 2002
conforming compiler) be coded that way or as

  Perform
     Varying Ind1 from 1 by 1 until Ind1 > 10
         After Ind2 from 1 by 1 until Ind2 > 100
                     Move Whatever (Ind1 Ind2) to someplace
   End-Perform
```
