I am somewhat curious about how readers of this newsgroup feel
concerning boolean data items. Micro Focus sent in a comment on the
new standard recommending deleting them (along with lots of other
stuff like exception handling). Boolean items are defined with PIC 1.
For example for 10 boolean characters you would say PIC 1(10). You
can only use boolean operators on them. They are B-AND, B-OR, B-XOR,
and B-NOT. You can specify boolean items in conditional expressions
(you can only test for equality) and in some other statements. They
are fairly restrictive in what they can do. A single boolean
character can be a simple boolean condition (you can say "IF
boolean-charcter THEN ..."). They have no intrinsic numeric value,
but there are intrinsic functions to convert a boolean string to a
numeric value and back again. Their default usage is display, but
USAGE IS BIT can be specified for a bit string.

MF says these are useless and dangerous. That is, you can redefine
stuff and "bit-fiddle" and end up with very non-portable programs. Of
course REDEFINES with any usage does this. Also, MF provides callable
subroutines to allow you to do exactly this. I suppose their
rationale is to lock people to their system only. If the standard has
boolean items the lock disappears.

What boolean items allow you to do is use logic concerning groups of
truth values just as you can do in lots of other languges. With a bit
usage you can also describe structures to interface to other
languages. Obviously, this can reduce portability, but also can
increase it if you are working with a language with a rigid standard
like Ada (not with C, which barely defines what anything is, including
an integer).

What I was wondering is what others thought about this. Obviously I
am a bit biased (clever, no?) and think they are worthwhile. Possibly
because I use lots of langauges and have become quite used to working
with bit strings. I might also add that I worked with one compiler
that had boolean items back in the 70s (at Control Data). They were
display only - no bits. ICL had PIC 1 items in the 60s. The concept
is nothing new.

boolean string.

AND would be ambiguous. OR would be ambiguous. XOR would not. In
order to make sure it is clear that the operators are different than
the regular AND and OR we had to do something, and I think we did
exactly the right thing.

opinions on the matter. While I would like to have seen hexadecimal
literals added to the language, I did not say so in my comment

hand USAGE BIT is 20 years overdue. Bit switches are useful tools
and they were on most of the major files in my previous shop's
billing system. Most of the fourth generation languages that I have
seen recognize them. It is a royal pain to have to use a called
subroutine to access the bit switches.

probably coding dependencies on a hardware family (and sometimes on a

geniuses who came up with little-endian (may they be consigned to the
ame place as the inventors of the IBM OS - now MVS JCL condition code

meanwhile the addition of BINARY-SHORT, BINARY-LONG, BINARY-DOUBLE,
FLOAT-SHORT and FLOAT-LONG to the language is another long awaited
feature.
