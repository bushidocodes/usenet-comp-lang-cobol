Do you have LINE SEQUENTIAL specified (in your Select/Assign - or via
directive)?  Have you tried copying this file to a printer - to see if the
blank lines do appear there?

The "issue" may well be that when you "look at" the file, you aren't seeing
carriage-control/line-feed "characters" that are actually in the file.
Assuming "normal" processing of print-files in MF, you do NOT get a complete
line of "blanks" when you do a WRITE AFTER ADVANCING - rather you get one or
more CR/LF characters.

write after.  Since a write with no before or after
can be either before or after, depending on where you run (before 1 on
pc/unix, who knows on mainframe), do not mix them in the same
program.  We used to be able to get odd effects by writing to the same
physical location more than once, but now that we have
to consider laser printers, that is no longer a usefull technique.

When using after advancing in one WRITE statement and not in all others to
same file, ADVANCING LINES is sometimes defaulted to 0 lines, thus over
writing the displayed line. Which may appear to eliminate blank lines
because the carriage control and line data is being over written.

>MOVE STAR-LINE TO PRINT-LINE.
>WRITE PRINT-LINE.


change to :
WRITE PRINT-LINE AFTER ADVANCING 1 LINE.

>
>
…[3 more quoted lines elided]…
>WRITE PRINT-LINE.

WRITE PRINT-LINE AFTER ADVANCING 1 LINE.

>
