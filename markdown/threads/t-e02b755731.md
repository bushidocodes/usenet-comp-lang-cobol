```
We finally have the required 6 students to teach Cobol again. (yeah!)

It is an elective course here. So, every three or four years we have enough
students to teach it.

It is too late now to change the textbook that I am going to use, but the more
I look at it, the less I like it. I will be using 'Comprehensive Structured
Cobol' by Horn & Gleason. This book seems woefully out of date. I pulled an
old copy of 'Application Programming & File Processing' by Uckan off my shelf
and, frankly, this old book seems more up-to-date than the one that I will
have to use.

What say the pundits here? Did I make a mistake just taking the book that the
Dean of the School of Computing wanted?

Clifford E. Williams
```

```
As a matter I am quite concerned with ( updating my skills )
I was taught COBOL in school in 1984 the book we used
was (Fundamentals of Structured COBOL Programming 4th Carl Feingold)
this book taught 74 standards in 84. When the new standards were well into the
works.
It really made no reference to the upcoming changes of the proposed standards.
I think in that respect, it short changed me. If a person is going to pay
good money for a programming course, I think it is in the school's, and students,
best interest to have the most up-to-date text available.
```
