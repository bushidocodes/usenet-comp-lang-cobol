```
In my opinion, there aren't enough differences to warrant paying for a
trainer to come in.  I expect that would cost several hundred dollars
per programmer who attended.  If your shop were still using OS/VS COBOL, 
for example, it would probably be worthwhile to get a trainer in and 
spend time on the many new features of newer compilers.

Instead, I would suggest applying the acronym RTFM (Read The Friendly
Manual).  Select one or two of your best programmers, someone who likes
to experiment, and provide her or him with some hours to learn how to
use the features you are interested in exploiting.  Let that person or
those people train the rest of you.

This implies getting the product installed, if it isn't already,
selecting the early users, and starting them to "work".  The person or
persons involved will likely appreciate the chance to do on the job
training.

You should easily be able to find the manuals in PDF format on the Web.
Google, as I did, on "IBM Enterprise COBOL z/OS", and the first result
should get you to a page where you can choose the "Library" link on the
left.  Download the Language Reference and Programming Guide manuals.
Using Adobe Acrobat Reader, send the manuals to a laser printer with the
duplex feature (you probably have some around - likely HP's), to get a
printed copy as good as what you'd get by ordering from IBM, for the
cost of the paper and ink.  (You could, of course, print only the pages
that apply to the new features, but I've always preferred having a
complete copy.)
```
