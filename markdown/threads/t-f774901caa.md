I worked with a flowcharter program for COBOL back in the early 70's. I
believe it was originally written (or at least used by) the Navy Department
to run on IBM 360 series computers . I converted the original source to run
on Honeywell 200 series computers to analyze a large number of relatively
undocumented programs that comprised a securities brokerage processing
system. It did a remarkably good job of documenting the logic of the
programs I analyzed with it. It produced printed output on a line-printer
and used patterns of asterisks to "draw" process boxes, diamond-shaped
decision points, etc.. IIRC, it was called FLOBOL. I probably have a copy
of it on a (memento!) MSPLT tape reel. Since that tape hasn't been hung on
a tape drive in over 25 years it is probably now unusable (what *is* the
shelf life of 9-track tape media, anyway?) I also once had a deck of
punched cards containing the converted FLOBOL programs but I recycled that
paper after I bought my first IBM PC and realized that IBM would most likely
not be marketing card reader/punch machines as PC peripheral equipment.
