Bill, if I remember correctly, irrespective of what the license says,
U.S. law allows you to sell property you have purchased on the free
market. And a use license is property. The Supreme Court has ruled
on a number of such use issues, and a prepackaged license agreement
is not the same as a signed contract. For example, no matter what
the license agreement says, you can make up to two legal copies of
software for backup purposes. Many manufacturers tried to forbid the
buyer from making ANY copies, but the Supreme Court squelched that.
