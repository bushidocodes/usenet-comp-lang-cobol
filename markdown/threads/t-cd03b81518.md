If the subject offended you, remember:
"The shortest way between 2 hearts is a smile. "

If you aren't a grumpy old man - why continue? You might be offended, but on
the other hand - it would be nice to see your reaction.

I feel that this newsgroup is for discussing matters related to the fine
computer language: COBOL.  Though it hurts me that students often are
treated like dirt and that is the reason for the title.

If you think about what you do, when you have a problem. You will consult
friends and collegues. No problem in the earth is more important than yours.
They can die from hunger in Africa as long as you get the answer to your
problem. Yours - and mine - network is huge compared to a students network,
so I think that it is important to let them in.

It is important because we owe it to the language. We owe it to the next
generation. What is expierience if nobody can benefit from it?

I think we need a 'greenhouse'-newsgroup if we cannot treat students
properly in this newsgroup. We could go there to help all the young students
with their homework - not by giving programs away, but by giving hints where
to look and what to do. Help them to be able to solve their own problems.

Think about it and let us see your opinion.

I am 53 years old and I am very fond og Cobol. I have been in this business
since 1963 but the next 2 days I'll be away for education (they are still
hoping, aren't they). When I am back, I will look into this thread and count
all the 'Grumpy old Men'.

Trust me: I will respect all views equally.

Regards
Ib

Howard, you have expressed what appears to be the 'conventional wisdom'
around here about helping newbies. :)  In most areas of learning, I would
agree.  But my own experience is that I learned *greatly* from seeing
how other programmers solved problems.  Programming is a mixture between
science, craft and art.  Do we expect writers, for example, to derive,
by themselves, all the principles of good writing?  No, we inundate them
with good writing examples, and explanations of why writing is good or
bad.  When we learn to program, we are learning much more than just 'how
to write code'.  We should be learning 'how to write great code'.  Unless
we expect every newbie programmer to reinvent all the wheels from
scratch, we should be giving them a lot of input on techniques for doing
things.  Every very good programmer I know has at some point spent a lot
of time examining other's code.  If the student is lazy and unmotivated
enough to use the code and fail to assimilate the techniques, they aren't
going very far anyway.  In the final analysis, a good programmer is going
to be motivated to learn all they can.  I believe giving them a well
thought out solution to a problem they are much interested in can be very
beneficial. :-)
