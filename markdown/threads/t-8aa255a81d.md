never has gone away.  Yet, COBOL continues to maintain the strongest
position in business data processing.

The "pundits" have predicted the demise of COBOL for many years.  The
"experts" scoff at the language and declare it to be "bad."  This
"bad, dead" language still maintains its dominance despite the
predictions of the self-appointed industry leaders.

The world's leading Aerospace engineers claim that based upon the

I think we're looking at two major problems:  one is that big booksellers
have to move product and therefore will carry "hot topic" books, as others
stated.

Second, and most importantly, is the ubiquity of the PC and the notion that
certain languages are "PC" or "mainframe" centric... the seeds of which were
sewn with Bill's invention of BASIC for the Altair and then subsequently for
almost ALL home PCs.   Obviously since a growing generation of kids
(including myself) didn't have access to mainframe COBOL, we were left
playing with VIC-20s, Apples, and Commodore 64s (all running Microsoft BASIC
of course)... and then C "came along" to replace ASSEMBLER... wow!  What
power at 4mHz!!!

The upshot is (IMO) that the combination of the availability of PCs to the
population in the 80s (definitely helped along by the growing demand for
better games) and the growing young generation that were weaned on BASIC and
C, fed each other.  I went into college with a PC centric mindset.  It
really wasn't until then that the mainframe world of COBOL and JCL was
opened up to me.  But how else was it supposed to be?

>There *IS* a serious STIGMA to COBOL - what can we do to eliminate
>it????

I'm glad that schools aren't pumping out COBOL programmers.  One of the
reasons I'm not too interested in BASIC and C anymore is that every kid and
his dog has access to these technologies and if kid programmers are the same
as when I was younger, they will probably be major contenders on these
platforms before they're 18!   The fact that the demand for COBOL
programmers exceeds its supply is a great thing... and in the end we win...
even if we lose at the bookstore.

RH
