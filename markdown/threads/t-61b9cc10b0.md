### 2 or 3 things I wonder about: MF and Fujitsu Cobol

- **From:** areymond@csi.com (Alain Reymond)
- **Date:** 1999-03-09T00:00:00
- **Newsgroups:** comp.lang.cobol
- **Message-ID:** `<36e4e186.1086233@news.skynet.be>`

```
Now that MF has bought Intersolv, I hope that they will develop and
put on the market their own ODBC driver for MF-ISAM files.  I have
always been disoriented by the ODBC strategy og MF: Correlate (too
expansive IMHO) then nothing then transfer to Transoft...

I have heard from some MF people that a Linux Object Cobol suite is
under development or at least that they are thinking of developpint
such a suite. We should all write to MF to ask them to port their Unix
suite to Linux. At least, good news.

I talked to some Fujitsu representative to know if they plan to be
able to read MF-ISAM files directly from their own FJ-Cobol or
PowerCobol with a compiling option like they have for C-ISAM files for
example. The answer was : no! I wonder why FJ doesn't want to
introduce such a option which could increase their selling potential.
I think that we are many developpers using MF products under Unix.
These files, residing on a Unix server, can be accessed from Windows
95-98-NT clients easely by mounting disks. The FJ environment for
Win95 is relatively cheap compared to others packages(!) and we could
thus develop some application under Win95 accessing native MF files.
Am I the only one in that situation? If not, I propose that we all
write to FJ to ask them such a possibilty. What can one programmer do?
Many can...

Have a nice day,

Alain Reymond
CEIA
```
