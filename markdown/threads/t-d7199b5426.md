The Institute for the Certification of Computer Professionals (ICCP)

http://www.iccp.org

offers a number of exams. Among them are: The ACP (Associate Computer
Professional) which consists of a core exam and a language speciality, and
a Proficiency exam only in a computer language. Languages include BASIC,
C, C++, COBOL, Pascal, and RPG/400. They also offer the CCP (Certified
Computer Professional) program. See their web site for details.

let's see you what a test to measure one COBOL syntax, logic knowledge, DB2
knowledge, or something that measures how well someone can write code?

I have over the years taken many so called Programming test. These test
measure very little! They give you something to measure but they are a weak
tool. Interview the person more than once, follow-up not only with
references, check out college grades, interview on more than just
programming skills, remember that this person must fit in the team. He
must also have the skills not to impact the teams performance in the wrong
way. COBOL or programming test fail to measure how someone will work, how
that person will fit into the team and how well that person response to
pressure work loads.

Learn good interview skills rather than trying to find a Test!
