I think perhaps the group perspective on this issue might be a little
skewed.

I'm not speaking here as a representative of Fujitsu - Although I do
represent the company on the COBOL Committee.

Fujitsu has shown that they are a company willing to give you current
development tools.  By that, I mean when Windows 2000 came out -
Fujitsu created a compiler compatible.  When XP came out - there were
some problems with 6.1 because of things in XP, not because of any
issue with 6.1 - and an update was released to those persons on
maintenance who had problems.  (6.1a).

Microsoft has decreed the .NET platform as the future development
platform for Windows.  They have updated all their development tools
for .NET.  Fujitsu, keeping current with development tools, has
released a .NET COBOL.  Now will .NET be the future direction of COBOL
for Fujitsu?  If you look at it from the perspective of Fujitsu
keeping tools available that support the latest MS Technology - then
yes, that's the future and they are on the path.

Does that mean the native code versions will die and not be updated? 
Well, will traditional VB die?  As goes .NET and Microsoft, so goes
Fujitsu's compilers for that platform.

What I realize is that Fujitsu is providing tools that allow the COBOL
developer - for nearly the first time - to stay CURRENT and not play
catch up all the time.  This is a good thing.

If suddenly all the highways were converted to a new scheme - where
the lanes were only 4 feet wide, and you needed a new car to drive
them, would you insist that the wider cars be converted or freely
updated for the new roads?  Would you complain against the car
manufacturers who created new cars for these new roads?  That's the
kind of situation faced here by third party tool vendors targeting the
MS platform.

I thank Fujitsu for constantly updating their product line to support
the latest available platforms and development environments.
