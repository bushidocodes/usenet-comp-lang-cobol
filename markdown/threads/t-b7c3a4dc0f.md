Richard and Joe,

Just in case you misread my quote about the "Apple" incident - it was 
not based on what was in the movie. I was aware of the incident from 
another source before seeing the movie. If I hadn't already known, the 
approximate 1 to 2 minute shot in the movie would have been meaningless; 
knowing what was going on I could spot the actress playing Adele - but 
there was no reference to her by name.

Now I thought the Pletzke, husband and wife team wrote this up (they 
were at PARC; they also write about Smalltalk in this same book) - not 
so. Adele herself. so :-

1. She gets her degree in Mathematics from U of Chicago in 1973 then 
joins PARC
2. Credits Alan Kay with being the starter
3. Makes specific reference - "The primary designer of Smalltalk 76 was 
Dan Ingalls"
4. I'm looking for a reference to the guy Joe referenced -  Raskin - he 
comes up in following quote.
5. Note I got my time wrong - Stevie had to wait three hours not one !

Object Oriented Program Languages Peter H.Salus Editor-in-Chief (Vol I)  
McMillan Tech Publishing. From the Section devoted to Smalltalk - 
written by Adele Goldberg as one of the contributors :-

-------------------------------------
3.4.6. 1979 : Steve Jobs, Sun and Standard Microprocessors.

Research is about taking risks. <snip - she's leading to saying what a 
bunch of dumbos the Xerox management were.....>

1979 was a year of ominous events. It was the year Kay did not return. 
He had gone on sabbatical, visiting Information Sciences Institute, USC. 
He left Xerox to join Atari. Eventually he went to Apple and then in 
1979 to Disney.

Steve Jobs then head of the fledgling Apple Corporation, visited PARC. 
He actually visited PARC twice. The first visit was hosted as a courtesy 
to the Xerox Venture Group, which had a significant investment in Apple. 
The visit involved a management demo, orchestrated in the foyer of the 
entrance to PARC, and presented to Jobs, John Couch, and *Jeff Raskin*, 
(who had visited PARC earlier when he was a UC professor and had 
encouraged Jobs to see for himself). ^^Ingalls and Tesler gave the 
demos. Then Jobs came back for a second demo. He came back with the 
entire Lisa programming team. (Lisa was the predecessor office 
workstation to the Macintosh and the competitor to the Xerox Star 
Workstation of the same period). Jobs sat with the Apple programmers in 
a conference room and waited. He waited three hours while I, as current 
LRG manager, argued in vain that a demo for the programming team was an 
inappropriate give-away of Xerox research results. Worse, Jobs insisted 
that I give the demo, knowing I was the group's teacher. My manager at 
the time was head of the PARC Science Center (PARC  was split into two 
while the Corporation sought a new PARC manager who could do a better 
job of handling the disagreements between CSL and most of the rest of 
PARC). My manager ordered me to give the demo, claiming later that that 
he was ordered to do so from corporate. It is doubtful that corporate 
understood enough to be giving any such orders. The plans for Lisa were 
revised."

Then she add a footnote - my ^^ above :-

"Tesler left PARC that year to join Apple, followed by IIngalls a year 
later".
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Straight from the lady's mouth. Doesn't sound much like Hollywood to me. 
Joe what's your source for saying Raskin was the whiz-kid ?

Jimmy, Calgary AB
