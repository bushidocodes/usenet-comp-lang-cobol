The following is the first syntax rule in the draft of the next COBOL
Standard,

"The EXIT statement shall appear in a sentence by itself that shall be the
only sentence in the paragraph."

There may be vendors who have an extension "removing" this restriction, but
I don't recall seeing them.  I would be interested in anyone who knows of an
LRM (Language Reference Manual) that actually DOES support a "variation" of
this rule.

FYI - even in the '85 Standard it was "legal" to have a paragraph header
with NO sentences in that paragraph.  Therefore, any compiler that doesn't
(didn't) support that was non-conforming.

Page IV-35 of the '85 Standard says,
 "A paragraph consists of a paragraph-name followed by a period and a space
and by zero, one, or more successive sentences."

and the "general format" for the Procedure Division "clearly" puts
"sentence" in square brackets - indicating that they are OPTIONAL within a
paragraph.

The answer to your question on HOW to get rid of "GO TO xxxx-EXIT" depends
on which compiler and what release you are using.

If you are using a compiler that supports the (new in the next COBOL
Standard) syntax of
   EXIT PARAGRAPH
      or
   EXIT SECTION

(I know that MERANT already does - but I don't know who else - if anyone
does),

Then replace your
   GO TO xxxx-EXIT
         with
   EXIT PARAGRAPH (or EXIT SECTION - depending on your structure)

Remove the xxxx-EXIT paragraph
Remove the THRU phrase of your PERFORM statement

and voila .... you will have "semantically" identical code.

If your vendor does NOT already support EXIT PARAGRAPH/SECTION, then contact
your vendor thru "normal enhancement request" channels and ask them WHEN
they will support it (and HOPE that it isn't going to wait until they have a
"full" 200x compiler)

Well, actually ....
 There is a SLIGHT ambiguity in the '85 Standard on what happens if you do a
PERFORM procedure-name-1 THRU procedure-name-2
statement and procedure-name-2 is an "empty" paragraph or section.  The '85
Standard has the following rules:

 " c. If procedure-name-2 is specified and it is a paragraph-name , the
return is after the last statement of the paragraph.
  d. If procedure-name-2 is specified and it is a section-name, the return
is after the last statement of the last paragraph in the section."

There are *no* rules on what happens if procedure-name-2 has no statements
in it.  THEREFORE, by the usual rules of the Standard, such behavior is
"implicitly undefined".

By the way, there is also a rule that says,
 "If there are two or more logical paths to the return point, then
procedure-name-2 may be the name of a paragraph consisting of the EXIT
statement, to which all of these paths must lead."

So that is (at least one) case where NOT having the "traditional" EXIT
paragraph/section is nonconforming source code.

what is in the field.
