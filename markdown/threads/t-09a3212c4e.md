If the coffee is Ok, stick with it.

Main thing is, you have seen something else - my first contract (after
working for a small company( was with a BIG company - I was surprised to
find I knew more than most of the people who had been there for ever. Main
tips are:
1. Don't be too much of a smart-ass (this doesn't mean you should back off,
just don't be loud telling them anything they don't know). Different
companies have different training policies - your new colleagues may have
been stuck with 'sit next to Eric for a while' training.
2. Most of the people you work with will be humanoid - turn up for the
department parties AND DON'T TALK ABOUT WORK.
3. Decide what you want to do and go for it. Companies (in the UK at least)
eventually notice that you enjoy the job, whether you are quite good it, and
your continual requests to be transferred to the systems group.
4. If you are just in it because it pays better than delivering the mail,
disregard the above - get in early, leave late (read the papers in between),
Brown-nose as much as possible, don't EVER make a decision and be miserable
the other 30 days of the month!

regards, DaveJ - perpetual 'new boy'.
