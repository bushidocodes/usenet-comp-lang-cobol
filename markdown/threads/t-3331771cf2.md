For more years than I care to remember, I have used MultiEdit
(www.multiedit.com).  I've used it for PC development and I have used
it for AS/400 development by sending the source back and forth with
FTP macros.  (You do lose the change dates off to the right of the
source when you upload).

A few years ago, I finally moved to NetExpress, so I use it's built in
editor on the PC, and I also decided to switch to Code/400 which works
great on 400 code (COBOL and RPG).  It took a little work to get it
setup but it works great when your not connected and then when you are
connected it is still great.  (I work on a notebook that I take with
me to various clients).  Getting Code/400 is tricky.  It used to be
available for download in a 'test' version, but now it comes with a
whole AS/400 set of development tools.

(Code/400 has a nice cross reference feature that brings up a list of
either field or paragraph names.  Then you can click on the list and
you go right to that point in your source code.  For big programs it
is a huge help).

Dave
