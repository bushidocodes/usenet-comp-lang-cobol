A 234 means your buffer is too small, which may be embarrassing depending on
the social situation in which you get a 234. Your calling parameters look ok
to a casual glance, although the naming is a bit confusing (I tend to
translate the 'lp' part to 'by reference' and drop it from the name of the
variable). I think all you need to do is set the length of the receiving
buffers.

    MOVE FUNCTION(LENGTH) REG-lpName to REG-lpcbName
    MOVE FUNCTION(LENGTH) REG-lpClass to REG-lpcbClass

The use of hungarian notation is all well and good, but lpName is translated
as long pointer to Name. If you want to be pedantic (I have no idea what
'pedantic' means but it sounds impressive) then you should declare a pointer
in working-storage called lpName and do a set lpName to address of Name.
However, with COBOL, the 'by reference' clause does this for you.

In effect, by using

    by reference lpName

you are saying 'a pointer to a pointer to a Name' which is one level of
indirection too many.

My opinion, humble or otherwise.
