An entry level position will probably pay close to the same in both
diciplines. You don't really become valuable to an employer until you
have a a couple years experience. The current market for COBOL
programmers is booming so you would find a job easier that way. I am
working in my first professinoal job now and learning more per month than
I
did in a semester at school. My advice to you is to choose the best
overall situation based on company history and what is right for you. The
money will be good no matter which side of the fence you land on, just get
some experience and the $$ will work itself out.

ps- just try not to work for the government.
