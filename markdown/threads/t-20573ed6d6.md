I imagine Core could. Try to e-mail them.

When they don't respond, look at the copyright notices
that are displayed at start up. It is a violation of many
licence agreements to not display a copyright holder's
name, at start up. You will generally find this after
"Portions copyright by:"

Find these companies names on the web to determine
if any sell COBOL or COBOL add-on's. You may have
a winner at this point.

Finally, if you have access to the binary executable, scan
the binary for copyright notices. Do not attempt to
disassemble the binary to find this information! It is a
violation of most licence agreements to do so!

Find these companies names on the web to determine
if any sell COBOL or COBOL add-on's. You may have
a winner at this point.

If none of the companies have anything to do with COBOL,
it probably was not written in COBOL.

If you do find that Tombraider was written in COBOL,
a short note to this newsgroup would be appreciated.

Good Luck!
