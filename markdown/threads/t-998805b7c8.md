There are many ways to do this. Here's an example:

03 WS-PATTERN PIC 9999BB999B9999.
03 WS-PATTERN-X REDEFINES WS-PATTERN PIC X(14).
03 WS-PHONE-NBR PIC 9(10) VALUE 7075956754.

MOVE WS-PHONE-NBR TO WS-PATTERN.
MOVE '(' TO WS-PATTERN-X (1:1).
MOVE ') ' TO WS-PATTERN-X (5:2).
MOVE '-' TO WS-PATTERN-X (10:1).

Result is ===> (707) 595-6754

Cheers,

WOB

Even though this probably is homework, it points up a serious flaw with
COBOL. The editing capabilities have not kept up with other standards. 
For example the date standard requires yyyy-mm-dd where yyyy, mm, and dd
are numeric.  One of the standard ways of expressing phone numbers in
North America (aaa) eee-nnnn and another is aaa-eeee-nnnn where aaa,
eee, and nnnn are numeric.  I suspect there are other places where the
current editing capability falls short.
