I don't know, Bill.  It should be noted that David Sutch changed his name 
by deed poll (I.e. a full legal change) to David Lord Sutch before the 
first time he stood for election to Parliament.  He'd previously had some 
small recording success as "Screaming Lord Sutch" back in the 60s.

At general elections he would stand in direct opposition to the Prime 
Minister of the day in their own constituency, and he would stand in just 
about every by-election.  One government proposed the idea that the 
electoral deposit (every electoral has to pay a deposit to the 
authorities) should be raised to a level that would make it financially 
impossible for candidates such as him.  (Candidates who score below a 
certain percentage of the vote lose their deposit.)  This proposal was 
rejected by Parliament probably because even the major parties have been 
known to lose their deposits.

Another little anecdote was when a major bank foreclosed on him - in 
court, in his defence, he said something like "It's their own fault.  
They knew I was a loony before they lent me the money, what did they 
honestly expect?".  He was a real character and his death was a genuine 
loss to the British political scene.

Some of us like to carry on because a vote for the loonies is either a 
vote for the party that knows how to party, or it's a damning "none of 
the above" vote.  The message may be small, but at least it gets a voice.
