The reason that the Fujitsu compiler is NOT ANSI conforming is that the ANSI
Standard requires that you be able to put EITHER a data-name (system-name or
other COBOL word)  or a literal in the ASSIGN part of *all* Select/Assign
statements.  For "normal" files, Fujitsu is fine and conforms to this -
however, for sort files (files that get associated with an SD), they only
accept ONE of these.  This means that they do NOT fully support all required
ANSI syntax.

NOTE: it is entirely true that the Standard allows the implementor to give
whatever meaning they want to the value in the Assign clause (data-name,
system-name, literal, or whatever) - but the Standard does NOT allow the
vendor to reject one of these two options.

This "issue" comes up constantly - mostly because one of the "beginner COBOL
books" gives an example that dies with the Fujitsu compiler - that comes
with the book.
