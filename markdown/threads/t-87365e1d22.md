It depends upon which PC COBOL you are using. Many of the older PC products
provide complete (or fairly complete) emulations of the IBM mainframe
dialects. This is true for both Micro Focus and Realia COBOLs. IBM's
VisualAge COBOL uses the exact same front end as its current strategic
mainframe COBOL - COBOL for OS/390 and VM. However, they have no support for
OS/VS COBOL, DOS/VS COBOL or even VS COBOL II when running with CMPR2.

Other compilers have some, many, or few of the IBM extensions (from either
the older or newer compilers).

The main differences between the three environments you mention mainly have
to do with the environment. The COBOL syntax and structure is nearly
identical--it's the use of DOS vs CL vs JCL and the availablility on PC's
of direct screen IO (no need for CICS or SHADOW) that _can_ make
cross-platform development difficult. Mainframe-based systems usually have
no limits on program size--I imagine current OS/2 and WinNT-based COBOLS
don't either.
