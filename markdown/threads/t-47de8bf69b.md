A little from column A, a little from column B.  Though it's true that 
bananas will obviously ripen themselves, the fact is that they are 
picked _very_ green for shipment, and shipped in airtight containers. 
They only take a few days to get to your local food depot, with maybe 
another day for your local grocery store to get a delivery from their 
own trucks, or from the distribution company they buy produce from.

At some point in this chain, the banana is often placed in an airtight, 
  temperature-controlled room, which is then flooded with ethylene.  The 
gas encourages bananas to offgas their own ethylene, which is the 
primary ripening agent in bananas.

Back in the old days, your local green grocer would often have to have 
his or her own banana ripening room, as the bananas came green and might 
take weeks to ripen.  They also often came with Central American fauna, 
as well.  Many green grocers that also had the facility to offer bananas 
would have a nice little display of spiders in glass jars on the window 
sill.
