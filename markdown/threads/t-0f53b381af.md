```
To comp.lang.cobol with various CC's

In response to a couple of ServiceWise incidents reporting ERRORS in Fujitsu
V7 COBOL for Windows ANSI/ISO conformance to the '85 (plus amendment)
Standard, the following reply was sent to me:

"Error noted. Due to current architecture of the compiler, a major rewrite
would be necessary to address this and related issues. Unless a strong
business need is identified, a fix will be low priority."

Note:
  This has *NOTHING* to do with their current OO and "miscellaneous" issues
where neither their Windows V7 nor .NET compilers do not meet the 2002
Standard definition (e.g. Local-Storage, Validate, etc).  This is simply an
acceptance that their CURRENT product does not (and architecturally CANNOT)
meet the ANSI/ISO '85 Standard.

The specific problems that these relate to are erroneous WARNING and
ANSI/ISO conformance messages.  However, (to me) this is also a statement of
how much (or little) business need they are CURRENTLY aware of in meeting
ANSI/ISO conformance requirements.

For future reference, I will stop suggesting that comp.lang.cobol
participants use Fujitsu's FLAGSW feature for determining ANSI/ISO
conformance.

If any readers of this note who do support the COBOL Standardization process
and use (or are considering using) the Fujitsu product line *are* interested
in current or future conformance, I suggest you communicate YOUR business
needs to them.
```

has, and actually uses, the ANSI standard document when they need to check
the syntax of a COBOL construct...)
4. Vendors developing COBOL compilers should try and achieve compliance with

are not 100% successful.

It really doesn't matter.
