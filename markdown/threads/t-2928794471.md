Presumably when you add C++ you would like COBOL programs converted
into a nice class hierachy so that you can avoid the necessity of
doing an object design ;-)

Many seem to think that it will be possible to feed a COBOL program
in at one end and obtain a C program out the other. As they know
C it would seem to be a shortcut that avoids learning COBOL or
understanding the COBOL programs.

Unfortuneately it is far more likely that the output will be the
original COBOL program, complete with GO TOs, reimplemented as
a C program by the simple expedient of replacing COBOL verbs
with functiona calls.

It is even likely that PERFORMs are implemented as gotos as C has
no similar usable construct.

For example:

01 Cobol-Value PIC S9(10)V99 COMP-3.
01 Page-Field PIC -(10)9.99 BLANK WHEN ZERO.

MOVE Cobol-Value TO Page-Field

cannot be done directly in C code. It is likely to be converted to

Cob_MOVE_Comp3(Cobol-Value,12,2,Page-Field,..);

I leave other horrors to your imagination.

The C version is likely to be less reliable, less understandable,
less maintainable, and probably would not even compile without
much work.

It is also probable that the results will not be identical to the
original program, and is unlikely to inter-work as the ISAM
formats will be different, record locking may be different, and
even the record layouts may be incompatible.

One large company took one of my systems in COBOL and then
contracted it to be converted to C. After several months
the company that contracted to do the job went belly up with
no visible results. They still run the Cobol version.
