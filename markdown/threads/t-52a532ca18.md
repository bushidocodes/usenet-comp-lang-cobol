On the Mainframe QW and Microfocus COBOL, the syntax and explanation are the
same.
My Mainframe "QW" gives an example like:
REPLACE ==RPL== BY ==EMP==.
01  RPL-RECORD.
    05  RPL-FIELD   PIC X(9).
REPLACE OFF.
Will give result:
01  EMP-RECORD.
    05  EMP-FIELD   PIC X(9).

I typed it in as above and nothing occurred.  When I changed the 01 to
simply "01  RPL ." then it replaced it to "01 EMP ."

I know that at other installations I used the REPLACE successfully.
Can this be an installation issue?

Harold

```
You are misreading the documentation.  The thing which can be "replaced"
*must* be a "text word".  This means that you cannot do "partial word
replacing" - HOWEVER, there are (supported) tricks to get around this.

Try,

  REPLACE ==(RPL)== BY ==EMP==.
   01  (RPL)-RECORD.
       05  (RPL)-FIELD   PIC X(9).
   REPLACE OFF.

and things will work as you want.  (You may use colons instead of parenthesis
also).

If you want "long and excruciating" explanations of what this works with the
"funny" characters and not when you just have a prefix, let me know (probably
by private email) as most of the newsgroup is tired of this particular
"oddity" of the COBOL language.

P.S.  The latest Micro Focus compiler (and the next Standard) support a
"leading" (or "trailing") option to the REPLACE and COPY/REPLACING syntax.
If you want something that will NOT compile on the mainframe (yet) then you
may want to look at that as an alternative.
```
