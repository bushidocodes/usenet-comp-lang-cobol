Hey guys!

Have a little more backbone would ya? Those of you still ranting about it,
you might think about e-mailing Fujitsu AND showing your CURRENT mailing
address.  It turns out that several customers did not inform us about their
current addresses.  Several packages went to the old address.  Even some
had gone out by FedEx and guess what? Even then the address was wrong. The
person(s) simply did not tell us to check their address.  Perhaps we should
have asked them if this was their current address when talking over e-mail
or on the phone. The database is current with information as far as we
know. In fact one of them that posted in this newgroup on this thread had
just moved from one end to the end of the USA coast and had not informed us
of the new address AFTER calling us where his package was. Had he given us
the new address, the address database would have been updated and his
package sent to him as we had done. Well, it got signed by someone at that
OLD office place and now that package is stuck because it got signed.  Some
that were supposed to go airmail did not go out airmail.  Trust us, anyone
that did not receive one and should have will likely have a replacement
sent. Just be sure the CURRENT address exists in our database.

Now on another note, to have us be called liars or to think you'd been
screwed and mentioning it here not only hurts us, but hurts those of you
that are promoting Fujitsu COBOL as the COBOL compiler/runtime of the
future.  This be far from the truth that we would lie on purpose. If you
had been there in the shipping department, you would have seen all the work
that went into getting these 4.2 packages shipped worldwide from San Jose,
California, USA. The people doing the shipments did a darned good job the
best they could with what support they had.  Remember this was a major
release and probably the BIGGEST shipment ever experienced.

Also remember with your maintenance agreements being low-priced as they
are, you have quite a good deal in getting a good compiler with
improvements and the power that comes with it. You have unlimited support
available plus the patches and upgrades through the year. Now can anyone
tell me that there is another company that comes low-priced like this and
yet offers this much? I think not.

Now for those who are still waiting for their 4.2 compilers, I have a
suggestion. Please e-mail a NICE note and give your name (company name
included if it is a company), CURRENT address (include old/new address if
you moved), CURRENT phone number AND your serial number.

Send this to cobol@adtools.com  and use the subject "4.2 not received.
Please confirm shipment address."

Thank you for your cooperation and understanding.

Regards,
Gary Roush
Fujitsu COBOL Support

P.S.  These comments are personal and do not necessarily reflect Fujitsu
statements. Only that I have witnessed all the events of this happening.
This was a MAJOR release and we are going to handle it differently the next
time.
