```
http://www.webpronews.com/microsoft-thinks-the-future-of-information-technology-lies-in-intelligent-technology-2013-03

A long time ago, when the World was all new and sparkly, commercial computer 
systems were used for "data processing". Those of us who worked with them 
used the generic term "EDP" (Electronic Data Processing) to describe what we 
did.

Then technology moved along and the spehere of electronic influence expanded 
to include networks and even telepone systems. A new breed of programmer 
came along and they said they were involved in "IT" (Information 
Technology).

Just as we managed to get our heads around the implications of that, those 
pesky technologists started producing devices where the software and 
hardware were blurred and computing devices no longer knew their place. A 
revolution in networking and communications brought computing to the masses; 
the ways in which people interact with machines became diverse and subtle. 
The days when Man told Computer exactly what to do and the relationship was 
very clear, started to disappear as the Man/Machine relationship underwent a 
paradigm shift.

Now, they are telling us we will interact with an environment rather than a 
device. And we will do it using what comes naturally to us (gesture, voice, 
movement...) The GUI will be replaced by the NUI (Natural User Interface).

This is the advent of "Intelligent Technology" and it is redefining not just 
the "IT" letters, but the whole way the world views computers.

It made me smile to see "Big Data" being recognised as available and 
processable, having spent most of my life working in places where they do 
exactly that.

It's a bit like watching your grandchildren suddenly become aware of what 
you have known all your life.

However, they are bringing fresh new ways of looking at and dealing with it 
and we can bet that the Electronic Data Processing in Corporations in 10 
years time, will be a far cry from how we did it back in the latter half of 
the last century.

So now I work in "Intelligent Technology"...

I must get my busness cards reprinted... :-)

Pete.
```
