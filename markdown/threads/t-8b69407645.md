The following is not a reply to any specific post within this thread but a
reply to the general (original) issues. It comes from a "usually reliable"
source within Micro Focus. I do not stand behind the reply (nor do I
disagree with it) but it does give an MF view.

"
Micro Focus, in North America, maintains its own Support offices. which are
tied together as one operation. The structure has a First Line team, a
Second Line team, and a small group of senior staff engineers. When new
calls come in, they go to the First Line team. The First Line team will
physically create the incident for your issue. If they can find an answer
to your issue within our database, they will give you the answer and close
the incident at that level. If the First Line team cannot provide and
answer, they will designate the incident to be escalated to the Second Line
teams (that escalation is done by a system that looks at the skill and
knowledge needed for the incident and those Support Engineers who are
available that have those skills, and assigns the incident to the best match
available). So, it is normal for you to have to talk to our First Line
team, first, so we can get information on your issue, get your incident
created, and get it routed to the best available Second Line support person.
If you are at the Standard Support level, you access to Second Line support
via the phone is limited (this is covered later in this note).

The Second Line team will then contact you to work through the issue,
working with our developers and others, until we come up with an answer for
the incident. The Second Line team also has the senior staff people, who
are the support owners of a product line, for assistance.

That all said, there are some caveats. Like many support operations, the
services that are available to you are tied to the amount of business that
you do with Micro Focus. Specifically, the amount of maintenance money
spent by you or your company each year. The specific support levels, or
tiers, and what is provided at each level is documented in our on-line
Support Services brochure that can be found on the Micro Focus web site at:
http://www.microfocus.com/Support/nabrochure/

If your reason for contacting Micro Focus support is a question about how
you would do something with the product, you may or may not be able to get
to a Second Line team member to ask that question directly. The lowest
support level, Standard Support, only provides phone support for
Installation and suspected product faults. How-To questions for Standard
Support people must be made through our Web based Micro Focus AnswerLab, a
WebBoard based discussion area where you can post as many product questions
as you would like and get answers from Micro Focus personnel (both Support
and in some cases Development personnel) and from other customers
knowledgeable in the product or product area.

If you are working on an Incident with Support and you enter your incident
number to the automated attendant when you call in, your call is routed
directly to the Support Engineer working on your incident. As usual when
you do that, they can be on another call when you call in, so you may have
to leave a message for them to call you back. We keep track of how long it
takes our people to return calls. The normal response both for new calls and
for callbacks, within 10 minutes for new calls and 20 minutes for callbacks
Obviously, this is counted by when we call you back, not when we may
actually get in touch with you after playing phone tag, waiting for you to
be back at your desk, etc.

We keep striving to meet or exceed our customer's Support needs. We have
made some significant changes this year to improve service to the lower
support plan level customers, including extending down the ability to get to
a Second Line Support Engineer for how-to question support. However, like
all companies, we have to fund our Support operation. This is done through
the annual maintenance contracts on our products. If a customer chooses to
not carry maintenance on their products, then the service that they can
expect will be minimal and strictly limited to electronic services on our
web site. But even there, we are making available a significant amount of
information from our Support operations in the form of articles (the Support
Wellspring) that describe common issues and how to address them, FAQs on
products, sample programs, and white papers on a variety of issues."
