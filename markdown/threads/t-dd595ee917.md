- **From:** "Chloe" <greg@primesoft.co.nz>

- **Message-ID:** `<7ivk7f$fpl$1@newsource.ihug.co.nz>`

```
Overview
I am currently looking at converting COBOL code from Microsoft (aka
Microfocus) COBOL to some compiler that will simplify the conversion to
windows. In a fit of extravagance I purchased Fujitsu Cobol for the task
however the conversion utilities are limited and there is alot of work just
to get the code compiling correctly. The program itself is a modular finance
package marketed primarily to the agricultural sector but very flexible in
it's use. It consists of a Cashbook/GL, Debtors, Creditors, Report
Generator, Payroll, Land, Livestock. There would be close to 400,000 lines
of code in approx 200 programs which have already been converted to be Y2K
compliant and are relatively bug free.
A user group has asked what it would take to make the product windows based
with the view of either funding as means to controlling development or as
equity partners. Based on this I have the following questions for this
newsgroup.

1.    Are there better development alternatives to Jujitsu for the Project?
2.    Is there anyone with experience in such projects interested in doing
freelance coding in COBOL?
3.    Is there anyone interested in providing equity in a company setup for
the project either in funds or time?

dev@sis.bytes.gen.nz
```

similar proposal.  My answer is always the same. I gamble on my own
judgement, not on the judgement of someone that cannot do the job
themselves, and cannot afford to pay to have it done.  I have never heard,
in all that time, of anybody making money as a programmer that way.

In the cases where I have made money, I was approached by someone with a
sound business plan, and enough capital to do the job.

Keep in mind that the marketing will cost at least triple the cost of
writing the software once the software works.  If you cannot afford the
software, then it is a sucker play to write it free, because you will not be
able to afford to sell it eithor.
