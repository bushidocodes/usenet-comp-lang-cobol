Yep!  You and Volker got it faster than I did.  I even printed and 
compared my compiler options to this one.  I missed the difference 
between $ and S.  Grrr...

Anyway, Swiss regional settings in Windows 95/98/NT  - have an S for 
currency, which Fujitsu picks up for the default compiler options.

Interestingly, you can override S with $ by specifying CURRENCY($) as 
a compiler option, or you can change your regional settings for 
currency to something other than a COBOL reserved picture.

You CANNOT, however (at least with V3) override a default CURRENCY($) 
with CURRENCY(S).

Since only Euro, US-Dollar and UK-Pound have a currency symbol and since we
trade with the whole world ;-) we are used to handle the currencies as the
are called. Our printed reports show 'SFr.' or 'USD' or 'EUR' or 'FFr' or....
The line with amounts in US-Dollar is often marked with the '$' sign, but not
as part of the PIC clause, but rather as a literal. We don't care about the
currency symbols in our programs, normally we leave the '$' sign as it is and
never use it. So it's a bug anyhow if the operating system sets or even
accepts an 'S' as currency symbol. Nobody ever uses this character!
