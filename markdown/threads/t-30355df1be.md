Matthias:

At the risk of starting yet another "language wars" I am truly curious
about your statement about "having" to translate the program from
COBOL to some other langauge.

Is it possible for you to divulge **why** you must translate the
program. Does the program no longer work? Did you run out of COBOL
programmers at your company because they all joined Cory and his band
of merry Y2K experts? ;-) (.....sorry, Cory, couldn't resist)

Is the COBOL too easy to maintain and you are looking for a greater
challenge?

One thing that seems a bit curious is that Matthias indicated that the
program could be translated to just about anything other than COBOL.
Fortran is an excellent language for scientific/statistical programs,
etc. and C is an excellent language for writing hardware device
drivers. I wouldn't necessarily lump them into the same pot. Each
one has a set of unique strengths.

One should choose the best language for the job at hand. What is the
purpose of the COBOL program? Is it a financial or accounting program
or is it a program that performs regression analysis on a massive
amount of data?

Matthias, one other thing is that there's an old saying where I come
from....."You get what you pay for" If you want to hire someone to
translate it to something else "for almost nothing" then you will
surely be unhappy with the results. If the current COBOL application
is causing problems, then the new, "improved" program, re-written "for
almost nothing" will cause bigger problems.....if it even runs.

When you pay nothing, then you should expect to get the same in
return.

My 15 year old kid might be willing to translate it to C++ for the fun
of it, but I'm afraid that you wouldn't like the results. He's only
written some tiny, "do-nothing" COBOL programs in a self-study course
and he's only been studying C++ for about 3 months now. He doesn't
have the experience to get the job done quickly, efficiently and
professionally. I have no doubt that some day, he will be a brilliant
programmer, but when that time comes, I'm sure that he won't work for
free.

If you want to get professional results, you need to find a
professional programmer and pay them professional level fees.

Just curious.
