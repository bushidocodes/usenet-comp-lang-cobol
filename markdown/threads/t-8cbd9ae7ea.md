```
The April issue of Software Development magazine has an article on the
growing presence of Cobol within World Wide Web applications. Certain
companies are modifying some of their Cobol code to make it Web-capable.
This allows existing applications to be ported to the Web without
extensive recoding in newer languages, which are less suited to business
programming.

It appears to me that the growing use of Web-enabled Cobol will fuel even
more demand for Cobol programmers. Web developers will be contending with
Y2K and conventional demand for the shrinking pool of experienced Cobol
talent. This could force rates even higher than Y2K by itself could
accomplish. Equally important, it will be a source of continuing
employment for Cobol programmers for many years past 2000.

Not bad for a 魹ｽdead魹ｽ language!

C F Barr 03/11/01997
```
