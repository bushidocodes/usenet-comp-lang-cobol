```
If you are looking for TODAY's day-of-week, just use the ACCEPT from
DAY-OF-WEEK syntax.  If you are trying to figure out the day of the week of
an "arbitrary" date *AND* if you are using a compiler that has intrinsic
functions then:

1) Get the date into YYYYMMDD format (or YYYYDDD format)

2) Convert the date to an integer via FUNCTION INTEGER-of-DATE (or
INTEGER-of-DAY)

3) Divide by 7

4) The remainder will tell you the day of the week.
```

```
Bill is correct.  But if you don't have intrinsic functions, here is
Zeller's Congruence, the method most commonly used to calculate day
of week from a date.  You can download DATES.ZIP from my website below.
It has a number of COBOL date routines, including this one.

                          Zeller's Congruence:

Zeller's Congruence is used to determine the day of the week of any
date in the Gregorian calendar, which was instituted October 15, 1582
(and various later times for various places).  The formula is:

w = (d + (m*2) + int((m+1)*3/5) + y + int(y/4)
        - int(y/100) + int(y/400) + 1) mod 7

Where w   = weekday (0=Sun,1=Mon,2=Tue,3=Wed,4=Thu,5=Fri,6=Sat)
      d   = day of the month
      m   = month of year  (Jan & Feb = 13 & 14 of prev year)
      y   = year (year - 1 if month is Jan or Feb)
      int = integer part (eg: int(3.5) = 3)
      mod = modulus or remainder part (eg: (10 mod 7) = 3)

Example Feb 29, 2000   d = 29, m = 14, y = 1999 (w = 2 Tue)
        Jul 4, 1776    d =  4, m =  7, y = 1776 (w = 4 Thu)
```
