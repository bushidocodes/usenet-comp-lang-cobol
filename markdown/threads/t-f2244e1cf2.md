As an extension to what Tony suggests, as you are talking prices, you
could create an ISAM with "negative keys" :-

	Input :	 Random		ISAM	Key	Price

		20.35			76.50	20.35
		 3.23			89.50	10.50
		10.50			95.74	 4.26
		 4.26			96.77	 3.23

In this example subtract 100 from the price.

It's a technique I'm using all the time. Having done a series of
calculations I write an ISAM with negative keys. As I need to produce
printed reports, by various categories, again descending, and show
within category, the values in descending sequence, I do this using this
'negative key' approach.

You haven't indicated which sort of prices you are talking about -
distrbution or retail. Assuming it was retail, say a supermarket, then
you could design your ISAM to take account of store numbers to produce
prices per store, per department etc.....

Jimmy
