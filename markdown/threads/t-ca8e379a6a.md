Natural and Cobol are similar in so far as they are both English-like
programming languages sharing much the same syntax. I went from Cobol to
Natural (and back again, and again...) with no problem and so should
you. Natural was designed to access the Adabas database from Software AG
in Darmstadt, Germany. There are two flavours of Natural, one and two.
Natural 1 is unstructured code and Natural 2 is structured only in so
far as you are forced to put your data items at the front of the
program. However, I like Natural chiefly because it allows a quicker and
easier way of writing online programs than Cobol/CICS. For the record,
you can do in a few lines with Natural what a 200 line Cobol program can
do. But there again you can do the same in two lines of Ramis. Go for
the Natural job. Don't worry about Amdahl doing away with IBM
compatibility. Even if there are few mainframe jobs using Natural you
can always find work on other boxes (I learned on an IBM mainframe and
now work on a Unix box).

The Amdahl box runs the IBM operating system (VM or OS/390 --
