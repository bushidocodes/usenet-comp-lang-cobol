The article is accurate. Every month, the well-known financial research data
provider where I work moves jobs to Bangalore and Manila. They are mostly
clerical and programming grunt work. Indians are good programmers who willingly
do the dirtiest jobs, such as 24/7 production support, without complaint.
Russians are even better programmers, but they have OPINIONS which they aren't
afraid to voice. Managers don't want to hear opinions, especially those
suggesting Americans are intellectually inferior. 

Years ago, when I lived in Japan, a girlfriend asked me to help her younger
brother with his high-school homework. He was in the equivalent of our eleventh
grade. I was astounded to see him doing differential equations. When I asked
whether he was in an accelerated  program, the answer was no, this was standard
high-school curriculum. I thought to myself, "We're sunk. Some day these people
are gonna bury us." Now that we see it happening, we have only ourselves and our
school systems to blame. 

Fortunately for us COBOL crankers, and getting back on topic, foreigners usually
don't know COBOL. They know C++ and later technologies. As a result, our jobs
are secure, although they'll probably pay less because top management thinks of
'programmer' as a single skill without regard to language. When the tide goes
down, all boats are lowered at the same rate. 

Robert
