Yes it is possible.

The VMSDATE field is a QUADWORD that can be defined as follows:

05 BINARY_DATE PIC 9(11)V9(07) USAGE COMP.

The date can be converted to an ascii value using the RTL (run time
library) call LIB$SYS_ASCTIM .

6. The ACCEPT statement transfers data to dest-item
according to the MOVE statement rules.

7. DATE, DAY, DAY-OF-WEEK, and TIME are not ac-
tual data items. Therefore, the source program must not
describe them.

8. DATE has three two-digit elements. From left to right,
they are as follows:

ï¿½ Year of century

ï¿½ Month of year

ï¿½ Day of month

The ACCEPT statement operates as if DATE were de-
scribed in the program as a six-digit, unsigned elemen-
tary numeric integer data item (PIC 9(6)).

For example, June 3, 1992 is expressed as 920603.

9. DAY has two elements. From left to right, they are as
follows:

ï¿½ Year of century (two digits)

ï¿½ Day of year (three digits)

The ACCEPT statement operates as if DAY were de-
scribed in the program as a five-digit, unsigned elemen-
tary numeric integer data item (PIC 9(5)).

The fifteenth day of 1992 is expressed as 92015.

10. DAY-OF-WEEK is a one-digit item that represents the
day of the week.
The values of DAY-OF-WEEK range from 1 (for
Monday) to 7 (for Sunday).

11. TIME represents elapsed time after midnight, as shown
on a 24-hour clock. It has four two-digit elements. From
left to right, they are as follows:

ï¿½ Hours

ï¿½ Minutes

ï¿½ Seconds

ï¿½ Hundredths of a second

The ACCEPT statement operates as if TIME were
described in the program as an eight-digit, unsigned
elementary numeric integer data item (PIC 9(8)).

The time 6:13 PM is expressed as 18130000. The mini-
mum and maximum values of TIME are 00000000 and
