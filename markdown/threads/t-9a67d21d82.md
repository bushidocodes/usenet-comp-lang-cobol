We are offering our two part class "OS/390 Assembler Workshop" in Denver. Part
1 will be January 25-29, and Part 2 will be February 8-12. This is an excellent
(and rare) opportunity for a) programmers new to Assembler and b) programmers
who once knew Assembler but need a refresher.

As the author and instructor, I am very excited about this class (I know, no
accounting for taste) because I enjoy Assembler and don't get too many
opportunities to teach it. But this class is going to happen. Right now we have
a small enrollment (5), so I am trying to get a few more students (the more
merrier when it comes to Assembler).

In addition to the outstanding student handout (thank you, thank you), students
will receive the latest edition of several IBM pubs in hardcopy format (read:
books, manuals) including the POO (or PoPs if you prefer), the reference
summary ("green card" which is currently a blue booklet), and several others.

If you are interested, check out the details on our web site (contains course
descriptions, objectives, outlines and public course schedule). Call or email
us to enroll. Time is getting close and I have to order those pubs.

A brief synopsis:

This two-week course covers all instructions except those that fall into these
categories: control instructions, floating point, vector, cryptographic. In
other words, the basics. It also covers the Assembler (assuming HLASM, but
pointing various differences as appropriate), debugging (including dump
reading), and basic system interfaces (DCB, OPEN, CLOSE, GET, PUT, SAVE,
RETURN, CALL, LOAD, LINK, DELETE, XCTL, GETMAIN, FREEMAIN, ABEND, WTO, TIME,
and more) and some insight into various coding techniques. Details on our
website at www.trainersfriend.com

I hope no one is offended by this brief commercial message, and I won't promote
this offering again on this newsgroup, because I do appreciate all the insights
and sharing and other benefits I receive from the group. Also this posting will
appear on three other related newsgroups once.
