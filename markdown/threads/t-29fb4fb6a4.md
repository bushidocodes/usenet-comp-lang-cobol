Should it investigate the use of silver nitrate, celluloid, and mention

You COULD outline the process whereby images are captured and appear to 
move, but it wouldn't necessarily be a "poor account" if you didn't. Not 
every book has to be a text book. (Some of us read books for fun, as well as 
information... :-))

Similarly with computers. The kind of person who would be interested in a 
history of computing throughout the 20th century would be someone who has 
probably had some association with it and would like a reference book of 
details, developments, and interesting stories. Explaining the use of binary 
notation in chapter one, can be left to other text books.

The fact is that in this day and age people know what a computer is.

I could see Richard writing something that would be a useful reference 
(maybe with a timeline in the appendix, showing when various machines and 
OSes came into being) and, at the same time, an interesting recreational 
reading book, not a text on the history of computing since the world began.
