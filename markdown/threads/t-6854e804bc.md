... I participate in a mailing-list subscribed to by the alumni/ae of my
Alma Mater.  Topics of discussion range from the mundane and gossip-y ('my
widdle kitty-kat did da kyuuuuutest fing!') to the tuesdane and obscure
('Who was that Indian geometer who came up with that alternate proof for
Euclid I.47 in which...').

Anyhow, recently it was the school's Annual Sporting Event, a
croquet-tourney played against the denizens of The Other School In
Annapolis; this is a time when various alums show back up on campus and
indulge in a bit of maudlin bemoaning.  ('Lo, in My Time we swilled down
swich liquors such as *ten* undergrads could not swill down today... why,
I recall we had slatterns who would...' you get the picture)  Anyhow,
there was a bit of trouble at what the undergrads quaintly refer to as
'Watch The Fogeys Try To Recapture Their Youth' parties; the stereo-system
was not quite up to snuff (or maybe completely dead, I do not know).

This, of course, has caused some Heated Exchanges on the mailing-list...
why, when *I* was an undergrad we had a stereo etc. ... and one fellow, a
more recent graduate who now works at ol' Alma in some capacity or
another, posted the following explanation of De Rerum Natura (often
mistranslated as 'The Nature of Things' but actually closer to 'The Way
Things Are', a book by Lucretius which is required reading by all
students).  My response to his explanation follows... perusing it might
give a bit of a hint as to how much I hold myself back in other fora.

--begin quoted text:

Mr xxxxxx observes and opines:

> There is no need to take up any Alumni collection
> though, and the administration of such moneys would be problematic.  Of
…[14 more quoted lines elided]…
> potato of tagged Alumni donations.  

So, let me get this straight... a 'specifically 'tagged'' donation for
the purchase of stereo equipment to the Waltz Committee, even though it
would wind up in with those you label as 'best suited to utilise it',
would have 'repercussions that would smack of unwanted intervention'?

How very... *intriguing*.  Hypothetically speaking, of course, if it were
possible that there might, perhaps, exist a person so petty, so 'small' as
to take a bit of utterly unsubstantiated-yet-smilingly-cruel delight in
the squabblings of ridiculously self-important College-Kid Committee
Members over the unexpected gift of negligible funds - a sum so small that
even in US Dollars it does not contain a comma! - earmarked for the
purchase of audio equipment which would bring some unexpected delights to
a portion of the Student Body larger than that which composes said
Committees...

... where would this person, so petty, so 'small', send a check and to
whom should it be made out?

--end quoted text


DD
