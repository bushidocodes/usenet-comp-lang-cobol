I would almost agree, however, programmers cost money.  The time it take a
programmer to develop/maintain code requires a very good understanding on
the technical end.  A programmer with a strong understanding of the computer
will debug his/her programs much faster than a person who just knows the
language.

When I went to college, Assembler was a required part of the MIS degree.
While I have not written an Assembler program in quite some time, the
knowledge it brought me makes me a better programmer.

Now, many colleges no longer require assembler.  This is a great disservice.
Anyone can write COBOL, but it takes understanding to efficiently
maintain/debug code.

I wonder how much time will/would be wasted because a programmer didn't
understand the internals well enough; and took 3 times as long to solve the
bug in the program.  (Person-hours add up).


Reminds me of a time when a new programmer could not figure how to read a
packed number.


In the end, a person who can learn COBOL is probably smart enough to learn
accounting (I find the reverse not the same).
