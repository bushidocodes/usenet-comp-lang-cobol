No, I don't intend this as a commercial. As an occasional poster to
this group, I am posting this to notify you that a new book on COBOL
is now at the printer and will be available in mid-August. This is the
3rd edition of the series that began with "COBOL II Power Programmers'
Desk Reference" back in 1991. This is not a book for trainees or for
people wanting to learn COBOL. The book is written for the
professional COBOL programmer who is looking for ways to optimize,
streamline, and use new features. For example, there are no
explanations of COBOL syntax or of purpose of basic verbs.

Topics include use of GLOBAL vs EXTERNAL, nested programs, 24-bit vs
31-bit modules, interfacing with assembler modules, performance tips
for IMS and CICS programs, Language Environment functions, Year2000
intrinsic functions, IBM's Multiple Language Extensions (MLE), and Y2K
conversion tips, and many others. The book also includes information
on the Linkage Editor and all primary compile and runtime options.
Focus of the book is on upgrading skills from VS/COBOL and/or COBOL II
to the newer platform of COBOL/370, COBOL for MVS and COBOL for
OS/390.

ISBN is 1-892559-02-1, title is "COBOL for OS/390 Power Programming"
and is available pre-publication from www.mvs-training.com. Amazon
books will have the book, once inventories are established. Again, the
book will be available by mid-August.

History of book:
version 1: COBOL II Power Programmers' Desk reference -- focused
solely on the upgrade from vs/cobol to cobol ii
version 2: COBOL/370 for Power Programmers -- retained upgrade info,
plus focused on new CALLable and new intrinsic functions.
version 3: a refinement of version 2, adding focus on Y2K issues while
retaining all material from version 2. Covers COBOL II, COBOL/370,
COBOL for MVS and COBOL for OS/390.

Yes, I wrote it. Again, I only wanted to make you aware, as I
sometimes see posts seeking COBOL books.
