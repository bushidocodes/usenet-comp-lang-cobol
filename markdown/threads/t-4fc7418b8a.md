Marty's translation is wrong of course, it does not skip the rest of the 
loop, an additonal if is required, and in fact it is quite a nice example
of how one can slip up in the attempt to avoid the quite reasonable use
of GOTO for an exit. It is of course extremely annoying not to have a
nice syntax for exiting from a loop (something like the exit in Ada).

This is one of those cases where, given that COBOL's syntax is defective
at this point, many people are perfectly happy to use a goto to achieve
the exit in a direct manner, rather than coding it in Marty's style with
an explicit boolean.

Part of the reason for banning gotos is that a lot of people have trouble
understanding when gotos violate structure and when they don't.
