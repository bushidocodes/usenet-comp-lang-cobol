I develop and maintain my own COBOL/CICS application from my home, and am
also interested in making a buck on Y2K work at home. I had an old
version of Realia which ran on a recently demised PS/2 mod 80; I now have a
spanking new PC (Pentium 200 MMX, Windows 95 version B) and would
appreciate input on whether to get the latest Realia (no, no discount for
old users) for $2500 or switch to Microfocus for $5000. I understand that
MF has the elephant's share of the market, but hope that that may be for
reasons more related to corporate use (network integration, multiple
platforms, etc.) than to the relative merits of the development and
maintenance functionality of the products. Thanks for your help.
Dino.

Given your two choices I would definitely say MicroFocus. On the other hand
(yes I used to be an economist) I would suggest IBM VisualAge fo COBOL, you
get the equivalent of MF compiler, toolset, and workbench for about $800. The
COBOL is the same as the mainframe compiler (except for some minor differences
which never effected us) supported by the same people. The manual for the
product is the same manual as for the mainframe product. Also, I would
recommend that you get their CICS product over the "MF CICS option". Even
if you choose MF COBOL and IBM CICS, which I did for many years until our
clients converted to IBM VisualAge COBOL, I can tell you that the MicroFocus
COBOL and IBM CICS people work very well together.

I you want to really feel as though you're using a mainframe on your desktop you
could also get DB2 loaded on your machine.
