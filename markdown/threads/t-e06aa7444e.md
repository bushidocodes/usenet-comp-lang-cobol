In as much as Robot rights are concerned (they are , after all, theoretical
constructs) , Robots should heve been equipped with some basic undelying
fundamentals , in a set of sophistication that Asimov gave to the Solarian
and Spacer Robots in books such as 'The Naked Sun' and 'The Robots of Dawn'.
Here are some examples :
1. A Robot's design should contain assembly parts, that , upon removal , are
so designed as to cause injury to a Human being, thus preventing the Robot
from self disassembly from a Human, as the First Law takes precedence in
such cases.
2. Robot self repair should require tools that no Robot has immediate access
to. This prevents the Robot from undergoing due processes of assuming harm
done to a human being as a result of inability to perform disassembly , and
also as a due consequence of the First Prerogative of dissasembly.
3. A Robot should not have the knowledge required for self dissasembly, as
this will prevent any conflict with the First and Second Prerogatives.

This set of short ' Laws' would have prevented the incident that nearly
caused severe problems with Andrew Martin on his first trip to the library.

Another set of 'Laws' could govern Robotic adherence to the Second Law :
1. Robots should not have to obey orders where irrepairable harm done to
themselves would harm their employer/owner in direct disobedience to the
First Law.
2. Robots should not harm other Robots as a direct form of harm to a Human
owner/employer of the said Robots, which would be a direct violation of the
First Law.

This is fairly comprehensive : Robots should not be able to harm themselves
or fellow Robots, or be able to Irrepairably damage themselves by humans
giving orders of malicious intent.

Comments, anyone ?
