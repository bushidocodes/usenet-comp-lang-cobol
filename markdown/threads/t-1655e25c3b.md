Well, John,
I wouldn't mention it otherwise, as I believe in keeping newsgroups non-commercial.
However, since you asked, may I suggest my book, "Cobol/370 for Power Programmers",
in print by John Wiley Publishers. The book assumes you know Cobol and want to
learn new features of COBOL II, COBOL/370, plus some performance and compatibility
considerations for CICS and IMS applications. Focus is IBM mainframe environment.
Covers all new facilities, intrinsic functions, JCL, all compile and runtime
options, plus debug facilities. Been in print approx 2 years now. It is the
successor to my earlier book, also by Wiley, "COBOL II Power Programmer's
Desk Reference". Books are used by several mainframe training companies. May
be what you're looking for. Just a thought.

Note: IBM renamed cobol/370 to ibm cobol for the 2nd release. Whether or not
that will generate a name change in book is unlikely, as the language didn't change.
