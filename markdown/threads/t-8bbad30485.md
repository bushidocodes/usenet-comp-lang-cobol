You know, just as an aside here, *why* are PC COBOL vendors,
with the notable exceptions of Fujitsu and IBM, so darned
persnickety - as well as expensive? You talk to them, and 
they act wierd. If you are talking less than $100K, they
don't even want to bother with you. Exceptions of course,
because all of the companies have some good people, but
I've had a real go around with RM (Liant), and a minor
tussel with AcuCOBOL. Microfocus is a great company, but
they are tunnel vision set on the Mainframe market, or
at least they were last year. 

For $300 I can pick up a very decent C compiler with tons
of documentation that has no runtime issues. Same with 
Pascal (Delphi) and Basic. The lowest cost COBOL compiler
is Fujitsu, which runs around $500 I think. VA COBOL is 
expensive, but carries no run time usage (to the best of
my knowledge that is...) and is a purely wonderful 
compiler. Fast too. (List is $2K I think, but you can pick 
it up very inexpensively though several IBM programs...) 

So why is it $3 to $4K for AcuCOBOL? Plus runtimes?
