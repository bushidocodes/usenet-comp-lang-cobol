The "S" is not a visible minus sign, it just says your number is a signed
numeric.  But Z says that your number isn't a numeric anymore - a number
gets moved to it and it gets translated to a display only field with those
characteristics.

A "-" displays minus sign, similarly to the way Z displays.  I don't know
whether or not you want PIC --9 or whether you want SIGN IS SEPARATE or
what.  I would look up these options in your text to determine which fits
your needs.

As others have pointed out, the 'S' is used to indicate that a data
item is signed, not to show a visible sign.  There are several ways
you can create a visible sign for fields displayed or printed.  The
sign editing symbols are '+', '-', 'CR' and 'DB'.

The '+' editing character will display '+' for zero and positive
fields and '-' for negative fields.

The '-' editing character will display a space for zero and positive
fields and '-' for negative fields.

Either the '+' or '-' editing characters can be used in three ways:

1. Single character to the left of the data field:
    PIC  +999.999
    PIC  -999.999

2. Single character to the right of the data field:
    PIC  999.999+
    PIC  999.999-

3. As a 'floating zero suppression' editing character:
    PIC  +++9.999
    PIC  ---9.999

The 'CR' and 'DB' editing symbols will display two spaces for zero
and positive fields and 'CR' and 'DB' respectively for negative
fields.  They can only be used to the right of the data field, like
this:
    PIC  999.999CR.
    PIC  999.999DB.

If you desire a space between the rightmost digit and the symbol,
you can interpose the 'insert blank' symbol 'B':
    PIC  999.999BCR.
    PIC  999.999BDB.

You can use only one sign symbol in a picture (the series of zero
suppression sign symbols is counted as one symbol for this purpose).
