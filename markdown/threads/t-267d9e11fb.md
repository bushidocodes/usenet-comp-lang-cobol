Go googling on 'COBOL FAQ', 'COBOL books' etc., but rather than get 
overwhelmed getting a set of hard copies that are repetitive, and you 
don't really need, look for following, via Amazon.com or your local 
tecchie bookshop

  - Thane Hubbell, "Teach Yourself COBOL in 24 Hours".

Some years now since it was written, but still perfectly valid as a 
learning tool - BUT - use it as as a backup to the text or examples your 
instructor will give you.

As need arises you can google for COBOL sites that offer sample 
programs, usually universities or COBOL compiler manufacturer's sites. 
In my opinion the University of Limerick in Eire has a good basic set of 
examples :-

http://www.csis.ul.ie/COBOL/

Don't run before you can walk, and learn to digest the 'appetizers' 
first before you get on to the 'main course' (meat and potatoes); nor 
try and jump ahead of where your instructor is taking you - do your 
research in tandem with what he is currently covering.

"I am a beginner". A Newbie we call you. Totally new to IT and COBOL is 
your first shot, or do you have already a smattering of say Java or one 
of the "C" languages ?

Satisfy my curiosity a bit, because COBOL has gone down the tubes when 
it comes to teaching languages - (1) Why COBOL ?, and (2) Location - 
State/Province ?
