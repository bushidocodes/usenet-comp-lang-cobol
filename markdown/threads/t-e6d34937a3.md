```
From a quick look - I don't see any major problems with your program (and
certainly not with your table-handling).

What I *think* might be happening is that you are processing your "input
record" - not when you really want to.  You try and read the values in your
table - but only AFTER you have hit an "end-of-file" condition.

Normally, I don't "give the answer" away - but try to "hint" at it - but in
this case, I think you are so close, that I am going to make two suggestions
(I think either would work):

Option 1:
   Change
>            READ TEMP-FILE
>                AT END MOVE 'N0 ' TO ARE-THERE-MORE-RECS
>            END-READ
  to
>            READ TEMP-FILE into Working-storage-copy-of-record
>                AT END MOVE 'N0 ' TO ARE-THERE-MORE-RECS
>            END-READ

  and then process the table from working-storage - instead from the FD.

Option 2:
  Again Change

>            READ TEMP-FILE
>                AT END MOVE 'N0 ' TO ARE-THERE-MORE-RECS
>            END-READ
  to
>            READ TEMP-FILE
                   Not At end
            PERFORM VARYING SUB FROM 1 BY 1
                    UNTIL SUB > 10
                        MOVE TEMPERATURE (SUB) TO TEMPERATURE-OUT
                        WRITE PRINT-REC FROM TEMP-OUT-RECORD
            END-PERFORM
>                AT END MOVE 'N0 ' TO ARE-THERE-MORE-RECS
>            END-READ

(In other words, move your PERFORM statement from AFTER the "at end" to
while you still have a "good record" in the FD.)

NOTE:
  I haven't tried to compile and run your program (with or without my
possible changes) so there may be some other problem (missing/extra period
or whatever).

P.S.  You don't tell us what compiler you are using - or how you are
"looking" at your output but you may want to add an
   "organization is record sequential"
statement to BOTH your select/assign statements.

P.P.S.  I am assuming that your input really "looks" like you show.  If your
input actually includes 10 separate records - each with one temperature in
it, then you would have to do a bunch of logic changes.
```
