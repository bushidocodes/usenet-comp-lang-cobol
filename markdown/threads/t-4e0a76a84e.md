```
I can't get Animator to stop at the breakpoint I have marked, instead
of a couple of other places I haven't marked. Am I doing something
wrong, or is this just a "feature" of Animator?

Background:

I'm automating a program which contains three separatedly compiled
sections. One of the subprograms is getting a failure at a certain
point and I'm trying to set a breakpoint just before the statement
that fails.

In the main program, I do Environment, Program, Select, and then select
the subprogram. Then I do a zoom which stops at the top of that
subprogram. I do a Breakpoints, Cancel to get rid of that breakpoint.
(Is there another way I could get inside the subprogram in order to
designate my breakpoint there? It always comes up at the beginning of
the main program, which is reasonable, but it's not where I want to look.)

I use 'find' to locate the statement before which I wish to break,
and then use up-arrow to select the preceding statement.

What Happens:

When I do a zoom to continue, it stops with "breakpoint encountered"
at two other places in that subprogram (about 50 lines down, in a
different paragraph), but never stops at the statement I tried to mark.
It also continues to stop each time it enters the sub-program, in
spite of the Cancel I asked for upon my first entry. If I ask for
Breakpoints, Examine, it displays the statement where I wanted to stop.
When it has encountered one of its "spontaneous" breakpoints and I ask
to Unset, it denies that a breakpoint exists there.

I've removed all temporary files (.idy, .o, .int, etc.) and have
recompiled (cob -x -a .... -B static) to get a clean slate, but I
always end up in the same situation.

Any help would be appreciated. - Victor Odhner Phoenix AZ USA
```

```
animate the main program. Breakpoints are saved to disk.

from the main program, the animator will pop up with the sub program ready to
animate.
```
