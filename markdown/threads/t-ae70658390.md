Pardon the newbie intrusion, please, and feel free to point me to a
FAQ.

I'm a C/Perl/Oracle/Unix geek.

I've been tasked with providing a Unix based environment for our
Mainframe programmers under Solaris. I've installed WorkGroup's
UniSPF (they are used to Wylbur on the MF, an SPF like
editor/environment), MicroFocus Object Cobol 4.1, and SyscSort.

My users are coming to the box via Telnet sessions from Win95 PCs. We
use Attachmate Extra to speak to both the MF (3270) and the Unix box
(Vt420 under attachmate, TERM=dtterm from the Unix point of view).

Using this emulation, the SPF editor is fine. Colors and functions
keys work great. On the other hand, Microfocus (MF) hates it! MF has
it's own terminfo subdirectory hiarchy and I've copied over the dtterm
files to enable it to access dtterm as a terminal, but function keys
and line drawing characters don't work.

MF support says they REALLY like PowerTerm VT220 from Ericsoft, so I
downloaded it to try, and found that while MF is ok (for the most
part), then ISPF spews chunks. I really don't want my COBOL
programmers to have to deal with "Gee: Which terminal program to I
need to execute for this task?"

Does anybody have any experience with the above mentioned programs,
and can recommend a terminal program (or tell me how to setup what I
got?).

Thanks.

I will monitor this group for responses, and post the solution when I
achieve it. If you could also email me any suggestion, it would be
appreciated.

I don't have the answer to your terminal problem, but I do have a question
for you.

What is the target platform for the applications that these programmers are
developing/maintaining the programs? That is, are they "port" applications
that used to run on the mainframe to now run (in production) on UNIX - *or*
are they trying to "offload" development of mainframe applications, i.e..e
doing the development and testing on UNIX but the programs will run (in
production) on the mainframe?

If it is the latter (offloading development), then you are in for MUCH
BIGGER problems than just termdefs. Micro Focus has always (and still)
makes it clear that their offloading tools and products are PC-based - and
that their UNIX tools just are not intended for this. (I know this is well
documented - because I wrote some of the documentation that supports this -
when I worked for MF).

Please, PLEASE, don't try and get your programmers into thinking that UNIX
can be used as an off-loading solution. It can't and all you will be doing
is causing greater and greater frustrations.

If, on the other hand, you really are porting your final productions
applications from the mainframe to UNIX, then you should have fairly good
luck and MF does provide the tools and support that you need.
