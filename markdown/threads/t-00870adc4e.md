-------- Original Message --------
> A Cobol program contains these comments:
> 
…[5 more quoted lines elided]…
> (Assume BILL-MTH = 10) 

The best advice you have received is from Richard.

Check with the Business.

If you can't do that, then point out that the comments are ambiguous and seek
advice from a senior programmer or analyst. 

 BILL-MTH +6/01 = It could mean the first day of the month 6 months from the
month of billing date or it could mean 6 calendar months from the billing date
plus 1 day. (Although you might expect it to be BILL-DTE, rather than BILL-MTH
if that were the case)  BILL-MTH +7/30 = It could mean the 30th day of the
month 7 months after the month of the billling date (if the derived month
actually has at least 30 days in it, as Richard noted), or it could mean 7
calendar months from the billing date, plus 30 days from that date. 

Either way, the comments are too ambiguous for anyone to expect you to amend or
write code based on them. 

Pete.
