At a guess, wouldn't have thought there would be any obvious resource 
for this. So some off the cuff suggestions.

1. Do a google - you will have to be fairly 'clever' on your word 
searches, "internalization", "atlas", "maps", "Spanish", "English", 
"translation" and various combinations of those sort of key words.

2. Not sure where you are located but "fuentetaja' to an 'Anglo' does 
seem to indicate Spanish origin ? So contact as appropriate your Spanish 
or UK/US embassy or consulate - see if they can point you in the right 
direction.

3. Now that the EU embraces some 40 (?) languages with the inclusion of 
the new members from the old Soviet bloc, each document has to be 
translated for each member country. Maybe there's some secretariat in 
the EU that you could quiz, or your local country's EU branch if you are 
a European. (Seriously - you could give the CIA a shot - they aren't 
solely cloak and dagger ! They keep a watching brief on international 
politics/economics, and do have Web topics that you and I can access).

4. Pick and shovel approach - and hardly desired I'm sure. Use your 
translators with a Spanish atlas using the index to create a lookup 
table in English. (I'd be willing to bet this feature exists within the EU).

Your request for info kind of reminds me of something that occurred here 
some years back. Poor old Jose in Portugal was having problems with Net 
  Express. So I wrote to him using Babelfish which did a 'fractured' 
translation into Portuguese. Jose was impressed, thinking at last he had 
found a soul mate. We got nowhere. Subsequently somebody from Brazil 
jumped into help. The Brazilian's final comments about Babelfish, "Oh my 
God. It isn't Portuguese and it isn't English either !"  -:)


Jimmy, Calgary AB

The DMS Software Reengineering toolkit could be used to help do this.
DMS is a tool for making massive, regular changes to source code.
It understands the target language the same way the compiler does.
DMS could be configured to extract all the literal strings from you
code, and put them in a dictionary to be translated; it could
then be used to replace the original strings with the translated strings,
and/or replace the original strings with a dictionary lookup.

See http://www.semanticdesigns.com/Products/DMS/DMSToolkit.html.
DMS is available with an IBM COBOL front end.
