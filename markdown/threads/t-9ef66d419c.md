The hex constant is not standard. LOW-VALUES is standard, but depends
on the program collating sequence. A standard way is to use SYMBOLIC
CONSTANTS in SPECIAL-NAMES equating the alphabet to STANDARD-1. This
is standard and will always be binary zero. Also, it is not clear
what is meant by a "blank field". A content of binary zeros is not
necessarily blank. Perhaps something else is meant.

This sounds as though someone has pressed the EOF key while the cursor
was positioned in that field. In my system (CICS), the field is
returned with LOW-VALUES, and we simply check the field for less than
SPACES. You could code as follows to check for less than or equal to
SPACES:

IF A-FIELD <= SPACES THEN
PERFORM RTN-A
END-IF

You might also check to see if the user pressed the EOF key. I believe
this can be done, but am not sure off the top of my head. Our shop
chooses to implement the former method to check this condition.

Hope this helps.
