I worked for them and they aren't bad. It really depends on the branch
office. The bennies are good although it takes a few years to get eligible
for all of them. You can easily transfer across the country if you are
aggressive enough. One of my cellmates at AT&T got a transfer to San
Francisco after her husband took a job there. Keane paid for the move AND
bumped her salary. The Triad NC area was a really good spot because they
had so many open positions. AT&T/Lucent was a good account although boring
at times. The politics in the company are entertaining which is always a
plus. They sometimes do some really stupid stuff which kept us in stitches
around the laser printer.

In short, Keane is good if you are willing to stand up to them. The pay is
lower than strict contracting but you get some decent bennies and an
occaisonal hand-job. I would work for them again but they have nothing in
this area.

Having just left Keane, after working for them for a year, I can say
that their pay scale is way below market. I was quickly able to find an
equivalent job that paid 20K more ! Overall, they are not bad to work
for, but it depends greatly on the rapport you are able to develop with
your Consulting Manager, or as they're now called 'Service Delivery
Manager'. They have grown tremendously over the past year due to Y2K
and the factories they have set up around the country. But don't hold
your breath for any "paid training" - there is no way they will pull you
off a billable project for 5 days for a class ! Take the class at
night, they will say (if you can find nite training in mainframe skills
like DB2 or CICS), and if you sign an agreement to stay with them for
another year, they will reimburse you.

All things considered, I was let down after a year, but don't have any
regrets. It was just time to move on.
