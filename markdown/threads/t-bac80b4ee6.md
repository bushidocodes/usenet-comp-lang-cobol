Now it is time for... A Story!  Years ago I was watching a TV show about
the siege of Stalingrad; one of the fellows they were interviewing was a
Red Army veteran who was reminiscing about the Good Old Days. ('We khad,
forrr feefteen maan, wan po-tay-to forrr thrrreeee veeks... it vas so
cold dat yourrr peess vould frreeeeze beforrre it khit da ground...
ahhhhh, good old days!') Anyhow, he told a tale about Field Marshall
Zhukov, who had started as a cavalry sergeant in the Czar's army...
Zhukov was being driven from the front back to HQ when he saw two
injured soldiers hitch-hiking back to the hospital tent (funny how they
keep that tent with the big Red Cross right next to the HQ tent, neh?)

Zhukov stopped his driver and asked the soldiers how long they had been
there; they told him an hour, maybe an hour and a half... he said 'Wait
a while longer, it will be worth it'... and had his driver continue down
the road, around the bend, where he then stopped the car.

Every car which drove past was also stopped; when he did not see the
injured soldiers Zhukov ordered the officers out of their vehicle and
sent their drivers on... in a half-hour or so he had quite a crowd
around him, maybe 20 - 30 officers, and the car he stopped contained the
soldiers.  In front of these enlisted men Zhukov then publically reamed
his own officers, demoted each of them two grades in rank and sent them
*back* to the front with the admonition 'YOU DO NOT TREAT MY MEN IN THIS
WAY'.

The reminiscer then said 'The next day this story was over *all* the
armies...' ... and I remember thinking, Sergeant that I was at that time
(weekender, AFRES), 'Hot *damn*... I would march into hell for such a
man.'

The troops eat first, *then* the officers... maybe this is one of the
reasons I never went into Management.
