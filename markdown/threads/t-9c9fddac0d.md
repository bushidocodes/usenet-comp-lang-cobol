```
I'm confused!  I keep hearing all the media reports and propaganda from
various recruitment agencies about how there is a worldwide shortage of
programmers to convert systems to be Y2K compliant.  So, being a COBOL
programmer, I thought I would see about getting a little contract work
converting exisiting systems across.

I have sent my details to dozens and dozens of IT recruitment/placement
companies as well as posting to relevant newsgroups.  So imagine my
amazement when I didn't receive a single offer of work.  The few responses
I did get were to simply inform me that they (whoever) had received my
details and that I had been added to a database for future reference.

I have been looking now for near on 6 months with still no response.  I am
gradually getting the impression that there is NO year 2000 problem and
that the whole issue is just plain media hype.

I have heard quoted that there are...
"3 million COBOL programmers required worldwide [to meet Y2K demand] but
only 40,000 available."
-- what a load of bull dust!!!

Mark Hallett
DHM Consulting
mark@timaru.com
```
