Ditto what Thane says.
1. You don't need to read barcodes. With keyboard wedges, whatever
is on the barcode is presented to the computer as if you typed it in.
2. "Blaster" is but one model of barcode label printer. The cheapest
I've seen retails for $249 (the Eltron Companion). You simply send
the printer ASCII strings telling the printer what barcode you want
(out of, maybe, 20 varieties), the size, rotation, textual matter, quantity
of labels to make, etc. and stand back.
