There are two ways to respond to your question:

1.  Look in your local area to see what types of jobs "seem" to be highly
sort after.
I would suspect (if it's like Australia) to see lots of
* Microsoft jobs like Visual Basic, SQL Server Admin, MS C++ etc
* Internet technologies like Java, Javascript/HTML, ASP and with buzz words
like eCommerce experience.
Look in papers and online web sites and decide for yourself what seems to be
popular.

2.  The second way to answer your question is to say you should pursue
something your interested in.
Even if it doesn't look like there are lots of opportunities for direct
commercial applicatiosn for what you
do.  You will enjoy learning, it won't be as difficult to motivate yourself,
it will most likely still be beneficial
for future employement and it will be fun.

Ray Smith
