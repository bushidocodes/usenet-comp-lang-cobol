Warren,
  Have you actually looked at the Fujitsu NetCOBOL product?

Whatever it is, it MOST CERTAINLY IS NOT anything like their previous
compiler(s) - or anyone else's.  It is missing BUNCHES of ANSI'85 Standard
stuff (at compile-time) and what it "produces" is totally different than
"traditional" object code.  (Obviously, it also include bunches of NEW stuff
as well as what it doesn't support)

So far, no other compiler vendor (that I know of) has indicated a desire
(much less an intention) to provide a ".NET" COBOL compiler - although the
.NET environment does provide "reasonable" tolerance of non-.NET programs.

I believe that Fujitsu is providing a "good" play-toy to get started with
this new environment (for learning purposes).  Furthermore, I think there is
at least a 50-50 chance that this will be an important market (probably less
than 50-50 for COBOL) some time in the future.

But whatever you think of what Fujitsu is doing (or might do in the future),
this is a "new world".

NOTE WELL:
  Warren may have confused ".net" with "supporting internet applications".
The latter is QUITE common and almost all PC compilers DO provide that
capability today. However, that isn't what the book is talking about.
