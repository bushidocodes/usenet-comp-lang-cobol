Over the years I've often wondered just how many people on Earth make a
living out of writing software.

It is almost impossible to come by any kind of credible numbers but a
serious attempt has been made to do so and the results are here:

http://www.infoq.com/news/2014/01/IDC-software-developers

Interesting that the USA has almost double the numbers of the nation in
second place (China).

(Now, if only they could determine how many of these people write GOOD
software... :-))

I remember some claims from about 20 years ago that China was training
millions of COBOL programmers.

I wonder what happened to them?

I think it would be fair to conclude from the figures that the number of
COBOL people would be less than a million (given COBOL's share of the
overall market), and this surprised me. I thought there must be at least a
few million.

Anyway, as a percentage of the around 6.5 billion inhabitants of the planet,
we are indeed a very small number.

The influence of what we do is probably way out of proportion to the number
of people doing it.

Pete.
"I used to write COBOL...now I can do anything."
