You might want to scan thru bit.listserv.ibm-main for a couple or three
days to get a flavor of what the MVS heavyweights are discussing these
days.

MVC = "Multiple Virtual Storage" (I'm pretty certain) It's IBM's premier
OS for the big mainframes (bigger than AS/400's). The other OS's for
mainframes are VM (Virtual Machine) and VSE (might be DOS/VSE, used on
the smaller machines).

JCL = "Job Control Language" - MVS is very batch oriented. One uses JCL
to describe a job, which may contain one or more steps. JCL is submitted
and the job runs independently of your TSO session, CICS regions, etc.,
in its own region. Strictly speaking, production CICS & IMS (I think)
regions are not jobs, they are "started tasks", which have less overhead
than jobs (this is fairly deep into MVS internals). (DOS/)VSE has its
own version of JCL and they are not portable. Assume you've coded a
COBOL program, you need JCL to compile & link edit your source. The JCL
to accomplish this would have at least two steps, COBOL compiler and
linkage editor. As usual, the real world is generaly more complex than
this.

Bill Lynch
