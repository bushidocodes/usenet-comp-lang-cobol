The phrase 
"Abend 0C4 in IGZESMG"
is not consistent with notes to 
the effect
<<
    l 15,496(0,2)     v(igzesmg)
    la 1,3191(0,10)
    balr 14,15
MOVE lh 2,124(0,13) next instruction to execute
>>

If the load half-word (lh) is the next instruction to execute, then the Branch
and Link on Register (balr 14,15) did not work. That means you did not get into
IGZESMG.
So you are not abending 'in' that module.

The module address load instruction
    l 15,496(0,2)     v(igzesmg)
failed. That would be for environmental reasons. Since we are looking at IGX
modules, this is COBOL II, and the environment can be extremely tricky. but the
solution is numbingly simple.

In your test or training environment either
1) systems programming needs to place the COBOL runlibs onto the linklist (if
you are a newbie, don't ask why, just ask if so), or
2) it may be your shop standard to get the COBOL runlib into your execution via
a JOBLIB DD statement, or 3)  it may be your shop standard to get the COBOL
runlib into your execution via a STEPLIB DD statement, in the program execute
step.

If you are a newbie, just ask for an example of a working execution JCL model
(of course, I am assuming this is in batch, just switch all the DD
recommendations  to ALLOC commands if in TSO). 

If an uninformed person has twiddled with the concatenation sequence of the
JOBLIB or STEPLIB statement, do your best to get back to the original
cannonical sequence that systems programming provides. ((look for overrides of
the nth STEPLIB DD in PROC invocations for example)).

The modern COBOLs pay attention to LINKLIST, JOBLIB and STEPLIB concatenation
sequences only temprorarily. They load the intial part of the execution
environment, but are then are on their own for resolution of the vector of
COBOL service routines.

Divide 496 by 4, get 124: The instruction
    l 15,496(0,2)     v(igzesmg)
is trying to get the 124th address off of a vector of addresses (a list of
addresses).
This is really involved.  But really you just need a working example of proper
JCL (or CLIST), and stick to it.

Hope that helps,
