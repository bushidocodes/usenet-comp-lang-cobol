```
Robert Wagner - wherever he is - made some comments on this subject which I
disagreed with, but I have an example of what he may have been talking
about.  It's "E-Week", Sept. 7/09.  It's "34" pages long but that's
misleading, because page 1 & 2 are the outside & inside front covers.  Of
the remaining "32", 12 are full-page ads; 3 are the table of contents.  Of
the "17" left, just 8 are full-page articles.  The major part of the
contents is devoted to Windows Server 2008 R2, MS System Center Virtual
Machines Manager, and Windows 7.  How are the mighty fallen!  E-Week used to
be 70 or 80 pages, with several columnists, content from all over the I/T
universe, and always enough variety to keep up the interest.  I'd say that
this publication doesn't have too long remaining in print in this world.
And it isn't the only one.  I used to have subscriptions to "Computerworld
Canada" and "Computing Canada" and "Infosystems Canada" - all free, all of
wide interest.  Now "I-C" is long gone, "CwC" and "CC" have merged and the
result is much skinnier than either one before was; and it's bi-monthly.  At
least it's still free.

Some of the non-IT magazines to which I subscribe - and all of which I pay
for - are doing well and even expanding.  I wonder if there's a pattern
here?

Good night, Mrs. Calabash - wherever you are.  J.D.


PL
```
