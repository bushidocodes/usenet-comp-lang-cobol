Couple of points:

First, de-editing in '85-vintage COBOL isn't a "basic" feature, it's a Level
2 feature even though it's in the Nucleus module.  Implementors *could*
choose to implement only Nucleus Level 1 (which does *not* include
de-editing) and still be compliant.  While I too would be surprised if IBM
*chose* to implement only Nucleus Level 1, compilers that don't implement
Nucleus Level 2 aren't out of compliance.

Second, what "it" is in your second sentence is ambiguous.  The '85 standard
*presumes* that an edited data item that's being de-edited was produced
through the very PICTURE clause associated with the item, and the results
are undefined if they don't match.  It's the *user's* responsibility to
ensure that the data and the associated PICTURE match, not the compiler's.
The compiler may *try* to de-edit the data from the PICTURE clause, but it's
not out of compliance if it fails when they don't match.
