Welcome to the world of EBCDIC notation.

In a nutshell, what you have here is a +0000984.38

The "H" that you see translates to "C8" in hexidecimal
notation, the C being the sign of the numeric field.  (C is
+, D is -).  The other numbers that you can clearly see
translate to Fx, where x is either 9 or 8 or 4, etc. in
hexidecimal notation.

S9(7)V99 means that you have 7 bytes to the left of the
decimal point and 2 bytes to the right.  The V is an
implied decimal point and the S indicates that the number
is signed (hence the C8).

Not necessarily.  This could be either EBCDIC or ASCII - there is no indication of which 
in this example. It is "zoned decimal" where the last character is a letter to indicate 
the sign.  It started in the days of punch cards, when there was an extra hole punched 
in the last digit position in one of the top two rows of the card to indicate sign, in 
addition to the digit itself being punched in one of the lower ten rows. Since with 
punch cards, two holes also indicated a letter this shows up as a letter in the last 
line.

Although the letter was based on EBCDIC interpretations, the same letters have 
conventionally been used in ASCII representations.
