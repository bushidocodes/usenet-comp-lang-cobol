Bill:

Your statement shows why I fear that COBOL will die.  The functions that
are in the draft standard that are well defined such as the screen
section, true binary, EXIT PERFORM and others have been needed for the
past 10 years.  The fact that the committee can not get increments to
the standard out at least every other year or every third year is a
disgrace to the computing profession.  Most operating environments get
upgraded at least that frequently.  At age 60, I will probably be
retired before these things get into the language.  The reason
non-vendor organizations find it hard to justify belonging to any
standards committee is that there is no payback in terms of improved
function within any reasonable time period.

The first attempt at an addendum took four years so instead of figuring
out how to streamline the process, the committee gave up.  Thus I will
have to wait another 3 to 5 years for all of the functions that I want
NOW and really wanted 10 years ago.  

Unless the venodrs want excuses for not improving COBOL, they should
light a fire under there respective representatives and tell them to get
the uncontroversial parts out NOW or else find another job.  Getting
them out NOW would probably take a year and another 1 - 2 years for the
vendors to implement the functions but that is better than 5 - 6 years
in the future.
