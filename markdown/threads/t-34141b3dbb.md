This comment is telling about one of the things that has happened that
disadvantages COBOL versus other languages.  In the beginning things
were fairly loose in application development, fixing and upgrade (at
least in many shops).  As the field matured more bureaucracy was put
in place and standards were put in place and enforced.  Some shops had
to be dragged kicking and screaming off COBOL 74 because management
wanted NO change.  The rigidity of the change process was part of the
reason for migration off the mainframe and bootleg applications.  Many
shops still have different standards for the change process in the
non-mainframe area.  As the poster below points out, great things can
be done in Java.  Many OO applications are well managed but in many
shops the amount of control differs by platform when it shouldn't.

you need to keep in mind: Change Costs Money.  You have to train the

demonstrate the need for Something Other Than How It Has Always Been,
