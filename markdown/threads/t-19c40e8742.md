```
For those interested in the "history" (and implications) of allowing
non-COBOL characters in COPY REPLACING (and by extension REPLACE) syntax,
you might want to look at the section beginning at:


http://publibz.boulder.ibm.com/cgi-bin/bookmgr_OS390/BOOKS/igy3mg10/4.7.1.5

For those not "familiar" with IBM compiler options, the distinction between
CMPR2 and NOCMPR2 is (sort-of) the difference between running their compiler
in '74 Standard (cmpr2) and '85 Standard (nocmpr2) modes.

The referenced sections includes some of the "migration" issues that
occurred when "new" characters (lower-case letters and colon) were ADDED to
the COBOL character set in the '85 Standard.  IBM's *current* recommendation
is simply,

"You should remove all non-COBOL characters from your source programs and
COPY libraries, and replace them with COBOL characters.
This removal of non-COBOL characters will protect you against new problems
in later releases of Enterprise COBOL. Future releases may assign meaning to
one of these characters (as with the colon) and results might be different."
```
