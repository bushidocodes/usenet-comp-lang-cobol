There were at one time a number of vendors of this type of thing. Now that
you have asked, there will probably be a couple of enterprising souls who
will start into this business (the time is ripe, and all that...).

I did have to take one of these applying for a job at Burlington Northern
Railroad about ten years ago. Unfortunately I cannot tell you for sure the
name of the company, but I believe it is something like 'Fox Publications'.

After taking the test I took the time to find some reviews of the popular
ones. The outside reviews universally said that they were overpriced and
of limited, in any, value.

You could undoubtedly construct your own test from any of the popular COBOL
textbooks, as they all include exercises at the end of each chapter, etc.
Notwithstanding copyright issues this would be much cheaper than the $300
or so they were charging back when I looked at the reviews (and that was
just for the base kit...there was also a $50 or so per use fee).

I took the Wolf test twelve years agos. Its for newbees whose's real
world experience is under two years (just out of college). It's (if
memory serves) about boolean algerbra, basic logic, and a few data
processing problems but nothing specific about COBOL. The test took
three hours IMSR (if memory serves right).

I also took about the same time period the US civil service test for
programmers. One is given a small programming guide, a small
reference manual and programming problems to solve while refering to
the two manuals. It was tough, one had to learn the programming
language "on the fly" while taking the test. And one can't use any
preconceived notions of previous languages or the test will trip you
up; so pay attention to what you have at hand. And one has to have
strong priciples in data processing to absorb the test's language
easily.

Just my two cents worth; adjusted for inflation,
