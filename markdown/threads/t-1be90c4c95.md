First, don't do it like that. Don't move alphanumeric fields to numeric
fields. The main reason for avoiding this practice is you can't guarantee
the content of the alphanumeric field. In your case, what if YEARSTR
contained "Wombat?"

Secondly, you've hit the confusion the compiler faces: It thinks your entire
YEARSTR is a number and forces the last byte to be a 'positive' integer
(zero). The compiler further believes the intervening bytes to be zeros. If
you added 11 to YEAR, you'd get: 1994    11.

If you can't use FUNCTION NUMVAL, then you'll have to decide the size of the
sending field on your own and move it to the appropriate-sized receiving
field.

Alternatively, you can move non-blank bytes (from right to left) to the
receiving field.
