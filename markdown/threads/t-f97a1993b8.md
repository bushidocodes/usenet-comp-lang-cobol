```
This piece of code requires support of "reference modification" by the
compiler

move zero to out-idx
move space to out-field
perform varying in-idx from 1 by 1 until in-idx > 19

  if in-field(in-idx:1) not = space
    add 1 to out-idx
    move infield(in-idx:1) to out-field(out-idx:1)
  end-if
end-perform
```

```
01  DESTINATION PIC X OCCURS 19.
01  I1  PIC 9(9) COMP.
01  I2  PIC 9(9) COMP.
....


MOVE 0 TO I2.
MOVE SPACES TO DESTINATION.
PERFORM VARYING I1 FROM 1 BY 1 UNTIL I1 > 19
  IF ( SOURCE (I1) NOT = SPACE)
    ADD 1 TO I2
    MOVE SOURCE (I1) TO DESTINATION (I2)
  END-IF
END-PERFORM.



And as a bonus, I2 is the length of the moved text.
```

```
Several people have already responded, but to answer your question:
you have to do it a byte at a time. That is, select an input byte,
decide whether the byte belongs in the output field, if so, move the
inspected byte to the appropriate place in the output field.

There is no verb, function, or command native to COBOL that does what
you want, or anything close.
```
