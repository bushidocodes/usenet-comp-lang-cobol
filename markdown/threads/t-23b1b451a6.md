> Is dificult to find developers with acucobol know how?
Acucobol specific know-how is not really an issue: The incredibly flexible
Acucobol compiler has switches that can make the COBOL language itself
compatible with almost any other COBOL.  I have two clients who were able to
compile and execute their ancient DG Icobol COBOL with nominal changes. At
the Acucobol developer conferences I have met other developers who have had
similar experiences migrating from MF, Liant/RM COBOL.
> Its an standard?
It is fully ANSI 85 standard with the later 'function' extensions.  They
also have a lot of significant non-ANSI extension such as multi-treading and
intrinisc GUI controls.  It is never required that you use any of their
extensions.
> Is Acucobol fast to develop?
Yes. Their debugger and their 'Alfred' data tool are wonderful.  I'm just
starting to use their workbench and I like what I see so far.
> Is Acucorp a big company?
No, and that's a good thing!  In almost 10 years of association, I have
never had a unresolved problem with any of their products.  They are very
responsive.

Regards,
Sal Cambareri

Medium size I would say.

> Saludos

      We have an ERP(Municipal Government Accounting) system written
in Acucobol. Also our in-house developed Tax and Billing modules are
written in Acucobol. The system runs on an RS6000(AIX). The back end
is in an Oracle database. The front end UI is character based but
within the next couple of months will be converted to a graphical WEB
based interface. This interface tool has been developed by our vendor
and is not part of the Acucobol development set.
      I think if you look at the number of systems that an Acucobol
program can run on (about 600 I believe), the maturity and stability
of the compiler, the powerful graphical UI, the add on compliment
products like Acu Bench, Web Plug-in, AcuODBC, Acu4GL, AcuServer,
AcuConnect, AcuSQL, Thin Client and Totem, it would be hard to find a
more powerful and robust language and development
environment anywhere. The combination of Acucobol, AIX and Oracle is
virtually bullet proof. The only problems are occasional application
bugs that the vendor takes care of.

Now for the negative:
 1.  Acucobol and its compliment products are expensive.
 2.  I have always thought that Acucorp is a bit to proprietary with 
     its COBOL solution and tools.
 3.  Because Acucobol programs are compiled to bytecode like Java they
     may not run as fast as other vendors.(This issue is being
     addressed with the latest versions of the compiler having the
     ability to compile code on some hardware/OS combinations to 
     native executable)
