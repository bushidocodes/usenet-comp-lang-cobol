```
For those who don't get the "weekly" notification of what's new at
  www.cobolreport.com

the following may be of interest:

"Former Vice President Al Gore to deliver Cobol World 2001 Keynote

Great news - We are thrilled to announce that former Vice President Al Gore
will deliver the keynote address at Cobol World 2001. Mr. Gore gets hundreds
of requests to speak at various engagements and we are proud that he
personally chose to speak at Cobol World 2001. We will be making a public
announcement within the next 3 to 4 weeks.

A word about "Inventing the Internet" thing: Whether you are a Republican,
Democrat, or other Al Gore is one of the most knowledge nationally elected
leaders when it comes to understanding, funding, and promoting information
technology.

No less an authority that Vint Cerf - who is considered the father of the
Internet - states, "The Vice President was among the first of the members of
Congress to recognize the importance of the Internet. optical networks, high
performance computing, that led to programs such as the National Research
and Education Network program, the Next Generation Internet program and so
on." "[H]e'd be quite correct to say, 'I helped create the Internet' -
because of his work to provide an environment of support for research,
technology transfer and ecommerce initiatives so fundamental to the Internet
today.

I think the Vice President is very deserving of credit for his active
support for the Internet and the businesses that depend upon it daily."

Vint Cerf was tapped earlier this year to chair the Internet Corporation for
Assigned Names and Numbers (ICANN) and cleanup the mess left by outgoing
chair Esther Dyson. Cerf is also a MCI WorldCom vice president.
http://www.worldcom.com/about_the_company/cerfs_up/internet_history/q_and_a.
phtml#question_11

(go to www.cobolreport.com for more information on the COBOL World 2001
conference)
```
