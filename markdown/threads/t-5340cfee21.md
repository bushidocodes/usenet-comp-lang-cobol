Just a "funny" on this topic.

When I first went to work for a PC COBOL compiler vendor (whose name, Micro 
Focus, shall remain named <G>), I was primarily responsible for "IBM mainframe 
compatibility".  On the first day that I went to work there, I asked if they had 
access to an IBM mainframe with a COBOL compiler and I was told that they did. 
I then asked what operating system they had, and was given the answer:

  PROFS

This answer should be "widely humorous" to IBM mainframers of the 1980's and 
90's and totally incomprehensible to those who didn't work in that environment 
(and who don't search the internet for the reference).

As has been indicated in the other thread, "computer literacy" is a term with 
multiple (situation dependent) meanings.

No, they were not pulling my leg.  Almost none of the workforce used the 
"mainframe" for anything BUT email.  (They didn't even use the PROFS "document" 
feature with SCRIPT for documentation - until I showed them how).

And, yes, the only IBM mainframe compiler they had was under VM/CMS.  I had to 
"refresh" my XEDIT (not to be confused with the Unix/Linux Xedit) knowledge.  I 
also had to install an "add on" SORT and VSAM support to be able to do "basic" 
mainframe compatibility testing.  We even had the "short-lived" CICS/CMS at one 
time, but mostly I did IMS and CICS testing via "time-share".

Later on (before MERANT days), then did get an in-house MVS system.

P.S. "PROFS" made it into (US) national news around the Watergate period of 
time.  There were several White House executives who claimed to be VERY 
surprised that when they "hit send" on email, that it didn't magically disappear 
as soon as someone read it, i.e. that there were backups of all their 
"inappropriate" email notes.  (This was relatively early in the days of "common 
emails for businesses)  Having been a systems programmer at various times, this 
always amused me as I am certain that these were the same people who EXPECTED 
"IT" to be able to "restore" anything that they ever deleted.

There are a few "interesting" news stories on "missing" email from a later 
(still Republican) White House where email backups WERE lost in the 2003-2005 
period of time.  The *claim* was that this was due to a LOTUS (follow-on product 
to PROFS - sort-of) email system to a Microsoft Outlook (or exchange?) system.
