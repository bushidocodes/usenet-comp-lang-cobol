Here's the drill, John. You can contract with :Dave, Infomagic, Shmuel,
my pal Rex  "the software archeologist" ... there are about a half dozen
other tuff-guys that I'm aware of on c.s.y2k,  please don't bother 
asking for a guarentee, and don't dicker on price, we don't have time to
discuss price.

For a job like this, you really want a tuff-guy.  If there's a lot of 
source, you want a team of pro's.  You don't want to dance with one of 
the low cost Y2K remediation companies.

Here're a few questions for you:

1) Do you have a working compiler and development environment?

2) Are we talking batch COBOL or something extra like IMS, CICS, etc?

3) In general what does the program/system do?

4) What operating system and what level?

5) Can we telnet in?

6) Anyone around who worked on the source?

7) Do you want us to Y2K the code too or is producing the source 
adequate?

8) How much time do we have?  I'm guessing that this is a Y2K job.  If 
so, is December 1998 too late?

9) Is Assembler source acceptable or are you stuck on COBOL?

10) If you're stuck on COBOL, does the job include rolling the source
ahead to a current compiler/environment?

Any of us can do the job.  Depending on the amount of source and our 
schedule, we may decline to take the assignment.

hmm. i would go for C, instead of assembler or COBOL.

the code will not be as fast as assembler. but you
will be able to fix the problem within a time frame
at a cheaper price.

the thing with C is, if the machine has a stack, you
have to make utterly sure that you have access
to the push and pop commands in the compiler. another
thing to watch for is re-entrancy.

most machines already have decompilers that creates C
code. its not a good decompiler, but the hard part will
have to be done by human hands anyways.

and teaching alot of people a way to write assembler for
