```
1) COBOL questions are often answered more quickly and accurately in the
comp.lang.cobol newsgroup - which I am adding to this list.  (And then again,
CICS question may need to go to the CICS list-server).

2) "COBOL3" is a CICS translator option - it has MINIMAL relationship to any
IBM product called (correctly or incorrectly "COBOL3"). It is ABSOLUTELY the
correct term to use - when talking about a translator option.

3) The response that pointed to APAR PN70904 *does* explain some of the
enhanced features (from the newer IBM COBOL compilers) that may be used in
CICS programs - when this translator option is in effect.

4) I suggest that you look at your CICS Programming Guide and/or look at

 http://www.s390.ibm.com/bookmgr-cgi/bookmgr.cmd/BOOKS/DFHJAP33/1%2e2%2e4

This talks (briefly) about the COBOL2, COBOL3, ANSI85, and OOCOBOL translator
options - and points to other references - if you need them.

5) As far as the compilers themselves go:

  IBM SAA AD/Cycle COBOL/370 - is already out of service
  VS COBOL II - before release 4.0 - is already out of service
  VS COBOL II, R4 - will go out of service at the end of March 2001
  IBM COBOL for MVS & VM
     and
  IBM COBOL for OS/390 & VM
   (either with MLE extensions)
      are both supported and have no announced drop dates.
  This support (or non-support) for each of these compilers apply to both
BATCH and CICS (and IMS/DC and DB2 and whatever).

"OBJECT CODE" compiled with earlier compilers will continue to be supported
after March 2001 - but only if linked and run with the LE run-time/link-edit
libraries.

NOTE: Some differences exist if you are talking about COBOL and CICS under
VSE - but I don't think that is your specific concern.

6) Co-existence of various "flavors" of COBOL compiled programs in the same
CICS "unit of work" is a non-trivial topic.  It is discussed in the current
COBOL migration guides. The easy answer (from your original question) is that
VS COBOL II and "COBOL for this-and-that" programs MAY <normally> co-exist
within the same CICS unit-of-work.

  ***
I hope this helps
```
