Hehe. I still remember having to key in my COBOL programs off a form
onto cards in my early college days around 1982 (the computer was an
IBM 1401, I think). The blasted card-punch machine always jamed. And
you always had to stand in line or select odd hours just to use the
machine. So one had to be *real* good in program desk-checking on
paper before card-punching and become a trained touch-typist to
minimize card-punching time.

Wylbur, terminals and no waiting in lines. Transfered there the
following year. I still touch-type.
