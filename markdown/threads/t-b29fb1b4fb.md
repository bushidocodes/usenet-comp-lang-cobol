Don't be mislead by advertised rates. I don't know how many times I've seen
an ad that says something like this:

COBOL, IMS, VSAM, Y2K project yadda yadda yadda RATE: $35-$65/hr

Call up the recruiter. Conversation goes something like this:

ME: I'm calling about the COBOL job that pays $65/hr, could you tel...

R: Oh, man, you know COBOL ? We've got to have you right now. No, yesterday
would be better. When can you start.

ME: Does this job actually pay $65/hr?

R: Oh, um, well, you see, uh, what I can do is submit you to the client for
that rate and we'll go from there. Hey, how about $35? I know we could get
you that right now and this job is in an exotic tropical city and includes
your own personal gorgeous blond secretary and yadda yadda yadda...


Anybody else experience this or is it just me?

-George

This is referred to by the technical name of 'Bait 'n Switch'... it is
right up there with advertising an outrageous day-rate ($20,000/DAY!!!)
and then saying 'Oh, to get *that* rate you need to have ghost-written
von Neuman's papers... but I have a *lovely* position at $25/hr, do you
have any Visual BASIC?'

I usually sit through the sales-pitch very quietly and then say 'Oh,
$35/hr? That's *very* nice, I hope they are happy with whomever they
get at that rate... it ain't gonna be me'... and then I home back in on
the high end.

In response to the usually disappointed 'Ummmm... $65/hr? Are you
flexible on that?' I invariably respond -

'Of *course* I'm flexible... I'm always willing to go higher.'

your service the way the client would pay for any other service. You're
