```
I think that Mr. Webb and many of the persons responding to him are
missing the issue here.

Job listings *should* be posted to the xx.jobs newsgroups or
alt.computer.consultants.ads, and not to a technical newsgroup such as
comp.lang.cobol.  Let the issue of disclosing rates be an issue in
*those* newsgroups, and not in this one !  Let this newsgroup be a forum
for sharing ideas, experiences, and techniques about Cobol programming,
differences in Cobol implementations, tools for effective business data
processing solutions, etc.

I, for one, don't mind if students ask questions, as long as it shows
they have done *some* research on their own before posting.  I enjoy
reading threads about organizational problems in corporations, whether
they be written humorously or sarcastically.  This is human interaction,
the same as if we were speaking to each other face-to-face.

DD and others - maybe a more effective approach would be to harass the
job posters about posting to this newsgroup at all - rather than asking
them to post a second time with rates.  

Food for thought - Regards to all

Bob Berman
```

```
I view (rightly or wrongly, of course) this group to be a sort of
'programmer's playground'... but even when programmers play there's an
air of Work to it.  (Perhaps that is why I enjoy my trade... when it
gets *really* good the differentiation between Work and Play
disintergrates)  We joke, we kid, we lambast the newbies and call each
other poopie-heads... all in good fun, of course.

A pimp is always welcome, in my book, to ask if someone is interested in
working... but in 'our' playground this should be according to 'our'
rules.  Yes, we enjoy coding... but one needs a job, at times, and
finding a job needs a pimp... at times.  Dealings with pimps can be...
distasteful, to say the least (Mr Webb's recent postings being an
example... he's not even a pimp so he does it *exquisitely* badly) and
so should be kept as businesslike as possible... location of slot,
duration of slot, type of work and rate.  Anything else ('We're the nth
largest stable in the Universe, growing *really* fast and are known for
our proactive people-oriented...
something-or-other-buzzword-of-the-week' is something I have yet to find
anyone who needs).

Did this make any sense?  If so answer 'maybe'.

DD
```
