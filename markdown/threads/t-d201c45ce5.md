```
if March 1 was the first day of the year.  Before using them, check the
month for jan. or feb., and if found, decrement the year by one. Convert the
month so that march is month 1, december month 10, jan 11, and feb is 12

number of leap year days since your base date is the revised year divided by
4. The day integer is (until 2400) (CURRENT-YEAR - BASE YEAR)/4
```

```
We don't have the integer-of-date function in our shop, but have copybooks
to do the following:

We assume Jan 1, 1901 is day zero and do offsets from that point.  First
subtract 1901 from the year.  The "days" part is equal to the (julian
days -1) plus years * 365.  Add (year - 1901) / 4 to get the count of prior
leap year days.  I'll leave the computation of julian days (day within a
year) and the checking for a Feb 29 before the date you are working on as an
exercise.

To find the number of days between dates, run each date through the above
logic and subtract.

To convert days back into a date,  first divide by 1461 (number of days in 4
years) and be sure to save the remainder.  After checking for specific
values which represent Feb 29 and Dec 31 of a leap year, divide by 365 and
you will be able to determine the year while converting the (julian days -
1) will give the month and day.

This example will work through 2099.  Correcting this logic to handle
century years not divisible by 400 would add a few more levels of complexity
to the process, starting by dividing by (365 * 400 - 3).  A new pivot date
(1/1/1701) would be needed for both parts.
```
