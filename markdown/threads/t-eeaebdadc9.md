Please excuse the brief OT discussion below. I don't want to start a
religious debate, only to clarify a very common misconception. What
Pete has written above makes an excellent point on what is really meant
by the expression "An eye for an eye, a tooth for a tooth" in the Bible.
This is probably the most misunderstood and wrongly applied verse in
the Bible. It's easy to verify by looking them up in a concordance and
reading the context, that in every single use, this verse applies entirely
to judicial punishment, and never once does it apply to personal revenge.
In other words, don't execute a person for stealing bread, and don't slap
them on the wrist for murder; the punishment should be in proportion to
the crime itself. It is another way of saying "let the punishment meet the
crime," a foundation principle of all modern Western law. Had it been
applied, the atrocities Peter lists above would not have occurred.
