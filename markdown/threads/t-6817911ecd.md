All righty... actually you don't need *all* the division-level entries, 
just the 18 or so that would fill the detail-line area of your screen (24 
lines per screen minus three lines for header, two lines for footer, one 
line for error-msgs and maybe a couple of blank lines 
here-and-there for 'eye-ease').  Assuming a full 18 lines, though, this 
can be accomplished in a maximum of 54 I-O operations.

Your program is passed *something*, I hope... a company, company and store 
or company, store and division.  Whatever you get you move to your RIDFLD 
and do a START NOT < and a READ NEXT; the least you will get will be a 
company record.  If you get a company record (blank store and division) 
you do another READ NEXT and you have a division record.  These are the 
three I-Os you'll have to do to get each first division record for a given 
store.

Anyhow, after you have the first one how do you get to the next?  
Ahhhhh... take the last character of the division field that your read 
returned (reference-modification makes this a trivial task) and MOVE it to 
the second position of a two-character alpha field which you have 
REDEFINED as a binary field... my memory, admittedly porous, has it as 
something like

01  WS-FLDS.
    05  WS-CHARFLD.
        10  WS-CHAR1 PIC X.
        10  WS-CHAR2 PIC X.
    05  BINFLD REDEFINES WS-CHARFLD PIC 9(4) COMP.

... or something like that, so your code becomes

MOVE DIVISIONFLD(LENGTH OF DIVISIONFLD:1) TO WS-CHAR2.
ADD 1 TO BINFLD.
MOVE WS-CHAR-2 TO DIVISIONFLD(LENGTH OF DIVISIONFLD:1).

... and then you do your I-Os again, START NOT <, READ NEXT (and check if 
a new company rec is there, if so another READ NEXT).

(using a binary field to increment an alpha is an Anciente, Dirtye 
Techniquee... and someone is bound to ask where you learned it from 
because *nobody* uses such things any more)

Alexa, the real solution to this is to move the VSAM rubbish onto a
Relational Database which supports Company, Division, and Store Objects.
Your Users can then organise and analyse this information in any of the
possible ways they might want to....and they can call off their own Queries
and Reports.

However, that is not going to be practical in the circumstances you
describe, so your best solution is to define an Alternate Index on the VSAM
KSDS. You do this with IDCAMS. Simply set an AI that has the
Company>Store>Division sequence. If you are unfamiliar with it, seek advice
from someone who knows it, and learn something at the same time...

The impacts are the setting up of the new index, the loading of it, and the
amendment of JCL for existing jobs that reference the Dataset, where/if
required. There is a performance hit, as the new index must be updated
whenever the dataset is updated. If updating occurs on-line this hit will
not be noticeable; if it occurs in Batch, the overnight run may be
prolonged.

  Read the input file sequentially, RELEASE the records to a SORT, and

 01 FILLER
