Cheesle wrote:
>
>I still mean that we that are veterans could be better to show indulgence
>with newbies. Note that I am saying: we.

I agree.  I often wish we (collectively) were a bit less hasty to dump
on some of the naive questions we get, particularly from students.  I
don't care if somebody wants an answer to a COBOL related question
because they need to write a COBOL program for work or school, or if
they simply need the information for a research project of some kind,
or or simply curious.  All are learning.

In my first programming class, I was the very last person to 'see the
light'.  For several weeks I went through the motions, but I didn't
understand what I was doing, or why.  Finally, it dawned on me that I
was making it much too complex, and that the computer was much dumber
than I had thought.  I had seen too many 'computers' in movies and on
TV, I suppose. :-)  But once the light dawned, from then on, it was
like eating candy.

Some of the beginner questions we get remind me of myself at first.
They have been given a lot of information, but they have not yet been
able to assimilate it into a coherent 'vision' of what it is a program
actually does, and how the programming process works, or perhaps just
some aspect of the language.  That leap of perception may be a tiny
one for most, but for me it was a big one.  But no one since has ever
suggested that I was a less capable programmer because of it.  I would
like to see us be more willing to respond to those 'clueless' beginner
questions.  I think some of those 'do your own homework' questions are
because the person really doesn't know where to begin. :-)
