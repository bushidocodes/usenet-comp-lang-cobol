I do NOT think that anyone thinks that the type of code in that examples is
the reason that the restrictions on A-/B-margin were removed.  HOWEVER, what
the Standards committees did think (as I recall) is that there is ABSOLUTELY
no place in Standard COBOL that you "need" to enforce A-/B-margin rules.
Furthermore, as there are many COBOL implementations (not IBM) that have not
"enforced" them for years - and their programmers have not felt this caused
any problems,
 therefore, it was felt that this restriction SHOULD be removed from the
Standard.

There is NO reason whatsoever that programmers can't still code the "old"
way - it is simply that they won't be FORCED to.

Interestingly enough, I wrote a paper on this very topic requesting J4 (the
ANSI-ish COBOL committee) to at least allow for an OPTION to require
paragraph and section names to be the first thing on the line (to avoid
paragraph names in the middle of a line of source code - as in that example).
The committees rejected this paper.

If you think that it would be DESIRABLE to have such an option, then please
look at my paper (in MS Word format) at:
  http://www.ncits.org/tc_home/j4htm/m225/00-0391.doc

and send your comments (not to me) but to
   doncobol <at> mediaone.net

asking for them to be submitted to J4 as an "input document".

*ALTERNATIVELY* (or in addition),
  Submit a SHARE requirement (or support REQUEST) that IBM provide such an
option - even if the next COBOL Standard doesn't require it.
