```
Just laid hands on another copy of "Systemantics: How Systems Work and
Especially How They Fail," which, when I first read it many years ago
encouraged me to become an asparagus farmer.

In summary, here are the "Rules" (like for dating) thoroughly explained in
this slim tome:

1. Systems in General work poorly or not at all.
   a. Nothing complicated works.
   b. Complicated systems seldom exceed 5% efficiency.
2. New systems generate new problems.
   a. Systems should not be unnecessarily multiplied.
3. The total amount of anergy (the amount of human energy required to reach
a goal) in the universe is constant.
   a. Systems operate by redistributing anergy into different forms.
4. Systems tend to grow, and as they grow, they encroach.
   a. Systems tend to expand to fill the known universe.
   b. The system itself tends to expand at 5-6% per year.
5. Systems display antics.
   a. Complicated systems produce unexpected outcomes.
   b. The behavior of a complicated system cannot be predicted.
   c. A large system, produced by expanding a smaller system, does not
behave like the smaller system.
6. Complex systems tend to oppose their own proper function.
  a. Systems get in the way.
   b. The system always kicks back.
   c. Positive feedback is dangerous.
7. People in systems do not do what the system says they are doing.
8. The system itself does not do what it says it is doing.
   a. The function performed by a system is not operationally identical to
function of the same name performed by a human.
9. Fundamental Law of Administrative Working (FLAW):
   a. Things ARE what they are reported to be.
   b. The real world is what is reported to the system.
   c. A system is no better than its sensory organs
   d. To those within a system, outside reality tends to pale and disappear.
10. Systems attract systems people.
11. The bigger the system, the narrower and more specialized the interface
with individuals.
12. A complex system cannot be MADE to work. It either works or it doesn't.

   a. Pushing on the system doesn't help. It just makes things worse.
13. A simple system, designed from scratch, sometimes works.
   a. A simple system may or may not work.
14. Some complex systems actually work.
   a. If a system is working, leave it alone.
15. A complex system that works is invariably found to have evolved from a
simple system that worked.
16. A complex system designed from scratch never works and cannot be patched
up to make it work. You have to start over with a working simple system.
   a. Programs never run the first time. Complex programs never run.
17. Functional Indeterminacy Theorem (FIT): In complex systems, malfunction
and even total nonfunction may not be detectable for long periods, if ever.
18. Large complex systems are beyond the human capacity to evaluate.
19. (Newton's law): A system that performs a certain function or operates in
a certain way will continue to operate in that way regardless of the need or
of changed conditions.
   a. A system continues to do its own thing, regardless of need.
20. Systems develop goals of their own the instant they come into being.
21. Intrasystem goals come first.
22. Complex systems usually operate in failure mode.
23. A complex system can fail in an infinite number of ways.
24. The mode of failure of a complex system cannot ordinarily be predicted
from its structure.
25. The critical variables are discovered by accident.
26. The larger the system, the greater the probability of unexpected
failure.
27. "Success" or "Function" in any system may be failure in the systems to
which the system is connected.
   a. In setting up a new system, tread softly. You may be disturbing
another system that is actually working.
28. When a fail-safe system fails, it fails by failing to fail-safe.
29. Complex systems tend to produce complex responses (not solutions) to
problems.
30. Great advances are not produced by systems designed to produce great
advances.
31. Systems run better when designed to run downhill.
32. Loose systems last longer and work better.
   a. Efficient systems are dangerous to themselves and to others.

Advanced Concepts
1. Everything is a system.
2. Everything is part of a larger system.
```
