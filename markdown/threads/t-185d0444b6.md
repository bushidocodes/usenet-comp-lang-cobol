An interesting q, please summarise your replies to the newsgroup.

If you take one step back, you really want to know who is going to
be a useful development programmer, currently for COBOL projects.

In my opinion, and from my experience of other programmers, the
method of approach to a problem (any problem) is a far more
reliable indicator of programming ability than mnemonic recall of
the manual for an implementation of a language.

So set the candidate an open problem (eg: mail order sales to
football fans) and see how they approach it - analysis of the
primary problems, alternative approaches, simplicity, failsafe
methods etc.
