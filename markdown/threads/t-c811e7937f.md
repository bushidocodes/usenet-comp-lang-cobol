```
Something you can use in place of the disallowed nested copy-with-replace is
the following:

replace ==(ws)== by ==xx==.
copy  copybk1.

where COPYBK1 is
01 (ws)-data
   02  (ws)-name               pic x(40)
   02  (ws)-addr               pic x(200).
   02  (ws)-addr-parsed        redefines (ws)-addr.
   copy  addrpars.

This works on my compiler, in any case.  I assume it's standard, but I have
not checked.

Frank
```

```
Yes, it is legal to have REPLACE outside or inside of nested COPY statements (in 
the '02 Standard and as an extension in many - not all - '85 Standard 
compilers) - while mixing COPY/REPLACING with nested copies is not allowed.

There are good (technical) reasons for this having to do with the "required" 
order in which COPY statements are processed.
```
