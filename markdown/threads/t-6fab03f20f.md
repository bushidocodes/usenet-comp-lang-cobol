Fay,
I am currently using CSP and have been for just over a year. It is a
*VERY*
easy language to learn - 2 weeks to become productive if you already
know
COBOL. It is very much like COBOL - yet do not try to make it work like
COBOL.
I do not know what type of environment that you will be using CSP in,
but some
environments are better than others.

CSP is not used very extensively in the US ~5,000 - 7,000 sites. It is
used
more extensively in Europe. From what I understand, most of CSP has
been
developed in Germany and written in C.

CSP is very COBOL-like. You should have no trouble learning it if you
are familiar with COBOL. Whether this is a "dead end" has more
to do with what kind of employer you have than what language you may
be programming in right now.

To improve your career options, I advise learning one or more
"modern" computer language such as C. Not because it is a "better"
language, or that it will get you a better job *now*, but just so
you'll know more things that are likely come in handy eventually.

The only real dead end is not to be adaptable.
