I had an interesting day on Saturday with a Win XP machine belonging to my
next door neighbour.

It was inaccessible. All attempts to log on failed with an "invalid
password". Booting into safe mode made no difference so you could not
restore from last known good configuration, and the system restore points
had been disabled.

Given that many of the people who use this forum are running Windows
machines, I thought I'd relay this experience, on the chance it may ease the
despair that you get when some pond life locks your machine so it cannot be
logged into.

Most of us here have enough computer experience to NOT press keys when
someone on the phone tells you to, but these guys are quite agressive and
persuasive and I can understand people falling for this scam.

You have someone on the phone (usually with a pronounced Indian accent) who
tells you he is a Microsoft technician and your machine is causing problems
on the network (internet). But don't worry, he can fix it for you online.
You are required to press the Windows/R combination and they connect to you.
At that point, you are owned. They install a syskey password, mess around a
bit, then tell you you must pay for their time. They ask for your credit
card. My neighbour, who is a gentle, honest, not young man, baulked at this
and refused. They then became agressive but he still wouldn't give them his
credit card. They told him his system would be locked until he decided to
pay them $200 and they gave him a phone number (in New York) to call when he
was ready to get his machine back.

Nice one, isn't it?

As a typical Windows family user his machine has all his emails, photos of
the family, and documents that are precious to him. As a typical user he has
no backup. He bought the machine from a dealer so it was an OEM install and
he has no windows recovery disks. (They wouldn't work anyway because the
scammers fix it so the recovery disks can't see the windows install.)

He came over to my place and asked if I could take a look. I did and it was
it locked.

Shouldn't be a problem... "all you gotta do" is recover the password... :-)

I booted to a WinPE disk and took an image of the partition so at least his
data wasn't lost.

Next, boot to a Linux password recovery disk, remove the syskey setting,
reset the Admin password to blank, and the user password to blank. Rebooted
from the hard drive. Still locked out.

By now it was getting interesting, so I asked him to leave it with me. He
had contacted his local computer dealer who told him there was no recovery
possible and the system would have to be rebuilt and everything
re-installed. (He had had several people with the same problem and all
attempts to recover had failed.)

I don't want to give away publicly what I did or how I did it because it
helps the scammers and they may modify their existing software to prevent
what I did. I can tell you I spent a fair bit of time researching the
Windows boot process and trying to get into a disabled restore point using
DOS commands just like the old days. Eventually I managed to get a boot that
bypassed their lockout. Then it was just a question of creating a new
account and password, restoring the WiFi config and his printer driver,
removing some rubbish that was left over from a previous virus infection,
and giving him back his system, with his original password restored.

No re-install of windows, no rebuilds, no software re-installs, and no copy
of the Windows install disk required. In fact, nothing but the broken
machine and some partition manager and DOS tools on a boot disk. (But there
was some serious low-level hacking of the OS to get it done. In fact, I got
sick of copying/editing system files and configs around manually and wrote a
script to do it, which I have placed on his machine in a folder called
"Petefix". This will be a last ditch recovery if he has a problem again. The
first thing I did once it was back and stable was take a partition image to
an external hard drive and give it to him to keep safe.)

When I last saw him he was working through the 58 emails he had waiting in
his restored Outlook... :-)

So, I got an education into reverse engineering the Windows XP (SP3) OS and
it was a pleasant enough way to spend Saturday... :-)

If you or any of your friends/family have a similar problem, mail me
privately and I'll be glad to help.

Don't give in to the wave of despair you feel and that awful knot in the pit
of your stomach. It isn't the end of the world...

The most important thing about this is that the scammers didn't get away
with it... These people are low order bottom feeders. If they have the skill
to disable someone's system, they surely have the skill to get a job.

Pete.
"I used to write COBOL...now I can do anything."
