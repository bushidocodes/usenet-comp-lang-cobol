```
CarlosS940 wrote:
>programming game application is much harder than programing business
>application.  Anyone agree?

Games take a half dozen programmers several months to complete.  Some
business applications take fifty or a hundred programmers *years* to
complete.  See a pattern?  You think a game program is more difficult
than a payroll/personnel system for a city with 100,000 employees and
seventy-five unions to deal with?  Some business logic is so convoluted
by illogical laws, regulations and rules that it is almost impossible
to even *define* the correct logic, let alone program it.  Sometimes
you can't even find out what the correct logic is because it is so
complex and obscure that no one can tell you.  Then you have to program
by 'best guess' and correct errors as they crop up.  I and an associate
had to design and program a system like that a couple of years ago.
The client loves the system, but there is a certain small part of it
which may never function completely as they want.  A totally correct
logic simply does not exist to satisfy all the conflicting rules and
exceptions and exceptions to the exceptions in the boneheaded state
regulations.

A game takes the form the designers want, and they are free to modify
their design to fit the situation.  In business programming the system
must take a form dictated from outside, often by non technical people
who have no clue what the ramifications are of one design decision vs.
another, and do not care, or even want to know or hear.
```
