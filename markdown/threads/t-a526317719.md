There is an argument that says if you DON'T speak up when you see bad 
management it will never get fixed.

There is another argument that says if you DO speak up when you see bad 
management you may be out of a job. :-)

As DD has sagely observed here on more than one occasion: you can be fired 
for being wrong, but you can also be fired for being right...

The best approach is to try and cultivate a skill set that ensures you can 
always get a job, then speak your mind. Tell the truth and shame the Devil. 
Rock the boat, get things changed. Make it a better place to work and be 
glad to work there. If that is not a viable course of action for you, then 
you have two options:

1. Leave.
2. Stop worrying about it. Keep a low profile, don't let it get to you, and 
take the money.

If you prefer to have a quiet life and wait it out for retirement, then 
there is really no point in having any opinion about anything that is likely 
to rock the boat. Keep your head down, do the miles, then take the pension 
and don't really give a damn whether they give you a watch or a plaque or 
lunch with the team, or nothing at all. Remember, you don't need their 
approval; just their paychecks... :-)

Try not to feel bad, Charlie. Look on it as a new phase of your life where
