Howdy, I have a little question I am hoping to get some help with. I have
finished a lab that uses a double level table. The form of the table is
different than any examples that are in the books or on-line references I have
seen in that the first level of the table is a group item with two elementary
items, one of which is the second level of the table. That is the table
contains a machine number that is used to look up the standard amount of pieces
that can be made of various parts on that machine. Oh, the first level, the
machine number is hard coded, the second level with machine number and standard
is input loaded. The book shows two level tables thusly:

01 TABLE.
05 1ST-LEVEL OCCURS 10 TIMES.
10 2ND-LEVLE OCCURS 10 TIMES.
15 T-ARG PIC XXX.
15 T-FUN PIC XXX.
When I try to bastardize this for my own nefarious purposes I wrote it like
this:

01 TABLE.
05 TABLE-DATA.
10 F PIC XXX VALUE '123'.
ETC.
10 F PIC XXX VALUE '220'.
05 MACHINE-TAB REDEFINES TABLE-DATA OCCURS 20 TIMES.
10 T-MACHINE PIC XXX.
10 T-PART-STAND OCCURS 5 TIMES.
15 T-PART PIC X(5).
15 T-STAND PIC X(4).

When I try to load the second level of the table, T-PART-STAND, using the
following line of code I get errors, something like a value not defined error:

READ MACH-PARTS INTO T-PART-STAND(X).

If I used two subscripts I also get an error, although it might not be the same
error, I forget. What I ended up doing to fix it was to add the following code
to the table definition:

10 T-PART-STAND PIC X(9).
10 TT-PART-STAND REDEFINES T-PART-STAND OCCURS 5 TIMES.
15 T-PART PIC X(5).
15 T-STAND PIC X(4).

What I think is happening is that you cannot move a 45 character record into
T-PART-STAND (from my original code) for some reason, but can when you redefine
it. In the lab I added an index to the first level and moved the input record
into T-PART-STAND(T-INDX) without any problems. I played with it with and
without indexes and couldn't get it to work. Any clues?

Thanks
