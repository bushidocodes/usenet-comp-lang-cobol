When you move an elementary numeric field to another elementary numeric field,
the conversion will be done for you automatically.  Without the "LEADING
SEPERATE" clause the (ususally) last digit will contain both the digit and the
sign of the field.

01  FIELD-1     PIC S9(5) SIGN LEADING SEPERATE.
01  FIELD-2     PIC S9(5).
01  FIELD-3     PIC ZZ,ZZ9-.

If Field-1 contains "+00024", after MOVE FIELD-1 TO FIELD-2, Field-2 would
contain
00002D on many systems.  If you move Field-1 to Field-3 (or move from Field-2
after the above move), Field-3 would contain "bbbb24b" ("b" represents a
space).  The trailing "-" in the picture field would only contain a "-" if the
source field was negative.  HTH

WRITE ... FROM is an implicit MOVE, in your case from TEMP-REC to
OUT-REC, and then the WRITE to the file.

Both TEMP-REC and OUT-REC are aggregates rather than elemntary items. As
you should have learned, MOVEs from and to aggregates never edit fields,
but are treated as strings.
Cut off a lading separate sign and translate it into a 'standard' number
is a sort of edit, so you have to MOVE the elemntary items:

MOVE IN-CODE to TEMP-CODE
MOVE TEMP-CODE to OUT-CODE
WRITE OUT-REC

and yes, you have to define the OUT-REC as:

01 OUT-REC.
   03 OUT-CODE PIC S9(7)V99.

or even easier, you forget the TEMP-REC and MOVE IN-CODE TO OUT-CODE.

Cheers
