Mark, you may want to consider offering your services on a contractual
basis, rather than as an employee.  Companies are less reluctant to try
you out on contract, because you can be out the door in a flash if you
don't work out.  And for your first contract, consider going for less
money than you might like.  Also consider taking something which is a
longer commute than you might want.  Is moving an option?  Once you get
some experience, you can look around for something better.

I'll give you the answer in strategic wording.

First: shoot MORE "bullets" (job applications).

Second: shoot DIFFERENT "munition". Like visiting places where you can
meet personell managers, publishing nice superficial essays on
programming
or whatever your possibilities are. Have nice telephone chats with the
chaps who reject your applications. Ask them for advice. And if you like
to, ask them how they got their first job without practical experience.
And after that, send them a card to thank them. Etcetera.

Third: persevere at the WEAKEST point of the "enemy's frontline".
Once someone might want to hire you, you might be tempted to think:
o boy, this works so easily, it can't be a good company!
(Or even: they must be mad to take a looser like me.)
In fact the company that wants you is your STRONGEST point.

Fourth: WIDEN the attack. Career change? There's no such thing, never
say
that any more! You only increased your possibilities to find a job! And
loving both professions is not adultery ...

Fifth: create FUN. Take a break once a wile, to give yourself the
opportunity to get some distance and regain self esteem, no matter
what the circumstances suggest.

With kind regards,
Rick
