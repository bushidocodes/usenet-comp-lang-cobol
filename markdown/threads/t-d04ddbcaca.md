Good question -- my suggestion is to stop by a large local library if
available to you and look through the books on job opportunities.
Books on 'getting a job' tend to have good sections on the hard
questions you'll be asked -- and the hard quesions typically aren't on
COBOL syntax or computers at all. Besides, if it's Y2K work, the tough
decisions will have already been made or will typically be done by
others.

However, a tip: be prepared to explain some project or other technical
achievement that is on resume. And explain in the first person. When I
interview someone who consistently says "we did this..." and "we did
that..." I get the opinion that the person being interviewed did
nothing special. People want to know what YOU did, so practice using
the 'I' pronoun. Anyway, that's my take. Good luck.
