There is a national organization of micro focus user groups (consiting
mostly of presidents of the various regional groups).  We meet every
month by telephone, and have been doing so for 4 or 5 years.  When
Merant first announced that there would be no 2000 annual users
conference, that organization responded (almost immediately) with a
position paper.

The postion of the user group presidents was that the annual conference
was extremely valuable for learning about merant products and for
networking with other users.   Merant had intended to replace the annual
conference with increased internet seminars and meetings.  we pointed
out that the networking would not happen via the internet.

In any case Merant announced that the annual conference was extremely
costly and they felt they could use that budget in a more cost effective
manner.  So far I have not seen much in the way of regional seminars or
internet offerings.  There have been a few that have been offered, but
nothing that comes close to the content available in just a half day of
the old annual conference.  There has been no word at all about a
conference for 2001.

Since the lead time for a conference that size is a year or more, I am
fairly sure there will NOT be a Merant users conference in 2001, unless
it is rather late in the year.....  there has not been as much as a hint
thta Merant is considering bringing back the old annual conference, or
even some scaled down, cheaper version.

Too bad.  I think MERANT is being very short sighted on this.

Even small software houses with niche products hold user conferences. 
They may not be at some fancy hotel in Las Vegas, or they may not rent

create feedback between customers and the developers--all of which are
highly valuable.

exactly what they want, what it should cost, and how it should be
delivered (with or without 'amnesty')?  Do MERANT customers buy whatever

not needed.  :)
