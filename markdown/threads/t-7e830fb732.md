Fujitsu Power COBOL is the closest thing I have seen to "Visual COBOL"
to date.  In "theory" with these visual tools, you should be able to
click up an application, code mini procedures for events, and not
worry about the ACTUAL final mixmash of code.  I think with 4.2 they
come pretty darn close to pulling it off.  

I have NOTHING against PowerCOBOL and I think it is a FINE product,
but I do not agree that this type of development is adequet for
enterprise driving systems.  One gets so bogged down with the event
driven dependancies that development is driven more my the user
interface than by the business requirements.  The same is true of ANY
of these visual development tools.  In fact, C, and C++ programmers
are starting to see the light in this regard.  What goes around comes
around.  Go to http://www.download.com and search for "Winzilla" and
you will see what I mean.
