Hi:

Good luck with your search.

I suppose it is the same in England as in America and elsewhere
that most body shops or whatever they call themselves these days
are nothing more than skill-set-matching automatons.

Since you are a beginner, your skill-set will be less than
adequate and therefore will fail the match.

Although a resume may give one some idea of what a person knows,
it will not reveal how well they know what they know nor how
good they are.

Anyway, in the olden days when I hired programmers, I would never
speak to anyone who would not bring in samples of their work.
Programs and stuff which they had written.

So, people got hired based on an interview and the samples of
their work.

It probably might not make any difference whatsoever, but I
always suggest creating a portfolio of your work to show to
people who might be capable of appreciating it. Show them
what you have done. All sorts of other professions do this,
but not programmers, I wonder why?

You might also want to create an HTML version of your
portfolio with screen shots and sample code which you could
email to people. As a beginner, it might not be much, but
it is better than nothing.

Thanks

Tony Dilworth
