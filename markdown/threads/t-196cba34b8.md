```
Facts:
  The following vendors have at one time participated in the J4 (X3J4) ANSI-ish 
or CODASYL (CCC) process - but no longer do:
   Dec, Tandem, Compaq, HP (divide how you will <G>)
   Fujitsu
   AcuCOBOL/AcuCorp
   Liant RM

IBM and Unisys remain on J4 (Micro Focus may or may not also remain)

    ***

The following companies have participated in the WG4 (ISO-ish) process.  As far 
as I know they no longer do.
    France
    Austria
    Canada
    Germany (I think they STILL participate, but I am not positive)
    New Zealand  (I can't remember whether they actually became a "P" member or 
not - nor what their current status is)

    ***

The only vendor who ever publicly promised a "fully conforming" '02 Standard 
compiler was Micro Focus - and they backed away from this promise soon after the 
Standard was approved.  (I think that Fujitsu/Siemens has actually done a LOT of 
work on creating a conforming compiler, but I don't know the status of that 
project.)

* * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * *
My opinion - NOT "fact"

    The COBOL "standardization" process is so close to dead that any difference 
between life and death is in the eyes of the (few remaining) participants. 
Furthermore, there seems to be the remarkable ability to "die retroactively" - 
i.e. make the '02 Standard as irrelevant as many would have wished it to be in 
2002.
```
