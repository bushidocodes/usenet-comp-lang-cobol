I've worked and am working with many tools for this project. At "work" we
are using Micro Focus COBOL and their DIALOG system. I have also looked at
the Power COBOL and their method for scripting screens.

There are basically 2 ways of going about this. The TRUE COBOL method
where everything happens in your COBOL program, and the "Scripting" method,
where another language, be it Visual Basic, Fujitsu's Scripting language or
Dialog System language.

There are many disadvantages associated with the scripting method, of which
the major one is learning curve. There are several things you will have to
deal with when moving to windows. The major consideration is the fact that
the user has much more control, the applications will now be "event" driven
rather than guided by your program flow.

I have now written from scratch and converted several programs using
various different tools. Here are my conclusions.

When converting to Dialog system the applications require an entire
rewrite. It's a pain, and very slow going. Little progress in a lot of
time.

I have also used an EXCELLENT product called COBOL SP2. This product works
will all the major compilers in all their "flavors", from DOS to UNIX to
WINDOWS to OS/2. It is a "True COBOL" interface in that it uses a call
interface.

If your programs us any third party screen package or if they use screen
sections the conversion to SP2 is a SNAP. EASY, and painless. You get to
keep 95% of your code, UNCHANGED. They are true windows programs. They
work great and are not rife with the bugs and snags we have found in Micro
Focus's Dialog system.

You can check out SP2 for 60 days for free, by going to
http://www.flexus.com

I HIGHLY recommend the product. I have been extremely productive using it
and I have converted several systems that now are Windows instead of DOS
and they work great.

Another advantage is speed. SP2 is lightning fast, especially compared to
Dialog system.
