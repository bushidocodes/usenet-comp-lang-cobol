As Howard said, they are not similar.  Easytrieve is best used for
"quick and dirty" reports although I do know of sites that create
files, do complex reports and other things and freeze the software so
that it can be run like a compiled and linked Cobol program.  It is a
good tool to have in your arsenal, but Cobol is far more flexible and
do things Easytrieve struggles with (like 2 or 3 dimensional table
handling).

I know some people who choose Easytrieve to write their quick programs.    But I
speak CoBOL natively, my Easytrieve isn't so natural.   And even experienced
Easytrieve programmers get caught by some not obvious quirks of the sort that
CoBOL doesn't have.

Writing a quick and dirty CoBOL program is real quick - except that it isn't
dirty as I have done these programs so many times.

And if the program is a complex thing that will need lots of maintenance - the
choice between languages is shared by all of us - CoBOL.
