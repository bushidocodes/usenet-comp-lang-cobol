Both are software emulators. Ten years ago there were hardware emulators. For
about $2K you could buy a mainframe on a card that plugged into your PC bus.
Speed was said to be comparable to a 4381 -- a low-end mainframe that served the
IT needs of medium-sized companies in the '80s. Those hardware solutions are now
gone, thanks I'm sure to IBM licensing roadblocks. 

Rather than pretending to be a mainframe, a better solution is porting source
code to an OS outside the control of IBM.
