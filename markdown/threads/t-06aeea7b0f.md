I have seen a number of studies that come to an invalid
conclusion.  One was a comparison between Cobol and C based
on the amount of new work and of maintenance work.  Much
more of Cobol was maintenace, much more of C was new
development.  It concluded by saying that if the Cobol
systems were converted to C then the maintenance costs
would be reduced.

What the author failed to take into account was that the
types of system were completely different.  The Cobol
systems were being maintained, not to eliminate bugs or
make them work better, but to change the way they worked
to match the changes in the businesses they worked for.

The C systems, in general, were more stable in that their
*requirements* didn't change.

Convert the Cobol systems to C and they will require at
least as much maintenance, probably more.

You seem to say here that Cobol programming is boring, use
Perl and it will stop being boring and will become interesting.
Sorrreeee, Cobol programming is about orders and invoices,
debtors balances and creditors ledgers.  About getting the
discounts rights and the sales report laid out the way
the sales manager wants it.

Many find that this type of work is boring because they
don't care about the nature of the work, which is typically
adding a new way of adding a special promotion to the
order system.

Changing to writing this in C or Perl or Ada does not change
the nature of the work.  It won't make it more interesting,
it just substitutes on set of problems for another.

Now it may be that the sort of things done in Perl are
inherently interesting: CGI scripts, processing text
in various ways.  But I would suggest that trying to 
replace existing Cobol with Perl (or C or Ada) will
do **nothing** for the systems themselves.

Please post advocacy into appropriate groups only.
