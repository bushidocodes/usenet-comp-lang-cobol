I was repeatedly told that in the current

object-oriented trend , Cobol is no match for Delphi.

This is the standpoint of new ,small programming

companies. They argue that Cobol is a vintage

language and therefore the companies should discard

all their Cobol source and replace it by Delphi.

However...

I am a local MBA and know that this is NOT the

standpoint of larger organizations and there are

some organizational reasons NOT to use Delphi.

For example, I have seen Cobol programs longer than several

million lines and Delphi cannot do this.

And a Korean company is working on a Win95/NT COBOL

which works with 2-byte character sets(what MF Cobol

cannot do->therefore useless in Korea:our Hangeul

has several peculiarities and this makes the whole thing

difficult).

The people I work with(the aforementioned ones) are very

young and have up to now no experience with larger corpo-

Now... since I am no programmer...

who is right? ANd why??

Dear Lim:
I got a copy of Delphi at a trade show, and it looks nice. But
COBOL is a useful 3GL which you can write programs in that are
flexible.

Can Delphi handle several terminals connected to a server? COBOL
has been doing this via Teleprocessing monitors suchs as CICS or
IMS/DC for years...

Delphi and Visual BASIC are fine for one or two person small operations,
but COBOL can process vast amounts of data in text format, such as
on tapes.

Can you imagine trying to process Credit card or income tax information
on these small computers with these semi-toy 4GL's?

Sure you can make a push button and a data entry edit control quickly,
but can you process all of the credit card purchases for one store
in Korea (or the daily cash balance or inventory?)
