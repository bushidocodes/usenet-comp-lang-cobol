```
In my recruiting a placement practice I specialize in assembling a group
of qualified personnel who want to make a change for various reasons and
then I market them to the appropriate clients. I've been following the
discussion in this group and am convinced of the increasingly critical
shortage of cobol programmers who will be needed for these positions.

In the past month, two of my candidates wanted to find positions in
specific major metropolitan areas. In marketing them I called IT depts of
the utility, banking and insurance companies in these areas. The
responses I got were, "We have1person working on it-that's enough". "Our
software vendor will take care of it." Our programming isn't in cobol, so
we don't need anyone to work on it".
etc. In addition I made contact with some major pharmaceutical companies
with which I have a regular business relationship.

Most of the people I spoke with were supervisory level IT people, not HR
people. No one showed any sign of interest. I'm sure the need is there.
Am I contacting either the wrong companies or the wrong people within
these companies? Is this need limited to certain areas and certain
industries?

If anyone can help, I'd appreciate the names and contact persons at
companies who are actively building their capabilities to solve 2K
problems now. Also if anyone wishes be on the candidate list so that
their skills can be marketed please send your response so I may contact
you. (That is, once I find out what the key is to reach the right
decision makers within the companies who have, or will have, the need.

Erwin Posner

Professional Advancement
& Placement Institute
dafyomi
```
