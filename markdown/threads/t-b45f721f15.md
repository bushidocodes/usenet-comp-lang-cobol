Alan_Browning wrote:
›
› Just be aware that any random number generator is actually a pseudo-random
› number generator. Some algorithms are better than others. Anyone know what
› method COBOL uses?

The method depends entirely on the implementor. All the standard says
is that the returned value is greater than or equal to zero and less
than one. That for a given seed value on a given implementaion, the
sequence of pseudo-random numbers will always be the same. And, that
the implementor states what the domain of the seed is but that it must
include 0 through 32,767.

Many of us use the same routine used by the other languages (FORTRAN,
C, etc.).
