```
We distributed a new application about 18 months ago.

Since then, we've fixed an number of reported problems and added a number of
features, distributing a new version about ever 6-8 weeks.

Then, in an Epiphany, a moment of clarity, it came to us: why not distribute
this stuff over the web?

Oh, obvious, you say. Everybody does it. Well, not us dummies. We were so
far down in the weeds we overlooked the practicality, thought web
distribution would not be able to easily handle the
installation/repair/replace facility of a full-blown inventory application,
or would require diversion of massive programming effort to implement.

Then we looked again.

It wasn't all that hard.

The creation of a couple-hundred CDs and the printing of same, labeling of
envelopes, update documents, postage, blah-blah-blah -- all went away.

On our last distribution we added code allowing the user to access the
download area of our web site, FTP the patch file, and apply same to every
machine on the network.

Since then, our updating protocol involves uploading the patch to our
website and launching several hundred emails - taking, in total, about 30
minutes.

Boy are we embarrassed we didn't do this sooner. We're not so embarrassed,
however, that we can't pass on what we think (now) is a good idea.

GOOD IDEA FOLLOWS:

For a new application, build in a web-update feature from the get-go. A
small investment up front pays off in much time saved later. I bet there are
even update-from-the-web packages available that only require bolting down
and you'll have everything you need.
```
