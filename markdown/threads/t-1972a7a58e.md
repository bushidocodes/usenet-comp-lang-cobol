> RoadRaat

As with programs, most JCL is "cloned" (copy a working set - and modify it
for the new system). When it comes to things like running existing systems,
you probably will use lots AND LOTS of PROCs.  If you know how to override
PROCs, this will get you thru much of what you need to do. (and if you don't
know how to do this yet, this is something that you really should study)

If you are talking about IBM utilities (which are often taught in JCL
courses - even though strictly speaking it isn't JCL), then it is nice to
know how to do this yourself,  but you probably will find interactive
"tools" to do most of what these can do for you.  I know that I almost
always had to "pull down the manual" whenever I had to do much beyond an
IEBCOPY PDS compress.

Finally, the latest PDF (and most 3rd party products) DO have "JCL
generators" for many of their facilities.  How much these are used and how
useful they are depend entirely on the site that you end up working at.
