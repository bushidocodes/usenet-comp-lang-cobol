(Posted to comp.lang.cobol - CC's to various MF people)

It seems to me that NOW is the time for all the "small site" and
"individual" vendors of COBOL applications to communicate with the *new*
Micro Focus concerning the issues around "run-time license fees".

To see that such communication *does* work, I suggest that you look at
www.google.com via the thread at:

http://groups.google.com/groups?q=g:thl2299475642d&hl=en&safe=off&selm=8ibga
0%24f7d%241%40nnrp1.deja.com

(You may need to "paste" it to get that full "URL" - or alternatively go to
 http://groups.google.com/advanced_group_search?hl=en&oe=ISO-8859-1&safe=off

and search comp.lang.cobol for
  Hill NetExpress 3.1 Runtime Fees

It seems to me that Tony Hill (now "head" of the new Micro Focus) was
responsive to "customer input" - as reflected in this newsgroup when he said
(among other things),

"On behalf of MERANT, I would like to thank this group for their comments on
the Net Express run-time licensing policy.

Following a thorough review of your comments, and extensive discussion
within MERANT,  I have discovered that, although we have had a policy of
charging run-time license fees for Net Express for some time, we have not
communicated this at all well to our customers in North
America. Accordingly, I am pleased to inform you that, effective
immediately, we plan to implement a North American run-time amnesty program
for Net Express 3.1 targeted at small development organisations.
  ...
Finally, thanks again for your interest in MERANT. Please feel free to
e-mail me directly on tony.hill@merant.com if you have any urgent comments."

Given his previous comments (when still a part of MERANT) it seems to me
(and of course I could be wrong <G>) that NOW is the time to try and
convince the NEW Micro Focus to take a NEW look at the entire area of
"run-time license fees".

It seems to me that given enough input from current, past, and prospective
"customers" for whom this SINGLE issue may determine whether or not they use
any MF product to produce applications intended for "distribution", that he
(they? MF?) might be able to come up with a GOOD business case for modifying
their entire approach and NOT just going with a "one time" or "one
continent" amnesty program.

I don't think that you can/should expect an INSTANT change in direction (as
the new company is "finding its way" back to meeting COBOL customer needs).
However, I do think that if enough sites can explain

 - Why they have converted from Micro Focus to Fujitsu (or IBM or AcuCorp)
 - Why they are dropping COBOL in favor of other "workstation" programming
languages
 - Why they have NOT upgraded to NetExpress V3.1
 - Why they purchased a competitor's product
 - etc, etc

then, this MIGHT provide MF with the "business case" to change their
"business plan's" reliance on run-time licenses from "small ISV and
developers".

As with many other topics, it is by belief that "user input" DOES make a
difference. Therefore, if you don't ASSUME that someone else is taking care
of this - or that your input won't really make a difference, it is my hope
that the "new" Micro Focus may "re-enter" the small developer COBOL market
as a serious "player".
