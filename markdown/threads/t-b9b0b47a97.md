I have been working very hard on the PowerCOBOL migration and I've decided
I'm getting too old for this stuff.

The days when I could work 10 hours and never notice the passage of time,
are gone. These days I do 4 hours and I'm looking for a break. Concentrating
for very long periods of time is getting more difficult.

But I still want to know, I still want to learn new stuff (fortunately, my
capacity to do so doesn't seem significantly dimininshed) and I still get
excited by solving problems.

(I've noticed I seem to enjoy quiz shows on TV more than I used to... don't
know if that means anything.)

Anyway, I was wondering if it is just me, or if others here still enjoy
doing stuff but don't have the energy they once had to do it?

The bottom line for me has been to try and stop being obsessive about
computer programming problems and realize that everything doesn't have to be
critical... :-)

(It's like the old bull and the young steer standing on the hill looking
down at the herd of heifers in the valley.
The young bull says excitedly: "Let's run down and shag a few of them.!!!"
The old bull replies calmly: "Let's walk down and shag them all...")

I took a day off yesterday from the PowerCOBOL stuff (which is so difficult
there is an elegant beauty about it :-)) and decided to address the COBOL21
side of the PRIMA web site. I'm really hoping to have this site completed
for launch of some new products in September. The COBOL21 side is intended
to be a COBOL friendly place for programmers (old and new) to relax, find
support, and learn, if that is what they want to do. The thought struck me
that it is a bit like this forum in that regard; except that here the
different uses get a bit mixed up and it results in people complaining that
they didn't find what they expected... wanted a code sample and found the
thread very quickly went off-topic and wandered into other things. (For
myself, I have enjoyed the OT threads every bit as much as the COBOL ones
and sometimes they are more interesting.) When the new site is fully
functional it will be pretty easy to see exactly what areas do what, and you
can browse for education, help and support, or simply relaxation (I have
created a "Lounge" tile which will be more for socializing and there will be
a blog attached eventually...)

Anyway, I spent the day working on stuff about Scripting. I know it's a bit
like new tricks, but there are lively minds here that may enjoy playing with
this. I decided to start at the beginning so you don't need any previous
knowledge to pick it up. I had fun doing it and today, I'm happy to be back
on the PowerCOBOL code-behind.

You can see the resultsof yesterday's labour for COBOL21 at:
http://primacomputing.co.nz/primametro/scripting.aspx
... and the progress on PowerCOBOL migration is being reported at:
http://primacomputing.co.nz/primametro/pwrCOBMigration.aspx

Any comments/feedback gratefully received; I can't make it better if no-one
complains :-)

Pete.
"I used to write COBOL...now I can do anything."
