I would also like any information that you find out (good technical experts to
talk to, quality of their standard support line vs 900 number, etc.). I can
tell you three things from our experience with FS.NLM:

1) If you like your network administrator, warn him beforehand that the Novell
Monitor will be giving an "inaccurate" utilization percentage (+90%) anytime
the FS.NLM is running and any file is open - ours almost had a coronary.

2) Be sure you have a good daily backup. MF's "suggested" procedure is to
bring down the NLM, run a backup, and then LOAD FS.NLM. You can bring down
the NLM through your program, but I have found no way yet to "LOAD FS" from
the console via a program. FS graps files exclusively; if any apps
workstation "crashs" with files open, FS keeps those files open until it goes
down. If the server crashes with open files, then those indexed files can
become corrupt - I have always had to go back to my last backup to recover.
Their rollforward recovery is very poorly documented.

3) We can not upgrade to Novell 4.11; the latest FS.NLM grabs all available
cache buffers and croaks the server. MF support has not responsed to my
problem.
