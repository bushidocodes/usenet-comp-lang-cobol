```
Since the intellectual and industrial revolution which
began in Europe around the turns of the 18th century,
the world has been shifted from the belief in nature to
a firmer conviction in the human mind. Analysis which
is the product of man's thinking on his own 2 feet takes
priority over the practice of other kinds of witchcraft.
Even so, we can still classify analysis as a craft to
implement new systems of values in a world of human
communities.

When Jesus Christ walked on the water, he did not go
into some kind of developmental stage and do the work
to implement the process of walking over the water. It
was a straightforward moral and spiritual affirmation.
The heart, the mind, and the action all synchronize to
make life happening at the instance of practical necessity.
This sounds more like Hollywood where light, camera,
actions are the way to channel human creativity toward
the medium of story telling and public entertainment for
the masses.

Walking over the water, as what Jesus Christ had done
to prove the validity and power of the divine mind, was a
spiritual affirmation at best. This is a one shot deal to
demonstrate the efficacity of the combined spiritual and
moral wills.

The arts and sciences of analysis is based on the
works of observation, experimentation, and intellectual
exercise. Although a lot of analytical work would take
tremendous amount of patient coordination among
groups of people over a long period of time, modern
societies do not recognize spiritual inputs into the
equation of analysis. They tend to look at the process
as a pure cerebral activity. Moreover, any other practices
that believe in the convergence of the stars and folksy
remedies are considered as witchcraft that may play
game on the human mind and they are deemed to be
as not trustworthy or totally effective.

There seems to be a conflict between the rigueur of
scientific methodology and the magical formula of
traditional and folksy practices. Modern industrial
methods insist upon verification. Folksy practices
propagate themselves through faith and tradition.

The witchcraft of analysis depends on intuitive reasoning
and may depend on facts, figures, and tons of solid data.
With the advent of computer technology, it can generate
fairly reliable information upon which one can go about to
make better decisions. It may also depend on physical
evidence and value representation of its arguments in
terms of ideas and thought processes. Walking over the
water, however, is outside the realm of analytical methods
and norms of expanded intellectual perspectives. As such,
the witchcraft of analysis cannot go about to reconstruct
that kind of model and implement the idea. Man of normal
range of behaviors cannot walk over the water.


Chim Sotuon
```
