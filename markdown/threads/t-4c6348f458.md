If a large employer believes that treating the employees like mushrooms is
good for the bottom line (which many do), and if there is a pool of
potential workers suitable for the job, then whether or not a particular
single employee quits will have little effect on the employer (even though
losing the job may easily have a significant negative effect on the
employee).  But if, as you say, ALL the suitable workers can together
credibly threaten to withdraw their labor power, then that does have a
significant effect on the employer.  That's what unions try to do.

Commonwealth countries), the employer - employee relationship is termed a
"master - servant" one.  For example, the crime of embezzlement is called
"theft as a servant".
