› It's safe to speculate that it won't be any time *this* year! The first validated Cobol-85 compilers
› (from IBM and Tandem) appeared on the market in mid to late '86, I think.
›
It's even safer to speculate that a validated compiler cannot be available
until the proposed draft COBOL standard is adopted as an ISO standard, probably
in 1997. Until then no implementation can claim to be a validated COBOL 97
implementation.

The current state of the proposal is that it is in Working Draft form awaiting
its first formal public review as a Committee Draft. When comments from the
review have been processed a Draft International Standard will be issued for
review. Comments from this review will be processed and the International
Standard will be published (probably at the end of 1997). If there is
insufficient support (from the National Standards bodies) for the standard at
any stage it may be required to go back to a previous stage of public review.
Each stage takes about 6-8 months to complete so any delay will take the
publication date into 1998 (at least).

Rod Grealish
