books that I've read). But "neat hacks" are only programmer ego boosts and
really poor programming practice. It's a sign of poor design when a change
to the video driver can crash the print driver. They should be totally
separate (except perhaps for some generic rasterizing subroutines), however
I have this happen. This indicates, to me, that there are "global" variables
that are passed all over the stupid system. Well, enough of my ranting.
If you want better, but admittedly not perfect, code, then look at Linux.
At least if the GUI crashes, I can get running in character mode. I haven't
found a way to do that with WindowNT 4.0!

Well, there is comparision to AS400 another place in this thread, and I
would like to emphasize that this comparision is somewhat unfair.

One issue is that AS400 on the one side, is proprietary, and if any third
party vendor comes in, it is after a number of extensive qualifications.
While the Wintel platform on the other side, is open to anyone which has a
piece of HW/SW to supply.

Another issue is that because the AS400 is targeting businesses only, more
money are put in to quality test that HW/HW and HW/SW cooperates well. You
can get Wintel computers too, that are rock solid as long as you don't mess
with them, and in particular if you are willing to pay the price.
Obviously, if you buy that smash offer from company xyz by mail, you have no
idea what you get. If, you on the other hand purchase a dedicated server
from Compaq, Dell you will have to pay the price, but they will also
guarantee you that the parts and software cooperate well.

One should also note the difference in that while the AS400 is primarily a
server computer, what one compares it to on the Wintel side is a
workstation. Wintel servers, while not as reliable as AS400 is still way
more reliable than its workstations.

The basic problem with Wintel and its instances of sudden death is that the
device drivers are too close to the kernel. It could have been moved out,
but given the heavy graphics of the Wintel computers, this is a choice of
stability versus speed. Device drivers are often done by third party, hence,
Wintel stability are then relying on the "good" work of people that are not
blamed in this context. Microsoft could have demanded to do the drivers
themselves, and put them through the same quality criteria as for an AS400.
But I be damned if Mr. John Doe would like to a) pay the extra cost this
would imply and b) accept the limited market of cheap peripheral equipment
this would force.

Finally, let's not forget that the OS may be blamed for many things, but it
can't be blamed for the fact that the Intel CPU's still today has big flaws,
because of the poor design done back then when the first 8080 was
introduced. It is called backward compatibility.

Wintel is a success because of our demand to have as much as possible for as
little as possible. Ever thought about why mainframes are as expensive as
they are?

I am no admirer of Microsoft as such myself, as I have pointed out earlier
in this NG, I used to be a die hard OS/2'er. But for the case of seriousity,
we should differ between facts and fiction.

Someone will certainly point out that Linux is on the Intel hardware as
well, indeed they are, and indeed it is a puzzle, for those that would like
to make their Linux installation cooperate a tape streamer, CD Rom, sound
card, nic, powerful graphics and may be even an USB or two. Particularly if
playing around with some games.
