To answer your second question first: Win2000 is on the way out.  I
don't know offhand if Microsoft's announced anything about when
they'll end support for it, but with Vista/Longhorn on the way - with
major changes from XP/2003 - they're not going to want to devote
resources to maintaining it any longer than they have to.

Most NT 3.1 programs should run without problems on XP.  And on the
off chance that this isn't one of them, if you still have your NT 4
installation media, you can always get VMWare or Virtual PC and run
NT 4 as a guest OS under XP.  Performance is not bad (I recently had
XP running in VMWare under XP on my Thinkpad R40 - 2.3GHz CPU, 728 MB
RAM - and it was responsive), and I believe there's now a free version
of VMWare available.
