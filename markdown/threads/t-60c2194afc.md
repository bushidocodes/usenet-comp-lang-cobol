	I worked on DG systems of various types in the 1980s.  The obstacles to
people from other environments are not that great if the person's
experience includes UNIX or something like it, DEC, HP, etc.  AS/400
systems are IMHO another area conceptually and I would expect someone
coming from that area to be slower to adapt than the others.  Actually I
would consider INFOS_II, DG's DBMS, to be a greater obstacle to someone
new to the breed; it had its own language and was pretty closely
integrated into DG's COBOL.  There was also a DBMS that spoke a
reasonably standard variety of SQL.  If you have any assembler in the
system it will be "interesting", however.
