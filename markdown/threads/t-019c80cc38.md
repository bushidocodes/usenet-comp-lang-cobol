Scott Wilks wrote:
›
› I have been asked to set up a COBOL training program in the office I
› work at for new programmers.

Might it be asked... what were your duties before you became a
Curriculum Designer?

› This should include self-teaching
› primarily with proffessional supervision from our in-house programmers.

What facilities will be available to the students? Although
'self-teaching' is ideal, true, how much time are you allocating for
classroom-work? How much time will the in-house programmer be
*mandated* (preferably with a separate charge-off code for accounting)
to spend with the students? What are the projected student:teacher and
student:mentor ratios?

› I was wondering if any one had recommendations for introductory
› teaching materials, ie. textbooks or interactive programs, that might
› be useful or anywhere else where I might look for such information.
› Thanks for your time and any advice you can pass along.

Oh, one last question... what is the background of the 'average'
student... high school grad, tech-school grad, some college, BA/BS,
technical expertise... the 'admissions specs', as it were? It is rather
difficult, if not impossible, to design a curriculum without addressing
these and the abovenoted matters.

DD
