>I never saw one without at least a drum.

The first punches I used, IBM 026 and 029 used drum cards for
formatting, columns, etc.  These machines actually had drums on which
said drum cards resided.  I think there was a card verifier (056?)
that had a similar drum setup.

An other machine I later used (I think it was a Univac punch/verifier,
don't remember model) used a similar card that was read into memory.
I also remember some incombatibility with the 026/029, but I think
this was mainly due to the concurrent switch from BCD to EBCDIC.

A later IBM punch (I think the 129) worked similarly; the drum card
was read into memory; there was no drum.

AFAIK, the card you just punched was in a read station and you could either
punch a column or duplicate the same column on the previous card.  Before the
129 one could insert a column in an otherwise duplicated card by holding the
card in the read station while punching the new column.  A column could be
removed by pulling the read card by one column.  While the 129 added some nice
features, being able to shift duplicated fields was no longer possible.
