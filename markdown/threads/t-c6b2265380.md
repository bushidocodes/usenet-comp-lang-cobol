active in COBOL and I don't even remember where the article is".

including lots of helpful stuff, rather than leave him with the

Well "Last Hurrah - Part 2" will be about OO Factory. The others - 
haven't yet thought of the sequence.

Jimmy,

I appreciate your consideration.

However, I am not offended if people don't respond to posts. Everyone here 
has a perfect right to respond or not and to post or not. It is important 
not it take it too seriously.

If CLC is not meeting your requirements ("overall, it doesn't do anything 
for me...") then it is probably best for you not to frequent it. However, 
your posts are often interesting and it is a pity. Times and people change. 
The nice thing about it is that you can always return if you want to.

As for the frequency of posts, and the number of posters diminishing, this 
has a lot to do with the topics under discussion. Every so often, somone 
posts something that strikes a chord and the threads erupt into action. Very 
often the OT posts are much more interesting than the directly COBOL related 
posts.

COBOL is in its twilight and there isn't much to say about it. The few 
youngsters who are picking it up are not like newbies who are beginning 
programming. They have come off computer science courses where they have 
learned about principles of programming. They have grown up with desktop PCs 
and are facile with the tools and environments used on them. In other words, 
they have a much higher degree of computer literacy than we had at their 
age.Instead of stumbling through the dark and learning from bitter 
experience, these people know what they need to do and  make a point of 
finding out how to do it.

We get very few homework assignment posts these days. (partly because there 
is much less COBOL homework being done than there was 20 years ago.)

For myself, I find that pressure of work prevents me having all the time I'd 
like to be able to ponder things here, but I do enjoy the interchanges, and 
CLC overall has been a growth experience for me. I can look back on posts I 
made 10 years ago (and more) and think about how I felt at the time, then 
compare that with how I feel now. I can see how I dealt with things then and 
how I would do so now, and I am persuaded there has been growth... :-) I 
have had cause to consider some of the posts and viewpoints here at length. 
It is a diverse canvas. We've covered everything, including religion and 
politics which are supposed to be taboo subjects for strangers... Minds may 
not have been fundamentally changed, but there have been some elegant 
arguments and viewpoints expressed, and some really good wit sprinkled into 
it. :-) We may not always hear what we want to hear, but this forum is a 
bastion of free speech in a world that increasingly tries to control what 
people say and think.

If everyone stopped writing COBOL tomorrow, this forum would still continue. 
(They'd spend the next ten years reminiscing about the last 30... :-) but 
there's really no harm in that.)

You mentioned earlier that you had seen a dramatic decline in posts to the 
MicroFocus COBOL forum. Perhaps it is related.

The last bastion of fortress COBOL is the mainframe and I suspect the IBM 
List forums are active on matters COBOL and the associated environments. I 
was interested to see the post Bill relayed about the CICS white paper.  (it 
is kind of surreal to think that there is at least ONE piece of software I 
learned 35 years ago, that is still going strong.:-) )

Said it before, and I'll say it again: I intend to post here until it stops 
being fun.

Pete.
