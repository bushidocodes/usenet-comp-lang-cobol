Rex C. Widmer 

Monday, January 18, 1999

A Metro East native who wrote software that identifies computer
problems associated with the year 2000 bug was killed Thursday night
(Jan. 14, 1999)   when he drove his car off a rural highway and struck
a tree near Jefferson City, authorities said.

Rex C. Widmer, a native of Highland, died after visiting a friend's
son at the University of Missouri at Rolla, where Mr. Widmer earned a
bachelor of science degree in computer science in 1972. Police said
the cause of the crash on Missouri Highway 63, almost a mile north of
Highway E, is unknown.

Mr. Widmer, 49, of Shawnee, Kan., had worked with computers at TWA for
25 years. He then traveled the world as a computer expert as demand
for his programming skills increased.

Six years ago, Mr. Widmer wrote a program called Portfolio Analyzer,
which allows a personal computer to scan the language in an IBM
mainframe. His friends said there was little demand for the program
until firms began buying it to update their mainframes to recognize
dates starting Jan. 1, 2000.

Mr. Widmer's company, Edge Information Group of Des Plaines, Ill.,
ended up selling more than $2 million worth of the software.

Mr. Widmer pledged 1 percent of the software's gross revenue to
Missouri-Rolla.

"He used to say we're in the rabbit-pulling business," longtime
business partner Carl Gehr said. "People would have a problem, and
we'd go in and pull a rabbit out of his hat."

Among the survivors are his parents, Cletus M. and Jane L. Widmer of
Highland; a sister, Kim M. Widmer of Columbia, Ill.; a brother, Rad M.
Widmer of St. Louis; and his former wife, Donna L., of Kansas City.

Visitation will be from 4 to 8 p.m. today and 8 to 11 a.m. Tuesday at
Spengel-Boulanger Funeral Home, Highland, with the funeral service
immediately following. Burial will be at St. Joseph Catholic Cemetery,
Highland.
