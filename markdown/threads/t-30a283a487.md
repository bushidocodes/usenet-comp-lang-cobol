I wish you luck.  I think general acceptance of OO COBOL will wait
till after the standard that defines it is approved and implemented.  

There is too much risk of change in this area at present, and no
common implementation.  

Based on PROGRAMMER acceptance of the '85 COBOL changes, (Just NOW
getting into most shops) I'd say it'll be 2010 before you see any
breadth of OO COBOL development.

>Does anyone have any opinions on whether there's a future in
>object-oriented Cobol programming? Could this become a good niche
>expertise? Might some Cobol-using clients be more comfortable moving in
>this direction rather than to different
>languages/packages/systems/environments?

*Most* (Not all) COBOL new development is extending existing systems.
Fitting OO COBOL into existing code is a HARD HARD fit.  For BRAND new
development, you have two hurtles.

1.  Finding someone willing to use COBOL for a brand new project.
There is still considerable Myopia and just plain ignorance about just
WHAT COBOL can do TODAY.  There's NO reason to use any other language
for business development.  None.

2.  Finding staff willing to embrace the OO Philosophy.  It borders on
religious, and mixed with the politics of business you have a mess.
