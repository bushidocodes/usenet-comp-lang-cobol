First of all, OS/VS COBOL Release 2.3 was NOT suppose to ever replace "part"
of a word (i.e. "THIS" and "NEXT" in the middle of a word).  When I worked
for Micro Focus, we heard lots of reports of the mainframe doing this, but
when we asked customers to show us actual compiler output where this worked
this way - they never could provide us with examples (except one company who
had actually HOME modified the IBM compiler).  Therefore, I suggest that you
try the code again with the OS/VS COBOL compiler and see if it really works
the way that you think it does.

Having said that, if (by any chance) you do have a version of OS/VS COBOL
that really does have that "bug" (or home-grown modification) to allow
partial word replacement, then NO, there is no way to do that in VS COBOL
II - or the newer (strategic IBM COBOL for this-and-that).  The correct way
*IS* to put the entire part that you want in colons (or parenthesis), e.g.
":THIS:" in your (every one) copylibs.

P.S.  The IBM documentation (just like the ANSI Standard - '68, '74, AND
'85) is quite clear that COPY/REPLACING occurs on an ENTIRE "text-word" - so
this is NOT a case of the compiler not working "as documented".
