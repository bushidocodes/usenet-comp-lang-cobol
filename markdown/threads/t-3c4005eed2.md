Yes, it is pretty hairy looking at the code.

There are several things here that are immediately "obvious":

1. Manual LOCK is specified but the files are opened without LOCK on either
of them.

2. The whole process of transferring the FCD so it can be used by the EXTFH
is fraught, unnecessary, and best avoided. I see this a lot in Micro Focus
code; it's like programmers do it "because they can"... You could do the
whole process using the standard COBOL file definitions. If you want the
header locked while you process details then read it with lock and forget
about the EXTFH...

3. The "Securing of the file" through the EXTFH is probably unnecessary and
may be the cause of your problems. I note that although the FCD is
transferred TO the EXTFH copy, it is never transferred BACK FROM the FCD
copy... So, in effect, the code is creating more logical instances of the
two files (everytime it OPENs them) but it is only "flushing" one of them.
Sounds like a recipe for disaster.


My advice:

1. Take out all reference to the EXTFH and FCD. Do the job in standard
COBOL. This may have an effect on your network mapping also. (Which FCD is
currently being mapped to by the system? If you keep swapping the known file
that was established in your NET USE you can't expect things to work...)
There is no reason to "flush" the file; just CLOSE it.

2. Have ONE instance of an FCD (implicit through COBOL and let COBOL manage
it.)


Now to specifics...

1. The status of 10 is not happening on a CLOSE. That's not possible. And
yet you are receiving it. So... it is probably being left behind from a
previous OPEN which failed. Look carefully at the processing around your log
buffer.

2. The 41 is telling you that the file is already open. There is obviously a
flaw in the logic that is causing this. Given the messy swappings of
multiple FCDs, combined with multiple OPENs and CLOSEs it is probably true.

Have you stepped through the code in debug? Have you run a watch on the
status? When does it change? Don't rely on the logging as a debug tool.
The logging itself may not be working as you think. There is no substitute
for tracing and debugging.

Let us know how you get on.

Pete.
