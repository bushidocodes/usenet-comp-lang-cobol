To determine if your data files are of vision data type, you may do the
following:

vutil -info <filename>

This should report an error if the particular file is not a vision file.

To export data from the file:

vutil -unload -v -t <input filename> <output filename>

There is two options:

a) It is not a vision filetype. Acucobol does also support B-Trieve, if you
have access to any B-Trieve utility, you may give it a shot.

b) It is not indexed files but relative files. In that case vutil will do no
good because it is intended for indexed files.

In either case, you definitely should have someone experienced with Acucobol
to look at it. At least someone who is experienced with Cobol in general.
Trying to figure out on your own without knowing what to look for is at best
a lottery.

Have you tried to look at the .DAT file in a binary fileviewer? It may give
you an idea if you could hack the data.

Cheesle
