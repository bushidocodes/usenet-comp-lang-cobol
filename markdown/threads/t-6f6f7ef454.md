One of the things I miss about not being in Europe any more is visits to 
Stratford-upon-Avon.

The RSC has done so many innovative and imaginative interpretations of 
Shakespeare that I used to await each new season with some anticipation.

Although I didn't always like what they did I had to admit it was 
stimulating.

Traditionally, in the final scene of Hamlet, the stage is littered with dead 
bodies and there is usually a pretty good swordfight. The duelling is 
supposed to be for sport but there are shenanigans going on which render the 
supposedly harmless sport lethal.

The latest production hints at a novel approach to this situation.

Check this out:

http://www.rsc.org.uk/whats-on/hamlet/trailer-and-videos.aspx

It would be a 26 hour flight from where I am, but I'm thinking about it :-)

are still emerging. There was at least one eyewitness and police arrived

but were too late to save the man. It appears the culprit was a great white, 
at least 3 metres in length. (It was filmed from the helicopter). Other 
sharks were attracted and there were several in the vicinity by the time 
police got there.

This is the 15th death from shark attack in NZ since 1830 and the last one 
was in 1975. When you consider the hundreds of thousdands of people who go 
in the water every year, it is not something we normally think about a lot, 
but everyone is certainly thinking about it at the moment.
