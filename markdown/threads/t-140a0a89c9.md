I will assume that SMS controlled volumes have been ruled
out (I can understand why, I have more battles with SMS and
the ACS than I care to think about -- and I am one of the
ones that writes the ACS!).

First we need to understand the usage of the data sets. Are
they indexed, and do we want the index to be on a different
volume than the data set?

Next, we need to know if this is RAID based DASD or real
3390 type volumes (actually makes a difference in tuning and
VSAM parameters used!).

If we need the data to be scattered from each other, then
what we do is we take the volumes and we put them in some
kind of order (it would be nice to have them ordered such
that they aren't on the same paths...).

Data gets laid down, then the index gets allocated by
pulling from opposite ends of the volume list (we are
assuming that the opposite end of the volume list does not
share paths with the start). Meanwhile, keep track of the
storage used on the volume(s).

As we do this, we also use a round robin and last least used
methodology for allocating the volumes (yes, having them
sorted would make life easier!). Since you KNOW what the
volumes will have, you also know how much space is committed
on each volume. If the commit is over a certain % then you
go to the next least used volume.

If you can control the volumes, the ones with the LARGE
FILES on them (assuming that they would take up the whole
volume), then you do not want a VTOC-IX (waste of time &
space). Also, you will want the VTOC to be small and as
early on the VOLUME as is possible.

On the Volumes with lots of files, you may well want the
VTOC to be centered (again, with RAID based, this is a waste
of time!) and you will want a VTOC-IX.

This is just off the top of my head, not knowing many issues
that I would need to know to help you do allocation that is
also tuned for what you are doing.

I believe the simple answer to your question is yes there is a way to simply
distribute files to multiple volumes without sorting them provided you don't
need the "best", just a good approximation which will be true if file sizes
are not large compared to the size of the volumes.  I suggest keeping track
of the current total for each volume, and then add each file to the volume
with the smallest total.  This will ensure that each volume only differs in
total space by up to the size of the largest file.
