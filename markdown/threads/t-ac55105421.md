```
FYI,
  As of this "posting" the current tally of Public Review comments on the
reaffirmation of the '85 Standard -  that have made it to J4 is
   18 - Yes, reaffirm
     1 - No, don't reaffirm (and this was mine)

Several of the ones asking for "reaffirmation" have asked for ANSI/NCITS
(whoever) to find a way to make an "exception" to their normal rules to
allow the base document and its two amendments all to be reaffirmed - even
though an administrative "glitch" would seem to make this impossible.

I have NO idea if this is/will be possible - but I would add to Thane's
previous request for "input" that those of you who haven't commented yet but
want this Standard reaffirmed *might* want to explicitly ask for this
"exemption" in your Public Review comment.

On the other hand, if there are actually any readers who agree with me that
J4 doesn't have the resources/priorities to "maintain" the current Standard
(and get the next one out as soon as possible), then I would suggest that
you also send in your comments.  Similarly, if you (like me) think that the
current system IS broken and that the only hope for the future of COBOL is
to get it "out of the hands" of ANSI and ISO, (both the current Standard and
the new one), then I suggest that you send that in as well  and NOT assume
that the "administrative snafu" will necessarily do this for you.

As before, the way to submit your comments has been documented in
comp.lang.cobol - or can be found in my article at
    www.cobolreport.com
```
