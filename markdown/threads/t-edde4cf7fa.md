SENIOR APPLICATIONS SYSTEM ENGINEER
Req #: 3489
Department: COMPUTING & COMMUNICATIONS
Job Location: Seattle Campus
Posting Date: 10/29/2004
Closing Info: Open Until Filled
Salary: Salary and benefits are competitive. Salary is commensurate with 
qualifications and experience.


General Duties/Description:

SENIOR APPLICATIONS SYSTEM ENGINEER
COMPUTING & COMMUNICATIONS

This posting is for three (3) current openings in Administrative 
Information Systems Division of Computing & Communications.

Applying to this position places you in consideration for all three (3) 
current opportunities.

The University of Washington is seeking a Senior Applications System 
Engineer to work as a member of a team supporting financial, budget and 
planning, payroll, benefits and human resource information systems.  The 
successful candidate will play a key role in developing and supporting 
applications on mainframe platforms and distributed databases, on-line 
and batch systems, and interfaces across multiple computing platforms 
utilizing COBOL, mainframe ISAM, distributed databases and Internet 
based technologies.

Requirements:
This senior position requires a minimum:

A Bachelors degree in Public Administration and Finance, Business 
Administration, Computer Science, a related field or equivalent 
experience in a higher education environment.

A minimum five years experience performing progressively more complex 
and responsible tasks spanning all phases of information systems 
development.

Extensive on-line, database and batch application development and 
support experience with demonstrated competence in enterprise mainframe 
COBOL, supported file systems and job/workflow control languages.

Extensive on-call/production support experience.

Proven ability to work as a member of a team.  Demonstrated excellent 
skills for client interaction and written and verbal communication.


Desired:
It is desired that candidates also possess skills, knowledge and 
experience that include:

Special consideration will be given to relevant work in the Unisys NX 
series mainframe development environment, WFL programming, DMS II 
database maintenance and CANDE experience.

Extensive knowledge and working experience with large, complex heritage 
COBOL based financial, payroll, benefits and human resource systems.

Prior experience with administrative systems in a large university setting.

Specific experience with ARMS-based financial or ISI-based payroll, 
benefit and HR heritage systems.



Condition of Employment:
A satisfactory outcome from a criminal background verification may be 
required prior to hire.


The University of Washington is an equal opportunity, affirmative action 
employer. To request disability accommodation in the application 
process, contact the Disability Services Office at 206.543.6450 / 
206.543.6452 (tty) or dso@u.washington.edu.
