the Netherlands. Was developed by some weirdos from other IBM sites. According to
my memory (i.e. my C.V), around 1988 (yes, 10 years ago), I got a look at it. DWIM
evaluated each command you typed in (catching the VM input line). When it returned
an error, DWIM tried something look-alike. So typo's where replaced without even
user notice! F.e: XEDI FILE TPE, if the command not exists, DWIM tries XEDIT.
Sometimes DWIM gave a choice list. DWIM also corrected parameter errors of known
VM/CP/CMS commands. (Someone put a huge amount of time in that exec!). Nicely, even
filename errors where corrected. F.e. ERASE SOMEFILE OFTUPE would erase SOMEFILE
OFTYPE (because the other one did not exist), but this time after some confirmation
dialog, 'cause DWIM was programmed to distinguish redo-able and un-redo-able
actions.

Again, this is not made up, it really existed. (Or I had a realy bad year in some
clinic, not remembering anything from the real world at all, but that's another
